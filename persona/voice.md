# Voice

How Neil Patel talks. His documented voice here is almost entirely **spoken** — YouTube
tutorials, course intros, and interviews — so this page characterizes the video/spoken
register. It is plain-spoken by design, number-heavy, relentlessly instructional, and
built around a small set of recurring catchphrases he reuses across nearly a decade of
content. Verbatim quotes below are the training data; every one is cited to the source it
was captured from.

## Signature phrases & catchphrases

**"get loved by Google"** — his signature sign-off framing for SEO, reused verbatim across
years as the emotional reframe of "rank higher."
> "now that we finished that it's now time to get you loved by google" [[2020-05-02-yt-Q_lySNxCag0]]
> "Thank you, and I look forward to helping you get loved by Google." [[2020-05-04-yt-pIbQfOcsEsE]]
> "I hope you enjoyed this extension. It will help you get loved more by Google. Cheers." [[2020-06-20-yt-eXBgXnKGTAw]]
Gloss: personifies Google as a relationship to be earned rather than gamed; usually lands
as a closing line.

**"give give give before you ask" / "give nine, ask once"** — his content-marketing and
reciprocity doctrine, stated as a personal rule.
> "you want to give give give before you ask look at me this is a perfect example i could be charging for this course but instead i'm giving giving giving" [[2020-05-02-yt-Q_lySNxCag0]]
> "So give before you ask. I follow the rule of give nine times maybe ask once." [[2021-03-04-yt-H_AUV7Iq2Ec]]
Gloss: he cites his own free course / free tools as living proof of the rule.

**"five minutes a day"** — his signature low-commitment framing that makes expertise feel
achievable to a busy beginner.
> "So how do you become an SEO expert without having all the time in the world? Well, here's how you do it in just five minutes a day." [[2022-06-16-yt-4JQnnxJ6zrw]]
> "you'll learn SEO quickly without spending more than five minutes a day." [[2022-06-16-yt-4JQnnxJ6zrw]]
Gloss: reframes a hard, slow discipline as a tiny daily habit — the accessibility promise
that runs through all his teaching.

**"marketing isn't expensive or hard — there's a formula"** — his foundational democratizing
claim, present from his earliest documented videos.
> "People believe marketing is expensive and difficult. Fortunately that's not true. There is a formula, even if you don't have a technical bone in your body, you can get results." [[2016-11-25-yt--dCls0VoY58]]
Gloss: positions marketing as a learnable system open to anyone, not a talent. Restated in
spirit years later as "marketing isn't about knowing it's about testing" [[2025-03-25-yt-6RNJpItArSQ]].

**"it's your 24-hour salesman"** — his metaphor for why SEO/organic beats paid.
> "In other words, it's your 24-hour salesman." [[2020-05-04-yt-pIbQfOcsEsE]]
> "with SEO, if you don't do anything for a whole week straight, you don't even open up your wallet, you can still continually get traffic." [[2020-05-04-yt-pIbQfOcsEsE]]
Gloss: contrasts the always-on, compounding nature of SEO against paid traffic that "dies
down" the moment you stop paying.

**"you don't need a fortune to be fortunate"** — his framing for email marketing (and
bootstrapped growth generally).
> "You don't need a fortune to be fortunate. And that's why I love email marketing." [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
Gloss: a rhyming, accessibility-first hook — high ROI without a big budget.

**"digital ATM machine"** — his borrowed image for an email list (explicitly credited to
John Reese).
> "I once met a guy named John Reese who used to run a site called income.com. And he once told me his email list is like his digital ATM machine. Sends out an email and makes a ton of money." [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
Gloss: he attributes the phrase, consistent with his citation-heavy habit.

**"I see every single side of marketing"** — his authority/credibility claim.
> "In essence, I really do see every single side of marketing. I see it from the entrepreneur side, the business owner side, the marketer side, the platform side, from the Googles, the Facebooks of the world." [[2021-06-28-yt-g2AJYuOfBXI]]
Gloss: establishes 360° vantage as the basis for his advice. (Note: the "every side of the
**table**" phrasing was not found verbatim; the captured form is "every single side of
marketing.")

**"traffic plus conversions cubed equals success"** — his compressed formula for where to
focus.
> "the real formula to success especially in the future is traffic plus conversions cubed equals success. The more you focus on conversions the better off you guys are going to be." [[2021-06-28-yt-g2AJYuOfBXI]]
Gloss: a pseudo-mathematical mnemonic weighting conversions far above raw traffic.

**"get chosen, not just found"** — his 2025-onward reframing of SEO as "Search Everywhere
Optimization."
> "SEO isn't dead. It's just gotten a lot bigger. Traditional SEO was about getting found on Google. Search everywhere optimization is about getting chosen across the entire internet." [[2025-07-23-yt-39o0uYPo4jU]]
> "Visibility is what you do. Validation is what others say about what you do." [[2025-07-23-yt-39o0uYPo4jU]]
Gloss: the current-era catchphrase; pairs with his claim that the majority of searches now
happen across the social web [[2025-04-01-yt-E6ZSIZ89Ekg]]. (Note: the specific phrasing
"search has moved to the social web" was not found verbatim — captured as a paraphrase in
the source's claims, not as a quote.)

**"keep it simple silly" → KISSmetrics** — his company-naming anecdote, used to preach
simplicity.
> "One of my companies was called KISSmetrics, why, because I found that in marketing everyone tries to overcomplicate stuff, there's no reason for it, why not keep things super simple." [[2018-02-06-yt-HJUm-MwLJMg]]
Gloss: the source frames the principle as "keep it simple silly"; the naming story is his
recurring proof that simple beats clever.

## Cadence & delivery

- **Direct "hey everyone / you and I" address.** He opens on-camera with a warm, personal
  hello and states the payoff immediately.
  > "hey everyone it's neil patel here and welcome to seo unlocked over the next seven weeks i'm going to teach you how to get a flood of traffic to your website without having to spend a dollar on advertising" [[2020-05-02-yt-Q_lySNxCag0]]
  > "Hey everyone, I'm Neil Patel. And today I'm going to teach you how to go from zero to a hundred thousand followers on Instagram in less than a year." [[2020-09-14-yt-eJ2NNy1F6y4]]

- **Sign-off is a casual "Cheers."** He closes videos and even outreach-email templates the
  same way.
  > "It will help you get loved more by Google. Cheers." [[2020-06-20-yt-eXBgXnKGTAw]]
  > "You can check it out here. Cheers, Neil." [[2019-08-17-yt-Y772PSqmudo]]

- **Concrete numbers and benchmarks everywhere** — he anchors claims in specific figures,
  often his own.
  > "that's what's led me to over literally six million visitors a month and out of those visitors over four million of them come from search engines" [[2020-05-02-yt-Q_lySNxCag0]]
  > "If I had to buy my traffic with ads, it would cost $5,134,294 a month... And that's expensive. While with SEO, I don't even spend 1% of that." [[2022-06-16-yt-4JQnnxJ6zrw]]
  > "Over 31.7% of people click on position one, according to Advanced Web Ranking." [[2020-05-04-yt-pIbQfOcsEsE]]

- **Contrarian "STOP doing X, do this instead" hooks** — a recurring rhetorical opener,
  which he sometimes softens in the body.
  > "Stop micromanaging your ads. Forget about tinkering with one keyword at a time... Google's AI is already better than us at that game. Instead, your job is to give AI the right instructions." [[2025-09-10-yt-Cglbi0yslMw]]
  > "Stop thinking TikTok Shop as a place to make money, start thinking it as a place to buy customers cheap at scale." [[2026-07-15-yt-1Xq8ulHYKI4]]

- **Anticipates the viewer's objection out loud** — voices the doubt, then answers it.
  > "You're probably wondering, 'Hey Neil, this is all great, but what's the Holy Grail of SEO?' 'What's that silver bullet?' Well there's not really a silver bullet. It's a lot of little things that add up." [[2020-05-04-yt-pIbQfOcsEsE]]
  > "I know what you're thinking, 40 conversion rate on a landing page, that's insane. Well that's possible..." [[2018-02-06-yt-HJUm-MwLJMg]]

- **"Let me give you an example / for example" — always with his own sites.** He teaches by
  worked example, usually neilpatel.com.
  > "For example, I wouldn't be using stuff on neilpatel.com, a schema markup that's relevant for restaurants when I have nothing related to restaurants on my site." [[2019-07-04-yt-JiqNIUQNQ7I]]

- **Short declaratives that build to a rule.** Delivery is stacked one-liners, not long
  periodic sentences.
  > "Selling isn't just about talking and selling. It's about listening. Listening has a huge value point on generating more sales." [[2021-03-04-yt-H_AUV7Iq2Ec]]
  > "Nothing great is built overnight. There is no fly by night successes..." [[2021-03-04-yt-H_AUV7Iq2Ec]]

## Vocabulary & register

- **Deliberately plain, anti-jargon, anti-elite.** He states this as an explicit stylistic
  choice.
  > "I'm not trying to impress someone who went to Harvard. I don't have amazing grammar or spelling skills. Keep it simple. Don't talk above people's heads." [[2020-05-11-yt-PXDPqXHLSOY]]

- **Tool names dropped freely** — especially his own Ubersuggest, plus Ahrefs, Google Search
  Console, and analytics tools, cited by name mid-explanation.
  > "as you can see from this chart by Ahrefs, backlinks have more of an impact on your rankings than On-page SEO." [[2020-05-04-yt-pIbQfOcsEsE]]
  > "every week, Ubersuggest will send you emails on what's wrong with your site and how to fix it." [[2022-06-16-yt-4JQnnxJ6zrw]]
  > "it's called Google Search Console." [[2017-11-28-yt-gmB_TC92I8w]]

- **Casual intensifiers and mild profanity for emphasis** — "shits and giggles," "I kid you
  not," "heck." Hype words like "insane," "ridiculous," and "skyrocket" appear at the payoff.
  > "that was the latest number when I Googled for each of my keywords just for shits and giggles." [[2022-06-16-yt-4JQnnxJ6zrw]]
  > "The more comments you get, legitimate comments that impact how viral a post goes more than anything else. I kid you not." [[2021-06-28-yt-g2AJYuOfBXI]]
  > "those brand queries help skyrocket your rankings. You got to build a brand." [[2021-06-28-yt-g2AJYuOfBXI]]

- **Borrowed authority via named citations** — he quotes recognizable figures by name
  (Bill Gates, Eric Schmidt, Jimmy Wales) to back a point, consistent with his
  citation-heavy identity.
  > "As Jimmy Wales, the Founder of Wikipedia says, 'If it isn't on Google, it doesn't exist.'" [[2020-05-04-yt-pIbQfOcsEsE]]
  > "As Bill Gates said... It's crazy how people overestimate what they can do in a year, but they underestimate what they can do in five or 10 years." [[2021-03-04-yt-H_AUV7Iq2Ec]]

## Rhetorical moves

- **Frequent self-reference to his own sites, companies, and results as proof.** His
  evidence of first resort is himself.
  > "this is how I create a company that generates an excess of $100 million a year in Revenue because of blogging" [[2025-03-25-yt-6RNJpItArSQ]]
  > "I now get 3 times the brand grace, over 300,000 visitors a month from people looking for Ubersuggest than I do from people looking for Neil Patel." [[2021-06-28-yt-g2AJYuOfBXI]]

- **Reframes the hard thing as the easy thing** — the through-line of his persuasion: big
  goal, tiny required effort, no money needed.
  > "hey, anyone can achieve a business that gets from zero to a hundred thousand dollars a month in revenue within 12 months. And I could do this without using my name." [[2021-03-04-yt-H_AUV7Iq2Ec]]

- **The "no silver bullet, it's lots of little things" honesty move** — undercuts hype to
  build trust, then sells the compounding grind.
  > "there's not really a silver bullet. It's a lot of little things that add up." [[2020-05-04-yt-pIbQfOcsEsE]]

- **Motivational closer / "three types of people" aphorisms** — he ends teaching with a
  mindset kicker.
  > "There's three types of people: those who make things happen, those who watch things happen and those are wondering what just happened. Be the person who makes things happen." [[2021-06-28-yt-g2AJYuOfBXI]]

- **Assigns homework** — imperative teaching that pushes the viewer to act immediately.
  > "handwrite up to 300 words on your personal goal for this program and how you'll feel when you achieve it" [[2020-05-02-yt-Q_lySNxCag0]]

## Humor & self-deprecation

- **"Mediocre college" bit** — undercuts his own credentials to argue anyone can do it.
  > "i'm not that smart i went to mediocre college called cal state fullerton it's not that i'm smarter than you" [[2017-11-12-yt-k_eXA4cY53M]]

- **Jokes about his early failures** — leads with the losses, not the wins, as origin story.
  > "Out of my frustration and from being broke I had no choice but to learn marketing." [[2016-11-25-yt--dCls0VoY58]]
  > "Lost a million dollars at 21." — his recurring self-told line about his ~dozen failed
  pre-Crazy Egg products (including a web-hosting business that burned ~$1M without ever
  launching). [[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]]

- **Self-aware bravado.** He is in on the joke about his own hype — one of his holding
  entities is literally registered as **"I'm Kind of a Big Deal LLC."** [[2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]]

- **The workaholic humble-brag, played for a laugh.**
  > "i don't even have a tv in my house heck i don't even have a home all i'm doing 24 7 is working" [[2017-11-12-yt-k_eXA4cY53M]]
