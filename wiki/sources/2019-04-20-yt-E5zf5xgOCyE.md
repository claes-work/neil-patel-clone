# 7 Online Marketing Mistakes You Need to Stop Making

## Summary

Neil lists seven marketing mistakes that suppress results even when you are "doing the
right things": (1) **not collecting emails** — since only a tiny fraction of visitors ever
convert, capture emails via sliders/exit pop-ups (free with **Hello Bar**), treating an
email opt-in as a "micro-commitment" that makes conversion likelier; (2) **not collecting
push-notification subscribers** (free via **Subscribers.com**, a JS snippet / WordPress
plugin) so you can re-notify people about new content and products; (3) **not building a
brand** — Google increasingly favors brands over "fake news," so use the **Rule of 7**
(get people back ~7 times) via great UX/product; (4) **not interlinking** — Neil credits
his page-one ranking for "online marketing" largely to sidebar links to popular pages and
in-content links back to his pillar guide; (5) **only doing text content** — video is
where marketing is moving (LinkedIn/YouTube/Facebook/Instagram all want it to "crush the
TV networks"), so add video without dropping text; (6) **sticking to only one or two
channels** — marketing is competitive and venture-funded, so diversify; (7) **not asking
for the sale** — traffic that is never asked to buy won't convert.

- As of 2019-04-20, Neil said to run **more than one to three marketing channels** because
  marketing is now competitive and venture-funded, and to always **ask for the sale**.
