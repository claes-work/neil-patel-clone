# How to Build An Ultra Engaged Audience That Buys From You

## Summary
Five steps to turn an audience into buyers: (1) build an email list (any tool,
Mailchimp/ConvertKit), calling it "your digital ATM" — every blast to a promotable
product/service generates revenue; collect via landing pages, exit popups, or content
upgrades; (2) follow a ~90/10 ratio — 90% educational/value emails, ≤10% selling (his own
ratio is "less than 10%"); over-selling burns the list and prevents relationship-building;
(3) engage directly — answer audience questions, though it takes years of consistent
responses to create loyal "brand evangelists"; (4) avoid generic, cookie-cutter replies —
personalized advice converts followers into loyal followers, generic advice pushes them
away; (5) create video content — video conveys emotion better than text; his own cadence
is a few 5+-minute videos weekly plus several sub-minute clips, with long-form repurposed
into TikTok/Reels/Shorts.

## Key claims
- States a personal email-selling ratio of "less than 10%" of sends being promotional,
  against a general "90% educational" guideline (as of 2022-09-10, self-reported).
- Frames the email list explicitly as a "digital ATM" — a recurring metaphor in the
  corpus (previously attributed to a John Reese anecdote); here stated as his own framing
  without crediting Reese.
- States his own video cadence as "a few" 5-minute-plus videos per week plus "a handful"
  of sub-minute videos (as of 2022-09-10, self-reported).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Having followers and visitors is useless if you can't get them to buy from you.

> If you're too aggressive on the selling, you won't have an engaged audience. You're not building a relationship with them.

> Videos help convey emotion, and it's easier for people to connect with you through video, over text.
