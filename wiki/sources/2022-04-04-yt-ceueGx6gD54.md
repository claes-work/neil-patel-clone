# The Perfect Landing Page Blueprint

## Summary
A structured landing-page framework distilled from "over 20 years" of marketing
experience and "millions of dollars" driven through landing pages at his ad agency.
States his core operating value is data — "the closest thing to science that I
know" — and claims minor element-level tweaks have driven results ranging from
thousands to hundreds of thousands of dollars in additional revenue in various cases.
Walks through the blueprint element-by-element: minimal navigation (only critical
links, to avoid distracting from conversion points); a headline built around the
unique selling proposition, with a sub-headline that extends or adds a secondary
persuasive message; a hero shot showing the product/service in context of use; a
clear, simple, conversational call-to-action (e.g. "Start my free trial," "Get 50%
off") matched to the actual offer; industry-specific compliance elements where
needed; social proof (customer quotes, logos, case studies, video testimonials,
unboxing content, third-party review scores from Yelp/Amazon/Capterra/G2); benefits
(the positive impact of a feature) highlighted alongside features themselves; and a
guarantee (e.g. 30-day money-back, free shipping) to reduce purchase risk.

## Key claims
- States data, not intuition/art, is what he values most in landing-page design,
  calling it "the closest thing to science" available (self-reported operating
  philosophy, dated 2022-04-04) — analytics-cro.
- Claims that minor tweaks to individual landing-page elements have driven results
  ranging from "thousands of dollars" to "hundreds of thousand dollars" in additional

  — analytics-cro.
- Distinguishes "features" (a product/service quality) from "benefits" (the positive

- Self-reports his own software companies use conversational, offer-matched CTA
  copy such as "Start my free trial" (self-reported practice, dated 2022-04-04) —
  analytics-cro.
