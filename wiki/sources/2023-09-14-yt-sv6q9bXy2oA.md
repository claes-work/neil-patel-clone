# The LinkedIn Algorithm Changed - Here's How To Win

## Summary
Marketing School (co-hosted) episode breaking down a 2023-era LinkedIn algorithm change.
Neil frames LinkedIn as protecting its B2B-social-network niche rather than chasing viral
content like Facebook/Twitter/TikTok, and predicts that discipline will keep LinkedIn
growing. The two changes discussed: (1) a ~10% increase in followers seeing posts from
people they follow, and (2) posts that "share knowledge and advice" now get prioritized
platform-wide (~40% increase in reach for such content), which LinkedIn identifies via
audience specificity, staying in the author's core subject area, and comment quality —
meaning low-effort "engagement pod" comments ("great post!") no longer help as much
because the *comments themselves* now need to earn engagement.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- LinkedIn is a B2B social network by design and, per Neil, benefits from staying in
  that lane rather than trying to compete with Facebook/Instagram/TikTok for general
  virality; going outside that vertical would cause LinkedIn to "lose." (Neil,
  2023-09-14) — social-media.
- LinkedIn's 2023 algorithm change increases follower-content visibility by ~10% and
  prioritizes "knowledge and advice" posts platform-wide by ~40%, per LinkedIn's own
  reporting (Neil relays LinkedIn's stated figures). (Neil, 2023-09-14) — social-media.
- LinkedIn identifies "knowledge and advice" content via: the post speaking to a
  distinct/relevant audience, the author staying within their core subject area (not
  posting on everything), and posts drawing meaningful (not generic) comments. (Neil,
  2023-09-14) — social-media.
- Engagement groups / comment pods (generic "yes," "awesome," "amazing" comments) are
  losing effectiveness because LinkedIn now also weighs whether a comment itself
  receives engagement, not just whether a comment exists. (Neil, 2023-09-14) —
  social-media.
- Cites LinkedIn's acquisition of Lynda.com as evidence the platform is deliberately
  reinforcing an educational/knowledge-sharing identity. Self-reported interpretation,
  dated 2023-09-14. — social-media.
