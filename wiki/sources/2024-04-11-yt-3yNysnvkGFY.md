# People are saying they no longer use Google, Creator payouts grew 17% YoY, and more

## Summary
Three segments. (1) A SimilarWeb/Google-Trends-based rebuttal to "Google is dying to ChatGPT" claims: in Jan 2024, OpenAI had 1.639B visits (+7% MoM) vs. Google's 86.5B (+2.88% MoM); Bing grew 3.9% MoM from a smaller base. Feb 2024 (a shorter month) showed both Google and Bing dip in raw terms but roughly flat once day-count-normalized, while OpenAI still grew +1.9% MoM despite fewer days. Google Trends (trailing 12 months) shows ChatGPT search interest still rising but decelerating (from ~84 to ~99-100 on Google's index) versus its earlier explosive growth. Conclusion: ChatGPT is a genuine new "channel" (analogous to Facebook/Instagram/TikTok coexisting) that will shift *some* use cases away from Google over time, but the data does not support imminent Google collapse; a bigger near-term marketer concern raised is a possible US TikTok ban and no confirmed buyer. (2) A creator-economy growth recap (source: Alexis Ohanian, citing eMarketer): total creator earnings from social media projected at $13.7B in 2024 (+17% YoY); sponsored content $7B→$8.1B, platform payouts $2.9B→$3.2B, affiliate content $894M→$1.1B, subscriptions $212M→$274M (a ~30% YoY jump cited). The stated interpretation is that money is concentrating toward creators seen as genuinely high-ROI within a specific industry/niche (not just follower count), a dynamic compared to the "hollowing out of the middle class" pattern in the broader economy, alongside a personal aside about explaining homelessness to his four-year-old. The podcast's own multi-year timeline (approaching its 8th year) is offered as an example of how long durable audience-building can take. (3) A recap of three first-party SEO/content statistics: 94.29% of indexed web pages get zero organic-search traffic in a trailing 30-day window; a 5-month AI-vs-human content study again found human content earning 5.44x more traffic (repeating the 2024-04-03 episode's figure); and 59.41% of analyzed social content (from accounts with existing followers) had literally zero engagement (no likes, shares, or comments) in a trailing 30 days. Roughly 1.27B of Google's ~8.5B daily searches (~15%) are queries never searched before in Google's history — cited as the rationale for having acquired AnswerThePublic. Closing riff: an interview with writer Dave Perell (who has interviewed Marc Andreessen, Sam Altman, Chamath Palihapitiya, Tim Ferriss) on his heavy editing discipline (cutting up to 60% of an interview) and a related Paul Graham anecdote about aggressive editing as a hallmark of top-tier writing; contrasted with the hosts' own lighter editing approach to their podcast and blogging.
