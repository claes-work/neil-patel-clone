# How to Qualifying Your Leads | Ask These 4 Questions to Generate Quality Leads

## Summary
A short lead-generation video in which Neil Patel argues that most leads any business
generates are low-quality regardless of price point, and that you should qualify leads
rather than waste time chasing bad ones. Instead of collecting only name/phone/email, add
form fields that qualify: how can I help you, and in his own agency, questions like
revenue, profit, goals for the next 12 months, timeline to start ("in a year" = weak
lead; "within a month" = hot), and budget. He cites a friend (Ryan, who runs marketing at
HubSpot) that they sometimes ask 15-20 questions / use 20 form fields to instantly know if
a lead is qualified. He also recommends Clearbit to enrich a lead with company and job
title so you can cross-reference — e.g. an associate at a big company still can't close
unless they bring their boss onto the call. The governing principle: don't optimize for
lead volume, optimize for lead quality — 10 qualified leads beat 200 junk leads.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-02, Neil states:
- Most leads a business generates are low-quality, regardless of whether you sell something
  for $10 or $1M (as of 2017-09-02).
- Rather than collecting only name/phone/email, add form fields that qualify the lead —
  "how can I help you," and in his agency: revenue, profit, 12-month goals, timeline to
  start, and budget (as of 2017-09-02).
- Timeline is a strong qualifier: "start in a year" is a weak lead; "start within a month"
  means hot and ready to move (as of 2017-09-02).
- His friend Ryan, who runs marketing at HubSpot, says they sometimes ask 15-20 questions
  / use 20 form fields to instantly know if a submitted lead is qualified (as of
  2017-09-02; second-hand/self-reported).
- Use Clearbit to enrich a lead with company and job title and cross-reference it; an
  associate at a large company can't close unless they get their boss on the call (as of
  2017-09-02).
- Governing principle: don't optimize for lead volume, optimize for quality — 10 qualified
  leads beat 200 mostly-junk leads (as of 2017-09-02).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Your website is generating leads, but most of them are shit ... I don't care if you're selling something for 10 dollars or a million dollars. Most the leads you generate are going to be crap."

> "In my business, we ask questions like, 'What is your revenue? What is your profit? What do you want to accomplish in the next 12 months?' ... If they say they're looking to get started in a year, that's not really a great lead. If they say they want to get started within the next month, that means they're hot."

> "It's better to have 10 qualified leads than it is to have 200 leads that are mainly all junk ... don't just optimize for lead collection, optimize for quality leads."
