# Reddit Marketing 101: 5 Steps Ways to Drive a Ton of Free Traffic from Reddit

## Summary
Neil frames Reddit as the modern successor to Digg — a top-100 website that most marketers wrongly ignore — and lays out a 5-step process for earning free traffic from it. Step 1: participate in the community so you learn what content works and what people like. Step 2: join subreddits, the topical subsections covering everything from fashion and marketing to Bitcoin and Ethereum, and keep participating in them. Step 3: be transparent, because redditors can smell marketers from a mile away and will sniff out and reject non-transparent promotion. Step 4: replicate and outdo content that is already performing, invoking Brian Dean's (Backlinko) Skyscraper Technique — take an existing "10 ways" piece and make a "101 ways" version that is 10x more amazing so competitors won't bother copying it. Step 5: submit your content, at which point your built-up following upvotes it and drives ongoing traffic. He closes with a caveat: aggressive email-capture tactics (sliders, pop-ups) like those on neilpatel.com annoy redditors, and recommends using Hello Bar's setting to hide opt-ins from Reddit visitors, or else people will downvote and refuse to share your content.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-28, Neil states:
- Reddit is the modern equivalent of Digg (which is now largely abandoned) and is one of the top 100 websites on the web; ignoring it for traffic is a mistake (as of 2017-10-28).
- The first step in Reddit traffic generation is to participate in the community, because participation teaches you what people like and what content works (as of 2017-10-28).
- The second step is to join subreddits — topical subsections of Reddit that exist for virtually every topic, including fashion, marketing, and cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum (as of 2017-10-28).
- The third step is to be transparent, because redditors can detect marketers easily and non-transparent promoters get sniffed out and their content fails (as of 2017-10-28).
- The fourth step is to replicate content already performing well on Reddit and create a better version of it (as of 2017-10-28).
- Brian Dean of Backlinko coined the "Skyscraper Technique": find strong existing content (e.g., a "10 ways" post) and create something more detailed (e.g., "101 ways") that is 10x more amazing, so much so that others won't bother copying it (as of 2017-10-28).
- The final step is submitting your content; a built-up community and following on Reddit will then upvote it, generating traffic and more visitors over time (as of 2017-10-28).
- Aggressive email-capture elements like sliders and pop-ups (as used on neilpatel.com) annoy redditors and hurt Reddit traffic (as of 2017-10-28).
- Hello Bar has a setting to suppress opt-in displays for visitors coming from Reddit, which prevents irritating redditors (as of 2017-10-28).
- If your site is generally too aggressive, redditors won't share your content, will downvote it, and you'll get no traffic from Reddit (as of 2017-10-28).
