# How To Skyrocket Your SEO with This SIMPLE Link Building Process

## Summary

A solo tactical monologue on automating the most time-consuming part of link building —
**outreach**. Neil frames links as one of Google's biggest ranking factors and outreach (the
manual work of contacting sites to convince them to link) as the bottleneck, then walks
through a four-tool automated pipeline. **(1) Dux-Soup** on LinkedIn: it auto-visits many
profiles in your space, prompting connection requests, and lets you send automated mass
messages — use those to ask for links, or at minimum a social share of your content (more
LinkedIn/social traffic → more eyeballs → more chances at a link). **(2) ScrapeBox**: scrapes
Google for all relevant sites in your niche (e.g. everyone writing about web design if you made
a web-design infographic), producing an outreach target list. **(3) FindThatLead**: pulls the
contact/email info for those domains automatically so you don't hunt each site manually.
**(4) Mixmax or Mailshake**: mass-emails the contacts you gathered, with scheduled follow-ups
for non-responders, auto-responders, and sequences — maximizing links with the least human
effort. His pitch: link building feels expensive and manual, but these tools make it cheap
and largely automated. He notes he still prefers directly asking for a link over just a
social share.
