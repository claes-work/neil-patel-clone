# Do Popups Still Work? Or Is it Bad For Your Digital Marketing Efforts? EXPLAINED

## Summary
Neil Patel's verdict is that popups still work — the reason they remain ubiquitous despite users hating them is that they convert, and site owners would remove them if they didn't. He acknowledges Google changed its algorithm to penalize overly intrusive popups, especially on mobile, and predicts Google will eventually extend that to desktop. His workaround is timing: he still runs mobile popups on his own sites and his friends' sites (he cites TimothySykes.com) without losing traffic because they don't fire the popup the moment someone arrives — they wait until a visitor has been on the site at least 20–30 seconds and is about to leave, triggering the popup on exit (exit intent). He recommends the free tool Hello Bar (Hellobar.com) to build an exit popup, and notes full-screen popups (a "page takeover," as used on the NeilPatel.com blog for first-time visitors) convert best. He stresses the offer must give real value — an eBook, guide, PDF, or white paper, or for eCommerce a 10% off coupon or free shipping — rather than a generic "subscribe" or "buy now," because the visitor is leaving precisely because they didn't find what they wanted. Bottom line: use popups, but only on exit, which he says protects Google rankings while still lifting conversions.
