# Content Marketing is Changing - This is Where it is Heading in 2020 ★

## Summary
Solo forecast on where content marketing goes in **2020** (the current YouTube title says
2023; the captions say 2020 throughout). Premise: content marketing stays one of the
strongest channels, but with **over a billion blogs** the old tactics stop working. Five
shifts. (1) **Structured markup / rich snippets / voice** — mark up the site and use proper
header hierarchy so Google can crawl and decipher the important content; he repeats the
Comscore claim that over 50% of 2020 searches will be voice-related as Alexa and Google Home
spread, and cites **Search Engine Land** that roughly 50% of searches end in **no click**
because Google answers on the SERP — so the play is to win the snippet, examining whoever
currently holds it and doing a better job on that page. (2) **Make E-A-T a priority**
(expertise, authority, trust): he uses himself as the example of what Google's **Medic
Update** was designed to stop — a marketing expert blogging medical or financial advice
should not rank no matter how many backlinks the site has, adding "what do I know about
401(k)s and retirement? Heck, I'm 34 years old." Practical steps: publish only in your area
of expertise, quality over quantity, put your **bio on every page**, link out to your social
profiles, and guest-post on popular niche sites for authority. (3) **Personalization** — his
own blog covers everything from e-commerce to paid ads to analytics, which interests nobody
entirely; his fix is the "I want to learn about ___" dropdown on the Neil Patel blog, one of
his most-clicked elements, which reshapes the content to the reader's chosen topic and, he
says, raises engagement and return visits while cutting bounce rate. (4) **AI/ML keeps taking
precedence** — he describes Google's **BERT** update (transcribed as "Blur") understanding
that "Brazilian citizen looking for US visa" is about a Brazilian needing a US visa rather
than the reverse; the survival strategy is answering **precise questions** exceptionally well
(mine Quora and Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas questions report), because Google now prefers the
most relevant page even with fewer backlinks and lower domain authority. (5) **Interactive
content and imagery take off** — beyond video and podcasting, the under-discussed format is
swipeable Instagram carousels of bite-size educational micro-content ("infographics you can
engage with"), where he claims about half his likers also share the post (2,000 likes →
another 1,000 shares). Closes with an agency pitch.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil said content marketing would remain one of the strongest channels in 2020, but that with **over a billion blogs** the old tactics no longer work.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil recommended structured markup plus proper header hierarchy so Google can crawl and decipher the important pages/data — the route to rich snippets and voice traffic.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil repeated the **Comscore** claim that over 50% of 2020 searches would be voice-related, driven by Alexa and Google Home adoption ("whether you like them or not").
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil cited **Search Engine Land** that roughly **50% of searches result in no click** because Google answers on the results page via rich snippets.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil's snippet tactic was to look at whoever currently answers the question in the rich snippet and, if you can do a better job, rewrite that page to displace them.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil defined **E-A-T** as expertise, authority and trust, and explained Google's **Medic Update** using himself as the negative example: a marketing expert blogging medical advice or surgeries should not rank regardless of site authority or backlinks.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil extended the same logic to finance and stated his age — "What do I know about 401(k)s and retirement? Heck, **I'm 34 years old**" — a self-reported biographical data point consistent with a 1985 birth year.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil's E-A-T checklist was: write only where you are an expert, prioritize quality over quantity, put your **bio on every single page**, link out to your social profiles, and guest-post on popular sites in your niche.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil predicted increased **personalization** because broad blogs (his own included, spanning e-commerce, paid ads and analytics) can't interest everyone.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil said the "I want to learn about ___" dropdown on the Neil Patel blog — which re-filters all content to the chosen topic — is one of the most-clicked areas of his website, and that it increases engagement and return visits while lowering bounce rate.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil described Google's **BERT** update (captioned "Blur") as parsing "Brazilian citizen looking for US visa" correctly instead of returning results about US citizens travelling to Brazil.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil said the way to survive AI/ML-driven Google updates is answering **precise questions** better than anyone — sourcing them from Quora and Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas questions report — because "it's not about having 5,000 blog posts, it's about having the best blog post that answers that question."
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil said Google is shifting from ranking high-authority sites that answer generically to ranking the **most relevant** page even when it has fewer backlinks and lower domain authority, because that is the better user experience.
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil said interactive content and imagery would take off, naming swipeable Instagram carousels of bite-size educational micro-content as the format nobody is talking about — "infographics, but more so infographics that you can engage with."
- As of 2020-01-04, Neil claimed that on those carousels roughly **half** of the people who like the post also share it (2,000 likes → ~1,000 shares), which he called crazy-high engagement.
