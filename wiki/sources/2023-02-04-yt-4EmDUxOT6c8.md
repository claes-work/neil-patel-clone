# 3 Easy Ways to Boost Conversions

## Summary
Opens with average website conversion rate stats (~2.35% average, up to 11% for
top-performing/highly-targeted landing pages) before three CRO tactics. (1) Exit
pop-ups — cites Sumo data of a 3.09% average pop-up conversion rate; argues the
"no one likes pop-ups" objection doesn't apply to exit-intent pop-ups specifically,
since the visitor was already leaving with nothing to lose, and that the perceived
spamminess comes from a weak offer ("give me your email") vs. a strong one (free
software, e-book, coupon). (2) Testimonials, reviews, and logos — cites a
ConversionXL interview with "growth marketing expert Angie S." claiming quality
social proof can lift conversions up to 400% when it addresses buyer uncertainty;
self-reports seeing "massive conversion lifts" from reviews/testimonials at his
agency; recommends placing social proof on both the product page and the checkout
page, not just one. (3) Countdown timers — cites a ConversionXL study of one brand
raising sales 33% via a single limited-time offer with a countdown timer, while
self-reporting his own agency typically sees a smaller 7–8% lift during offer
periods rather than 33%. Frames the accumulation of small CRO wins via a British
cycling coach Dave Brailsford "marginal gains" analogy (aerodynamic helmets, better
mattresses, more comfortable bike seats) — small individual improvements compound
into a decisive edge.

## Key claims
- Cites (source unnamed): average website conversion rate ~2.35%, best-performing
  sites up to 11% (typically for targeted, non-purchase or highly targeted purchase
  flows).
- Cites Sumo: average pop-up conversion rate of 3.09%.
- Cites a ConversionXL interview with "Angie S." (surname unclear/mispronounced in
  video) claiming social proof addressing uncertainty can lift conversions up to
  400%.
- Cites a ConversionXL study: one brand raised sales 33% using a countdown timer on a
  limited-time offer.
- Self-reports NP Digital's own countdown-timer campaigns typically produce a 7–8%
  conversion lift during the offer window, not the 33% cited in the ConversionXL case
  study (self-reported, dated 2023-02-04) — a smaller, more conservative real-world
  figure explicitly contrasted against the cited case study.
- Attributes the "marginal gains" framing (small compounding improvements win
  competitions) to British cycling coach Dave Brailsford, applying it as a metaphor
  for CRO practice.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> These are all examples of how you win in the long run by doing all those little things, because they add up 1% here, another percent there... and that's enough to win and beat the competition.
