# The Perfect SEO Setup for WordPress: 8 Plugins to Skyrocket Your Rankings and Traffic

## Summary
Neil Patel walks through his recommended SEO setup for WordPress websites, framed around eight specific plugins. He opens by naming the ranking factors he says Google weighs most — user experience, on-page SEO, site speed, mobile-friendliness, internal link structure, and structured markup — while noting Google's algorithm has over 200 factors. Each plugin is mapped to one of those factors: caching, image compression, on-page optimization, security, mobile/AMP delivery, lazy loading, rich snippets, and SSL. Throughout he argues that modern SEO requires doing "every little thing right" rather than most things, since the tactics that worked five or six years ago no longer suffice. He closes by directing viewers who want more help to his agency, Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- Google looks at hundreds of ranking factors — over 200 in its algorithm — including user experience, on-page SEO, site speed, mobile-friendliness, internal link structure, and structured markup (as of 2019-07-04).
- W3 Total Cache improves load times, helps connect to CDNs, and caches content so the server does not repeatedly push the same content; Neil recommends it because most hosting providers he has seen do not have caching built in (as of 2019-07-04).
- Smush Image Compression reduces the server/file size of images while keeping them sharp, so pages load faster (as of 2019-07-04).
- Yoast SEO handles on-page optimization, meta tags, title tags, content analysis, sitemap creation, and structured markup; WordPress is fairly SEO-friendly out of the box, but Yoast adds the important final touches (as of 2019-07-04).
- Doing "most" SEO things worked five or six years ago, but Google now wants every detail done right, and combining all of them is what produces ranking climbs and extra search traffic (as of 2019-07-04).
- Sucuri helps prevent hacking; Neil says a hacked site's rankings and traffic tank and can be hard to recover — sometimes taking two or three months even after the fix (as of 2019-07-04).
- AMP for WordPress helps mobile users and getting into Google News; Neil observed little added mobile traffic in the US from AMP, but a large increase in countries with weaker mobile infrastructure such as Brazil (as of 2019-07-04).
- a3 Lazy Load makes images load as the user scrolls, improving user experience; Neil says search engines prefer this and notes neilpatel.com uses this behavior (as of 2019-07-04).
- All In One Schema Rich Snippets improves search visibility via rich snippets, but schema markup should only be applied where it is relevant to the site — using irrelevant markup (e.g. restaurant markup on an unrelated site) will "irritate Google" and hurt rankings over time (as of 2019-07-04).
- Really Simple SSL (or SSL from a host/domain provider) is effectively a prerequisite for ranking; Neil claims 90% of websites that rank at the top have SSL installed, and non-secure sites trigger browser warnings that cause bounces and can decrease rankings over time (as of 2019-07-04).
