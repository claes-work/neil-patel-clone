# How to Climb Google's Rankings From Pages #2 to Page #1

## Summary
Opens with the recurring "page 2 = where you bury the dead bodies" bit (already
logged), then splits page-2-to-page-1 tactics into two decision paths. (1) Optimize an
existing page when the target keyword is closely related to that page's topic: analyze
the current top 10 for the keyword, compare content depth/length, mine Quora and Reddit
for audience questions the page doesn't answer, check Google's "People Also Ask" box for
gaps, then rewrite to beat the top 10. Names the tool ClickFlow for A/B-testing title
tags/meta descriptions to raise click-through rate, framed as often faster than
backlink-building for moving from page 2 to page 1 — argues Google reranks pages based
on aggregate click preference, independent of backlink counts, as a direct user-signal
mechanism. (2) Create a new page when the ranking keyword is unrelated to the existing
page's topic (uses a hypothetical "How to Sell on Instagram Without Spending a Dime"
page ranking for "buying Instagram followers" as an example of topic mismatch he says
he'd never actually recommend to clients), then internally link the old page to the new
one.

## Key claims
- States plainly that click-through-rate optimization (via title-tag/meta-description
  A/B testing, e.g. with the tool ClickFlow) can move rankings from page 2 to page 1
  "not through backlinks," framing aggregate user click preference as a direct Google
  reranking signal (self-reported SEO tactic/opinion, dated 2021-12-11) — seo. Extends
  the existing CTR-from-paid-ads/pogo-sticking-signal material with an explicit
  backlinks-vs-CTR contrast.
- Gives a concrete decision rule for optimize-vs-create: optimize the existing page only
  if the target keyword is topically close to it; otherwise build a new page and
  internally link the old one to it (self-reported tactical framework, dated
  2021-12-11) — seo. A more explicit articulation than prior general internal-linking
  guidance.
- States he would not advise clients or visitors to rank an off-topic page for an
  unrelated keyword just to "trick people" — an ethics-adjacent aside on manipulative
  SEO (self-reported, dated 2021-12-11) — seo.
