# How to Improve Your Mobile Website Load Time

## Summary
Neil Patel explains three tactics to decrease mobile website load time, arguing that faster mobile pages directly protect revenue. He cites an Amazon study finding the retailer loses roughly 1% of income for every additional second of load time. His three recommendations are: (1) implement the Google AMP (Accelerated Mobile Pages) framework — via WordPress plugins for WordPress blogs, or custom coding (following Google's documentation) for non-WordPress sites; (2) run the site's URL through Google PageSpeed to see what is done right, what is wrong, and what to change, paying attention specifically to the mobile version rather than the desktop breakdown; and (3) remove unnecessary elements, avoiding replicating the full desktop experience on mobile and instead keeping mobile pages short, minimal, and to the point.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-08, Neil states:
- Most people visiting your website now come from mobile devices (phones or tablets), and more people use Google on mobile than on desktop (as of 2017-09-08).
- The slower a site loads, the more money you lose, so decreasing load time protects revenue (as of 2017-09-08).
- An Amazon study found that for every additional second the site loads slower, Amazon loses about 1% of its income — billions of dollars at Amazon's scale (as of 2017-09-08).
- Using the Google AMP framework ensures a website loads extremely fast on mobile devices (as of 2017-09-08).
- WordPress blogs can become AMP-compatible via plugins; non-WordPress sites require custom coding, which Google's documentation walks you through (as of 2017-09-08).
- Google PageSpeed shows, for the mobile device, what you are doing right, what you are doing wrong, and what to change, breaking down both mobile and desktop versions — and you should focus on the mobile version (as of 2017-09-08).
- Many mobile websites wrongly replicate their full desktop site; users will not scroll through long, replicated pages on mobile (as of 2017-09-08).
- Minimizing the elements, text, and information on the mobile screen and keeping it short decreases load time and results in more visitors and more sales (as of 2017-09-08).
