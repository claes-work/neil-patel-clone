# 5 Ecommerce Optimization Tips to Improve Your Product Page Conversion Rates

## Summary
Neil teaches five optimization tactics for ecommerce product pages, framed around the argument that product pages generate almost all of a store's income because they are where visitors add to cart and buy. The five moves are: (1) optimize meta-tags — the title tag and meta-description should spell out what the product is, its benefits, and why people should buy, while including the product's main keywords, because this is what searchers read in Google results; (2) improve user metrics by making the page compelling enough that visitors don't bounce back to Google — via appealing descriptions, high-quality images or video reviews, and customer reviews, using amazon.com as the model; (3) leverage H1/H2 headings that describe the product to add keywords and rank higher; (4) include a bullet-pointed feature list because people often Google product features, which drives search traffic and boosts conversions; and (5) cross-link to related and add-on products (again the Amazon model) so Google indexes more product pages and they rise in rankings together, yielding more traffic and sales. The talk blends SEO and CRO but leans heavily on on-page SEO signals.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-07, Neil states:
- Ecommerce site owners generate almost all of their income from their product pages, since that is where visitors add to cart and buy (as of 2017-08-07).
- Product-page title tags and meta-descriptions should describe the product, its benefits, and why to buy it, and should include the product's main keywords, because Google pulls the title and description shown in search results from the page's source code (as of 2017-08-07).
- ★ If a visitor lands on a product page and bounces straight back to Google search, it signals to Google that they didn't find what they wanted and the page's ranking should drop; keeping visitors engaged (and converting) requires focusing on the product itself (as of 2017-08-07).
- To improve user metrics, product pages should have appealing descriptive text, high-quality images or a video review that shows what buyers will get without being present, and customer reviews so shoppers can judge whether it's a good or bad product (as of 2017-08-07).
- Using H1 or H2 headings that describe the product adds a keyword to the page and helps it rank higher (as of 2017-08-07).
- Including a bullet-pointed feature list captures searchers who Google product features, driving more search traffic and boosting conversions (as of 2017-08-07).
- ★ Cross-linking to relevant, add-on, and similar products helps Google index more of a site's product pages, lifting them all in the rankings and producing more traffic and buyers (as of 2017-08-07).
- Amazon is cited as the reference model for product reviews, headings usage, and cross-linking of related/add-on products (as of 2017-08-07).
