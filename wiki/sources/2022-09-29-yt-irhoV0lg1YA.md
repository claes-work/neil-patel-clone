# 7 Lessons Learn From Hiring a CEO That Is Smarter Than Me

## Summary
A first-person explanation of Neil's stated business model: he says he is "never the CEO"
of his own companies, instead hiring people smarter than him who have already done the
job elsewhere. He frames this as following two self-assessed weaknesses — being "a
terrible manager" of large (100+ person) organizations, and not enjoying most of the
CEO job (meetings, quarterly planning) despite enjoying teaching/mentoring. He gives
seven lessons from hiring CEOs/executives: (1) hire for proven industry experience,
people follow good people; (2) longevity/promotion history at prior jobs signals
quality; (3) experience doesn't equal being right — seasoned hires can be jaded and
resist new (AI/ML-era) approaches; (4) let hires do things their way, not "your way or
the highway"; (5) don't sacrifice culture fit even for an otherwise-qualified candidate;
(6) look for "player coaches" who still do hands-on work, not pure managers; (7)
prioritize hard work over pedigree/credentials (explicitly says he doesn't care about
Harvard/Stanford or GPA).

## Key claims
- States he has been "an entrepreneur for roughly 22 years" as of 2022-09-29
  (self-reported; consistent with a mid-2000s entrepreneurial start).
- Self-assessment: "I am a terrible manager when it comes to managing a large amount of
  people" (100+); says he "don't enjoy most of" being a CEO/leader despite liking
  teaching (as of 2022-09-29, self-reported).
- States his business-model pattern is to never be CEO of his own company — instead
  hiring proven outside executives as CEO (as of 2022-09-29, self-reported; consistent
  with NP Digital's Mike Gullickson / Mike Kamo CEO structure already documented in
  `persona/biography.md`).
- States he doesn't weight candidates' college pedigree or grades ("I don't even care
  what their grades were... or even if they have a college degree") — hard work is the
  filter (as of 2022-09-29, self-reported hiring philosophy).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Instead, I hire people who are smarter than me and have done it multiple times before and I make them the CEO, right?

> Good people follow good people around. And good people don't follow bad people around.

> There's multiple ways to skin a cat. Your way may be correct, but their way may also be correct too.

> If someone isn't a hard worker you are screwed, because if they aren't willing to work hard how can you expect the rest of the company to work hard?
