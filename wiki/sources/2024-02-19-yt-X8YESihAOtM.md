# Marketing's Open Air Conspiracy, Big changes to paid media, Brandformance, & more (Marketing School)

## Summary
A crossover Marketing School episode: hosts Neil Patel and Eric Siu bring on guests **Ralph Burns** (Tier 11) and **Kasim Aslam** (Solutions 8), co-hosts of the "Perpetual Traffic" podcast. The bulk of the talk time is the two guests. Ralph drives the "brandformance" thesis (performance/DR agencies moving up-funnel into brand and consideration), and Kasim delivers his signature "there's no such thing as attribution" rant (measurement as an "open-air conspiracy," 99% black box, spend where you can't see, over protracted periods not 30-day windows). **Neil's own contribution is comparatively small and is the only persona-trainable material here:** an interjection pushing the guest for concrete revenue/spend numbers, and a measured take on measurement — the worst players in the attribution problem are the ad platforms themselves (they can build billion-dollar AI but won't accurately report ROAS or sync with your bank/inventory/CRM), data will keep getting worse (cookie deprecation) and can never track everything (especially top-of-funnel discovery on Instagram/TikTok), yet analytics is more important than ever because it gives *directional movement*, not a crystal ball — and lazy operators blame "the algorithm" or "the economy" instead of digging into the data. Eric largely moderates and tees up (but does not develop) Neil's older "business coaches" controversial take (see [[wiki/sources/2024-01-11-yt-f96p3Jj1MFI]]). Because captions are unlabeled and two non-host guests speak most of the episode, guest identities are inferred from context and quarantined below — the "brandformance" and "full-funnel / no-such-thing-as-attribution" headlines are GUEST material, NOT Neil's.

## Key claims
- The worst players in the measurement/attribution problem are the ad platforms themselves — Facebook most of all: they can spend billions of dollars building AI but won't spend to accurately report return on ad spend. (Neil, 2024-02-19)
- It is not genuinely hard for platforms to sync with bank accounts, inventory software, and CRM/Salesforce to compute true ROAS and see which leads actually closed — they just don't, and instead report inflated numbers (e.g. "1,700% ROAS") and tell you to keep spending. (Neil, 2024-02-19)
- Data and analytics will keep getting worse (cookie deprecation, privacy) and can never measure everything — you can't track everyone who saw your Instagram/TikTok video (the platform has that data and won't pass it to you), and a non-follower can discover you on Reels/TikTok, later Google you, and buy, with most of that journey untrackable. (Neil, 2024-02-19)
- Despite that, data/analytics is MORE important than ever, especially post-cookie: it is not a crystal ball and not perfect, but it gives directional movement/improvement — a mild pushback on the guest's "1% / total black box" framing (you can measure quite a bit, just not everything). (Neil, 2024-02-19)
- Organizations without real data/analytics teams lazily blame "the algorithm changed" or "the economy" when a campaign dips (e.g. not realizing a top-of-funnel campaign turned off months earlier fed the channel that just died), instead of diving into the data to figure it out; the discipline is to investigate, not excuse. (Neil, 2024-02-19)
- Interviewing method (characteristic): when a guest describes a strategy, Neil pushes for a concrete real-life scenario — revenue tier, dollar spend, and the % split between retargeting and awareness — rather than accepting abstractions. (Neil, 2024-02-19)

**Ralph Burns (guest — Tier 11; context):**
- "Brandformance": performance/DR agencies are moving up-funnel into brand. You don't shift 100% of budget to awareness; you reallocate some conversion budget toward consideration (first touch, coupon code, opt-in) and pure awareness with no CTA. CPMs are far smaller up-funnel, so blended ad spend drops and ROAS/CPA improve — "counterintuitive and not very popular." (guest, 2024-02-19)
- You can build your OWN conversion/lookalike audiences using reach, awareness, traffic, link-click, and video-view campaigns instead of only hammering website-conversion. Meta is nudging high-volume performance agencies toward these other objectives — analogous to how Performance Max was created to monetize the display network. (guest, 2024-02-19)
- Fits mid-size brands (~8-figures, roughly $100M–$200M) with a marketing director/VP; not SMBs or hyper-local (real-estate agent, lawyer with a 10-mile radius), and enterprise is avoided (bureaucracy). Personal-injury-law signed-case value ~$500–$5,000; e-com AOV ~$50–$200. (guest, 2024-02-19)
- The measurement "leap of faith": cites a study of 800 campaigns / 30 years / 83 industries showing activation (conversion) campaigns plateau and top-of-funnel impact surfaces ~6 months out; go up-funnel in increments, be patient. "David Ogilvy didn't have a pixel and he was a pretty good advertiser." (guest, 2024-02-19)
- View-through illusion: a client with millions of YouTube followers saw Facebook claim 13x ROAS, but ~80% of "conversions" were 1-day view-through; stop the Facebook ads and revenue barely changes — "we just saved you $50,000." Agencies take credit (plus fees) for demand they didn't create. (guest, 2024-02-19)
- AI won't replace the best media buyers; it makes good ones and good agencies MORE important — ~42,000 agencies shake out, ~100 survive. (guest, 2024-02-19)

**Kasim Aslam (guest — Solutions 8; context):**
- "There's no such thing as attribution" — an "open-air conspiracy." You can see maybe 1% of the customer journey (impression→click, top→bottom of funnel); everyone treats that 1% as the single source of truth and models the rest off it, which is false. "It's 99% black box with little pinpoints of light." (guest, 2024-02-19)
- All in-app metrics, ROAS, and tCPA data are effectively lies; agencies claim "1600% ROAS," but the bank account doesn't reflect it — if it were real, you'd mortgage everything to pour money into the top of the funnel. (guest, 2024-02-19)
- Prescription: go full-funnel and spend "where you can't see" — impression-based channels you can't track — over protracted periods, not 30-day windows. No real sales cycle resolves in 30 days; a top-of-funnel campaign turned off 7 months ago can kill a Google campaign today and you'll never attribute it. (guest, 2024-02-19)
- "Instant popcorn" metaphor: 10 years of obsession with instant direct-response compressed everyone into the bottom of the funnel — "we stopped planting corn" — which is why CPMs and CPCs went insane. (guest, 2024-02-19)
