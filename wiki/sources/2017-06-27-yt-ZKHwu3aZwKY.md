# 7 Untapped Traffic Sources For Marketing Your Website

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that beyond the obvious channels of Google and Facebook there are underused traffic sources marketers can leverage, and walks through seven of them. (1) Quora — find questions in your niche and answer them, inserting a link to your site where relevant; he says his own profile generates over 100,000 views a month from consistently answering questions. (2) Reddit — participate genuinely in topic-specific subreddits, upvoting, downvoting and submitting others' content rather than only self-promoting. (3) Forums — still alive despite Facebook, participate and answer questions in niche forums (he cites Warrior Forum and Digital Point) and use the signature to link back to your homepage. (4) LinkedIn publishing — rather than just clicking share, republish the first few paragraphs of a blog post natively on LinkedIn and add a "Click to continue reading" link back to your site; he says he gets over 30,000 visitors a month from this tactic. (5) Small niche blogs — comment with genuine insight on smaller, more relevant blogs (not just TechCrunch/Mashable/Forbes) and use the name/URL comment field to link back, without spamming. (6) StumbleUpon — buy clicks cheaply ("pennies on the dollar") targeted by industry, or get free traffic via the toolbar by sharing web content. (7) Facebook groups — join niche groups, help answer questions, and link to relevant blog posts; he notes groups escape the algorithmic reach restrictions that fan pages face, so good comments are seen by everyone in the group.

## Key claims
- There are valuable traffic sources beyond Google and Facebook that marketers can leverage (as of 2017-06-27).
- On Quora, you should find and answer questions related to your space and insert a link to your website where there is a good, relevant place to do so (as of 2017-06-27).
- Neil Patel's Quora profile generates over 100,000 views a month, driven by continually answering more questions in his space (as of 2017-06-27).
- Reddit has subreddits for nearly every topic and industry, and the right approach is to participate in the community — upvoting, downvoting, and submitting others' content — not just submitting your own (as of 2017-06-27).
- Forums are not dead despite Facebook; many popular niche forums (e.g. Warrior Forum, Digital Point) still exist, and answering questions with a homepage link in your signature drives traffic (as of 2017-06-27).
- On LinkedIn you should publish native content rather than only using the share button: copy the first few paragraphs of a blog post onto LinkedIn and add a "Click to continue reading" link back to your site (as of 2017-06-27).
- Neil Patel generates in excess of 30,000 visitors a month purely from LinkedIn using the republish-with-continue-reading tactic (as of 2017-06-27).
- Smaller niche blogs have more relevant audiences than large publications like TechCrunch, Mashable, or Forbes, and leaving insightful comments with your name and URL lets readers click back to your site (as of 2017-06-27).
- Blog comments should add value and never be spam; if you are not adding value, do not leave a comment (as of 2017-06-27).
- StumbleUpon is more popular than it seems and lets you buy industry-targeted clicks for "pennies on the dollar," plus free traffic via its toolbar when you share content (as of 2017-06-27).
- Facebook groups can be found by searching Facebook for relevant terms; joining and helping answer questions, then linking to relevant blog posts, drives traffic (as of 2017-06-27).
- ★ Facebook groups do not adhere to the same reach restrictions or algorithm as a Facebook fan page, so a good comment can be seen by everyone in the group (as of 2017-06-27).
