# How To ACTUALLY Write A Blog Post From Start To Finish | Neil Patel

## Summary

In this solo tutorial, Neil Patel walks through the repeatable formula he uses to structure every blog post on neilpatel.com, which he says generates at least 37,000 visitors per post. The formula has five sequential parts: start with a strong title/headline (written before any body copy), open with an introduction that hooks the reader with a bold statement and previews the content, write a skimmable body using subheadings and short paragraphs, include outbound links to other sites for credibility, and close with a labeled conclusion that summarizes the post and ends with a question to drive comments. He argues the headline is decisive because eight of ten people read it but only two click through, and he recommends grocery-store magazines as a source of catchy title inspiration, favoring "How To" and list posts. He supports the conclusion-with-a-question tactic with Crazy Egg scroll-map testing showing readers often jump to the conclusion first. He closes with a bonus outreach tip: after linking to someone, email them a short flattering note that invites a social share, and plugs his agency Neil Patel Digital.
