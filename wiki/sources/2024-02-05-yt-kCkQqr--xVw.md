# Eightify Uses AI to Get 70M Users, LA Times Walkout, Google Web Stories, & More

## Summary
Reacting to an AI-summarizer Chrome extension (aifi.app / "Eightify") that scaled to
2.1M pages and ~70M annual visitors by mass-publishing AI-generated YouTube-video
summaries, Neil and Eric use it as a springboard for a durable belief: short-term
black-hat/programmatic tricks ("SEO Heist"-style) produce a dopamine hit but don't
compound, and both admit to having taken SEO shortcuts early in their careers that
"bit us in the butt." Neil reframes the tactic in "white hat" terms — summarize your
own content responsibly with human review rather than publish tens of thousands of
thin AI pages. This leads into Neil's own AI-content data: in an NP Digital survey of
1,511 marketers, 640 (>60%) use pure AI content with no human edit, and 282 combine
AI+human; in head-to-head testing, human-written content ranked higher 94.12% of the
time, and human-written articles averaged 283 monthly visitors vs. 52 for AI-written
ones. Neil also shares a 2022-vs-2023 revenue breakdown showing ultra-long-tail
keywords (7+ words) grew from 3.21% to 48.95% of SEO revenue while making up only 24.2%
of SEO traffic — his conclusion: avoid pure-AI content, and prioritize long-tail
keywords. The episode closes on Google Web Stories (a case study of 2.2M organic
visitors from Google Discover in 6 months) and a first mention of NP Digital exploring
M&A-via-equity-swap deals with complementary agencies in the down economy.

## Key claims (dated, speaker-tagged, paraphrase)
- Long-term, sustainable strategies beat short-term SEO shortcuts/black-hat tactics —
  both Neil and Eric say taking shortcuts earlier in their careers ultimately cost them
  more than it gained; framed via an addiction/dopamine-hit analogy. (Neil & Eric,
  2024-02-05) — seo / mindset.
- Advice from Ryan Deiss (Digital Marketer): take tactics that "shady" marketers use and
  apply them, legitimately, inside an above-board business — you'll grow faster without
  breaking rules. Applied here to AI-summarization content: fine if human-reviewed and
  not spammy at scale. (Neil, via Ryan Deiss anecdote, 2024-02-05) — content-marketing.
- **AI vs. human content survey (NP Digital, Neil Patel site, n=1,511 marketers)**: 640
  (>60%) create content using pure AI with no human edit; 282 combine AI+human. In a
  separate test across 68 websites, human-written content outranked AI-written content
  94.12% of the time; average traffic per article was 283 visits/month (human) vs. 52
  visits/month (AI). (Neil, 2024-02-05) — content-marketing / ai-marketing. ★ (strong,
  quantified stance — synthesis candidate for a content-marketing/ai-marketing topic page
  on "don't publish pure-AI content.")
- **Long-tail keyword revenue shift**: keywords of 7+ words made up 3.21% of NP Digital's
  SEO revenue in 2022 vs. 48.95% in 2023, while representing only 24.2% of SEO traffic —
  the largest revenue bucket despite lower traffic share. Neil's 2024 SEO-opportunity
  recommendation: target ultra-long-tail (7+ word) keywords, not the 3-6 word range.
  (Neil, 2024-02-05) — seo. ★ (quantified, dated framework — synthesis candidate.)
- Media-industry job cuts as a "humans still win" signal: LA Times staff walked out
  (first time in 142 years) over impending layoffs; Sports Illustrated laid off its
  entire staff after reportedly relying on AI-generated content; Neil's read: the best
  writers/editors keep jobs, the weakest don't — human-in-the-loop remains necessary,
  but only the best humans. (Neil, 2024-02-05) — content-marketing.
- Google Web Stories case study (via CXL): an operator grew organic Google Discover
  traffic to 2.2M visitors in 6 months for an e-commerce brand by researching high-share
  topics (using BuzzSumo) and packaging them as web stories, styled like Reels/TikTok;
  framed as an underused, low-competition format. (Neil & Eric relaying a CXL case
  study, 2024-02-05) — seo / content-marketing.
- Recurring belief: traffic/lead-count metrics are vanity without bottom-line tracking —
  Neil recounts a corporate talk where a client's traffic and consultation-signups rose
  ~25% while lead count *dropped* 25%, netting out to a wash; the fix is diagnosing the
  full funnel, not just the top. (Neil, 2024-02-05) — analytics-cro.
- First mention of an M&A-via-equity-swap growth strategy: in a down economy, agencies
  can merge complementary service lines (e.g., an SEO/paid-media agency partnering with
  a social-only agency) by swapping equity rather than cash, cross-selling each other's
  client base to increase revenue-per-client and reduce churn — "private equity's 1+1=3."
  Neil references hearing from M&A brokers that agency "aqua-hire" deals are increasingly
  available at low/no premium given the current down economy; cites David Rubenstein
  (Davos) forecasting a pickup in PE/M&A activity in 2024. (Neil, 2024-02-05) —
  agency-entrepreneurship / wealth. ★ (a new, developing framework — flagged for
  follow-up in later episodes per the hosts' own note; worth tracking across future
  ingests.)
