# The Definition of Digital Marketing by Neil Patel

## Summary
An introductory/definitional video: Neil defines digital marketing as any use of
digital tools/channels (social media, search engines, email, sites) to communicate
with an audience, contrasting it with traditional marketing (billboards, print,
direct mail, cold calls). He pushes back on "email is dying" narratives, framing email
as a durable cornerstone of corporate communication. He frames digital marketing's
importance around attention: Google and Facebook generate more revenue than any
traditional media company because they control more eyeballs/attention. He argues
digital marketing is worth it "for almost every business" except government-contract-
type businesses, and points beginners to Ubersuggest to find competitor keywords, ad
spend, and top-performing content as a starting point.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-27, Neil said digital marketing is any marketing that uses digital
  tools/channels (social, search, email, other sites) to communicate with an audience,
  as opposed to traditional/physical marketing (billboards, print, direct mail, phone
  calls).
- As of 2020-02-27, Neil said email is not dying despite over a decade of people
  predicting its death, calling it a cornerstone of corporate communication that is
  "being used more and more."
- As of 2020-02-27, Neil said Google and Facebook generate more revenue than any
  traditional media company because they control more audience attention/eyeballs.
- As of 2020-02-27, Neil said digital marketing is worth pursuing for almost every
  business, with the exception of government-related/contract-based businesses.

## Notable verbatim quotes
