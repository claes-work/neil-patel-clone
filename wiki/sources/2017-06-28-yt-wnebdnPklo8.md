# How to Drive More Website Traffic From Facebook Groups

## Summary
In this short solo tactical video, Neil Patel explains how to use Facebook Groups as a channel to drive website traffic and convert visitors into customers. He advocates two complementary moves: create your own Facebook Group and join others' Groups relevant to your industry. In both cases, the core mechanic is the same — genuinely help members and answer their questions, then share links to your content or products when they naturally add value. Patel argues that traffic earned this way converts better than cold traffic because engagement builds rapport and connection, making community members more likely to visit, share, promote, and buy. He stresses that relevance is the key constraint: the Group must match the industry you serve, or the audience will not convert regardless of size, and recommends doing both (own and join) for best results.

## Key claims
- Businesses should either create their own Facebook Group or join existing ones — ideally both (as of 2017-06-28).
- Within your own Group, driving traffic is as simple as sharing a link whenever people ask questions or when you publish new content (as of 2017-06-28).
- ★ Traffic from a Facebook Group where you have built rapport is more likely to convert into customers than ordinary traffic, because engagement builds connection and trust (as of 2017-06-28).
- Consistently helping members and creating engagement builds a supportive community that will visit your site, read your articles, share and promote your content, and buy your products or services (as of 2017-06-28).
- A private Facebook Group can still generate high engagement — Patel cites his own private Group as an example (as of 2017-06-28).
- You can find and join relevant Groups via Facebook search; many are public and accept members on request (as of 2017-06-28).
- When members have questions related to what you offer, help them first rather than only promoting, while mentioning your product or service in context (as of 2017-06-28).
- ★ The decisive constraint is relevance: the Group must match your industry, or it will not drive converting traffic even if it is large (e.g., a dog-products seller joining marketing Groups will not benefit, and the popularity of dog ownership does not fix the mismatch) (as of 2017-06-28).
