# NEVER Rely on a Single Traffic Source for Your Business

## Summary

Solo strategy/warning. Neil's thesis: **your traffic will drop — it's only a question of
when** — so never depend on one channel. He's seen it repeatedly: people making "tons of
money" from Facebook (or ranking #1 on Google) whose traffic craters when the algorithm
changes, and often **there's nothing you can do** (Facebook hacks like "check the first
comment" for the outbound link are only temporary; your only lever may be to buy more ads).
Because you can't predict when Facebook/Google/Twitter/Pinterest will change their
algorithms, you must **diversify traffic channels in advance** — SEO, paid advertising,
email marketing, and **push notifications** (he plugs his free tool **Subscribers**). The
resilience payoff: when one channel drops, your eggs aren't all in one basket and the
business stays afloat while you find the next channel. He adds a pointed tactic: **don't
only chase the "sexy" channels** (Facebook, Google) — go after the **unsexy, underrated**
ones "craving for your attention," which are more generous with reach/views. His own
example: **his videos perform better on LinkedIn than on Facebook or YouTube**, even though
YouTube/Facebook are the more obviously popular networks. He drives home the anti-pattern
with affiliate-conference peers (Affiliate Summit, Las Vegas) making "a million dollars a
month" all from Facebook — then months later their revenue is zero because they never
diversified.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-09-01, every traffic source eventually drops (algorithm changes on
  Facebook/Google/Twitter/Pinterest) — treat it as *when*, not *if*.
- As of 2018-09-01, when an algorithm turns against you there's often nothing to do;
  workaround "hacks" (e.g. Facebook "link in first comment") are only temporary, and you may
  be left only able to buy more ads.
- As of 2018-09-01, diversify traffic in advance across SEO, paid ads, email, and push
  notifications (he names his free tool **Subscribers**) so no single channel can sink you.
- As of 2018-09-01, don't only chase "sexy" channels (Facebook/Google) — target the unsexy,
  underrated channels "craving for your attention," which are more generous with reach.
- As of 2018-09-01, Neil states his videos perform better on **LinkedIn** than on Facebook or
  YouTube (self-reported, consistent with his 2018-08-16 LinkedIn-video observation).
- As of 2018-09-01, he cites (self-reported) Affiliate Summit peers earning ~$1M/month all
  from Facebook whose revenue later dropped to zero because they never diversified.
