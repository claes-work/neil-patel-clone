# Humans using AI outperform humans without AI by a LOT (case study)

## Summary
Discussion of a BCG/Wharton (Ethan Mollick) study finding consultants using GPT-4
outperformed those who didn't "on every dimension." Neil agrees AI use raises
performance but warns fully AI-only workflows (no human review) still produce
frequent errors; his framework is that people overestimate AI's short-run impact and
underestimate its long-run impact (citing a Bill Gates-style formulation Eric also
raises). He gives two concrete NP Digital AI use cases: analytics-driven ad-spend
waste flagging (human-reviewed, not auto-executed) and programmatic SEO page
generation via GPT-4 with human spot-checks.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Companies using AI *without* humans in the loop see a lot of errors that require
  human correction; combining AI + human review gets more done, faster, at high
  quality — pure automation isn't there yet. (Neil, 2023-10-28) — ai-marketing.
- States roughly 20% of ChatGPT outputs are inaccurate in his experience, reinforcing
  the need for human review rather than snap-of-the-finger automation.
  (Neil, 2023-10-28) — ai-marketing.
- Framework: people overestimate what AI can do for their marketing/business in the
  short run, and underestimate what it can do in the long run — in the short run it
  requires heavy human intervention and isn't fully reliable; in the long run he
  expects it to become an ever-present life/business assistant (analogizes to the
  film *iRobot*). (Neil, 2023-10-28) — ai-marketing.
- Recommends a specific, underused AI use case: analyzing ad-spend analytics to flag
  wasted spend for human review (not auto-executing changes), noting the aggregate
  addressable savings (cites ~$160B/year Google ad spend, ~$100B+/year Facebook) as
  larger than novelty use cases like AI blog-post generation. (Neil, 2023-10-28) —
  ai-marketing / paid-ads.
- Describes NP Digital's programmatic-SEO workflow: topical mapping + keyword
  research → page templates at scale → GPT-4 generation → human spot-check → scale up
  — caveats that this only works for sites with strong domain authority in
  businesses suited to many permutations (his example: a TripAdvisor-style "best
  places to eat/best hotels" site). (Neil, 2023-10-28) — seo / ai-marketing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> The way you can get it to work really well in the short run is you integrate
> humans throughout the process when you're leveraging AI. In the long run though
> the capabilities are endless.

> Use AI to analyze your analytics data and figure out where you're wasting money
> and where you're not. Don't have it make changes to your ad campaigns instantly
> but more so just flag it to someone internally on your team.

> There's more savings to doing that than it is to saying, hey, let's have AI help
> me write a blog post whoop de doo. That would've cost you 15, 20 bucks anyways.
