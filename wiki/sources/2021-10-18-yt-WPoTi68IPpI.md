# How To Get a Digital Marketing Job with No Experience

## Summary
Career-entry advice for breaking into digital marketing without prior experience.
Recommends starting with fast-feedback channels (paid ads, email, social) over SEO,
because SEO is slow to show results while YouTube SEO can show results quickly;
traditional Google/Bing SEO takes longer. Recommends specializing in a local or
e-commerce niche (dentistry, real estate, financial services, doctors) for high LTV and
abundant prospects. The core job-getting tactic: perform free audits on prospects (SEO
via Ubersuggest's site audit report, or paid-ad audits), identify what competitors are
doing that the prospect isn't, make advice maximally actionable (even if the prospect
could execute it without hiring the candidate — argues transparency builds more trust,
not less), tie recommendations to measurable ROI, undercut agency/professional local
pricing while job-hunting, offer some work for free to build trust, and pursue platform
certifications (HubSpot, Google AdWords, Facebook, Snapchat, Reddit, email platforms) —
paired with hands-on practice, not just passive study, using free ad credits where
available.

## Key claims
- States a sequencing rule for new marketers: learn fast-feedback channels (paid ads,
  email, social) before SEO, explicitly contrasting YouTube SEO (fast results) against
  traditional Google/Bing SEO (slow) (paraphrase, dated 2021-10) — seo,
  agency-entrepreneurship.
- Recommends free competitive audits (via Ubersuggest's site audit report) as the
  fastest path to a first client/job, prioritizing maximal actionability of the advice
  even at the cost of the client not needing to hire the marketer, reasoning it builds
  disproportionate trust (self-reported tactic, dated 2021-10) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Recommends specializing in local/e-commerce niches for beginners because they are
  easier competitive environments to show fast, measurable results in than national/
  global markets (paraphrase, dated 2021-10) — agency-entrepreneurship.
