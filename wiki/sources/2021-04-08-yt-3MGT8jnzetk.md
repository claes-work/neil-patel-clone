# How to Convert Blog Readers Into Customers

## Summary
Solo tactical video walking through his own neilpatel.com blog live, demonstrating a
six-point CTA placement system for converting blog traffic (he states ~9M visitors/
month) into leads/customers: (1) make the first post/link on the blog homepage a
direct CTA to the product/service page or lead form; (2) a yes/no interstitial
call-to-action near the top of the blog asking whether the reader wants the offering;
(3) a sticky top bar with a yes/no CTA that scrolls with the page; (4) a bottom bar
on individual post pages; (5) in-content CTAs embedded within blog posts; (6) sidebar
CTAs. Reports an internal A/B-style finding that placing a lead form directly
in-context (bottom bar / in-post) beat sending readers to a separate consulting page.
Recommends cheap Upwork developers or his own tool, Hello Bar, for readers without
dev budget. Bonus tactic: writing "best X companies" listicle content that ranks for
high-intent commercial-investigation terms (his example: "SEO company") to funnel
searchers to his own agency. Notes one CTA experiment ("do your marketing for you")
that he says isn't working well, as a candid negative example.

## Key claims
- States he converts blog readers via CTA placement engineering rather than relying
  on content topical relevance alone ("even if you write related content, there's no
  guarantee that your readers are going to convert") — paraphrase, self-reported
  operating philosophy.
- Reports an internal test finding that placing a lead form directly in-line (bottom
  bar or in-post CTA) produced roughly a 30% increase in conversions versus routing
  readers to a separate consulting/service page (paraphrase, self-reported, unaudited,
  no methodology given).
- States his own blog traffic is roughly 9 million visitors/month at the time of
  recording (self-reported, dated 2021-04-08).
- Recommends "best [category] companies" listicle content targeting commercial-intent
  keywords (his own example: "SEO company") as a funnel into agency lead generation —
  demonstrated live by his own ranking for that term (paraphrase, tactical/self-
  demonstrated).
- Candidly flags one of his own CTA experiments ("do your marketing for you" link) as
  underperforming — a rare self-reported negative result rather than a success case
  (paraphrase).
