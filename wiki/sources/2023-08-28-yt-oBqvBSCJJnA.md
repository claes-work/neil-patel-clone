# Surprising AI Strategies You Haven't Considered Yet

## Summary
Solo guest interview covering NP Digital's origin, culture, and strategy, plus an AI/SEO
outlook. On the agency's founding mission: Neil says he started NP Digital because he was tired
of hiring marketers who weren't creative/scrappy and kept asking for more budget instead of
better ideas; he wanted a team willing to try "cutting edge" (but not unethical) tactics. Asked
about generosity/content-giving, he frames it as partly selfish — he loves educating and learns
from audience feedback as much as he gives — rather than pure altruism, and downplays his own
donation generosity relative to wealthier philanthropists. Asked how NP Digital selects
"partners," he admits he doesn't track day-to-day company details closely at ~750-800
employees and self-deprecatingly disclaims being CEO ("I would be a terrible CEO"), framing his
role as setting direction rather than operational oversight; he says he works 60-70 hours/week.
He describes NP Digital's ideal customer as a global company needing multi-region/multi-language
traffic and lead generation, and frames the agency's edge over larger holding-company
competitors as avoiding internal silo/earn-out conflicts because NP Digital is privately held
with Neil as majority decision-maker. On staying private, he says NP Digital has received (and
continues to receive) acquisition/SPAC/public-listing offers but rejects them because going
public means answering to shareholders over quarterly performance rather than doing what's best
for clients and the team; he'd consider outside capital only from patient investors who "stay
out of your way." On the economy, he says the same marketing fundamentals (SEO, omnichannel,
webinars, paid ads, landing-page optimization) apply in good and bad economies alike, and that a
bad economy is actually a good time to expand internationally because hiring is cheaper. On
AI/search: he reiterates the short-run-overestimate/long-run-underestimate framing, states
content now needs "more stories and more experiences" since tactics alone are commoditized by
ChatGPT, and predicts search queries are becoming much more specific/conversational because
users want AI to answer directly (illustrating with a hyper-specific vacation-planning example).
He predicts Google will ultimately win over Bing/ChatGPT because its data/index inputs are
superior, and forecasts a long-run future where AI-driven search enables one-click purchases
(e.g. "buy these Jordans") personalized to known preferences — but says this is likely years
away and Google won't sacrifice its ad-revenue model in the near term. He also restates the
free-tool-for-backlinks strategy and the quarterly money-page-refresh tactic (both previously
logged), and reaffirms NP Digital's global-expansion trajectory (targeting 20+ countries in
2023).
