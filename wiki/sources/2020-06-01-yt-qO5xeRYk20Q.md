# How to Get POWERFUL Backlinks for Faster Rankings - Module 05 - Lesson 2 - SEO Unlocked

## Summary
SEO Unlocked link-building module, lesson 2: a tactics rundown for finding link
opportunities, framed as a "deliberate practice" (Seinfeld/LeBron/Jordan/Tiger Woods
analogy for continual skill-building) that works in any industry, even against giants
like GoDaddy. Six sourcing strategies, all routed through Ubersuggest: (1) **competitor
link building** — pull a competitor's backlinks report, filter to dofollow links, sort
by domain score, check referring-page relevance; (2) **content backlinks** — find who
ranks for your target keyword, pull their backlinks report the same way; (3) **guest
posting via author outreach** — find sites with many bylined authors, connect with an
author on social, then run a scripted three-email sequence (compliment → constructive
feedback → pitch for an editor introduction), claims ~30% of author intros convert to
an editor introduction; guest-post links should be nofollow, valued for referral
traffic/leads, not "manipulating Google"; (4) **Google search operators**
(`intitle:`/`inurl:`) to surface link-friendly resource pages; (5) **broken link
building** — find dead external links (via search operators or Ubersuggest's site
audit tool) and pitch a replacement; (6) **reverse image search** — publish custom
images (Canva/Infogram), then reverse-image-search them to find uncredited reuse and
request a link. Closes urging traffic-quality screening (don't pursue links from
zero-traffic sites) and three action items: download the search-operators PDF, fill
in the link-building scorecard, join 10 Facebook groups.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-06-01, Neil said link building works in any industry, including
  hyper-competitive ones — citing HostGator ranking well for "web hosting" despite
  being far less known than GoDaddy.
- As of 2020-06-01, Neil's guest-post author-outreach sequence is three scripted
  emails (compliment on a specific post → constructive feedback on a follow-up post →
  a direct ask for an introduction to the editor), and he said roughly 30% of authors
  who respond will make that introduction.
- As of 2020-06-01, Neil said guest-post links should be set to nofollow as "common
  standard practice," and framed their value as referral traffic/leads/revenue and
  brand exposure rather than direct ranking manipulation.
- As of 2020-06-01, Neil's stated criterion for pursuing any link is the linking site's
  traffic level — low-traffic sites are "not going to be that valuable" regardless of
  domain score.
- As of 2020-06-01, Neil recommended a reverse-image-search workflow (publish custom
  Canva/Infogram graphics, then Google-reverse-image-search them) to find sites that
  reused an image without credit and request a link.
