# How to Generate 355 Backlinks With 29 Dollars

## Summary
Solo tactical video reinforcing his "tools generate natural backlinks" doctrine, with
a specific low-cost sourcing method. Shows his neilpatel.com A/B-Testing Calculator
(843 backlinks from 355 unique referring domains) and points out it was never actively
promoted and was built when his site had little traffic — the links accrued
organically over time simply because people like linking to useful calculators/tools.
Recommends sourcing cheap, ready-made calculator/tool scripts from marketplaces like
CodeCanyon (most under $29) rather than building custom tools, embedding them on a
site, and optionally having a developer restyle them. Explicitly disclaims that this
is link-buying: no payment is exchanged for links, only for the tool/content itself,
which he frames as a legitimate content-marketing spend versus manual outreach
"begging" for links. Cites Ubersuggest itself (a larger, custom-built tool) as
generating 17,671 unique referring domains.

## Key claims
- States his A/B-Testing Calculator tool has accumulated 843 backlinks from 355
  unique domains without active promotion, since launch on a then-low-traffic site
  (paraphrase, self-reported analytics read, dated to this recording).
- States Ubersuggest (a larger, custom free tool) has accumulated 17,671 unique
  referring domains (paraphrase, self-reported, dated to this recording).
- Frames buying a cheap pre-built tool/calculator script (e.g. from CodeCanyon, "most
  under $29") and embedding it as a superior ROI strategy for natural link acquisition
  versus manual outreach/"begging" for links, and explicitly distinguishes this from
  paying directly for links, which he says he would never recommend (paraphrase,
  tactical + ethical stance).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> I'm not saying buy links... but you're spending money on content... whatever version
> you're spending money on... it has a much better ROI than going out there and
> manually trying to hit people up and say, "Hey, give me a link," and begging for it.
