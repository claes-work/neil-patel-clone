# Shoppers Expected to Spend the Same or More this Holiday Season

## Summary
A news-reaction episode: Eric shares his screen on an ICSC headline projecting $1.6T in holiday spending (and a Deloitte report projecting 3.5–4.6% retail sales growth), and the two react live. Eric isn't shocked spending is holding despite inflation, illustrating with a (friend's) anecdote about someone deliberately taking on more credit-card debt for holiday gifts because "the interest isn't bad" and jobs feel steady. Neil supplies the persona-relevant analysis: a quick 2023 earnings scan (Microsoft strong, Meta strong-but-hit-by-Middle-East-conflict fears, Alphabet's cloud growth disappointing) and a call that 2024 will be a "reality check" year as tech layoffs resume and higher-for-longer interest rates finally squeeze consumers and businesses that had been trading on future potential rather than actual discipline. The persona-defining core of the episode is Neil's detailed, first-person **M&A anecdote**: a ~$5M-revenue Mexican business he tried to buy, whose EBITDA had fallen from ~$1M to under half that, still wanted the same ~$5M price (~11x profit) it could have fetched at its peak, on the promise of nearly tripling EBITDA next year with unbuilt, unsold new service lines. Neil refused the ask, told the seller a normal multiple only applies once the numbers are actually hit, and kept the relationship open either way — a concrete instance of his "you can't trade on potential" discipline. *(Note for synthesis: this Mexico-agency anecdote — declining EBITDA, seller wanting ~11x, Neil refusing and using an earnout/wait-for-real-numbers approach — closely parallels the "rejected 8x profit on a shrinking company" / earnout M&A framework later summarized in [[2026-07-20-yt-0Pg-dNUWaI8]]; likely the same or a directly analogous recurring anecdote — worth reconciling numbers (11x vs. 8x) at the next synthesis pass.)*

- 2023 holiday spend is projected to hold flat or rise (ICSC: $1.6T headline; Deloitte: retail sales +3.5–4.6% YoY), which Eric reads as unsurprising given people are still spending despite inflation — just not growing spend at the past rate. (Eric, 2023-12-07) — wealth.
- Consumers are willing to go deeper into revolving (credit-card) debt for discretionary holiday spending because steady jobs let them rationalize "I could pay it off later, the interest isn't bad" instead of saving. (Eric, 2023-12-07, via a friend's-girlfriend anecdote) — wealth.
- 2023 Big Tech earnings snapshot: Microsoft "did really well"; Meta reported great earnings but gave back gains on Middle East conflict-risk concerns; Alphabet did not report amazing earnings because cloud division growth disappointed the market. (Neil, 2023-12-07) — wealth. *(ephemeral market commentary, not durable doctrine.)*
- Neil calls 2024 a "reality check" year: tech layoffs (per recent TechCrunch coverage) are increasing again across industries NP Digital works with, because money isn't cheap — high rates make it harder for businesses to invest and for consumers to spend, and "the world isn't going round and round." (Neil, 2023-12-07) — agency-entrepreneurship / wealth.
- M&A anecdote (Neil, first-person): a Mexican business doing ~$5M revenue, whose profit had been ~$1M (with a past $5M sale offer on the table), had its EBITDA fall by more than half; the owner still wanted the original ~$5M price (~11x reduced profit) based on an unbuilt plan to nearly triple EBITDA next year via new, not-yet-sold service lines. Neil refused, told him a normal multiple only applies once the higher numbers are actually achieved, and offered to revisit "either way" once real numbers came in rather than pay on projected potential. (Neil, 2023-12-07) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Durable M&A/economic discipline: "you can't trade on potential" — the last ~10 years rewarded raising on future earnings/potential rather than building an actual disciplined business; Neil expects (welcomes, even for himself) a forced return to discipline as the economy "gets punched in the face." (Neil, 2023-12-07) — agency-entrepreneurship / wealth.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> No one's going to give you 11 times profit for a declining business where a lot of your revenue is project based and there's no outlook over the next few years.

> Once you come back down to reality, I'm here to buy the company when you're ready.

> Here's the thing, you can't... One thing people should understand is, you can't trade on potential. In the last 10 years or so, we traded on potential. We traded on future earnings, right? People weren't really focused on driving, creating actual business, creating customers and doing a good job for them.

> Now it's about efficiency. And what's going to need to happen is, the economy's going to get punched in the face. People need to get punched in the face a little bit, ourselves included, right? It's not that we want people to get punched in the face, but it's going to bring us back to discipline.
