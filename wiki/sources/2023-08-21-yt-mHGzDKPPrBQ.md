# 11 Strategies To Help You Creatively Grow Brand Awareness

## Summary
Conference fireside chat / audience Q&A. Neil argues personal brands and company brands are
complementary, not substitutes: the "ideal world" combines a Kylie-Cosmetics-style personal
brand with L'Oreal-style operations/distribution — a personal brand can launch a company but
"you're not going to get a massive company without the business operations and systems and
processes down." He describes NP Digital's own funnel: ~750 employees, 71% of business (per
year-to-date figures at time of talk) sourced from RFPs, employee referrals, and client
referrals; his personal brand generated the agency's "first probably 10 plus million dollars"
but doesn't scale further for enterprise deals, since large publicly-traded prospects (e.g.
Microsoft) care about ROI data, not who Neil Patel is. On influencer marketing, he says paying
a well-known niche influencer to genuinely integrate a product (cites Ryan Reynolds/Mint
Mobile, where Reynolds took equity rather than a flat fee) converts far better than one-off
micro-influencer posts. On content format, he says video should be the primary format because
it's most efficiently repurposed into stills, audio/podcast, and transcribed blog/text. Asked
whether AI will "kill us," he gives his standard short-run-overestimate/long-run-underestimate
framing, then extends it with a new "Where's Waldo" metaphor: because AI outputs are built from
the same scraped web inputs, everyone's content is starting to look alike ("now everyone looks
like Waldo"), and creators who add genuine E-E-A-T (experience, expertise, authority, trust) —
lived experience competitors can't replicate — are the ones still standing out. He illustrates
search competition at scale with "auto insurance" (~1 billion+ results, ~300K monthly US
searches, virtually all clicks on page one).
