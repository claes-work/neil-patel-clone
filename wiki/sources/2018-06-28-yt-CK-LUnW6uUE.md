# Fiverr Marketing Hack: Should You PAY to Game the System?

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode (Neil with interviewer Adam from Viewership) answering viewer "518
Painters," who asked whether paying someone on Fiverr for 200 citations for $20 is risky.
Neil first distinguishes **citations** (an unlinked mention) from **links**, and notes an
old (Moz) finding that mentions can correlate with rankings — but says Google's algorithm
is now far more sophisticated, so random Fiverr citations won't help. His broader stance:
**don't use Fiverr (or similar) for anything marketing-related** — it's fine for adjusting
an audio clip or modifying an image cheaply, but paying for links, fake Facebook likes, or
fake social shares is a great way to get banned. He generalizes to Upwork/crowdsourcing:
you get what you pay for; Upwork has good talent if you pay for it. The strongest material
is autobiographical: Neil openly recounts his own past black-hat tactics and the
consequences (see flags). His moral: gaming the system yields short-term money that
evaporates, forcing you back to the drawing board — think long-term instead.
