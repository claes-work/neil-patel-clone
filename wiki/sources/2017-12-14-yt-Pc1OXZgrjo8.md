# 4 Simple Business Tips I'd Give My Younger Self | Follow These And You WILL Be Successful

## Summary
Neil Patel gives four lessons he'd tell his younger self, opening by pushing back on "follow your passion": passion alone doesn't make money — it just means you'll put in the time and not give up. (1) **Focus on a big idea with a large total addressable market** — passion plus a small market caps you; he uses his own **Crazy Egg** as the example: it makes "millions and millions of dollars in profit" and customers love it, but it will never be a billion-dollar company because the TAM isn't big enough. He suggests sizing markets via Google Finance / Google Trends (be even 1% of a $300B company and you're a multi-billion-dollar business). (2) **Move fast** — as a young founder he "treated my business like a baby," precious and protected, but that's not reality; execute quickly, imperfect is fine. (3) **Learn from mistakes but don't repeat them** — Zuckerberg/Musk/Gates/Buffett all made big mistakes and stayed billionaires (Zuckerberg failed to buy Snapchat, failed to clone it, but Instagram eventually overtook it). (4) **The 80/20 rule** — a good education/hard work doesn't guarantee success; put 80% of your energy on the vital 20%. He gives a strikingly self-aware example of applying it to himself: he focuses only on **traffic generation and brand building** because that's what he's best at and what drives revenue — and openly admits he is a **terrible manager** ("worst boss ever ... I pay well but management-wise I just suck"), so other people manage the company.

## Key claims
As of 2017-12-14, Neil states:
- **Passion is necessary but insufficient**: "just because you're passionate it doesn't mean you're gonna make money" — passion means you'll put in time and won't give up, not that you'll succeed (as of 2017-12-14).
- **Focus on a big idea with a large TAM**: **Crazy Egg** — his "first real business" — makes "millions and millions of dollars in profit" with hundreds/thousands of paying companies, but "it's not a billion-dollar company and it never will be" because "the total addressable market isn't big enough" (as of 2017-12-14).
- Size the opportunity via **Google Finance / Google Trends** — being even 1% of a $300B company still makes you a multi-billion-dollar business (as of 2017-12-14).
- **Move fast, don't treat the business as precious**: as a young founder he treated it "like a baby" wanting everything perfect; in reality, "if you don't move fast people are gonna trample all over you" — "it's okay not to be perfect it's more important to move fast" (as of 2017-12-14).
- **Learn from mistakes, avoid repeating them**: Zuckerberg, Musk, Gates, and Buffett all made big mistakes and remained billionaires; Zuckerberg failed to buy Snapchat and failed to clone it, but Instagram eventually overtook Snapchat because "Mark learned from his mistakes" (as of 2017-12-14).
- **80/20 rule**: education and hard work don't guarantee success (he cites job applicants from Stanford willing to work for $10–15/hr); "putting a lot of time and energy on the right thing is what will make you successful" (as of 2017-12-14).
- **Self-aware application**: he focuses on **traffic generation and brand building** because it drives leads/revenue and is his strength; he openly calls himself "a terrible manager ... one of the worst boss[es] ever — I pay well but management-wise I just suck," so "other people manage everyone else in the company" (as of 2017-12-14).
- If a focus area shows no results "week after week," shift your energy elsewhere (as of 2017-12-14).
