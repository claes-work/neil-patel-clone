# Is Your Social Media Marketing Working? Here's How to Track Your Social Media Efforts

## Summary
Neil Patel gives the main social-media metrics to track so you know whether your effort is working before traffic and sales show up. (1) New fans/followers gained on a consistent basis — not total count but the rate, which should accelerate month over month (100, then 150, then 250, then 500 is healthy; a flat 100/month means you'll cap out). (2) Competitors' growth, ideally slower than yours — if they grow faster they're doing something you're not; track it with the free tool SocialBlade. (3) Engagement — comments per post, likes, retweets, LinkedIn shares; a falling engagement percentage means you're not building a thriving, loyal community, which you cultivate by responding to and engaging with people (as he does on his own Facebook/Twitter). (4) Volume of content pushed out — multiple times a day, not necessarily your own business material (industry or educational works). Early on you won't see much traffic or sales, so these leading metrics tell you if you're going in the right direction.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-16, Neil states:
- Without tracking the right metrics you can't tell if your social efforts are working, broken, or just need more time (as of 2017-11-16).
- Metric 1 — new fans gained on a consistent (daily/weekly/monthly) basis; the goal is an increasing growth rate (e.g., 100 → 150 → 250 → 500), not a flat rate; adding a flat 100/month means slow growth that eventually caps out (as of 2017-11-16).
- Metric 2 — competitors' growth rate; you want them growing slower than you, because faster growth means they're doing something you're not; SocialBlade.com is a free tool that compares your growth to competitors' (as of 2017-11-16).
- Metric 3 — engagement (comments per Facebook post, likes, retweets, LinkedIn shares); a declining engagement percentage means you're not building a community that helps content go viral; you cultivate loyal followers by responding to and engaging with people (as of 2017-11-16).
- Neil personally tries to engage on his Facebook page and Twitter profile and responds to Facebook messages when he can, to build a more loyal audience (self-reported, as of 2017-11-16).
- Metric 4 — how much content you push out; posting multiple times a day (business, industry, or educational) is needed to build a thriving community; Neil posts multiple times a day across profiles, tracks competitors, and aims to post and engage at least as much as them (as of 2017-11-16).
- Ultimately you can judge by traffic and sales in Google Analytics, but early on those are thin, so the leading metrics above tell you if you're on the right track (as of 2017-11-16).
