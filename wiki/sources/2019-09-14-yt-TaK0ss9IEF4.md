# 6 Reasons You Still Aren't Ranking: The Cold, Hard Truth

## Summary

Neil pushes back on "SEO is too hard now / algorithms changed" excuses, saying he gets more
search traffic under harder algorithms than he did under easy ones, and names six reasons
people fail. (1) **Not consistent** — he tells the Quick Sprout story: he stopped blogging
for a month, traffic dropped ~30% and took 3+ months to recover; SEO is no longer "set it
and forget it." (2) **Tactical, not strategic** — stop chasing tricks; find real
search-demand topics (Ubersuggest + Content Ideas), write consistently, earn shares/links
from competitors' linkers, and internal-link. (3) **Content sucks** — check Google
Analytics monthly, study dwell time, use the Ubersuggest Content Ideas report to see what's
*not* working (page 10–100) and avoid it, and use Crazy Egg heatmaps to find drop-off; write
for humans, not search engines (per Moz, 20%+ of ranking content is video; podcasts now get
indexed). (4) **Not enough / wrong-quality links** — quality over quantity (cites Penny
Hoarder outranking with fewer, better links). (5) **Niche too competitive** — narrow to a
sub-niche (small-business loans, CBD-for-weight-loss) and expand up later. (6) **Thinking in
keywords, not people/problems** — searches are problems; write the solution even if it means
fewer exact keywords.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-09-14, Neil said he gets **more search traffic now under harder algorithms**
  than under the earlier easy ones; algorithm difficulty isn't the real blocker.
- As of 2019-09-14, Neil recounted that pausing **Quick Sprout** blogging for one month cut

- As of 2019-09-14, Neil said to be **strategic, not tactical**: research real demand

- As of 2019-09-14, Neil said **link quality beats quantity**, citing **Penny Hoarder**

- As of 2019-09-14, Neil said to **write for humans/problems, not keywords**, and to pick a
  **sub-niche** first (e.g. small-business loans, CBD-for-weight-loss) then expand up.
