# 5 Beginner SEO Mistakes That Are Ruining Your Website Traffic And What You Should Do Instead

## Summary

Neil lists five common SEO mistakes: (1) **dates in your URL** — Google reads them as
date-relevance and stops treating content as evergreen; removing them from neilpatel.com raised
his search traffic **58% within 30 days**; (2) **treating your site as siloed sections** instead
of one interconnected domain — internally link relevant posts so pages rank higher; (3) **thinking
of SEO as just SEO** (on-page, links, shares) rather than **brand-building** — he cites ex-Google
CEO Eric Schmidt that "brands are the solution," and ties brand authority to combating fake news;
collect emails/push subscribers (Hello Bar, subscribers.com) to bring people back; (4) **writing
content but never updating it** — refreshing old posts (even a paragraph) signals freshness and
lifts rankings; (5) **not thinking about the user** — Google tracks user metrics (e.g. pogo-
sticking / fast Back-button clicks) via browser and toolbar, so a poor experience tanks rankings.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-04-01, Neil said dates in a URL make Google treat the page as tied to that date,
  hurting evergreen ranking; removing the `/year/month/` structure from neilpatel.com lifted his
  search traffic by **58% within 30 days** (on a base already in the hundreds of thousands of
  monthly Google visitors). [self-reported]
- As of 2019-04-01, Neil said to treat your website as one interconnected domain, not siloed
  categories, and internally link relevant posts so all pages rank higher.
- As of 2019-04-01, Neil said SEO is fundamentally about **building a brand**, citing ex-Google
  CEO **Eric Schmidt**'s "brands are the solution" remark, and framed brand authority as Google's
  way to combat fake news; suggests capturing emails and push subscribers (Hello Bar,
  subscribers.com) to reinforce the brand via return visits.
- As of 2019-04-01, Neil said **updating existing content** (even a paragraph) signals freshness
  to Google and lifts rankings, since Google favors fresh/updated content over old content.
- As of 2019-04-01, Neil said Google tracks **user metrics** through browsers and toolbars — e.g.
  if a searcher clicks a result and hits Back within a second, it signals the page isn't
  relevant — so putting the user first beats putting SEO first.
