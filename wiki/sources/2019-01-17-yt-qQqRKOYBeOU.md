# How to Develop a Growth Hacking Mentality | Neil Patel

## Summary

Growth hacking is framed as a **mentality/mindset**, not just tactics. (1) **Document
everything** — every SEO change, ad-cost spike, etc., so you (and your marketing team)
build a repository of what moved the needle; most things won't drive growth, a few will,
and you can't know which without documenting. (2) **Never be satisfied with your growth**
— even at 15–20% month-over-month you could grow faster; complacency stops growth.
(3) **Spy on competitors** — you don't need to be creative; SEMrush, SpyFu, Ahrefs,
BuzzSumo, WhatRunsWhere, BuiltWith reveal what they do. Multiple competitors chasing the
same term signals it converts; BuiltWith reveals A/B-testing tools, then Wayback Machine
shows their landing/home-page variations (changes made under A/B software are likely
winners). (4) **Stay up to date** with Product Hunt to adopt new tools before competitors.
(5) **Never stop learning** — Neil says he's not "good" at SEO, could be better, and
learns from competitors like HubSpot rather than dismissing them. (6) **Always be testing
— and retest**, because market conditions change.

  few will — documentation is how you find the few to double down on.
- As of 2019-01-17, Neil said to never be satisfied or complacent with growth (even at
  15–20% month-over-month), because complacency is when growth stops.
- As of 2019-01-17, Neil said spying on competitors via SEMrush/SpyFu/Ahrefs/BuzzSumo/
  WhatRunsWhere/BuiltWith substitutes for creativity, and that multiple competitors
  targeting the same keyword signals it converts.
- As of 2019-01-17, Neil described a tactic of using BuiltWith to detect a competitor's
  A/B-testing tool (Crazy Egg/Optimizely) then Wayback Machine to view their page
  variations — inferring those changes are conversion winners.
- As of 2019-01-17, Neil said he gets ~1.8 million unique visitors a month while HubSpot
  gets five-to-seven times more, using that gap to argue you should always keep learning
  from competitors rather than dismissing them. [self-reported/comparative figures]
- As of 2019-01-17, Neil stressed always testing AND retesting, giving a worked example:
  forcing Google-only login once boosted conversions, but on retest (amid rising security
  concerns) conversions tanked until he re-offered email/password alongside Google.
