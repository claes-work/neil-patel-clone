# 3 Insider Tips: How to Give a Money Back Guarantee Without Getting a TON of Refunds

## Summary

A solo tactical monologue on offering money-back guarantees while minimizing refunds.
Neil's framing: don't offer a guarantee only to lift conversions — offer it to create the
best possible customer experience (he cites Amazon's no-hassle returns as the reason
customers keep coming back and trust builds). His tips: **(1) Build an amazing product/
service** — if it's bad, you *should* get refunds; a great product earns word-of-mouth
referrals. **(2) Offer a longer guarantee than 30 days.** He A/B-tested a 30-day vs. 60-day
guarantee and the 60-day generated ~7% more sales (people read the longer window as
confidence in the product), while the refund percentage stayed the same — more sales, no
extra refunds. Windows can be 40/45/60/90 days or even a year. **(3) For physical/
e-commerce, require the product be shipped back** to get a refund — many people want to
keep the product and just get money back, so requiring return reduces refunds. **(4) For
SaaS, don't allow one-click cancel/refund** — require an email or a form asking, in at
least a few sentences, why they're canceling; this yields meaningful insight to improve
the product and can boost conversions. The same "ask why" applies to physical refunds.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-06-12, Neil says money-back guarantees should be offered primarily to create

  do lift conversions.
- As of 2018-06-12, Neil says a strong product/service naturally minimizes refunds and
  drives word-of-mouth; a bad product deserves its refunds.
- As of 2018-06-12, Neil reports an A/B test where a 60-day money-back guarantee generated

- As of 2018-06-12, Neil says SaaS businesses should not allow one-click refunds — require
  an email or a multi-sentence form explaining why, both to reduce refunds and to gather
  product-improvement insight.
