# Keywords Insights by Ubersuggest Chrome Extension: How to get Keyword Ideas From the SERP

## Summary
Neil Patel demonstrates the free Ubersuggest Chrome extension, which overlays keyword and SEO data directly onto the Google search results page so users can research keywords without leaving the SERP. He walks through installing it (via the Chrome Web Store or by searching "Ubersuggest chrome extension," where it is the first result) and shows the data it surfaces for any query typed into Google: monthly search volume, cost per click, seasonality trends over time, mobile-versus-desktop click split, the share of clicks going to SEO results versus paid results versus no results at all, and the age range of searchers. He argues these signals matter for judging real traffic potential and commercial intent — a high-volume term where most people click nothing, or one searched mostly by under-18s, is a poor target. He also covers the competition data shown per ranking result (referring domains, domain score on a 0–100 scale, backlinks, social shares), the ability to pull and export the list of sites linking to any ranking page on demand, related-keyword suggestions with volume/CPC/SEO-difficulty (abbreviated "SD"), and the extension's tabs for suggestions, questions, prepositions, and comparisons that deep-link into the full Ubersuggest app for more keyword ideas. He closes by stressing the tool is 100% free, requires no registration or account, and collects no user data. Note: this overlaps the broader Ubersuggest workflow already ingested (yt-_ve9cmRaem0, 2020-03-02), but is distinct in focusing specifically on the Chrome extension's on-SERP keyword-insights feature rather than the standalone app.

## Key claims
- The Ubersuggest Chrome extension makes it easier to find lucrative keywords and get more SEO traffic by surfacing data directly on the Google results page (as of 2020-06-20).
- The extension is free, requires no registration or account, and does not require any of the user's data (as of 2020-06-20).
- Install it via the Chrome Web Store (type "Ubersuggest," first result) or by googling "Ubersuggest chrome extension," where it is the first result; setup takes only a few minutes (as of 2020-06-20).
- For any query typed into Google, the extension displays the monthly search volume and cost per click alongside the normal results (as of 2020-06-20).
- Clicking "view all" reveals seasonality — spikes and dips in searches over time — plus the mobile-versus-desktop share of clicks (as of 2020-06-20).
- The extension shows the percentage of clicks going to SEO results, paid results, and no results at all; Neil notes that, surprisingly, many people click on no results (as of 2020-06-20).
- The no-click share matters for traffic potential: a term searched thousands or tens of thousands of times will not yield much traffic if ~90% of searchers click nothing (as of 2020-06-20).
- The extension shows the age range of searchers, which signals commercial intent — a term searched mostly by people under 18 is less likely to convert because younger people usually have less money (as of 2020-06-20).
- It provides an overview per keyword of search volume, SEO ranking difficulty, paid difficulty, and rough cost per click (as of 2020-06-20).
- Data can be localized: changing the country in settings updates both the keyword data and the CPC currency for that country (as of 2020-06-20).
- Ubersuggest reports general competition data for the average page ranking in the top 10 — its number of backlinks and its domain score, a metric from 0 to 100 where higher means more authoritative (as of 2020-06-20).
- Pages in the top 10 with many backlinks and a high domain score signal a more difficult keyword to rank for (as of 2020-06-20).
- News-oriented terms like "stocks" change constantly, so ranking pages tend to have few backlinks (some only hours old), whereas more evergreen terms tend to have older, more-linked pages (as of 2020-06-20).
- Worked example — "marketing": the average page ranking in the top 10 has 586 backlinks and a domain score of 90, indicating a competitive term (as of 2020-06-20).
- The link count shown under each result is the number of referring domains — the count of unique domains linking to that URL (as of 2020-06-20).
- Clicking the down arrow on a result makes Ubersuggest pull, on demand, the full list of sites linking to that page, which can be viewed and exported for any ranking result (as of 2020-06-20).
- For each ranking page the extension also shows social shares, domain score, and links (as of 2020-06-20).
- Below the results, the extension surfaces Google's related marketing terms with searches per month, cost per click, and SEO difficulty (abbreviated "SD" to save space) (as of 2020-06-20).
- On the right side of the SERP the extension shows related keywords plus tabs for suggestions, questions, prepositions, and comparisons; clicking a tab opens Ubersuggest with more keywords of that type, each showing volume, CPC, and difficulty (as of 2020-06-20).
- At the bottom the extension shows who ranks (e.g., Wikipedia at #1, HubSpot at #2 with 127 links) and adjusts the number of listings to match the SERP — 10 listings for 10 results, eight for eight, six for six (as of 2020-06-20).
- Clicking a related keyword (e.g., questions) opens Ubersuggest in a new tab showing question-oriented terms such as "what marketing is" or "why marketing research is important," giving content and keyword ideas (as of 2020-06-20).
