# 27 Content Types For Instagram — Instagram Unlocked, Module 1, Lesson 3

## Summary
Third lesson of the Instagram Unlocked "Zero to 100,000 Followers" course, surveying
Instagram content formats: daily Stories (kept raw/unpolished — "people want to see
the real you"; organized into categorized Highlights); giveaways (cites Domino's;
recommends sponsorship swaps with other accounts and asking entrants to enable
notifications); tutorials/carousels (his own most-used format); quotes (screenshots
his own tweets onto Instagram); product/demo videos (e.g. Ubersuggest walkthroughs)
and citing Gary Vaynerchuk's interview-style videos as a reference pattern; caption
craft (first 125 characters are all that show in-feed); open-ended questions;
before/afters (cites Billy Gene as a positive fitness-transformation example);
promotional posts (his own agency and Ubersuggest); behind-the-scenes (a format he
says he uses less now than before); carousel storytelling with a "swipe right" arrow
CTA (notes these get fewer likes but the most impressions on his profile); brand
story; lifestyle content (cites Grant Cardone's car-heavy lifestyle content alongside
Cardone's own "I got to where I am by investing in myself, not buying cars" framing);
Instagram Live interviews (says a weekly Thursday interview slot reliably grew his
follower count; the key is guests of similar-or-greater following size); content
repurposing from blog/YouTube/Facebook; AMAs; trend-jumping (cites the ALS Ice Bucket
Challenge); user-generated content (with a personal aside that he wants realistic,
non-airbrushed body imagery — contrasts Victoria's Secret-style shows unfavorably,
citing a skincare ad using a model with visible skin discoloration approvingly, and a
comment about not wanting his daughter to grow up believing unrealistic body standards
are the norm); a GIF anecdote (his wife made a GIF of their baby that became one of
his most-engaged posts); and closes with posting best-practices (image sizing,
audience-time testing, frequency testing, CTA measurement, closed captions, Social
Blade analytics) and a Kayla Itsines case study (his stated source of inspiration/
benchmark for what's achievable: born 1991, ~$63M net worth as of 2018 per his
citation, "Bikini Body Guides" and "Sweat with Kayla" app), plus a 2015 Apple-ad case
study about brand-fit discipline in what UGC/sponsorships to accept.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-09-19, Neil's caption doctrine: Instagram only shows the first 125
  characters of a caption in-feed, so the opening line has to earn the tap-to-expand.
- As of 2020-09-19, Neil claims a weekly (Thursday) Instagram Live interview series he
  ran previously reliably grew his follower count each time it aired (self-reported,
  no figures given).
- As of 2020-09-19, Neil's stated observation on his own profile: carousel/multi-image
  posts get fewer likes but the highest impressions of any format he uses.
- As of 2020-09-19 (self-reported family anecdote, not registry-verifiable): Neil's
  wife made a GIF of their baby that became one of his most-engaged Instagram posts;
  no names given for wife or child.
- As of 2020-09-19, Neil cites (without an independent source) Kayla Itsines as having
  ~$63 million in wealth "as of 2018" — flagged as an unverified third-party claim,
  not about Neil himself.

## Notable verbatim quotes
