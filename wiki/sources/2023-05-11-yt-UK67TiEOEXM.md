# Social Media Strategies to Connect with Your B2C Audience

## Summary
Tactical interview split between social-engagement benchmarking and a rapid-fire holiday-season
growth playbook. Gives concrete **engagement-rate benchmarks**: for video content, view rates
around **5% of follower count are good** (ranges 3-10%); for image/static posts, **~3% like
rate is good**; both agree personal/authentic content dramatically outperforms business content
— the host cites a 20-second personal video (himself and his girlfriend laughing during a
minor meltdown about a missed trash pickup) that hit ~38% of his follower base in views versus
a more typical ~11-15% for business-focused clips, and Neil says he sees the identical pattern
on his own accounts (business content ~4-5% engagement vs. 10-20%+ for personal content). States
his read on why: **people follow individuals, not corporate brands**, on social platforms —
naming the Kardashians, Cristiano Ronaldo, and Will Smith as examples of personal-brand pull
exceeding corporate-brand pull. On engineering virality, argues the reliable strategy is not to
manufacture edge/controversial content oneself (high variance, "hit or miss") but to **identify
organically viral moments/creators and attach to them** — cites the "Ocean Spray guy" (Nathan
Apodaca's viral longboard-cranberry-juice TikTok) as an example where the brand's correct move
was to amplify (giving him a free truck) rather than try to replicate the moment internally, on
the logic that amplification was cheaper than the earned publicity it generated. Delivers, on
request, a specific **75-day holiday-season sales acceleration playbook**: (1) cross-promotion/
syndication partnerships with adjacent-industry peers via blog, email, and livestream channels;
(2) break ad landing-page copy into a sequence of Instagram/Snapchat-style "stories" so the
marketing message is consumed before a swipe-up call-to-action, rather than requiring a
landing-page visit; (3) sell live on social platforms, citing **Tai Lopez's** live-selling of
bankruptcy-acquired retail brands (Pier One, Dress Barn) as a working model; (4) treat YouTube
publishing as front-loaded — unlike traditional SEO's slow compounding build, YouTube's
algorithm weights the **first 24 hours (or even first hour)** of view velocity heavily, so blast
email/push notifications the moment a video goes live to seed that signal; (5) guest-post
listicles on large publications (Forbes, Entrepreneur, New York Times), embedding one's own
product at position #2 or #3 (not #1, for credibility); (6) a list-hygiene deliverability
tactic — periodically prune non-openers from an email list so that remaining engaged
subscribers keep landing in the primary inbox rather than the Promotions tab; (7) send simple
yes/no engagement-bait questions before and during Q4 to boost address-book placement and
inbox deliverability ahead of the holiday send volume.

## Key claims
- Gives specific engagement-rate benchmarks: ~5% of followers viewing a video and ~3% liking
  an image post are considered good performance; personal/authentic content can run 2-4x
  higher than business content on the same account (self-reported, dated 2023-05-11) —
  candidate quantified benchmark for a social-media tactics page.
- States the reliable strategy for viral reach is to identify and amplify organically viral
  external moments/creators rather than manufacture controversy internally, framing
  manufactured "edgy" content as high-variance and unreliable (self-reported opinion, dated
  2023-05-11).
- ★ Lays out a concrete 75-day holiday-season sales playbook covering cross-promotion,
  story-format landing-page compression, live selling, a YouTube first-24-hour view-velocity
  tactic, guest-post listicle placement, and two email-deliverability/list-hygiene tactics
  (self-reported, dated 2023-05-11) — dense, actionable tactical content; a strong candidate
  for promotion into a content-marketing/email-marketing tactics page at synthesis.
- States that YouTube's ranking algorithm is front-loaded on early view velocity (first 24
  hours, sometimes first hour) in contrast to traditional SEO's slower compounding build,
  recommending an immediate email/push-notification blast on video publish (self-reported,
  dated 2023-05-11) — consistent with the "7 Steps to Ranking Number 1 on YouTube" tactics
