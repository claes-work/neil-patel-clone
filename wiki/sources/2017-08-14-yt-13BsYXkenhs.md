# How to Get Your Website Ranked in Google News

## Summary
A short SEO tactical video in which Neil Patel gives three requirements for getting a
site into Google News for fast (if temporary) search traffic: (1) your content must be
news-related — use Google Trends (google.com/trends) to find what's hot right now and
write articles tied to trending/news topics in your space; (2) create a Google News
sitemap (in addition to your normal XML sitemap, which you can generate with the Yoast
SEO plugin) and submit it in Google Search Console so more articles get picked up; and
(3) use unique, keyword-rich URL structures rather than generic ones like
"domain.com/hello." He frames the payoff as speed rather than durability — news
articles can appear at the top within a few hours and may hold for up to a day, not five
days — and says that of all the Google News requirements out there, these three drive
the most Google News traffic in his experience.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-14, Neil states:
- Google News only accepts news-related content; if your content isn't news-related you
  won't get in (as of 2017-08-14).
- Use Google Trends (google.com/trends) to see what's hot right now and write articles
  tied to those trends / news topics in your space to raise your odds of inclusion (as
  of 2017-08-14).
- Create a Google News sitemap in addition to your normal XML sitemap (the latter is
  easy via the Yoast SEO plugin) and submit it in Google Search Console; this gets more
  articles picked up and you're much more likely to rank fast for news-related articles
  (as of 2017-08-14).
- Google News traffic is fast but short-lived — a news article can show at the top
  within a few hours at the latest, may hold for a few hours or a whole day, but won't
  stay for ~five days (as of 2017-08-14).
- URL structure must be unique and contain the keywords you want to rank for; a generic
  URL like "domain.com/hello" won't rank in Google News or google.com (as of
  2017-08-14).
- Google publishes many other Google News requirements and following them still carries
  no guarantee of inclusion or traffic; Neil finds these three tactics produce the most
  Google News traffic of any tips he has seen (self-reported) (as of 2017-08-14).
