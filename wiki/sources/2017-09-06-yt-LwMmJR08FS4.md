# 5 Ways to Make Google AdWords More Profitable (Improve Your CTR!)

## Summary
Neil Patel explains how to make Google AdWords cheaper and more profitable by raising click-through rate (CTR), arguing that Google ranks ads on a blend of CTR and revenue-per-impression rather than bid alone — so a lower bidder with higher CTR can outrank and pay less than a higher-bidding competitor. He walks through five tactics: (1) research who is buying ads on your keywords and what copy they use (via SEMrush, entering a URL or keyword to see all bidders and their ad copy), (2) rewrite that copy into a better version, modeling on the top-ranked ads; (3) include the searched keyword inside your ad copy, since ads missing the keyword get clicked less; (4) daypart — run ads only during hours your buyers are active (e.g., B2B during work hours) and pause them at times when searchers are unlikely to be ideal customers; and (5) geotarget ads to the regions you actually serve, using the example of a frozen-yogurt shop wasting money advertising to him 50 miles away. Following the steps, he says, raises CTR, lowers cost per click, and generates more sales.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-06, Neil states:
- Buying ads is one of the quickest ways to get more traffic from Google, but AdWords is expensive unless you optimize your click-through rate (as of 2017-09-06).
- CTR rises when a higher percentage of the people who see your ad click through (as of 2017-09-06).
- Because Google makes money per click, an advertiser paying less per click but earning far more clicks per impression can be shown above competitors — e.g., a competitor paying $2/click getting 1 click per 10 impressions vs. you paying $1/click getting 5 clicks per 10 impressions, and Google shows your ad on top because it makes more money from you (as of 2017-09-06).
- Google optimizes ad placement for a combination of click-through rate and income, so the ads at the top tend to have the highest CTR (as of 2017-09-06).
- In SEMrush you can enter any URL or keyword and see every company bidding on that term and the ad copy they are using (as of 2017-09-06).
- You should model your ad copy on the top-ranked ads and create a better version of it (as of 2017-09-06).
- The keyword the searcher types should appear within your ad copy; if the keyword isn't there, people are less likely to click through (as of 2017-09-06).
- Dayparting — turning ads on during specific hours and off when you won't get business — improves results; e.g., a B2B advertiser should not run ads at night when searchers are unlikely to be the ideal customer (as of 2017-09-06).
- Geotargeting ads to the regions you serve avoids showing irrelevant ads to people who won't click; an out-of-area ad (a frozen-yogurt shop 50 miles away) just wastes money (as of 2017-09-06).
- Following these steps raises CTR, lowers ad cost, and generates more sales (as of 2017-09-06).
