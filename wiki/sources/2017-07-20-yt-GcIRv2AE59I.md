# A Brilliantly Simple Way to Boost Website Traffic - How to Get International Blog Traffic

## Summary
Neil's growth lever here is **internationalization**: he says expanding globally — not writing more content — is the easiest way to grow search traffic, because there's little competition overseas. He reports the US makes up under 50% of neilpatel.com traffic, with strong regions like India, Brazil, and Spanish-speaking markets. Three-part method: (1) translate content (hire cheap translators on Upwork rather than relying on the weak Transposh WordPress plugin); (2) implement **hreflang** so search engines serve the right language version per region, keeping translated pages internally linked to same-language pages; (3) geo-redirect *users* (not search-engine bots) to their region based on browser settings. He flags the bot exception explicitly: Google crawls from US servers, so redirecting bots would hide your translated versions.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-20, Neil states:
- The easiest way to get more search traffic is expanding internationally, not writing more content — because there's no competition overseas, and it's been one of the biggest drivers of his growth (as of 2017-07-20).
- The US does not make up even 50% of neilpatel.com traffic; popular regions include India, Brazil, and all the Spanish-speaking markets (as of 2017-07-20). [Self-reported.]
- Step 1 — translate your content into your target languages; most of the world doesn't speak English (Hindi, Mandarin are huge) and there's a lot of money outside the US. The Transposh WordPress plugin is "not that effective"; hiring translators on Upwork is better and cheap (~$50–$100 to translate many pages) (as of 2017-07-20).
- Step 2 — use **hreflang** code to tell search engines which page is the English vs. e.g. Portuguese-for-Brazil version; neilpatel.com uses hreflang for German, Spanish, and Portuguese/Brazil. In a market like Brazil ~5% speak English and ~95% Portuguese, so hreflang gets the Portuguese page ranked there (as of 2017-07-20).
- Internal-linking rule for international: Portuguese pages should link to other Portuguese pages, not back to English pages (as of 2017-07-20).
- Step 3 — geo-redirect users to their region using browser settings (e.g. Chrome reports the user's language); hire an Upwork developer to build it — but instruct them **not to redirect search-engine bots**, because Google crawls from US (English-speaking) servers and you want it to read both your English and translated versions (as of 2017-07-20).
