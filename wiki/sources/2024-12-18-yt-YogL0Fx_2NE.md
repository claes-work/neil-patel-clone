# Did the Hawk Tuah Girl Really Steal $50M?

## Summary
A short co-hosted Marketing School episode reacting to the December 2024 "$HAWK" crypto scandal involving Haliey Welch (the "Hawk Tuah girl"). The hosts walk through the on-chain story — a meme coin that ran up to roughly $50M market cap and then collapsed in a "rug pull" — and pivot to a personal-brand lesson: a brand built fast can be tarnished even faster, and an inexperienced creator surrounded by profit-seeking stakeholders gets pulled in the wrong directions. The primary narrator (context-inferred to be Neil) is careful not to accuse Welch herself of theft, framing her as naive and taken advantage of rather than a mastermind, and reads a mid-roll ad for NP Digital's "Carrot/Karrot" LinkedIn account-based-marketing product. Eric handles interjections and the outro (Agency Owners Association / "marketing school.agency"). No landmark marketing framework appears. Note: coffeezilla's on-chain critique and Welch's own audio clip are quoted/played media, not host speech.

## Key claims
- The "Hawk Tuah girl" (Haliey Welch) launched a meme coin (referred to as "$HAWK" / "my Hawk meme coin"); it ran up to ~$50M and then collapsed — a crypto "rug pull." Neil explicitly withholds judgment on whether *she* stole the money, saying it looks like it was her staff / the people who came up with the idea who dumped tokens. (Neil, 2024-12-18) — much of the on-chain detail he reads aloud from third-party sources (see Attribution notes)
- Core brand lesson: a personal brand built up very fast can be tarnished even faster. (Neil, 2024-12-18)
- Welch got "15 minutes of fame" from TikTok and now has a podcast that performs well, but because she is inexperienced in business she is being pulled in different directions by people who want to make money off her brand, and "she doesn't know any better." (Neil, 2024-12-18)
- She "got into bed with the wrong people" — many stakeholders (including podcast partners) have "their tentacles" in how she operates, directing what she does, what she talks about, and who she talks to. (Neil, 2024-12-18)
- Neil says he feels bad both for Welch and for the people who lost money; he bets she had no clue what was happening and does not know much about crypto — and stresses he is not calling her dumb ("she's not dumb"), just taken advantage of, and likely to "lose most if not everything." (Neil, 2024-12-18)
