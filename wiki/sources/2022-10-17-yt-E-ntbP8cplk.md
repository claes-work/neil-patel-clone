# The Dirty Little Secret About Social Media Marketing

## Summary
Argues that nobody — "no matter what guru you talk to" — can guarantee viral/famous
status on social media; growth is "purely through experimentation," not a formula.
Frames this via a personal disclaimer (can't be a shirtless male-model like David
Gandy, can't dance like Julianne/Derek Hough, can't sing like Justin Bieber) plus the
example of TikToker Khaby Lame (100M+ followers gained by silently reacting to memes —
"no one in the world would probably have guessed" that format would work). Gives a
concrete cadence playbook: post at least once/day (ideally 3x/day) across all major
networks (TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube, Pinterest, Snap); rotate all
main content types (text, video, quotes, images, live, stories) at least weekly to
learn what a given platform's audience wants; notes the same content performs
differently by platform (his Elon-Musk-marketing content flops on YouTube but does
well on Instagram). Advises patience (results are not immediate), paid kickstarts
(ad spend or paid cross-promotion) to overcome a slow start, and prioritizing audience
quality over raw follower count. Closes with an NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- States his agency NP Digital employs "over 700 marketers" and serves "over 500
  companies" worldwide, including social media marketing services (as of 2022-10-17,
  self-reported).
- Claims Khaby Lame (referred to as "Khaby") has "over 100 million TikTok followers"
  gained purely by pointing out flaws in memes without speaking (third-party example,
  not self-reported).
- States his own content about "how Elon Musk leverages marketing" underperforms on
  YouTube but performs better on Instagram — a platform-specific performance
  comparison (self-reported, undated beyond the video's publish date).
- Recommends posting cadence: minimum 1x/day, ideally 3x/day, across all major
  networks; rotate all main content types at least once/week (tactical prescription,
  dated to 2022-10-17).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> No matter what guru you talk to or who you follow, no one can really help you get a million engaged followers or help you be the next Kylie Jenner. It's a lot of hit and miss. The only way to really do well is purely through experimentation.

> See, I'm not a male model. I can't take off my shirt and flex and get tons of likes like David Gandy. And I can't record a video of me dancing like Julianne or Derek Hough because I can't dance like them. And I can't sing like Justin Bieber either. In essence, you have to leverage your own X factor.

> People want results tomorrow. They want to go viral right away. It just won't happen. When you do something long enough, you'll get better at it.
