# 4 Ways to Decrease Your Bounce Rate (Rank Higher in Google)

## Summary
In this solo video Neil Patel argues that bounce rate is one signal Google uses to decide whether to rank a site higher or lower, framing a high bounce rate as evidence that visitors are not finding what they came for. He walks through practical page-design tactics to keep visitors engaged: show the product or content first (rather than opt-ins or ads above the fold), use a large-enough text size, keep pages minimalistic by stripping sidebar clutter and calls-to-action, cross-link within content to pull readers deeper into the site, and add non-text media like images, video, or audio to hold attention. He draws on his own experience—both his blog and the Crazy Egg blog—as evidence that giving visitors what they want first lowers bounce rate, and closes by asserting that longer on-site engagement leads to lower bounce rates and better Google rankings over time.

## Key claims
- Bounce rate is one factor Google looks at when determining whether to rank a site higher or lower; a high bounce rate signals visitors are not finding what they are looking for (as of 2017-06-13).
- Showcasing the product or content first—rather than opt-ins/ads above the fold—lowers bounce rate; Neil says removing his above-the-fold opt-ins and ads reduced his own high bounce rate (as of 2017-06-13).
- Text size that is too small increases bounce rate because it is hard to read, especially on mobile devices (as of 2017-06-13).
- Making webpages minimalistic and removing sidebar elements/calls-to-action lowers bounce rate; Neil cites a test on the Crazy Egg blog (crazyegg.com/blog) where showcasing content before the blog post decreased bounce rate versus using big sidebars (as of 2017-06-13).
- Cross-linking within content to other pages of the site keeps people moving through the whole site instead of bouncing off a single page (as of 2017-06-13).
- Adding non-text content—images, video, or audio—is visually appealing and keeps people engaged, which decreases bounce rate (as of 2017-06-13).
- Keeping visitors on the site longer lowers bounce rate and leads to higher Google rankings over time (as of 2017-06-13).

## Notable verbatim quotes
