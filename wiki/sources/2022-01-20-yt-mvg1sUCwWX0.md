# The Most Realistic Advice On How to Start a New Website (And Actually Get Traffic)

## Summary
A tactical checklist for launching a new content site. Prerequisites before starting:
a hungry market validated via Ubersuggest keyword volume, a realistic time commitment
(he states 60+ hours/week ideally, 8 hours/week "at minimum" but calls that
insufficient), acceptance that organic traffic is a 1+ year payoff, baseline SEO
knowledge (or use Ubersuggest/his blog/videos to get it), a stable host (WordPress +
DreamHost + WooCommerce cited as his preferences), and a memorable, easy-to-spell
domain. Execution steps: map keywords via Ubersuggest (own or competitor domain),
prioritize low-difficulty/page-one-potential keywords, build topical content clusters
(niche specialization — cites his own single-topic focus on digital marketing as the
reason he avoids talking about entrepreneurship/crypto/medicine, framed as necessary
for Google to see him as a specialist), install Google Search Console from day one,
create content daily to build the habit ("flex your creation muscles"), and do
technical SEO monthly via Ubersuggest's Site Audit report, working top-to-bottom by
impact.

## Key claims
- States a specific minimum-effort threshold for a new site: "eight hours a week in
  your first six months at minimum," but says the realistic number to succeed is
  "60-plus hours a week" (self-reported tactical opinion, dated 2022-01-20) —
  agency-entrepreneurship, content-marketing.
- Frames organic/SEO traffic as a strictly long-term play: little reward before ~1
  year of consistent effort, meaningful reward after (self-reported tactical claim,
  dated 2022-01-20) — seo.
- Argues topical specialization is required to rank, illustrating with his own
  channel: if he talked about entrepreneurship, NFTs/crypto, or medicine alongside
  digital marketing, "Google would be like, this Neil guy's not an expert in
  anything" (self-reported SEO opinion, dated 2022-01-20) — seo.
- Recommends prioritizing on-page technical fixes using Ubersuggest's Site Audit
  report "top to bottom," on the premise the tool orders fixes by ranking impact
  (self-reported tool workflow, dated 2022-01-20) — seo.
