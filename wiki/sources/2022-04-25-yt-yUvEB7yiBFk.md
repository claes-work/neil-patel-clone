# The Easiest Way To Build Backlinks FAST (Works In 2024)

## Summary
In this short tactical video, Neil Patel argues that backlinks do not have to be hard to build and pushes back on the common advice that simply writing amazing content is enough to earn links. He contends that unpromoted content goes unseen and unlinked, and that even promoted content is hard to get links for because people are less willing to link out than they were in the past. He then breaks down two methods, ordered from his second-favorite to his favorite. The second-easiest method uses the backlink opportunity report in Ubersuggest: a user enters their own domain plus three competitors, and the tool surfaces every site that links to those competitors but not to the user. Neil reasons that anyone willing to link to three different competitors is not loyal to any one of them and is therefore likely to link to a better page, so the tactic is to identify the exact linking pages, create a superior version, and email the linkers to request a swap. His favorite method is building or acquiring a free tool. He cites his own neilpatel.com/ubersuggest tool — a free SEO tool with an optional paid version — noting that offering for free what competitors charge for attracts links because people love linking to things that are easy, cheap, or free and that require no payment or registration; he claims that tool has over 10,000 backlinks. For those without large budgets, he recommends buying inexpensive tools on CodeCanyon (roughly $10-$30), white-labeling them, and placing them on your site so they generate links over time even with poor promotion. He frames the tool approach as a long-term play that works best when combined with continued content and other link-building efforts, and closes by pointing viewers to his agency, NP Digital, for strategy help.

## Key claims
- Backlinks do not have to be hard to build, and the standard advice to "write amazing content and you'll get links" is insufficient on its own (as of 2022-04-25).
- If you write content but do not promote it, no one will see it or link to it; and even with promotion, links are still hard to get (as of 2022-04-25).
- People used to be more willing to link out than they are now (as of 2022-04-25).
- The second-easiest link-building method is Ubersuggest's backlink opportunity report: enter your domain plus three competitors and it shows everyone who links to those competitors but not to you (as of 2022-04-25).
- Someone willing to link to three different competitors is not loyal to any one of them, so they are likely to link to you if you offer a better page (as of 2022-04-25).
- The workflow for that report is to see the exact linking pages, create a better version of those pages, and email the linkers to request a link swap; not everyone will agree, but a good share will (as of 2022-04-25).
- Neil's favorite link-building method is offering a free tool, exemplified by his own neilpatel.com/ubersuggest, a free SEO/traffic tool with an optional paid version (as of 2022-04-25).
- Releasing for free what competitors normally charge for (whether $5, $10, or $100) attracts links because people love linking to things that are easy, cheap, or free (as of 2022-04-25).
- Free tools earn links more easily because they require no credit card, no payment, and no user registration (as of 2022-04-25).
- The Ubersuggest tool has over 10,000 backlinks (as of 2022-04-25).
- You can build a tool cheaply by buying one on CodeCanyon for roughly $10, $20, or $30, white-labeling it, and putting it on your website, where it builds links over time even if you are bad at promoting it (as of 2022-04-25).
- Alternatively, you can Google for free white-label tools in your industry (e.g., a free SEO tool or a real estate tool) and place them on your site to generate backlinks over time (as of 2022-04-25).
- The free-tool approach is a long-term strategy that works very well and should be combined with, not substituted for, writing content and other link-building approaches (as of 2022-04-25).
- Combining the free-tool method with existing link-building efforts is how you beat competitors at building backlinks and outrank them (as of 2022-04-25).
