# What are the MAJOR changes in SEO for 2020? (YouTube title later updated to "2023")

## Summary
Solo talking-head forecast video in which Neil Patel lays out four structural changes he
expects in Google search going into 2020, then gives five tactical responses. The changes:
(1) zero-click search keeps rising — he cites Jumpshot/SparkToro that 49% of Google searches
already end in no click; (2) organic visits keep declining — citing Merkle, organic visits
fell 6% YoY in Q2 2019, with mobile visit growth dropping from 13% (Q1 2019) to 5% (Q2 2019);
(3) Google competes harder with Amazon via commerce features such as "Buy on Google", which he
frames as an opportunity for Shopify-style store owners to sell without a site visit; and
(4) rich snippets answer more queries outright — citing a Path Interactive study, 40% of
13–18-year-olds and 20% of 18–30-year-olds get what they need from a snippet without clicking.
He explicitly refuses the "Google is evil" framing: better UX means more total searchers, more
people coming online, and the traffic you do get should convert better because the low-intent
answer-seekers were never going to buy. His five tips: master search intent (problems, not
keywords — via Ubersuggest's questions/prepositions/comparisons); raise CTR (compelling titles,
unused keywords, strong meta descriptions, breadcrumb markup, ClickFlow); use schema markup
aggressively (he uses FAQ schema on neilpatel.com and says it *improved* rankings and clicks
despite answering the question in the SERP); build a loyal owned audience (Hello Bar email
opt-ins, Subscribers push notifications, social, chatbots like ManyChat/MobileMonkey); and go
omnichannel, invoking the marketing "rule of seven" to argue combined channels lower CPA.
Closes with a plug for Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil said Google makes over 3,200 algorithm updates per year, so a lot will change in 2020.
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil cited Jumpshot and SparkToro that 49% of all Google searches end in a no-click, and predicted that share would increase further in 2020.
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil cited Merkle that total visits from organic search fell 6% year over year in Q2 2019, with mobile hit hardest — visit growth dropping from 13% in Q1 2019 to 5% in Q2 2019 — and predicted organic traffic would keep declining in 2020, though probably more slowly.
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil predicted Google would compete more heavily with Amazon in 2020, pointing to the 2019 "Buy on Google" feature and to Shopify as the Wall-Street-anointed Amazon alternative; he expected iterations to make buying so good that shoppers never visit the merchant's website.
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil cited a Path Interactive study that 40% of people aged 13–18 get the information they need from a snippet without clicking through, versus 20% for ages 18–30.
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil argued lost traffic to snippets is not a disaster: better Google UX brings more searchers, more people come online, and the visitors Google diverts largely weren't going to buy — so conversion rates on remaining traffic should rise.
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil's tip 1 was to master uncovering search intent — "it's not about keywords, it's about problems" — using Ubersuggest to surface the questions, prepositions and comparisons people type.
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil's tip 2 was to increase CTR with compelling titles, keywords competitors aren't using, strong meta descriptions, breadcrumb markup, and title-tag testing via ClickFlow (which he noted has a free tier); he stressed a lower listing can out-click the one above it.
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil's tip 3 was to explore schema markup as much as possible; he said he uses FAQ schema often on neilpatel.com (visible on searches like "digital marketing") and that despite answering questions in the SERP it improved both his rankings and his clicks.
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil's tip 4 was to build a loyal audience you can bring back: Hello Bar for email opt-ins, Subscribers for push notifications, plus social channels and chatbots (he named ManyChat and MobileMonkey).
- As of 2019-12-05, Neil's tip 5 was an omnichannel approach — not relying on SEO or paid or social or content alone — arguing combined channels lower CPA and invoking the marketing "rule of seven" (seven exposures makes conversion much more likely) as brand-building.
