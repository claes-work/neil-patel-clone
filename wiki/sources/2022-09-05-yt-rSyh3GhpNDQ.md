# My Favorite Type of Marketing

## Summary
Neil names in-person events as his single favorite marketing channel, over SEO/PPC/
social/email/podcasting, arguing in-person meetings build deeper connections and close
deals faster. He recommends speaking (not just attending) at industry conferences with
your ideal-customer audience, holding a workshop immediately after your talk (worked
example: Collision conference in Toronto, team walking the floor meeting
clients/prospects — he notes they lacked a booth and wished they'd had one), and, where
possible, trading a speaking fee for a booth + advertising perks instead of cash, which
he says produces better ROI. He caveats that in-person events shouldn't be a sole
channel: he relays an anecdote from an Expedia team member (met at one of their Canada
offices) that Expedia's omnichannel approach lowers blended customer acquisition cost by
roughly 10% across all channels, due to continuous brand exposure.

## Key claims
- Names in-person events/conferences as his personal favorite marketing channel,
  citing faster deal-closing from in-person connection (as of 2022-09-05, self-reported
  opinion).
- Recommends trading conference speaking fees for booth space + advertising perks

- Relays a third-party claim from an unnamed Expedia team member: Expedia's
  omnichannel marketing approach reduces blended customer-acquisition cost by
  roughly 10% (as of 2022-09-05, third-party-sourced, not independently verified).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Believe it or not, it's in person events. I found nothing beats seeing and meeting people directly in person.

> Instead of charging for speaking that some events pay, you can trade that money for a booth space and also advertising perks.

> Their customer acquisition costs for all channels goes down by roughly 10% because people are continually seeing their brand.
