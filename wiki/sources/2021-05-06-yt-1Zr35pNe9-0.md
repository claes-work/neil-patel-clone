# Bullet-proof Ways to Increase eCommerce Sales (Without Getting More Traffic) - CRO Unlocked

## Summary
CRO Unlocked Week 2, Lesson 2. Opens with e-commerce market sizing (cites $4.2T in 2020
global e-commerce sales, projected to $6.54T by 2023) and a "lessons from the world's
largest retailers" (Amazon, Walmart, Costco, Alibaba, JD, eBay, Shopify) segment: protect
success and learn from every mistake, be relentlessly competitive, prioritize customer
retention and team wellbeing, keep innovating (cites delivery drones as an example),
obsess over customers (Amazon), remove risk where possible, take short-term losses for
long-term gains, build the right team (credits Bezos's and Musk's teams, not just the
founders), stay "stubborn but flexible" on data-backed decisions, and diversify
offerings. Pivots to tactics: claims retargeting is one of the highest-converting channels
(Facebook, YouTube, Google, and banner networks) because the audience is already warm;
covers Messenger chatbots (names **MobileMonkey** specifically) as a lead-capture/quiz/
webinar-signup tool with an 80%+ open rate vs. ~20% for email — but warns of platform rate
limits (one follow-up message per 24 hours unless the user replies) and advises caution
to avoid getting blocked. Introduces three competitive-analysis frameworks for the first
time in the ledger: **Porter's Five Forces**, **PEST analysis**, and **SWOT analysis**, as
inputs for generating test ideas, plus the A/B-vs-multivariate testing distinction (uses
neilpatel.com/ab-testing-calculator or Crazy Egg's built-in significance calculator).
Closes with a rundown of how Amazon, Newegg, Shopify, and BigCommerce use CRO in practice:
site-speed optimization, nav improvements, CTA/headline testing, social proof + fake-
review combat, and simplified/multi-method checkout (cites Shopify's "Shop" one-click
login/pre-fill flow).

## Key claims
- Cites global e-commerce sales at $4.2 trillion in the prior year (2020), projected to
  reach $6.54 trillion by 2023 (self-reported/uncited statistic, 2021-05-06) —
  analytics-cro.
- States retargeting is one of the highest-converting acquisition/re-engagement channels
  because the audience is already warm, and names Facebook and Google as the two primary
  retargeting channels (paraphrase, 2021-05-06) — paid-ads.
- Names **MobileMonkey** specifically as his recommended Messenger-bot tool, cites an
  80%+ open rate for Messenger vs. ~20% for email, but flags platform restrictions
  (max one follow-up message per 24 hours to non-responders) and general caution needed
  to avoid account blocks (self-reported, 2021-05-06; ⚠️ dated — the wiki already logs
  a later reversal on Messenger-bot enthusiasm as the channel declined, see the
  2021-02-27 source) — paid-ads / email-marketing.
- Introduces **Porter's Five Forces** (threat of new entrants, threat of substitutes,
  buyer/supplier bargaining power, competitive rivalry), **PEST analysis** (external
  environment: political/economic/social/technological), and **SWOT analysis**
  (strengths/weaknesses/opportunities/threats) as frameworks for generating CRO/strategy
  test ideas — first appearance of all three named frameworks in the ledger (paraphrase,
  2021-05-06) — agency-entrepreneurship / analytics-cro.
- Distinguishes A/B testing (two variants) from multivariate testing (many combined page
  elements tested together), recommending A/B testing by default unless traffic volume is
  high enough to support multivariate testing (paraphrase, 2021-05-06) — analytics-cro.
- Credits Shopify's "Shop" one-click pre-filled checkout and the shared Amazon/Newegg/
  BigCommerce playbook (site speed, nav improvements, CTA/headline tests, social proof,
  fake-review moderation, multi-method checkout incl. PayPal) as the common CRO pattern
  among large e-commerce platforms (paraphrase, 2021-05-06) — analytics-cro.
- Leadership/team belief: credits Bezos's and Musk's *teams*, not just the founders
  themselves, for Amazon's and SpaceX's success — "I don't care how smart you are... it's
  also his team" (paraphrase/near-verbatim, 2021-05-06) — agency-entrepreneurship.
