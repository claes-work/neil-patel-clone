# How To Build Paid Campaigns On YouTube, Etsy & Instagram - Module 4 - Part 2 - eCommerce Unlocked

## Summary
eCommerce Unlocked lesson covering marketing channels beyond Facebook/Google/Bing Ads,
opening with a strong claim that email is likely a business's single highest-converting
channel (cites 3.9B global email users), and that "all the big ecommerce companies"
Neil has consulted call email their #1 channel. Walks through three email use-cases —
cart-abandonment recovery (~10% win rate cited), win-back sequences for lapsed
customers, and customer-nurture flows — with real brand examples (ReserveBar coupon
abandonment sequence, Blue Apron win-back). Gives email best-practices (avoid
"no-reply" addresses, send mornings, personalize subject lines) and seasonal-spend
stats: back-to-school ($696.70/shopper), back-to-college ($976/shopper, $80.7B K-12+
college market combined), winter holidays ($1,000+/shopper, $730B+ total retail). Then
covers YouTube Ads, Instagram Ads (bio links, Bitly-tracked links, engagement-driven
ranking), and Etsy Ads setup (Shop Manager → Marketing → Advertising), noting Etsy's
$700M+/year revenue, 2.5M+ sellers, 7M active advertisers, and lower competition than
Facebook.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-07-16, Neil said "all the big ecommerce companies that I've consulted and
  worked with over the years they all tell me one thing, email is their number one
  channel" (self-reported consulting-experience claim).
- As of 2020-07-16, Neil cited average shopper spend as $696.70 for back-to-school,
  $976 for back-to-college, and $1,000+ for winter holidays, with combined K-12 +
  college back-to-school/college spend at roughly $80.7 billion and total holiday
  retail sales over $730 billion (unsourced/aggregator-style stats, presented as
  fact).
- As of 2020-07-16, Neil advised against using "no-reply" sender addresses, favoring a
  named person, company-name-based address, or mascot, and recommended sending emails
  in the morning for better open rates.
- As of 2020-07-16, Neil said Etsy does over $700 million/year in revenue with 2.5M+
  sellers and 7M active advertisers, positioning it as "much less competitive than
  Facebook" though lower-volume.

## Notable verbatim quotes
