# Mastering Email Marketing: Here's What's Working NOW - Email Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
The recap/closing lesson that **COMPLETES Email Marketing Unlocked**, explicitly framed
as reviewing the prior two weeks (following
[[wiki/sources/2021-02-04-yt-LqFX-81m2Qk]]). Opens with a run of industry statistics on
email-marketing health and organizational investment, then restates the course's core
levers (one-on-one personalization, A/B testing, segmentation-by-activity-level) before
addressing send-frequency ("no right answer" — match cadence to list expectations:
daily digest lists get daily email, weekly digest lists get weekly, promotional
lists should not be sold to every few days). Walks through a four-stage webinar email
sequence (invite → replay offer → offer → countdown, three days per stage) as the
recommended B2B/B2C nurture tool, noting webinars work for both but is calling out B2B
specifically because "not everything that works for B2C works for B2B" (the reverse he
says usually holds). Closes by naming the course's real takeaway — there is no single
right email strategy, only metrics-driven iteration — and pivots to email as one leg of
an omni-channel strategy alongside SEO, content marketing, and Facebook marketing.

## Key claims
- Cites that 78% of email marketers observed a rise in email engagement over the prior
  year (2020), and that marketers who segment their lists see a 760% increase in
  revenue (paraphrase, cited as industry statistics, source not named in-video, not
  independently re-verified).
- Cites that in 2020 the majority of companies had at least four people involved in
  their email review/approval process, up 52% — and states he expects that number to
  keep increasing (paraphrase, cited statistic + personal forecast).
- Cites that ~87% of marketers say they use email to distribute their content
  (paraphrase, cited statistic).
- Lays out a four-stage, 3-days-per-stage webinar email sequence — invite, replay
  offer, offer, countdown — as his recommended nurture flow, paired with an ad → landing
  page → email-collection → sequence funnel that reinforces the registration decision
  with data, process detail, testimonials, and case studies (paraphrase, previously-
  unlogged structured framework).
- States webinars should not be evergreen "at least at the beginning" because urgency
  (a webinar not lasting forever) measurably improves both show-up and registration
  rates (paraphrase, self-reported).

## Notable verbatim quotes
