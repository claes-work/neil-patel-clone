# Why B2B Creators Are The Next Big Thing - Neil Patel

## Summary
Following a conversation at the INBOUND conference with Wistia CEO Chris Savage and
Jay Clouse, Neil and Eric discuss the rise of B2B creators, noting B2C marketing
trends typically run a couple of years ahead of B2B. Neil's core caveat: B2B
creator marketing works well for brand awareness and low-end/mid-market revenue but
is weak for closing enterprise deals, which still run through RFPs, outbound sales,
and SDRs. The two disagree on where creator-driven audience growth stops paying off
(Neil: ~$10M; Eric: ~$100M).

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Argues B2B creators are highly effective for brand awareness and for
  low-end/mid-market revenue conversion, but explicitly NOT effective for closing
  enterprise revenue, which is driven by RFPs, outbound sales, and SDRs/BDRs
  instead; creator content still helps indirectly by making the brand recognizable
  when outbound teams reach out. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — content-marketing /
  agency-entrepreneurship.
- In a direct disagreement with Eric, estimates that a strong B2B creator audience
  helps a business grow from $0 to ~$10M revenue "super fast," but that the growth
  benefit "really slows down" above that mark — a lower ceiling than Eric's
  $100M estimate. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- States B2B content creators are most effective when focused on a specific
  vertical/niche; the common failure mode is going too broad or leaning on
  B2C-style content to chase audience size, which then fails to convert when they
  promote a B2B product/service because the audience isn't B2B-relevant.
  (Neil, 2023-11-02) — content-marketing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> B2B creators for enterprise just doesn't work too well... it just doesn't close
> as much revenue for enterprise.

> I don't even know if it's 100 million. I actually think the real honest answer is
> it helps you go from 0 to 10 million super fast. I think above 10 million, it
> really slows down on its benefit.

> The problem that most people face with B2B content creators is they go too broad
> and they try to leverage consumer base or B2C-based content just so they can get
> a massive audience.
