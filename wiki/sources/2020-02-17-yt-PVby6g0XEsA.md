# Can Social Media Still Bring You ANY Website Traffic in 2023

## Summary
Neil presents findings from a study of 483 companies across Facebook, YouTube,
Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Pinterest, focused on organic (not paid) social
traffic. (Title again says "2023"; see attribution note — content/publish metadata
place this as a 2020 video.) Headline finding: organic traffic per 100 followers fell
from ~3 visitors/month in 2015 to ~2/month now, a 38.6% drop. By channel, Pinterest
drives the most traffic per 100 followers, followed by LinkedIn and YouTube; Facebook
and Instagram are weakest (Instagram because links aren't clickable in posts). Posting
more content helps up to about 8 posts/month, after which returns diminish due to
engagement-driven algorithm distribution — Neil advises limiting volume to match
content quality rather than chasing frequency (contrasting himself with high-frequency
posters like "the Gary Vee's or the Grant Cardones of the world"). Instagram leads in
video views (inflated by autoplay), followed by LinkedIn and YouTube; YouTube views are
the most "real" since they require a deliberate click. Live video underperforms regular
video across channels. Revenue from organic social has declined over five years but is
flattening, which Neil attributes to omnichannel diversification rather than algorithms
alone.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-17, Neil said a study of 483 companies found organic social traffic per
  100 followers dropped from ~3 visitors/month in 2015 to ~2/month currently, a 38.6%
  decline.
- As of 2020-02-17, Neil said Pinterest generates the most organic traffic per 100
  followers, followed by LinkedIn and YouTube; Facebook and Instagram are weakest.
- As of 2020-02-17, Neil said the study found diminishing returns on organic reach after
  roughly 8 posts a month, and recommended matching posting volume to sustainable
  content quality rather than a fixed frequency.
- As of 2020-02-17, Neil said Instagram and Facebook video-view counts are inflated by
  autoplay, whereas YouTube views require a deliberate click and are therefore more
  meaningful.
- As of 2020-02-17, Neil said live video underperforms regular (non-live) video across
  channels, likely because non-live video can be better optimized for SEO/discovery.
- As of 2020-02-17, Neil said the multi-year decline in social-driven revenue is
  flattening, attributing this to businesses diversifying into omnichannel approaches
  rather than solely to worsening algorithms.
