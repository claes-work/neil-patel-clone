# 18 Link Building Strategies (Templates Included) - Module 3 - Lesson 2 - Content Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week-three lesson 2 of **Content Marketing Unlocked**
([[2020-08-10-yt-mbIt8Sd2N2w]]): a link-building deep dive. Opens with a Credit
Karma case study — "nearly one billion in unaudited revenue" for calendar 2019 (up
20% YoY), acquired by Intuit for $7.1B in stock and cash, with over 600,000
backlinks from ~17,000 referring domains as the traffic driver (third-party
figures, not verified here). Defines link building and walks the process: confirm
keywords/URLs → competitor backlink analysis (via NeilPatel.com/backlinks and
Ubersuggest) → build an outreach spreadsheet (using Whois/GoDaddy WHOIS and
Hunter.io for contacts) → outreach with tools like **Mailshake** (says "we use it
all the time, we love it"), Ninja Outreach, BuzzStream, or Pitchbox → follow-up →
repeat. Advises targeting "do-follow" links from in-content placements (not
sidebar/footer) on relevant, high-domain-score sites, and avoiding reciprocal-link
deals or paid links ("too risky... frowned upon"). Shares a subject-line tactic
("[Name], I think I'm in love with you") he says works well but is now overused
since "a lot of people are copying me on that one." States a realistic outreach
conversion benchmark of 5-10% of contacted sites linking back, and cites Moz
research that it takes roughly 10 weeks to see ranking improvement after starting
link building, with agency ramp-up of 4-16 weeks vs. in-house link building
requiring at least 4 weeks setup.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-08-13, Neil cited Credit Karma's ~$1B unaudited 2019 revenue (up 20%
  YoY), its $7.1B Intuit acquisition, and 600,000+ backlinks from ~17,000 referring
  domains as a case study of link building's revenue impact (third-party figures).
- As of 2020-08-13, Neil's realistic outreach-conversion benchmark: roughly 5-10% of
  emailed sites should link back.
- As of 2020-08-13, Neil cited Moz research that ranking improvements take roughly
  10 weeks to appear after link-building begins.
- As of 2020-08-13, Neil stated he/his team use Mailshake for scaled outreach
  ("we use it all the time, we love it") — a tool endorsement, not a paid
  placement disclosure in this transcript.
- As of 2020-08-13, Neil explicitly warned against paid links ("don't pay for
  links, it's too risky... don't break the rules") and reciprocal link-exchange
  deals.
