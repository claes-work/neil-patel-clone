# Brands add AI restrictions to contracts, Can Google detect AI content?, and Deepfake crypto scams

## Summary
Three segments. (1) Reacting to an Ad Age article: brands are increasingly adding contractual clauses banning agencies from using AI to create content/creative, driven by copyright-liability concerns (AI models may pull from copyrighted sources like Disney/Marvel) rather than quality concerns; framed as a rational, temporary "cover your ass" conservatism until AI/legal frameworks mature, plus an agency-pricing angle — clients should expect a discount if agencies are allowed to use AI and save on production cost. (2) A rebuttal to "Google killed AI content" claims: SEO practitioner Matt Diggity reported his purest AI-generated site had the *biggest* traffic gains in a March 2024 Google update. The stated position (attributed to Neil by direct NP Digital framing) is that Google isn't targeting AI vs. human authorship per se — it targets content *quality*, and most AI sites underperform simply because they skip human editing/E-E-A-T additions, not because they're AI. A parallel concern for large global brands: AI content copyright law varies by country, so multinational clients avoid AI content less for detection risk and more for jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction lawsuit exposure. (3) A deepfake warning: a fake AI livestream impersonating Michael Saylor on the Patrick Bet-David podcast asked viewers to scan a QR code to "double their Bitcoin" and ran uninterrupted for over an hour on a repurposed/renamed dormant YouTube account (~1.49M subs) — framed as an early sign public figures/celebrities need proof-of-identity verification (blockchain-based, in the host's speculative framing) and that platforms need scam-labeling mechanisms. Closing anecdote: a personal decision to buy a Tesla Model 3 (the cheapest trim) primarily for parking-assist/safety features relevant to being a self-described "terrible driver," LA's tight parallel parking, in-building free charging, and pricing — despite finding the seats/comfort inferior to a Mercedes.
