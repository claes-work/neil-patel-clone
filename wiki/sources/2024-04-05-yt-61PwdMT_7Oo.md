# Amazon's $8M risk, If you use AI content don't forget about DRIVE, ROI of 150 million shorts views, & more

## Summary
Three segments, undiarized. (1) Reacting to a viral text/tweet (shared by newsletter operator Matt Paulson) about a $40M-lifetime, $8M/year Amazon 1P/3P seller whose business was terminated after Amazon renegotiated a supplier directly (1P) at lower prices, wiping out the 3P seller's exclusivity overnight; framed as a platform-risk lesson ("don't build a business that relies on one big tech company's whims") and used to justify diversifying distribution beyond a single channel (their own YouTube channel is cited at 167K views in the trailing 30 days at the time). (2) Eric describes coining the "DRIVE" acronym (Data, Review, Insights, Visuals, Energy — for what AI-written content needs to perform) as an X/Twitter post that did unusually well (359 likes, 482 bookmarks, 36 comments, 60 reposts) despite the underlying concept not being new; the broader claim is that coining your own memorable terminology/acronyms (vs. reusing others' hook formulas) reliably outperforms on social, citing "skyscraper technique" and Google's "E-E-A-T" as decade-plus-lived examples, plus the durable speaking-fee/authority value of having "coined a term" (Sean Ellis/"growth hacking" cited). (3) A follow-up ROI check-in on a company paying agencies ~$45-50K/month for ~5,000 short-form pieces/month reaching 100M+ views: the CMO reportedly could not measure ROI/attribution from the volume despite anecdotal brand-awareness signals (unsolicited texts from industry contacts). Personal example: 10M combined views across Instagram/YouTube/TikTok in a trailing 28 days, framed as "dark social" with real but hard-to-measure ROI. Editorial-process note: the podcast targets ~2 weeks lead time (down from ~1 month) specifically to "trend-jack" — episodes/topics flagged as trending get moved up in the production queue since a 2-3 week delay can mean a topic has already cooled.

## Key claims
- **A ~$40M-lifetime ($8M/year) Amazon 3P seller was put out of business after Amazon renegotiated directly with the seller's supplier (1P) at a lower price, illustrating acute single-platform dependency risk.** `(third-party case study, dated 2024-04-05)`
- **Coining your own memorable acronyms/terminology for a concept tends to outperform reusing existing hook formulas on social content, independent of whether the term itself catches on broadly** — durable examples cited: "skyscraper technique," Google's "E-E-A-T," "growth hacking" (Sean Ellis). `(opinion, dated 2024-04-05; attribution: uncertain — undiarized, though the "DRIVE" example itself is Eric-attributed)`
- **A company paying ~$45-50K/month for ~5,000 short-form content pieces reaching 100M+ views could not measure attributable ROI after several months, despite anecdotal awareness lift.** `(third-party anecdote, dated 2024-04-05; attribution: uncertain)`
- **10M combined social views in a trailing 28 days (2.8M Instagram, 4.1M YouTube, ~4M TikTok), described as "dark social" ROI that is hard to track but believed to compound.** `(self-reported, dated 2024-04-05; attribution: uncertain — undiarized, but consistent with the hosts' shared content-distribution efforts)`
- **Editorial "trend-jacking" workflow: production lead time was intentionally compressed from ~1 month to ~2 weeks so trending topics can be flagged and moved up in the queue** (All-In Podcast cited as a comparison for current-events-driven formats). `(operational claim, dated 2024-04-05; attribution: uncertain)`
- **"Forget about the overproduced videos... just tell decent stories and you'll get good views"** — and genuinely novel (non-regurgitated) topics reportedly outperform, regardless of subject matter. `(opinion, dated 2024-04-05; attribution: uncertain)`
