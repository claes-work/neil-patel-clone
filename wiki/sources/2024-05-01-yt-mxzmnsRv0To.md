# Marketing takeaways from Andrew Huberman, Dr. Peter Attia, Bryan Johnson, and more

## Summary
A recap of a YPO (Young Presidents' Organization) conference both hosts attended (described as
one of the best YPO events either had been to, ~400 attendees with a 600-person waitlist),
featuring speakers Andrew Huberman, Dr. Peter Attia, Jim Kwik, Dr. Mark Hyman, and others.
Marketing takeaways: Andrew Huberman reportedly spends 12-100 hours preparing for a podcast
episode (solo vs. co-hosted) and 3-11 hours recording, explaining his content's high perceived
quality. Jim Kwik is described using a subtle repeated self-promotion technique on stage —
"98% value, 2% pitch," repeatedly anchoring back to his book *Limitless* and his podcast — framed
as an effective, low-friction way to convert a portion of an audience over time. Brian Johnson
(known for extreme longevity/anti-aging practices, including a father-son-plasma-transfusion
experiment) is discussed, along with a claim that public hate/criticism motivates rather than
discourages him. A broader observation: most of these figures (Huberman, Attia, Johnson, Kwik,
Jay Shetty, author Sara Gottfried) are functionally content creators — some monetize purely via
ads (Huberman), while the more profitable path is described as building owned
products/services/courses (Kwik's courses/coaching, Mark Hyman's "Function Health" biomarker
SaaS). A closing point on information reliability: even reputable sources contradict each other
(e.g., competing dietary advice), and a self-critical example is given — a claim about crawling
"5 million YouTube posts" for a stats project is flagged as a meaningful but non-exhaustive sample
relative to daily global content volume, illustrating that no single data source is "the end all
be all." Health/longevity asides (marked non-core to the marketing focus): chronotype-based work
scheduling (a sleep-doctor anecdote about shifting an employee's hours from 9-5 to 11-7 based on
"bear" vs. "lion" chronotype, said to be in active internal testing with a remote employee),
removing visible clocks to reduce sleep anxiety, an ACE (adverse childhood experiences) score
discussion, and VO2 max / grip strength as the two most emphasized longevity biomarkers discussed
at the event (Brian Johnson's VO2 max cited as ~58, described as near-Olympic level).
