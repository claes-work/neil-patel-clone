# How Inbound Marketing Is Rapidly Changing

## Summary
Co-hosted Marketing School segment (main-channel republish, "[Neil]"-tagged outro). Opening framing:
every marketing channel — SEO, social, email, paid, even TV — gets harder to produce the same ROI
from as it becomes more competitive; that's normal, not a sign inbound is dying. Restates his
channel-agnostic ROI test ("you spend $1, whether in ad money or in time, can you make more?") as the
only distinction that matters to him between inbound and outbound. Names three real shifts he sees
happening: (1) companies doing multichannel when they should be doing true omnichannel — congruent
cross-channel experiences that lower CAC — citing Nike and Disney as strong examples and Timberland's
use of near-field technology for in-store experience; (2) undifferentiated companies copying
competitors' channel mix getting poor ROI, versus creative plays working — cites giving an influencer
(Ryan Reynolds) equity and founding-team status rather than a standard paid endorsement, referencing
the outcome at Mint Mobile/MNTN as a strong example of standing out in a crowded market; (3) AI
adoption making companies more efficient, which compounds into better inbound ROI via margin
efficiency. Also flags conversion-rate optimization as an unglamorous but high-leverage lever that
most companies underinvest in relative to its ROI impact ("every time we talk about it or I blog
about it, I've seen very few people want to read about it"). Eric adds that "inbound" as a
five-to-ten-years-ago concept (content + SEO) is too narrow now and should be understood to include
wherever organic reach is currently strong (calls out LinkedIn, Twitter, and Shorts specifically as
of this recording).
