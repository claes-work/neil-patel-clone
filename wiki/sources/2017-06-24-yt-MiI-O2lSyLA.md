# How to Make A Good LinkedIn Profile For Sourcing Leads: The Ultimate Business Marketing Strategy

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out a five-step tactical playbook for generating B2B leads through LinkedIn, which he frames as the single best social network for B2B. The steps are: (1) share existing blog content on LinkedIn, teasing the first few paragraphs with a "click to continue reading" prompt to drive traffic back to your website; (2) fully and specifically complete your LinkedIn profile so interested prospects understand what you offer and reach out; (3) use the paid LinkedIn InMail feature to contact members you are not connected with; (4) accumulate skill endorsements and recommendations as social proof; and (5) apply for and deploy LinkedIn AutoFill on your website's lead forms to reduce form-fill friction and lift conversions. Throughout, Neil ties each tactic back to the underlying logic that more LinkedIn-sourced traffic and lower friction translate into more leads.

## Key claims
- LinkedIn is one of the most popular social networks and, for those in the B2B space, the best social network available because it can drive a lot of leads (as of 2017-06-24).
- Sharing existing blog content on LinkedIn — posting the first few paragraphs of old posts with a "click to continue reading" link at the bottom — drives people from your LinkedIn profile back to your website, and more LinkedIn-to-website traffic means more leads generated (as of 2017-06-24).
- Completely filling out your LinkedIn profile, as specifically as possible about where you have worked and what you do, makes interested prospects more likely to contact you; a vague skill set means no one contacts you to hire you (as of 2017-06-24).
- LinkedIn InMail is a paid feature costing roughly $79 a month that lets you contact other LinkedIn members even ones you are not connected with, and this alone can generate leads (as of 2017-06-24).
- ★ Almost all B2B companies' sales teams generate the majority of their leads and sales — especially in outbound sales — through LinkedIn InMail, which Neil calls a good feature for "pennies on the dollar" (as of 2017-06-24).
- Getting more skill endorsements and recommendations acts as social proof (other people vouching for you), and the more you have, the more likely you are to get leads from LinkedIn (as of 2017-06-24).
- ★ LinkedIn AutoFill — a feature you must apply/email LinkedIn for, not available to everyone — lets a logged-in LinkedIn user click a button on your website's lead form to auto-populate fields like name, email, company address, company size, and telephone number, reducing friction and boosting conversions and leads (as of 2017-06-24).
