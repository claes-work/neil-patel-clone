# Why I Decided To Disrupt the SEO Industry | My Marketing Plans for 2020

## Summary

Neil explains why he gave away Ubersuggest for free and where he's taking it. He says he
isn't doing it primarily to make money but because the SEO industry — while full of smart
competitors with good products — **hasn't innovated**, and he wants to bring machine
learning and AI to it. His 2020 vision is to **automate SEO**: have you install a simple
JavaScript snippet (like Google Analytics) that lets him, with your approval, adjust your
HTML/title tags, read your Search Console data to suggest and split-test on-page changes,
auto-find competitors' backlinks and run templated outreach, and hit up people you link
out to for shares — all automated. He draws an analogy to **Uber** (mentions meeting
ex-CEO **Ryan Graves** early on, disclaiming he knows him well) evolving from black-car
dispatch to UberEats, self-driving, drones, and flying cars, arguing SEO — older than Uber
— is still done manually and should be automated. He concedes it won't be easy and he
doesn't know how far he'll get, but he'd rather try than give up, and believes most of
marketing is heading toward automation.
