# Should You Use Sliders on Your Website? Try THIS Online Marketing Technique

## Summary
Neil argues that website sliders — which he frames as a "less aggressive version of a pop-up" — do work and are worth using, but only when they are relevant to the page the visitor is on and when the messaging stays congruent from slider to landing page. He recommends making the slider's message match the page content, keeping it clean and simple, and driving clicks to an appropriate landing page (he says his own Neil Patel blog slider carries an online-marketing message and links to his online-marketing homepage). He notes sliders work best combined with other on-site elements (pop-ups, modals, exit takeovers, alarm bells) but warns these elements cannibalize each other: a slider adding 5%, a pop-up adding 5%, and an alarm bell adding 5% won't stack to 15% — combined you might see roughly 12%, which is still a net gain. He cites timothysykes.com as an example of a relevant slider (promoting the "millionaire challenge" course to a course-interested audience) and points to hellobar.com as a free tool to deploy all these elements.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-01, Neil states:
- Sliders are a less aggressive version of a pop-up and they do work — "the reason you see them is because they work" (as of 2017-08-01).
- Sliders only perform well if they are relevant to the page they appear on; irrelevance kills performance (as of 2017-08-01).
- ★ Message congruence end-to-end (slider message → click destination → landing page) is what drives conversions; incongruent messaging both fails to convert and annoys readers (as of 2017-08-01).
- Sliders work well alongside other elements — pop-ups, modals, exit takeovers, alarm bells — all of which can be run for free via hellobar.com (as of 2017-08-01).
- ★ On-site conversion elements cannibalize each other: individually a slider, pop-up, and alarm bell might each add ~5% conversion lift, but combining them yields roughly 12% rather than a stacked 15% — still better than baseline (illustrative self-reported figures, as of 2017-08-01).
- timothysykes.com uses a relevant slider promoting his "millionaire challenge" to a course-interested blog audience (as of 2017-08-01).
- Neil's own Neil Patel blog slider carries an online-marketing message and clicks through to his online-marketing homepage, keeping messaging congruent (self-reported, as of 2017-08-01).
