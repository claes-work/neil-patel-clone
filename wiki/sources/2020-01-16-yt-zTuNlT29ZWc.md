# How to Do an SEO Audit In Under 30 Minutes And Discover Hidden Opportunities to Rank #1

## Summary
Solo tactical video compressing an SEO audit into two steps and 30 minutes. **Step 1 —
Google Search Console.** (a) Find **pages that rank for similar terms** and combine them:
creating many pages on one topic makes them compete and confuses Google about which to
rank; consolidate into a **pillar page with clusters**, using **Moz's Beginner's Guide to
SEO** (which he says ranks #1 in the US for "SEO") as the structural model — an
introductory hub linking down into sections that also rank for their niche terms. (b)
Find **pages with high impressions but low CTR** — they rank, but nobody clicks because of
the title tag or meta description — and A/B test the titles with **ClickFlow**, which also
suggests improvements so you don't have to invent them. (c) Take the pages that are
already **popular** and run **land and expand**: pages that already rank respond to being
expanded, whereas pouring effort into a page that ranks nowhere has "slim to none" odds.
His procedure is to feed the page's existing keywords into **Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas
report**, take only the long-tail variations genuinely related to the article, and expand
the article to cover them — with the same user-first gate as the 2020-01-12 video: "don't
just put the keywords in there for Google." **Step 2 — the Ubersuggest site-audit
report.** A URL crawl takes ~3 minutes over hundreds of pages and reports the technical
layer (duplicate meta descriptions and title tags, titles too short/long, wrong headings,
broken links, internal links, sitemaps, URL structure, "another hundred plus factors"),
page by page. The key discipline is not to be overwhelmed: the report **orders fixes by
impact and ease of implementation**, so work top-down. It also reports **site speed**;
faster loading ranks better, and since **more than 50% of Google searches are on mobile**
— where reception, not 5G/LTE, is the real constraint — speed matters.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "A lot of people over-complicate SEO audits. Yes, you can go crazy by fine tuning your website almost to perfection. But look, there's a lot of little overlooked things and if you did them, you can get huge gains without much effort."

> "You don't want to create a ton of pages on the same exact topic because they are going to compete and that's going to conflict with Google quick, which page do I rank about Dogs?"

> "If something already ranks really well, the chances are if you expand upon it and you put more meat behind it and you make it even better, it should in theory even do better."

> "But if a page doesn't rank well at all and you put a ton of emphasis and effort behind it, the chance of it doing really well in the future are slim to none."

> "Don't just put the keywords in there for Google. Do what's best for the user."

> "Instead of being like, oh well, this is overwhelming, there's so many things to fix… start with those, because it will be at the top of the recommendations and then work your way down the list."

> "More than 50% of people who search Google are doing so on a mobile device. If they're on 5G, LTE, it doesn't matter. They may be in a spot that doesn't have the best reception. So speed matters."
