# Social Media Influencer - Is Instafame REALLY Achievable?

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode with co-host **Adam (from Viewership)**, answering a viewer
(Sorup) — a 16-year-old asking how to become a social-media influencer. Neil's core
argument: **don't waste your time trying to be an influencer**; the money is in building
a business that solves a problem. He contrasts the small ceiling of even top influencers
(he cites the Paul brothers, Logan and Jake, reportedly making "around a million a
month," at the "0.001%") with large, unglamorous B2B companies (he names ServiceNow) that
few have heard of yet are worth billions. He argues you don't have to be talented or
famous to make over a million dollars a year in business, but you DO have to be
exceptional to monetize as an influencer. If you still want to be an influencer, his
tactical advice: do something controversial or get on a platform early, lean on
storytelling and humor, and **pick ONE platform**, grow it, then repurpose content to
promote the others. He also reframes: make your *business* the influencer, or become
known as a business person (as he has). Adam's own two businesses (an 8-year-old dating
site vs. an 8-month-old "Viewership" YouTube-marketing company) are used as a live example
that a much newer, lower-traffic business can already match an older one in revenue —
reinforcing "you don't have to be popular to do well in business."

## Key claims

- As of 2018-05-31, Neil advises aspiring influencers (esp. young ones) NOT to chase
  influencer status: "it's not worth the money"; spend the energy building a business that
  solves a problem or pain point instead (self-reported opinion).
- As of 2018-05-31, Neil claims very few social-media influencers make over $100M or are
  worth that much; even top creators (he cites Logan/Jake Paul, "around a million a month,"
  "0.001%") hit a ceiling far below what businesses reach.
- As of 2018-05-31, Neil claims you don't need talent or fame to make over $1M/year in
  business ("there's a lot of shitty entrepreneurs... that make over a million a year"),
  but influencer success requires being the "cream of the crop."
- As of 2018-05-31, Neil says people who get famous usually either got on a platform early
  OR did something controversial (he cites the Kardashians' origin), and that being famous
  "for no apparent reason" is very unlikely.
- As of 2018-05-31, Neil's platform tactic: master ONE platform (Instagram, Facebook, OR
  YouTube), then repurpose and cross-promote to the others — "it's too hard to be famous on
  all of them."
- As of 2018-05-31, Neil suggests making your business (or yourself as a business person)
  the influencer, so you're both an owner and an influencer.
