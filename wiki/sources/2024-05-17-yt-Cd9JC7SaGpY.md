# Alex Hormozi's content shift, Is 'giving value' overrated?, & Underrated vs overrated marketers (Marketing School)

## Summary
Three topics on this co-hosted Marketing School episode: (1) Alex Hormozi is narrowing/lengthening his content to stop chasing broad-TAM views that don't convert; (2) Nick Shackleford's claim that "giving value is overrated," and whether strategy/concepts beat step-by-step tactics for generating leads; (3) Brian Halligan's line that analytical skills are overrated and "taste" is underrated. Attribution splits heavily toward **Eric Siu** here — the Hormozi-content-shift reporting, the interview-cutting decision, and (critically) the entire "giving value is overrated / strategy beats tactical how-to" argument are all Eric's, even though the SEO-presentation anecdote inside it sounds like classic Neil. **Neil's** own contributions are narrower but clear: interview podcasts pay off mainly when leveraged to interview your ideal customers (CMOs) and win business; analytical skills are a necessity, not sufficient, and most marketers are actually bad at analytics; what's genuinely underrated is creativity/thinking-within-constraints; and "taste" is really about *coming up with* what's good, not just spotting it. A lot of the middle of the episode is banter (M&A ops, sleep/sickness, Bryan Johnson) with several garbled names.

## Key claims
- Interview podcasts work best when leveraged for customer acquisition: NP Digital's country leaders interview CMOs at large corporations that are their ideal customers, build relationships, and win business from it. (Neil, 2024-05-17)
- Pushing back on Brian Halligan's framing: analytical skills are NOT overrated, because most marketers actually have terrible analytical skills; strong analytics is a *necessity* in marketing but is not enough on its own. (Neil, 2024-05-17)
- What is genuinely underrated is creativity and thinking outside the box — hitting a business's goals within its real budget and marketing constraints. (Neil, 2024-05-17)
- Reframes "taste" as creativity/creation: spotting what's good or bad marketing isn't the hard part; *coming up with* what's good is the hard part, and that skill will stay in demand. (Neil, 2024-05-17)
- Sharing your strategy / how you think (rather than only step-by-step execution) teaches people to think differently, and that's why they remember you. (Neil, 2024-05-17) — Neil affirming the episode's Shackleford/Siu theme.
- Ops reality of scaling a global company (NP Digital, incl. India teams): you get up around 4am, work "the randomest hours," and the big fires become global — and it gets worse, not better, as you grow. (Neil, 2024-05-17)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> So I see interview podcasts work the best if you leverage it to get customers. So for example, in different countries some of our leaders interview CMOs on a podcast... you interview CMOs like at really big corporations that [are] your ideal customers, you build good relationships and you actually get business.

> I don't think good analytical skills is overrated, because I see most marketers having terrible analytical skills. But I don't think having good analytical skills is enough — I think that's just a necessity in marketing. ... What I feel is underrated is creativity and thinking outside the box. Everyone has budget constraints, everyone has marketing constraints — how can you achieve the goals that a business needs or wants within their constraints?

> I don't think it's hard to spot what's good or bad — I think it's hard to come up with what's good.

> Usually [I] get up at four. Dude, that's [one] problem of having a company where you're global — you work the randomest hours, and it doesn't get better, it actually gets worse.

## Eric Siu (co-host, context — NOT persona data)
[[wiki/entities/eric-siu]]
- Alex Hormozi texted Eric (April 19) praising his X content; Eric replied that his chart/data posts get the least views but the best B2B leads, and that a stat Eric posted prompted Hormozi to say he switched his own strategy toward deep/B2B content. (Eric, 2024-05-17)
- Hormozi is shifting away from broad-TAM content (e.g. a "protein bomb" video, a relationships video) that gets lots of views but doesn't drive customers or applications for acquisition.com — going narrower and more focused. (Eric, reporting Hormozi, 2024-05-17)
- Eric is cutting his own interviews to ~20-30% (not eliminating them), inspired by Huberman-style solo research/experiment content, and upgrading his home studio setup ("Brad from cord edit podcast.com" — name garbled). (Eric, 2024-05-17)
- Peep Laja (CXL) is also dropping interviews; Tim Ferriss said on My First Million that interview podcasts are now a "red ocean" — everyone books the same guests and asks the same questions, so it's no longer interesting. (Eric, citing others, 2024-05-17)
- A prospective partner told Eric he was "a lot more legit than I thought" because his YouTube was "just interviews" — which pushed Eric to change his format. (Eric, 2024-05-17)
- Eric now declines guests he isn't personally curious about (e.g. a VC's portfolio company doing ~$15M, +60% YoY) rather than book them on the Leveling Up podcast. (Eric, 2024-05-17)
- **The full "giving value is overrated" argument is Eric's:** he read Nick Shackleford's tweet, then elaborated that his own tactical step-by-step presentations ("10 ways to boost your SEO rankings," Google Search Console screenshots, redirects) generated almost no customers ("you overgave"), whereas sharing strategy / trend-data / concepts — which he calls "way less value" — generated far more leads and customers, because step-by-step bores people while a strategic reframe gives a memorable "dopamine hit." (Eric, 2024-05-17) — see Attribution notes; this is NOT Neil.
- Interpreting Halligan's "taste": if forced to choose he'd pick the higher-taste person; taste is very hard to screen for and will stay in demand because AI models (trained on data) still need people with taste to generate new ideas. (Eric, 2024-05-17)
- Read the Brian Halligan (HubSpot co-founder) line: overrated = marketers with good analytical skills; underrated = marketers with good taste. (Eric, 2024-05-17)
- Single Grain ad read (Eric's agency): programmatic CRO, auto-updating SEO, and AI-based visitor enrichment / custom messaging. (Eric, 2024-05-17)
