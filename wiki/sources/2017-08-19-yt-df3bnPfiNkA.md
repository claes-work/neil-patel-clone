# How to Increase Your Search Traffic Using Google Search Console

## Summary
A short SEO tactical video in which Neil Patel gives a four-step workflow for mining
Google Search Console to grow search traffic on existing pages. (1) In Search Console,
open Search Analytics → Pages to see your pages ranked by search traffic, pick a page to
grow, then click Queries to get the list of keywords already sending it traffic. (2) Go
into that page and naturally integrate those keywords into the content — don't keyword-
stuff; also make the content more thorough and detailed. (3) Add the main keywords to the
page's meta description and title tag so Google understands the focus keywords. (4)
Resubmit the URL in Google Search Console to prompt a recrawl of the updated page. He says
doing this across your popular pages should yield a search-traffic increase within one to
two weeks, and warns that unnatural keyword stuffing makes visitors bounce, which hurts
you.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-19, Neil states:
- Step 1 — In Google Search Console, open Search Analytics → Pages for a list of pages
  ranked most-to-least by search traffic; select a page to grow, then click Queries to
  see all keywords driving traffic to that page (as of 2017-08-19).
- Step 2 — Integrate those keywords naturally into the page's content (only ones not
  already present), and make the content more thorough and detailed; adding relevant
  keywords should increase search traffic (as of 2017-08-19).
- Keyword stuffing backfires: if the integration isn't natural, visitors see it, bounce
  off the page, and it hurts your rankings (as of 2017-08-19).
- Step 3 — Add at least the main keywords to the page's meta description and title text so
  Google knows the keywords you're focusing on (as of 2017-08-19).
- Step 4 — Resubmit the page's URL in Google Search Console to tell Google to recrawl the
  updated page and refresh the search results (as of 2017-08-19).
- Doing this across your popular pages should produce a search-traffic increase within one
  to two weeks at the latest (as of 2017-08-19).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Click on Pages. When you click on Pages, you get a list of all the pages that are driving you search traffic ... in order from the most popular to the least popular."

> "You can't just stuff your article or your webpage with these new keywords that you're not already including within your page ... You got to also update your content and make it more thorough, more detailed, and naturally blend in those new keywords."

> "You then want to take that page and submit it within Google Search Console ... that tells them, 'Hey, crawl this page because it's updated.'"

> "Once you do that, within a week or two weeks at the latest, you should see an increase in search traffic."
