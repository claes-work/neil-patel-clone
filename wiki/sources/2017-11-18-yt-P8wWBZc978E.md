# 6 Tips to Writing an Amazing Blog Post That Drives a TON of Traffic to Your Website

## Summary
Neil Patel gives six traits of a great blog post, opening with his recurring self-deprecating note that bad grammar and spelling ("like me") is no excuse not to write. (1) A conversational tone using "you" and "I" to create connection. (2) A strong headline — 8 of 10 people read the headline but only 2 of 10 read on, so use BuzzSumo to see what's popular in your space. (3) Educational, step-by-step content that leaves the reader knowing what to do next (vague "10 ways" listicles that don't explain how are bad). (4) Trendy, up-to-date material — don't regurgitate ten-year-old information. (5) Multiple media formats — images, infographics, podcasts, videos, audio — since not everyone is a text reader. (6) Cite your sources, which builds reputability and shows you did your homework; he frames this within his "want to be the dumbest person in the room" learning ethos.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-18, Neil states:
- You can write great content even with terrible grammar and spelling ("like me") — if he can, anyone can (self-reported, as of 2017-11-18).
- Tip 1 — a conversational tone using "you" and "I" creates a connection with the reader and makes content come out better (as of 2017-11-18).
- Tip 2 — the headline is critical: 8 out of 10 people read the headline but only 2 out of 10 click through to read the rest; use BuzzSumo (which aggregates the main social networks) to find popular headlines/topics, or check Reddit and Twitter (as of 2017-11-18).
- Tip 3 — educational content that teaches step by step and leaves the reader knowing what to do is amazing; a listicle that names "10 ways" but doesn't explain how leaves readers lost and is bad (as of 2017-11-18).
- Tip 4 — write trendy, up-to-date content; people don't want regurgitated information, and content that was great ten years ago no longer serves an audience that has moved on (as of 2017-11-18).
- Tip 5 — leverage different media formats (images, infographics, podcasts, videos, audio clips) because not everyone is a text-based reader (as of 2017-11-18).
- Tip 6 — cite your sources by linking to others who did the research; it makes you more reputable and shows you did your homework; nobody knows everything, and it's fine to use Google to research what you don't know (as of 2017-11-18).
