# The Future of Digital Marketing Will Surprise You

## Summary
This is a recorded conference/live-event talk (co-hosted with Marketing School podcast partner Eric Siu) in which Neil Patel lays out his forward-looking worldview on where digital marketing is heading. Framing his authority through NP Digital's work with clients ranging from Fortune 100 firms to mom-and-pop shops over roughly 16-17 years — and his unusual position of "seeing every side of the table" as entrepreneur, marketer, and paid speaker for platforms like Google and Facebook — Neil argues that the market is getting saturated and more competitive, and that surviving future algorithm and platform updates requires specific structural shifts. His central predictions: (1) marketing is going omnichannel out of necessity because no business can grow on a single channel anymore, and going omnichannel drives down cost-per-acquisition across all channels (he cites an Expedia test where CPAs fell by double-digit percentages); (2) user metrics/engagement signals (comments, dwell time, CTR) are the key to beating Google and going viral, more so than links; (3) branding is the future — brand queries and brand signals are how Google and Facebook sort quality from the "cesspool," and Neil credits his Ubersuggest acquisition with generating 3x the brand traffic of his own name; (4) conventional CRO tactics (popups, quizzes) are decaying and personalization is "the new conversion rate optimization," with chat and product tours driving real lifts; and (5) to win you must think like a winner and invest in mentorship. Throughout, Neil hammers his core formula — traffic plus conversions cubed equals success — using his failed Kissmetrics startup (which out-trafficked competitor Mixpanel yet was worth a fraction of Mixpanel's $865M valuation) as proof that traffic alone is worthless without conversion optimization. He also weaves in tactical asides on translations as the biggest untapped traffic opportunity, updating old content over creating new, and CRO being the least glamorous but highest-ROI channel. The talk was recorded around the COVID period, with recession-era predictions about ad spend behavior.

## Key claims
- Marketing is going omnichannel, and businesses no longer have a choice but to leverage all channels because each channel is saturating fast; you can no longer grow a business off a single channel the way Facebook grew via referrals or Dropbox grew via social sharing (as of 2021-06-28).
- Going omnichannel lowers cost-per-acquisition and cost-per-click across all channels; Expedia ran a test adding TV, radio, and PPC and saw their PPC CPCs and CPAs drop by double-digit percentages (as of 2021-06-28).
- There are at least a billion blogs on the web against roughly 7 billion people — about one blog per seven people — so the world does not need more content; the opportunity is in updating and re-optimizing existing content, not creating more (as of 2021-06-28).
- Translations are the single biggest untapped traffic opportunity because up-and-coming markets like Brazil and India have money and low competition, and Google needs content in those regions (as of 2021-06-28).
- Conversion rate optimization is the least "sexy" channel but delivered the biggest ROI for the smallest investment across the hundreds of companies NP Digital surveyed (as of 2021-06-28).
- Paid advertising converts better than SEO largely because of bottom-of-funnel intent and dedicated landing pages; with PPC you can send traffic to a bare squeeze/landing page, whereas an SEO page must carry substantial content (e.g., ~1,000 words) to rank (as of 2021-06-28).
- Google does not penalize duplicate content across platforms, so marketers should publish once and republish everywhere (Medium, Tumblr, Facebook, YouTube, LinkedIn, Twitter) and re-upload the same video to every platform that accepts video (as of 2021-06-28).
- Google's revenue rose during the 2008 recession, and Neil predicts it would rise again in a future recession because advertisers keep spending on performance ads as long as a dollar in returns more than a dollar out, regardless of market conditions; ad dollars shift from traditional to performance-based marketing during recessions (as of 2021-06-28).
- User metrics are the key to beating Google; you don't have to raise rankings to get more search traffic — optimizing title tags, content, and meta descriptions to lift CTR on pages with low click-through (targeting keywords under ~5% click rate and pages under ~4% CTR) drives search gains, and this is where NP Digital sees its biggest current SEO wins (as of 2021-06-28).
- Words like "how to," numbered lists, "free," "you," "tips," "tricks," "best," and "why" have tested as reliable CTR boosters in titles (as of 2021-06-28).
- Comments are the single strongest engagement signal for going viral on Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn — more than time or shares — and legitimate comment activity from ~50 friends can push a post viral; platforms have since started detecting explicit "leave a comment" asks and dampening that tactic (as of 2021-06-28).
- Branding is the future: brands are how Google and Facebook sort quality content from the "cesspool" and combat fake news via brand signals, so building a leading brand within your niche makes your content rank and go viral more than competitors' (as of 2021-06-28).
- Brand queries skyrocket rankings; Neil's Ubersuggest acquisition (bought for ~$120,000 from an owner in Italy) now generates over 300,000 visitors a month from brand searches — about 3x the ~100,000+/month he gets from people searching "Neil Patel" — and growing brand searches lifts his rankings for terms like "online marketing" without new link building (as of 2021-06-28).
- Building a brand runs on the "rule of 7" — getting people back to your site repeatedly via email marketing, push notifications, and Messenger bots (as of 2021-06-28).
- Conventional CRO tactics (popups, interactive quizzes) still work but are decaying because they are now everywhere; personalization is "the new conversion rate optimization" (as of 2021-06-28).
- Personalized chat drives major conversion lifts; chat is responsible for 28% of NP Digital's sales and roughly a third of sales at Nextiva, and it is starting to help e-commerce too (as of 2021-06-28).
- Product tours (as used inside Google and Gmail, and animated elements on checkout pages and lead forms) lifted conversions by 13% in NP Digital's testing; stacking many small optimizations, rather than one big win, is what lets you afford rising ad costs (as of 2021-06-28).
- Traffic is not everything: Kissmetrics (raised ~$17M) out-trafficked competitor Mixpanel roughly 3-to-1, yet Mixpanel raised at an $865M valuation while Kissmetrics was worth roughly one-fifteenth to one-twentieth of that — proving traffic without conversions has little value (as of 2021-06-28).
- Neil's success formula is "traffic plus conversions cubed equals success," and he predicts that within about 12 months many current marketing techniques will stop working unless marketers focus on conversions (as of 2021-06-28).
- Most startup funding today goes to sales and marketing (growth) rather than to product and engineering as it did historically (as of 2021-06-28).
- Rising ad prices on Google and Facebook are driven by supply and demand as more competitors pile on, not arbitrary increases, so CRO is what lets you keep competing (as of 2021-06-28).
- To win you must think like a winner and invest in mentorship — which need not be paid (free events, podcasts, articles) — and continually test because marketing never stops and what works today may not work tomorrow (as of 2021-06-28).
- Reid Hoffman's "Blitzscaling" concept — spending at all costs to generate revenue even while unprofitable — screws up margins for most companies, reinforcing the need to focus on conversions (as of 2021-06-28).
