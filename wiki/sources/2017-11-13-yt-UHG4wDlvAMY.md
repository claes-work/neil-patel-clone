# 4 Local SEO Marketing Strategies to Build Your Brick And Mortar Business

## Summary
Neil Patel walks through how a localized brick-and-mortar business can rank on Google for free instead of paying for AdWords. Steps: set up Google My Business (company name, address, phone) so you can appear in the localized "pack"; gather Google reviews (not just Yelp) because more/better reviews rank you higher; put your business name, address, and phone number (NAP) on your contact page so you surface for localized queries; if on WordPress, use the Yoast SEO plugin to optimize without technical skill (otherwise manually set title tags, meta descriptions, and on-page keywords); create topical content (e.g., a pizza shop writing about making pizza, flavors, pairings) to rank for local terms; and sign up for Google Search Console to find site errors, submit an XML sitemap, and specify which region you target.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-13, Neil states:
- A localized business can rank on Google for free — you don't need to keep spending hundreds/thousands on Google AdWords (though you can if you want) (as of 2017-11-13).
- Set up Google My Business (company name, address, phone number); once listed you can rank in the localized "pack" that appears for queries like "pizza restaurant" on mobile, which drives more business (as of 2017-11-13).
- Get reviews — the more people who say you're amazing / have a good product, the higher you rank; ask in-store ("rate us on Google, leave a review") and many will (as of 2017-11-13).
- Don't focus only on Yelp reviews (nothing wrong with Yelp) — also focus on Google reviews because they help you rank higher (as of 2017-11-13).
- Include your business name, address, and phone number on your contact page so you're more likely to appear for localized queries (as of 2017-11-13).
- If on WordPress, use the Yoast SEO plugin to optimize most of what's needed without being technical — no need to pay an agency thousands (as of 2017-11-13).
- If not on WordPress, manually adjust title tags and meta descriptions to include your keywords, and include the target keywords in the text/pages, or Google won't know what to rank you for (as of 2017-11-13).
- Create more content around your niche (e.g., a pizza shop writing about making/cooking pizza, flavors, wine pairings) to rank for pizza terms within your city (as of 2017-11-13).
- Sign up for Google Search Console to find issues/errors Google has with your site, submit your sitemap, and (for national/multi-location businesses) specify the region you target (as of 2017-11-13).
- If you lack a sitemap, create an XML sitemap using free tools found via Google (as of 2017-11-13).
