# How to Build an Audience (Even if You're a Nobody)

## Summary

Solo tactical. Neil gives three tactics for building an audience from zero. **(1) 1,000
True Fans** (credited to Kevin Kelly): whatever your niche, there's an active community
(he names Moz, WordStream, neilpatel.com, Facebook groups, GrowthHackers) — find them and
**engage one-on-one**, genuinely, not just broadcast. Those first thousand die-hard fans
become your first thousand visitors/subscribers/customers and, crucially, they **share and
evangelize**, which is what carries you to 10,000 and then 100,000. He anchors this in his
own origin: on his first blog he personally replied to every comment, email, and tweet to
help people for free — deliberately building his first thousand fans through one-on-one
engagement. **(2) Guest post where the super-fans already read** — a handful of dominant
industry publications (he names Backlinko, Moz, HubSpot) rather than hundreds of sites;
appearing on the few sites your audience trusts makes them feel you're "everywhere" and
remembers your name with far less effort. **(3) Get influencers to share via a give-first
loop**: cite and link to thought leaders in your content, then email them ("I'm a huge fan,
I linked to you, feel free to check it out — P.S. share if you enjoyed it"); when the
influencers in your space talk about you, their audiences follow. He closes on the payoff:
a loyal, engaged base that's waiting for your content — and eventually buys from you.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-21, audience-building tactic #1 is Kevin Kelly's **1,000 True Fans**: find
  your niche's active community and engage one-on-one, genuinely; those first 1,000 fans
  become your first visitors/subscribers/customers and evangelize you to 10k then 100k.
- As of 2018-08-21, Neil recounts building his first blog's audience by personally
  responding to every comment, email, and tweet to help people for free (origin tactic).
- As of 2018-08-21, tactic #2: **guest post only on the handful of top industry
  publications** your super-fans actually read (he names Backlinko, Moz, HubSpot) — far
  less work than mass guest posting, and it makes you seem omnipresent.
- As of 2018-08-21, tactic #3: **link out to/cite influencers**, then email them; when
  thought leaders in your space share your content, their audiences follow — a give-first
  outreach loop.
- As of 2018-08-21, he stresses the engagement must be sincere ("if you don't truly care
  for people they're gonna see through it") or it won't build a loyal following.
