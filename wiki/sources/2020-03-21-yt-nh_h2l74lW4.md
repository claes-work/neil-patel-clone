# How Does Google Make Money? Google Business Model Explained

## Summary
An explainer (not a marketing-tactics video) where Neil breaks down Alphabet/Google's
business model. He says Alphabet got about 85% of its revenue from advertising in 2018
(~$116B per Statista.com) despite owning many non-Google businesses. He frames Google's
model around three parties: users (value = "find the answer to anything," free, funded
by ads), businesses (value = more sales via targeted ads — search ads on a cost-per-click
basis, ranging "a few cents" to over $50/click for competitive terms like auto insurance
or loans; and display ads across Gmail, YouTube, Google Maps, AdSense partner sites and
apps, contextually matched), and publishers (value = monetization via AdSense on their
own content/traffic). He notes Google's "other bets" revenue grew from $0.8B (3.2% of
revenue) in 2009 to $20.5B (15%+ of revenue) in 2018, and cites Sensor Tower that the
Google Play app store grew revenue 27.3% year-over-year. Frames Google's growth as
mutually reinforcing for advertisers/publishers: more Google usage → more ecosystem
traffic → more opportunity for businesses via paid and organic channels.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-03-21, Neil said Alphabet earned roughly 85% of its 2018 revenue
  (~$116 billion) from advertising, citing Statista.com.
- As of 2020-03-21, Neil described Google search ads as cost-per-click, with click
  prices ranging from a few cents up to over $50 for highly competitive terms such as
  auto insurance or loans.
- As of 2020-03-21, Neil said Google display ads run across Gmail, YouTube, Google
  Maps, AdSense partner sites, and mobile apps, contextually matched to page content
  and user intent.
- As of 2020-03-21, Neil cited Google's "other bets" revenue growing from $0.8 billion
  (3.2% of total revenue) in 2009 to $20.5 billion (15%+ of revenue) in 2018.
- As of 2020-03-21, Neil cited Sensor Tower data that Google's app store (Play)
  revenue grew 27.3% year-over-year.
