# YouTube SEO - 3 Steps To Rank Number 1 on YouTube

## Summary
A tutorial on ranking on YouTube, framed by an origin-story anecdote: Neil Patel says that when he
started his YouTube channel in 2017 he already knew how to do SEO and drive millions of visitors via
Google, but that knowledge "didn't translate directly" to YouTube's different algorithm, so he began
publishing at least one video a day to gather data on what worked. He cites YouTube as the second-largest
search engine after Google (Google owning both), with roughly 2.3 billion monthly users, and argues
YouTube is far less competitive than Google search even though it's getting more crowded. He distills
three durable steps: (1) find great topics by exploring broad themes (not narrow keywords) that resonate
with your audience, then find unique angles within a theme (his example: "Facebook Ads vs. Google Ads"
outperformed a plain "Facebook Ads" video) — sourced via Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas report, Google
site-search operators against quora.com/reddit.com, and Answer the Public; (2) create a compelling
thumbnail — give a glimpse of the payoff (e.g., an analytics snapshot for a traffic video, a before/after
for a conversion-rate video), use photos of himself reacting emotionally to match the video's tone, use
contrasting colors, and avoid red (YouTube's brand color) so thumbnails stand out; (3) do on-page SEO —
optimize title and description with the focus keyword (primary/secondary keywords from tools like
Ubersuggest in the first paragraph of the description), and pin a comment with an engaging question to
drive comment-driven virality. Closes with a direct engagement CTA and the standard agency pitch is
notably absent from this one (video ends on the question, not the NP Digital pitch).

## Key claims
- Neil started his YouTube channel in 2017; despite already being SEO-proficient for Google, that
  knowledge "didn't translate directly" to YouTube's algorithm, requiring a dedicated learning process
  of near-daily publishing (as of 2022-06-27, self-reported biographical detail).
- YouTube is the second-largest search engine after Google (Google owns both) with ~2.3 billion monthly
  users; YouTube is "nowhere near as competitive as Google" for ranking (as of 2022-06-27).
- Topic selection should start broad (audience-resonant themes) then narrow to specific angles — e.g.
  "Facebook Ads vs. Google Ads" got more engagement than a plain "Facebook Ads" video (as of 2022-06-27,
  self-reported result).
- Topic research tools: Ubersuggest keyword-ideas report, Google site-search operators
  (`site:quora.com`/`site:reddit.com` + keyword), and Answer the Public (as of 2022-06-27).
- Thumbnail rules: show a glimpse of the payoff, use emotionally reactive photos of yourself matched to
  the video's tone, use contrasting (non-red) colors to stand out against YouTube's UI (as of 2022-06-27).
- SEO step: optimize title/description with focus + secondary keywords (sourced via tools like
  Ubersuggest) in the first paragraph of the description, and pin a comment with an engaging question to
  drive comment volume, which helps YouTube's algorithm (as of 2022-06-27).
