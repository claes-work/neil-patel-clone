# Is Blogging Still Worth it?

## Summary

Neil answers his own framing question — "the short answer is no, the long answer is yes."
Traditional text-only blogging (crank out text, rank, drive traffic) is **not** worth it
anymore, but blogging adapted to 2019 still delivers ROI. His prescription: (1) don't
limit yourself to text — publish **video and audio** on the blog too, because people
consume on mobile; (2) **repurpose the same content everywhere** (Facebook, LinkedIn,
Medium, YouTube, IGTV) — he explicitly rejects the duplicate-content penalty as a myth
Google has publicly denied; (3) **continually update old content** to keep it fresh (he
says he employs three full-time people who do nothing but update his old text content),
citing Wikipedia's constant updates as why it perennially ranks; (4) accept that blogging
ROI is lower than 4–6 years ago and is harder to convert on mobile / off-platform, so
(5) **continually capture emails and push subscribers** (subscribers.com, Hello Bar,
Mailchimp) to sell to people over time rather than expecting an immediate sale.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-07-13, Neil said traditional text-only blogging is **not worth it**, but
  adapted blogging (multi-format + updated + email capture) still produces ROI.
- As of 2019-07-13, Neil claimed there are **over 1.94 billion websites**, over a billion
  blogs (~one blog per seven people), and **4 million+ blog posts published per day**.
- As of 2019-07-13, Neil said **Google does not penalize duplicate content** — he calls
  the penalty a myth Google has publicly denied — so repost the same content on Facebook,
  LinkedIn, Medium, YouTube, IGTV, etc.
- As of 2019-07-13, Neil said **content freshness** is a major ranking factor; sites that
  don't update their content slip in rankings after Google updates, especially long-tail
  pages. He said he has **three full-time employees** who only update old content.
- As of 2019-07-13, Neil said blogging ROI is lower than 4–6 years ago because it's
  **harder to convert users on mobile and off-domain** (YouTube/Facebook keep users on
  their own platforms for ad revenue).
- As of 2019-07-13, Neil said the key to making blogging worth it is to **continually
  collect emails and push subscribers** (subscribers.com, Hello Bar, Mailchimp) and sell
  to that audience over time.
