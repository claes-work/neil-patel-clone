# This Email Marketing Tip Takes 10 Minutes

## Summary
Argues that subject lines are the most commonly under-optimized part of email
marketing, and that fixing them is a fast, high-leverage tip. Cites HubSpot: 37% of
brands are increasing their email budget. States email remains a favorite channel of
his for driving leads, promoting content, and keeping his audience engaged, and calls
it "an intimate way to build a relationship" with an audience. Gives four subject-line
tips: (1) keep them short — cites 82% of marketers send subject lines under 60
characters, and that curiosity-evoking short lines ("the five benefits of green tea,
number four will shock you") work best; (2) actually use A/B test data to inform
future subject lines rather than just picking a one-off winner — cites 93% of US
companies A/B test email; (3) convey excitement/urgency about the offer directly in
the subject line rather than saving it for the body — cites 8 in 10 people read only
headlines/subject lines vs. 2 in 10 who read the rest; (4) avoid emojis in subject
lines, citing a Search Engine Journal study finding they increase unsubscribes,
especially for a professional brand. Also self-reports that putting the recipient's
name in the subject line "increased open rates massively." Cites 69% of recipients
mark email as spam based on the subject line alone and 33% open due to catchy subject
lines.

## Key claims
- Cites HubSpot: 37% of brands were increasing their email marketing budget (as
  cited, dated 2023-01-23).
- Cites (source unnamed): 69% of email recipients report an email as spam after
  reading the subject line; 33% open due to a catchy subject line; 82% of marketers
  send subject lines under 60 characters; 93% of US companies do email A/B testing;
  8/10 people read only headlines/subject lines vs. 2/10 who read the rest.
- Cites a Search Engine Journal study finding emojis in subject lines led to more
  unsubscribes.
- Self-reports that personalizing subject lines with the recipient's name "increased
  open rates massively" (self-reported, dated 2023-01-23).
