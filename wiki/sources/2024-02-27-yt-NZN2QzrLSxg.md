# Google's huge flop with Gemini, Search getting worse, Google News has a political bias, & more

## Summary
A politically-charged "reactions" episode covering Google Gemini's image-generation controversy (depicting historically-white figures, e.g. Vikings/Founding Fathers, as other races; Google pulled the image feature), a claimed Google-employee parody tweet, Google News' left-lean per an AllSides study Neil cites with numbers, Trump-branded merchandise as a brand-power case study, and — the most durable Neil-attributed material — a first-person complaint about Google ranking plagiarized/re-posted copies of his tweets over his own original content, plus a prediction that AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini) will fragment search away from a single "winner-take-all" engine. Neil also states, in first person, a personal policy of avoiding political topics in his own published content after one of his political shorts appeared to get suppressed (one view vs. thousands on non-political shorts), and lays out a merit-only hiring philosophy in response to a lunch conversation about DEI. Much of the Gemini/Trump-brand commentary is genuinely co-produced banter without clean per-speaker captions and is quarantined as joint/uncertain per SUBJECT.md's attribution rule.

## Key claims
- **Google News political skew, sourced with numbers.** Citing an AllSides analysis of a 5-day period of Google News articles: 61% of articles came from left-leaning outlets, 3% from right-leaning outlets, ~25% from centrist outlets, and 11% unrated/no political affiliation. Neil states this directly when Eric asks him for the figures. (Neil, 2024-02-27)
- **Google appears to be ranking scraped/republished copies of his content over his own posts.** Neil describes routinely Googling the headline of his own tweets to retrieve graphs he'd made (faster than searching his own files) and repeatedly finding that other people — including low-follower LinkedIn accounts — had copied his tweets word-for-word, swapped his NP Digital logo for their own on his graphs, and were still getting ranked in Google's rich snippets ahead of his own higher-follower, originally-published profile. He calls this "getting worse." (Neil, 2024-02-27)
- **Prediction: search will fragment, not stay winner-take-all.** Neil argues future search won't be a single dominant engine — Perplexity AI, ChatGPT, and Gemini will all take share — partly because different AI systems carry different biases (tying back to the Google News left-lean data), so people will gravitate to whichever system's outputs match their own confirmation biases rather than converging on one "neutral" tool. (Neil, 2024-02-27)
- **States a personal policy of avoiding political content**, prompted by noticing one of his political shorts (about media losing influence over elections) got only 1 view versus thousands for his non-political shorts, which he interprets as likely algorithmic suppression. "This is why [I] don't talk about politics when I publish content online." (Neil, 2024-02-27) `(paraphrase; direct quote below)`
- **States NP Digital's hiring philosophy is merit-only**, prompted by a lunch conversation where someone asked if he'd hire regardless of race/sexual orientation/gender identity: "we do try to give equal opportunity and try to hire people of all races, genders, etc., but the big thing we really look for in [an] organization is qualifications" — and that team members' personal lives/beliefs outside work are their own business as long as performance is there. (Neil, 2024-02-27) `(self-reported hiring philosophy, not independently verified)`
- **States personal political self-description as "right in the middle"** and says he does discuss Elon Musk/Trump/Tucker Carlson on the show without blanket endorsement, though he explicitly says he supports Elon Musk. (Neil, 2024-02-27) `(self-reported opinion, dated)`
