# 7 Unknown Marketing Tools to SKYROCKET Your Traffic

## Summary

Solo tactical listicle. Neil walks through seven less-common tools he uses to grow traffic:
1. **MeetEdgar** — social-sharing tool that **continually re-shares the same article over
   time** (4–7×), so followers who missed the first post still see it; "double, triple the
   traffic from Twitter" without complaints. He's used it for himself and clients for years.
2. **Subscribers.com** — free **browser push notifications**; visitors subscribe and you can
   ping them whenever you publish new content or launch a product to drive return visits/sales.
3. **Ubersuggest** (his own tool) — took paid features from SEMrush/Moz/Ahrefs and released
   them free: keyword phrase suggestions, traffic estimates per URL, competitor top pages,
   social shares, and ranking keywords to build better content against.
4. **Typeform** — a **survey** tool; survey your audience on what they want you to write, then
   produce that content so it actually earns traffic (vs. guessing topics).
5. **Canva** — a **design** tool; custom blog graphics beat overused stock photography and
   perform better on Twitter/Facebook/LinkedIn, driving more social traffic.
6. **A/B test your headlines via Twitter (strategy + tool)** — instead of changing a title and
   waiting ~30 days for Google, **tweet 4–5 headline variations of the same URL at the same
   time each week (Mon–Thu)**, track clicks with Bitly, and adopt the winner to lift Google
   click-through rate (higher CTR signals relevance and can lift rankings).
7. **LeadQuizzes** — **quiz** your audience (very engaging) and collect an email at the end;
   example **Legion Athletics** runs a sidebar quiz; email those leads when you publish new
   content/products to pull them back.
Framing: these work partly *because* competitors aren't using them.
