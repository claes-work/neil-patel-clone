# Learn SEO For Free! How to Jumpstart Your SEO Career Without Spending a Dime

## Summary

Neil lays out a free, tool-driven workflow for learning and doing SEO with no budget, built
around **Ubersuggest** and competitor mining. Steps: (1) run your (or a competitor's) URL
through Ubersuggest's **SEO Analyzer** to get a prioritized, plain-language list of technical
errors (duplicate titles/meta descriptions, critical vs. warning) and fix them one by one,
re-checking as you go; (2) use the **Traffic Analyzer** report to see which competitor pages
get the most search traffic and the keywords driving it; (3) take a high-traffic competitor
page and apply **Brian Dean's skyscraper technique** — make a substantially better version
(4,000 words vs. 2,000, add images + video); (4) use **search.twitter.com** to find who shared
the competitor's article and email them a short pitch that yours covers more; (5) pull the
competitor's **Backlinks report** in Ubersuggest and outreach every linking site. He closes on
his recurring theme that **user metrics** (bounce rate, time on site) are an underrated ranking
factor — survey visitors with **Hotjar**, find the common complaints across 30–40 people, and
fix them, because "Google wants to put sites at the top that people truly genuinely love."

## Key claims

- As of 2019-05-30, Neil said you can learn and do SEO for free using **Ubersuggest's SEO
  Analyzer** (technical audit) and **Traffic Analyzer** (competitor traffic + keywords) — no
  technical background required because the tool prioritizes fixes step by step.
- As of 2019-05-30, Neil recommended the **skyscraper technique** (attributed to **Brian Dean**):
  find a competitor's high-traffic page and create a materially better, longer, media-richer
  version so people prefer it over the competition.
- As of 2019-05-30, Neil said to find sharers via **search.twitter.com** and linkers via
  Ubersuggest's **Backlinks report**, then outreach each with a short "mine covers 1,2,3 that
  theirs didn't" email to earn shares and links.
- As of 2019-05-30, Neil reiterated that **user metrics** (bounce, time on site) are a real
  ranking input most SEOs ignore; survey with **Hotjar**, act on the common feedback, and
  rankings climb over time.
