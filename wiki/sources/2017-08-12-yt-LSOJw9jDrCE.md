# 5 Hacks to Get More Social Shares | Online Marketing Advice

## Summary
A short tactical video in which Neil Patel walks through five low-effort "hacks" for getting more social shares and social traffic from blog content. He recommends the Click to Tweet WordPress plugin (claiming three to four tweetable factoids per post can roughly double Twitter traffic), scrolling/floating social icons and hover-to-share image buttons via the AddThis tool (free) to lift Pinterest and overall sharing, personalized outreach emails to anyone you link out to in a post, and — the one he calls most important — genuinely participating in the social communities (Facebook groups, subreddits) before sharing your own content, so you build standing and avoid looking purely self-promotional.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-12, Neil states:
- Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn are some of the most popular sites on the web and a valuable source of social traffic (as of 2017-08-12).
- **Hack 1 — Click to Tweet:** Use the Click to Tweet WordPress plugin to let readers tweet out factoids/quotes embedded in your blog content; placing three to four Click to Tweets within a post should roughly double your Twitter traffic (self-reported / rule-of-thumb) (as of 2017-08-12).
- **Hack 2 — Scrolling social icons:** Social sharing icons should scroll/float alongside the reader as they move down the page; this generates more clicks than static icons. He recommends the free, easy-to-use AddThis tool to create them (as of 2017-08-12).
- **Hack 3 — Shareable images:** Make blog images easy to share (hover-to-share buttons, also via AddThis) so readers can pin them to Pinterest, generating more Pinterest traffic (as of 2017-08-12).
- **Hack 4 — Email the people you link to:** When you mention or link to someone in a post, email them a short, flattering note ("I'm a huge fan… I even linked out to you in my latest blog post… feel free to check it out") to prompt them to view and share it (as of 2017-08-12).
- ★ **Hack 5 — Participate first (called "one of the most important"):** Actively participate in whatever social communities you prefer (Facebook groups, subreddits) — leave comments, submit other people's content, vote up and down — to become well-known in the community before sharing your own content; without participating first, people see you as self-promotional and ignore your content (as of 2017-08-12).
