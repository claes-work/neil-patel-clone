# You Will NEVER Rank For These Types of Keywords (STOP Setting Yourself Up For Failure!)

## Summary
Argues that new/small/young sites chasing high-volume head terms (credit cards, auto
insurance, SEO, dog food) are set up to fail: those terms need backlink volume and
domain authority that take years to build, and by the time a site catches up,
established competitors have grown further — a compounding disadvantage. Prescribes
building authority gradually via long-tail variations first (e.g. "how to get started
in SEO", "what is SEO" before "SEO" itself), which signals topical relevance to Google
and eventually earns the right to compete for the head term. Workflow: type head terms
into Ubersuggest's Keyword Ideas report, filter by page-one ranking probability against
your own URL (only keywords achievable *today*, not in 6-12 months), group the
resulting keywords by subtopic, and for each group Google the topic to reverse-engineer
what page-one competitors include (content types, depth, format — not word count for
its own sake) before writing a better article. Closes with the same backlink-
opportunities-report outreach tactic (target sites already linking to competitors) used
elsewhere in the corpus.

## Key claims
- Explicit compounding-disadvantage argument for why small/new sites should never
  target head terms directly: while a challenger builds toward the authority needed,
  incumbents keep growing too, widening the gap rather than closing it (self-reported
  reasoning, dated 2021-11-27) — seo. A clearer articulation of the "why not head
  terms" logic than prior tactical videos on the same long-tail-first doctrine.
- Explicitly rejects word count and image/video count as the differentiators for
  outranking competitors, instead prescribing a manual page-one teardown per keyword
  group to identify what content elements (step-by-step instructions, GIFs, videos)
  page-one pages include (paraphrase, dated 2021-11-27) — seo, content-marketing.
  Reinforces, does not contradict, the length-matches-topic stance on
  [[wiki/sources/2021-11-20-yt-1odgi9VSx7Q]].
