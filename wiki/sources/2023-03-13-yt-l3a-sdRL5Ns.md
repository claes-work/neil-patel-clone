# 5 Marketing Predictions for 2023

## Summary
Five dated 2023 marketing predictions, framed with the general advice to have a
solidified-but-adaptable strategy since "things can change in marketing at a drop
of a dime." (1) More brands will move off third-party cookie data; notes
Chrome's third-party-cookie phase-out starts 2024, but recommends preparing early
(prediction, dated 2023-03-13). (2) Voice search grows further — cites voice
search sales were roughly $40B the prior year, projected to exceed $50B+ in 2023
(as cited); recommends fast mobile load times, concise/direct answer-style
content, long-tail and conversational/question-based keywords (findable via
AnswerThePublic), answered in as few sentences as possible. (3) Live shopping
takes off — cites live commerce generating a predicted $387B in China alone (as
cited); notes it hasn't taken off as much in the US but is growing, especially
with influencer-led live selling. (4) Social commerce drives conversation — cites
54% of users use social media to research products (as cited); recommends
showcasing products via unboxings, reviews, and service-results content on
social. (5) Podcast advertising explodes — cites British audiences consumed
almost 60M hours of podcasts weekly (as cited); cites 75% of mobile gamers prefer
audio ads over video ads (as cited); notes fewer than 10M podcasts exist globally
vs. over 1B blogs, implying a comparatively uncrowded ad market. Closes with an
NP Digital plug.
