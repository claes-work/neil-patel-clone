# Affiliate Marketing: Maximize Income with ClickBank and AdWords

## Summary

Q&A Thursday (Neil with interviewer Adam). Question: how to promote ClickBank products on
Google AdWords. Neil's blunt take: driving AdWords traffic **straight to a ClickBank
offer rarely converts**, and there's "a lot of hocus pocus" on ClickBank and affiliate
networks (false-claim offers like "lose 20 lb in 2 days" / "get a girlfriend in three
hours"), though legitimate offers exist (e.g. via Affiliate World). The tactic that works
is a **tripwire funnel**: first sell a cheap, irresistible offer (his example: $7 one-time)
that converts at a high rate, then **upsell** the more expensive core/ClickBank offer right
at checkout — analogous to a McDonald's 99-cent Big Mac then "want fries and a Coke?" or
Taco Tuesday. Even non-buyers of the upsell gave you their **email via the tripwire**, so
you **drip** them and sell over email — and switch the emotional register: if the ClickBank
landing page pitched *emotionally* and they didn't convert, email a *logical* (stats/data)
pitch, and vice versa. Expect a ~20–30%+ take rate on the core offer. The economics: if the
tripwire offsets your traffic cost, you're building a real business on effectively free
leads. He notes Google increasingly inspects the whole funnel to block shady offers, though
it rarely follows all the way past checkout. Aside: Neil references running a **dating
company with ~9 products**, and the principle that once someone buys once, that buyer list
converts better on additional products.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-09, sending AdWords traffic directly to a ClickBank offer converts poorly;
  a tripwire funnel is the fix.
- As of 2018-08-09, a tripwire = a cheap irresistible offer (e.g. $7 one-time) that converts
  high, followed by an upsell to the core/expensive offer at checkout.
- As of 2018-08-09, capture the email at the tripwire and drip non-buyers; invert the pitch's
  emotional register vs. the landing page (emotional page → logical email, and vice versa).
- As of 2018-08-09, expect roughly a 20–30%+ take rate on the core offer; if the tripwire
  offsets traffic cost you acquire leads for free and can sell more products to that buyer list.
- As of 2018-08-09, Google inspects funnels to block shady offers but rarely audits past
  checkout; ClickBank/affiliate networks contain both legitimate and false-claim offers.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's really hard to go use Google AdWords and drive them to a ClickBank page and generate a sale. Doesn't work too well."

> "What a tripwire is, is you're getting someone to buy something that's so amazing, it's such an irresistible offer they can't say no."

> "if the ClickBank page is logical using stats and data to make the sale and someone doesn't convert, then your email pitch should be emotional. It should be the opposite."
