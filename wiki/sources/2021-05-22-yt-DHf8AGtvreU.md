# Advanced Growth Tactics - Steal The Top-Performing Tech Companies' Best-Kept Secrets

## Summary
Final lesson of Growth Hacking Unlocked (Week 2, Lesson 3) — **COMPLETES the course**
(started 2021-05-13). Covers the "marketing" discipline (after psychology and technology
in the prior two lessons) through three big-brand case studies. Procter & Gamble: owns
22+ brands with $1B+ in annual sales each, plus 19 more brands at $500M+; Tide has been a
top brand for 75+ years; the lesson is to stay focused on what works and double down
rather than chase shiny objects, reusing the same frameworks across brands. Nike: the
"Just Do It" campaign grew North American domestic sport-shoe market share from 18% to
43% and worldwide sales from $877M to $9.2B (states the range as "1988 to 1998," with an
initial verbal slip saying "1998 to 1990"); credits decades of athlete endorsements
(citing Michael Jordan, no longer playing, still pushing the brand) for sustained growth.
Facebook: its growth team's singular focus for roughly three years was getting any new
user to 7 friends within 10 days as the retention-predicting metric, driving the company
past a billion users; frames this as an example of company-wide focus on one growth
metric. Advises viewers with no budget to start small (video, guest posting, blogging,
podcasting) and scale content production with freelancers/interns/an agency (plugs NP
Digital) once budget allows, tracking KPIs from clicks through to sales/upsells. Makes an
SEO claim: brand-name search queries (e.g., "Nike," "BMW," "Procter & Gamble") affect
organic rankings, and this factor is under-discussed relative to backlinks. Closes with
course wrap-up encouragement and points to downloadable worksheets (content marketing,
tools/scripts, case studies/market data) at neilpatel.com/training.
