# Tiktok releases new SEO feature, US House Bans TikTok, and more

## Summary
Several segments. (1) Neil walks through a new TikTok feature, "Creator Search Insights" (searchable in TikTok's own search bar), which surfaces popular and trending keywords/phrases in a table with volume-style metrics, historical trend behavior, related-keyword suggestions, and the top-performing videos for each keyword — explicitly framed as a TikTok-native alternative to using Tweet Hunter or manual X-trend-watching for content ideation; he states he's begun using it for ideation but hasn't yet produced videos from it, and that his own ideation process has cycled through several tools over time before settling on his current habit of freeform note-taking. (2) An extended, largely undiarized discussion of the US House's vote to ban TikTok (not yet law; still requires Senate passage and a presidential signature, expected from Biden), covering App-Store-delisting mechanics, ByteDance's likely fight to avoid a forced divestiture/total loss of a $20–50B asset, a comparison to how China restricts US platforms (no Facebook/Gmail access, a separate, more-censored Douyin instead of TikTok), and a tangent into the "Target figured out a teenage girl was pregnant from her purchase patterns before her father did" data story and Section 230. (3) A largely undiarized ideal-posting-frequency Q&A across platforms (X ~4–5x/day with high tolerance for low-quality volume; Instagram ~1–3 posts/day, spaced Stories; LinkedIn: space same-day posts ≥7 hours apart; TikTok ~1–3x/day; YouTube: max one short + one long per day, spaced ≥5 hours; Facebook: no reliable pattern found even after analyzing 300,000+ posts), during which Neil states his own Instagram profile logged 2.7M views in the trailing 28 days. (4) A Daniel Priestley/Clubhouse-sourced case study on the traffic/lead spike (800K YouTube views, 400K Spotify plays, 1,700 webinar signups, thousands of new LinkedIn/Instagram followers in one week) from a single "Diary of a CEO" podcast appearance, and a Las Vegas poker-night anecdote about meeting a stranger who'd bought four $2,500 Eight Sleep units for his family after hearing a Huberman Lab podcast ad — used to argue podcast advertising works when the show's audience is well-matched to the product.

## Key claims
- **TikTok's "Creator Search Insights" tool (in-app search bar) surfaces trending/popular keywords, a TikTok-native volume metric, historical trend charts, related-keyword suggestions, and top-performing videos per keyword** — positioned as a free content-ideation alternative to tools like Tweet Hunter. States he has started using it for ideation but not yet produced video content from it. (Neil, 2024-03-18) `(self-reported, dated tactical detail)`
- **His own ideation process has moved through phases** (Tweet Hunter, manual keyword research, other tools) before settling on simply freeform-noting ideas and picking whichever resonates, with no claimed "right" method. (Neil, 2024-03-18) `(self-reported)`
- **Personal Instagram analytics self-report:** 2.7M views on his Instagram profile in the trailing 28 days as of this recording, driven substantially by one particular video. (Neil, 2024-03-18) `(self-reported, dated)`
- **Ideal posting-frequency benchmarks by platform (source: an internal shared dataset, "we analyzed 300,000+ posts" for Facebook alone without finding a reliable pattern).** X ~4–5x/day (the platform's algorithm doesn't heavily punish low-quality volume; text-preceding-video and on-post dwell time matter more than video itself); Instagram ~1–3 posts/day depending on format, Stories 2–4x/day, diminishing returns from over-posting unless every post is a hit; LinkedIn: same-day posts should be spaced ≥7 hours apart or the first post's reach suffers; TikTok ~1–3x/day; YouTube: max one short + one long per day, spaced ≥5 hours apart; Facebook: no reliable frequency pattern identified despite a large analyzed sample. `(attribution: uncertain — undiarized "we" framing; likely joint NP Digital/Marketing School research)`
