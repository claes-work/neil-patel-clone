# 5 Tips For Writing An Awesome Blog Post

## Summary

Neil Patel outlines a five-tip system for writing high-quality blog posts faster by templatizing the process. He argues that once a writer settles into a style, they can codify structure, image sourcing, and formatting rules so that any post takes under two hours. The video pairs a repeatable template workflow with a personal ruleset for readability and closes with fixes for the common blockers that slow writers down.

## Key claims

As of 2017-08-30, Neil Patel advises:

- **Take your time on the first few posts, then templatize.** Writing the first several blog posts slowly helps a writer discover their own style and learn what readers respond to; only after that should they standardize the process into a repeatable template.
- **Build a basic template and fill it in a fixed order.** Use an introduction–body–conclusion structure. Patel writes the introduction first, then creates three to seven headings for the body from the post's main ideas, then writes the conclusion, and finally goes back to fill each heading with content (paragraphs or bullet points).
- **Source images fast.** Every post should have images because "a picture says a thousand words" and readers do not want plain text; he recommends running quick searches on stock photography sites (he pays for and uses Fotolia) to add images with speed.
- **Create a personal set of writing rules.** His own rules: use "you and I" to make the post feel conversational; keep paragraphs short (never longer than six lines, ideally four or five, sometimes one line); include six or seven images or more throughout the post; and link out to sites that cite his research.
- **A post should take under two hours — fix the blocker if it doesn't.** If writing takes longer, diagnose the cause: for idea generation, use BuzzSumo by typing in keywords to see what is hot; for writer's block, step outside and do an activity before returning; for distraction, move to a different place to write.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> The first thing you need to do, and I know this sounds counterintuitive, is take your time writing the first few blog posts.

> Without images, your blog post won't do as well because a picture says a thousand words. No one just wants to read text.

> I always use the words "you and I" within my blog post. It makes it feel like it's a conversation between you and I.

> If you follow those tips, you'll be able to templatize your blog writing process and it shouldn't take you more than two hours to write a blog post. If you're spending more than two hours, there's something wrong.
