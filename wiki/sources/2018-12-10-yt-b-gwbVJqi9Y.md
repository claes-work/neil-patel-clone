# How Digital Marketing Will Change in 2019 | Neil Patel

## Summary

In this predictions video, Neil Patel argues that the single-channel growth tactics of the prior few years no longer work as well and lays out seven shifts marketers must embrace to thrive in 2019: (1) move to an omnichannel approach because individual channels have become crowded; (2) prepare for voice search, which he says Comscore estimates will make up half of all Google searches by 2020; (3) invest in conversion optimization as advertising costs keep rising; (4) leverage full marketing funnels tracking upsells, downsells, cross-sells, lifetime value, and churn rather than just campaign-level sales; (5) recognize that content marketing "just won't work as well" unless it delivers genuinely new information; (6) prioritize video content, which platforms like Facebook, YouTube, and LinkedIn favor; and (7) start a podcast, citing that podcast listeners tend to be affluent (45% with household income above $75,000) and therefore better buyers. Throughout he recommends specific tools (Crazy Egg, Hello Bar, ClickFunnels), most of which he is associated with.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-12-10, Neil claims digital marketing "is gonna change in 2019" and that what has worked for the last few years no longer works as well (he stresses tactics don't work "at all," just not as well).
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil says digital marketing is moving to an omnichannel approach; you used to be able to build a business on a single channel (he cites Facebook's early growth via automatic address-book invites, "even without your permission"), but those tactics have faded.
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil says the more channels you use (growth hacking, PPC, SEO, content marketing, social media marketing, banner ads), the better off you'll be, because channels are crowded with so many online businesses.
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil says search is moving to voice search, citing a Comscore estimate that by 2020 half of all searches on Google will be voice search, and that "right now, two out of every five adults are using voice search."
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil's three tactics to win at voice search: (1) make your site HTTPS, since most top-ranking sites are HTTPS; (2) make your site load fast; (3) keep answers short and to the point (one sentence beats a paragraph), because people type/speak longer-term question phrases.
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil says advertising is getting more expensive over time and "that's not gonna change," so conversion optimization is the only way to compete and stay ahead.
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil recommends Crazy Egg for A/B testing and Hello Bar for exit-intent email collection to squeeze more conversions and revenue from existing traffic; he warns that otherwise competitors who outspend you "are gonna crush you."
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil says marketing funnels will be more popular than ever in 2019; instead of optimizing campaigns to ad-to-sale only, marketers must track upsells, downsells, cross-sells, lifetime value, and churn across physical products, digital products, or lead generation — from visitor to conversion. He points to ClickFunnels (clickfunnels.com) for building funnels.
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil says content marketing "just won't work as well" because blogging is played out and everyone regurgitates the same information; only amazing, genuinely new content will earn links or social shares.
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil says video content is the future because people want to engage through video, and Facebook, YouTube, and LinkedIn all reward/give more views to video.
- As of 2018-12-10, Neil says "podcasting is taking over," citing that 45% of people who listen to podcasts have a household income above $75,000, making them better buyers than most marketing channels; he urges creating a podcast so people can listen on the go while commuting.
