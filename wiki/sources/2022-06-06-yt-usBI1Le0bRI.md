# Lazy Marketers Tend to be Amazing

## Summary
A hiring-philosophy video arguing that some of the best marketers are the "lazy" ones — not lazy in
the sense of unwillingness to work long hours, but people who resent doing repetitive, mundane tasks
and therefore automate or systematize them rather than grinding through them by brute force. Neil
frames this as a creativity necessity: since a scrappy company never has as many resources as
larger, longer-established competition, it needs people who find shortcuts. He's careful to
distinguish this from laziness-as-low-effort: the "lazy" hires he wants are smart, ambitious, and
still put in 40–50+ hours a week; they simply refuse to repeat inefficient processes and instead
build tools/templates to eliminate them. He gives a staffing ratio (roughly 1-in-10 hires at a small
company, or 3-in-100 at a larger one like his) and a detailed case study: Kyle Baleza, who joined as
a link builder in Brazil and, with Neil's permission to use his name, climbed to help build the S&B
division and later global expansion — illustrated by three concrete automations Kyle built
(proposal templates instead of ad hoc proposals; a pricing calculator so sales reps stop asking Kyle
whether they can close at a given price; and a backup-payment-method process — collecting ACH info
alongside credit cards — to reduce follow-up chasing when cards fail).
