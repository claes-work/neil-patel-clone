# THIS Might Disrupt the $2.2B SEO Industry: Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)

## Summary
★ L3-candidate: an early (Jan 2024) dated origin-point of Neil Patel's AI-search-visibility thesis — the foundation of what he later calls "Search Everywhere Optimization" — including his proprietary "82 ranking factors" study and its two headline findings (relevancy + brand mentions). A co-hosted Marketing School episode in which **Eric Siu drives the segment**: he screen-shares and reads through the academic "GEO: Generative Engine Optimization" paper (a collaboration of Princeton, Georgia Tech, the Allen Institute for AI, and IIT) that proposes optimizing content to appear in AI-generated answers from systems like Bing Chat, Google's SGE, and Perplexity. Neil is seeing the paper "in real time" and reacts. Neil's substantive contribution is his own framing and data: he and Eric had already been writing/tweeting about optimizing for ChatGPT and Bard; NP Digital has picked up clients directly from ChatGPT recommending the agency; NP Digital ran a study of 82+ factors driving LLM recommendations and found relevancy (keywords) and brand mentions to be the two dominant ones; and AI search will be closer to "winner-take-all" than traditional SEO. Eric relays the paper's GEO methods (authoritative tone, keyword inclusion, statistics, citations/quotations, fluency, easy-to-understand language) and its result that the best methods lift baseline visibility by ~30–40%, closing with a "cautiously optimistic, stay adaptable" caveat.

## Key claims
- "Generative Engine Optimization" (GEO) is emerging as the next form of SEO — optimizing your content to be surfaced and recommended inside AI-generated answers rather than in a list of ten blue links. (Eric relaying the GEO paper / both hosts framing, 2024-01-05)
- Neil and Eric had already been working on and publishing about optimizing for ChatGPT and Bard before this paper; the "GEO" label was new to Neil but the practice was not. (Neil, 2024-01-05)
- NP Digital has already picked up paying clients from ChatGPT recommending the agency; if you can get LLMs to mention your company and products inside their answers/suggestions, you pick up more business. (Neil, self-reported, 2024-01-05)
- NP Digital studied 82+ factors behind ChatGPT and Bard recommending sites and found the two biggest were **relevancy** (largely keyword-driven) and **brand mentions**. (Neil, self-reported study, 2024-01-05)
- Relevancy is not just the keywords on your own site — it also depends on what keywords other people use when they mention your products and services in their articles. (Neil, 2024-01-05)
- Brand mentions in PR and third-party articles (e.g. being named "performance marketing agency of the year") drive LLM recommendations, because the model surfaces you when people ask related questions. (Neil, 2024-01-05)
- ChatGPT's index (at time of recording) was from September 2021, so to "optimize for ChatGPT" you largely have to wait for the index to update; SEO work done pre-September-2021 still helps. Bard's index is much fresher (~2–3 weeks old). Freshness will improve over time. (Neil, 2024-01-05)
- AI search will be closer to "winner-take-all" than traditional SEO: in classic SEO ranking #2–4 still earns traffic, but AI answers tend to recommend only the top one-to-few options — though for some queries it may still name two-to-four (e.g. "things to do in New York" can surface both a pizza shop and the Statue of Liberty; an "ad agency" query could name both Neil's and Eric's firms). (Neil, 2024-01-05)
- The GEO paper's proposed optimization methods are: authoritative/persuasive tone, keyword inclusion (classic SEO), adding quantitative statistics, citing sources, adding quotations from relevant sources, easy-to-understand/simplified language, and fluency optimization. (Eric relaying the paper, 2024-01-05)
- The paper's methods are "not necessarily a lot of new things — this is just good writing at the end of the day." (Eric, 2024-01-05)
- The paper reports the best-performing methods improve on baseline by 41% and 29% on position-adjusted word count and subjective impression respectively (~30% average), and can boost source visibility by up to 40% — with citations, quotations, and statistics being especially effective, and effectiveness varying by query domain. (Eric relaying the paper, 2024-01-05)
- The space is very early and could change substantially in the next 6–12 months, so be cautiously optimistic and stay adaptable. (Eric, 2024-01-05)
- The GEO paper is a legitimate academic collaboration (Princeton University, Georgia Tech, the Allen Institute for AI, and IIT); IIT is "harder to get into than MIT." (IIT remark: Neil; provenance framing: Eric, 2024-01-05)
