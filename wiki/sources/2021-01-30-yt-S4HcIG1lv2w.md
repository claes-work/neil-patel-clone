# High-Converting Workflows & Sequences That You Can Copy, Paste & Send - Email Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week One, Lesson Two of **Email Marketing Unlocked**, explicitly referenced in-video as
following the prior lesson
([[wiki/sources/2021-01-28-yt-dG-6A8_R_MY]]). Covers email ROI (cites a $38-per-$1-spent
average email-marketing ROI), then the "dating, not marriage-proposal" framing already
established elsewhere in the corpus: a welcome sequence over ~14-15 days nurtures a new
lead before asking for a purchase, rather than pitching immediately. Defines
mechanics: autoresponders (triggered by list membership) vs. broadcasts (unplanned
one-off sends) vs. campaigns (which can be "stacked" — one sequence educates, a
following sequence sells). Segmentation is framed as essential to conversion — sending
identical content to a full list (e.g. "buy a home" emails to people who already own
one) wastes the opportunity; segments named: behavior (scrolling, clicking, video
views, time-on-email), demographic (age, gender, ethnicity, income, position), and
geographic (time zone, country, state, population density). Covers "journeys" per
segment (sale emails to existing buyers, personalized welcome-question emails, deal
emails, abandoned-cart-recovery emails, feedback/NPS requests, event invitations,
promotional-calendar emails like Black Friday) and closes on re-engagement emails for
lapsed buyers. Subject-line tactics: curiosity ("I wish I would've seen this five years
ago"), scarcity ("last chance," with the caveat that scarcity claims must be true), and
humor (demoed with a joke example). Ends on list-hygiene (scrubbing "cold subscribers,"
a term/feature named in ConvertKit specifically) as essential to sustained open rates.
