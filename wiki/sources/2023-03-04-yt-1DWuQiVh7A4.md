# Step-by-Step Strategy I Use to Drive 1 Million Visitors to My Blog Per Month

## Summary
Self-reports his blog gets "well over a million" and "actually over 4 million"
visitors a month (self-reported, dated 2023-03-04), and lays out the promotion-side
playbook he credits for it (assumes content already exists). Steps: (1) prune
low/no-traffic, outdated pages (via GSC 30/60/90-day views) and 301-redirect them
to the most relevant page or homepage; (2) refresh pages that have measurably lost
traffic year-over-year (compare current 30-day GSC traffic to a year prior),
citing Wikipedia's continual-freshness habit as the reason it "continually ranks";
(3) combine near-duplicate pages targeting the same topic into one comprehensive
page with 301 redirects — cites About.com's traffic gains from consolidating
content after it split into several sites; (4) outreach to everyone cited/linked
in a post, asking for a social share rather than a backlink ("much easier for them
to just click the share button"); (5) collect email addresses via exit-intent
offers (guide/quiz/ebook) and email new posts 2–3x/week; (6) use Ubersuggest's
Backlinks Opportunity Report (neilpatel.com/backlinks) to find sites linking to 3+
competitors but not you, then pitch them; (7) internally link new posts to
relevant older posts for immediate "link juice"; (8) use the free tool
"Subscribers" (browser push notifications) to alert readers to new posts without
requiring an email address. Frames all of this as the "promotion side" that
complements — but does not replace — writing good content. Plugs NP Digital,
self-reporting it won "SEO Campaign of the Year."

## Key claims
