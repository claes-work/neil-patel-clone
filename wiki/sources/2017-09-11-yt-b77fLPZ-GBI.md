# How to Write Catchy Headlines That Convert | Copywriting Secrets For Digital Marketers

## Summary
Neil walks through a simple framework for writing homepage headlines that convert. The core method is a two-part structure: a short headline (five or six words, under ten) that identifies the visitor's problem, paired with a longer sub-headline (a full sentence is fine) that presents the solution. He cites the Crazy Egg homepage as an example of this problem/solution split and stresses that no headline is final — headlines and sub-headlines must be A/B tested continuously (he points to Crazy Egg's own AB testing tool) because market conditions change and a winner from a year or two ago may stop converting.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-11, Neil states:
- 8 out of 10 people read your headline, but only 2 out of 10 read the rest of the page (as of 2017-09-11).
- Most homepage traffic lands on the homepage but does not convert, and weak headlines are a primary reason (as of 2017-09-11).
- A headline should identify the problem in roughly five or six words, and under ten for sure; anything hitting 12-14 words is too long (as of 2017-09-11).
- The headline should state the problem while a sub-headline (which can be a full sentence) states the solution (as of 2017-09-11).
- The Crazy Egg homepage exemplifies this split — headline discusses the problem, sub-headline discusses the solution — though Neil notes even it may not "hit it hard enough" and the current variation may not be the winner (as of 2017-09-11).
- You should never stop A/B testing headlines and sub-headlines, because market conditions change and a headline that worked a year or two ago may no longer work (as of 2017-09-11).
- Crazy Egg's AB testing solution lets you add many headline/sub-headline variations and shows which one drives the most sales (as of 2017-09-11).
