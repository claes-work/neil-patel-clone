# Why Your Content Gets ZERO Attention and Traffic (Even When It's Fully Optimized for SEO)

## Summary
Six reasons SEO-optimized content still fails to get traffic, citing an Ahrefs study
that 90.63% of content gets no Google traffic: (1) targeting only head terms instead
of long-tail variations while authority is still low; (2) optimizing for search
engines instead of matching search intent (e.g., "iPhone 13" queries are
transactional, not informational — a long-form guide won't rank/convert regardless of
optimization); (3) missing contextual internal links between topically-related pages,
but cautions against forcing irrelevant links purely for SEO; (4) insufficient
backlinks, with the same Ahrefs study correlating link count with traffic; (5)
content isn't evergreen — recommends up to 40% of content effort go to refreshing old
posts rather than only publishing new ones, and discloses he personally writes four
new articles/month while a team refreshes 100+ older posts (bio/operational detail,
dated 2022-01-15, consistent with the existing "25x content-refresh ratio"/"21:1 GSC
workflow" theme already logged from 2020-12-19 and 2021-12-20 batches — same
recurring content-ops philosophy, different numeric framing, not a contradiction);
(6) slow page load — cites a general "no one waits more than 3 seconds" user
tolerance threshold.
