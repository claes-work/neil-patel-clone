# How SEOs Should Use Google Analytics

## Summary
Solo tactical video walking through which Google Analytics/Search Console reports an
SEO should regularly check: (1) conversions/goal tracking overview; (2) week-over-week
traffic compared same-weekday-to-same-weekday, filtered by the specific
countries/regions that actually convert for the business (using his own agency's
US-vs-UK-vs-Brazil traffic as the example, and a Crazy Egg comparison showing the same
US/UK decline pattern across unrelated sites in the same period — attributed to
COVID-era conditions, not something-is-broken); (3) overall organic search-traffic
trend over a year via both Analytics and Search Console (Search Console preferred
because it filters out non-search "junk" traffic); (4) real-time content report; (5)
device split (desktop vs. mobile), with a callback to his own earlier finding that his
AMP pages carried heavy mobile traffic but historically had no conversion elements
(the same AMP-monetization fix covered in the 2021-03-22 video).

## Key claims
- Advises comparing traffic same-weekday-to-same-weekday (e.g. Thursday-to-Thursday)
  rather than raw week-over-week, and filtering by the specific target
  region/geography that actually generates revenue rather than total traffic, because
  total-traffic growth can mask revenue-relevant regional decline (paraphrase, tactical
  method).
- States his own US traffic declined across several comparison windows during this
  period, cross-validated against a second unrelated site he has access to (Crazy Egg)
  showing a similar US/UK decline, and attributes the pattern to COVID-era conditions
  rather than a site-specific problem (paraphrase, self-reported observation, dated to
  this recording, COVID-era).
- Recommends Search Console over Analytics for judging organic-search-specific trend
  because Search Console strips out non-search "junk" traffic that inflates Analytics
  numbers (paraphrase, tactical advice).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Don't just look at it as pure traffic... it's deceptive. If you look here... you see
> a total number of users increasing, which is great, but if I'm losing traffic in the
> main region that I care about... that's kind of all that matters.
