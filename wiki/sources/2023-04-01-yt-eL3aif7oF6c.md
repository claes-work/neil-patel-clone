# Get More Traffic Now: Insider Tips for Success

## Summary
Reflective, values-oriented interview ("Success with Soul" podcast). Self-
description: "I'm a serial entrepreneur, most people think of me as a marketer,
but I'm really more so entrepreneur first, marketer second"; NP Digital in
8-9 countries, expecting ~20 by year-end; describes himself as father, husband,
living a "pretty normal, plain life," delegating headcount management to
"great managers." On SEO vs. social media: prefers SEO because it favors
smaller players without large ad budgets ("David versus Goliath"), produces
durable/evergreen traffic vs. social media's 24-48-hour content half-life, and
isn't degraded the way social reach is by ever-changing algorithms. Cites NP
Digital's "21st (or 22nd) fastest-growing company" Inc. 500 placement, attributing
much of that growth to SEO. On AI content (ChatGPT-era, 2023): says AI is useful
as a starting point but current tools can't yet produce content with genuine
"unique experience, expertise, authority, trust" (Google's E-E-A-T); illustrates
with a live example of searching "population of Las Vegas," showing four
top results give four different population figures, to argue AI output is only
as reliable as its (often inconsistent) training sources, so human review/
editing remains essential; expects this limitation to persist "for the next
five plus years." Describes the origin of Ubersuggest (bought for ~$120K, then
"millions" spent improving it) and AnswerThePublic ("~$8.6 million," repeating
the figure from other 2023-era interviews this batch) as deliberate "free tool"
link-magnet plays that also drive organic backlinks and agency leads. On
keyword-research rules of thumb: recommends targeting keywords with 1,000+
monthly searches, since long-tail variations naturally ride along; cites Quora's
top-100-website ranking (per SimilarWeb, self-reported as approximate) as an
example of aggregating many low-volume long-tail terms at scale. ★ **Personal/
values segment**: confirms two children, ages three and one, as of this
recording; self-care = time with his kids (mentions a reading-focused board
game, "Zingo"); time management via Google Calendar plus periodic iPhone
screen-time review (previously also used an app called Rescue Time); favorite
business book again Ray Dalio's *Principles*. ★ **Names his uncle as the source
of his recurring "there's always someone better/worse off" leveling mantra** —
"my uncle taught this to me a long, long time ago" — a first-person attribution
not previously recorded for this recurring quote (the quote itself already
appears in the wiki without an origin). Mentions his **father-in-law "crushes
it" on a Catalina Island Airbnb** as an aside example of SEO/marketing applying
beyond traditional business (Airbnb listing SEO). Defines "success with soul" as
succeeding ethically and enjoyably enough that he can "look yourself in the
mirror and be proud."
