# The Right Way to Segment Your Content

## Summary
Argues against one-size-fits-all targeting/retargeting, framing segmentation as
understanding what different audience slices are struggling with and want. Cites
segmentation making businesses 60% more likely to understand customer challenges and
130% more likely to know customer intentions. Gives four tactics: (1) treat messaging
as a sequence, not a single interaction — e.g. sending a generic email, then branching
into two different follow-ups depending on how the recipient interacted with the
first; (2) build buyer personas, citing HubSpot data that effective personas make
websites 2–5x more effective, and noting free persona templates are easy to find via
search; (3) use social platforms' native targeting — Facebook targeting by
likes/demographics/interests/location, LinkedIn filtering by industry/company size;
(4) diversify cross-channel presence by platform demographics, citing 43% of TikTok's
global audience being 18–24 and 27.5% of Instagram users being 25–34. Downplays the
need for heavily customized copy per segment, arguing the more important lever is
what content/journey is shown to which behavioral segment (buyer vs. non-buyer,
opened vs. not-opened, clicked vs. not-clicked), and notes most marketing automation/
ad platforms/CMSs already support easy segmentation.
