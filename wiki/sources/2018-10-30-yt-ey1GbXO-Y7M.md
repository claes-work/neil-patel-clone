# How to Get More Traffic by Blogging LESS | 4 (Sneaky) Tips

## Summary

Solo tactical. Neil's counter-intuitive claim: he **cut blogging from 7×/week to 1×/week and
traffic went UP** — from **800,000 visitors/month in January to 1.6 million/month** in under a
year. The lesson: **more content ≠ more traffic**; Google won't rank you just for volume — you
must **promote**, and daily blogging leaves no time (and no goodwill) to promote or email. Blogging
weekly frees time to market each post (he notes traffic **spikes every Tuesday**, his usual publish
day, sometimes **90,000+ visitors in a single day**). His **80/20 promotion process**, four tactics:
(1) **Email your list on every post** — possible now that he doesn't blog daily (daily emails would
feel like spam); send a **customized** email (post title in subject, what it covers, how it helps,
where to read). Collect emails free with **Hello Bar**. (2) **Browser push notifications via
Subscribers.com** (free) — claims **40,000+ visitors/month** (not all unique). (3) **Outreach to
sites/people you cited** — email sources you linked/stat-quoted ("I mentioned you… love your
content"); a **10–30% share rate**. Use **BuzzSumo** ("view shares") to find who shared similar
articles and email them too. (4) **Work your headlines** — **8 of 10 read the headline but only 2 of
10 click through**; write **5–10 headline options**, pick the most appealing, aim for **~5–7 words
(6 ideal)** to avoid truncation in Google, and **evoke curiosity** ("The 7 benefits of green tea —
number six will shock you"). Rule of thumb: **spend ~1 hour writing, ~4 hours promoting** (80/20).
