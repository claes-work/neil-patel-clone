# This is the best way to hit your revenue goals ★

## Summary
An unusually personal, reflective video on what motivates Neil versus what motivates most people he
works with. Neil says he's had five co-founders across his entrepreneurial career and that NP
Digital, his latest company, has 700 employees with a projection of ~900 by end of year. He
frames his own drive as pushing revenue "as a sport" rather than for personal enrichment — he
explicitly disclaims caring about money personally (citing his plain white t-shirts and Honda
Odyssey as evidence of low personal consumption) and states that he and his wife plan to donate
essentially everything they don't need, deliberately not leaving an inheritance ("my kids can go
earn it, just like everyone else"). He says only one person he's worked with shares this pure
number-chasing motivation — his brother-in-law, who Neil notes still lives in the same house
despite having much more money now. Since most people (including his co-founders) aren't wired that
way, Neil says the practical lesson for a founder/leader is to identify and lean into *other*
people's personal material goals — bigger homes, cars, being able to fund causes — as the lever that
keeps them motivated to keep pushing revenue, rather than assuming everyone shares his own
"just hit a number" drive. He names three co-founders/colleagues and their specific goals: Mike Kamo
(building an eight-figure home in Las Vegas, with a possible future home in San Diego), "Caio"
(building a million-plus-dollar home), and Nick Roshan (restoring/collecting old cars). He also
describes his wife's motivation as cause-driven — funding large families' needs, nutritious
food/clean water, and eye surgeries — as their shared family "why." Closes with an explicit ethical
caveat: use this goal-pushing tactic responsibly, never pushing someone toward an unaffordable goal
(e.g., a home) that could hurt their family financially.
