# Effective Growth Hacking Tips to Increase Your Traffic

## Summary
Interview opening with the same cold-open line as [[2021-07-31-yt-O6-PxcW1dZ4]]
("I'm getting super creative on how I do growth, and I always have been. It's just the
way my mind works.") — these two videos are very likely clipped from the **same
recording session**. New, humanizing personal-life anecdote (voice/appearance data):
Neil describes applying a "growth hacking mentality" to parenting — coming to pick up
his daughter with a toothbrush already in his mouth so she'll want to "brush" his teeth
instead of crying for her mom, shaving 20–30 minutes off the morning routine. Business
content: **Ubersuggest's planned 2021 email-marketing feature** will be permanently free
with unlimited contacts/sends, justified by unit economics (self-reported: ~$20,000 to
send 400 million emails/month) — extends the already-known "give away what's cheap to
acquire users" freemium doctrine with concrete new cost figures. Describes an evolved
referral-incentive idea beyond the standard "invite X people, get a free month" model
(explicitly compares it to Dropbox's referral program): offering higher-value, real-
labor rewards instead — a free SEO site audit or a written blog post from his team —
reasoning that tangible deliverables beat free subscription time. New international-
business insight: **India SMS costs are counterintuitively higher than in the US**
despite lower willingness/ability to pay, which Neil attributes to market economics not
yet aligning; he name-checks **Jio** (India's low-cost mobile carrier) as an admired,
more-extreme version of his own give-it-away-free strategy — giving away the underlying
utility (phone service) rather than a tool, achieving mass adoption via celebrity
endorsement (Amitabh Bachchan named).
