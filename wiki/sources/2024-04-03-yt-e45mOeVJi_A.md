# 3 new email marketing rules from Google and Yahoo, Google's new AI algorithm update, and more

## Summary
Three segments, mostly undiarized. (1) Google and Yahoo's 2024 bulk-sender email rules for anyone sending 5,000+ emails/day: spam-complaint rate must stay under 0.3% (ideally under 0.1%), SPF/DKIM/DMARC authentication is required, and one-click unsubscribe requests must be honored within 2 days. Framed as a net-positive forcing function ("marketers tend to abuse things until they get bad") that rewards senders who already send valuable (not purely promotional) content. (2) A first-party experiment across 68 websites / 744 articles (half AI-written, half human-written per site) tracking organic-traffic-per-article over 5 months: AI content converged on roughly 52 visits/article vs. 283 for human content by month 5 — a **5.44x** human advantage — attributed to Google's "helpful content" signal favoring higher perceived quality rather than penalizing AI per se; programmatic SEO (cited: TripAdvisor, Expedia, Zillow) still works but leans on domain authority, and the AI-assisted content on the Marketing School site itself was said to be accelerating post-update. (3) A tangent reacting to a Pepsi ad accidentally featuring a Coke can (bad), contrasted with a prior Pepsi Halloween ad (good, clear brand focal point), plus a SmartBear blog post (author: Jason Cohen) arguing AI-era startup dynamics favor incumbents (Google/Microsoft/OpenAI) over disruptors because incumbents already own distribution + proprietary data + capital for compute, unlike prior "innovator's dilemma" tech cycles (transistor radio analogy).

## Key claims
- **Google/Yahoo's 2024 bulk-sender rules** (effective for 5,000+ emails/day): spam rate must stay under ~0.3% (target under 0.1%), SPF/DKIM/DMARC required, unsubscribe requests honored within 2 days. `(dated 2024-04-03; third-party regulatory change, not opinion)`
- **A 68-website / 744-article, 5-month first-party experiment found human-written content earned 5.44x more average monthly organic traffic per article than AI-written content by month 5** (52 vs. 283 visits/article), with the gap attributed to perceived content quality rather than an explicit AI penalty. `(claim, dated 2024-04-03; attribution: uncertain — undiarized, but consistent with Neil's established first-party SEO-experiment body of work and NP Digital/Ubersuggest data access — likely Neil)` ★ recurring stat, cross-referenced in later 2024-04 episodes (e.g. 2024-04-11 batch)
- **Programmatic SEO still works but requires domain authority and (increasingly) human modification of AI drafts** — cited long-standing examples TripAdvisor, Expedia, Zillow. `(opinion, dated 2024-04-03; attribution: uncertain — undiarized)`
- **AI-assisted content (vs. pure programmatic AI templates) was reportedly accelerating in traffic on the Marketing School site after a recent Google update.** `(self-reported, dated 2024-04-03)`
- **SmartBear's Jason Cohen thesis (relayed, not authored by the hosts): AI-era startups face a harder incumbent-advantage dynamic than prior tech disruptions** (mobile, web) because incumbents (Google, Microsoft, OpenAI) already have distribution, proprietary data, and the capital required for compute — the classic "innovator's dilemma" transistor-radio pattern is argued not to repeat this cycle. `(third-party argument, relayed and endorsed; dated 2024-04-03)`
