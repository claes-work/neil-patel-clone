# 4 Ways to Sell High Ticket Services Online | Learn to Make BIG Ticket Sales!

## Summary
Neil outlines four tactics for selling high-priced (thousands-of-dollars) products and services online, arguing that expensive offers require more trust-building than cheap ones. He recommends capturing emails to build a relationship over time through drip content, case studies, and success stories; driving prospects to educational webinars that warm them up before a pitch; using long-form (or short-form) sales pages built on storytelling with integrated case studies and testimonials; and following up with leads and buyers via a sales rep to upsell or close them over the phone. He cites his own website's webinar registration options and his neilpatel.com consulting page as examples, and mentions Hello Bar as a free tool for email capture.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-22, Neil states:
- Selling something expensive differs from selling something cheap because buyers need to be comfortable with and get to know the seller first (as of 2017-10-22).
- The first step for selling high-ticket offers is collecting emails, since without them you cannot build a relationship with website visitors (as of 2017-10-22).
- Continually emailing and dripping subscribers with information, case studies, and success stories makes them more comfortable and more likely to buy (as of 2017-10-22).
- Hello Bar can be used to collect emails; it is simple and free and offers sliders and pop-ups (as of 2017-10-22).
- The second strategy is driving people to a webinar, where educating them for 30 to 45 minutes before selling makes them much more likely to buy (as of 2017-10-22).
- A webinar option on his home page lets people get to know him for 45 minutes to an hour (as of 2017-10-22).
- The third strategy is using long sales pages (or general sales pages) built on storytelling; his neilpatel.com consulting page tells a story integrating case studies, results, and testimonials to make people more comfortable and more likely to buy (as of 2017-10-22).
- Long-form or short-form storytelling sales pages must always include case studies and testimonials, or people are less likely to convert into a lead or paying customer (as of 2017-10-22).
- The fourth tactic is following up with leads or online buyers via a sales rep, who can upsell buyers into more products/services or get a lead on the phone with a customized presentation to close them into a customer (as of 2017-10-22).
