# Google's Helpful Content Update

## Summary
Explains Google's "helpful content update" (2022) as a response to content written
purely for search engines that "provides little to no value" to readers. Paraphrases
Google's own stated guidance in two halves: "focus on writing content for people"
(does it demonstrate first-hand expertise, leave the reader satisfied, help them
achieve their goal) versus "don't focus on writing content for search engines" (mass
production across unrelated topics, thin summarization, heavy automation). Distills
four practical responses: (1) niche down — focus content on a specific
industry/expertise rather than many unrelated topics; (2) make content "executable"
with a clear takeaway, illustrated by his own habit of closing articles with a labeled
"Conclusion" section summarizing key points; (3) continually refresh content at least
once a year, using competitor gap-analysis (search your target keyword, see what
higher-ranking pages do differently) as the refresh method; (4) add a "secret sauce" of
personal experience/unique knowledge rather than regurgitating what's already out
there, which he ties to earning social shares and backlinks. Closes with an NP Digital
plug, self-describing the agency as "Awarded Agency of the Year."

## Key claims
- Paraphrases Google's helpful-content-update guidance (quality signals: first-hand
  expertise, reader satisfaction, focus/niche, avoiding automation-heavy mass content)
  — attributed to Google, not self-reported (dated 2022-10-20 per this video).
- States his personal content practice: labeling a closing "Conclusion" section in his
  articles to summarize key takeaways for skimmers (self-reported habit).
- Recommends refreshing content "at least once a year" and using competitor-gap
  analysis (searching target keywords and studying what outranks you) as the refresh
  method (tactical prescription, dated 2022-10-20).
- Self-describes NP Digital as "Awarded Agency of the Year" (self-reported, no
  awarding body named).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Don't assume that if you write a piece of content it'll keep getting traffic. As things get stale, your traffic will go down. It's sad, but it's reality.

> What do you know about the others don't? What have you experienced that others haven't? ... If you're just regurgitating what everyone else is already saying, then there's nothing unique.
