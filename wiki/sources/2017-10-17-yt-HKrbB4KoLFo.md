# 3 Effective Shopping Cart Abandonment Strategies That Can Boost Sales by 10-20%

## Summary
Neil addresses shopping cart abandonment, where many people add items and start to check out but leave without buying, costing you sales. He lays out three tactics to recover those potential customers. First, use a two-step checkout process: ask for name and email (or a sign-up) first, then collect credit card and shipping details on a second page — this way, if someone abandons on the second step, you already have their email and can follow up to get them to finish the transaction. Second, use remarketing: after someone adds items but doesn't buy, show them your products and services as they browse the web, and when they return, take them straight back to the cart to complete the purchase. Third, continually optimize the checkout page to maximize conversion rate so fewer people abandon; he recommends Crazy Egg, whose heatmap shows which fields cause drop-off and how far people scroll, and whose WYSIWYG editor and A/B testing let you validate changes against data rather than gut feel. Following these three steps, he argues, gets more people to complete their carts and makes you more money.
