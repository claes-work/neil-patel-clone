# 7 Underrated Marketing Lessons

## Summary
Seven "lessons I didn't take seriously enough" framed as personal mistakes: (1) TAM
(Total Addressable Market) matters more than execution — go after 100B+/trillion-dollar
markets; (2) every channel eventually gets crowded/less effective, so go omnichannel
rather than over-relying on one; (3) branding compounds over time and requires patience
(Nike/Visa/Mastercard/Amex/Tesla examples); (4) join new platforms early even if some
(e.g., Clubhouse) go nowhere — it's a numbers game; (5) marketing results take years,
not immediate wins; (6) traffic isn't everything — repeats the KISSmetrics-vs-Mixpanel
comparison (KISSmetrics had 10x Mixpanel's traffic but a sub-$50M valuation vs.
Mixpanel's ~$800M); (7) the best marketing is a genuinely great product/service,
which generates organic word of mouth rather than requiring continuous paid traffic.

## Key claims
- Restates the KISSmetrics-vs-Mixpanel valuation gap (KISSmetrics ~10x the traffic,
  sub-$50M valuation, vs. Mixpanel's ~$800M) as a "traffic isn't everything" cautionary
  example — same figures as prior corpus entries (self-reported).
- States a personal regret pattern across his early companies: markets that were too
  small structurally capped growth regardless of marketing quality (as of 2022-09-03,
  self-reported).
- Recommends adopting new marketing platforms early, accepting some (e.g., Clubhouse)
  will not pay off, as an asymmetric numbers-game bet (as of 2022-09-03).
