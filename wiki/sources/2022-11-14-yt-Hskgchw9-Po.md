# Amazon SEO - Tutorial For Beginners

## Summary
Introduces Amazon SEO as distinct from Google SEO: while Google SEO doesn't directly
make Google money (absent AdSense), Amazon profits from every sale, so Amazon's
ranking algorithm is explicitly incentivized to rank listings with the best sales
velocity higher. Frames two core Amazon-ranking components: keyword relevance (does
the query match what shoppers expect the product to be) and sales velocity (selling
faster/at a higher rate than competitors). Gives six tactical tips: (1) Optimize the
title using Amazon's own category-specific field recommendations (brand, model,
product type, size/quantity, etc. — gives TV, video-game, bedding, bath-towel, and
tableware examples). (2) Optimize the description to expand on (not repeat) the title,
foreground benefits, optionally tell an origin story, and keep it concise/readable. (3)
Write bullets focused one-benefit-per-bullet, noting Amazon's 200-character-per-bullet
limit. (4) Use multiple high-quality (ideally studio-quality) photos from different
angles, including detail shots like nutrition information for food products. (5) Use
Ubersuggest's Keyword Ideas (Related/Questions/Prepositions tabs) to source popular
keywords, same workflow demoed in other episodes. (6) Focus on generating genuine
reviews (not fake ones) and use negative-review feedback to improve the product;
suggests early discounting to help bootstrap review volume. Closes with an NP Digital
plug.

## Key claims
- States Amazon's algorithm favors higher sales-velocity listings because Amazon earns
  a cut of every sale, contrasting this incentive structure with Google's (self-
  reported analysis, dated 2022-11-14).
- Cites Amazon's own per-category title-field guidance (brand/model/type/size/etc.) as
  the basis for title optimization (attributed to Amazon, dated 2022-11-14).
- States Amazon bullet points are capped at "200 characters per bullet" (platform-limit
  claim, dated 2022-11-14).
- Recommends early-stage discounting as a way to accelerate review accumulation
  (tactical recommendation, dated 2022-11-14) — explicitly distinguished from fake
  reviews, which he warns against.
