# The Biggest Social Media Opportunity That You Aren't Leveraging

## Summary
Argues LinkedIn is under-leveraged: cites (per "Kinsta") that only ~3 million of
LinkedIn's 830M+ users create content (<1%), versus ~87% of Instagram's ~1.2B users
(per "True List") — framing LinkedIn as low-competition for organic reach because the
platform "doesn't have the luxury to be picky" about what it shows. Gives a four-part
LinkedIn content playbook: write stories rather than link out (citing "my friend Brian
Dean" as an example who keeps content native to the platform); post native video at
least 3x/week, under 10 minutes, citing his own average video getting "over 13,000
impressions" on LinkedIn versus lower engagement for the same video on Facebook/YouTube
despite 1M+ followers there; use PDF carousels (his example: a 5-step success carousel
opening with "start a blog," including a stat that email marketing earns "$42 on
average" per dollar spent); and self-promote your own offers without hesitation. Closes
recommending LinkedIn ads for tightly-targeted B2B use cases (his example: targeting
VP-of-Marketing/CMO titles for webinar promotion).
