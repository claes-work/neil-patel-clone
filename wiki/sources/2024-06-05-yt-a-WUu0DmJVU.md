# What ChatGPT-4o means for marketers, What signal-based selling is, and LinkedIn playbooks

## Summary
Neil describes early hands-on use of GPT-4o: translating live conversation with his mother
(English⇄Chinese) while identifying plants during a walk at the Huntington Library, and imagining
future real-time multilingual conversation and AI tutoring for kids. He also recounts using Gemini
(not ChatGPT) to prep for the Bryan Johnson interview — asking it, since Google owns YouTube and
therefore has access to Johnson's back catalog of interviews, to generate "unorthodox" questions;
rates 4 of 7 AI-suggested questions as genuinely good. His stated view on AI's marketing use: it is
excellent for research/data-analysis/informed decision-making, and poor-to-actively-bad when used
merely to mass-generate content — "one of the worst things AI can do." The episode pivots to
"signal-based selling" (a rebrand of account-based marketing/ABM): identifying high-intent visitors
within a large top-of-funnel audience via engagement signals, then routing sales outreach to them.
Neil discloses a specific NP Digital strategy shift: traffic fell at least 2x from its peak while
revenue grew 3-4x over the same period, because the agency deliberately stopped chasing broad/
generic-keyword traffic (which converted poorly — "I just found out what SEO is, what can you do for
$100/month?") in favor of ultra-long-tail, Enterprise-specific traffic; multi-language blog
structuring drove disproportionately high lead value despite low lead volume. The episode closes
with two LinkedIn/ABM "playbooks" credited to Adam Robinson (Retention.com CEO): a thought-leadership
ad promoting a founder's organic LinkedIn post to ungated content (1,600 click-throughs cited), and
an enrichment-tool chain (Retention.com/an enrichment tool → Clay.com firmographic/technographic
enrichment → GPT-drafted outreach email → Smartlead sending) that produced one of the two example
results — Michael's campaign booked a demo within the first 15 emails sent; Taylor's campaign is
credited with 20% of Retention B2B's signups and an 11% conversion rate.

## Key claims
- **GPT-4o real-time multimodal use case, personally tested:** used it to identify plants while
  walking with his mother at the Huntington Library and to live-translate English⇄Chinese
  conversation with her; flags that voice-accent customization within the app "doesn't work well"
  yet. (Neil, 2024-06-05; personal anecdote)
- **AI's best marketing use is research and data analysis, not content generation.** "AI can help
  you with content creation — that's one of the basic things AI can do, and that's one of the worst
  things AI can do." Marketers should use AI to analyze the data they already have (most don't) and
  make decisions from it, rather than to mass-produce content. (Neil, 2024-06-05)
- **Used Google Gemini (leveraging Google's ownership of YouTube) to generate interview-prep
  questions for the Bryan Johnson interview**, rating 4 of 7 AI-suggested "unorthodox" questions as
  genuinely good — cites one example: "if you reversed your biological age to 18, what impact would
  you want to have on the world, beyond physical/cognitive health — what about emotional health?"
  (Neil, 2024-06-05; personal workflow disclosure)
- **NP Digital deliberately traded traffic volume for revenue by targeting Enterprise-specific,
  ultra-long-tail queries instead of broad/generic keywords:** overall traffic fell "at least 2x"
  from its peak while revenue rose "3-4x" over the same period; generic-keyword leads ("I just found
  out what SEO is") converted at a "pathetic" close rate, while updating and doubling down on the
  content actually driving revenue (identified via lead-source-to-content mapping) — including a
  multi-language blog restructuring aimed at Enterprise questions — drove the smallest share of
  leads but the largest share of revenue, even at the cost of losing rankings on abandoned terms.
  (Neil, 2024-06-05; self-reported, NP Digital-specific)
- **"Signal-based selling" is a rebrand of account-based marketing (ABM):** among millions of site
  visitors, a small subset fits the buyer ICP; tracking their engagement signals lets sales reach
  out to the right subset rather than blanket content production. (Neil confirms/reframes Eric's
  framing, 2024-06-05)
- **Two cited LinkedIn/ABM "playbooks" (Adam Robinson/Retention.com, third-party case studies):**
  (1) Samantha McKenna's thought-leadership-ad play — promoting a founder's organic LinkedIn post via
  paid thought-leadership ads to ungated content, reportedly driving 1,600 click-throughs on one
  campaign; (2) Michael Leben/Taylor Haren's Clay-based enrichment chain — site visitors enriched via
  Retention.com/an enrichment tool, pushed to Clay.com for firmographic/technographic enrichment and
  GPT-drafted, page-view-personalized emails, sent via Smartlead across multiple sending domains;
  cited results: one demo booked within the first 15 emails, and one contributor credited with 20% of
  Retention B2B's signups at an 11% conversion rate. (Eric Siu relaying third-party case studies,
  2024-06-05; unverified, self-reported by the named practitioners)
