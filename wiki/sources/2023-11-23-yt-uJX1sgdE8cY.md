# How to spend on marketing the right way?

## Summary
Recorded in "a tough economic period" (Q4 2023), Neil contrasts the prior era's
"growth at all costs" mentality (fine to lose money if you scale fast) with a
current, more disciplined approach: spend on marketing in a slow, steady, profitable
way, treating experimentation with new/less-competitive channels as necessary but not
requiring every dollar spent to be immediately profitable. His central warning is
against abrupt spend scaling (e.g., jumping from $1,000/day to $1M/day, or $10K/month
organic to $200K/month) — regardless of company size or cash reserves, scaling too
fast breaks marketing efficiency and burns money with little ROI; the fix is ramping
gradually. Eric frames the same shift in macro/startup terms: the "easy mode" era of
2020-2022 (pre-privacy-changes, cheap VC growth capital, raising rounds to boost
valuation) is over, replaced by a need for efficiency, creativity, and willingness to
cut what isn't working.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Contrasts pre-2022ish "growth at all costs" marketing mentality (acceptable to lose
  money for growth) with the current, more disciplined mindset: spend on marketing
  slowly, steadily, and profitably in any economy — you don't need every dollar
  profitable, but you do need a methodical approach to which new channels you test.
  (Neil, 2023-11-23) — paid-ads / agency-entrepreneurship.
- Core warning: scaling marketing spend abruptly (e.g., $100-1,000/day to
  $1M/day, or $10K/month to $200K/month organic) burns money and produces little-to
  -no ROI regardless of company size or cash reserves — scale itself is "what tends
  to break marketing," and the fix is ramping slowly and steadily.
  (Neil, 2023-11-23) — paid-ads.
- States it's "not hard to run profitable Google ads or Facebook ads or to do SEO" at
  small scale — the actual difficulty is scaling those same profitable tactics up
  (ranking for millions of keywords, or spending $1-2M/month across many countries)
  without breaking their efficiency. (Neil, 2023-11-23) — paid-ads /
  agency-entrepreneurship.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> If you spend a dollar and you lose $2, why would you spend the dollar? ... We
> believe that in any kind of economy, the best way to scale marketing and spend on
> marketing is in a slow and steady and a profitable way.

> I went from spending, you know, $1,000 a day to now, or $100 a day to like a
> million dollars a day. It just doesn't work that way.

> Scale is what tends to break marketing. And you can't just scale up and you can't
> just spend, you got to slowly ease into it, and if you don't, you'll just find that
> you're burning a lot of money.
