# On-page and technical SEO Part 2 - SEO Unlocked - Free SEO Course with Neil Patel

## Summary
Part of the "SEO Unlocked" free course; despite the "off-page SEO" framing in the
opening line, the lesson is a technical/on-page walkthrough of user-engagement
metrics and mobile/site-structure factors. Centers on "dwell time" as a composite of
session duration, bounce rate, and SERP click-through rate (CTR), and walks through
measuring each in Google Analytics and Google Search Console. Gives industry-benchmark
bounce-rate ranges by site type (retail 20-40%, landing pages 70-90%, portals 10-30%,
service sites 10-30%, content sites 40-60%, lead-gen 30-50%) and a general rule (0-40%
good, 40-60% fair, 60-100% poor), plus a target of 2+ minutes average session duration.
Covers fixing high-exit pages via Analytics' exit-pages report, improving CTR via
title/meta-description quality (cross-referencing an earlier lesson on titles) and via
targeting long-tail keywords (cites WordStream: long-tail average CTR/conversion
context vs. head terms). Covers mobile: Google's separate mobile/desktop indexes,
responsive design, avoiding Flash, and the AMP framework for faster mobile blog
loading. Ends on internal-linking/site-structure as a driver of Google sitelinks and
crawlability, with the caveat to link for user experience, not just for Google.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-05-14, Neil said dwell time (session duration + bounce rate + CTR) is an
  under-discussed ranking factor, and that Google Analytics session duration should be
  targeted at "at least two minutes."
- As of 2020-05-14, Neil gave industry bounce-rate benchmarks: retail sites 20-40%,
  landing pages 70-90%, portals (MSN/Yahoo) 10-30%, service sites 10-30%, content sites
  40-60%, lead-gen sites 30-50%; general scale 0-40% good, 40-60% fair, 60-100% poor.
- As of 2020-05-14, Neil said new websites often show artificially low bounce rates
  because the owner is visiting their own site repeatedly, and that bounce rate
  typically rises once real traffic arrives, then should be brought back down over time.
- As of 2020-05-14, Neil cited a Google Search Console average CTR benchmark of "2.2%"
  as a general figure to improve against (unclear if this is a stated industry average
  or an example figure from a specific account).
- As of 2020-05-14, Neil recommended the AMP framework specifically for faster mobile
  blog-article loading, and flagged that Google maintains two separate indexes
  (mobile and desktop) that can rank the same site differently.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Don't do stuff just for Google, put the user first because Google in the long run will rank the websites at the top that are best for users."
