# An Easy Way to Rank For Competitive Keywords (Without Being a Professional SEO)

## Summary
A solo screen-share SEO tutorial: Neil demonstrates that many competitive, high-volume
head terms (his live examples: "Voice over IP"/"VoIP," "SEO," less consistently
"insurance") are dominated on page one by "what is X" / "how does X work" informational
articles, even from non-giant sites (Nextiva alongside Wikipedia, FCC.gov, PC Magazine).
He argues this is because Google favors ranking informational content for informational-
intent queries regardless of the searcher's ultimate commercial intent, and walks through
a real example (Nextiva's "What is Voice over IP" article) to show how such a page can
still convert: embedded CTAs, floating header CTA, internal links to product pages, and
clickable in-content product links.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-11-09, observed pattern (via live SERP review, not a formal study): a
  meaningful share of competitive head-term SERPs are topped by "what is" / "how does X
  work" informational articles, even displacing purely commercial pages, which Neil
  frames as a repeatable content-strategy opportunity for ranking competitive terms
  without needing to out-authority every commercial competitor (paraphrase, self-
  reported observation, explicitly caveated as "it doesn't work for every single thing
  out there").
- As of 2020-11-09, his recommended conversion mechanism for informational "what is"
  pages: a floating/sticky CTA in the header, embedded product/CTA images within the
  body, and internal links from informational content into commercial product or
  lead-gen pages (paraphrase, tactical pattern demonstrated on Nextiva's article, a
  third-party example not built by Neil).
- Uses Ubersuggest to source live search-volume/CPC data during the demo (e.g., "Voice
  over IP" at ~40,000 monthly US searches, ~$34 CPC) — consistent with the corpus's
  recurring Ubersuggest-as-primary-research-tool pattern.

## Notable verbatim quotes
