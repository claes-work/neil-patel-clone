# How to Use Advanced Features in Google Analytics - Module 06 - Lesson 3 - SEO Unlocked

## Summary
SEO Unlocked module 6, lesson 3: intermediate Google Analytics workflows, opening
with a W. Edwards Deming data quote. Covers uncovering **"not provided" keywords**
(Google's October 2011 privacy change hid most organic keyword data) via an Analytics
filter (Admin → Create View → Filters, with an explicit warning never to apply the
filter to the main/all-traffic view, as it destroys unrecoverable data) plus linking
Search Console to Analytics as an easier alternative source of query data. Explains
**"dark traffic"**: apparent "direct" traffic (normally expected around 20%, rarely
above ~38-40%) that is often actually unlabeled Google/search traffic; cites a
**Groupon experiment** (blocking Google for a few days) that showed both organic
*and* direct traffic dropped sharply, demonstrating direct-traffic overcounting;
recommends a custom segment ("Dark Traffic": direct traffic excluding the homepage)
to isolate genuinely-direct visits. Frames Analytics as the ROI-measurement layer for
SEO — traffic isn't the goal unless the campaign is explicitly brand awareness;
revenue/leads/sales are — and recommends **annotations** (flagging the exact day a
change was made) and **year-over-year comparison** to causally link changes to
results. Also covers content/landing-page performance reports (Behavior → Site
Content/Landing Pages), social-traffic-by-channel and referral reports (Acquisition),
campaign/PPC reporting (flags that Analytics conversion counts may not perfectly match
backend order counts — "not always match up 100%," use as a directional signal), and
a Search-Console-to-Analytics feedback loop: find pages with high impressions/low CTR,
rewrite the title/meta description, then track the ~30-day impact via URL Inspection.
Recommends the tool **ClickFlow** for title rewriting/testing.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-06-11, Neil said direct traffic should typically be around 20% of total
  traffic and rarely more than 38-40%; higher shares often indicate mislabeled
  organic/search traffic rather than genuine direct visits.
- As of 2020-06-11, Neil cited a Groupon experiment (blocking Google for a few days)
  in which both organic search traffic *and* direct traffic dropped sharply — evidence
  that a large share of "direct" traffic is actually unlabeled Google traffic.
- As of 2020-06-11, Neil said Analytics goal-completion/conversion counts don't always
  match backend order data 100% (his example: 100 reported conversions vs. 89 actual
  orders) and should be treated as directionally accurate, not exact.
- As of 2020-06-11, Neil's page-improvement loop: find pages with high impressions but
  low click-through rate in Search Console, rewrite the title tag/meta description,
  then re-check performance via URL Inspection roughly 30 days later.
- As of 2020-06-11, Neil recommended the tool **ClickFlow** for rewriting and testing
  page titles.
