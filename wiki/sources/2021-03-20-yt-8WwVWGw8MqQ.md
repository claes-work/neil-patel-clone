# Here's What You Need to Outrank Your Competition

## Summary
Solo tactical video demonstrating a live competitor-analysis workflow using HubSpot
(vs. neilpatel.com) as the running example. Walks through: (1) domain-level vs.
URL-level backlink/domain-authority comparison in Ubersuggest, warning that raw
backlink counts can be misleading if concentrated in one referring domain; (2) content
quality assessment (depth, images, stats, video); (3) UX review of the competitor page
(pop-ups, above-the-fold clutter); (4) brand-query volume as a Google trust signal,
illustrated by comparing HubSpot's ("$20B+ company") brand-search volume against
Neil's own (a stated ~14x gap); (5) Ubersuggest's SEO Analyzer score for a
competitor's whole-site health; (6) a qualitative step — surveying real users/customers
(e.g. via usertesting.com) about what a competitor, or the viewer's own site, could do
better. Frames SEO as dependent on branding, backlinks, product quality, and UX
together, not backlinks/content alone.

## Key claims
- States brand-query volume ("how many people are typing in a company's name") is a
  trust signal Google weighs, and estimates HubSpot outranks him partly because it
  gets roughly ~14x his brand-query volume as a "$20B+ company" (paraphrase,
  self-reported estimate, dated to this recording).
- Recommends a four-part competitor-outrank workflow: domain + URL backlink/DA
  comparison in Ubersuggest, content-quality/depth comparison, UX audit (pop-ups, fold
  placement), and direct user surveys of what a competitor's (or your own) experience
  is missing (paraphrase, tactical framework).
- States SEO "is dependent on a lot of external factors like branding, backlinks" and
  that you can't manufacture a large backlink profile or brand "out of thin air"
  without a genuinely good product/service underneath (paraphrase, belief, reinforces
  the existing brand-is-the-moat / distribution-is-the-moat framing).
