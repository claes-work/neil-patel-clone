# 5 Underrated SEO Lessons

## Summary
Five personal SEO lessons from "22 years" of SEO (self-reports age 37, started at 15,
i.e. as of 2022-09-12): (1) updating content frequently beats building more links for
established sites (Wikipedia analogy repeated) — new sites should still prioritize
link-building; (2) shortcuts aren't worth it — retells the "Battlefield Bypass" story:
bought an expired national-park domain with strong natural authoritative links, converted
it into an online-casino site, ranked top-3 for "online casino" within months, then got
manually penalized by Google and lost all rankings; frames it as an early "shortcut
mentality" mistake he'd reverse if he could; (3) brand queries help rankings — working
with large corporations at NP Digital taught him that high-volume, high-CTR brand search
signals a strong brand to Google's algorithm, so he now focuses more on brand-building
(says it takes "well over five years"); (4) the fastest way to grow SEO traffic is going
global — retells a Google-employee tip that most searches aren't in English and many
languages lack sufficient content, prompting a transcription (not pure translation)
program; US traffic is now "25% or less" of his total; (5) don't rely purely on SEO — he
used to believe SEO was the best channel, now believes no single channel is "the best"
and diversifies across all channels that work, partly as an algorithm-risk hedge.
