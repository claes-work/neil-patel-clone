# Copy the 5 Winning Strategies Behind My 238% Traffic Growth in 2019 ★

## Summary
Solo case-study video: Neil says he **more than doubled neilpatel.com traffic in 2019 vs.
2018 — 238% growth** — via five simple strategies plus relentless execution. (1) **Pillar
pages and content clusters** replace the old habit of writing 20–40 articles on one topic,
which confuses Google about which to rank while the articles compete for the same terms; his
proof point is **Moz's Beginner's Guide to SEO** — a main introduction linking down to
subsections that link back up — and the fact that Moz ranks #1 for "SEO" in the US. (2)
**Update old content at scale.** He discloses his actual operation: he personally writes only
**four blog posts a month** (five in a five-week month) while a **team of three updates 90
articles a month** (~30 each) — and states plainly that updating old content produced more
traffic gain than writing new content, his single biggest gain of the year, invoking Wikipedia
again as the model of continuously improved pages. (3) **Land and expand** — since no amount
of skill guarantees a piece ranks, take the winners, pull the queries from Google Search
Console, and fold the relevant ones into the title tag, description and new paragraphs, then
push further with Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas report. He repeatedly gates this on user value:
don't keyword-stuff, and "if it makes sense for a searcher but not a user, do not do it."
(4) **International expansion** — the origin story: years ago a **friend who works at Google**
told him most Google users don't search in English, so he began translating his content,
starting with **Brazil**, then Spanish across Latin America, then German, French, Italian and
more languages than he counts; as a result **the United States is now less than 25% of his
traffic**. He attributes his lead over competitors not to better marketing, product or content
but to this move, and notes foreign markets have less competition, so algorithm updates hurt
less. (5) **Build a popular free tool** — "tools is a new form of content marketing";
**Ubersuggest is the number-one traffic section of neilpatel.com**. For those who can't fund
a tool, buy a ~$10 niche tool from **CodeCanyon**, put your name on it and host it — which he
says he did on **QuickSprout** and earlier on his second-ever website, **ACS**, his original
marketing ad agency, where he built an SEO analyzer. His verdict: a tool is the most viral,
easiest traffic you will get.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil claimed he more than doubled neilpatel.com traffic in 2019 vs. 2018 — **238% growth** — from five simple strategies plus relentless execution, not a complicated one.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil said writing 10–40 separate articles on one topic confuses Google ("which one should we rank?") because they compete for the same terms, interlink and hold backlinks; **pillar pages with content clusters** are the replacement.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil cited **Moz's Beginner's Guide to SEO** as the model pillar structure — main intro, linked subsections, subsections linking back up — and said Moz ranks #1 for "SEO" in the United States because of it.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil disclosed his content operation: he personally writes **four blog posts a month** (five in five-week months), while a **team of three updates 90 articles a month**, roughly 30 per person.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil said **updating old content produced more traffic gains than writing new content** and called it one of his biggest gains of 2019 — citing Wikipedia's constant updating as the reason it performs.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil said there is no guarantee any article ranks regardless of SEO skill — some pieces he expected to do well simply didn't.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil's **land and expand** procedure was: identify pages that already perform, pull their driving queries from Google Search Console, add the genuinely relevant ones into the title tag, description and additional paragraphs, then use Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas report for further related terms to expand into.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil gated expansion on user benefit: don't stuff keywords, only add them where it makes the article more thorough — "**if it makes sense for a searcher but not a user, do not do it, it's all about putting the user first**."
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil's rationale for land-and-expand was that Google and users already like these pages, so expanding them lifts rankings for both the existing and similar terms.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil credited **international expansion** to advice from a **friend who works at Google** (referred to as "she"), who told him the majority of Google users don't search in English and suggested translating his content by region.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil said his translation rollout began with **Brazil**, then Spanish for all of Latin America, then German, French, Italian "and so many languages I don't even count anymore."
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil said **the United States makes up less than 25% of his traffic** as a result of going international — and explicitly denied that his lead over competitors comes from better marketing, product, service or content.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil argued international markets are a wide-open field with less competition, so Google's algorithm updates hurt less there — not because updates don't happen but because you have fewer competitors to lose ground to.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil said "**tools is a new form of content marketing**" and that **Ubersuggest is the number-one traffic section** of the Neil Patel website.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil's low-budget version was buying a ~**$10 tool on CodeCanyon** (licensed to rebrand under your own name) and hosting it — he says he did exactly this on **QuickSprout** and on his second-ever website **"ACS," his original marketing ad agency**, where he added an SEO analyzer.
- As of 2020-01-12, Neil called a tool "the most viral and the easiest traffic you'll get."
