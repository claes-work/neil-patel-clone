# The Hard Truth About Passive Income (Is It Still Possible From Blogging?)

## Summary

Neil debunks the "make money without working" pitch sold in YouTube/Facebook ads (beach,
mountains, fancy house). His verdict: truly passive income "from just your website" is **no
longer possible** — the only people who make easy money on that promise are the ones **selling
the dream** ($1,000 courses). He gives four reasons the old blog→traffic→affiliate model is
dead: (1) **content saturation** — "over a billion blogs… one blog for every seven people," so
Google now ranks the freshest/best and punishes un-updated content; (2) **ad-cost inflation** —
paid arbitrage funnels get out-competed as CPMs rise year over year unless you keep optimizing
upsells/downsells; (3) **luck & hidden effort** — anyone claiming an easy, quick win "put in a
lot of hours" and is hiding it (and if it were that easy, why sell you the formula for $1,000?);
(4) **algorithms** — ranking now can take "one, two years," and Facebook/LinkedIn/Instagram keep
restricting reach, so you must continually adapt (read, experiment, learn) or competitors crush
you. His close: real passive income mostly comes from **investing** the proceeds of hard work
(e.g. real estate), not from a website that someone will eventually outrank/outspend.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-05-11, Neil said truly passive income from just a website/blog is **not really
  possible** anymore; the only "easy" money is made by people **selling others the dream** of
  passive income (the $1,000-course pitch).
- As of 2019-05-11, Neil cited **~1 billion blogs vs. ~7 billion people ("one blog for every
  seven people")** as the reason Google ranks the freshest/best content and demotes pages that
  aren't updated. (Recurring stat across this era.)
- As of 2019-05-11, Neil said **ad costs rise year over year**, so affiliate/paid-funnel
  arbitrage stops working unless you keep fine-tuning the funnel, conversions, and upsells.
- As of 2019-05-11, Neil said "anything worthwhile takes time" — a quick win with little effort
  means either the person is hiding their real work or "duping you"; ranking can now take
  **one to two years**, not three-to-six months.
- As of 2019-05-11, Neil said the passive income he actually sees comes from people who worked
  a core business or 30–40 corporate years, then **invested** (typically real estate) — passive
  income "is typically going to happen from investments," not a website.
