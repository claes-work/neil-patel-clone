# 5 Mistakes (Almost) Every New Marketer Makes

## Summary
Five common new-marketer mistakes, framed around the self-reported/cited stat
that 95% of people fail online, lose money, or never make a penny from internet
marketing (per LinkedIn, as cited, dated 2023-03-09), with a personal aside that
he's made "so many" mistakes he "can't even count them on both hands" and keeps
making them (self-reported). Mistakes: (1) lack of research — cites the market
research industry growing from $75B (2021) to a projected $90.79B (2025) (as
cited); argues marketing should address customer problems with solutions rather
than personal preference. (2) Poor brand positioning/lack of omnichannel
consistency — cites brand consistency can increase revenue by 33% (as cited);
relays a named anecdote from "Tony," an Expedia Canada SEO/marketing lead, who
told him going omnichannel (vs. single/dual-channel) with data-driven
per-channel optimization generated at least 10% more ROI per channel
(third-party self-report, not independently verified). (3) Ignoring repeat
customers — cites a 5% increase in retention can raise revenue 25–95% (as
cited); notes Amazon makes the majority of its money from repeat, not new,
customers (as cited/self-reported). (4) Not using an omnichannel approach —
cites 73% of customers prefer shopping across multiple channels (as cited).
(5) Lack of authenticity — cites 88% of customers say authenticity affects brand
preference (as cited); argues UGC/unboxing-style authentic content outperforms
high-production content. Closes with an NP Digital plug citing "Agency of the
Year" among other self-reported awards.
