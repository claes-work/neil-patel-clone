# Amazon's social attribution breakthrough, Apple's iPad Pro apology, Kid gains 241K IG followers (Marketing School)

## Summary
News-roundup episode: an Amazon/Meta/Snap social-checkout attribution partnership, focus/notification
habits, an Eight Sleep product update, a "create an enemy" controversial-marketing pattern (Brian
Johnson vs. AG1, with a Scott Adams/Trump framing), the Apple iPad Pro ad apology, and a viral
"clap for every follower" IG growth story. Neil's contributions are mostly tactical/behavioral
(notification discipline, low personal social-media usage, early-career "crappy Mac" anecdote at
W Promote) rather than framework-level, but they're clear, dated, and voice-rich.

## Key claims
- Neil barely uses social media personally — "my team does it for me" — and keeps his phone fully
  silent (no buzz, no notification banner) for texts, WhatsApp, and email, to protect focus; he
  responds to messages an hour or more later by choice. (Neil, 2024-05-22)
- Neil worked at **W Promote** at age 24–25 and was given "the crappiest Mac in the world," which
  he didn't want to use — cited as a lesson in equipping teams well (Apple's leasing program lets
  a business keep rotating in the newest hardware every ~2–3 years for a flat monthly cost).
  (Neil, 2024-05-22)
- On the iPad Pro "Crush" ad backlash and Tim Cook's apology: strong product + strong brand still
  drives sales even through a PR misstep; the free move in a crisis is simply to apologize and move
  on rather than over-invest in defending the ad. (Neil, 2024-05-22)
- Neil researched the iPad nanotech-glass upgrade before buying and found (via Reddit, then
  corroborating articles) that it's worse for general brightness despite being marketed as
  anti-reflective — a case of using Reddit/forum answers over generic Google results for a
  purchase decision, echoing the AI-search theme from other May 2024 episodes. (Neil, 2024-05-22)
- On the viral "clapped his way to 241K Instagram followers" video: the lesson isn't the specific
  stunt but that with no budget you need something bold and repetitive to get initial attraction —
  same mechanism as MrBeast repeating "Logan Paul" or Patrick Campbell repeating "churn" hundreds of
  times early on. Growing followers is only the start; the harder part is sustaining delivery
  afterward. (Neil, 2024-05-22)
