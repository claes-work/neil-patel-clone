# Mind Blowing Marketing Experiments — Unconventional Tactics Tried by a Marketing Guru

## Summary
★ Landmark-quality financial/lifestyle interview on the **My First Million** podcast (Sam
Parr, Shaan Puri) — first of two uploads from the same conversation (continued in
[[2023-04-22-yt-jI34N2EUHCI]]). ★ **Attributes the "Neil Patel" personal brand with
driving the agency to roughly $30-40M in revenue**, after which word-of-mouth, awards, and
employees bringing their own deals took over as the larger growth engines. ★ **Minority-
business-certification anecdote**: describes learning, initially to his surprise, that as
an Indian-American he qualifies as a "minority" for Fortune 500 RFP diversity quotas
(alongside women-owned/veteran-owned/LGBTQ-owned categories), requiring an **MBE
(Minority Business Enterprise) certification** process (birth certificate, etc.); says
this became a genuine tiebreaker advantage in competitive bids (cites losing a Boeing RFP
on price, but winning others where credentials were close). Relays, with an explicit
uncertainty caveat ("I don't know if this is true... that's the caveat"), an unverified
secondhand account of a **"Magic Johnson formula"**: minority-owned shell businesses
partnering with large incumbent suppliers to win quota-driven government contracts and
subcontract the actual work — presented as hearsay, not a personal practice. Retells the
origin story: **$20K/month at 15-16, growing to millions by 21, then losing it all plus
going into debt** through loss of focus (consistent with the version in
[[2023-04-17-yt-D74zHhxQ1RE]] this batch). States **Crazy Egg was his main income source
for most of his life** before the ad agency took over, and that **Quick Sprout was "just a
blog"** later handed to co-founder **Hiten Shah**, who now runs it. Confirms **Hiten Shah
is his brother-in-law** (host initially guesses "cousin") — a third instance of this
framing in the corpus, alongside the co-founder-identity contradiction already flagged on
[[../entities/hiten-shah]] and [[../entities/crazy-egg]]; adds that Neil handed Hiten the
ongoing Crazy Egg distributions to go start something else, framed as neither wanting
money for its own sake. States he and Shaan Puri are **both personal investors in Hiten's
company Nira**. Describes deliberate "expense experiments" purely for curiosity/content —
spending **$100K-200K+ on clothes** and always flying first class — explicitly not
reflective of daily habits (says he wears stained T-shirts day to day). ★ **Detailed
personal burn-rate breakdown** when pressed by the hosts: **$120,000-180,000/month**
total, no mortgage on either home, ~$200K/year combined property tax + HOA, a **whole-life
insurance policy costing $25,000-300,000/year** (framed as an investment vehicle, not a
simple death benefit), and **~$57,000/month on household staff** (cleaners, nannies, a
full-time driver via a Las Vegas limo service chosen for convenience/parking privileges
over Uber). Also states he flies private for time-optimization (to be home for school
pickups), not comfort, and that he and his wife are (as of this interview) reconsidering
private flights and reverting to commercial, since the high spend traces mainly to
COVID-era caution around young kids.
