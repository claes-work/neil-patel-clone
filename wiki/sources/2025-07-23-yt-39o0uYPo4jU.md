# The New Rules of SEO (2026)

## Summary
Neil argues that SEO isn't dead but has grown far bigger than Google, and that businesses still chasing rankings, meta descriptions, and page-one spots are fighting a game that "ended 3 years ago." His central premise: consumers no longer "search" in the old linear-funnel sense — they make rapid, simultaneous micro-decisions scattered across TikTok, Reddit, Amazon, YouTube, ChatGPT, Instagram, and the app store, and roughly 73% of search activity now happens outside Google. He reframes the buyer journey as a "constellation of micro-decisions" where each platform serves a distinct psychological function (what to click = Google, what to trust = Reddit, what to buy = Amazon/TikTok Shop, what to believe = AI models, etc.). The prescribed fix is "Search Everywhere Optimization" — optimizing to get *chosen* across every platform where decisions are made, not just found on Google. He stresses this requires platform-specific strategy (each platform has its own "decision code"), a shift from chasing visibility to earning validation (third-party mentions and trust signals that AI summarizers pull from), and disciplined prioritization via the RICE framework (Reach × Impact × Confidence × Ease) to focus on two to three platforms rather than being everywhere. The payoff is compounding cross-platform authority once a brand becomes woven into how decisions get made in its industry.

## Key claims
- SEO is not dead but has expanded well beyond Google; the discipline shifted from "getting found on Google" to "getting chosen across the entire internet" (as of 2025-07-23).
- Google handles about 13.7 billion searches a day, but that represents only ~27% of all search activity, with the other ~73% scattered across Instagram, TikTok, Amazon, Reddit, YouTube, and ChatGPT (as of 2025-07-23).
- Even businesses that win on Google still lose customers, because traffic and rankings can look healthy while conversions stay flat and sales stagnate — Neil calls this the "Google trap" (as of 2025-07-23).
- Consumer behavior has fundamentally shifted: people no longer type keywords and scan ten blue links, but make rapid-fire, simultaneous decisions across multiple touch points (as of 2025-07-23).
- The modern buyer journey is a "constellation of micro-decisions," where each platform serves a different psychological function — what to click (Google), what to trust (Reddit/reviews), what to buy (Amazon/TikTok Shop), what to try (app store ratings), what to think (YouTube/podcasts), what to believe (ChatGPT, Claude, other AI models), who to follow (Instagram/LinkedIn) (as of 2025-07-23).
- These decision moments happen simultaneously, not sequentially — a buyer can see a product on TikTok, check Amazon reviews, validate on Reddit, and ask ChatGPT for alternatives, then purchase, all without ever visiting the brand's website (as of 2025-07-23).
- The new playbook is "Search Everywhere Optimization": optimizing presence, content, and brand for every platform where decisions get made, including Google — but it is about strategic presence, not high-volume posting everywhere (as of 2025-07-23).
- Each platform is its own "decision engine" with its own psychology and requires a platform-specific strategy rather than copy-pasting one blog post everywhere — e.g., TikTok runs on emotion/novelty, YouTube on retention/perceived expertise, ChatGPT on citations/semantic clarity, Amazon on social proof/reviews, Instagram on aspirational identity, Reddit on raw authenticity (as of 2025-07-23).
- Visibility is only the entry fee; validation — being mentioned, cited, and referenced by others — is what actually drives decisions, because AI summarizes based on who is mentioned most and trusted fastest (as of 2025-07-23).
- If a brand is not part of the cross-platform validation network (Reddit threads, article citations, Amazon reviews, podcast references), it effectively does not exist in AI's decision-making process (as of 2025-07-23).
- Neil recommends the RICE prioritization framework — Reach, Impact, Confidence, Ease, each scored 1–10 and multiplied by reach — to choose which two to three platforms to focus on first rather than pursuing omnipresence (as of 2025-07-23).
- Influence compounds across platforms: a Reddit mention gets indexed by Google, a ChatGPT citation reinforces authority elsewhere, and Amazon review dominance influences decisions that began on TikTok (as of 2025-07-23).
- Neil states his agency bought an app store optimization company called ASO (transcript garble "Yo") as part of pursuing every platform where someone might discover, validate, or choose a brand (as of 2025-07-23).
- The immediate action step is to start with one platform outside Google — the one where customers are most likely to validate decisions — and earn trust there before expanding (as of 2025-07-23).
