# My Management Style Secrets | How to Grow Any Business (Churn and Burn Model)

★ **L3-candidate for next synthesis** — Neil's clearest statement of his hiring/management
philosophy, plus a genuinely new bio detail on **Mike Kamo** and a concrete SEO win.

## Summary
Neil's self-described management style: he **hates managing** and admits he's "a terrible
manager." Asked by Mike to shadow the sales and production teams for a week, he ignored the
coaching brief and just asked salespeople "did you close any deals?" His actual model: **find one
hard-working, process-oriented person who does it all**, and guarantee quality by **overpaying and
hiring smart people** so nobody needs training. He runs **"Lunch and Learns"** — recording new
marketing strategies he or David uses and teaching them to the team — which feeds a **~176-step
marketing document** the team runs down for every client and internal project. The very first
step: **"remove dates from URLs."** On the Neil Patel blog they moved from
`neilpatel.com/year/month/day/topic` to `neilpatel.com/blog/title` with breadcrumbs and
categories, and within **30 days got a 54% increase in search traffic** (significant on
hundreds-of-thousands of monthly search visitors). On hiring he prefers **entrepreneurial people
without capital** over trained marketers — people who know how to hire, fire, be creative, and
build processes — and puts them on **profit/revenue sharing** to grow a business. His blunt
"**churn and burn**" model: hire a few, test them (even letting them live in his spare rooms so
they learn by osmosis), keep the ones who generate results. It's expensive — "well into the
hundreds of thousands, if not millions" — but worth it. A **new Mike Kamo bio detail**: Mike came
from the **car-dealership industry**, running a struggling **Mazda dealership** where he learned to
hire, grow, and be creative — which is why Neil valued him. Neil also riffs that he doesn't get
the Lamborghini/marketer flex and got Mike to sell his for an electric bike.

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-10, Neil states:
- He **hates managing** and calls himself "a terrible manager"; asked to coach sales/production
  for a week, he just asked salespeople whether they closed deals (and admits they came to
  resent it).
- **Core model: find one hard-working, process-oriented person who does everything**, rather than
  managing a large team; he won't ever manage 20–30 people — just 2–4 people who can do what he
  needs, in daily contact.
- **Ensure quality by overpaying and hiring smart people** — "just overpay, hire the best" —
  so you avoid training/teaching costs.
- Runs **"Lunch and Learns"**: records new marketing strategies (his or David's) and teaches them
  to the team.
- These feed a **~176-step marketing document** run top-to-bottom for every client and internal
  project.
- **Step #1 of that doc: "remove dates from URLs."** Moving the NP blog from a dated URL structure
  to `neilpatel.com/blog/title` (with breadcrumbs + categories) produced a **54% search-traffic
  increase within 30 days** (self-reported; blog gets multiple hundreds of thousands of monthly
  search visitors).
- **Hire entrepreneurial people without capital**, not trained marketers — people who can hire,
  fire, be creative, and build processes — and give them **profit/revenue sharing** with a mandate
  to grow the business.
- Prefers marketers who run **their own side blogs/channels** and bring learnings back, over those
  who'd "only work for you."
- **"Churn and burn" hiring**: hire a few, test them (even letting them live in his spare rooms to
  learn by listening), keep the ones who produce; it's costly — "well into the hundreds of
  thousands, if not millions" — but the good ones make far more than they cost.
- **Bio (Mike Kamo)**: Mike came from the **car-dealership industry**, running a **struggling
  Mazda dealership** where he learned to hire, grow, and be creative — the traits Neil hired him
  for (context on [[../entities/mike-kamo]] if present).
- Aside: he doesn't understand the **Lamborghini/marketer** flex; got Mike to sell his for a
  ~$2,000 electric bike; the "smart ones" lease/rent theirs out to offset the payment.
