# Is Artificial Intelligence the Future of Marketing?

_Q&A Thursday; **Adam** (Viewership) hosts, question from viewer **Sasha**; Neil answers.
Only Neil-attributed material is persona data._

## Summary
Neil's **2018 prediction** on AI in marketing. His thesis: **AI will not overshadow SEO/digital
marketing — it will be *integrated into* marketing**, including offline life. His worked
example: a smart **refrigerator** that detects you're low on almond milk and offers to reorder;
brands then **pay the appliance maker** (he names Sub-Zero as the "publisher") to place
switch-offers inside the fridge UI ("89% of people who drink Silk also like Blue Diamond… get
it for half the cost"), with **Google/Facebook powering the technology and the ads** and
kicking back a share to the device maker — explicitly modeled on **AdSense economics** for
offline products. He predicts AI will **persuade, not force**, brand switches (escalating
discounts, free samples). He expects **VR ecommerce try-on** (Shopify) to arrive *before* full
AI, and flags that **laws/privacy** (Europe stricter than the US) — not the technology — will be
the real constraint. Timeline: **"give it three, four, five years."** He also name-checks
early conversational AI (Drift, Intercom, Facebook Messenger canned responses) and Amazon
frictionless retail (Dash buttons, Amazon Go in Seattle) as precursors.

## Key claims
As of 2018-03-15, Neil predicts:
- **AI won't replace SEO/marketing** — it will be **embedded in** marketing campaigns and
  everyday devices.
- **IoT programmatic ads**: smart fridges auto-detect low/expired products and surface
  competitor switch-offers; **Google/Facebook power the ads**, the **appliance maker acts as
  the publisher**, paid on an **AdSense-style kickback** model for offline products.
- AI will **persuade** buyers to switch brands via escalating discounts and free samples,
  "it won't necessarily force you into it but it'll really persuade you."
- **VR ecommerce try-on** (via Shopify, trying on clothes/shoes virtually) will arrive
  **before** mature AI marketing.
- **Laws, legalities, and privacy** — with **Europe stricter than the US** — will constrain AI
  marketing more than the technology itself.
- Timeline: meaningful marketing integration is **~3–5 years out** from 2018.
- Precursors already visible: chatbots (**Drift, Intercom, Facebook Messenger** canned
  responses) and Amazon frictionless retail (**Dash buttons, Amazon Go**).
