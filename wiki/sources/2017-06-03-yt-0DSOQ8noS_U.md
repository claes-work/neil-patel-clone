# How Virtual Reality Is Going to Affect Digital Marketing

## Summary
In this short 2017 forward-looking video, Neil Patel predicts how virtual reality will reshape online marketing. Speaking as VR headsets from Samsung, Facebook, and Google were entering the market — with companies spending as much as a billion dollars acquiring players in the space — he frames VR as an inevitable, here-to-stay channel and asks marketers when they will start leveraging it. His core thesis (a then-prediction, not an observed result) is that brands will convert flat video and static product pages into immersive, first-person experiences: instead of merely watching a Red Bull stunt clip on YouTube, viewers will feel as if they themselves are skydiving over Abu Dhabi; and in eCommerce, shoppers will be able to virtually see, try on, feel, and touch products from home. He acknowledges the tactile sensation won't fully match real life at first but expects those feelings to increasingly transfer through the web as the technology matures. The overarching claim is that VR lets companies take real-life experiences and deliver them to people online — a prediction offered in 2017 about where marketing was headed.

## Key claims
- Virtual reality is becoming ubiquitous, with Samsung, Facebook, and Google all competing in the space, and companies spending up to a billion dollars acquiring players in it (as of 2017-06-03).
- VR will let brands turn passive video content into first-person experiences — e.g., a viewer experiencing a Red Bull-style stunt or skydive themselves rather than just watching a YouTube clip (as of 2017-06-03).
- In eCommerce, VR will let shoppers virtually see, try on, feel, and touch products from home before buying (as of 2017-06-03).
- The tactile/sensory experience won't initially equal real life, but those sensations are expected to increasingly transfer through the web as the technology develops (as of 2017-06-03).
- The essence of VR marketing is taking real-life experiences and showing/delivering them to people online, in their homes (as of 2017-06-03).
- VR is "here to stay," backed by billions in company investment, and the open question for marketers is when — not whether — they will start leveraging it for their business (as of 2017-06-03).
