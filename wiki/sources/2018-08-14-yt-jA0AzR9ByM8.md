# How to Promote Your YouTube Video When You Have ZERO Subscribers

## Summary

In this short 2018 solo video, Neil Patel argues that a new YouTube channel with no ad budget and no subscribers can still grow views by pushing external signals into YouTube rather than relying on the platform's own discovery. He gives four tactics: (1) get bloggers who write on related topics to embed your videos in their blog posts, (2) translate and transcribe videos into other languages via uploaded subtitles because non-English YouTube is far less competitive, (3) leverage your existing owned audiences — an email list and a push-notification subscriber list — to blast new videos, and (4) find your most-visited pages in Google Search Console/Analytics and embed the most relevant video into those posts. His underlying thesis is that YouTube weighs many external signals, so demonstrating that people love the content off-platform improves on-platform ranking; embedding video into blog posts also increases site dwell time, which he says lifts Google rankings — a "win-win."

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-14, Neil argues that without ad budget or a social following you probably won't get subscribers, and generic YouTube tips work poorly when you have no money and no subscribers — so promotion should happen *outside* YouTube.
- As of 2018-08-14, Neil's team reaches out to bloggers who write articles on concepts similar to their videos and asks them to embed the video within the blog post; he says this drives more views, sends "positive signals" to YouTube, and improves rankings.
- As of 2018-08-14, Neil claims YouTube is very competitive in English but "not that hard to get views" outside English, so creators should translate/transcribe videos into other languages.
- As of 2018-08-14, Neil says his team uploads this (English) video with subtitles in Portuguese, Spanish, German, and many other languages, which earns views beyond English.
- As of 2018-08-14, Neil states he does not have a ton of subscribers in many non-English-speaking regions, but his popularity there is growing per his YouTube stats because his videos are subtitled into those countries' languages.
- As of 2018-08-14, Neil leverages his existing audience: when he makes a video he knows people will love, he sends an email blast from his email list to it to drive views, engagement, subscribers, and higher ranking.
- As of 2018-08-14, Neil does the same with his push-notification subscriber list, and says he uses subscribers.com to generate more push-notification subscribers, sending a push when he releases a video viewers would like.
- As of 2018-08-14, Neil frames the mechanism as YouTube looking at many signals, many of them external: if external signals show people love the content, it performs better on YouTube too.
- As of 2018-08-14, Neil's fourth tactic: check your most popular pages in Google Search Console or Google Analytics, find the video most relevant to that page, and embed it into that blog post (as he does at NeilPatel.com) for consistent video views.
- As of 2018-08-14, Neil claims embedding video also keeps people on the website longer, improving user metrics and thus overall Google rankings — a "win-win" of more YouTube views plus longer on-site time.
