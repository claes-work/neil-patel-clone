# Advice Every Online Business Owner Should've Heard Before Starting SEO

## Summary
Four pieces of "know this before you invest in SEO" advice, with several concrete
new anecdotes. (1) SEO is long-term — a business needs other traffic sources
(paid, sales) to generate cash now, since post-COVID Google ad revenue jumped
"60-something percent," making paid acquisition more expensive; conversion-funnel
upsells/downsells (built on speed + automation) are framed as the fastest lever, with
the NP Digital chain example (SEO client → upsold on PPC → Amazon ads → CRO)
reinforced. (2) Older sites carry more authority that can't be bought — reinforces the
patience/brand-building doctrine. New anecdote: Neil says NP Digital has turned down
multiple **financing/investment offers** over "the last six months, a year" because
investors don't always grasp the long-term approach, and he's "willing to take losses"
for the long run — illustrated with a story about an SMB-division sales rep who
underpriced a client at a few hundred dollars/month; rather than cancel, Neil
personally spent his and his team's time to make the economics work for the client at
a loss. Cites a **Warren Buffett letter to CEOs** (paraphrased, no date given) advising
against short-term gains that hurt customers long-term, and the Amazon
reviews-and-star-ratings decision (credited to Jeff Bezos) as a customer-first bet
that paid off. (3) The Zillow **Zestimate** is given as a new "build a better
mousetrap" example, alongside the existing Ubersuggest-as-free-mousetrap self-example.
