# The Secret to Becoming the Top Website in Any Popular Niche

## Summary
A tactical walkthrough of how to improve a site's **Alexa.com** popularity rank (the 2017-era third-party web-traffic ranking owned by Amazon — distinct from the Alexa voice assistant). Neil frames Alexa rank as a proxy for traffic where a *lower* number is better (1 = most popular site on the web), and gives four quick wins that all reduce to "get more traffic": install the Alexa toolbar for your visitors, promote content on social, do link-out outreach emails, and reverse-engineer competitors' backlinks via Ahrefs. Note: this is a period-specific tactic — Alexa.com was retired by Amazon in 2022, so the underlying metric no longer exists.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-16, Neil states:
- Alexa.com rates sites from a large number down to 1, with 1 being the most popular site on the web (e.g. Google or Facebook); it publishes a list of the top 100 sites (as of 2017-07-16).
- You want your Alexa number to continually *drop* toward the top; the driver is traffic — more visitors = better (lower) Alexa rank (as of 2017-07-16).
- Quick win 1: install the **Alexa toolbar**, so more of your visitors browse with it and Alexa can collect data from them, improving your rank (as of 2017-07-16).
- Quick win 2: promote every new/updated post on the social web (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Pinterest); push images to Twitter and Pinterest (as of 2017-07-16).
- Quick win 3: whenever you link out to someone, email them ("Hey John, I loved your content so much I linked to you — feel free to share") to earn social shares and traffic back (as of 2017-07-16).
- Quick win 4: use **Ahrefs.com** to pull competitors' backlinks, then email each of those linking sites for links of your own (as of 2017-07-16).
- Realistic expectation: reaching 1 or the top 1,000 is very hard, but you can get to ~20k–100k; improvement shows over a 30–60 day window after implementing (as of 2017-07-16).
