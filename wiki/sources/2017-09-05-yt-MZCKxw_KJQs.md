# How to Improve Your Conversion Rate When You Have Little to No Website Visitors

## Summary
Neil addresses conversion rate optimization for sites with very little traffic (roughly a thousand to a few thousand visitors). He argues that conventional A/B testing tools like Optimizely need large traffic volumes to reach statistical significance, requiring hundreds of conversions before declaring a winner — a threshold low-traffic sites cannot hit, leaving them unable to tell whether changes help or hurt. His solution is bandit testing (which he attributes to Crazy Egg), a never-ending test that automatically shifts traffic toward better-converting variations. Testers add variations continuously via a WYSIWYG editor (new headlines, images, removed elements) without deleting old ones, and the tool reallocates traffic to maximize income. When unsure what to change, he points to visual diagnostic reports — heatmaps showing where people click and don't click, and visitor/mouse-movement recordings — to generate change ideas.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-05, Neil states:
- Low-traffic sites are those with around a thousand or a few thousand visitors, and they need a different CRO approach than high-traffic sites (as of 2017-09-05).
- Conventional A/B testing solutions like Optimizely require a lot of traffic because they wait for statistical significance before declaring a winning variation (as of 2017-09-05).
- Reaching statistical significance requires "hundreds and hundreds and hundreds" of conversions before a winner emerges (as of 2017-09-05).
- Without a statistically significant winner, you cannot know whether your changes are improving or hurting conversions (as of 2017-09-05).
- Crazy Egg offers bandit testing that is designed mainly for smaller websites (as of 2017-09-05).
- Bandit testing automatically adjusts traffic across variations based on how each version is converting (as of 2017-09-05).
- The bandit test is never-ending: you continually add variations, never have to delete them, and traffic is automatically shifted to maximize income (as of 2017-09-05).
- Changes and variations can be created through a WYSIWYG editor — adding headlines, adding images, or removing page elements (as of 2017-09-05).
- If you don't know what to change, Crazy Egg's visual reports (heatmaps of where people click and don't click, and visitor/mouse-movement recordings) give you ideas on what to change to maximize conversion rates (as of 2017-09-05).
