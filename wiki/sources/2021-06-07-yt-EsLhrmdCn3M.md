# My Biggest SEO Mistake (And Why Your Organic Traffic Might Be Garbage)

## Summary
Solo confessional/tutorial framed around a self-critique: despite driving **58.6 million
visitors from Google over the trailing 12 months**, he calls the traffic largely
vanity — much of it came from brand-name and irrelevant keywords (e.g. "how to increase
Instagram followers") that don't convert for an ad agency that doesn't sell social-follower
growth. Explains that NP Digital's Salesforce lead tracking (via "Let's talk"/contact-form
submissions) revealed most of that high-volume traffic drove little to no leads, while his
actual converting content is narrow and low-volume (e.g. "customer reviews in video ads" —
zero monthly search volume — because searchers for it are CRO-service prospects; "how to
use YouTube ads to grow your business" — 20,000 searches but "willing to spend money").
Gives a live CPC comparison in Ubersuggest: "digital marketing" at $18/click (high-intent,
relevant) vs. "Instagram followers" at 60,000 searches but only ~$1.98/click (high volume,
low buyer intent) — a roughly 9x CPC gap he uses as the relevance signal. Contrasts his own
traffic (~5.8M monthly per the video) against agency competitors iProspect (~17,000
monthly visitors, self-estimated $500–700M/year revenue) and Performics (3,700 visitors,
~3,000 employees) to argue traffic volume is a vanity metric divorced from revenue
("I'd rather take 17,000 [visitors] and generate all that revenue"). Explicitly frames
this as his biggest SEO mistake: optimizing for brand-awareness/traffic instead of
revenue-qualified relevance.
