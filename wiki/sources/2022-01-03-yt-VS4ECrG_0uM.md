# 20 Years Of Marketing - 7 Most Important Lessons Learned ★

## Summary
A 20-years-in-marketing retrospective naming seven lessons: (1) start small even at
scale — he says at NP Digital's size ("over 500 employees") he still starts test
budgets under $1,000 rather than scaling immediately; (2) execute fast over
perfecting — credits copywriter **Frank Kern** with "it's better to have some copy
than nothin'"; ties this to continuous A/B-testing websites rather than "launching" a
finished redesign; (3) learn from mistakes — reflects on starting with black-hat SEO
as a self-described "foolish 16 year old kid" (reinforces existing biography beat, see
[[persona/biography.md]] "$25M first client" era commentary); uses an Elon
Musk/SpaceX analogy; (4) hire people who've already solved the problem — **new
biographical detail**: names NP Digital CEO **Mike Gullickson**, previously ran
operations at iCrossing (acquired by Hearst/"Hers" in transcript — likely
caption-garbled for "Hearst"), then CEO of Covario (acquired by iProspect, owned by
Dentsu), then president of iProspect, before joining NP Digital; gives a "done it
successfully twice, not just once" heuristic for hiring; (5) be consistent — cites
neilpatel.com's longevity as its edge over other marketing blogs; (6) build a brand —
uses Nike/BMW/Amex/Airbnb and FTX's 2021-era sports sponsorships (MLB umpires, Miami
FTX Arena, Lewis Hamilton/Mercedes F1) as examples of brand-building spend, dated
just before FTX's Nov-2022 collapse; (7) make purchasing frictionless — cites a
concrete NP Digital/Ubersuggest checkout test: adding PayPal as a payment option
produced roughly an 18% increase in conversions (100→118).
