# How to Speed Up Your Website And Increase Your Website's Revenue

## Summary
A short technical/CRO video in which Neil Patel frames site speed as a revenue lever —
citing an Amazon study that each additional second of load time cost Amazon ~1% of revenue,
and arguing smaller sites face roughly the same dynamic — then gives four ways to speed up
a site. (1) Use Google PageSpeed to diagnose what is slowing the site and get a score plus
fix recommendations. (2) Use the Google AMP framework so pages load very fast for the
mobile users who are often the majority of traffic. (3) Use a CDN (content delivery
network) that replicates your site to servers worldwide so visitors in, say, India load a
nearby copy even if the site is hosted in the US — he names Akamai as expensive but very
good. (4) Cache your pages — trivial on WordPress with the W3 Total Cache plugin, otherwise
ask your developer or host. Faster sites reduce visitor frustration and lift sales.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-24, Neil states:
- Amazon found that every additional second of load time cost it ~1% of revenue; smaller
  sites face roughly the same relationship — slower load, more lost revenue (as of
  2017-08-24; attributed to an "Amazon study," not independently cited here).
- Step 1 — Use Google PageSpeed to see what is slowing the site; it scores the URL and
  shows what to fix (as of 2017-08-24).
- Step 2 — Use the Google AMP framework to make pages load very fast for mobile users, who
  in many cases are the majority of traffic (as of 2017-08-24).
- Step 3 — Use a CDN (content delivery network), which replicates the site across servers
  in major global regions so a visitor loads a nearby copy (his example: India loading a
  local replica of a US-hosted site); Akamai is expensive but really good (as of
  2017-08-24).
- Step 4 — Cache your pages; on WordPress use the W3 Total Cache plugin, otherwise ask your
  developer or hosting company (as of 2017-08-24).
- A faster site loads without frustrating visitors, which grows sales (as of 2017-08-24).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Amazon did a study and they found that every second delay their website loads, they lose 1% of their revenue. That's a lot of money. Sure, you're not as big as Amazon but it's roughly the same."

> "A CDN is a content delivery network. What that means is, they take your website and they put it all over the globe ... they'll replicate your site and put it on a server in India, so that way all the people in India can see your website right away."

> "When people come to your site they won't get frustrated anymore, and they're going to be like, 'Ah, site loads fast, I like it, might as well buy.'"
