# How to do SEO on a One Page Website

## Summary
As of 2019-10-26, Neil Patel breaks down how to do SEO on a single-page website, conceding up front that multi-page sites are clearly better for SEO but insisting one-page sites can still perform. He lays out four tactics: (1) optimize every section of the page — header, body, footer — using subheadings (H1/H2/H3) to insert keywords and improve skimmability, grouping related keywords into dedicated sections (e.g., separate sections for dog food, cat food, shipping) and researching variations/long-tail phrases with Ubersuggest; (2) a "land and expand" approach — use Google Search Console to find terms with high impressions but low clicks, and if they fit the product and can drive conversions, adjust the page (including title tag and meta description) to include them; (3) put the main keyword in the root domain name (not a subfolder), citing ClickFlow that a keyword in the domain drives more clicks; and (4) continually update the page every quarter with substantial changes — adding/deleting content, images, and videos — because Google doesn't like ranking old, outdated sites. He closes by plugging his ad agency, Neil Patel Digital, noting they'd probably still try to get you to add more pages.
