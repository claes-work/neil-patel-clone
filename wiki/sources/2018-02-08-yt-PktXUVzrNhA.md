# How to Rank Video on YouTube and Blow Up Your Channel | Does YouTube SEO Impact Your Google Rank?

_Q&A Thursdays; **Adam Lodolce** (viewership.com, whose company specializes in YouTube
marketing) hosts/asks and adds YouTube expertise. Neil credits Adam for several of these
tactics; only Neil-attributed material is persona data._

## Summary
Neil's answer to "how will YouTube impact SEO?": **treat YouTube as its own search engine,
separate from Google.** YouTube videos don't rank well or drive much traffic from Google
organic — Google does surface some videos now, but it's not where users want video most of the
time — so **optimize for YouTube search, not Google.** His proof point: he ranks very high for
"**seo**" on YouTube (with Adam's help) and that video pulls ~400–500 views/day passively, even
though it doesn't rank page-one on Google for "seo." He rejects the idea that Google organic will
"go video": if users wanted more video in Google results, Google's data-driven ranking would have
pushed it already. Tactics to rank on YouTube: (1) **upload a real transcript** so YouTube knows
the topic (don't rely on auto-captions); (2) **make videos 5+ minutes** — Adam converted Neil from
2-minute videos to 5+ and it "made a world of difference," though 20–30-min videos aren't
meaningfully better than 5; (3) **use the right keywords** in tags, titles, and descriptions;
(4) spy on competitors with **vidIQ** (vidiq.com) to see what tags/terms are working — a good term
is one where a video ranks high *without* many subscribers (room to win). The biggest lever Neil
says he learned from Adam: **the first 1–24 hours determine everything** — unlike normal SEO that
climbs over time, a YouTube video that crushes its first 24 hours keeps winning, and a first-day
dud stays a dud. So drive your list, tweets, and shares hard on day one. Net: Google organic and
YouTube organic are **two separate channels**; do both.

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-08, Neil states:
- **Treat YouTube as a separate search engine from Google.** YouTube videos don't rank well or
  drive much Google-organic traffic; optimize for YouTube search, not for Google.
- He **ranks very high for "seo" on YouTube** and that video gets ~**400–500 views/day**
  passively without ongoing work — even though it isn't page-one on Google for "seo."
- Google organic **won't shift heavily to video**: if users wanted more video there, Google's
  data/pattern-driven ranking would already be surfacing it.
- **Upload a manual transcript** — don't rely on YouTube auto-captions — so YouTube clearly knows
  what the video is about. (He also notes repeating the target phrase, e.g. "YouTube SEO," in the
  video itself reinforces relevance.)
- **Make videos 5+ minutes**: he previously did 2-minute videos ("short and to the point"), but
  YouTube dislikes very short videos; Adam had him switch to 5+ min and it "made a world of
  difference." **20–30-minute videos aren't meaningfully better than 5** — about 5 minutes is
  the good-to-go threshold.
- **Keyword optimization matters**: right keywords in **tags, headlines/titles, and description**
  drive rankings.
- **Spy with vidIQ** (vidiq.com): for any search term, see which videos rank and what tags they
  use. **A strong opportunity term = a video ranking high with few subscribers** (room for others,
  including bigger channels, to win).
- **The first 1–24 hours are decisive** (credited to Adam): unlike normal SEO that climbs
  gradually, if a YouTube video crushes its first 24 hours it keeps crushing; a first-day dud
  stays a dud regardless of later effort. So on launch day, email your list, tweet it, and share
  it hard.
- **Google organic and YouTube organic are separate channels** — pursue both; don't worry whether
  your YouTube videos rank on Google.
