# Stop Obsessing Over Traffic So You Can Start Getting Sales With Your Online Business

## Summary
Argues traffic is a slow, secondary metric and lays out what to prioritize instead
across two halves. **Focus on**: (1) the offer — nailing what converts before scaling
traffic; (2) a lead-generation strategy (SEO, paid, cold outreach — no single right
answer); (3) lead nurturing rather than expecting leads to convert on their own; (4)
funnel/conversion optimization — auditing the product-to-checkout path (images,
reviews, testimonials) for the specific drop-off point; (5) new content built around
topic clusters (paid ads as the hub topic, with Facebook/LinkedIn/Google/Twitter/
Instagram/Reddit ads as sub-topics); (6) refreshing existing content using Search
Console/Ubersuggest keyword-opportunity data; (7) internal linking between old and new
content, without over-optimizing anchor text; (8) content pruning — merging duplicate
pages on the same topic (his example: multiple yearly "SEO guide for 20XX" posts) and
fixing/removing dead internal links. **Don't focus on**: checking traffic/rankings
daily (weekly/monthly plus alerts is enough unless actively running ad campaigns); and
obsessing over competitors instead of customers — reframes competitor analysis as
customer-driven, via Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas and backlink-opportunities reports
(finding sites linking to 2-3 competitors but not you as high-probability targets).

## Key claims
- States SEO's typical time-to-traction explicitly: 3-6 months for a new site (up to 12+
  months or multiple years without existing site authority) before results "really
  start kicking up" (self-reported/observational, dated 2021-10) — seo.
- Frames content pruning concretely via a recurring "SEO guide for 2021 / 2022 / 2025"
  duplicate-post example, prescribing merge-then-delete-outdated plus internal-link
  cleanup as standard practice (paraphrase, dated 2021-10) — seo, content-marketing.
- Reframes "don't obsess over competitors" as a customer-centric doctrine (citing Amazon
  as the model) rather than an argument against competitive research altogether — the
  backlink-gap tactic (targeting sites linking to 2-3 competitors but not you) is kept
  as the sanctioned form of competitor analysis (paraphrase, dated 2021-10) — seo,
  agency-entrepreneurship.
