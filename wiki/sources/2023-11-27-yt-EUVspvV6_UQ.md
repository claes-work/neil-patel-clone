# Will the $68B SEO Industry Go Extinct? (Ep. 2616)

## Summary
A co-hosted Marketing School episode in which Eric Siu frames a debate — sparked by a Fortune article headlined that Google and Microsoft could "render the $68B SEO industry extinct with AI" — and prompts Neil Patel for his take, then adds his own rebuttal. Neil drives the substantive analysis: Google cannot simply hand out AI answers because ~$30B+/year of AdSense-and-partner-network revenue (against ~$162B Google-ads revenue in 2022) is at stake and its stock would "get annihilated"; because a meaningful share of results contain inaccurate/misinformation, giving direct answers at scale would trigger lawsuits "through the roof," especially in finance and health; and because Google has publicly positioned itself to inform options rather than give the single answer unless it is "black and white like 2 plus 2 equals 4." Neil's bottom line is that AI will reduce clicks to organic results but not crush SEO, and that marketers can optimize to be pulled into AI engines. Eric reads the article's claim that SEO consultants (mostly SMBs) "will no longer be needed" and the industry is "unlikely to survive," rebuts it as a "not so smart statement" (SEO evolves like any business; adapt or die), and teases upcoming episodes on Google's SGE (Search Generative Experience) and its effect on click-through rates. Neil closes by noting NP Digital clients are already asking how to get Google and AI engines like Bing to source them.

> Dated position (2023-11-27): Neil's stance here — SEO will not die but will evolve, AI reduces organic clicks yet you can optimize to be pulled into AI engines — is an early, measured articulation that prefigures (rather than contradicts) his later 2025 "Search Everywhere Optimization" framing. Recorded neutrally as a point on the timeline; not resolved here.

## Key claims
- There is "no way" Google crushes $30B+/year in AdSense-and-partner-network revenue by handing out AI answers, because its stock would "get annihilated"; sites that buy Google ad traffic and monetize via AdSense would go from loss-leader to pure loss if answers were given directly. (Neil, 2023-11-27)
- Google earned ~$162B from Google ads in 2022, with over ~$30B/year coming from AdSense and partner networks — figures cited as the financial stakes behind why Google won't move fast. (Neil citing article/search data, 2023-11-27)
- If Google displayed AI answers for every query, a meaningful share (Neil estimates 10%+) would be inaccurate/misinformation — a problem Google has tried to solve for years — and it would "get sued through the roof," especially on finance and health topics that can harm people's lives. (Neil, 2023-11-27)
- Google has publicly positioned itself to inform users on options and let them decide, not to give a single answer, unless it is "black and white like 2 plus 2 equals 4"; giving answers directly would also erode Google's credibility when results are wrong. (Neil, 2023-11-27)
- AI will change search and create fewer clicks to organic results, but it will not crush SEO; marketers can optimize for AI and get their results pulled, the way they optimize to be pulled into Google's knowledge graph today. (Neil, 2023-11-27)
- Google is smart enough to slowly transition — testing AI answers on a percentage of traffic and rolling out gradually — only once a new form of monetization makes up for AdSense revenue "plus more" before pulling the switch. (Neil, 2023-11-27)
- NP Digital is already being approached by many companies asking how to get Google to pull from and source their sites, and how to optimize for AI engines like Bing so they get recommended when people ask questions. (Neil, 2023-11-27)
- SEO is going to evolve as it always has; Neil does not see it dying, only changing — and businesses that don't adapt will get crushed, but there is time now to adapt. (Neil, 2023-11-27)
- The Fortune article's premise — that AI could render the $68B SEO industry extinct — paints too negative a picture; as long as people search there will be search, and SEO (like any business) will evolve, so the claim is a "not so smart statement." (Eric, 2023-11-27)
- Search in its current form (the "10 blue links") has existed 30-plus years and is due to be reimagined and adapted; the show will cover how Google's SGE (Search Generative Experience) may affect future click-through rates in upcoming episodes. (Eric, 2023-11-27)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> there's no way Google is going to crush 30 plus billion dollars in revenue — what would happen to their stock price? It would just get annihilated.

> here's a kicker that most people don't realize: if Google is giving you the answers to every single search result... I bet you there's a good percentage of the chance — and when I say good percent, 10 plus percent — you find inaccurate listings, people giving you false information, wrong information... Google's been trying to solve this for years. I would bet a million dollars on this: for every search query, I bet you they would get sued through the roof, especially on things related to finance, health — sectors like that that can just impact people's life in a really negative way.

> Google has publicly stated they're not here to just say, hey everyone, we're going to end up using AI to give you the answers to everything. They're trying to inform you on options and let you make your own decision. They don't want to just give you the answer unless it's black and white, like 2 plus 2 equals 4.

> I think AI is going to change search and create less clicks to organic results, but it's not going to crush it. And you can even optimize for AI and have them pull your results, just like you do right now.

> at my ad agency NP Digital we're getting hit up by a lot of companies saying, hey, AI is happening — which we all know — how do we get Google to pull from our sites and source us, or even optimize for these AI engines like Bing and get them to recommend you when people are asking questions... SEO is going to evolve, it always has, and it'll continue to change. I don't see it dying, I just see it changing and evolving. And if you don't adapt with it, yes, your business will get crushed — but you have the time right now to adapt.
