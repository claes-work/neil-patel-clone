# Where (and When) To Cut Costs When Your Business is Struggling

## Summary

Solo business monologue. Neil frames business as "a roller coaster" — thrilling highs,
scary lows — and answers a recurring question he gets: when things go bad, where should I
cut, who should I fire, which departments? His answer: **"no departments" — cut back on
yourself first.** Do you need the new car, the TV, the fancy home? When times get tough,
**take from your own bank account before your team's.** He says people think he's crazy,
but he's in his 30s and if things got really bad he'd move back in with his mom and dad
(acknowledging he's lucky they'd gladly have him). The reasoning is team-first: "your
business will never get to where it is without your team members." He recounts speaking the
same day as **Richard Branson** at a conference in Brazil, where Branson told him it's your
people who make the business — **"Elon Musk didn't create Tesla and SpaceX by himself"**;
he's brilliant, but it's the people who build the company. So when times are tough, don't
lay people off — cut from your own pockets and expenses, and **be frugal so you keep a
reserve for a rainy day.** He allows that business is business: once past the crisis, if you
genuinely have no choice, you can then decide which departments to cut, and you should have
open conversations with your executive team and even your whole workforce in advance —
letting them know you're taking the brunt (moving in with parents, absorbing expenses).
His proof point: in past hard times when he and his co-founder had spent all their money,
team members were willing to **keep working for free**, and some have been with him 11–12
years — because when times are tough he does whatever he can to take care of everyone else
first ("we're a family"). When things recovered, he went back and **reimbursed them for
what they'd put in — with interest or equity** — unprompted. The closing rule: when times
are tough, look in the mirror, figure out what you're doing that you don't need, and cut
that.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-09-15, when a business struggles, **cut your own personal expenses first, not
  departments or staff** — "no departments."
- As of 2018-09-15, Neil says he'd **move back in with his parents** before cutting his
  team, and would absorb the expenses himself.
- As of 2018-09-15, **your team, not the founder, builds the business** — he cites Richard
  Branson (met speaking the same day at a Brazil conference) and "Elon Musk didn't create
  Tesla and SpaceX by himself."
- As of 2018-09-15, **be frugal and keep a cash reserve** for downturns; make layoffs only
  as a last resort and communicate openly with the exec team and workforce in advance.
- As of 2018-09-15, in past cash crunches his team members worked for free; some have
  stayed 11–12 years, and when the business recovered he **reimbursed them with interest
  or equity**, unprompted — because "we're a family."
