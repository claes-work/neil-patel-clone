# Setting Up Your Business on Facebook - Module 1 - Lesson 2 - Facebook Unlocked Course

## Summary
Facebook Unlocked Module 1, Lesson 2: a setup walkthrough covering personal profile →
Business Page → Business Manager → Groups, in that order. Personal profile first
(required to run ads or manage a page); pick a business Page name people already know
you by (not a keyword-stuffed name) and a category customers would use (his McDonald's-
is-a-fast-food-joint-not-a-dessert-place example); fill out full contact info and
publish. Content strategy note: post genuinely helpful content even when it doesn't
directly drive leads, since a follower base won't grow on pure self-promotion. Covers
the Offer feature (gated behind 100+ followers). Business Manager is framed as the
centralized, secure way to manage ad accounts/pages/team access (vs. advertising from a
personal profile, which he advises against) — covers adding people/partners/pages,
connecting Instagram/WhatsApp, shared pixel/payment data. Closes on Groups: 10M+
Facebook groups used by 1.4B people; recommends naming a group differently from the
page, defaulting to public over private groups, staying highly active as moderator, and
notes engagement (not just traffic) tends to be markedly higher in groups than on pages
or profiles.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-12-24, restates and extends the standing "brand-name over keyword-name"
  positioning doctrine to Facebook Page naming and category selection specifically
  (paraphrase).
- Advises against running ads from a personal profile; Business Manager is the
  recommended, more secure/organized structure for ad accounts, team access, and
  cross-app data sharing (paraphrase, standing agency practice).
- Cites platform data: 10 million+ Facebook Groups, used by 1.4 billion people
  (paraphrase, third-party platform statistic).
- Operational claim: Groups generate meaningfully higher engagement (likes, comments)
  than a Facebook Page or personal profile, but only if the admin stays continuously
  active — many group owners "push hard the first week or two" then taper off and lose
  momentum (paraphrase).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> When you're picking your business name, it should be the name people know you for,
> not just keywords that you want people to type in.

> If you don't have useful content — content that just doesn't sell, but content that
> helps educate and helps people out — you're not going to really get a ton of
> followers on Facebook.
