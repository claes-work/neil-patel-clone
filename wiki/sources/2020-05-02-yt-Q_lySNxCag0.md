# SEO Unlocked: FULL Free SEO Course With Neil Patel (4+ HOURS)

## Summary
This page covers only the introduction/overview lesson of Neil Patel's flagship free "SEO Unlocked" course, not the full 4+ hour program — the transcript is just the opening welcome segment, so the substantive lessons (keyword research, on-page/technical SEO, content marketing, link building, etc.) are only previewed here, not taught. Neil frames the course as a seven-week program that promises to drive a flood of organic traffic without spending on advertising, and he positions himself as living proof that SEO works, citing his own 6M+ monthly visitors (4M+ from search). He lays out the seven-week curriculum (intro + keyword research, on-page/technical SEO, content marketing over two weeks, link building, Ubersuggest, then branding/customer experience), describes six support mechanisms (weekly content, slides/templates/worksheets, live Q&A calls, email support, community, and his agency), and repeatedly points viewers to neilpatel.com/training for downloadable templates. A large portion of the lesson is motivational rather than tactical: he argues it's never too late to start (citing late-blooming founders), lays out an SEO mindset (anticipate failure, delegate, keep learning, ask for feedback, "give give give," know your "why"), and assigns action items — a personal-goal worksheet and downloading the program itinerary. He closes by previewing week one and his signature framing of getting "loved by Google."

## Key claims
- The course is a seven-week program that teaches how to get a flood of website traffic without spending money on advertising (as of 2020-05-02).
- Neil positions himself as living proof that SEO works, noting he grew up in an average neighborhood and did not start with much money, and chose SEO because it let him rank and compete with big companies without spending heavily (as of 2020-05-02).
- His website receives over six million visitors per month, of which over four million come from search engines (as of 2020-05-02).
- Timothy Sykes is cited as an SEO success story: roughly $50,000/month when Neil first met him, later well over $15 million per year, attributed to SEO (as of 2020-05-02).
- The seven-week curriculum: week 1 = intro to SEO + keyword research; week 2 = on-page and technical SEO; weeks 3–4 = content marketing (two weeks due to depth); week 5 = link building (without links you won't rank as high as you want); week 6 = Ubersuggest, the tool on neilpatel.com and its free features; week 7 = branding and customer experience, which Neil argues has a lot to do with SEO (as of 2020-05-02).
- Six support mechanisms back the course: weekly video/audio content, slides/PowerPoint templates/worksheets/PDF templates, weekly live Q&A calls (roughly every other week due to travel, all recorded), email support at support@neilpatel.com, community/social/blog comments, and his ad agency Neil Patel Digital for done-for-you help (as of 2020-05-02).
- Templates, worksheets, PDFs, and the program itinerary are free and downloadable at neilpatel.com/training; the newsletter opt-in is at neilpatel.com/newsletter (as of 2020-05-02).
- Neil's team has over one hundred people available to answer questions from businesses of any size (as of 2020-05-02).
- Google uses over 200 ranking factors, but Neil says he will deliberately narrow the course down to the central actions needed to get results rather than cover everything (as of 2020-05-02).
- It is never too late to start, illustrated with founders who started late: Sam Walton founded Walmart at 44, Adolf "Adi" Dassler founded Adidas at 48, Gary Burrell co-founded Garmin at 51, and Charles Flint founded IBM at 61 (as of 2020-05-02).
- The SEO mindset Neil prescribes: anticipate failure (much of SEO is experimentation and won't always raise rankings), learn to let go and delegate to a team or contractors, continually learn new skills, don't be afraid to ask for feedback, "give give give before you ask," and understand your "why" for wanting more traffic (as of 2020-05-02).
- Neil says the course could be charged for but is given away free, and that his team spent thousands of dollars just on the course design, reflecting a "give give give before you ask" philosophy (as of 2020-05-02).
- Action items: download and handwrite up to 300 words on a personal-goal worksheet describing your goal for the program and how you'll feel achieving it, then download the program itinerary; Neil claims people who do this exercise are much more likely to complete the program (as of 2020-05-02).
- Completing all seven weeks is framed as necessary for results; people who drop off after a week or two, or who don't implement what's taught, are the ones Neil sees not getting results (as of 2020-05-02).
- Week one preview: uncover the SEO factors that win traffic, why SEO offers a better ROI than any other marketing channel, how to find the perfect keywords that drive traffic and sales, and how to "steal" (i.e., outrank, not unethically take) competitors' keywords (as of 2020-05-02).
