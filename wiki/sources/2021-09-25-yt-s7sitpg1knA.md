# The Optimization TRAP: When SEO Actually HURTS Your Traffic

## Summary
Warns against four over-optimization mistakes: (1) changing a page too frequently
without waiting to observe impact — cites Google making 8+ algorithm changes per day,
but argues optimizations take roughly a month to show impact and 6–12 months for
"optimal results," so testing should be paced, not daily; contrasts with his own early
SEO practice ~15–16 years prior of tweaking pages constantly; (2) keyword-rich anchor
text over-optimization on internal/external links, recommending naturally varied
anchor text (brand name, "click here," "learn more") over repeating the exact target
keyword; (3) the keyword-density myth — states Google doesn't count keyword
occurrences per paragraph and instead uses LSI-style topical understanding (Apple the
company vs. apple the fruit as the illustrative example), and warns that
over-optimizing keyword repetition can undo good rankings; (4) building backlinks too
fast, including manual outreach at high volume, which reads as unnatural even when
fully organic (no paid/bought links) — reiterating "slow and steady wins the race."
