# Start Selling 20% More Online in Less Than 10 Minutes | Growth Hack Your eCommerce

## Summary
A standalone eCommerce CRO tutorial pitched around COVID-accelerated online-shopping
growth (Neil cites online commerce jumping ~3-5 years ahead of schedule; roughly 2
billion+ people buying online, ~25% of world population). He frames good UX as
compounding into both sales and SEO (lower bounce, higher dwell time indirectly
lifting rankings) and gives four concrete tactics: (1) simple navigation with few
options, illustrated with his own neilpatel.com blog, where he says the most-clicked
navigation element is a generic "Learn More" dropdown rather than any specific post —
and warns against a "buffet" of too many links, both for user experience and because
excess navigation links dilute SEO "link juice" across too many pages; (2) filters,
so shoppers can narrow large catalogs by attributes (his own example: gender, price,
color) rather than browse everything; (3) advanced on-site search, naming Doofinder
by name as the tool his agency uses — it learns from aggregate customer behavior to
surface items people actually buy rather than just keyword-matching, and offers
Google-style autosuggest; and (4) checkout simplification, illustrated with a case
study from his own former company Crazy Egg, where splitting a single-page checkout
into a three-step flow (pricing → account info → checkout) lifted conversions by
roughly 10%. He names this last principle the "hoop theory": once someone gives a
small amount of info (name/email), they're far more likely to complete the rest of a
multi-step flow than to complete one long form up front.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-22, Neil cites COVID as having accelerated online commerce
  adoption by "three to five years" (self-reported estimate, no source given).
- As of 2020-10-22, Neil states the single most-clicked navigation item on his own
  neilpatel.com blog is a generic "Learn More" dropdown, not the top post or
  pricing/training links (self-reported, site-specific).
- As of 2020-10-22, Neil names Doofinder as his agency's advanced-search tool of
  choice, citing that it typically increases site sales by roughly 20% by learning
  which products customers actually purchase versus merely keyword-matching
  (self-reported vendor claim).
- As of 2020-10-22, Neil cites a Crazy Egg case study (his own former company):
  splitting a one-page checkout into a three-step flow (pricing page → account
  creation → checkout) increased conversions by roughly 10%.
- As of 2020-10-22, Neil names this checkout principle the "hoop theory": once a
  user completes a small initial commitment (e.g., name/email), they are
  significantly more likely to complete subsequent steps of a multi-step flow than
  a single long-form up front.
- As of 2020-10-22, Neil cites third-party stats in support of these tactics: Clutch
  (94% of customers want an easy-to-navigate site), HubSpot (80% of people stop
  doing business with companies over poor customer experience), Moz (onsite-search
  users convert at 4.63% vs. 2.77% for non-searchers), and Baymard (21% of cart
  abandonment attributed to overly complex checkout) — all third-party/self-reported
  citations, not independently verified here.
