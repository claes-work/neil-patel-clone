# 4 Tricks to Driving More Traffic to Your Website (Hint: They Utilize Your Email Signature)

## Summary
A short tactical video in which Neil Patel explains how to turn the email signature —
which sits at the bottom of every email you send — into a low-effort traffic channel.
He offers four tricks: (1) link back to your website in the signature; (2) mention one
social profile (Facebook, Twitter, or LinkedIn — pick just one) with a short call to
follow, since giving people too many options overwhelms them; (3) update the signature
with each new blog post ("Check out my latest blog post and please share it"); and
(4) link out to press coverage of you (CNN, Entrepreneur.com, etc.), which drives more
traffic to those articles, earning them more shares and backlinks, ranking them higher
in Google and indirectly sending traffic back to your site. He caps the tactic with an
overwhelm rule: never combine more than two of these asks in a signature at once. Neil
notes his own signature jokes about a fax machine and carries a "cocky disclaimer" that
lawyers love and readers ask to copy.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-13, Neil states:
- Email is still ubiquitous despite claims it is "dying in popularity"; if you have a
  job you are using email, so the signature is a reliable place to promote your business
  (as of 2017-08-13).
- Trick 1 — Link back to your website in the signature; without a link, no one will
  visit your site (as of 2017-08-13).
- Trick 2 — Mention one social profile (Facebook/Twitter/LinkedIn) with a simple ask to
  follow; pick only one, because giving people too many things to do is overwhelming and
  they end up doing nothing. More followers then convert into repeat traffic when you
  share site-related content on that network (as of 2017-08-13). (If you're already
  popular on social, you can skip this.)
- Trick 3 — Every time you publish a new blog post, update your signature to say "Check
  out my latest blog post and please share it"; readers will click through and share (as
  of 2017-08-13).
- Trick 4 — Link out to press/articles written about you (as startup founders do — e.g.
  a CNN or Entrepreneur.com feature). Driving traffic to those articles earns them more
  traction, social shares, and backlinks, ranking them higher in Google and indirectly
  driving traffic back to your website (as of 2017-08-13).
- Overwhelm limit: use no more than two of these tricks in a signature at the same time;
  three or four asks is too much and asks people for too much (as of 2017-08-13).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "In my email signature, I promote my own business. If you look at mine, it's kind of funny. I talk about a fax machine, what is a fax? I even have a cocky disclaimer."

> "Giving people too many things to do is overwhelming and they won't do anything, but if you just give them one they're much more likely to do it."

> "By driving more people to the articles that talk about you those article are going to get more traction, more social shares, more back links, they're going to rank higher in Google, and that indirectly is going to drive more traffic back to your website."

> "So, only pick two of them and do them at the same time. Never do three or four at the same time, it's just too overwhelming from people and it's asking them for too much."
