# The Number 1 Hack to Converting Visitors into Leads (Quizzes)

## Summary

Neil's #1 lead-conversion hack is **quizzes** — he says his own (neilpatel.com/quiz)
lifted his leads by **280%** vs. plain lead forms, but only if every step is followed.
Build it with a tool (**LeadQuizzes, Bucket.io, or Interact**) rather than custom code.
Rules: (1) **provide real value at the end** — deliver a report/asset that fixes the
taker's problem, or they feel cheated; (2) use **qualification questions** that both help
the taker and tell you if they're a good-fit lead (his own quiz asks whether they use
Google Analytics / run paid ads, which signals whether a site is established); (3) **send a
custom asset based on their answers**, tailored to their actual situation; (4) **never use
the word "survey"** — completion drops sharply; (5) **keep it to ~7–13 questions** (he aims
7–10) with only **1–4 answer options** each — too few questions floods you with unqualified
leads; too many kills completion.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-08-31, Neil said **quizzes increased his lead count by 280%** vs. lead forms,
  citing neilpatel.com/quiz.
- As of 2019-08-31, Neil said a quiz must **deliver an end-of-quiz asset of real value**
  or people won't convert and will be upset.
- As of 2019-08-31, Neil said quiz questions should double as **lead qualification** — e.g.
  asking about Google Analytics / paid-ad usage to gauge how established a prospect is.
- As of 2019-08-31, Neil said **avoid the word "survey"** (it lowers completion) and keep
  quizzes to **~7–13 questions** (ideally 7–10) with **1–4 options** per question.
- As of 2019-08-31, Neil said **too few questions (3–4)** yields more but poorly
  qualified leads and a worse close ratio.
