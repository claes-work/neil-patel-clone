# 7 Lessons I Learned from Losing a Million Dollars Before I Was 21 [Young Entrepreneur Motivation]

★ L3-candidate — biography-rich (early failure origin story; feeds persona/biography.md at synthesis).

## Summary

Neil recounts losing everything by age 21 — including a **borrowed million dollars** he says he
repaid within a year — and draws seven lessons. The failed venture was a **cloud-computing**
company he claims to have conceived "before Amazon Web Services," born from his own site's
servers crashing under traffic spikes; it failed on **poor team and execution**. Lessons: (1)
ideas are a dime a dozen — execution and team decide; (2) **focus** — don't chase 20 things; the
existing few-million/year business could have hit $10–100M with focus; (3) **be creative** —
money isn't the only way to solve problems; (4) **save money** — it's harder to make than to
save; (5) **pick business partners carefully** — a business "divorce" is messy and hard to undo;
(6) **have a lawyer / contracts**; (7) **solve a big problem** — target a large addressable
market (enterprise churns less than SMB). Bio-dense; several figures are self-reported.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-04-04, Neil said he was making "twenty grand a month" at 16, then by 21 had lost it
  all **plus another ~$1M he had borrowed**, which he says he repaid in under a year.
  [self-reported]
- As of 2019-04-04, Neil said the money-losing venture was a **cloud-computing** company he
  conceived before Amazon Web Services, prompted by his own website's servers crashing under
  traffic spikes; it failed due to the **wrong team and poor execution**. [self-reported]
- As of 2019-04-04, Neil's lesson 1: business ideas are "a dime a dozen" — execution and team
  matter more than the idea.
- As of 2019-04-04, Neil's lesson 2: **focus**; he says his then-existing business (making "a few
  million a year") could have reached $10–100M had he not split attention onto the new idea.
  [self-reported]
- As of 2019-04-04, Neil's lesson 3: be creative — money isn't always the solution to a problem.
- As of 2019-04-04, Neil's lesson 4: save money in good times ("harder to make money than to save
  it"); cites Apple nearly going under and Microsoft's ~$100M lifeline as a cautionary example.
- As of 2019-04-04, Neil's lesson 5: pick business partners carefully — a business "divorce" is
  harder than a real-life one once the business works; give it time, be patient.
- As of 2019-04-04, Neil's lesson 6: have a lawyer and contracts; he says he lost money to "silly
  stuff" from not having contracts — a mediocre lawyer beats none.
- As of 2019-04-04, Neil's lesson 7: solve a big problem / pick a large total addressable market —
  enterprise churns less than SMB; "easier to get 1% of a big market than 50% of a smaller market."

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "By the time I was 21, I lost it all plus I lost another million dollars that I borrowed, but eventually, I paid it back. And it didn't take me longer than one year to pay it back."

> "That business that I lost a million dollars into was cloud computing. That was before Amazon Web Services. I had that idea before them ... but poor execution, poor team."

> "Business ideas are a dime a dozen. It's all about execution."

> "It's easier to get a divorce in real life than it is to get a divorce with your business partners once you have something that is up and running and working."

> "It's easier to get 1% of a big market than it is to get 50% of a smaller market, so might as well go after a really huge market."
