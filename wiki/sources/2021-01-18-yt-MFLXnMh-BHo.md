# How to Find Lucrative Link-Building Opportunities in Under 60 Seconds

## Summary
A screen-recorded Ubersuggest tutorial: reverse-engineer competitor backlinks to find
outreach targets. Workflow: go to neilpatel.com/backlinks, enter a competitor's domain
(not your own), and pull up Ubersuggest's Backlinks report (domain authority, backlink
count, referring domains, organic traffic, new/lost links over time). Use "Advanced
filters" to show only followed links (no-follow links don't help rankings), one link per
domain, and filter for anchor text containing your target keyword (demoed with "SEO").
For each resulting linking site, click through to the source page that links to the
competitor and pitch a more detailed/updated version of your own content as a
replacement link. Use hunter.io (free tier: 50 searches) to find a contact email at the
target site when no author byline is available. Frames this as a "tedious but effective"
manual-outreach process: doing it for an hour a day for a year would meaningfully move
rankings. Explicitly tells viewers not to focus on domain authority as a target metric —
focus on link quality/relevance and the mine-your-competitors'-linkers method as the
easiest way to find who's likely to link to you too.
