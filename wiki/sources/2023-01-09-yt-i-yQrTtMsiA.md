# The Biggest Lie You've Been Told About Social Media

## Summary
Rebuts the claim that "social media marketing doesn't drive bottom-line
results," which he attributes to old-school business owners who treat social
presence as merely keeping up appearances. Backs the rebuttal with a stat
bank: 34% of consumers use social media to learn about brands/products; 70%
of marketers use Facebook ads (10M+ active advertisers); 83% of people used
Instagram to discover new products and 87% took a specific action after
seeing more information; 64% of buyers prefer messaging a brand on social
media over calling customer service; 43% of Gen Z consumers have purchased
directly from social platforms; 79% say user-generated content (UGC) impacts
their purchasing decisions. Prescribes a five-step tactical playbook: (1) be
present on every platform your ideal customers use; (2) post at least once a
day, repurposing content across platforms with adjustments; (3) respond to
every comment to build word-of-mouth and brand evangelists; (4) track which
content resonates and do more of it; (5) study competitors' campaigns via
Facebook's Ad Library for organic and paid ideas. Closes with an NP Digital
plug.
