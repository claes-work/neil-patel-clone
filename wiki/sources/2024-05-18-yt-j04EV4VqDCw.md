# Bonus episode #3: A sneak peek of what our AOA hangouts look like (Marketing School)

## Summary
A "bonus episode" that is a raw excerpt of an **Agency Owners Association (AOA)** coaching call
run by **Eric Siu** for AOA member-owners (Brandon, Kevin, Danielle, Ezra, John Abanov and others
named on the call). **Neil Patel does not speak on this episode** — he is referenced only in the
recurring AOA ad read ("group that Neil and I have") and once at the end ("once we get to ~50
paying members we'll do a call with Neil"). Eric walks through his internal "memo culture"
(citing Frank Slootman's *Amp It Up*), a velocity/speed framework for agencies, an anecdote about
a friend's $400M-ARR company that lost its edge after over-hiring, and fields member questions on
scaling processes and on why Reddit/Quora traffic is rising in Google results. Because none of
this is Neil-attributed, it trains **no persona data** — filed for completeness of the ledger and
because it documents Eric Siu / AOA context relevant to the co-hosted show.

## Key claims (Eric Siu — context only, NOT persona data)
- Eric writes internal "memos" (e.g. one titled "The Silent Killer of Companies") to drive
  organizational velocity, citing Frank Slootman's (ex-CEO, Snowflake/ServiceNow) formula: "if you
  narrow the focus, you increase the tempo, and you raise the standards." (Eric, 2024-05-18)
- Speed-of-execution analogy: a company completing a task in 2 days vs. 7 days is a 3.5x
  difference that compounds to ~75 vs. ~21 initiatives over 150 days. (Eric, 2024-05-18)
- A friend's ~$400M ARR company (raised $100–200M from a large financial institution) got sloppy
  after over-hiring and nearly fell apart before recovering; Eric frames this as a cautionary tale
  about losing organizational velocity. (Eric, 2024-05-18)
- Eric writes 1–2 memos per week; invites written feedback in the margins of a shared Google Doc
  rather than assuming he's right. (Eric, 2024-05-18)
- Reddit/Quora referrals and Discord community-management requests are rising because Google is
  favoring forum content in search results — framed as users wanting a proxy for other people's
  real experience rather than polished brand copy. (Eric + David Kim, member, 2024-05-18)

## Notable verbatim quotes
