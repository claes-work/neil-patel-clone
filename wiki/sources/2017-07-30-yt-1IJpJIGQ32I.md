# How to Build a Personal Brand Using Facebook | Be Like Neil Patel, Gary Vaynerchuk or Seth Godin!

## Summary
Neil gives a three-step Facebook playbook for building a personal brand, opening with Seth Godin, Gary Vaynerchuk, and Guy Kawasaki as examples of large personal brands. Step 1: post on a daily basis — consistent daily engagement is required, and every post (quotes, industry content, daily videos) must stay tied to the specific brand you want to build; he keeps his own content marketing-focused with occasional personal posts to connect on a human level. Step 2: engage within your community — reply to every comment and message, and participate in other pages and groups in your niche (helping people there pulls them back to your page). Step 3: leverage Facebook Live, which Facebook is heavily promoting as "the new version of reality TV"; Gary Vaynerchuk grew his brand partly through Facebook Live, and because Facebook notifies followers when you go live, more live video grows your fan base faster.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-30, Neil states:
- Facebook is the most popular social network and, per alexa.com, the third most popular site overall (as of 2017-07-30). *(Alexa metric; Alexa was retired in 2022.)*
- Step 1 — post daily; without daily engagement you won't build a big brand. Every post must relate to the type of personal brand you want (he keeps his marketing-focused, with occasional personal posts to connect on a personal level) (as of 2017-07-30).
- Step 2 — engage in your community: respond to every comment and message, and participate in other niche pages/groups (helping people there draws them to follow you) (as of 2017-07-30).
- Step 3 — leverage Facebook Live; Facebook pours attention into live video as "the new version of reality TV," and Gary Vaynerchuk built a large personal brand partly by leveraging Facebook Live (as of 2017-07-30).
- Because Facebook notifies people the moment you go live, more live video grows your fan base more quickly (as of 2017-07-30).
