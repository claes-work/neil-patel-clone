# The Two Marketing Careers That Will CRUSH IT in 2023

## Summary
A career-advice video (note: title says "2023" and "crush it," but spoken content and
ledger metadata both say the video is about "2020" trends — see attribution note on the
title/content date mismatch). Neil names two marketing careers he sees taking off:
(1) marketing data scientist — driven by the proliferation of ad platforms/data sources
(Google, Facebook, Pinterest, Taboola, Outbrain, Bing) making it hard to reconcile
conflicting metrics; cites a Data Mania job description and a Glassdoor average salary
of $61,473/year for the role; (2) director of growth marketing — driven by plateauing
growth as competition increases (quotes Howard Schultz on how cheap it now is to start
a business), tasked with cross-department ROI improvement (sales, engineering,
marketing, product); cites an Indeed average salary of $96,790/year. Neil frames both
through his own agency's lens, saying he regularly sees clients struggling to make sense
of fragmented data and plateaued growth.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-16, Neil said the proliferation of paid channels (Google, Facebook,
  Pinterest, Taboola, Outbrain, Bing) is what makes the marketing-data-scientist role
  newly necessary, versus 5-10 years ago.
- As of 2020-02-16, Neil cited a Data Mania description of the marketing-data-scientist
  role (generating insights, exploratory data analysis, advising management) and a
  Glassdoor average US salary of $61,473.
- As of 2020-02-16, Neil quoted Howard Schultz (Starbucks) saying it is now the cheapest
  and most affordable time to start a business — which Neil says has also made
  competition, and therefore growth, harder for existing companies.
- As of 2020-02-16, Neil cited an Indeed average US salary of $96,790 for a director of
  growth marketing, describing the role as identifying company-wide gaps and improving
  cross-departmental ROI (sales, engineering, marketing, product).
- As of 2020-02-16, Neil said he sees plateauing/stagnant growth as a common problem
  among his ad-agency clients.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Now is the cheapest and most affordable time to create a business." [Neil quoting Howard Schultz]

> "A growth marketer is someone who identifies a gap in the company, and they work with all departments to increase the overall ROI."
