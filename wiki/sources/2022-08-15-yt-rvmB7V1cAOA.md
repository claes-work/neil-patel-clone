# How to Get 1,000 Followers a Week on Instagram Organically

## Summary
A tactical Instagram-growth playbook. Neil cites Statista's 1B+ Instagram user figure to
justify the platform's marketing relevance, then lays out five organic-growth tactics:
(1) post at least daily, varying formats (images, video, quotes, memes, reels,
carousels, stories) to learn what the audience responds to; (2) go live weekly with
other Instagram accounts (starting with sub-10K-follower accounts and scaling up) rather
than solo; (3) post carousels at least weekly — he notes they don't always get the most
likes but drive strong engagement (multi-slide views, comments, follows); (4) respond to
every DM and comment; (5) leave thoughtful, helpful comments on other accounts' posts in
the same niche. He frames the underlying goal as follower-quality (conversion-capable
followers), not raw follower count, and estimates ~6 hours/week effort dropping to
~3-4 hours/week with practice.

## Key claims
- Cites Statista: 1B+ Instagram users worldwide (as of 2022-08-15, third-party-sourced).
- Five-part organic playbook: daily varied-format posting; weekly co-hosted lives
  starting with smaller accounts; weekly carousel posts; responding to all DMs/comments;
  thoughtful commenting on others' posts in-niche (as of 2022-08-15).
- Explicit belief: followers who don't convert "don't really matter" — the goal is
  brand-building followers who become customers, not follower count itself (as of
  2022-08-15).
- Effort estimate: ~6 hours/week initially, reducible to ~3-4 hours/week with practice,
  to sustain 1,000 new organic followers/week (as of 2022-08-15, self-reported).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Remember followers that don't end up converting don't really matter. You want followers to help you build a brand that converts people into customers.

> Don't go live by yourself. DM other Instagram users and ask them to be on an interview with you.

> It requires effort on your part, but it works. It's not a lot of steps, but it works.
