# My 7 Favorite SEO Strategies

## Summary
A recurring-favorites SEO playbook framed around "trends come and go but search stays."
Cites 68% of online experiences beginning with a search engine, 91% of searches
happening on Google, and 61% of B2B marketers saying SEO/organic drives more leads
than any other tactic. Seven strategies, all illustrated with Ubersuggest/NP Digital
tools: (1) choose relevant keywords via Ubersuggest's keyword-suggestion tool,
including its "ranking potential" feature that shows which keywords a given domain
has a realistic shot at ranking page one for; (2) optimize for voice search — cites
58% of consumers using voice search for local business info and Comscore data that
50%+ of US people have tried voice search; recommends AnswerThePublic.com for
question-based content; (3) make the site mobile-friendly — cites mobile as 58% of
Google searches, recommends CDN use (explains CDN mechanics with an Austin,
Texas-hosted-site example serving India/UK visitors from local servers) and
Ubersuggest's site-audit report; (4) optimize title tags — cites 15–40-character
title tags getting an 8.6% higher CTR than longer ones, and that curiosity-evoking
titles (repeats the "five benefits of green tea, number three will shock you"
example also used in the 2023-01-23 email-subject-line video) drive more clicks; (5)
improve site speed — cites ~70% of consumers say page speed affects purchase
willingness, ties to CDN/Ubersuggest site-audit again; (6) prioritize backlinks — cites
53% of SEO experts believing backlinks will one day be as important as search itself;
describes the neilpatel.com/backlinks → Ubersuggest Backlink Opportunities workflow:
enter your URL + 3 competitors, target sites linking to multiple competitors but not
you, and pitch them on superior content/design/product; (7) perform audits — checking
manual actions, organic traffic, HTTPS issues, and index issues via Google Search
Console and neilpatel.com/seo-analyzer, prioritized by ease-of-fix vs. ranking impact.
