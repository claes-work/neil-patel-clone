# 7 Steps to Ranking Number 1 on YouTube

## Summary
YouTube-SEO checklist. Opens citing YouTube has 1.7B unique monthly visitors, 62%
of whom access it daily, averaging 19 minutes/day on-platform (as cited, dated
2023-03-11). Seven steps: (1) keyword research via AnswerThePublic.com (trending,
daily-updated queries with popularity data) and Ubersuggest.com (broader keyword
lists); (2) incorporate researched keywords into the title while matching video
content to search intent, warning that keyword-stuffed titles with mismatched
content cause early bounce and hurt rankings; (3) optimize the video description
(YouTube recommends key terms in the first sentences, description under 200
words) and the video file name; (4) use relevant hashtags in titles/descriptions
for discoverability; (5) select the most specific, relevant category rather than
a generic one; (6) upload a custom thumbnail (YouTube-recommended 1280×720px,
≤2MB) that "pops" and drives click-through; (7) upload an SRT captions/subtitles
file rather than relying solely on YouTube's auto-generated captions, self-
reporting this genuinely helps rankings. Closes with an NP Digital plug for
YouTube SEO / social media marketing services.

## Key claims
- Cites: YouTube has 1.7B unique monthly visitors, 62% of whom visit daily,
  averaging 19 minutes/day on the platform (as cited, dated 2023-03-11).
- States keyword-stuffed titles whose video content doesn't match the keyword
  cause early viewer bounce, which hurts rankings (paraphrase, dated 2023-03-11).
- Self-reports uploading an SRT captions file (vs. relying on YouTube
  auto-captions) meaningfully helps video rankings (self-reported, dated
  2023-03-11).
