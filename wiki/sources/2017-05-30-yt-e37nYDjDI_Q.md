# 8 Steps to Making Your Blog Successful - Passive Income Online Blogging

## Summary
Neil Patel walks through an end-to-end playbook for launching a blog that generates income, pitched at aspiring bloggers who see stories of others earning five-figures a month. He starts with niche selection driven by personal passion (arguing you'll quit a lucrative-but-boring niche like mesothelioma) validated against market size, points to his neilpatel.com/howtostartablog tool for gauging whether a niche is too broad or narrow, and recommends one-click WordPress hosting (naming BlueHost, HostGator, GoDaddy, with BlueHost preferred). He then covers writing: only publish articles you yourself would read, validate topics by surveying friends, write persuasive headlines (pointing to Copyblogger's headline formula), outline intro/body-with-subheadings/conclusion, keep paragraphs to five or six lines, write conversationally using "you" and "I," ask a closing question and reply to every comment, and link out within posts. He stresses consistency (one post a week minimum, ideally three) over a six-month-to-a-year horizon to build a reader base, capturing those readers with free tools Subscribers.com and HelloBar.com for email and browser-push subscriptions. Finally he lays out three monetization paths — Google AdSense, affiliate links (naming ClickBank), and his favorite, selling your own products/services (subscription boxes à la Birchbox, or a $5–$9 e-book) — and closes with a patience message that meaningful income takes six months to a year.

## Key claims
- A lot of people generate five, 10, or 20 thousand dollars a month from a blog, though the kids earning thousands per day are rare (as of 2017-05-30).
- Niche selection should be driven primarily by passion, because you'll eventually quit a niche you don't love even if it's lucrative (e.g. mesothelioma / legal keywords) (as of 2017-05-30).
- A niche must be validated against market size — big enough that enough people search it, but not so broad it's unfocused; Neil's neilpatel.com/howtostartablog tool flags whether a niche is too big or too small (as of 2017-05-30).
- Beginners should use one-click-WordPress hosting to avoid needing technical skill; BlueHost, HostGator, or GoDaddy work, with BlueHost recommended (as of 2017-05-30).
- Only write articles you personally would want to read; validate topic interest by surveying friends and acquaintances before writing (as of 2017-05-30).
- Headlines must be appealing; Copyblogger's headline formula/tool lets you plug in keywords to generate persuasive headlines (as of 2017-05-30).
- Structure a post like a school essay — introduction, body with subheadings, conclusion — by writing the intro and conclusion, adding subheadings, then filling in the rest (as of 2017-05-30).
- Keep each paragraph to no more than five or six lines, because longer paragraphs (e.g. 10 lines) overwhelm readers (as of 2017-05-30).
- Write conversationally using the words "you" and "I" to drive engagement and comments; ask a question at the end of the conclusion to spur more comments, and reply to every commenter (as of 2017-05-30).
- Link out within your posts to back up your points or to other blogs that benefit readers (as of 2017-05-30).
- Publish consistently — at least one post a week, ideally three — and over six months to a year you'll build a large base of readers (as of 2017-05-30).
- Capture readers with the free tools Subscribers.com (browser push notifications) and HelloBar.com (email subscriptions) to bring people back to the site (as of 2017-05-30).
- Monetize via three routes: Google AdSense (pay-per-click ads), affiliate links (e.g. ClickBank commissions on others' products), and — Neil's favorite — selling your own products/services such as a subscription box (like Birchbox) or a $5–$9 e-book (as of 2017-05-30).
- You can mix all three monetization methods, and income rises over time; expect to wait a good six months to a year before earning decent money (as of 2017-05-30).
