# The Truth About Funnel Hacking: Is it Possible to Copy Someone Else's Business Model?

## Summary

Neil answers whether you can "funnel hack" — copy another business's funnel — and profit like
them. His verdict: **"the short answer is no, but the long answer is yes."** Blindly copying a
funnel fails because success depends on factors you can't clone (personality, voice, traffic
skill). To copy well: (1) don't copy the layout, **understand WHY it's built that way** — what
the upsells/downsells do and whether they get people results faster; (2) check **audience
relevance** — the more your visitors resemble theirs, the higher the chance it transfers;
(3) check whether you can **sustain the ad cost** — most funnels run on YouTube/Facebook ads,
and even if you spy on and clone their ads, they may have assets you don't (e.g. Lookalike
Audiences) that lift their ROI, so it comes down to **cash flow** to sustain months of spend;
(4) don't copy blatantly — **test in small segments** (start with a simple webinar or the
upsell/downsell), and expand only what proves out on your own audience. He plugs Neil Patel
Digital for funnel/marketing help.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-06-01, Neil said copying someone's funnel does **not** guarantee their results —
  personality, voice, and traffic ability are uncontrollable factors that gate success.
- As of 2019-06-01, Neil said to copy a funnel effectively you must **understand its structure
  and intent** (why the upsells/downsells exist, whether they speed up results), not just clone
  the pages.
- As of 2019-06-01, Neil said **audience relevance** is decisive: the closer your visitors are
  to theirs, the more likely a copied funnel converts; low relevance means no guarantee.
- As of 2019-06-01, Neil said cloned ads carry no guarantee because competitors may have
  **Lookalike Audiences** and other assets you lack; sustaining a funnel is ultimately a
  **cash-flow** question — it may take months to reach their ROI.
- As of 2019-06-01, Neil recommended **testing funnel elements in small segments** (e.g. a
  simple webinar) and scaling only what works for your audience, rather than copying wholesale.
