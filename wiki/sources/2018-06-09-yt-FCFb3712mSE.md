# How to Buy a Business: 4 Important Factors to Consider

## Summary

An interview-prompted monologue (a viewer references Neil's stated plan to acquire several
companies in 2018) laying out his acquisition criteria — which he says he usually keeps
quiet to avoid tipping off competitors. **(1) EBITDA / profit history:** he wants at least
three years of running revenue and profit, and pays **2.5–3× yearly profit — "not a dollar
more."** **(2) Fat to cut:** he targets companies with high expenses he can trim to make
more profitable ("cut the fat... it's a reality"). **(3) Strategic roll-up + traffic:** he
wants businesses in his own space that fit his core business, even with zero revenue, as
long as they bring traffic he can monetize — citing buying **kissmetrics.com** (the website
and its million-plus-visitors/month blog, NOT the company, software, or revenue) to merge
into neilpatel.com. **(4) Market leadership via multiple arbitrage:** he buys smaller
players (sometimes paying a struggling leader "a few hundred grand... a million dollars"
even while they lose money), typically not retaining teams, and merges them into his core
site to become the largest in his space — because leaders command far higher valuation
multiples (he cites 3–5× profit/revenue for normal companies vs. "10–20× revenue" for a
category leader). He frames this as "arbitrage on the multiple." He acknowledges cutting
teams "sounds ruthless" but says he goes "above and beyond" to place laid-off people in
other jobs and retains genuinely talented staff.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-06-09, Neil says he buys companies at 2.5–3× yearly profit and "won't pay a

- As of 2018-06-09, Neil says he targets companies with high expenses so he can "cut the

- As of 2018-06-09, Neil says he generally does not retain acquired teams, calls it

  people.
