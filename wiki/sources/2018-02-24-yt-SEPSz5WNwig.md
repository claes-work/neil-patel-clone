# How to Elevate Your Marketing Business | Why You Should INNOVATE, Not Copy Strategies

_Neil solo._

## Summary
Neil's argument: the biggest mistake marketers make is **copying** what works for others
while ignoring **timing** — what worked in 2012/2014 rarely works today, and if it does it's
**harder, slower, and more expensive**. He explains this structurally: **Google (worth ~$700B,
~80%+ of income from AdWords)** and **Facebook** have a financial incentive to make organic
results harder, to push you toward **paid ads** — so any organic tactic decays over time.
People copy NP Digital's content-marketing playbook and fail because **rankings were far
cheaper to earn back then**; spending $20–30K now won't replicate the old results. He states
his **internal hiring/philosophy standard**: if someone "can't one-up others with marketing
they don't belong in our company" — the job is to **innovate and do it better**, not copy.
As a dated proof point, he recalls **spending $10K+/day buying Facebook fans 3–4 years ago**
when organic reach was high, versus **not spending even $1K on fans now** because Facebook has
crushed organic reach — better to move that budget to another channel. Takeaway: you can
borrow a strategy, but you must **improve it** (cheaper, more efficient, better promotion),
or it won't work for you today.

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-24, Neil states:
- **The recurring marketing mistake is copying** — "just because something works for someone
  else doesn't mean it's going to work for you."
- **Timing is the forgotten variable**: tactics from 2012/2014 don't necessarily work now;
  when they do, they're **harder, take longer, and cost more**.
- **People copy NP Digital's content-marketing strategy and fail** because rankings were far
  easier/cheaper to earn years ago; **$20–30K spent now won't match** the old results.
- **The platforms have a paid-ads incentive**: Google is worth **~$700B** with **~80%+ of
  income from AdWords**, so it won't make rankings easy; Facebook similarly suppresses organic
  reach so you'll buy Facebook ads.
- **Internal standard/philosophy**: "if people can't one-up others with marketing they don't
  belong in our business… our company" — innovation over imitation is a hiring filter.
- **Facebook organic-reach decline (dated example)**: 3–4 years ago NP was **spending $10K+/day
  buying fans** on high organic reach; **now he wouldn't spend $1K** on fans because organic
  reach is crushed — reallocate that money to a different channel.
- **If you borrow a strategy, make it better** — cheaper, more efficient, better promotion —
  because "whatever you're copying is harder, takes longer, and is more expensive."
