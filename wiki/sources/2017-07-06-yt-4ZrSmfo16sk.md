# What is Affiliate Marketing And How Can You Leverage It

## Summary
A tactical primer on affiliate marketing pitched at founders with little or no marketing budget. Neil frames affiliate marketing as performance-based marketing: you pay affiliates a cost-per-acquisition (a lead or a sale you define) only when they deliver, which makes it capital-efficient for cash-poor businesses. He covers the two practical hurdles — recruiting affiliates (via networks and conferences) and making sure your own site actually converts the traffic they send — and closes with a fraud/refund safeguard (pay on Net 30).

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-06, Neil states:
- Affiliate marketing is performance-based marketing: you pay per acquisition (a lead or a sale, your choice) only when an affiliate drives it (as of 2017-07-06).
- You set the payout; his worked example: a $500 product at a 50% margin ($250) could pay affiliates $100 per sale (as of 2017-07-06).
- Affiliates choose their own traffic method — SEO, PPC, Facebook ads, etc. — and that's up to them (as of 2017-07-06).
- Recruiting affiliates is hard; rather than cold-hunting marketers on the open web, join networks like ClickBank (he also names JVZoo) which advise you on competitive CPA pricing so you neither overpay nor underpay (as of 2017-07-06).
- Pricing must be competitive: if a sale costs an affiliate ~$20 to generate, a $10 payout won't attract them — they'd lose money and demand more (as of 2017-07-06).
- Conferences like Affiliate Summit and Affiliate World are where affiliates look for new offers, and they also teach you how to price offers and incentivize affiliates (as of 2017-07-06).
- If affiliate traffic isn't converting, the usual culprit is your landing page, not the traffic quality — most affiliate marketers send qualified traffic (as of 2017-07-06).
- Fix conversion by running on-page surveys (he names Qualaroo), waiting for 30+ responses to surface objections, then addressing them; and by using Crazy Egg heatmaps (scroll/click/mouse tracking) (as of 2017-07-06).
- Pay affiliate payouts on a Net 30 basis to protect against refunds and credit-card fraud — don't pay out on sales you later refund (as of 2017-07-06).
- Affiliate marketing is "one of the most powerful" tactics especially for businesses without much capital, because you only pay for sales (as of 2017-07-06).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The cool part about affiliate marketing is that it's performance based marketing. If someone drives you a lead or a sale, you pay them out. You pay them on a cost per acquisition."

> "The last thing you want to do is be willing to pay someone $10 per sale when it costs them $20. ... They're going to be like: 'no, you gotta offer more.' You have to be competitive."

> "If people are driving traffic, but those visitors aren't converting into customers ... your web page not converting is usually the issue. So you want to run surveys. You can use Quaraloo.com."

> "When you're paying out the money for these sales, pay on a Net 30 basis. Because sometimes you'll get refunds or credit card fraud. You don't want to give out payouts for people who you're refunding."
