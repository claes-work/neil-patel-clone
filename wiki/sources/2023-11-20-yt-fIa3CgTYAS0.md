# Threads Is Generating 8.3X More Engagement Than Twitter

## Summary
Neil describes a personal, informal experiment run over a weekend: posting the same
content simultaneously to Twitter and the newly-launched Threads, then comparing
engagement-per-follower (controlling for the fact most people have more followers on
Twitter than on Threads and adjusting for posting-time differences) via an Excel
spreadsheet. His conclusion: Threads posts got roughly 8.3x more engagement per
follower than the same content on Twitter. He and Eric attribute the surge to Meta
deliberately boosting organic reach on a brand-new platform (comparing it to
LinkedIn's early push behind LinkedIn Live) both to bootstrap usage and, per Eric, to
undercut/"crush" Twitter — while noting Meta can't simply acquire Twitter due to
antitrust/monopoly concerns. Neil's practical takeaway: get on Threads now while
organic reach is inflated, since that boost tapers off as the platform matures. The
recording is briefly interrupted by one of Neil's children, confirming (self-reported,
no name given) that he has kids and prioritizes brief family interruptions during
recording.
