# Boosting Conversions with Email Marketing

## Summary
Solo interview covering personal branding, targeted-traffic quality, omnichannel marketing, email
marketing, personalization, influencer marketing, and career lessons. On personal branding: build
it through consistent multi-format content (video/audio/text, repurposed across formats and
platforms), networking/participating on others' channels, going live with other creators, building
an email list, and speaking at events — done consistently for three to five years, since "no one's
a fly by overnight success" and single-tactic efforts don't work anymore given market saturation.
On traffic quality: the common mistake is chasing volume over relevance, illustrated by his own
site ranking for "Discord" (hundreds of thousands of visits, zero conversions) versus lower-volume
but high-intent terms like "digital marketing agency." On omnichannel vs. multichannel: true
omnichannel requires the teams running different channels (SEO, paid, organic social) to actively
share lessons across channels; multichannel is just channel presence without that cross-pollination
and converts worse. On email: scrub unopened subscribers every 30 days to protect deliverability;
prefer text-based templates over HTML (cites a HubSpot test showing HTML emails had 20-25% lower
open rates, and states NP Digital gets 30%+ open rates with text-based emails); keep the
value-to-sell ratio at roughly 80-90% value / 10-20% sell (higher sell-ratio is fine for
e-commerce). On personalization: valuable at the segment level (a handful of audience buckets) but
diminishing returns from over-personalizing to the individual, even with AI making variation
cheaper. On influencer marketing: prioritize niche, well-engaged micro-influencers over follower
count, and feature the influencer on the landing page they drive traffic to. On career lessons:
focus (stop doing too many things at once) and pursue a large TAM (total addressable market) since
capturing a small fraction of a huge market beats a large fraction of a small one; building a
business and penetrating a market realistically takes five to ten-plus years.

## Key claims
- Personal-branding framework: consistent, repurposed, multi-format content across platforms,
  sustained for three to five years minimum; rejects "overnight success" as rare and short-lived
  when it does happen (self-reported framework, dated 2023-08-07).
- Traffic-quality principle: relevant, high-intent traffic beats raw volume, illustrated by his own
  site's non-converting "Discord" ranking versus converting "digital marketing agency" traffic
  (self-reported example, dated 2023-08-07).
- Omnichannel requires cross-team collaboration so lessons transfer between channels (SEO informing
  social, organic informing paid/email, etc.); without that collaboration it's multichannel, not
  omnichannel, and converts worse (self-reported framework, dated 2023-08-07).
- Email tactics: scrub unopened subscribers every 30 days; prefer text-based over HTML templates
  (cites a HubSpot test: HTML emails ~20-25% lower open rates; NP Digital's text-based emails get
  30%+ open rates); keep emails ~80-90% value content, ~10-20% sales content (self-reported/cited
  data, dated 2023-08-07).
- Personalization is valuable at the audience-segment level but shows diminishing returns from
  hyper-individual personalization, even with AI-enabled content variation (self-reported opinion,
  dated 2023-08-07).
- Influencer-marketing framework: prioritize engaged niche/industry micro-influencers over raw
  follower count, and include the influencer on the landing page they send traffic to
  (self-reported framework, dated 2023-08-07).
- Career-lesson pair: (1) focus — doing fewer things at once produces better results; (2) pursue
  large TAMs, since a small share of a huge market beats a large share of a small one; expects
  five to ten-plus years to penetrate a market (self-reported reflection, dated 2023-08-07).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> If they're not converting, it doesn't matter. What you need is the right quality, the right
> targeted audience and quality traffic.

> Omnichannel's when the channels are working together, multichannel is just you're on a lot of
> different channels but the experience isn't cohesive and similar and it just doesn't convert as
> well.

> Don't just sell in your emails, provide value 80 to 90% of the time and then sell 10% of the
> time.

> The biggest lesson I learned is focus... The other thing I learned is you got to go after big
> TAMs... It's really hard to capture 50% of a million dollar market. It's easier to capture 0.01%
> of a hundred billion dollar market.

> Reading isn't enough. You got to actually practice on your own site and that's the best way to
> learn.
