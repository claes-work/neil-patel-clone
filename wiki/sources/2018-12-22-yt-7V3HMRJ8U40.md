# Organic VS Paid Marketing Search Strategies: The Pros and Cons | Neil Patel

## Summary

A compare-and-contrast monologue on paid advertising vs. SEO that lands on "do both."
Paid pros: (1) scales as fast as your spend and delivers traffic today; (2) geo-targeting
down to a city; (3) dayparting — only run ads during business hours (e.g. a B2B firm
wanting phone calls shouldn't pay for 6pm clicks no one will answer); (4) aggressive
short landing pages that convert, whereas SEO's top-ranking pages tend to be ~2,000-word
content that gets read rather than bought. SEO pros: (1) traffic persists even if you
slow down, unlike paid which stops the moment you stop paying; (2) it's effectively free
(costs time/energy or some spend on content/links, but you can do it yourself); (3) most
searchers click organic listings over paid. Trade-offs: paid scales up/down faster and
controls audience/geo; SEO is slower (3–6 months, often over a year) and unguaranteed —
algorithms can "slap" you, while paid rarely gets pulled because you're paying. Verdict:
leverage every profitable channel — if paid is profitable, SEO will be too — because the
online market is now too competitive to rely on a single channel; you need an
omni-channel approach.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-12-22, Neil listed four pros of paid advertising: instant/scalable traffic
  tied to spend, precise geo-targeting (region or city), dayparting (choosing the hours
  ads run), and aggressive short landing pages built to convert.
- As of 2018-12-22, Neil said SEO's top-ranking pages are typically long (~2,000 words),
  so a 2,000-word PPC landing page gets read instead of converting — paid lets you use a
  short, aggressive, offer-focused page that converts a higher percentage of visitors.
- As of 2018-12-22, Neil listed three SEO advantages: traffic persists even if you slow
  down (paid stops the instant you stop), SEO traffic is "free" and can be done yourself,
  and the majority of searchers click organic over paid listings.
- As of 2018-12-22, Neil said SEO takes long and is not guaranteed — it can take 3–6
  months and usually over a year to see results, and algorithm updates can "slap" your
  rankings, whereas paid is rarely pulled because you're paying.
- As of 2018-12-22, Neil's verdict: don't pick SEO vs. paid — leverage both plus every
  other profitable channel (Google AdWords, Facebook Ads, content, social, email, push);
  if paid is profitable, SEO will be too, and today's competition demands an omni-channel
  approach.
