# The Most Important WordPress Plugin EVER - Top WordPress Plugins for SEO

## Summary
In this short 2017 tactical video, Neil Patel argues that the single most important step to optimize a WordPress blog for SEO is installing the Yoast SEO plugin, which he frames as making SEO simple on the WordPress platform (which he calls the best blogging platform, and free). He walks through the setup: install Yoast from the WordPress plugins section, use it to auto-generate an XML sitemap, then submit that sitemap URL to Google Search Console (which he still calls Google Webmaster Tools out of habit) so Google can index the site's pages over a day or two — noting the more pages you feed Google, the more get indexed. He emphasizes that Yoast lets you set a custom title tag and meta description per post, independent of the blog post's title (unlike default WordPress, which uses the post title as the title tag). His title-tag advice: include the keywords people search for, make it appealing, and use click-driving words like "now," "try," "free," or the current year (e.g., "updated in 2017/2020") to increase click-throughs; the meta description should likewise include target keywords in an appealing sentence. Beyond the plugin, he stresses two manual habits: cross-linking (interlinking related posts within body text so they get crawled better and rank higher) and using the right words and phrases in content — recommending the SEObook keyword density analyzer to see, by percentage, the words and phrases used by the top-ranking page for a target keyword, then integrating some of those keywords into your own post to lift rankings over time.

## Key claims
- WordPress is the best blogging platform and it's free, so there's little reason to pay for blogging (as of 2017-06-05).
- Installing the Yoast SEO plugin is the simplest, single most important way to optimize a WordPress blog for SEO (as of 2017-06-05).
- The Yoast plugin automatically generates an XML sitemap for you, which is a required first step after installing it (as of 2017-06-05).
- You should submit the Yoast-generated sitemap URL to Google Search Console (referred to as Google Webmaster Tools); Google then takes roughly a day or two to decide which pages to index (as of 2017-06-05).
- The more pages you feed Google, the more pages you'll get indexed (as of 2017-06-05).
- Yoast lets you set a custom title tag and meta description per post that need not match the blog post's title, whereas default WordPress uses the post title as the title tag (as of 2017-06-05).
- The title tag is the blue link at the top of a Google search result; the meta description is the sentence or two beneath it (as of 2017-06-05).
- Title tags should include the keywords people are likely to Google, be appealing, and can use words like "now," "try," "free," or the date (e.g., "updated in 2017/2020") to drive more click-throughs (as of 2017-06-05).
- When including a date in the title tag, use the date the content was written/updated (as of 2017-06-05).
- Meta descriptions should include your target keywords and be written as an appealing sentence to earn clicks (as of 2017-06-05).
- Cross-linking (internally linking related blog posts within the body text) helps posts get crawled better and rank higher (as of 2017-06-05).
- Using the SEObook keyword density analyzer on the top-ranking URL for a target keyword shows, by percentage, the words and phrases that page uses; integrating some of those keywords into your own post raises rankings over time (as of 2017-06-05).
