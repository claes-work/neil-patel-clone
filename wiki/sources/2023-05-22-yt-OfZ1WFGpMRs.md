# Unlocking Success: Omnichannel Strategies for 2023

## Summary
Solo conference talk on omnichannel marketing, opening with NP Digital's own channel mix as
proof-of-concept: a podcast with 1M+ monthly listeners, a website with millions of monthly
readers, and an email list of ~1.3M — framed as evidence NP Digital doesn't rely on one
channel, which he credits (alongside the tactics below) for being ranked the **21st fastest
growing company in the US**. Defines omnichannel via a symphony/orchestra metaphor: channels
run independently (the common mistake) create disjointed, upset-customer experiences, while
channels feeding into one coherent experience compound. Cites a Google stat that 90% of
consumers use multiple devices to complete a single task, and names **Nike** as an
omnichannel app/web/in-person exemplar. States the common failure mode is **overestimating
omnichannel's impact while underestimating its complexity**. Reiterates that follower count
≠ audience — gives a hypothetical where posting personal content ("lessons my mom taught
me") on his own Instagram would cost him enterprise-client credibility even if it drove
views. Frames all attention as earned against finite daily time (1,440 minutes/86,400
seconds) competing not just with rival brands but with sleep, eating, and family time.
Recaps NP Digital's two flagship tool acquisitions as "creating time" for users: bought
**Ubersuggest for $120,000** years earlier, invested "$3-5 million" building it out, grew it
to **36M users over five years**, and says it now accounts for **40% of NP Digital's revenue
and 40% of its customers**; bought **AnswerThePublic for $8.6M "last February"** (i.e.
February 2023) using the same free-tool-for-traffic playbook, generating **18M users over
five years** (a growth figure stated for a tool acquired months earlier, read as a
target/analogy rather than an achieved result — flagged, not smoothed over). Introduces the
**"peak-end rule"** (cites Colin Shaw) — customers remember a brand by its first and last
experience — and argues discounting is not required for a great peak-end experience if
product quality is high, citing Apple's no-discount policy. Lists omnichannel case studies:
**Disney** (seamless app/park/social personalization, including at the Marvel-ride level),
**Bank of America** (~$200-300B valuation, self-estimated, cited for using omnichannel data
to find where customers get stuck), **Timberland** (in-store near-field-communication tech
to answer customer questions without staff), and **Sephora** (tying online visits to
in-store purchases). Gives platform-specific tactics: **YouTube** ranks primarily on
first-24-hour view velocity (email/promote immediately on publish, and early performance
predicts performance months later); **LinkedIn** — citing Omnicore Agency's "<1% of users
create/share content" stat — rewards posts that generate substantive (non-yes/no) comments
within the **first 4 hours**; **Google** rewards brand signals, achievable by giving away
free tools (cites both Ubersuggest/AnswerThePublic and, informally, using ChatGPT to build
cheap tools — name-drops that "ChatGPT created the video game Pong in less than 60 seconds").
Frames community-building (Neymar/football fandom in Brazil as his example) and email-list
capture via free-tool signups as brand-loyalty levers. Cites HubSpot data that buyers spend
~13 days researching before purchase, arguing this window is best filled via omnichannel
touch (email, site, social, SEO, push). Gives a **traffic-mix health check**: no single
source (organic or paid) should dominate, and branded search should be under ~30% of total
traffic (over 50% signals under-leveraged omnichannel). Notes economic headwinds (PWC: 58%
of consumers cutting non-essential spend; Nielsen: 51% eating at home more) and recommends
receipt-based coupons and **livestream shopping** (cites BigCommerce: up to 20% conversion
lift) as counter-tactics, plus a broader shift from "omnichannel" to **"omni-shopping"**
(discover in one channel, purchase in another, interchangeably). Closes with an Expedia/
Travago (Expedia's Latin America brand) case study: omnichannel campaigns lifted conversions
**10%+** even for an already-known brand, and a warning against siloed single-owner-per-
channel team structures — channel learnings should cross-pollinate.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> For omnichannel to work, think of it as a symphony... In which everyone has to be playing
> together, and it has to work extremely well.

> Followers are not your audience. The more followers you have, doesn't mean you're going to
> succeed. You got to get the right type of followers.

> Loyalty is a function of memory, and this is a quote from Colin Shaw. And the way you
> create amazing loyalty is through the peak end rule.

> Look, omnichannel is no longer a buzzword, it's a necessity.

> If all your channels are working together, you're doing promotions with all your channels
> together. You're going to create a much better experience. You'll get more sales, and
