# 8 Unbelievable Marketing Tools That Will Transform Your Business — Ep. 2594 (Marketing School)

## Summary
Short classic daily Marketing School episode: a rapid tools roundup where Neil and Eric
alternate naming marketing tools they use. No speaker labels in the captions, so every
attribution here is inferred from content anchors (see Attribution notes) — but the
handoff chain resolves cleanly into an alternating split of roughly four tools each.

**Neil's tools/tactics (persona data):** (1) **Rask** for dubbing content into many
languages to get more views — hit-or-miss by region, so lean into the languages that
work and skip the ones that don't; video dubbing still looks off because the mouth
doesn't match, which he expects to be fixed within ~12 months. (2) **Mailmodo** for
AMP/interactive emails (buy a product, take surveys/polls inside the email) — conversions
"somewhat decent" but placement/deliverability is the problem (landing in the Promotions
tab). (3) A **custom AI-analytics tool** his team paid devs ~$15–20k to build (scoped at
<$5k, tripled on scope creep): all data plus per-channel LTV feeds an AI that emails
daily "nuggets" of what to change — with the caveat that it's not perfect (it told them
to stop profitable brand-term bidding, which would have let competitors bid and cost
revenue), so keep a human in the loop; anyone can now build this far cheaper. (4) His
**Content Decay tool** (~$13k to re-build): find declining articles, have AI update them,
republish → ~6–8% traffic lifts, "at least from an SEO standpoint."

Eric's tools (quarantined below): **Opus/Opus Clip** (viral clip + headline generation),
**Beehiiv** (newsletter platform, incl. its "Beehiiv Boost" CPA ad network), **retention.com**
(visitor→email identity match, now with a B2B version), **Gong** (call-scoring /
conversational intelligence + internal ops dashboards), and **Humanic** (buyer
intelligence for revenue teams).

Neil's closing meta-point is the reusable takeaway: he has blog posts breaking down tools
but most people use none of them — trying new tools gives a dopamine hit without output,
so pick ONE tool and actually use it (easiest: dubbing). Solid on-domain Neil material but
it restates known Neil pragmatism/tactics rather than introducing a framework — **not**
flagged ★ L3-candidate.

## Key claims (dated, speaker-tagged, paraphrase)
- Dubbing your content into multiple languages (via Rask) is an easy way to get more
  views, but it's hit-or-miss: dubbing 10–30 pieces, some regions/languages perform and
  some don't — do more in the languages that work, don't waste time on the ones that
  don't. (Neil, 2023-11-04) — content-marketing / ai-marketing.
- Video dubbing still looks "funny" because the mouth doesn't move in sync with the dub;
  he expects someone to fix this within ~12 months. (Neil, 2023-11-04) — ai-marketing.
- Mailmodo enables AMP/interactive emails — let people select and buy (e.g. a T-shirt +
  size) or answer surveys/polls inside the email instead of visiting the site;
  conversions are "somewhat decent," but placement/deliverability is the weak point
  (emails land in the Promotions tab), and it should convert better once that's cracked.
  (Neil, 2023-11-04) — email-marketing.
- Most people never act on their analytics: at conferences, few log in daily, more weekly,
  many monthly — but <5% actually make changes based on the data, and even most of those
  don't make truly actionable changes. (Neil, 2023-11-04) — analytics-cro.
- His team paid devs ~$15–20k (scoped <$5k, tripled on scope creep) to pipe all data into
  one place, feed it per-channel customer value/LTV, and have AI email daily "nuggets" of
  what to change; anyone can now build this much cheaper. (Neil, 2023-11-04) —
  analytics-cro / ai-marketing.
- The AI analytics isn't perfect — e.g. it told them to stop spending on brand terms
  ("profitable but you already rank"), but stopping lets competitors bid on those terms
  and costs more revenue; so it's a human-in-the-loop tool that makes the team more
  efficient at allocating marketing dollars, not an autopilot. (Neil, 2023-11-04) —
  analytics-cro / paid-ads.
- Content Decay play (~$13k to re-build the tool): find articles declining in popularity,
  have AI update/refresh them, and republish → ~6–8% traffic increases; "not crazy but it
  all adds up, at least from an SEO standpoint." (Neil, 2023-11-04) — seo / content-marketing.
- Meta-point: he has blog posts breaking down tools, but most people use none of them —
  just pick ONE of the tools mentioned and use it and you'll be better off; the easiest to
  start with is dubbing. (Neil, 2023-11-04) — content-marketing.
- Trying a new tool "feels so good — it's the dopamine hit," but you don't get much done;
  people spend time using tools instead of looking at the output. (attributed Neil — see
  notes, 2023-11-04) — content-marketing.
- A $25/month AI scheduling tool can replace an EA for booking calls. (attributed Neil —
  see notes; the co-host dismisses it, 2023-11-04) — ai-marketing.
