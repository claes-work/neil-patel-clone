# The Dumbest SEO Hack (That Works)

## Summary
Tactical video, self-described as "dumb" only in the sense of being dead-simple and
underused. Live-demoed on the neilpatel.com blog: every blog post's sidebar carries a
"Guides" section linking to ~13 cornerstone pillar pages (digital marketing, online
marketing, Google AdWords, Facebook advertising, remarketing, SEO, content marketing,
growth hacking, social media marketing, affiliate marketing, conversion optimization,
marketing automation). Google-searches each target keyword live (including a repeat
check in an incognito window to preempt a personalization objection) and shows
neilpatel.com ranking on page one for all ~13, attributing this to the sitewide
internal-link "juice" flowing to those pages from every post's sidebar. Advises using
the exact phrasing an audience actually searches/uses rather than the "correct" or
highest-volume term — e.g., choosing "AdWords" over the higher-search-volume "Google
Ads" because his audience still says AdWords, and "online marketing" over "internet
marketing" for the same reason — over keyword-stuffing for volume.

## Key claims
- States that neilpatel.com ranks on page one of Google for all ~13 of its sidebar-
  linked cornerstone "Guides" keywords, attributing it to consistent sitewide internal
  linking from every blog post (paraphrase, self-reported, demoed live including an
  incognito re-check).
- Cites the "Google AdWords" keyword at 550,000 searches and an $8.46 cost-per-click
  (paraphrase, tool-cited data point, source tool not shown on-screen in this video,
  not independently re-verified).
- States a deliberate practice of choosing internal-link anchor phrasing based on what
  his own audience actually calls a topic (e.g., "AdWords" not "Google Ads," "online
  marketing" not "internet marketing") rather than optimizing purely for search volume
  (paraphrase, previously-unlogged internal-linking-anchor-text principle).
