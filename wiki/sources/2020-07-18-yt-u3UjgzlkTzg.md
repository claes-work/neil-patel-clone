## Summary
This is the final lesson of the **eCommerce Unlocked** course (Module 4, Part 3),
marking the course's completion. Neil opens with a Marcus Aurelius quote ("Ambition
means tying your well-being to what other people say or do. Sanity means tying it to
your own action.") and frames COVID-19 as an accelerant for ecommerce adoption, citing
Amazon and Shopify stock gains during the pandemic's early days as evidence. He
recaps core metrics to track (cost of goods sold, ROAS, ROI) and names brands to study
across sizes (Skincare By Alana up to Louis Vuitton, Rolex, Dollar Shave Club). The
bulk of the lesson is an eight-point mindset/habits framework for ecommerce success:
(1) become a people-watcher to understand buyer psychology, (2) use Ubersuggest
regularly for keyword research, (3) be willing to adapt, (4) stay curious, (5)
"embrace your teammates" (recounts a Richard Branson anecdote: Branson credited his
team, not himself, for building the Virgin companies — Neil generalizes this to
Zuckerberg/Musk/Gates), (6) be persistent, (7) keep existing customers happy (cites
personal Amazon customer-service anecdotes: keep-the-wrong-item, surprise overnight
shipping), (8) form the right daily/weekly/monthly habits and find the part of the
work you love ("I don't take holidays... I don't count hours"). Closes recommending
students review the course material at least three times.

  framing.

  (being told to keep a wrongly shipped item at no charge; unprompted free overnight
  shipping) as examples of the "delight your existing customers" principle.
