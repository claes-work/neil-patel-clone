# How to Design A Beautiful Homepage That Ranks on Google - The Non-Designer's Guide

## Summary
Neil gives a homepage-SEO/CRO checklist: state the value proposition and unique selling
points immediately (example: TOMS Shoes' one-for-one donation pitch); add customer
testimonials for trust (cites a Boston Consulting Group study on authenticity driving
brand attraction); make the homepage mobile-friendly (over 50%, and in some industries
over 90%, of searches are mobile; Google does mobile-first indexing); simplify
navigation (recounts a Quick Sprout test where cutting nav items from eight to five cut
bounce rate 3% and added 2,049 extra clicks/month); optimize the homepage title tag and
meta description with keywords, not just brand name (cites that 8/10 people read a
headline/title but only 2/10 click through; ~six-word headlines do well); pull recent
blog posts onto the homepage to increase engagement (his own agency site NP Digital
also surfaces videos/podcasts on its homepage); for e-commerce, drive to content instead
of straight to product (cites Olay's eye-care test: driving to an article on dark
circles produced an 87% increase in click-throughs and 100% increase in conversions);
and close with clear, repeated calls to action throughout the page, not just above the
fold.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-03-07, Neil cited a Boston Consulting Group study finding authenticity is
  a top quality that attracts customers to a brand, tying this to the value of homepage
  testimonials.
- As of 2020-03-07, Neil said on his former site Quick Sprout, reducing homepage
  navigation elements from eight to five cut bounce rate by 3% and added roughly 2,049
  extra clicks per month.
- As of 2020-03-07, Neil said 8 out of 10 people read a title tag/headline but only 2
  out of 10 click through, and that roughly six-word headlines tend to perform well.
- As of 2020-03-07, Neil cited a test by skincare brand Olay: driving homepage traffic
  to an educational article about eye care/dark circles (instead of directly to
  products) produced an 87% increase in click-throughs and a 100% increase in
  conversions.
- As of 2020-03-07, Neil said his agency's own site (Neil Patel Digital / NP Digital)
  surfaces blog posts, videos, and podcasts on its homepage to keep visitors moving
  through the site and improve user metrics.
