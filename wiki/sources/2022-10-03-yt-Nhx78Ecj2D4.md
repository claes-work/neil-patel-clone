# Rethink Your Marketing With This Unorthodox Concept

## Summary
Opens with an extended retelling of his family-origin/mother story — born in London to
an Indian mother and Ugandan-born (Indian-diaspora) father, moved to California as a
baby — as the origin of a "give something away for free, then upsell" business
philosophy. New detail vs. the previously-documented 2018 telling: his mother, whose
Indian teaching degree wasn't valid in the US, offered a local preschool six months of
free teaching in exchange for a trial — and *also* negotiated that Neil and his sister
could attend that school for free during those six months (since she wasn't being paid
and couldn't afford daycare). After six months she was hired. Neil explicitly frames
this as "the free approach... she got her foot in the door... and eventually up-sold
herself," then maps the same structure onto Ubersuggest (bought for $120K in 2017,
rebuilt, given away free, upsold into NP Digital's ad-agency services) and a
third-party example (Orabrush's free tongue-cleaner → upsell to toothbrushes/mouthwash,
acquired by Dentek then a GTCR-owned roll-up). States three rules for the tactic: the
free item must have real perceived value; it must be cheap for you to provide; and you
need something bigger and higher-margin to sell the resulting leads.
