# SEO Hack: Internal Linking Strategy

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode (Neil with interviewer Adam from Viewership) answering a viewer
(Yusuf) on how to use internal links to rank a specific keyword (his example: "long
winter coats"). Neil's playbook: point internal links from other pages back to the main
target page (article or product page) using descriptive anchor text, but do it
**naturally, not from every page and not shoved in the footer** — in-content text links
outperform sidebar/footer/navigation links. Over-optimizing (500 pages all linking with
the identical exact-match anchor) risks looking spammy to Google. Vary the anchor text,
only link when contextually relevant, and never send users to a destination that
mismatches the link text. Internal linking works for any keyword/industry and applies to
both content and e-commerce pages. Key timing caveat: rankings don't move immediately —
Neil says he typically sees internal links "kick in" around **five months** later. Bonus:
go back and add internal links to OLDER posts/pages, not just new ones, for faster gains.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-06-21, Neil says internal links with keyword-relevant anchor text pointing
  to a target page build that page's authority and rankings over time, but the effect is
  slow — he "usually" sees it take **around five months** before internal links boost
  rankings.
- As of 2018-06-21, Neil says in-content ("in-text") links outperform links placed in
  sidebars, footers, or navigational elements for passing anchor-text value.
- As of 2018-06-21, Neil warns against over-optimization: linking from hundreds of pages
  with the identical exact-match anchor looks unnatural/spammy; vary anchors, use words
  that "make sense," and keep it readable.
- As of 2018-06-21, Neil says internal linking applies equally to content pages and
  e-commerce/product pages, and only add a link when it is contextually relevant (never
  mislead users about where a link goes).
- As of 2018-06-21, Neil recommends retroactively adding internal links to older posts
  and pages (not just new ones) to raise rankings faster.

## Notable verbatim quotes
