# A Simple Marketing Tactic That Still Works

## Summary
Neil Patel describes a decade-old, still-working tactic: proactively seeking out interviews (podcasts,
blog posts, video interviews) as a brand- and business-building channel. He self-reports it has helped
him close consulting deals "anywhere from six figures to even over seven figures." His method: email
people who already interview others in your space, state up front what value-adding topic you'd cover
(not a pitch about your own business), and use hunter.io to find their contact email when it isn't
public. He supplies a template pitch structure — compliment the show, note a covered topic (X, Y, Z) plus
a gap they missed (A, B, C) that the audience would value, reference existing proof of your expertise
(prior articles/appearances), and close with a low-pressure offer. He frames it as a numbers game: many
early interviews will get little to no audience, and that's expected — use small placements as a
springboard to progressively bigger ones, and always deliver strong content regardless of reach, since
mediocre content on a small show will cost you bigger invitations later. He emphasizes patience: results
compound over years, not one-off appearances. Closes with the standard NP Digital PR/sales pitch.

## Key claims
- Neil has used the "get interviewed everywhere" tactic for roughly 10 years (as of 2022-07-07,
  self-reported).
- Self-reported outcome: the tactic has been responsible for closing consulting deals ranging from six
  figures to over seven figures (as of 2022-07-07, self-reported/unaudited).
- Recommended tool: hunter.io, to find interviewer/show-host email addresses (as of 2022-07-07).
- Outreach template: compliment the show → cite covered vs. missed topics (value framing, not
  self-promotion) → reference existing proof of expertise (prior articles) → low-pressure close (as of
  2022-07-07).
- Strategic framing: treat interviews as a springboard — small/low-reach placements build toward larger
  ones; deliver consistently high-quality content regardless of an individual placement's reach, since
  mediocre content there will prevent bigger future invitations (as of 2022-07-07).
