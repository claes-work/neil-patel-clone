# YouTube Algorithm EXPLAINED (7 Insider Secrets)

## Summary

Q&A Thursday (Neil with interviewer/co-host **Adam Lodolce** of Viewership). Question from
"The Nine-to-Five Misfits" (~487 subs): Facebook reach is drying up under algorithm
changes — how do small creators grow on YouTube? Neil and Adam give a stack of YouTube
tactics: **(1) keyword research** for YouTube like you'd do for SEO (tools: vidIQ, Google
Ads Keyword Planner, Ubersuggest — which also pulls YouTube/Google Suggest). **(2)
Curiosity-evoking, click-worthy titles** — Adam's key point is that unlike Google (search-
driven), *most* YouTube traffic is **non-search** (browse/suggested/autoplay), so the video
must be click-worthy to earn those impressions. **(3) Upload a real transcription/SRT**
(from Rev.com, ~$1/min) rather than relying on YouTube's auto-captions — a manual SRT tells
YouTube definitively what the video is about and "holds more weight." **(4) Use
advertising/retargeting** to push new videos to people who liked or commented on past
videos — driving engagement, which YouTube rewards. **(5) Channel authority compounds**:
like Google ranking sites by domain authority, YouTube boosts channels whose videos perform
— strong videos lift the whole channel (a channel full of no-engagement videos is "a desert
of nothingness"). **(6) Personality/emotion in delivery** — Adam's biggest lesson to Neil:
decide *how you want the viewer to feel* (motivated, laughing) before filming; loosen up,
have fun, because if you're not enjoying it, the viewer won't either. **(7) Length &
watch-time**: aim ~5–6 (up to 10) minutes; very short 2–3-min videos hurt you, and **keep
people on YouTube** rather than pushing them back to your site (external-link CTAs hurt
rankings — treat YouTube as a branding channel). Closing tip: repurpose the same video onto
Facebook/LinkedIn anyway — "something is better than nothing."
