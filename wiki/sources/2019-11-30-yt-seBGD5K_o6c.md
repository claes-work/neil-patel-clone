# The Social Network That Will Explode in 2023 - Should You Leverage It?

## Summary
In this solo talking-head video, Neil Patel identifies TikTok as the fast-growing social network to watch and walks through a four-question decision framework for whether a given marketer should invest in it now. He opens with market stats (TikTok as a top-installed app, huge user base and time-on-app, heavy ByteDance ad spend) then gives the four questions: (1) Is my audience there? — TikTok skews young first and expands to other demographics later, so older-audience brands may need to wait; (2) Is first-mover advantage your edge? — if so, get in early because early traction is cheaper and easier than fighting a saturated platform later; (3) Can you create the content this platform likes? — TikTok rewards short-form humor/sarcasm and fast visual storytelling, which he says he personally can't repurpose from his serious/educational content (unlike creators like Logan/Jake Paul who could), so he'd have to make all-new content; (4) Do you play it safe? — if time is a commodity and you can't risk a platform that may not take off, dabble but don't go "full fledge" until it's proven, comparing it to how he was early on Instagram but only pushed hard once he knew it would blow up. He recommends risk-averse marketers wait ~6 months to see if it goes mainstream.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-11-30, Neil said a new social network (TikTok) has grown very fast since 2017 and in some months is downloaded more than Facebook and Instagram.
- As of 2019-11-30, Neil cited The Wall Street Journal that TikTok is the third-most-installed app worldwide with 1.2 billion monthly users globally, was the most-downloaded App Store app worldwide in Q1 2019 (followed by YouTube, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger), and that the average user spends 45 minutes on the app daily; and that ByteDance spent roughly $1 billion on ads (as much as $3 million/day) in the last year, much of it on traditional social networks.
- As of 2019-11-30, Neil said the first question is whether your audience is there — TikTok's dominant demographic is young people first, then it expands to other age groups, so older-demographic brands may need to wait while younger-demographic brands can use it now.
- As of 2019-11-30, Neil said if being first has always been your competitive advantage, it's worth getting on now, because early entry makes traction, followers and traffic much easier and cheaper ("pennies on the dollar"), whereas entering after saturation is harder, slower and costlier — analogizing to building a blog, Facebook or Instagram in their early days.
- As of 2019-11-30, Neil said the third question is whether you can create the content the platform likes: TikTok's short-form format lends itself to humor and sarcasm and fast visual storytelling, long-form doesn't work, and he personally couldn't repurpose his serious/educational YouTube/Facebook/Instagram content to TikTok — he'd have to create all-new content — whereas creators like Logan and Jake Paul could potentially repurpose theirs.
- As of 2019-11-30, Neil said if you play it safe and time is a commodity, you can't risk spending tons of time building a TikTok audience only to find the platform doesn't take off; he counts himself in this group — he loves being early but doesn't always go all-in, and cited being early on Instagram but only pushing hard once he knew it would blow up (now chasing one-to-two million followers).
- As of 2019-11-30, Neil recommended risk-averse marketers wait at least six months to see whether TikTok goes mainstream before investing heavily, though signing up and spending some time is fine; once it gets popular among other age groups, you should definitely leverage it.
