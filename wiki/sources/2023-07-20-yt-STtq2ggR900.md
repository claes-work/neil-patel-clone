# The Older Your Company The Better Your Marketing

## Summary
Co-hosted Marketing School segment (main-channel republish, Eric Siu named explicitly). Central
thesis: for the enterprise/Fortune-1000 clients NP Digital works with, the single biggest driver of
revenue is not SEO, paid, or social — it's brand, built cumulatively through word-of-mouth and
referrals that only accrue with time in business. States this holds "good economy, bad economy, B2B,
B2C" and kicks in meaningfully past the 10-year mark (some improvement at 5 years, a bigger shift
past 10), conditional on having a genuinely good product/service — a bad product doesn't improve
with age. Illustrates with a KISSmetrics-era anecdote: while bootstrapping the startup to avoid
dilution, he cold-called prospects for SEO consulting work, landed a ~$240,000 first-year contract
with early Airbnb (pre-major-scale), and observed that Airbnb's own growth was driven overwhelmingly
by paid advertising and product/brand strength rather than SEO — most of its organic traffic was
just branded ("Airbnb") search. Draws the counterintuitive point (counterintuitive given he and Eric
both run ad agencies) that great product + longevity in business beats marketing polish for driving
revenue at scale. Eric adds that trying to teach "it takes time" in a course format performs poorly
because people want fast results.

## Key claims
- States that for the Fortune-1000/enterprise clients NP Digital serves, brand — built via word of
  mouth and referrals accumulated over time in business — is consistently the largest driver of
  revenue, more than any specific paid or organic marketing channel, holding across economic
  conditions and B2B/B2C (self-reported, dated 2023-07-20).
- Frames the ~10-year-plus mark as the inflection point where longevity-driven brand/referral
  effects become the dominant growth lever, with a smaller shift noticeable around 5 years — but
  only for businesses with a genuinely good product/service (self-reported opinion, dated
  2023-07-20).
- Recounts cold-calling early Airbnb for SEO consulting work during the KISSmetrics era and landing
  a roughly $240,000 first-year contract; observes that Airbnb's own revenue at the time came
  predominantly from paid advertising and branded search rather than SEO (self-reported
  biographical anecdote, dated 2023-07-20) — new biographical detail (specific contract figure and
  client) not previously logged in the corpus.
