# Has Blogging Changed Too Drastically in 2023? (The Explanation Behind Your Weak Results)

## Summary
Neil argues blogging's underlying success rules haven't changed — the competitive
landscape has, due to market saturation (he cites 1.5B+ websites, 380 new sites created
per minute, and roughly one blog per seven people). (Title again says "2023"; see
attribution note — content/publish metadata place this as a 2020 video.) He says ranking
still takes patience (up to 3 months for early traction unless you're a top brand like
Amazon, Nike, or "even me"), and lays out tactics: target keywords with an Ubersuggest
SEO-difficulty score of 40 or under; optimize for mobile/voice search (citing that over
50% of Google queries are now mobile, and over 50% of Google searchers use voice, per
Comscore) via structured markup; use long-tail keywords early on; use Google Analytics
correctly to find which channels/pages actually convert, not just which get traffic;
don't be afraid to monetize content by mentioning products/services; and take an
omnichannel approach, citing Eric Schmidt's view that brands are how Google
differentiates good sites, and the "rule of seven" (7 brand interactions build strong
recall) as reasons cross-channel presence compounds SEO/brand signals. Recommends Google
Trends to benchmark brand-query volume against competitors.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-20, Neil said there are over 1.5 billion websites on the web, with 380
  new websites created every minute, and roughly one blog for every seven people —
  framing the challenge as market saturation, not algorithm changes.
- As of 2020-02-20, Neil said it can take up to three months to see initial SERP
  traction from consistent, correct SEO, and that top brands (Amazon, Nike, himself,
  Wikipedia, Moz, SEMrush) see faster results than newer sites.
- As of 2020-02-20, Neil said to target keywords with an SEO difficulty score of 40 or
  under when starting out (via Ubersuggest).
- As of 2020-02-20, Neil cited that over 50% of Google search queries globally are from
  mobile devices, and (per Comscore) over 50% of Google searchers use voice search.
- As of 2020-02-20, Neil said businesses often misuse Google Analytics — installing the
  tracking code isn't enough; you must set up conversion tracking to see which
  channels/pages actually drive sales, not just traffic.
- As of 2020-02-20, Neil referenced Eric Schmidt's view that brands are the signal
  Google uses to distinguish good sites from bad, and cited the "rule of seven" (seven
  brand interactions build a strong connection) as a reason omnichannel presence helps
  rankings via brand queries.
- As of 2020-02-20, Neil recommended Google Trends to compare a brand's search-interest
  trend against competitors' brand names.
