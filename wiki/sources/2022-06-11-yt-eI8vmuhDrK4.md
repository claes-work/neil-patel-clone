# Kylie Jenner is a Genius For This

## Summary
Neil uses Kylie Jenner as a case study for what he considers the real marketing genius behind
influencer success: not paid brand promotion (which he says converts poorly because audiences know
it's paid) and not the "Keeping Up with the Kardashians" fame itself (which predates her adult
involvement), but converting an already-fostered, topically-relevant audience into a self-owned
product. Because Kylie posted about beauty/makeup/cosmetics consistently, launching Kylie Cosmetics
converted that specific audience far better than a paid endorsement would have, since followers know
it's genuinely her product. He cites Forbes-sourced revenue figures and a 2020 partial sale to Coty,
then generalizes the audience-to-product model beyond Kylie's scale to Gary Vaynerchuk (VeeFriends)
and to himself — explicitly naming his own following as "a large part of the reason" NP Digital
succeeded — closing with the advice to build an audience, sell your own product/service to it, and
optionally sell the resulting business (in full or in part) once it has value.

## Key claims
- Kylie Jenner's audience size cited: 330M+ Instagram followers, 40M Twitter followers, 33M Facebook
  fans, 38M TikTok followers with 484M+ cumulative TikTok likes (as of 2022-06-11, figures as
  self-reported/observed by Neil, not independently sourced here).
- Neil's thesis: paid social promotion of someone else's product converts weakly because audiences
  discount it as paid; a creator's own product converts much better because the audience trusts it's
  genuinely theirs (as of 2022-06-11).
- Kylie Cosmetics generated $200M+ in sales in 2019 per Forbes; in 2020 her revenue dropped but she
  sold 51% of the business to Coty for $600M, implying a $1.2B valuation (as of 2022-06-11,
  registry/press-sourced via Forbes attribution, not self-reported).
- Model generalized to Gary Vaynerchuk's VeeFriends (cited at $90M+ in transactions) and to Neil
  himself, who states his own marketing-industry following "is a large part of the reason why my ad
  agency, NP Digital is successful," recurring the 700+ employees and INC Magazine 21st-fastest-growing
  claims (as of 2022-06-11).
- Advice generalized: build a following, sell your own product/service to it, and — once valuable —
  either sell the business (potentially repeating the cycle) or keep it and keep growing if you love
  the work (as of 2022-06-11).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> What she's a genius for is leveraging her audience to build a billion dollar company... if you create your own product or a service that resonates with your audience, you are much more likely to generate sales because they know it's your product and it resonates to them.

> Look at Gary V. He doesn't have many followers as Kylie, but he has had over 90 million in transactions for VeeFriends. Or look at me, I have just a fraction of the followings of even Gary V... but I'm in a lucrative industry, which is marketing. My following is a large part of the reason why my ad agency, NP Digital is successful.

> So build a following, sell your own products and services and that's a recipe for success. And when you can, if someone's willing to buy your business, sell it like Kylie Jenner and you can always rinse and repeat, or if you love what you're doing, sell a portion like Kylie Jenner and just keep going.
