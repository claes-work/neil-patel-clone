# The Content Marketing TRAP: Why Writing Content Can Drive ZERO Traffic

## Summary
Reinforces the topic-cluster / thoroughness doctrine already established in the wiki:
writing on a single keyword isn't enough — cover the full topic cluster, even
low-traffic adjacent keywords, using the recurring "how to tie a tie" length-vs-intent
example (short content for simple intents, long/thorough for complex topics — cites
Ubersuggest's Top Pages and Keyword Ideas ("keywords I can rank for") reports as the
research workflow, and the Backlinks Opportunity report for competitive link-gap
mining. Closes on a conversion-tracking point: driving 100,000 visitors from a keyword
with zero sales "doesn't matter" — recommends Google Analytics goal/conversion
tracking to focus content on topics that actually convert, not just topics that drive
traffic.

## Key claims
- Reinforces "traffic without conversions doesn't matter" — recommends setting up
