# 8 Things About SEO to Unlearn in 2019 (And What to Do Instead)

## Summary

Neil lists eight outdated SEO beliefs to unlearn — a compilation that consolidates several themes
from this era. (1) **External links aren't everything** — build **internal links** too (relevant,
not just keyword-rich anchors); his own rankings for "online marketing"/"SEO" rely on
interlinking through content and sidebar. (2) **Don't write content for search engines** — dumping
Ubersuggest keyword lists into a page is writing for engines; rewrite/extend/reformat for people,
because Google uses **user metrics** (bounce back to SERP = "your content sucks" regardless of
backlinks/keywords). (3) **High-traffic no-intent keywords are a trap** — "SEO" drives no
business; "SEO agency" converts. (4) **Quantity of backlinks is a myth** — cites friend **Todd
Malicoat**, who ranked a forex site on page 2 in under six months with **fewer than 20 links** by
getting already-top-ranking pages to link out; quality over quantity. (5) **SEO isn't tricks/
gimmicks** — it's putting the user first (mirrors Google's mission). (6) **Stop focusing on
rankings** — Ahrefs found #1 gets the majority of clicks only **49% of the time**; he out-traffics
higher rankers for "SEO tips" via **rich snippets ("ranking zero")** and better title tags/
descriptions. (7) **Stop keyword-focus the bad way** — exact-match domains and keyword-stuffed
titles/metas fail; use copywriting to earn clicks. (8) **"Content marketing replaces SEO" is a
myth** — without the fundamentals (CTR, on-page code, URL structure) content won't rank; he cites
**removing dates from his URLs → +100,000 extra search visitors/month**. Overlaps the 2019-05-12
"stop ranking #1" and 2019-05-09 "user-first" videos.
