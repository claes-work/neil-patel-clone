# How to Promote Your Blog Content When Nothing is Working

## Summary
A per-channel content-promotion playbook. Cites stats (40% of marketers call content
marketing very important per HubSpot; 94% use social/content distribution per SEMrush)
to frame omnichannel promotion as standard practice, and names his own five channels
(Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter). Per-platform tactics: Instagram —
Reels/Stories/IGTV outperform static posts, plus live video; LinkedIn — video and
long-form, thought-provoking text posts with a strong first-line hook (gives an
improvised example hook), reasoning LinkedIn's "see more" click is itself an engagement
signal that boosts distribution; Facebook — paid is explicitly said to outperform
organic, and paid spend to existing followers also lifts engagement on unpaid posts;
email — a blast every time he publishes (2-3x/week), using Hello Bar or OptinMonster to
build the list; push notifications — via subscribers.com, lower engagement than email
but incremental reach. General rule: make social content that supports/relates to the
blog post with a clear CTA. Bonus tip: use Ubersuggest's "content ideas" report (social
share counts per keyword) to plan content before writing it, not just to promote after.

## Key claims
- States explicitly that organic Facebook reach for content promotion "doesn't do as
  well" and that paid is the primary lever there, while also arguing paid spend lifts
  the engagement rate — and therefore the organic reach — of a page's unpaid posts as a
  secondary effect (self-reported, dated 2021-10) — social-media, paid-ads.
- Publishes his own concrete cross-channel promotion cadence: an email blast 2-3x/week
  tied to every blog post, plus a push notification via subscribers.com each time
  (self-reported, dated 2021-10) — email-marketing, content-marketing.
- Frames the Ubersuggest "content ideas" report as a pre-writing planning tool (not only
  a post-hoc promotion aid), reusing the same feature already documented for headline
  research (paraphrase, dated 2021-10) — content-marketing, seo.
