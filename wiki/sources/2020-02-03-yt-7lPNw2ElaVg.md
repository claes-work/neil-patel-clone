# 8 Things To Master in SEO - Do You Know Them?

## Summary
Neil lists eight SEO skill areas to master: (1) studying search results/competitors —
who ranks, what backlinks they have, what page types rank (use Ubersuggest to see
top-100 rankers, social shares, backlinks); (2) planning/strategizing — target keywords
under a 40 SEO-difficulty score early, save competitive terms (e.g. Wikipedia-level)
for later; (3) writing — outsource via Rev.com/Trint transcription or ProBlogger's job
board if you're not a strong writer; (4) basic HTML — title/meta tags, FAQ schema,
Google Analytics tracking, site hierarchy (or use Yoast/Rank Math plugins, or hire cheap
via Upwork); (5) analytics — measure dwell time, bounce, device-specific conversion;
(6) self-management — prioritize daily SEO tasks (Ubersuggest's site-audit report can
help sequence them); (7) constant learning — algorithm updates (cites the "medic
update"), industry blogs, conferences, documenting experiment conclusions; (8) cold
outreach — link-building via email/social/phone, recommending the tool Milkshake for
templates and live-chat support.

## Key claims

  their social shares, backlinks, and other ranking phrases.

- As of 2020-02-03, Neil said SEO results should be measured via dwell time, bounce
  rate, conversion rate by device, time on site, and traffic/growth rate by channel.
- As of 2020-02-03, Neil said Ubersuggest's site audit report can help prioritize which
  SEO tasks to tackle first based on ranking impact.
- As of 2020-02-03, Neil referenced Google's "medic update" as an example of the kind of

- As of 2020-02-03, Neil recommended the tool Milkshake for cold-outreach link-building
