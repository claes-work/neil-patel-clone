# How Google Bard Can Actually Do Marketing For You

## Summary
Short co-hosted segment, recorded the day after Bard's wider public release. Eric shares a live
demo: he asked Google Bard "What CRO suggestions would you have for singlegrain.com to drive
conversion rates up?" and Bard returned a suggestion to replace a generic "Get Started" CTA
button with something benefit-specific like "Sign Up for a Free Consultation." Neil frames why
this is notable: unlike generic web-search advice, Bard is analyzing the actual page content and
producing a contextual recommendation, and the specific CTA advice given (benefit-oriented CTA
copy outconverting generic CTAs) matches what he already believes is generally true. Both agree
the advice is decent but not great — "not the best changes, but they're kind of cool" — a useful
starting point, not a human-replacement. Eric notes Bard's response latency (30 seconds–2
minutes, possibly due to launch-day congestion) and highlights Bard's simultaneous plugin
rollout, which would let it integrate with tools such as Ubersuggest or AnswerThePublic for
faster, better-informed marketing insights.

## Key claims
- States that Bard's page-specific analysis (vs. generic web-search advice) is a meaningfully
  different, more useful capability for marketers (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-08-26).
- Affirms the CTA principle demonstrated by Bard — a benefit-oriented call to action ("Sign Up
  for Your Free Consultation") generally converts better than a generic one ("Get Started") —
  as an established, pre-existing belief of his (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-08-26).
- Assesses Bard-generated marketing advice, as of 2023-08-26, as "not bad" but also "not
  amazing" — a useful starting point that cannot yet replace a human (self-reported opinion,
  dated 2023-08-26).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> The advice isn't going to be bad, it's not going to be amazing. You can't replace a human yet,
> and it's far from it, but it's better than nothing. It's a good starting point.

> For it to know that this is your call to action button like Get Started and you should change
> it to something more custom and related to the offering... that's very relevant and that is
> generally true.
