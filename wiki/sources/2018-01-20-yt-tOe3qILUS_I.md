# How to Find Your Passion and Discover Work You Love (Hint: You Won't Learn THIS in School)

## Summary
A mindset/career talk arguing that school won't teach you what you actually want to do —
you only find it by trying. Neil (whose own mother is a teacher, "they are amazing") says
childhood dream jobs fade, and by your twenties you feel lost while assuming everyone else
has it figured out (they don't — plenty of law grads regret it after "seven years … and
200,000 of loans"). He's pointedly ambivalent about college: "I would have made … two to
three times more money in my life if I never went to college." His method: **try something
new every day, for free, and pay attention to what you hate** — eliminating what you don't
want eventually reveals what you do. He tells his **origin story** as proof: he wanted to
be a doctor (family of doctors, "Dr. Patel"), then a computer scientist (quit because he
hated lab classes), then a business owner — his **first business failed** because putting
up a site doesn't bring visitors; he **cleaned restrooms/toilets at a theme park** to pay
marketing agencies who failed him, ran out of money, learned marketing himself, got good
at it, and fell in love with it. Core belief: **what you're good at and what you're
passionate about go hand in hand**, discovered through trial (uses "you were best at
football" as the analogy). High-value persona/biography source; near-twin of the same-batch
[[2018-01-06-yt-tgiNidFnpyI]].

## Key claims
As of 2018-01-20, Neil states:
- **School won't prepare you for the real world** ("what you learn in school usually won't
  prepare you"), though teachers are valuable — his **mom is a teacher** and he thinks
  they're amazing (as of 2018-01-20). ★ persona/biography
- You **won't know what you like until you try**; childhood dream jobs fade, and in your
  twenties you feel lost while wrongly assuming peers have it figured out (as of 2018-01-20).
- On college he's neutral-to-skeptical: "I think I would have made … **two to three times
  more money** in my life if I never went to college because I lost a lot of years" — but
  it's a personal call (as of 2018-01-20).
- Method: **try different things each day, for free** — you don't need to get paid; you'll
  find you love a few and hate most, and identifying what you **don't** want leads you to
  what you do (as of 2018-01-20).
- Belief: "usually what you like and what you're good at tends to go hand in hand," and
  that's what becomes your career path (Gladwell 10,000-hours theme, as in the companion
  video) (as of 2018-01-20).
- **Origin story**: wanted to be a **doctor** (family of doctors, "my last name is Patel,
  I'll be Dr. Patel") → **computer scientist** (quit because he "hated … lab classes") →
  **business owner**; his **first business failed** — "popping up a business doesn't get
  people coming to your website" (as of 2018-01-20). ★ persona/biography
- He **cleaned restrooms and toilets at a theme park** and used that money to **pay
  marketing agencies who failed** to deliver; when he ran out of money he **learned
  marketing himself**, got good at it, and "fell in love with it" (as of 2018-01-20). ★ persona/biography
- Advice to young entrepreneurs/students: "go out there and try different stuff each and
  every single day or week — you'll figure out what you hate … what you like … and what
  you're good at" (as of 2018-01-20).
