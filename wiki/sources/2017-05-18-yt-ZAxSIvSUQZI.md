# 3 Ways to Promote Your Website When You Don't Have Any Money

## Summary
A short tactical video in which Neil Patel argues that a marketing budget is not required to grow website traffic, because social media is free and content can be created in any format (video, text, audio). He walks through three no-cost promotion methods — answering questions on Quora, distributing content across social networks, and posting in niche forums — each aimed at leveraging other platforms' existing audiences to drive visitors back to one's own site.

## Key claims
As of 2017-05-18, Neil states:
- A large marketing budget is not needed to grow a business; social media is free and content can be created in any form (video, text, audio/podcasts), then pushed out on social media to gain traffic.
- **Method 1 — Quora:** Search Quora for the questions people in your space are asking, find the popular ones (high votes), and answer them helpfully. Helping people raises your visibility, drives them back to your website, and can lead to purchases.
- He cites his own Quora profile as evidence, saying he has answered hundreds of questions and gets hundreds of thousands of views on his questions and profile each month, at no cost.
- **Method 2 — Social media distribution / repurposing:** After writing a blog post, share it across Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn, and share your content continually and everywhere.
- His specific tactic: write the post once on his site, then republish it on LinkedIn with a few added paragraphs before a "click to continue reading the rest" prompt that sends readers back to his site; on Twitter he posts a snippet, and the 140-character limit forces people to click back to the full article.
- A secondary Twitter tactic: search Twitter for people asking questions in your field, reply in 140 characters, and link to your own website when you have content that answers their question.
- **Method 3 — Forums:** If you lack a popular Twitter profile or don't want to use Quora, Google for forums in your niche (e.g. search "marketing forum"), answer questions you can help with, and place a link to your website in the response footer or signature to bring visitors back.
- The recurring premise across all three: leverage another platform's traffic to funnel visitors back to your own site, and more traffic means more sales.
