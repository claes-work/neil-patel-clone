# How to Get Millions of Visitors Like Quora

## Summary
A standalone tutorial reverse-engineering Quora's traffic model as a content-ideation
playbook. Neil pulls quora.com through Ubersuggest's Traffic Analyzer to show that
Quora's top pages are almost all narrow, question-phrased ("how many ounces in a
gallon," "what is delta S in chemistry") and get very few social shares yet still
drive massive organic traffic — proof, in his framing, that answering simple
questions in depth is a durable SEO channel independent of social virality. He then
walks the generalized method: type an industry seed keyword into Ubersuggest, open
the "Questions" filter under keyword ideas, and harvest the green (low-competition)
question keywords, doing this across several seed terms (his own examples: digital
marketing, affiliate marketing, Facebook) to build a large set of question-based page
ideas. The pitch is cumulative — no single question page rivals Quora's volume, but
hundreds of thorough, in-depth answer pages compound into millions of visitors over
time, specifically from Google rather than social referral.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-03, Quora's most-trafficked pages are narrow question pages with
  minimal social shares (e.g., a "how many liters in a gallon" page with a single
  Facebook share), which Neil cites as evidence that question content ranks and
  drives traffic independent of social distribution (self-reported observation via
  Ubersuggest's Traffic Analyzer).
- As of 2020-10-03, Neil's recommended workflow: seed Ubersuggest with an industry
  keyword, filter keyword ideas to "Questions," and prioritize the green
  (low-SEO-difficulty) terms — Quora's own success, per Neil, comes from targeting
  questions that are easy to rank for because they face little competition.
- As of 2020-10-03, the strategy's payoff is aggregate, not per-page: many individual
  question pages each drawing modest traffic accumulate into "millions and millions
  of visitors over time" from organic search.
