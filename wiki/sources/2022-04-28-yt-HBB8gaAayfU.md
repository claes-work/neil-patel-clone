# How to Grow a Brand New TikTok Account FAST (Step by Step)

## Summary
Neil Patel gives a six-step framework for growing a brand-new TikTok account, framed
around his own experience: he says he grew his own TikTok account from zero to over
30,000 followers in about six months (as of 2022-04-28), faster than he's been able to
grow on Instagram in recent years, and claims hundreds of thousands of views on three of
his videos — a scale he says he hasn't hit on Instagram. His steps: (1) find inspiration
from two-to-four smaller creators (not million-plus-follower accounts) similar to your
niche, studying the high-impact videos that broke through the algorithm early in their
account; (2) go broad on content topics rather than niching down early, testing formats
(vlogs, tips, education, entertainment, even dancing) to see what performs before
narrowing; (3) in the first month, batch-record content (he records 16 videos at a time)
and publish three-to-five times a day to gather data quickly; (4) study how top creators
hook viewers in the first seconds, hold attention through the middle, and deliver enough
value to earn a follow, using watch-time drop-off to diagnose weak spots; (5) always use
captions since many viewers watch muted; (6) track core metrics (views, likes, comments,
shares, and TikTok's in-app traffic-source data) and double down on what's working.

## Key claims
- Neil grew his own TikTok account from zero to over 30,000 followers in roughly six
  months, faster growth than he achieved on other platforms in recent years because they
  are more competitive (self-reported, as of 2022-04-28).
- Three of his TikTok videos reached "hundreds of thousands" of views, a scale he says he
  has not achieved on Instagram (self-reported, as of 2022-04-28).
- When starting out, model after two-to-four smaller creators similar to your niche
  (not accounts over a million followers) and study the specific videos that broke
  through the algorithm early in their growth (as of 2022-04-28).
- New TikTok accounts should go broad on content topics rather than niching down
  immediately, testing multiple formats before focusing (as of 2022-04-28).
- In the first month, plan and batch-record at least a week of content at once and aim
  to publish three-to-five times a day to collect data fast; Neil records 16 videos in a
  single sitting (as of 2022-04-28).
- Study how top creators hook viewers in the first few seconds, sustain engagement
  through the middle, and deliver value that earns a follow; use watch-time data to spot
  drop-off points (as of 2022-04-28).
- Always add captions since many viewers watch without sound, which increases engagement
  and reduces drop-off (as of 2022-04-28).
- Track views, likes, comments, and shares, plus TikTok's traffic-source breakdown, to
  identify and double down on what is working (as of 2022-04-28).
