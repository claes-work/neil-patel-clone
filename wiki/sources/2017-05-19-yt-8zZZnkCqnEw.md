# 2 Easy Steps to ALWAYS Go Viral - How to Make Viral Ads, Viral Content and Viral Marketing Videos

## Summary
Neil Patel explains a repeatable process for consistently producing content that goes viral, motivated by the frustration of pouring time into a blog post that no one reads. The method has two research steps plus an outreach follow-through. First, use Google Trends (google.com/trends) to type in keywords and phrases within your space and see what is currently popular or going viral, then write content tied to those rising topics so you "catch the wave" while it is hot rather than covering something that peaked months or a year ago. Second, especially in a niche, use BuzzSumo.com to find the latest content that has performed well over the past few months to a year, then write a more detailed version of it — his example is turning a competitor's "10 ways to do X" into "101 ways to do X" — on the premise that a more thorough take on an already-proven topic will outperform. He then reiterates the outreach tactic from prior videos: in BuzzSumo use "view sharers" to see everyone who shared a competing piece on Twitter, track down their email addresses, pitch them your related/better article, and after they agree, send it over with a light postscript asking them to share.

## Key claims
- To consistently create viral content, combine trend research, more-detailed content, and outreach rather than relying on luck (as of 2017-05-19).
- Use Google Trends (google.com/trends) to type in keywords and phrases in your space to see what is popular; its homepage also surfaces what is going viral right then (as of 2017-05-19).
- Writing content tied to currently-trending topics gets more traction than writing about something that peaked six months to a year ago — you want to "catch the wave" while a topic is rising (as of 2017-05-19).
- Google Trends provides this trend data for free (as of 2017-05-19).
- Use BuzzSumo.com, especially in a niche, to find the latest viral content from the last few months and last year, then write a similar but more detailed piece (as of 2017-05-19).
- Out-detailing a proven post drives virality: if a competitor wrote "10 ways to do X, Y, and Z," publish "101 ways to do X, Y, and Z" (as of 2017-05-19).
- In BuzzSumo, click "view sharers" to see everyone who shared a piece on Twitter; take their Twitter user IDs, Google their names, and find their email addresses for outreach (as of 2017-05-19).
- Outreach messaging: reference the specific article the person shared, offer your related/better article, and after they agree, send it with a P.S. asking them to share ("If you shared it, not only would it make my day, it would make my year") (as of 2017-05-19).
- Content that does not get read demotivates writers; a systematic viral process prevents that (as of 2017-05-19).
