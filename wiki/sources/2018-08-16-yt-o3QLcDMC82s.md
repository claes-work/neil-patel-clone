# How to (EASILY) Repurpose & Share Content Across Platforms

## Summary

Q&A Thursday (Neil with interviewer Adam Lodolce). Question from "Radoslav": should you
keep content native to one platform, or share it everywhere? Neil: if you've made a piece
of content, **publish it on every platform** to get the most views — YouTube, Facebook,
Instagram, LinkedIn. His standout data point: **LinkedIn video** currently outperforms
everything on first-week views — Facebook gets ~15,000–30,000 views, YouTube maxes around
~10,000 (but YouTube keeps growing over time thanks to search), while **LinkedIn "explodes"**
and delivers the most early traffic. Key mechanic when distributing everywhere: **upload an
SRT caption file** for every video (LinkedIn/YouTube/Facebook all accept them); he gets
transcripts from **Rev.com** at ~$1 per minute (faster turnaround costs a premium). Optimize
each cut **per platform** — LinkedIn caps at ~10-minute videos, IGTV allows longer than
regular Instagram's 60 seconds — so map each platform out with your editor. He cites **Tai
Lopez** as someone who does this aggressively: live-streams simultaneously to YouTube,
Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, filming with a rig of iPhones (Neil's been on his show). The
throughline: creating a lot of content and distributing it across every channel is how you
get ahead — "release it on every single platform out there, don't stick with one."

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-16, publish every piece of content on all platforms (YouTube, Facebook,
  Instagram, LinkedIn) rather than keeping it native to one — the marginal downside is nil.
- As of 2018-08-16, LinkedIn video gives the most first-week views (vs. FB ~15k–30k, YouTube
  ~10k), while YouTube compounds over time via search.
- As of 2018-08-16, upload an SRT caption file to every platform; Neil sources transcripts
  from Rev.com at ~$1/minute (premium for faster turnaround).
- As of 2018-08-16, optimize each cut per platform's limits (LinkedIn ≤~10 min; IGTV allows
  longer than Instagram's 60s) — plan with your editor.
- As of 2018-08-16, he cites Tai Lopez multi-streaming live to YouTube/Facebook/Instagram/
  Twitter simultaneously (filmed with multiple iPhones) as an example of aggressive
  cross-platform distribution.
