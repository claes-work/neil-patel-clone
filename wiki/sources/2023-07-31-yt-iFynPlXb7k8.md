# How to replace your paid ads with SEO

## Summary
Solo backstage interview filmed at "ADCON 2023" immediately after Neil headlined the event
(explicitly stated in the intro, and continuous with [[2023-07-27-yt-mI6oxV52OR0]]'s same
"adclients.com" host/coaching-business context). States he has been doing Google SEO for "almost 22
years-ish" as of this recording (dated 2023-07-31) — consistent with a ~2001 start in his teens.
Walks the host, whose coaching-education business has scaled to roughly $500K/month via paid
advertising with essentially no prior SEO investment, through a from-scratch SEO starting point:
(1) content — "give away the farm": convert the majority (cites 50–80%) of paid coaching-program
material into free, text-based blog articles, since text-based pages (not video) are what typically
rank; (2) keyword research via Ubersuggest — start from a broad seed term, narrow into
specific/long-tail variants, prioritize higher search-volume and higher-CPC keywords (higher CPC
signals more valuable/converting traffic, and contrary to the host's assumption isn't always more
competitive), and use Ubersuggest's per-domain filtering to surface "low-hanging fruit" keywords the
site already has a realistic shot at ranking for; (3) content quality — reiterates E-E-A-T
(Experience, Expertise, Authority, Trust) as the bar AI-assisted content (ChatGPT/Bard/Ubersuggest AI
Writer) still needs human modification to clear, since Google penalizes regurgitated,
easily-findable information; (4) backlinks — describes Ubersuggest's Backlink Opportunity report
(input your domain plus competitors' to find sites linking to multiple competitors but not you, which
signals topic-relevant, non-exclusive prospects to pitch) as the core manual-outreach workflow, with
infographics (embeddable, shareable) and free tools (cites BMI calculators as a fitness-niche
example, buildable via ChatGPT or bought cheaply on CodeCanyon) as scalable link-generation assets;
explicitly rejects automating outreach or delegating it to a generalist VA — recommends a specialist
instead. Flags on-page tactics like keyword bolding/underlining as no longer relevant, and states
old-school link sources (government/edu domains) still help marginally but aren't a priority. Sets a
timeline: "three articles a week" for about a year before meaningful traffic and ranking growth
appear, with backlink acquisition described as slow-and-steady (a few per week ramping toward
5–10/week over a year) rather than a numbers game — "you don't need quantity to rank, you just need
quality content." Closing framing: a page that ranks #1 but gets clicked-back-from ("bounce") signals
Google that the page is low quality and should be demoted, regardless of backlink count.
