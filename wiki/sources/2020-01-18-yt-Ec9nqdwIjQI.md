# How to Build an Email List Fast and for Free — My Secrets to Reaching 270k+ Subscribers

## Summary
Solo list-building video, framed against the cliché: everyone knows "the money is in the
list," nobody explains how to build one. Neil states his own number — **over 270,000
active email subscribers** — and gives four capture mechanics plus a maintenance
routine. (1) **Self-discovery / assessment giveaways**: audit tools (an accounting site,
a health/weight audit) and calculators (mortgage), buyable for a few dollars on
**CodeCanyon** if you can't build them; the email is the price of seeing the result. His
favourite form is the **quiz** — questions about strengths, weaknesses and capabilities
with an assessment delivered along the journey, built with **LeadQuizzes**; he points at
**neilpatel.com/quiz** as his live example, says it collects "a ton" of emails, and
explicitly invites viewers to copy his format and design. (2) **Evergreen webinars** via
**WebinarJam** — recorded once, replayed so it still feels live; because people engage for
20–30 minutes they are "much more likely to buy." (3) **Exit-intent popups** via
**Hello Bar**, using a **yes/no** question ("do you want to increase your traffic?") where
*both* answers route to an email ask — a "no" is read as "I already have traffic," so the
follow-up offers to double their conversion rate instead. He argues the downside is nil
("if someone's going to leave your website, what harm do you have just asking?") and
notes Hello Bar's **cookie-based suppression** so returning visitors aren't asked again,
and that it works on mobile. (4) **In-text lead magnets / content upgrades** — a cheat
sheet or PDF matched to the article, emailed in exchange for the address; he calls it one
of the best strategies he has ever used and says it works *because* it takes effort most
people won't spend. The counterweight is **popup restraint**: two or three opt-ins max,
five is already too many, some sites look "like NASCAR" with 13–14; prefer one vendor
that suppresses the others. Maintenance: **email at least weekly**, **scrub the list
monthly** (mailing non-openers pushes you from inbox to spam), and **sell at least
monthly** — "there's no point in having a list if you're not generating income from it."
