# How To Rank #1 For Competitive Keywords - Module 4 - Lesson 1 - Content Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week-four (final week) lesson 1 of **Content Marketing Unlocked**
([[2020-08-15-yt-NRU7Bsb9OEo]]): long-term ranking strategy centered on brand as an
SEO signal. States Google makes "roughly nine algorithm changes per day on average"
(no source cited for the figure). Quotes ex-Google CEO Eric Schmidt ("Brand is a
solution, not the problem. Brands are how you sort out the cesspool") and
ex-head-of-Web-Spam Matt Cutts on Google's site-quality classifier (citing IRS,
Wikipedia, NYT as high-trust examples). Argues brand strength (branded search
volume, brand mentions, share of voice) compounds with domain authority to drive
rankings, using Nike, American Express, Wells Fargo, BMW, Mercedes, and Emirates as
consistency-of-messaging examples. States a belief in the **"Rule of Seven"** — seven
brand touches before someone remembers/becomes an evangelist. Directs viewers to
worksheets for defining brand story, USP/value proposition, and target-market
insights. Shifts to conversion optimization: prioritize About Us, Product
Detail/Listing, Exit, Landing, Checkout, and Pricing pages; recommends exit popups
(anticipating pushback: "I know you're like, 'Neil, exit popups, it's intrusive, it
sucks'") and a lead-nurture cadence of roughly **three to four educational emails
before a sell**. States he does not recommend monetizing content via ads or
affiliates, preferring selling one's own products/services. Notes "over a billion
blogs on the internet" makes content marketing more competitive than when he
started.
