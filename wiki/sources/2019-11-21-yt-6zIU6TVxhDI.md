# How To Find the Perfect Affiliate Product to Sell That Your Audience Will Love

## Summary
In this solo talking-head video, Neil Patel lays out a five-step framework for choosing affiliate products that convert without damaging your reputation with your audience. Step one: survey and talk to your audience to learn their problems, needs, expertise level (beginner/intermediate/expert), preferred learning method, and prior purchases — using tools like live chat, Qualaroo, or SurveyMonkey — so you match product difficulty to audience level. Step two: brainstorm every possible solution in a filter-free blank document, browsing other affiliate sites for ideas. Step three: find category top-sellers, using Amazon's best-seller lists and the Affilitizer Chrome extension to surface affiliate programs; he lists retail networks (Amazon Associates, eBay Partner Network, Jet.com, Target, Walmart), general networks (ClickBank, JVZoo, OfferVault, Rakuten, Warrior Plus, FlexOffers, ShareASale, Commission Junction, Avangate, MaxBounty, PeerFly) and education networks (Udemy, Treehouse, Coursera, QuickStart, 360training). Step four: vet each product against criteria — it must be directly connected to your content, and it must genuinely help people (invoking a Warren Buffett-style "protect the brand over short-run money" philosophy). Step five: don't just plaster links/ads; blend products contextually into tutorials, in-depth reviews, and product comparisons with clear calls to action. He closes plugging Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil said one of his most-asked questions is how to choose affiliate products, and that following a step-by-step framework sells far more than just pasting an Amazon affiliate code and hoping for sales.
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil said to start by surveying and talking to your audience — asking their biggest challenges, burning questions, what product/service/tool they wish existed, their expertise level, preferred learning method, and past purchases — using live-chat tools, Qualaroo, or SurveyMonkey, or open-ended questions.
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil said matching product level to audience matters: selling beginner products to an all-expert audience (or vice versa) yields few conversions; sell beginner products to beginners, then intermediate and expert products as they progress.
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil said to brainstorm all possible solutions in a filter-free blank document and browse other affiliate sites for ideas.
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil recommended finding category top-sellers via Amazon's best-seller lists and the Affilitizer Chrome extension, which flags affiliate programs and gives direct sign-up links as you browse the web.
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil listed affiliate networks by type: retail (Amazon Associates, eBay Partner Network, Jet.com Affiliates, Target Affiliates, Walmart Affiliate program); general/software (ClickBank, JVZoo, OfferVault, Rakuten, Warrior Plus, FlexOffers, ShareASale, Commission Junction, Avangate, MaxBounty, PeerFly); education (Udemy, Treehouse, Coursera, QuickStart, 360training).
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil said the product must meet two criteria: (1) be directly connected to the content you write about, or you'll make no money and anger your audience; and (2) genuinely help people, or you burn your reader base and lose trust in the long run.
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil attributed a philosophy to Warren Buffett: given a choice between short-run money and something that burns your brand, take the option of not making the money and protect the brand, because it creates longevity.
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil offered a personal test for "does this help": ask whether it educates, solves a nagging problem, removes friction, speeds up a process, makes things easier/cheaper/more effective/more elegant, or raises the audience's status among peers — and whether you'd use and recommend it yourself.
- As of 2019-11-21, Neil said don't just scatter links and ads; blend products contextually into content via tutorials, in-depth reviews, and product comparisons (signing up as an affiliate for the compared products too), with a clear call to action.
