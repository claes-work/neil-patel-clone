# Double Your eCommerce Sales With A Few Simple Tweaks - Module 2 - Part 1 - eCommerce Unlocked

## Summary
eCommerce Unlocked lesson on conversion-rate optimization (CRO) for e-commerce.
Opens with a baseline: 1-2% of visitors convert to paying customers industry-wide
(cites Amazon's $75B quarterly revenue as the extreme upper end), then a **quantified
CRO-value example**: 600 visitors/day at $45/sale — a 1%→2.5% conversion lift moves
annual revenue from ~$98,000 to ~$246,000 (+$147,000/yr) with no added traffic cost.
Product-detail-page (PDP) checklist: HD hero image, logo top-left, phone number
top-right ("you're not Amazon... we have to go a little bit more further"), detailed
descriptions blending logic and emotion, multiple product angles/video/GIF ("humans
respond 6,000 times faster to visual images than text" — his framing), compressed
images for load speed, optimized meta descriptions (checked via Ubersuggest's site
audit), an FAQ section, and CTA-button wording ("Add To Cart" outperforms "Add to Bag"
per a cited Elastic Path study). Cart-abandonment section: cites a 69.23% average
cart-abandonment rate (~$260B lost, his framing) and a Baymard Institute figure that
better checkout design + abandoned-cart email sequencing can recover 35.26% more
conversions. Reviews: cites that 41% of shoppers will buy from unfamiliar brands based
on reviews and ~95% of customers consult reviews before buying ("I base almost all my
purchases on Amazon based on the reviews. If the review suck, I don't buy."). Closes
on scarcity/urgency tactics (real, not fabricated, inventory/shipping-speed framing)
and a Shopify upsell-app recommendation (Zipify Pages).

## Key claims
- As of 2020-06-29, Neil said industry-wide e-commerce conversion rates run 1-2% of
  visitors, illustrated with a worked example showing a 1%→2.5% lift turning
  $98,000/yr into $246,000/yr at 600 daily visitors and $45 average order value.
- As of 2020-06-29, Neil cited an Elastic Path study finding "Add To Cart" outperforms
  alternate CTA wording ("Add to Bag," "Add to Basket") — his advice: "don't get fancy...
  stick with what people know."
- As of 2020-06-29, Neil cited average cart abandonment at 69.23% (~$260B in lost
  sales) and a Baymard Institute finding that improved checkout design/abandoned-cart
  email sequencing can recover an additional 35.26% of conversions.
- As of 2020-06-29, Neil said he personally relies almost entirely on reviews for his
  own Amazon purchases — "If the review suck, I don't buy" — offered as the rationale
  for why sellers must prioritize collecting reviews.
- As of 2020-06-29, Neil's explicit ethical line on urgency tactics: "you don't want to
  trick or deceive them... if you're not running out of inventory don't say that on
  your page."
