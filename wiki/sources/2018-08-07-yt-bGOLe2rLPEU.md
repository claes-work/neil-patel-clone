# 5 (Simple) Facebook Video Hacks

## Summary

Solo tactical video: five ways to make a Facebook video perform. **(1) Show emotion and
smile** — a flat, dull delivery loses viewers; personality, emotion, and smiling lift
engagement/comments/likes. Neil admits he shows emotion but often forgets to smile, and
says A/B tests of smiling vs. not-smiling showed audiences preferred him smiling. **(2)
Make videos at least 5 minutes long** — Facebook wants to keep users on Facebook (more
watch time → more ad revenue), so it favors longer, engaging videos; he says 2–3-minute
videos underperform 5-minute+ ones, and his team's tests found the *longer* the video, the
easier it went viral (cites a ~60-minute cropped presentation). **(3) Write a real
description** using "bro language" — short punchy statements, not full sentences; he
credits Larry Kim (MobileMonkey) for the "bro language" idea and gives a sample
("some people either love me or hate me... today I'm going to share three bold techniques
that helped me make millions"). **(4) Add captions** — many people watch muted at work, so
subtitles let them read along without sound. **(5) Drive engagement** — explicitly ask for
comments/likes/shares (they signal Facebook to distribute more), and respond to commenters
so they come back and comment again. Facebook weighs both watch time and active engagement.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-07, showing emotion and smiling raises Facebook-video engagement; Neil's A/B
  tests found viewers preferred videos where he smiled.
- As of 2018-08-07, Facebook favors longer videos (aim for 5+ minutes) because it maximizes
  on-platform watch time and ad exposure; 2–3-minute videos underperform.
- As of 2018-08-07, his team found the longer the video, the easier it went viral on
  Facebook (referencing a ~60-minute cropped presentation).
- As of 2018-08-07, write a punchy "bro language" description (credited to Larry Kim of
  MobileMonkey) instead of full sentences.
- As of 2018-08-07, add captions (many watch muted at work) and explicitly ask for
  comments/likes/shares — engagement signals plus watch time drive Facebook distribution.
