# 7 Ecommerce Marketing Secrets You Can Learn From Big Brands

## Summary
Seven ecommerce conversion tactics: (1) nail the product page — high-quality/3D product
shots, full product story, and combine logical + emotional copy (states logic-alone or
emotion-alone both convert, but the combination converts far more); (2) use persuasive
copy/conversion-focused copywriting, name-checking **Spiralyze** (Atlanta-based CRO
firm) and its performance-based pricing model (pay only if they improve conversions) —
already documented in the wiki from an earlier source, repeated here without new detail;
(3) test call-to-action wording carefully, warning that novelty/rare CTA phrasing can
quietly hurt conversions; (4) answer FAQs proactively (cites a 95%-of-customers-rely-on-
reviews figure) — says he now adds FAQs to his own blog posts because it increases
engagement; (5) use scarcity/urgency (Amazon low-stock/fast-delivery-window examples,
countdown clocks, color-highlighted urgency, exit-intent offers); (6) run cart-
abandonment email sequences that are personal/brand-specific (gives an illustrative
mattress-brand email example) with an incentive; (7) run multivariate testing broadly —
not just product-page elements but supply chain and margins too.

## Key claims
- States a specific copy-combination finding: logic-only copy converts, emotion-only
  copy converts, but combining logic and emotion converts "way more" than either alone
  (self-reported, dated 2021-10) — analytics-cro.
- Cites a customer-trust statistic: nearly 95% of online shoppers turn to reviews and
  consider them the most important factor in purchase decisions (attributed to unnamed
  data, dated 2021-10) — analytics-cro.
- Discloses he has started adding FAQ sections to his own blog posts (not just product
  pages) because it measurably keeps readers engaged longer (self-reported, dated
  2021-10) — content-marketing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "When you use logic alone, you'll get sales. When you use emotion alone, you'll get
> sales. But when you combine logic and emotions, you get way more sales."

> "FAQs are so important I'm starting to even add them in my blog posts."
