# The 10 Commandments of Content Marketing - Content Marketing Part 2 - Lesson 1 - SEO Unlocked

## Summary
Advanced content-marketing lesson surveying content-format ROI, then introducing a
10-point outline checklist ("the 10 commandments of content outlines"). Format survey:
list posts (shorter lists go deeper per point; his own top pages skew list-based);
infographics, using KISSmetrics as the case study — 47 infographics drove 2.5M+
visitors and 41,000 backlinks from 3,000+ unique sites, for a cost of $28,200 versus
an estimated $948,000 to buy equivalent traffic; social-native content per platform;
video (his own YouTube: 800K+ views on a "bad month," 1M+ on a good month, multiplied
across LinkedIn/Instagram/Facebook repurposing); podcasts (references the daily
Marketing School podcast); webinars (says he'd used them more in the past and floats
restarting them); interactive tools (Ubersuggest named as his single highest-traffic
content asset, plus an SEO analyzer/site-audit report and an A/B-testing calculator);
checklists (framed as an email-capture lead magnet); and his own preferred "hybrid"
approach mixing list + video + images + worksheets, exemplified by SEO Unlocked itself
(a voiceover-over-slides format). Describes his "100K Challenge": building a business
to $100K/month revenue in under 12 months using content marketing alone, without using
his own name, in an audience-picked unfamiliar niche (nutrition) — the resulting site,
Nutrition Secrets, crossed $100K in revenue selling supplements; he later handed it to
a friend's company, Legion Athletics, which redirected it and grew its organic traffic
further. Closes with the "10 commandments of content outlines": word count (benchmark
against competitor length, trim fluff rather than pad), tone (positive, matched to
"musketeer" persona from earlier lessons), introduction (hook first sentence, ask
questions, paint a picture), pre-selling (cites Apple's staged product-launch
announcements as the model), trigger section (problem-agitation with data), solution
section, subtle "musketeer intent" (soft-sell product/brand mentions), business-wins
proof, internal linking (credited as a main reason neilpatel.com ranks well), and
personalization (personal stories/opinions as the differentiator against "over a
billion blogs"), plus citing source materials throughout.
