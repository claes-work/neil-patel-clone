# How to Track and Grow Your Google Rankings

## Summary
A two-part Ubersuggest tutorial: (1) setting up rank tracking for a new project
(domain, up to several target locations, keyword list seeded from Search Console plus
manual additions, daily tracking frequency, separate mobile-vs-desktop tracking), and
(2) a long-tail keyword-expansion tactic for pages that already rank for a head term.
Neil justifies daily tracking by citing that Google makes "roughly 3,200" algorithm
changes per year (~8+/day), and justifies mobile-specific tracking by citing that "more
than 50% of searches on Google are mobile" under Google's mobile-first index (rankings
can differ by device). For the growth tactic: take a page already ranking for a head
term (e.g. "digital marketing"), pull its Keyword Ideas suggestions/related/questions/
prepositions/comparisons in Ubersuggest, and edit the page to naturally include the
longer-tail variants it doesn't yet rank for — prioritizing variants with higher CPC as
a signal of commercial value. Frames head terms as almost always more competitive than
their long-tail variants, so "stacking" long-tail phrases onto an already-ranking page
compounds traffic (his stated example: neilpatel.com's "digital marketing" page ranks
#2–3 for the head term but doesn't rank for "digital marketing services" because the
phrase isn't in the text — despite Neil's own agency, NP Digital, selling exactly that
service).

## Key claims
- As of 2020-08-29, Neil cited "roughly 3,200" Google algorithm changes per year
  (~8+/day) as the justification for daily (not weekly) rank tracking.
- As of 2020-08-29, Neil's long-tail expansion tactic: audit an already-ranking page
  against Ubersuggest's suggestion/related/questions/prepositions/comparisons keyword
  tabs, and edit in the missing higher-CPC variants — "if you just do this with one
  page every single week, after a year, you should end up getting double, triple the
  amount of traffic" (self-reported, unaudited estimate).
- As of 2020-08-29, Neil noted his own neilpatel.com "digital marketing" page doesn't
  rank for "digital marketing services" (a term relevant to his own agency, NP Digital)
  simply because the phrase isn't present in the text — offered as a self-critical
  worked example, not a hidden strength.
