# The Ultimate Wordpress Marketing Setup: 7 Advanced Plugins to Catapult Traffic and Sales

## Summary
Neil Patel walks through his recommended stack of seven WordPress plugins for driving more traffic and sales, framing each as a marketing lever rather than a technical add-on. His core argument is that free tools, better headlines, low-friction lead capture, reliable email delivery, clean technical SEO, and viral giveaways compound into more traffic and conversions. He recommends Calculated Fields Form (build free calculators/tools that attract backlinks, citing Ubersuggest as one of his most-linked assets), Title Experiments (A/B test at least five headline variations because higher click-through rates lift rankings), Gravity Forms (multi-step lead forms that reduce friction and raise conversions ~10%+), WP Mail SMTP configured with SendGrid (keeps transactional emails out of spam/promotions), Yoast SEO (title tags, meta tags, schema markup, sitemaps for Search Console), Broken Link Checker (finds broken links and missing images to protect UX and rankings), and RafflePress (giveaways with social voting, email integration, and built-in retargeting for virality). He closes by pitching his agency, NP Digital, for WordPress traffic help.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-10-10, Neil said tools attract backlinks, visitors, and search rankings, and that Ubersuggest is one of the most linked-to sections of the neilpatel.com site precisely because it is a free tool.
- As of 2019-10-10, Neil recommended Calculated Fields Form as one of the easiest ways to build website tools (calculators for finance, mortgage, nutrition, health, A/B testing, car rental, quote generators, etc.).
- As of 2019-10-10, Neil said eight out of ten people read your headline but only two out of ten click through, so headlines matter most; he recommended creating at least five title variations and testing them with the Title Experiments plugin.
- As of 2019-10-10, Neil claimed higher click-through rates raise rankings: if searchers clicked the #2 listing over the #1, Google would read #2 as more relevant and promote it above the better-backlinked/better-optimized #1 result.
- As of 2019-10-10, Neil said Gravity Forms lets you build multi-step forms, polls, quizzes, and surveys, and that multi-step forms reduce friction and increase conversion rates by usually 10%+.
- As of 2019-10-10, Neil recommended WP Mail SMTP configured with SendGrid's SMTP to keep WordPress-sent emails out of the spam and promotions tabs; he noted SendGrid offers 100 free emails a day.
- As of 2019-10-10, Neil recommended Yoast SEO as a near-complete SEO plugin covering schema/markup, title tags, meta tags, and sitemap generation for submission to Google Search Console.
- As of 2019-10-10, Neil recommended Broken Link Checker to monitor the whole site for broken links and missing images, prevent search engines from following broken links, and improve UX and rankings over the long run.
- As of 2019-10-10, Neil recommended RafflePress for hosting giveaways that drive virality via social voting (more shares = more entries), email blasts, and built-in tracking and retargeting.
