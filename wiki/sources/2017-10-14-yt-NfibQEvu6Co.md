# 3 Reasons Why You Should Stop Copying Your Competitor's Digital Marketing Strategy

## Summary
Neil argues against copying a larger competitor's digital marketing strategy and gives three reasons. First, you don't have visibility into what's actually happening inside a competitor's business — a similar product may win for reasons you can't see, such as special features or accumulated brand credibility from being in the market far longer, which lets them "get away with murder" in ways you can't. Second, competitors often change their websites based on opinions and gut feelings rather than data; gut feelings don't build million- or billion-dollar companies, data does, so you should make website changes off facts and run split tests (he cites using Crazy Egg on his own site) to learn what works for your own audience. Third, you are different from your competition and need your own unique selling proposition — copying someone already bigger won't make you bigger than them. He illustrates the USP point with his younger agency days competing against iProspect: where iProspect touted big-brand accounts like Coca-Cola and McDonald's, Neil positioned himself as the personalized alternative where the client talks directly to him, he picks up the phone, and he is personally accountable for results. He never beat them on size, but differentiating let him grow much faster.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-14, Neil states:
- You shouldn't copy competitors just because they are larger than you (as of 2017-10-14).
- You don't always know what's happening inside a competitor's business, so you can't know why customers prefer their similar product over yours (as of 2017-10-14).
- A competitor's advantage may come from something special about the product, or from being in the marketplace much longer and building up brand credibility that lets them get away with things you can't (as of 2017-10-14).
- Many people, including competitors, change their websites based on opinions and gut feelings rather than facts (as of 2017-10-14).
- Gut feelings don't create multi-million or billion dollar companies — data does; you should use data to make changes on your website (as of 2017-10-14).
- Neil uses Crazy Egg whenever he makes changes on his website and continually runs split tests, because copying someone else won't necessarily work for his own audience (as of 2017-10-14).
- You are different from your competition and need your own unique selling proposition; copying someone already bigger won't make you bigger than them (as of 2017-10-14).
- In his earlier agency, Neil competed with iProspect, which promoted working on big-brand accounts like Coca-Cola and McDonald's (as of 2017-10-14).
- Neil's USP against iProspect was a personalized experience — the client talks directly with him, he picks up the phone, and he is personally held responsible for their results (as of 2017-10-14).
- He never beat iProspect on size, but by making himself different and standing out he grew his business much faster (as of 2017-10-14).
