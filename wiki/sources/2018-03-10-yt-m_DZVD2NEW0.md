# Unlock Self Confidence [Tips for Business Owners]

_Q&A-style interview; **Adam** (Viewership) asks, Neil answers. Only Neil-attributed
material is persona data._

## Summary
Neil's confidence/mindset framework, told as autobiography. He deflects the "successful"
label ("I don't think I'm successful"), then reframes success as a **subtractive process**:
the way forward is to **learn from your mistakes and stop repeating them** — do that long
enough and, by elimination, all that's left is the right things (you'll "stumble upon them —
it's just probability"). He credits **naivety/ignorance** as an asset early on: not knowing
"how the world works" let him try things. His only youthful mission was to be **better off
than he was** and eventually **take care of his parents**; he hated menial work (picking up
trash, cleaning restrooms). He insists there is **no overnight success** — Bitcoin/crypto,
Uber, and marketers like **Seth Godin, Guy Kawasaki, Gary Vaynerchuk** all took years — and
frames his own edge as persistence, not intelligence ("I'm not smarter than most people…
I didn't get the best grades… compared to my sister").

## Key claims
As of 2018-03-10, Neil states:
- He **doesn't consider himself successful**, even though others do — a recurring self-framing.
- **Success is subtractive**: keep making *new* (not repeated) mistakes, learn from each, and
  the pool of possible mistakes shrinks until you're mostly doing the right things.
- **Naivety helped him**: being young, "foolish," and ignorant of how the world works made
  him willing to try, take care of his parents one day was the "plus" goal.
- His **only life mission when young** was to be better off than he was; he disliked menial
  jobs (trash, restrooms).
- **Nothing is an overnight success** — Bitcoin was flat for a long time before "recently
  exploding"; Uber "didn't come out of nowhere." Success "takes years."
- He attributes his results to **persistence and learning, not raw intelligence or grades**.
