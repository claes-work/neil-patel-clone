# How Google Algorithm Updates Can Get You Blacklisted | Avoid These 3 SEO Mistakes!

## Summary
Neil's playbook for "Google-proofing" a website against algorithm updates (Panda, Penguin, Hummingbird, etc.). The frame is white-hat vs. black-hat: avoid stealing/scraping content and buying links; instead write amazing content, build a great user experience, and always put the user first. He then gives three concrete engagement levers pulled from Google Analytics: (1) raise time on site toward 2–3 minutes (under a minute signals people don't care); (2) lower bounce rate via large fonts, focusing the page on what the user came for, and mobile responsiveness (over half of search traffic is mobile); (3) continually put the user first — audit and update outdated pages, beat competitors' UX, and survey users for feedback. Sites that do these rarely get penalized.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-23, Neil states:
- Google continually releases algorithm updates named after animals (Hummingbird, Panda, Penguin); the worry is future penalties (as of 2017-07-23).
- Two kinds of SEO: **black hat** (stealing content, scraping websites, buying links) — which Google hates — and **white hat** (amazing content, great UX, user-first) — which Google likes; doing white hat means your site very rarely gets penalized and you don't have to worry about updates (as of 2017-07-23).
- Lever 1 — **time on site** (in Google Analytics): get it closer to 2–3 minutes; under a minute shows most people don't care to be on your site (as of 2017-07-23).
- Lever 2 — **bounce rate** (in Google Analytics): lower is better; people bouncing within seconds tells Google the page isn't relevant, so it shouldn't rank. Reduce it with large fonts (easier reading), focusing the page on what the user came for (e-commerce → showcase the product; blog → showcase the content), and making the site mobile-compatible/responsive since more than half of search traffic is mobile (as of 2017-07-23).
- Lever 3 — continually **put the user first**: audit the site for outdated content pages to update, check whether your UX beats competitors', and find ways to delight users so they return and refer others; survey and talk to users for feedback (as of 2017-07-23).
- Doing these things makes it "very rare" that your site gets penalized — that's how you Google-proof it (as of 2017-07-23).
