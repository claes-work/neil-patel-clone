# 10 Marketing Hacks To Get More Traffic and Build Your Personal Brand

## Summary
Solo keynote at a live conference (host referred to only as "Ryan" — the event context, "Ryan's
event," matches Ryan Pineda's WealthCon). Opens with NP Digital stats: ~750 employees, 60+
clients, grew from $0 to $100M+ revenue in under five years on ~$5M of Neil's own invested
capital, #21 on the Inc. 500 in its first eligible year. Frames personal branding as "the new way
to do marketing," citing Ryan Reynolds/Mint Mobile (sold to T-Mobile for $1.3B, Reynolds given
equity rather than a flat endorsement fee) and Kylie Jenner/Kylie Cosmetics as B2C proof, and NP
Digital's own $515M acquisition offer (received "in a bad economy") as the B2B proof that
personal branding drives enterprise value regardless of vertical. Discloses social follower
counts as of the talk (frames himself as "not that popular"): Instagram 408K, LinkedIn 495K,
Facebook ~1M (states Facebook engagement is likely low despite the raw number), Twitter 445K,
YouTube 1.15M, TikTok 85K (states he didn't know he had a TikTok account until his team told
him, doesn't have the app installed, and doesn't know his TikTok login). Delivers ten numbered
tactics: **(1) YouTube ranking** — push every new video hard in its first 24 hours (email blast,
text friends, ask for comments) because YouTube's algorithm rewards early-engagement velocity in
a way Google's does not (Google ranking can take a year+; YouTube ranking can shift in 24-48
hours). **(2) Free tools** — undercut paid competitors by giving away a free version of whatever
they charge for (Ubersuggest: 36M users over 5 years, $0 ad spend cited; AnswerThePublic, bought
for $8.6M in Feb 2023 and made more free, growing since); suggests smaller operators buy cheap
existing tools (codecanyon.net, $5-$500) rather than build from scratch, versus his own $8.6M
purchase which he calls "stupid" in hindsight for most people's budgets; cites HubSpot's
free-CRM-to-beat-Salesforce play and Canva as other examples. **(3) LinkedIn/TikTok virality via
comment velocity** — cites an Omnicore Agency study that <1% of LinkedIn users create/share
content, and states that LinkedIn posts collecting many detailed (not "nice post"-style) comments
within the first four hours go disproportionately viral; recommends attendees organize
reciprocal comment pods with people around them. On TikTok, recounts learning the same lesson
accidentally: a hostile top comment on one of his own clips sparked a comment war that drove the
video's reach, after which he "had my team do way more tests" and found engaging comments
(not necessarily within four hours) drive TikTok distribution too. **(4) Google brand-search
halo** — argues branded terms outrank generic-category terms (more people Google "Nike" than
"shoes") because search engines treat brands as lower fake-news risk; tells a family anecdote
about a nephew wanting Nike shoes "because it'll make him run faster" as an example of branding's
non-rational pull, and a separate anecdote about CNN mis-reporting his image alongside a
different "Neil Patel" running for US Senate. **(5) Facebook video length** — cites an NP Digital
analysis of 939 company Facebook pages finding videos over five minutes get 81.39% better
engagement than shorter-form content, contra the general short-form trend. **(6) Instagram live
video** — cites Earth Web (17.6% decline in users creating live content) and Growthoid (live
video usage projected to grow ~15x) as the opportunity gap; recommends going live twice weekly,
paired with a co-host who has a different but overlapping audience (cites doing a joint live
stream with "Ryan" before this event to cross-pollinate both their followings). **(7) Gated free
tools for email capture** — after a user hits the free-usage-limit on Ubersuggest/AnswerThePublic
(roughly the second use in a day), require Google sign-in to continue; states this move alone
roughly 5x'd daily subscriber signups (~2,000 to ~10,000/day, with weekday/weekend variance).
**(8) Google's "new search" opportunity** — cites that ~15% of daily Google searches have never
been searched before ("blue dot" new-keyword territory vs. "orange dot" saturated-keyword
territory), and recommends AnswerThePublic (free) to surface these emerging queries; mentions
ChatGPT or the (self-built) Ubersuggest AI Writer as a first-draft tool, with a caveat that AI
output still needs human editing to be competitive. **(9) Respond to every comment/DM** — frames
comment-and-DM responsiveness (himself, or delegated to his team) as relationship-building that
converts into sales later, name-checking a personal Gmail voice-to-text trick he uses to answer
emails from airport terminals. **(10) Landing-page conversion checklist** — cites a 27-point
Salesforce conversion-element study (virtual product tours, "give them 10 reasons to buy," star
ratings, reviews, testimonials, countdown timers) NP Digital compiled into a free PDF, claiming
up to 50% conversion lift.
