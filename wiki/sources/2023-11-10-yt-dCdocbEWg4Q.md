# 7 Shocking Business Trends (Behind the Scenes) (Ep. 2599)

## Summary
A short "behind the scenes" episode where Neil and Eric relay business trends they've heard from CEO friends and clients. Eric opens with a sourcing/hiring angle: a mutual friend refuses to hire from certain US states (California, New York, Colorado) as "litigious" and instead hires from LatAm/Eastern Europe, arguing overseas hires show less entitlement and align better with culture (Eric, 2023-11-10). Neil's angle is operational-throughput and capital, not geography-as-litigation: he explicitly disagrees that he screens by state, and instead frames offshore/multi-timezone hiring as a way to run 24-hour "shifted" dev sprints that move faster and cost less (Neil, 2023-11-10). Neil adds three trends of his own — founder-CEOs returning to the day-to-day ("the CEOs are back"), culture as a "50-day moving average," and downturns being the best time to deploy capital on acquisitions. Eric closes with the shift toward operating efficiency post-ZIRP and a decentralized "everyone's an entrepreneur" org model drawn from a book on Chinese appliance giant Haier.

## Key claims
- Neil says he does NOT screen hiring by US state; he hires the best talent wherever it is, regardless of whether a state's population is more litigious (Neil, 2023-11-10). *(This is Neil explicitly diverging from Eric's opening premise.)*
- Neil: hiring people across different time zones for staggered eight-hour shifts lets a team run a project ~24 hours a day — roughly three people rotating through a day, five days a week — used specifically for development/server projects; it moves the work much faster (Neil, 2023-11-10).
- Neil: hiring from other regions/time zones is also drastically cheaper than hiring in the United States, saving money in the long run (Neil, 2023-11-10).
- Neil: a trend of founder-CEOs returning to run their companies ("the CEOs are back"), citing Bob Iger's return to Disney and Flexport's Ryan Petersen having to remove the board-member CEO he'd installed after a "complete disaster" (Neil, 2023-11-10).
- Neil (claim to verify): Flexport's revenue dropped ~70%, and it was valued around $8 billion at one point (Neil, 2023-11-10). *(Neil himself hedges: "go look it up… you tell me.")*
- Neil: founder energy is hard to beat — a founder back in the day-to-day drives things faster and carries the company's culture (Neil, 2023-11-10).
- Neil: culture is best thought of as a "50-day moving average" — a phrase he took from a 37signals/Basecamp founder's podcast; acquisitions or a batch of great/bad hires can move that average up or tank it (Neil, 2023-11-10).
- Neil: downturns are typically the best time to deploy capital on acquisitions — when the economy is strong and rates are low, everything is expensive and hard to buy (he cites Berkshire Hathaway's war chest); when times are bad you can acquire at more reasonable or cheap prices despite the revenue/profit hit (Neil, 2023-11-10).
- Neil: if you don't have enough saved cash to deploy, you can get capital quickly via bank loans or financing providers like Capchase [sic? "cap Chase"] (Neil, 2023-11-10).
- Eric: some CEOs are refusing to hire from California, New York, and Colorado, viewing those states as too litigious, and are instead hiring from LatAm (e.g., Uruguay) and Eastern Europe (e.g., Ukraine) developers (Eric, 2023-11-10).
- Eric: overseas hires are perceived as appreciating having a job more, lacking the "entitlement" attributed to US workers, aligning better with culture and causing fewer problems (Eric, 2023-11-10). *(Reported second-hand from a dinner conversation; presented as others' view.)*
- Eric: the post-ZIRP shift toward operating efficiency — cash is no longer effectively free, so companies are cutting "extra-curricular" work and refocusing on core good work (Eric, 2023-11-10).
- Eric: a decentralized org model where every employee can act as an entrepreneur is a growing trend, drawn from a book on Haier ("hire" [sic]), the world's largest appliance company (Eric, 2023-11-10).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "I haven't seen much to people not hiring from specific States… we hire from wherever we don't really care if a state is people in a state are more litigious or not we just look for the best talent whatever they are." — (Neil, 2023-11-10)

> "in this world there's a lot of competition so speed is everything so we've been hiring people for eight hour shifts… if you hire them from different time zones and they're all working together on collaboration on a similar project… we can get people to work on a project for 24 hours you're just shifting three people in a day." — (Neil, 2023-11-10)

> "the trend here that we're seeing is CEOs are coming back… it's hard to beat founder energy… you carry the culture with you." — (Neil, 2023-11-10)

> "culture is just like it's not thing like it just is what it is… but it's a 50-day moving average… if you do an acquisition it can completely change your culture… I'm probably [going] to use that moving forward." — (Neil, 2023-11-10, relaying a phrase from a 37signals founder's podcast)

> "when the economy is really good and rates are low it's hard to deploy money because everything is expensive… when things get bad people start deploying the money… it's the best time to deploy your money because you can start gobbling up things at more reasonable prices." — (Neil, 2023-11-10)
