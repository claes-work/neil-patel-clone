# How to Find a Dream Job That You Love | Transition Your Career and Find Your Passion [2018]

## Summary
A career/mindset talk framed around a real example: a member of Neil's team who went to
law school, took on the debt, then decided he didn't want to be a lawyer — "too late,"
stuck with the loans and no plan. Neil's advice for anyone in a career they hate:
**don't jump straight into another corporate job you'll also hate** — you'll get trapped
by the paycheck and be in the same miserable spot five years later. Instead, take a
flexible **quick job to cover the bills** (he explicitly cites Uber / UberEats driving,
"nothing wrong with it," treat it as a stepping stone that buys you time), and use that
time to **try different things every day until you find what you're passionate about**.
His core belief: what you're passionate about is usually **what you're good at**, and
what you're good at is where you'll happily spend your time — invoking Malcolm Gladwell's
10,000-hours idea. He grounds it in his own story: he got good at marketing not by choice
but because he **had to market his own business** and couldn't get traffic, then realized
he loved it. Reinforces Neil's "passion follows competence, found through trial" career
philosophy (same thesis as the same-batch [[2018-01-20-yt-tOe3qILUS_I]]).

## Key claims
As of 2018-01-06, Neil states:
- Spending years and heavy debt on a credential (e.g. law school) and only then realizing
  you hate the career is a common, painful trap — "it's kind of too late" once you finish
  (as of 2018-01-06).
- Don't escape a hated job by leaping into **another corporate job**: the paycheck traps
  you, you won't leave, and "you're gonna be in the same position five years later"
  (as of 2018-01-06).
- Take a flexible **side/quick job to pay the bills** while you search — Uber/UberEats
  driving is a legitimate stepping stone, "there's nothing wrong with being a driver …
  your own boss"; it buys you the **time** to explore (as of 2018-01-06).
- Method: **try different things each and every day** until you discover what you're
  passionate about (as of 2018-01-06).
- Belief: "what you're passionate about is what you're good at, what you're good at tends
  to be what you want to spend most your time at" — pair it with Gladwell's **10,000
  hours** to reach expert level (as of 2018-01-06).
- It doesn't matter whether you pursue your passion by **working for someone else or
  building your own business** — just keep trying things until you find it (as of 2018-01-06).
- Personal origin: "I got good at marketing not because I wanted to be a marketer — it was
  more so I had to market my own business because I couldn't figure out how to get traffic
  … then I'm like wait, I love this marketing thing" (as of 2018-01-06). ★ persona/biography
