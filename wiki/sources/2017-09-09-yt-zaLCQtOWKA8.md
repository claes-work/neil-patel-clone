# How to Increase The Average Order Value For Your Ecommerce Shop

## Summary
Neil Patel gives a three-tactic playbook for lifting average order value (AOV) — the average amount a customer spends per purchase — on an ecommerce store, framed around getting a shopper who would spend $50 to spend $100 instead. First, add upsells on the checkout page the moment someone buys, using the OneClickUpsell Shopify plug-in by Ezra Firestone (or custom code off Shopify) to offer related products and services. Second, show bundle offers on checkout pages pairing complementary items (e.g. cameras with lenses, tripods, and cases). Third, showcase similar/related products on product listing pages the way Amazon does — mixing more expensive, bundled, and cheaper options — because it increases both the chance of a purchase and the amount spent.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-09, Neil states:
- Average order value is the average amount a customer spends each time they buy from you, and raising it (e.g. from $50 to $100 per order) is a direct way to grow revenue (as of 2017-09-09).
- Adding upsells at the point of checkout — the moment someone buys — is the first tactic to increase AOV (as of 2017-09-09).
- On Shopify, the OneClickUpsell plug-in by Ezra Firestone helps get more sales; off Shopify you must custom-code your upsells (as of 2017-09-09).
- Effective upsells offer products and services related to what the customer is already buying (e.g. cameras → lenses, tripods, cases) (as of 2017-09-09).
- Showing bundle offers on checkout pages is a good way to get customers to spend more (as of 2017-09-09).
- Showcasing similar products on product listing pages — a mix of more expensive, bundled, and cheaper related items, as Amazon does — increases both the chance of purchase and how much the customer buys (as of 2017-09-09).
