# This is What $1,500 Gets You in Website Traffic: Facebook Ads VS Google Ads

## Summary
A worked-example comparison of Facebook Ads vs. Google Ads spend efficiency, opened
with an eMarketer stat that Facebook ad spend hit $31.43B in 2020 (despite COVID) and
was projected to grow 20-22% the following year. Lists variables that affect ad results
(industry, competitor bidding pressure, ad quality, remarketing use, seasonality), then
runs a hypothetical Burbank/Los Angeles apartment-rental realtor campaign: on Google
Ads targeting three keywords (5,100 combined monthly searches per Ubersuggest, 3.71%
average CTR and $2.25 CPC per WordStream real-estate benchmarks) a $1,500 (referenced
in-video as ~$427-1,500 depending on pacing) budget nets an estimated 667 visitors over
roughly three months; on Facebook Ads (~$1.81 CPC per WordStream) a comparable budget
nets an estimated ~828 clicks, ~161 more visitors than Google. Conclusion: Facebook is
cheaper per click and yields more raw clicks, but Google converts meaningfully better
because search captures active intent while Facebook targeting reaches people who may
only fit a demographic/interest profile rather than being in-market. Recommends running
both, prioritizing Google for conversion quality and Facebook for volume/cost.

## Key claims
- Facebook ad spend reached $31.43B in 2020 despite COVID-19, with 20-22% growth
  projected for the following year, per eMarketer (third-party stat, dated 2022-02-17)
  — paid-ads.
- Direct causal explanation for why Google Ads converts better than Facebook Ads despite
  higher CPC: Google captures explicit search intent ("apartments for rent in Burbank")
  while Facebook targeting reaches people who merely fit a demographic/geographic
  profile and may not be actively in-market — more "look-loo" clicks (opinion/paraphrase,
  dated 2022-02-17) — paid-ads, analytics-cro.
- Worked real-estate example: modeled Google Ads CPC ~$2.25, ~189 visitors/month,
  ~$427/month spend, ~667 total visitors over ~3 months to exhaust a $1,500 budget;
  modeled Facebook Ads CPC ~$1.81, ~828 clicks from a comparable budget, ~161 more raw
  visitors than the Google model (self-reported worked example, dated 2022-02-17) —
  paid-ads, analytics-cro.
- Recommends running Google and Facebook Ads together rather than choosing one:
  "as long as it's profitable, keep scaling them both up" (opinion, dated 2022-02-17)
  — paid-ads, agency-entrepreneurship.
