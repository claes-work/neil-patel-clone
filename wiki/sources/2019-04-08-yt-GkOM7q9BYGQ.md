# 99.9% of eCommerce Products Will Never Sell Without Persuasive Copy

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that most eCommerce products fail to sell because their product pages lack persuasive copy, not because of a bad tool or platform. He frames the missing element as the digital equivalent of an in-store salesperson: on the web, copy has to do the persuading and objection-handling that a human associate would do face to face. He offers five tactics for making eCommerce copy persuasive: lead with the benefit/solution rather than the product, use strong professional imagery (including 360-degree views), gather many reviews, cover every feature and objection in depth, and extend copy optimization through the checkout and thank-you pages. He also promotes upsells, free-shipping emphasis, and two-step checkout as conversion boosters. He closes by pointing viewers to his agency, NP Digital (referred to as Neil Patel Digital), for help.

## Key claims
- The one thing 99% of eCommerce needs to sell is persuasive copy, not a tool (as of 2019-04-08).
- Over 95%, 98%, or 99% of a store's visitors don't convert, largely because the product copy fails to interact with and persuade the visitor (as of 2019-04-08).
- On the web there is no salesperson to guide the buyer, so persuasive copy must fill that role (as of 2019-04-08).
- Copy should emphasize the benefit and the solution the product provides to a customer's problem, not just describe the product (as of 2019-04-08).
- Strong professional product imagery, including 360-degree view cameras, boosts sales and conversions because "a picture says a thousand words" (as of 2019-04-08).
- Stores need many reviews, and Neil advises letting both good and bad reviews through because they help other people decide whether to buy (as of 2019-04-08).
- Copy should go in-depth on every feature and pre-emptively answer every objection a buyer might have (as of 2019-04-08).
- Copy optimization does not stop at the product page; the checkout and thank-you pages also count as copy and should be optimized (as of 2019-04-08).
- Emphasizing fast/free shipping, using a two-step checkout instead of a one-step checkout, and upselling additional products/services on the thank-you page all boost sales (as of 2019-04-08).
