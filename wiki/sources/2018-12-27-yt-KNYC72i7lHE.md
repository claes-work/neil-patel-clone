# How to Choose the Right Digital Marketing Agency for Your Business | Neil Patel

## Summary

A vetting checklist for hiring a digital-marketing agency, delivered from Neil's vantage
as owner of an agency. Seven criteria: (1) don't hire an agency that lacks traffic to its
own site — if they can't market themselves, they can't market you; (2) require case
studies and testimonials, ideally with bigger brands/logos (big companies vet contractors
more strictly, so their trust is a signal); (3) look for someone tactical who can actually
implement, not just strategize; (4) the founder's brilliance doesn't matter — the founder
isn't on your account minute-to-minute, so confirm the actual people assigned are
excellent (Neil says he personally does much of the hiring and is "picky," refusing anyone
who doesn't know more than him in specific tactics); (5) demand regular communication
(reports, weekly calls, email) or you won't know when things go wrong; (6) ask for
references — unwillingness to provide any means they've never done good work (he notes
references are always cherry-picked, so their existence matters more than the calls); (7)
prefer agencies that have worked with similar-sized businesses (a firm that only served
Google/Facebook won't fit an SMB or local business). He closes by asking viewers who got
"ripped off" to comment, attributing bad hires to skipping this process.
