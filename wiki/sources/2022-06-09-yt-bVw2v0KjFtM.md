# The Hack That Led Me to Build the 21st Fastest Growing Company ★

## Summary
Neil attributes NP Digital's growth — INC Magazine's 21st fastest-growing US company, reached within
four years, now 700+ employees and projected to double within two years — not to a marketing channel
(he explicitly rules out SEO, paid ads, blogging, social media, and conference speaking, which he
says he's "actually been doing... for ages") but to a hiring tactic on LinkedIn. The heuristic: hire
people who have already solved the exact problem you're trying to solve, successfully, two or more
times, ideally at direct competitors — because repeated success (evidenced by staying at a company
3–6 years and climbing the ranks/getting promoted, not just claiming credit in an interview) is much
harder to fake than an interview answer. His worked example is NP Digital's CEO, whom he names "Mike
Gullaksen" in the caption (almost certainly the same person as [[wiki/entities/mike-gullickson|Mike Gullickson]], already documented from a 2022-01-03 source with a matching but not identical career
path — flagged as a name-spelling variant, not reconciled here): ran sales, then fulfillment, at
iCrossing (sold to Hearst for $325M); became Co-CEO of an agency the caption renders "Cavario" (very
likely [[wiki/entities/mike-gullickson|Covario]], per the existing entity page — another apparent
caption garble), which was acquired by Dentsu; then climbed to President of Dentsu's iProspect
division (4,000+ employees). The tactical playbook: search LinkedIn by job title or by browsing
competitors' staff for people with long tenure and visible promotions at 1–2 prior competitors, then
approach with a "do you know anyone interested in this role" framing rather than a direct job pitch
(direct pitches "don't work too well"), interview multiple candidates, and apply "hire slowly, fire
fast" — a principle Neil says he originally learned raising venture capital for earlier businesses.

## Key claims
- NP Digital was named the 21st fastest-growing company in the US by INC Magazine, achieved within
  four years; the company has 700+ employees as of this video and Neil projects it will double in
  size within two years (as of 2022-06-09).
- Growth driver, per Neil, was not SEO, paid ads, blogging, social media, or speaking — it was a
  LinkedIn hiring tactic (as of 2022-06-09).
- Hiring heuristic: someone who has succeeded at a specific role twice-plus, especially against your
  same competitors, is very likely to succeed at it again a third time; tenure (3–6 years, not 1–2)
  plus visible promotions is the tell that a company found them valuable, which is harder to
  fabricate than interview claims (as of 2022-06-09).
- Worked example: NP Digital's CEO — captioned "Mike Gullaksen" here, likely the same executive as
  the existing [[wiki/entities/mike-gullickson|Mike Gullickson]] entity page (name-spelling variant,
  flagged not reconciled) — ran sales then fulfillment at **iCrossing**, sold to Hearst for **$325
  million**; became **Co-CEO of "Cavario"** (captioned spelling; likely Covario per the existing
  entity page), acquired by **Dentsu**; climbed to **President of iProspect** (a Dentsu division,
  4,000+ employees) — cited as the person who had "done this... three or more times" before joining
  NP Digital (as of 2022-06-09). New detail not previously in the corpus: the $325M Hearst
  acquisition price for iCrossing.
- Outreach framing on LinkedIn: don't pitch the job directly ("do you want a job?" underperforms);
  instead ask if the target knows anyone interested in the role, which surfaces either the target
  themselves or a qualified referral (as of 2022-06-09).
- Neil restates "hire slowly, fire fast," attributing the phrase's origin for him to lessons learned
  while raising venture capital for his earlier businesses (as of 2022-06-09).
