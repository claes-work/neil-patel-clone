# Mind-Blowing Marketing Secrets Revealed for 2024 | Ep. 2589 (Marketing School)

## Summary
Co-hosted Marketing School episode (Neil Patel + Eric Siu), a short classic daily ("secrets for 2024") recorded in person. Attribution is fairly balanced but the tactical marketing substance is mostly Neil: programmatic SEO + AI-enhanced content (turning podcast transcripts into fact-checked blog posts via the OpenAI API), AI-dubbing podcast/video into other languages (Portuguese) at scale, AMP emails (in-email e-commerce purchase; better conversions but worse inbox placement/deliverability), LinkedIn influencer/retargeting ads and skepticism about the new "AI LinkedIn ads / Accelerate" auto-campaign tools, the human+AI thesis on ad optimization (misaligned incentives at Google/Meta/Facebook reps), barter speaking deals (speak free in exchange for intro meetings with ideal customers), and the SEO core: updating old content beats writing new (the "Wikipedia model," ~98% of output as updates, single-digit % traffic lifts). Eric's contributions are smaller and mostly agreement plus a couple of his own points — the human-plus-AI case-study framing, the "make Marketing School into separate per-language audio podcasts" idea, and the newsletter-business economics (his friend's ~$20M/yr newsletter, another at ~$30M/yr) and his Leveling Up newsletter monetization.

This is standard daily-episode banter restating known Neil positions (offer quality decreases CPA; update-don't-rewrite SEO; human+AI over AI-alone). Solid, on-domain, reusable, but no new framework — **NOT flagged ★ L3-candidate**.

## Key claims (dated, speaker-tagged, paraphrase)
- Programmatic SEO + AI-enhanced content is his big 2024 play: take the podcast/video transcript, have ChatGPT (OpenAI API) draft a blog post, then check it's not duplicate, layer on additional content, fact-check, and add links before publishing. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — ai-marketing / content-marketing / seo.
- Programmatic SEO at scale: create hundreds/thousands of keyword permutation pages (e.g. "HubSpot alternatives," "HubSpot vs Salesforce"); what used to be gated by dev/design resources is now accessible to more people. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — seo.
- They AI-dub their podcast/major videos into other languages (Portuguese cited) at mass scale, replacing paid manual localization; results by country/video were previously random hit-or-miss, so they switched to tech to do it at scale. The unsolved problem is lip-sync — mouth movement doesn't align with the dubbed speech yet. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — content-marketing / ai-marketing.
- AMP emails let e-commerce buyers pick variations (color, size) and purchase/pay/ship from inside the inbox (Gmail/Outlook) without visiting the site; NP Digital is testing them and sees better conversions for recipients, but worse inbox placement/deliverability (lands in promotions tab) vs text-based email — balance not yet cracked. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — email-marketing.
- LinkedIn tactics: run "influencer ads" boosting whatever posts are performing/relevant, plus retargeting website visitors (e.g. people who stayed >1 min or visited services pages, since they're an agency). (Neil, 2023-11-02) — paid-ads / social-media.
- Skeptical of LinkedIn's new "just paste a URL and we'll build targeting + creatives + ads for you" auto-tool ("AI LinkedIn ads"): expects ad platforms to move this direction ("do 90% of the work for you"), but predicts it won't optimize spend efficiently — may optimize for getting you to spend more. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — paid-ads / ai-marketing.
- Human+AI beats AI-alone on ad optimization: platform reps (Google, Bing, Facebook) wine-and-dine large spenders to push more spend and hit their own numbers — incentives are misaligned, not a win-win. Performance Max etc. are genuinely good, but a human + those tools outperforms letting the AI have free rein. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — paid-ads / agency-entrepreneurship.
- The new paradigm on tools like Google Performance Max is to leave them running and let the AI work rather than constantly optimizing; not fully there yet, but may trend that way in the next couple of years. (Eric, 2023-11-02) — paid-ads / ai-marketing.
- Human-plus-AI case studies: AI alone won't outperform a human, but a human who already knows how to use AI plus AI performs better; the human should be verifying — "trust but verify." (Eric, 2023-11-02) — ai-marketing.
- Barter speaking deals: instead of charging speaking fees, they trade the talk for the organizer setting up one-on-one meetings with ideal-customer attendees — hear the talk (A), then a one-on-one to try to close (B). Works well; they do their homework, start the relationship small and expand. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Post-talk follow-up drives revenue: at a SaaS conference the organizer gave them the attendee leads; sales reps follow up ("here are the slides, did you miss Neil's/Eric's talk, need help?") and it books surprisingly many meetings and real revenue — if you're patient and willing to do the follow-up, which most people aren't. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- "Old secrets" still work: webinars still crush, mostly B2B but B2C too. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — content-marketing.
- Lower-ticket offers in quantity are outperforming high-ticket: rather than $1k/$3k/$5k/$10k courses, giving away a lot for free or pricing dirt cheap is converting better (at least via webinars), citing Alex Hormozi's "$100M Leads" launch. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — content-marketing / agency-entrepreneurship.
- Marketing 101 = how good your offer is (echoing Hormozi's "$100M Offers"): a great offer decreases CPA, improves everything across the board, and drives word-of-mouth/branding. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — agency-entrepreneurship / content-marketing.
- Biggest SEO traffic gains for established businesses come from updating existing content, not writing new; AI writing new content doesn't perform well, but AI updating old content works well, giving single-digit lifts (~6–8%, not 20–30%) across the board. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — seo / ai-marketing.
- The "Wikipedia model": ~98% of their ~1,000 articles/month is updates; they're reallocating the new-content budget strictly to updating (programmatic covers permutations, new content comes from repurposed YouTube videos), so full in-depth writers are less needed — like Wikipedia ranking for everything by continually updating old pages rather than creating new ones. (Neil, 2023-11-02) — seo / content-marketing.
- Email newsletters are a great standalone business: cited a guy spending ~$800k/month on ads to acquire emails whose newsletter business does ~$20M/yr, and another friend's newsletter at ~$30M/yr (Hustle/Morning Brew style). (Eric, 2023-11-02) — email-marketing / agency-entrepreneurship.
- Newsletters as relationship-building: NP Digital's newsletter improved on NPS; Neil uses it to drive webinars/book-a-call, Eric puts ads inside the Leveling Up newsletter — "what's old becomes new again." (Eric, 2023-11-02) — email-marketing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> we're just taking the content, we're taking the transcription, and then we're having Chat GPT, the OpenAI's API, create a blog post from it. We're analyzing to make sure it's not duplicate, we're layering on content, we're fact-checking it, we're adding links

> Wikipedia isn't out there just trying to create a ton of new pages. Wikipedia ranks for everything cuz they keep updating their old pages.

> AI writing new content, it just doesn't perform that well. But, AI updating old content, we're finding it to work really well

> Their goal is to get you to spend more. Your goal is to spend less and generate more sales... We just find a combination of that with humans performs better than just letting them have free rein.

> We don't want to charge to speak. We want you to set us up with meetings for your ideal uh your best attendees that would be ideal customers for us.

> At the end of the day, marketing 101 is how good is your offer cuz that's going to decrease your CPA... And by the way, if your offer is so good, people are going to talk about it, too.
