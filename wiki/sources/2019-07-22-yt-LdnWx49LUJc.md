# The Fastest Ways to Bring Traffic to a New Website

## Summary
Neil Patel outlines six low-cost tactics for driving traffic to a brand-new website that lacks the backlinks, authority, and budget of an established site. His methods emphasize borrowing other people's audiences (content partnerships, guest posting, blog commenting) and capturing/re-engaging visitors (giveaways with email collection, push notifications) rather than relying on SEO or large ad spend alone. He argues these tactics work even for sites with zero existing traffic, because tactics like interviews and roundup posts leverage the reach and flattery of the people being featured. He also recommends using free ad credits to get an initial paid-traffic boost. Throughout, he stresses ethical, non-manipulative practices such as no-following links in guest posts.

## Key claims
- The hardest situation for traffic is a brand-new website, because SEO is easy on old sites with many backlinks and paid ads are easy for already-rich, established companies (as of 2019-07-22).
- Method 1: Partner with other content creators via co-hosted blog content, joint webinars, joint podcasts, roundup posts, and interviews; both parties promote the content, so both gain traffic (as of 2019-07-22).
- Interviews and roundup posts work even without existing traffic because people are flattered to be interviewed or included, and they share the post when it publishes (as of 2019-07-22).
- If you email experts asking them to share content they were featured in, a large portion will — Neil estimates 30–40%+ (as of 2019-07-22).
- Method 2: Giveaways, contests, or promotions — reach out to other websites in your niche and offer something valuable to their audience so they spread the word (as of 2019-07-22).
- Tai Lopez is cited as an effective example of using frequent giveaways (money, cars, shoes) to generate traffic, followers, and engagement (as of 2019-07-22).
- During giveaways you should collect participants' email addresses so you can email them back to your site when you release new posts, features, or content (as of 2019-07-22).
- Method 3: Push notifications are the simplest way to keep getting more traffic; a visitor subscribes with one click and gives no personal information, then can be messaged whenever you release new content or promotions (as of 2019-07-22).
- Most first-time visitors never return; per Google Analytics' cohort report, you are lucky if even 1% of your audience comes back three months later (as of 2019-07-22).
- Neil recommends Subscribers (subscribers.com) as a push-notification tool (as of 2019-07-22).
- Method 4: Paid ads still work on a small budget; Google AdWords often gives $50 or $100 in free ad spend via findable coupons/offers, providing a quick traffic boost even if those visitors don't convert immediately (as of 2019-07-22).
- Method 5: Guest posting — build rapport with popular guest contributors on target blogs (e.g., someone who writes on Forbes) by giving them feedback and topic suggestions over a few weeks to a month, then ask them to introduce you to the site's editor; an intro from an existing author makes the editor more likely to accept you (as of 2019-07-22).
- When guest posting, no-follow any links back to your own site so you are not manipulating search engines, while still gaining referral traffic (as of 2019-07-22).
- Method 6: Comment on other blogs in your industry with detailed, feedback-rich comments (not "Nice content!"), filling in the name and URL fields with your own name and website so readers who like your comments click through to your site (as of 2019-07-22).
- Pete Cashmore, founder of Mashable, is cited as having grown the site's early popularity through blog commenting (as of 2019-07-22).
