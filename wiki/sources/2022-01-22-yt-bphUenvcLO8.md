# THIS Will Completely Change Social Media Marketing in 2022 (If It Hasn't Already...)

## Summary
Argues the pandemic-driven shift to digital permanently raised the bar for social
media marketing, citing Sprout Social's 2021 index (9/10 consumers buy from brands
they follow on social; 86% choose that brand over a competitor; 85% buy more often).
Prescribes differentiators for the now-more-competitive channel: fast customer-service
response times; content built around audience wants/needs rather than the brand
("it's not about you and your brand"); tracking engagement per content
format/type and retiring underperformers but periodically re-testing them (a few
times a year) since trends cycle back; culturally relevant content tied to real
events (cites Black Lives Matter, Me Too, the Olympics), with the caveat to only do
it if the company "truly cares," not for pure marketing gain; leaning on
user-generated content; educational (not purely sales) content about products, framed
as "selling vs. showing up" (illustrated with a makeup-removal-wipe example); and
active, visible engagement with followers' comments/profiles. Cites Whole Foods as a
brand doing this well (recipe-driven visual content, high responsiveness).

## Key claims
- Cites Sprout Social's 2021 index: 9 in 10 consumers buy from brands they follow on
  social, 86% choose that brand over a competitor, 85% buy from it more often
  (third-party-sourced stat, dated 2022-01-22) — social-media.
- Prescribes a content-testing discipline: track engagement by format/type, double
  down on what works, but re-test underperforming formats a few times a year rather
  than permanently dropping them, because "trends change and people may like them
  again" (self-reported tactical framework, dated 2022-01-22) — social-media,
  content-marketing.
- Frames brand-driven content around social/cultural moments as legitimate only when
  the company genuinely cares about the cause, not purely for marketing gain
  (self-reported values statement, dated 2022-01-22) — social-media.
- Cites Whole Foods by name as a brand exemplar for visual, recipe-driven, responsive
  social content (self-reported opinion/example, dated 2022-01-22) — social-media.
