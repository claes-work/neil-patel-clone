# How to Generate Millions of Visitors Like Wikipedia

## Summary
Neil reverse-engineers Wikipedia's traffic (344M+ ranking keywords, 6B+ visits per
Ubersuggest's estimate) to extract a replicable tactic: a huge share of Wikipedia's
top pages by traffic are simply *company name* pages (Amazon, YouTube, Facebook,
Walmart, etc.) — a low-competition keyword category because most sites don't bother
targeting competitor/company names directly. His prescribed tactic: write neutral,
accurate profile articles on every company/brand in your niche (using a site like
Clutch, a services review directory, as the category-discovery tool), which he frames
as a scalable, low-difficulty content play rather than genuine competitive analysis
content. Explicitly cautions against writing negative/bashing content about
competitors — the goal is accurate, trust-building neutrality, not attack content.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-12-10, reports via Ubersuggest that en.wikipedia.org ranks for 344M+
  keywords and gets 6B+ visits, with company-name searches (Amazon, YouTube, Walmart,
  Facebook, etc.) making up a large share of its top-traffic pages (self-reported
  tool data, not independently verified).
- Core insight (paraphrase): company/brand names are comparatively low-competition
  keywords because most SEO effort concentrates on generic terms (e.g. "hotels"),
  not competitor/company names — a content-topic gap most sites don't exploit.
- Tactic: use a services-review directory like Clutch to enumerate every company in a
  given niche/category, then write one neutral profile article per company; cites
  Clutch itself and one example listing (Ignite Visibility) ranking well from this
  exact pattern (paraphrase, case example).
- Explicit ethical guidance: write "the fairest most natural light" possible about
  competitors, not negative/bashing content — except where a company's reputation is
  already publicly and factually negative (his own example: Enron, a defunct company,
  hypothetically) — because trust-building accuracy, not attack content, is what
  converts readers into leads via a consulting/products CTA (paraphrase).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> By no means am I telling you to bash them. You shouldn't do that. You should write
> neutral articles that look good on you and look good on them.

> If you paint the competitor in the fairest most natural light, how consumers see
> them, that's great, because it helps you build trust, and that makes you look
