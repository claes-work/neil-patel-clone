# #1 Key to Long Term Brand Building: Avoid THIS Temptation

_Neil interviewed (off-camera interviewer). All Neil-attributed = persona data._

## Summary
Asked how founders can resist chasing short-term money at the expense of brand, Neil
anchors on a **Warren Buffett shareholder-letter** idea: when forced to choose between
extra short-term income and protecting the brand, **choose long-term brand preservation**
because quick cash that angers people can damage the brand. He contrasts affiliate-style
"just make the money, don't care about customers" operators (fine, but "not me") with
big enduring companies (Google, Microsoft, McDonald's, etc.) that — imperfectly — try to
put people first. His core belief: **brand is a byproduct of genuinely caring**, not of
trying to build a brand. He cites giving refunds and having **stopped making agency
referrals** because bad referral experiences risked tarnishing his name. He credits his
own care-first posture to being helped for free when he started out, and insists the
motive must be authentic care, not indirect benefit.

## Key claims
As of 2018-04-14, Neil states:
- **Warren Buffett** (in an annual report / shareholder letter) framed **short-term income
  vs. brand**: don't take quick cash that jeopardizes the brand; **protect long-term brand
  and reputation** and build a lasting company.
- Chasing short-term money at customers' expense is a **valid choice for some** ("go be
  you"), but it isn't his approach.
- **Large enduring companies** (Google, Bank of America, Microsoft, HSBC, Barclays, GE,
  McDonald's) take reputation hits but broadly try to **care / put people first**.
- He personally **gives refunds** and **stopped making agency referrals** (no kickback)
  after referral partners' behavior threatened to tarnish his name — he'll "do whatever
  to protect it," but cares about people more than the name itself.
- **Brand is a byproduct of caring**: his own marketing-world brand grew from consistently
  helping people (including those who couldn't pay) and publishing good information — **not
  from trying to build a brand**.
- The authentic motive matters: **care because you genuinely want to help**, not because
  of indirect benefit — you'll be better off long-term.
