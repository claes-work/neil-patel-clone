# How to Improve Your Yelp Reviews And Stay Above 4.5 Stars!

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out how a local business can maintain a 4.5-star-or-higher Yelp rating without deleting negative reviews (which he says you can't do anyway, and which would make your reputation biased). His approach is entirely operational rather than technical: listen to customer feedback and actually fix the underlying problems (e.g., bad or precooked food, prices out of line with quality), proactively refund unhappy customers when the fault is genuinely yours, and survey customers to learn how to improve the experience so they'll recommend you. He closes by reframing expectations: even after doing everything right you'll occasionally get a one-star review, that's normal and unavoidable, and just as top entrepreneurs like Musk, Zuckerberg, and Gates have failed experiments, you won't be perfect either — keep trying your best and ratings rise over time.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-29, Neil states:
- You can't delete Yelp reviews, and that's actually good because deleting them would make your store, service, or product appear biased (as of 2017-10-29).
- The first step to a high rating is to listen to feedback, because people will tell you why they're unhappy with your service (as of 2017-10-29).
- Review quality is driven by the product plus price relationship — bad food yields terrible reviews, bad food at high prices yields even worse reviews, and bad food that is very affordable might land around three stars (as of 2017-10-29).
- If customers tell you to fix something (e.g., food not fresh or seeming precooked), you should change it; if you refuse, you won't get great reviews, but if you fix it and genuinely care about customers, review quality goes up (as of 2017-10-29).
- Treat your business with the same care you'd give a newborn child or family member (as of 2017-10-29).
- The second step is to refund unhappy customers; Neil says he "bites the bullet" and gives money back when it's truly his fault, and as a result people never say bad things about him (as of 2017-10-29).
- Caring enough about your business that you don't want to issue refunds pushes you to go above and beyond and provide the best service (as of 2017-10-29).
- The third step is to survey people — ask how you could improve and what would make their experience so good they'd tell friends to come — and optionally reward feedback with something like a free dessert (as of 2017-10-29).
- Surveying customers helps you figure out how to improve, and as you improve you get better ratings and reviews (as of 2017-10-29).
- The fourth point: even when you delight everyone from entry to exit, you won't get five stars every time; occasional one-star reviews are normal and there's nothing you can do about them (as of 2017-10-29).
- It's okay not to be perfect — no one is, including successful tech entrepreneurs like Elon Musk, Mark Zuckerberg, and Bill Gates, who have run experiments that failed (as of 2017-10-29).
- Mark Zuckerberg took many attempts before succeeding, and is now "crushing" Snapchat with Facebook (as of 2017-10-29).
- As a side note, if you own Snapchat stock you should probably sell it because the company isn't doing well and Facebook is beating them (as of 2017-10-29).
- Over time, if you keep listening and trying your best, your ratings and reviews will go up (as of 2017-10-29).
