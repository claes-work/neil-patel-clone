# How to Become The Next Neil Patel | My Personal Branding Secrets

## Summary

A personal-branding playbook that opens with a **notable contrarian admission**: Neil says
that although his personal brand made him millions, if he had to do it over he would build
a **corporate brand, not a personal one** — personal brands (like Tony Robbins') aren't
worth much without the person, whereas McDonald's, Coca-Cola, Pepsi, Google, and Apple
(which grew even after Steve Jobs) endure because they don't rely on one individual. The
tactics work for either. (1) **Blog weekly** with fresh evergreen content and link
out/email those you cite. (2) **Speak at ~4 conferences/year** — apply to all in your
space; in-person events build emotional connection. (3) **Start a weekly podcast** (short
5–6 min episodes), host on Libsyn, and treat the episode description as "SEO for
podcasting" using Ubersuggest keywords. (4) **Create educational video** (LinkedIn/
Facebook/YouTube), 4–7 min, with Ubersuggest keywords and rev.com transcription/SRT files.
(5) **Genuinely care for people** — pay it forward as others did for him. (6) **Respond to
every comment**, even to say you don't know. (7) **Give it time** — brands take 2–3 years;
track yours in Google Trends.
