# How Long Should You Run Your A/B Tests For?

## Summary
A short CRO video in which Neil Patel gives three rules for how long to run an A/B test
before trusting the result. (1) Statistical significance — using a testing tool (his
example: Crazy Egg) look for an ideally 99% significance level, meaning the new variation
is 99% likely to beat the original. (2) Run every test for at least one full week (seven
days), because behavior differs by day: a Monday visitor is not a Sunday visitor
(weekday = more likely a business person, weekend = a different, more consumer audience).
(3) Get at least 100 conversions per variation regardless of what the tool says about
significance — with fewer, the data can swing minute-to-minute and you could roll out a
variation that is actually cutting sales. Follow all three and you can trust that a change
grows revenue rather than quietly hurting it.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-23, Neil states:
- First, judge results by statistical significance; using an A/B tool like Crazy Egg, aim
  for a ~99% significance level (the new variation is 99% likely to lift conversions vs the
  original) before believing it (as of 2017-08-23).
- Second, run every test at least one full week (seven days) because visitor behavior
  differs by day of week — e.g. weekday visitors skew toward business people, weekend
  visitors skew toward consumers (as of 2017-08-23).
- Third, require at least 100 conversions per variation no matter what a testing tool
  claims about significance; below that, data can swing minute-to-minute and you risk
  shipping a variation that actually decreases sales and revenue (as of 2017-08-23).
- Following these three rules lets you be confident a change is growing revenue rather than
  hurting the business (as of 2017-08-23).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You need to look for a statistically significant rate of ideally 99. That means the new variation is 99 likely to increase conversions compared to the original version."

> "A Monday is different than a Sunday. People act differently on your website depending the day that they're coming to your site from."

> "You need a hundred conversions per variation no matter what. I don't care if any A/B testing tool that you're using says that something statistically significant if each variation doesn't have at least 100 conversions ... the data can swing and change on a minute."
