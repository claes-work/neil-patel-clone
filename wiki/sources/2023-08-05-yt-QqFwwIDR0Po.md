# 2023 Ad Quality Score: Is It Still a Decisive Factor?

## Summary
Short co-hosted segment on whether Google/Bing ad Quality Score (a 1-10 scoring of ads that
affects cost and placement) still matters in 2023. Neil confirms it does — a good Quality Score
lowers cost-per-click and improves conversions — and identifies the common mistake as advertisers
fixating solely on ad copy and targeting while neglecting the destination experience: the landing
page, checkout flow, follow-up sequences, and upsell/downsell offers. On targeting precision, he
stresses keyword/intent disambiguation (his example: an ad for a hardware nail shown to someone
searching for cosmetic press-on nails, or "apple" the food vs. Apple the computer). He generalizes
into a broader marketing philosophy: metrics from a single platform (Google, an SEO tool, Facebook
Ads) can look good in isolation while the business still underperforms, because marketing's real
job is optimizing the whole funnel through to repeat purchase, not any one channel's stats — citing
Amazon's revenue being driven mainly by repeat purchases (toilet paper, toothpaste, baby lotion)
rather than first-time sales as the model to emulate. Closes by reframing marketing's purpose as
driving revenue and building brands, not chasing traffic or follower counts.

## Key claims
- Confirms a good Quality Score reduces ad cost-per-click and increases conversions; the most
  common advertiser mistake is over-focusing on ad copy/targeting while under-optimizing the
  landing page, checkout, and post-purchase follow-up sequence (self-reported opinion, dated
  2023-08-05).
- Frames ad-targeting precision as requiring intent disambiguation at the keyword level (ambiguous
  terms need explicit clarification of which meaning/product is intended) (self-reported example,
  dated 2023-08-05).
- Argues single-platform metrics (Google Ads, an SEO tool, Facebook Ads) can look strong while the
  business still underperforms if the full funnel — landing page through repeat purchase — isn't
  optimized; cites Amazon's repeat-purchase-driven revenue model as the target pattern
  (self-reported framework, dated 2023-08-05).
- States marketing's purpose is driving revenue and building brands, not raw traffic or follower
  growth (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-08-05).
