# 4 Growth Hacks to Supercharge Your Referral Program

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out four tactics for growing a business through referral programs as a cheaper alternative to paid advertising on Google and Facebook. He argues a referral program must be a genuine win-win, using Dropbox's storage-for-referrals model as the prime example, and stresses making sharing frictionless (tweet, email, link). He recommends using off-the-shelf referral software like Referral Candy (especially for ecommerce) instead of building from scratch, placing the referral prompt at onboarding when users are most primed to act (as Facebook does with "connect/invite friends" at signup), and integrating the referral offer natively into the product flow so it surfaces at natural moments—such as when a Dropbox user caps out on storage.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-30, Neil states:
- Referral programs are a way to grow a business for free or cheaply, avoiding continually paying Google and Facebook for advertising (as of 2017-10-30).
- A referral program must be a win-win; people won't refer customers if there's no benefit to them (as of 2017-10-30).
- Dropbox is a prime example of a great referral program and uses growth hacking, giving users more storage space when they refer more people—a win-win that beats paying five to ten dollars a month (as of 2017-10-30).
- Making referral easy—via tweet, email, or link—so simple that users will readily refer more people, is part of what makes Dropbox's program work (as of 2017-10-30).
- Instead of building a referral program from scratch (e.g., for an ecommerce site), you should use referral program software such as Referral Candy, of which there are many findable by Googling "referral programs" (as of 2017-10-30).
- Referral Candy is a preferred tool for ecommerce because it's simple and plug-and-play, avoiding heavy development cost (as of 2017-10-30).
- The referral prompt has to be at onboarding; the best moment to get someone to act is usually right when they sign up—this is what most people get wrong (as of 2017-10-30).
- Facebook demonstrates onboarding-stage referral by prompting new users to connect with or invite friends at signup, which is the best place to start putting or testing a referral program (as of 2017-10-30).
- A referral program must flow with, and be integrated within, the product or service so it surfaces at natural moments—e.g., Dropbox offering "pay or refer more people" when a user caps out on storage (as of 2017-10-30).
- Following these four tips results in more customers signing up as their sharing spreads the business (as of 2017-10-30).
