# What Should You Do If Someone Is Copying Your Online Marketing

## Summary
A short mindset/competition video in which Neil Patel argues that being copied is inevitable and should be treated as flattery — a signal you are doing something right. His counter-strategy is not to fight copycats but to out-work them: continually raise the effort and production quality of your own marketing until copying becomes too laborious for "lazy" competitors, who then only replicate you halfway. He frames the durable principle as competing against yourself, not your competition — being measurably better every month/quarter. He illustrates with his own escalation from 2,000-word posts to elaborate custom-designed guides, then podcasts, then daily videos.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-05, Neil states:
- Being copied is inevitable — it is only a matter of time before everyone, even people outside your space, starts copying you (as of 2017-07-05).
- ★ Copying should be read as flattery: if someone copies your marketing strategy, it means you're doing a good job (as of 2017-07-05).
- He cites being copied himself: he says SumoMe / sumo.com replicated some of his products/services like Crazy Egg and Hello Bar, and that Mixpanel at launch copied Kissmetrics "identically" — not just the product but many of its marketing strategies (as of 2017-07-05).
- The defense is to speed up your own innovative marketing and keep consuming marketing information (he names his own channel/"Neil Knowledge," neilpatel.com, Search Engine Land, Backlinko) to stay ahead across SEO, social, and PPC (as of 2017-07-05).
- Raising the amount of work behind your marketing raises long-run ROI; you may lose money short-run but make it up long-run, and high effort deters lazy copiers (as of 2017-07-05).
- His own escalation: from plain ~2,000-word blog posts → detailed guides with custom designs → podcasts → daily videos, deliberately going "so crazy" with marketing that few will copy (as of 2017-07-05).
- Competitors who don't copy often have MORE money than he does; what stops them is laziness, not budget (as of 2017-07-05).
- ★ Core principle: you don't one-up your competition, you continually one-up yourself — the goal is to be better than yourself each and every month, not merely better than rivals (as of 2017-07-05).
- Diagnostic: if on a quarterly review your overall marketing strategy isn't improving, you're doing something wrong / not investing enough time and energy (as of 2017-07-05).
