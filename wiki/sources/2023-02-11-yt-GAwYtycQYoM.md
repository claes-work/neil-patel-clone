# Avoid This Email Marketing Strategy At All Costs

## Summary
Warns against failing to scrub an email list. Self-reports a personal open rate of
over 30% and cites an average email-marketing ROI of $36 per $1 spent. Explains a
mechanism: recipients who never open emails "taint" deliverability for engaged
recipients too, because inbox-provider algorithms (Outlook, Gmail) treat a list with
many non-openers as more spam-like, pushing even engaged recipients' copies into spam
or the promotions tab. Tells an origin story from his early blog QuickSprout: at
~28,000 list contacts his open rate declined from 20%+ down to 10% because he kept
emailing unengaged subscribers. Recommends using a tool (he uses ConvertKit) to
segment "cold" subscribers (no engagement in a rolling 30 days) and delete them (or
run a re-engagement campaign with an irresistible offer first, for businesses that
sell more heavily by email than his own). States his own list is ~95% value-first,
~5% selling, which is why he skips the re-engagement step and deletes cold
subscribers directly; recommends most businesses (which he estimates run closer to a
70/30 value/sell split) try re-engagement first.
