# How To Measure ROI of Your Content Marketing (NEVER Waste Time or Money Again)

## Summary

Solo tactical. Neil concedes blogging/content marketing costs time, energy, and money, then
lays out four ways to measure its ROI so you know whether to invest more. **(1) Google
Analytics goal tracking** — not just pageviews, but conversions: if 50% of your sales/leads
first found you through the blog, you can put a number on content marketing's contribution.
**(2) Attribute remarketing sales** — much paid-advertising revenue comes from remarketing,
but you must look at the **first entry point**: if you know ~50% of traffic comes from the
blog, credit a proportional share of remarketing sales back to content marketing rather than
to paid ads. **(3) Brand mentions / brand lift** — track retweets, Facebook/LinkedIn
mentions and shares, and conference/industry chatter; brand has real (he says "priceless")
value (analogies: people buy Nike shoes, Amex/Visa/MasterCard, or default to Amazon because
of brand). Use **Google Trends** (google.com/trends) on your brand name to see whether brand
interest is flat, rising, or falling — rising brand queries alongside heavy content output
means content is working. **(4) Collect emails from blog posts** — blog readers rarely
convert on the first visit, so capture emails (he names **HelloBar.com** — free — via content
upgrades, pop-ups, sliders), then **drip** them over a week or two to build trust before
selling; the resulting direct email sales are attributable back to the blog and improve
measured ROI. Net: these give you a real ROI number *and* generate more sales.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-09-04, set up **Google Analytics goal tracking** (not just visits) to see what
  share of sales/leads first came through the blog — that quantifies content-marketing ROI.
- As of 2018-09-04, **attribute a share of remarketing sales** to content marketing by
  checking the first entry point (if ~50% of traffic is from the blog, credit part of
  remarketing revenue to the blog, not just paid ads).
- As of 2018-09-04, measure **brand lift** — retweets, FB/LinkedIn mentions/shares,
  conference chatter — and use **Google Trends** on your brand name to see if brand interest
  is rising; rising brand queries during heavy content output indicate it's working.
- As of 2018-09-04, brand is a major (he says "priceless") payoff of content marketing;
  analogizes to Nike/Amex/Visa/MasterCard/Amazon default purchasing.
- As of 2018-09-04, **collect emails from blog posts** (names HelloBar.com, free — content
  upgrades/pop-ups/sliders) and **drip** subscribers for a week or two to build trust before
  selling; the resulting direct email sales are attributable to the blog and lift ROI.

## Notable verbatim quotes
