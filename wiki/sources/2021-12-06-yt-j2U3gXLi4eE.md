# Auditing Your Website's SEO For Rankings (BEYOND Just Technical Fixes)

## Summary
Argues most SEO audits over-focus on technical fixes and under-focus on ranking
strategy for specific keywords. Prescribes an Ubersuggest "keywords by traffic" audit
to find page-two/three keywords: if directly on-topic for an existing page, add an H2
subheading plus supporting paragraphs and rich media (images/stats/video), then
re-check rankings monthly; if off-topic, create a new content piece by studying
page-one competitors' topics, length, and rich-content mix. Extends the "headline gets
8/10 reads, only 2/10 read the rest" doctrine (previously logged) to subheadings —
applying headline-level craft and storytelling to subheadings specifically to hold
readers through the body. Recommends embedding custom Canva images containing stats,
data, and the site's own logo/branding, arguing these get embedded and linked back to
by other sites. Describes a Twitter-search backlink-outreach tactic: search
search.twitter.com for tweets about a topic, build relationships with people who shared
similar articles, and pitch a personalized "mine covers A/B/C that theirs didn't" offer
with a reciprocal share-back gesture. Closes by naming lead-magnet creation (e.g. via
Hello Bar) as an underrated SEO factor, since email captures let him re-promote new
content directly rather than relying on organic discovery alone, with a caution against
over-emailing (curate to best posts, not every post, for higher-volume publishers).
