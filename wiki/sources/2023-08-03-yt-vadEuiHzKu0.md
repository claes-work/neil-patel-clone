# The Old School Approach to SEO That Still Works Today

## Summary
Solo guest interview on George Chasiotis's "The SaaS SEO Show" podcast. Neil gives an overview
of NP Digital as a global performance-marketing agency (paid ads, social, media buys, SEO, CRO,
email) naming enterprise clients Adobe, LinkedIn, Panasonic (recently closed), Accor Hotels, CNN,
ESPN, PwC, and Intuit, and describes a locally-staffed (not outsourced) international model —
teams in Brazil, India (serving Tata, which owns Range Rover, Jaguar/Aston Martin, and Taj
Hotels), Singapore, Malaysia, Philippines, Australia, with France/Italy/Spain just added and
Germany/Netherlands/MENA leadership hires in progress. Asked about generative AI and Google's
Search Generative Experience (SGE), he says NP Digital's analytics show no major traffic/search-
volume impact yet as of 2023-08-03, expects a net-positive long-run impact (a better search
experience grows platform usage rather than shrinking it), and flags two new AI-enabled revenue
angles: cheaper ad-creative production (AI-generated variations vs. filming in every market) and
being recommended directly by tools like ChatGPT — illustrated by an anecdote where a paid-
consultation broker (Alpha Insights) found him via a ChatGPT query asking who to talk to about
social media, which he uses to argue optimizing for LLM recommendations is an emerging, low-risk
marketing line item (a small percentage of budget, not a pivot). He declined that specific paid
call (countered with a deliberately high $10,000/hour ask) because he was with his kids getting
ready for school, framing it as a values statement that he doesn't believe money is everything.
On SEO's relevance, he rejects "SEO is dead" narratives (citing NP Digital's own past recognition
as the 21st fastest-growing US company per Inc. magazine, built via SEO) and, when the host notes
similar "email marketing is dead" claims, counters with EMO/email marketing (likely a caption
garble for "email marketing") still being provably alive — MailChimp's double-digit-billion-
dollar sale and Klaviyo's reported $600-700M/year revenue. General guidance for teams anxious
about SGE: spend 90-95% of time on what already works (traditional SEO) and carve out only 5% to
track and adapt to the new AI-search landscape, since impact and monetization are still unproven.
Closes by citing Google's $30B+/year AdSense-publisher revenue (sourced to Statista/Google
earnings) as evidence Google has strong economic incentive not to gut organic rankings overnight,
and describes internal "brand recall studies" showing that even when users don't click through,
seeing a brand name/answer in Google's knowledge-graph results still lifts overall revenue —
comparable to how offline TV-ad brand exposure (his example: Nike) drives sales without direct
clicks.
