# 8 Paid Ad Secrets You Can Learn From Big Brands

## Summary
Eight paid-advertising practices he attributes to big-brand advertisers, drawn from NP
Digital's managed-campaign data: (1) concentrate spend around seasonal moments (Easter,
Black Friday, back-to-school, etc.) because they convert higher; (2) avoid overspending
at the start/end of each quarter — pace budget instead of just chasing quarterly goals,
since constant on/off spending forces costly re-"warm-up" of campaigns; (3) prepare
seasonal campaigns (and inventory/products) months in advance; (4) optimize for mobile
(cites a 73%-of-2021-ecommerce-sales mobile projection) with one-click checkout options
(Shopify Pay, Google Pay, PayPal) and shorter paragraphs/more media in mobile content;
(5) A/B test everything continuously and retest past winners, since market conditions
change — uses the "sign in with Google" checkout option as a case where a once-strong
conversion lift (40-60%) reversed as users grew security-wary; (6) use Google Merchant
Center / Smart Shopping to let Google auto-place product ads; (7) advertise across
marketplaces (Amazon Ads, Etsy Ads, eBay Promoted Listings, Walmart Advertising); (8)
use Facebook dynamic product ads to automate audience-product matching.

## Key claims
- Cites a specific mobile-commerce statistic: mobile sales projected at 73% of total
  U.S. e-commerce sales in 2021 (attributed to unnamed data, dated 2021-10) — paid-ads.

  security concerns eroded its effectiveness, using it to argue for continuous
  retesting rather than treating past A/B winners as permanent (self-reported, dated

- States a budget-pacing principle specific to big advertisers: avoid concentrating
  spend at quarter boundaries; pace spend to preserve "firepower" and avoid the
  performance cost of repeatedly pausing/restarting campaigns (paraphrase, dated
  2021-10) — paid-ads.
