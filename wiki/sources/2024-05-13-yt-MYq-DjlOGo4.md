# Why book writing sucks (stats), Google Search revenue up 14% YoY, Pinterest shares soar 18%, & more

## Summary
Opens with a retrospective on the podcast's own format experiments: from daily 5-10
minute episodes (7 days/week) to 15-20 minute episodes 5x/week to a brief one-long-
episode-per-week experiment that reportedly cut total downloads by ~50% (data recalled
from memory, not re-verified live), prompting a reversion to 5x/week ~15-20 minute
episodes — framed as a lesson that shorter, more frequent output tends to outperform
fewer/longer pieces for total-views/impressions, even if per-episode engagement seems
deeper. Moves into book-publishing economics, reacting to a CB Insights-sourced antitrust-
trial breakdown (Penguin Random House/Simon & Schuster case, analysis by Ellie Griffin):
only 268 titles sold over 100K copies and 96% of books sold under 1,000 copies over the
period studied; roughly 50 authors sold 500K+ copies/year across a 4-year span; only
~10-15% of books with $250K+ advances "earn out"; framed by the hosts as a
venture-capital-style power-law business. Neil discusses his own NYT-bestselling book
*Hustle* (2016, co-authored, ~11,000-12,000 copies) at length: it did not help him get
speaking gigs (he was already booked out pre-book) or noticeably help win customers,
which he attributes partly to *Hustle* being personal-growth/gaming-audience content
rather than marketing content. He states that if he wrote a book again, he would write a
marketing book, write it solo (no co-author), skip a traditional publisher, run Amazon
ads on it, and treat it explicitly as a loss-leader/tripwire for customer acquisition —
none of which applied to *Hustle*. He also recounts publisher pressure ~30 days before
launch (unfavorable pre-order comparisons to another author) and defends his own
pre-order volume as coming from bulk corporate sales and course-bundle giveaways not
counted in "registered" retail sales. Closes with Q1 advertising-platform earnings
reactions: Google Search revenue +14% YoY to $46B, Pinterest revenue +18-23% (sources
disagree on exact figure), Amazon advertising revenue +24% to $11.8B — read by the hosts
as ad-market strength, while noting NP Digital/Single Grain's own agency lead flow has
felt weak/volatile since H2 2022 ("getting kicked" — the recurring in-house phrase for a
soft demand environment) despite platform ad-revenue growth.
