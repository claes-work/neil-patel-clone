# A Step-by-Step SEO Strategy For Beginners | Getting Traffic With Old and New Websites

## Summary
Screen-share tutorial interview: a beginner-to-advanced walkthrough of Neil's own SEO
process using Ubersuggest. Covers (1) Site Audit as the "lowest hanging fruit" — free,
no email required, prioritized fix list by implementation-difficulty vs. traffic-gain;
(2) keyword research — targeting "solid, non-opinionated metrics" like the average
backlink count/domain score of page-1 rankers, question-based and comparison-based
keyword discovery, prioritizing high-volume/high-CPC/low-SEO-Difficulty terms; (3) the
Content Ideas report for finding proven topics (social shares + search traffic +
backlinks) to model and out-do, not copy; (4) competitor backlink mining via Top
Pages + outreach ("I got a better article than them"); (5) paid amplification as
optional, not required (a simple Facebook post boost). Host self-identifies as a
"2012 SEO" person confirming the fundamentals (long-form content, backlinks, internal
linking/"page rank sculpting") still hold. Neil states current traffic: ~2.3M unique
visitors and 20M pageviews/month without ads (self-reported, dated to this interview).
Mostly a reinforcement of the already-documented Ubersuggest keyword-research/site-audit
workflow; no major new claims.

## Key claims
- Self-reported current traffic (as of 2021-07): ~2.3M unique visitors, 20M pages/month,
  entirely organic/no ads (self-reported) — seo.
- Site Audit-first methodology: run the free Ubersuggest Site Audit before any other SEO
  work, fix Critical Errors and Warnings (e.g., low word count pages, duplicate meta
  descriptions) in priority order by effort-vs.-traffic-impact (paraphrase) — seo.
- Keyword-selection formula: prioritize keywords that are high search volume, high CPC,
  and low "SD" (SEO Difficulty) — the combination signals both buyer intent and an
  achievable ranking bar (paraphrase) — seo.
- Content-idea sourcing: use the Content Ideas report to find already-popular articles
  (ranked by estimated traffic + backlinks + social shares) in a niche, then create
  something demonstrably better ("skyscraper" framing, reinforcing the existing
  Skyscraper doctrine) rather than copying (paraphrase) — seo.
- Competitor link-building: pull a competitor's (or own) Top Pages report, find who
  links to their best content, and pitch those linkers a better replacement article
  (paraphrase) — seo.
- Paid amplification is optional for SEO content — a simple Facebook post boost is
  described as "the easiest way to do paid" if any paid support is wanted at all
  (paraphrase) — seo, paid-ads.
- ranks #1–2 organically for "digital marketing" and "online marketing" for neilpatel.com
  (self-reported) — seo.
