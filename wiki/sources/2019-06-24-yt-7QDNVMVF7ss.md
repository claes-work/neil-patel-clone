# Marketing Storytelling: How to Craft Stories That Sell And Build Your Brand

## Summary

Neil calls **storytelling** the oldest marketing technique and gives a framework to use it
in marketing and personal branding. He opens with the **Significant Objects project** — a man
bought **200 items for $129**, wrote a story around each, listed them individually on eBay,
and sold everything for a total of **$8,000** — as proof of storytelling's power. His three
steps: (1) **define your core message** so the story has a point and doesn't ramble;
(2) **decide the story type** — incite action (motivate people to act), tell people about
yourself (ups and downs, grows the personal brand), convey value (shared values attract
followers/customers), or educate/pass on knowledge (keeps people hooked and able to execute);
(3) **establish a clear call to action** (follow, join the email list — it needn't be a
purchase) so a hooked audience knows the next step. Three bonus rules for a good story:
**characters** (so listeners see themselves in it), **conflict** (the hook), and
**resolution**.
