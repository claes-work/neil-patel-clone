# What Jimmy Fallon and James Corden Taught Me About Marketing

## Summary
Using Jimmy Fallon's viral TikTok-dance clip with Shakira (4.5M+ views) and James
Corden's "Carpool Karaoke" (148M+ views, Sia episode) as reference points, Neil argues
the common thread is entertaining content, and that marketers should mix entertainment
into their content as long as it still stays tied to their ideal customer and
product/service — not entertainment for its own sake. He names his own personal
consumption habits (Instagram shuffling/prank videos) as the source of two borrowed
techniques he says he has folded into his own marketing: (1) science-experiment-style
"what happens when you do X" demonstrations, and (2) TikTok/Instagram-style screen
slides/animations to keep viewers watching longer.

## Key claims
- States the shared trait behind Fallon's TikTok-dance clip and Corden's Carpool
  Karaoke is entertainment value, not production budget or celebrity alone (as of
  2022-08-22).
- Self-reports adopting two specific tactics into his own marketing videos, borrowed
  from Instagram/TikTok content he personally watches: experiment-style demonstrations
  (Mentos-in-Coke framing) and slide-in screen animations to boost watch-through (as of
  2022-08-22).
- Framing/caveat: entertainment should be mixed in, but content should still stay
  related to the ideal customer and the products/services offered — not entertainment
  purely for views (as of 2022-08-22).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> They both produce entertaining content. That content tends to go viral. Why? Because people like being entertained, by mind-numbing content.

> Now I'm not saying that you should create entertaining content, just for the sake of views. But you should generate content, that's related to your ideal customer, and the products and services that you offer.

> I'll try to bring in experiments, because it makes it more entertaining.
