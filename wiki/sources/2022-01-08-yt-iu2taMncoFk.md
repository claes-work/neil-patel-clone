# Marketing Color Psychology: The Meaning Behind Colors (And How They Affect Consumers)

## Summary
Breaks down color psychology for marketing/CRO: cites a study ("Impact of Color on
Marketing") claiming up to 90% of snap product judgments are color-based; notes color
perception varies by personal preference, culture (e.g. pink reads as
"princess/ballet" in the US vs. cherry-blossom in Japan), industry, and demographics.
Four tips: (1) choose CTA/background colors for contrast with brand colors, cautions
that red often *decreases* conversions for CTA buttons in his own testing (though not
universally); (2) use color to create visual hierarchy so visitors' eyes move toward
the intended action; (3) use Adobe's color-wheel tool (analogous/monochromatic/
complementary schemes) if the marketer lacks a personal sense of color/style; (4) test
rather than assume — cites a HubSpot finding that red outperformed green CTA buttons,
which he says his own tests mostly contradicted, underscoring "test for your own
audience" over generic rules.

## Key claims
- Self-reported CRO finding: red as a call-to-action color tends to *decrease*
  conversions in his own tests ("well over a hundred times"), contradicting the
  common "red CTA wins" folk wisdom and even a cited HubSpot test (red > green)
