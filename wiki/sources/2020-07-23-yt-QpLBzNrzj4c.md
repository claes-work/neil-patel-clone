# How to Optimize Your On-Page SEO in Less Than 10 Minutes

## Summary
Solo tactical walkthrough of Ubersuggest's Site Audit tool, demonstrated on
neilpatel.com. Neil explains the on-page SEO score (0–100) and says 75–80+ is "doing
a decent job" — a perfect 100 is rare and, in his view, actually a yellow flag
("you're probably not pushing hard enough... creating enough new pages, new content").
Walks through triaging critical errors → warnings → recommendations in that priority
order, using the low-word-count and duplicate-meta-description error types as worked
examples, and explicitly notes that not every error should be fixed (his own About
page has only 64 words by design; international-language duplicate-meta flags may or
may not need fixing depending on whether translation was actually intended). Cites
that the average page ranking on Google's page one contains roughly 2,200 words, but
frames this as a loose average, not a target. Covers site-speed fixes (CDN, caching,
lazy-loading images) and ends by demonstrating the "recrawl" feature, noting Ubersuggest
throttles its own crawl rate deliberately to avoid degrading the target site's server
performance.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-07-23, Neil said an on-page SEO score of 75–80 or higher on Ubersuggest's
  Site Audit is "doing a decent job," and characterized a 100 score as likely evidence
  of under-investment in new content rather than a goal to chase.
- As of 2020-07-23, Neil cited that the average web page ranking on Google's page one
  contains roughly 2,200 words, but immediately qualified it is "just an average stat,"
  not a target — some pages (e.g., "how to tie a tie") rank fine with little text if
  images/video better serve the query.
- As of 2020-07-23, Neil's fix-priority order for site-audit findings is: critical
  errors first, then warnings, then recommendations — and explicitly says not every
  flagged issue is worth fixing (e.g., a short About page is fine as-is).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "In general, if you're above like a 75, 80 you're doing a decent job. It's very rare that you get 100. And if you get 100, you're probably not pushing hard enough on your SEO and creating enough new pages, new content."

> "The average web page that ranks on page one of Google contains 2,200 words. It doesn't mean that you need 2,200 words. This is just an average stat."

> "Not all of your pages will be able to be solved when it comes to SEO issues, and that's okay. Solve the ones where it makes sense."
