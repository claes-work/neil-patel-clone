# How to Start a Digital Marketing Agency as a Beginner (Your First $10k+/month)

## Summary

Neil lays out the exact cold-outreach playbook he says he used to start an agency — no course or
paid service required. Steps: (1) **Find companies you can help**, specifically **venture-funded
companies via Crunchbase** — "if someone raised a lot of money… they got to spend it," including
on marketing help; (2) go through each target's website and identify, in detail, **what they're
doing wrong and how to fix it, line item by line item**; (3) **eliminate** any company you can't
give "pages and pages of feedback" for — depth is the differentiator; (4) **email the CEO/founder/
head of marketing** the teardown sheet, being blunt ("sorry to be blunt, I just want to help you
grow"), offering to help implement. He claims **~1–2 of every 5 such emails converts to a client
"like clockwork"** — and if it doesn't, you're targeting companies that haven't raised **at least
$1–2M**. Keep **following up** (email, text, call). The teardown doesn't need graphics; laying out
a do-it-yourself game plan paradoxically makes prospects more likely to hire you (trust +
time-strapped VC-backed founders move fast). This is a concrete, repeatable **origin/how-to
playbook** — a strong persona nugget on Neil's agency-acquisition method.
