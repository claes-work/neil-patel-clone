# How Google Uses NLP and How You Can Too With Your SEO

## Summary
Explains Natural Language Processing (NLP) as the mechanism behind Google
disambiguating queries (e.g., "Apple" the fruit vs. the company), citing algorithm
updates BERT and MUM as NLP-driven. Sketches a rough NLP pipeline (speech
recognition/text-to-speech, sentence/phrase meaning extraction, word-form recognition,
grammatical-function tagging, segmentation) to argue rankings are no longer purely
keyword-based, so SEO should be planned around topics, not keywords — content should be
thorough enough that "people are like, whoa, this article covered everything." Gives a
concrete Ubersuggest workflow: enter a seed keyword (example: "digital marketing") →
Keyword Ideas → check the Related/Questions/Prepositions tabs (NLP-derived) → weave
relevant terms into the content, skipping any that don't fit the topic. Also
demonstrates Ubersuggest's AI Writer (Lab section): pick a keyword (example:
"marketing") → choose a suggested title/meta description/headings → generate a draft
article — explicitly framed as a time-saving starting point ("save you around half the
time"), NOT something to publish as-is. Closes with an NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- States search engines use NLP (not just keyword-matching) to interpret content and
  match it to searcher intent, and cites BERT and MUM as NLP-based Google algorithm
  updates (self-reported explainer, dated 2022-11-10).
- Recommends optimizing for "topics" rather than "keywords," using thoroughness as the
  target signal (self-reported SEO stance, dated 2022-11-10).
- Demonstrates a specific workflow: Ubersuggest Keyword Ideas → Related/Questions/
  Prepositions tabs to source NLP-adjacent terms to integrate into content (tactical
  claim, ties to his own product Ubersuggest, dated 2022-11-10).
- States Ubersuggest's AI Writer generates a draft article from a keyword but should
  not be published unmodified — explicitly frames it as a ~50%-time-saving starting
  point, not a finished product (self-reported, dated 2022-11-10).
