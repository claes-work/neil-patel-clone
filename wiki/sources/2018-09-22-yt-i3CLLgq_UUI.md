# Business Never Sleeps (How to Stay Ahead of Your Competitors)

## Summary

Solo hustle-mindset monologue. Neil's message: if you're feeling sick or don't want to
work, "pull it together and do whatever it takes to get the job done" — because in
entrepreneurship **"business never sleeps"**: it doesn't matter if it's a holiday or you're
ill, there's always someone younger and hungrier willing to do whatever it takes to succeed
and get out of their parents' house. If you won't put in the hours, "you're gonna lose." He
admits people make fun of him for **working holidays (Thanksgiving, Christmas) and working
sick, even with a fever.** Part of the reason is passion, but part is competitive
positioning: he says he's often *already behind* his competition ("I don't have the biggest
business in the space, I'm not making the most amount of money"), so if he isn't constantly
working to get ahead, competitors will overtake him. He acknowledges the counterview —
some see this as a terrible, un-fun way to live — but reframes it: he's passionate, he
loves working, and he sees working holidays/sick not as sad but as **his opportunity to
catch up or pull ahead.** The blunt conclusion: if you're not willing to do whatever it
takes — including working when you don't feel like it, working holidays, and sometimes
**spending less time with your family** — you're "not cut out to be an entrepreneur." He
softens the edge only slightly: that's okay, there are "different places for different
people in life," and you have to figure out what's right for you — but if you *want* to
succeed as an entrepreneur, the sacrifice is required.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-09-22, **"business never sleeps"** — entrepreneurs must work through
  sickness and holidays because a younger, hungrier competitor always will.
- As of 2018-09-22, Neil **works holidays (Thanksgiving/Christmas) and works sick (even
  with a fever)** — driven by passion *and* by the belief he's behind competitors and must
  work to catch up.
- As of 2018-09-22, he characterizes himself as **not the biggest or highest-earning
  player** in his space, using that as motivation to out-work rivals.
- As of 2018-09-22, succeeding as an entrepreneur requires **doing whatever it takes,
  including sacrificing family time**; if you're unwilling, "you're not cut out to be an
  entrepreneur."
- As of 2018-09-22, he allows this path isn't for everyone ("different places for different
  people"), but frames the grind as non-negotiable for those who want to succeed.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "if you're not willing to do whatever it takes, you're not cut out to be an entrepreneur. It's sad but it's true."

> "sometimes spending less time with your family — I know that sucks, but sometimes it's a sacrifice that you have to make."
