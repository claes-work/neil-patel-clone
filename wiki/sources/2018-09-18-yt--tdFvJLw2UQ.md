# How to Start Social Media Marketing (4 ESSENTIAL Tips for Beginners)

## Summary

In this solo beginner tutorial, Neil Patel lays out a four-step framework (plus a bonus tip) for starting social media marketing from zero. He argues that beginners should first pick the single right social network for their space rather than spreading thin across all of them, because modern algorithms are strict and only reward top-tier content. Platform choice should follow the audience (B2B → LinkedIn/Twitter; B2C → Facebook/Instagram; YouTube works for both) and the creator's preferred content type and target demographic (older → Facebook, younger → Instagram/Snapchat). Second, start creating content by first completing the profile fully and then producing posts, using competitor research and BuzzSumo to find what resonates. Third, build genuine human connections by engaging, responding to questions, and reciprocating rather than just accumulating numbers. Fourth and finally, chase engaged fans over raw follower counts, because platforms reward high engagement percentages. A bonus tip warns beginners not to promote their business organically from day one — wait three to six months (Neil personally prefers nine months to a year) to build the relationship first, unless running paid advertising.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-09-18, Neil says beginners should pick the right single social network rather than being on all of them, because spreading across all platforms means you won't spend enough time to make each profile amazing; it's better to not be on a network than to do a mediocre job, since mediocre effort yields no reach.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil claims social media was "way easier to leverage to get traffic from" five to six years ago (i.e., ~2012-2013), and that algorithms are now so strict due to competition that they only surface "the cream of the crop."
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil's platform-by-space guidance: B2B → LinkedIn is typically best (Twitter also good); B2C → Facebook, Instagram, and YouTube all do well; YouTube works for both B2B and B2C.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil says YouTube offers longevity because people actively search for videos all day, so videos can keep earning views over time even without an existing subscriber base, whereas people don't really search on Facebook.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil's demographic guidance: older demographic → Facebook; younger demographic → Instagram and Snapchat.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil states Instagram has "over 100 million active users daily." [Note: this figure is well below Instagram's actual 2018 daily-active count; likely an understatement or transcript-era imprecision — treat with caution.]
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil says content creation starts with completing your profile fully (username, email, a proper nice image, a description of you or your company) and framing the profile around the benefits followers will experience, because an incomplete profile gives people no reason to follow.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil advises checking your closest competitors (direct or indirect) to see what content is and isn't working for them, to decide what to do more and less of.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil recommends BuzzSumo (buzzsumo.com) — type in industry keywords to surface the popular articles across Facebook, Twitter, and other social sites — to learn what content resonates on each network.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil says to build connections by friending/following people you know, engaging with people in your space, responding to and helping people who post questions (even on competitors' fan pages), resharing valuable content, and acknowledging/thanking commenters on your own posts.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil claims engagement compounds over time: consistent engagement brings people back to your site, turns them into loyal die-hard followers, and matters more than raw follower count.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil argues social algorithms judge engagement as a percentage: a million fans with only a thousand engaged reads as a terrible engagement rate and suppresses reach, whereas a hundred followers who all like, share, and comment signals amazing content that platforms like Facebook will push toward going viral.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil says don't chase follower count and don't pay models to talk about you to inflate followers; pursue the most relevant, engaged, die-hard fans instead.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil's bonus tip: don't push people to your business from day one; wait three to six months before slowly mentioning your business and sharing links (he personally prefers waiting nine months to a year). Exception: if you run advertising from day one you can promote instantly, but organic growth can't promote from day one.
- As of 2018-09-18, Neil invites viewers who are unsure which network to use to comment their business so he can help them choose the right network from day one.
