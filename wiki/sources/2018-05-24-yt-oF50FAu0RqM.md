# SEO Hack: Duplicate Content Strategy

## Summary

A "Q&A Thursday" episode with co-host Adam (Viewership) answering a viewer ("Cow"/"Kyle") on whether reusing the same article on a blog and on LinkedIn triggers a Google duplicate-content penalty. Neil's framework: (1) **Google does not penalize duplicate content** — if you copy someone's article verbatim, Google knows who published first and simply ranks the original higher; scraped copies just won't rank well, so it's a waste of time. Better to reference/quote others and link out ("don't reinvent the wheel"). (2) **On social media, duplicate/republished content works** — he republishes blog content on LinkedIn; a strategy "David" invented was to post only the intro (the intro "sells the rest of the article") plus a "click to continue reading" link driving traffic back to the site, which fixed the problem of LinkedIn readers not converting. Posting the same video/audio across Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, and LinkedIn increases total viewership despite audience overlap; LinkedIn is currently over-delivering video views (fewer followers but content-starved, so it pushes video harder). (3) **Video/audio on your own site won't get "Google love"** — Google prefers ranking YouTube (which it owns), so Neil hosts videos/audio on his site but **blocks them from indexing** so they don't dilute his site's authority; the real SEO win is **embedding videos alongside text**, which raises time-on-site and user metrics and thus overall rankings. Adam describes their own workflow (video → writer extrapolates a longer, separately-headlined SEO article → embed the video); Neil endorses it. The throughline: it's all about user experience — "if it's good for the user it's good for Google."

## Key claims

- As of 2018-05-24, Neil says Google does not penalize duplicate content: it ranks the original publisher higher and simply ranks scraped copies poorly; copying verbatim is a waste of time, but quoting and linking out to sources is fine and adds credibility.
- As of 2018-05-24, Neil says republishing blog content on LinkedIn works, and that a strategy "David" devised — posting only the introduction plus a "click to continue reading" link back to the site — drove traffic and conversions that full-verbatim reposting did not.
- As of 2018-05-24, Neil says posting the same video/audio across Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, and LinkedIn increases total viewership despite overlapping audiences, and that LinkedIn is currently delivering the most video views because it lacks video content and pushes it harder.
- As of 2018-05-24, Neil says Google prefers to rank YouTube (which it owns) over videos hosted on your own site, so he hosts video/audio on his site but blocks it from Google indexing to avoid diluting his site's authority.
- As of 2018-05-24, Neil says embedding videos alongside text raises time-on-site and user metrics, which increases the whole site's rankings — the key being text plus embedded video, not video alone.
