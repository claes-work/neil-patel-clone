# Pricing Strategies — How to Price Your Product or Service for Maximum Profit

## Summary

Neil warns against pricing off "a random number" and gives four pricing levers: (1) **Know your
costs** — price above fully-loaded cost (product/service **plus** shipping, returns, customer
service, office space); "you're not in business just for charity," and you can't run the Jeff
Bezos ignore-margins play without a big pool of money to burn; (2) **Understand perceived value**
— price signals quality. His case study: **Diamond Foundry** (lab-grown, above-ground diamonds
identical to mined ones) sold poorly when cheap because buyers read "too cheap" as fake; raising
prices lifted perceived quality and sales — so test whether cheaper means more sales or fewer in
your space; (3) **Anchoring** — offer tiers ($37 vs. $47 for "so much more value") so most buyers
pick the higher option, especially when the extras cost you little; (4) **A/B test pricing** with
**Crazy Egg** — sometimes fewer sales at a higher price yields more profit, sometimes more sales
does; test to find the profit-maximizing price. Overarching theme: optimize for **profit**, not
just revenue or sales volume.
