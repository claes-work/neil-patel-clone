# 5 Simple Steps to Driving Free Traffic and Sales From Your Facebook Fan Page

## Summary
Neil lays out five simple steps for turning a Facebook fan page into a source of free traffic and sales. He argues that because virtually every business and person is already on Facebook, the platform is worth optimizing: use high-quality images that represent your brand or you as an individual, test different post types to learn what your fans engage with, read Facebook Insights to target by geography and timing, set up a Facebook shop to sell products directly, and follow competitor fan pages to benchmark and borrow what works. Following these tactics, he claims, yields an "awesome and amazing" fan page.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-22, Neil states:
- The first step is to use high-quality images for both the header image and profile image; the image must be high quality and must represent who you are or what you're doing (as of 2017-09-22).
- Individuals should use their own photo; brands should use their company logo (as of 2017-09-22).
- The second step is to test different post types — images, status updates, videos, and even Facebook Live — because fans may engage differently with different content pieces; testing reveals what they like and don't like (as of 2017-09-22).
- If fans like a certain content type (e.g., inspirational quotes), post more of it; if they don't like your videos, stop posting them (as of 2017-09-22).
- The third step is to check Facebook Insights, which tells you when people are engaging with your content (as of 2017-09-22).
- Insights lets you target by geography: if only people in the United States are engaging and people in the UK aren't, you can target posts to the US and try a different post type aimed specifically at the UK — driving more engagement, more virality, and better results (as of 2017-09-22).
- The fourth step is to set up a shop: if you sell products, you can set up your own shop on Facebook, feature products at the top, and drive more sales by doing posts related to the products you're selling (as of 2017-09-22).
- The fifth/last step is to follow your competitor fan pages by adding them to your Facebook Insights, which lets you see whether what they're doing is good or bad and whether their engagement or fan growth outpaces yours (as of 2017-09-22).
- If a competitor is doing something you're not, check it out and mimic it — but add your own twist rather than copying blatantly; doing so helps you grow faster (as of 2017-09-22).
