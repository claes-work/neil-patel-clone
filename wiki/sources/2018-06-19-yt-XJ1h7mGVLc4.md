# WHAT is Data Protection? 4 (Simple) Tips to Get Your Company GDPR Compliant

## Summary

A solo monologue on GDPR compliance for marketers, published shortly after GDPR took
effect. Neil explains GDPR is an EU data-rights/privacy law, and non-compliance while doing
business in the EU can bring fines "well into the millions" (he cites 7M / 10M / 20M). He
repeatedly disclaims he's not a lawyer and says to consult one. His tips: **(1) Separate,
explicit email opt-ins** — a checkout or e-book opt-in does NOT authorize ongoing
promotional/newsletter emails. On neilpatel.com he uses two tick boxes: one to agree to
terms/privacy policy, and one to explicitly agree that opting in also means receiving weekly
marketing emails (a further box would be needed to send advertising emails, which he doesn't
do). **(2) Cookie notice** — for EU visitors, show a cookie banner ("this site uses cookies
to provide a better experience") with accept/deny, or a "learn more" link to the privacy
policy. **(3) Transparent privacy policy** — disclose tracking/third-party services (e.g.,
Google Analytics), how long cookies last, and how users can opt out or delete their data.
**(4) Document your data policies** — how you delete data on request, your procedures, and
how you train your team. His key experiential insight: he implemented the opt-in tick boxes
**globally, not just for the EU**, and his email opt-in rates did not change — so he
recommends applying strong privacy measures worldwide, which also avoids painful list-
scrubbing later if other countries (e.g., the US) pass similar laws. Bonus tip: US companies
can look into Privacy Shield.
