# 10 Title Tag Tweaks That'll Boost Your SEO Traffic

## Summary
Solo tactical video framing title-tag optimization through a "marginal gains"
philosophy — opens with the British Cycling coach Dave Brailsford anecdote (aggregate
of small improvements, e.g. seat comfort, sleep, mattress choice) as the model for SEO,
and Rand Fishkin's pogo-sticking/click-behavior experiment (searchers told to click a
lower result and dwell, moving "Serious Eats" from position 4 to 1 in ~70 minutes via
~400–500 interactions) as evidence Google uses click/user-engagement signals, not just
backlinks or code quality. Delivers 10 quantified title-tag CTR tweaks, each with a
specific cited percentage lift: questions (+14.1% CTR), 15–40 character length (+8.6%,
explicitly warns against going under ~15 words for keyword room, and that 50–60+
characters reduces CTR despite more keywords), exact query match in the URL (+45%),
power words/adjectives (+13.9%), emotional language (+17.3%), presence of a
meta description (+5.8%), including a year in the title (+4.9%, uses "2025" as the
illustrative year — an example, not a dated claim), curiosity-evoking phrasing (+5.9%),
and educational/informational framing (+10.4%). Final tip: study competitors' paid-ad
copy (optimized for Google Ads Quality Score) as a source of proven high-CTR title
language. Notes results take roughly 30 days to materialize.
