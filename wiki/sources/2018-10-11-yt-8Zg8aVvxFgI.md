# Improve Bounce Rate With One (Simple) Hack

## Summary

Q&A Thursday, co-hosted with **Adam Lodolce** (from Viewership). Viewer question (Serio
Design FX): "can I embed your videos on my website?" Neil reframes it to what they're really
after — **reducing bounce rate**. The hack: **embed video (and audio/podcasts, even simple
tools/calculators) into your content.** You don't need Neil's videos specifically — any
relevant video works; Neil embeds others' videos on his blog posts when someone has a better
tutorial (e.g. Google Analytics walk-throughs, a Google engineer's clip). Embedding media
**reduces bounce rate and, more importantly, increases time-on-site**, which helps with
signals like the **Google Panda update**. The critical caveat: the embedded content **must
be contextual/relevant to the page** — put the user first; an off-topic video won't be
watched, the visitor still bounces, and you've created a bad experience. Neil's tested many
bounce-rate tactics (images, larger fonts, etc.) and finds **embedding video/podcast files
works better than anything else** for cutting bounce and raising time-on-site; combining text
+ video in content (which most people don't do) works "amazingly well." Adam adds a
supporting data point (attributed to Adam): his other business grew from ~30K to ~700K unique
monthly visitors in about six months, which he credits to doing video plus long (3,000-word)
blog posts in an industry where no one else does video.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-10-11, **embedding relevant video (or audio/podcasts, tools)** into a page
  **reduces bounce rate and increases time-on-site** — the media doesn't have to be your own.
- As of 2018-10-11, higher time-on-site helps with algorithm signals like the **Google Panda
  update**; embedding is Neil's most effective bounce-rate tactic in testing.
- As of 2018-10-11, **relevance is mandatory** — embedding off-topic video hurts UX and won't
  reduce bounce; "put the user first."
- As of 2018-10-11, **combining text + video** in content outperforms and is under-used by
  most publishers.
