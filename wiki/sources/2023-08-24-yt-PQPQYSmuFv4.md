# The Biggest Lesson Neil Learned From Sending Out 255 Million Emails

## Summary
Short co-hosted segment. Neil screen-shares his own email dashboard: ~1.2 million subscribers
across many segmented lists, a 44% average open rate, 2.66% average click rate, and 255 million
emails sent lifetime. His stated biggest lesson: broad, non-segmented "email whatever" blasts
(e.g. one generic promo to the whole list) used to convert well for years but have stopped
working as well over roughly the last year, because audiences now expect relevance/segmentation
as a default. He segments by landing-page source (which blog category — SEO, affiliate,
paid-ads, social — a subscriber came from) to auto-tag interest and customize sends, and reports
segmented vs. non-segmented sends differ in revenue by roughly 57–58% on average. He says NP
Digital is starting to use Bard/ChatGPT to help draft the customized per-segment emails. He also
recounts meeting a founder at the "Pattern Accelerate" conference in Utah who built (with two
co-founders, in three months) an email tool letting recipients complete a purchase (pick size,
checkout) inside the email itself without visiting a website; Neil says he tried to buy the
business but the price didn't work out.

## Key claims
- States his email program: ~1.2M subscribers, 44%+ average open rate, 2.66% average click rate,
  255M lifetime emails sent (self-reported, dated 2023-08-24).
- States generic, non-segmented email blasts have stopped converting as well over roughly the
  past year because audiences now expect relevance/segmentation by default (self-reported
  opinion, dated 2023-08-24).
- States segmenting emails by landing-page/topic source (auto-tagging subscriber interest at
  signup) produces roughly 57–58% more revenue on average versus non-segmented sends
  (self-reported, dated 2023-08-24).
- States NP Digital is starting to use Bard/ChatGPT to draft customized per-segment email copy
  (self-reported, dated 2023-08-24).
- Describes a founder met at "Pattern Accelerate" (Utah) who built an in-email checkout tool
  (buy directly inside the email, no website visit) with two co-founders in three months; says
  he offered to buy the business but the offer wasn't accepted (self-reported anecdote, dated
  2023-08-24).
