# My (Daring) 5 Year Business Plan For The Neil Patel Brand

_Interview format; an unnamed interviewer asks, Neil answers. All Neil-attributed content is
persona data._

## Summary
Neil lays out his **5–10-year strategy: globalization**. His argument: nearly everyone in
marketing/business fixates on the **US / English-speaking market**, but the money and (more
importantly) the **lack of competition** are overseas. He notes **Salesforce's #2 revenue
country is Japan**, and that in markets like **Brazil** the currency exchange is ~**3–3.5x**
weaker (so lower revenue per transaction) but there is **little-to-no competition**, so it's
"much easier to get social shares" and **Google rankings** and to "dominate even the
venture-funded US companies." He reveals his **capital-allocation philosophy**: with **150+
employees** and "millions in profit," his **personal salary is $0** — he **dumps all profit
back into buying competitors, building his brand, and global expansion**. He references
wanting to build **Moz / SEMrush / Hotjar competitors** (the Ubersuggest ambition), working
with **Nana from Inbound Dungeon** on Product Hunt launches, and that his business partner
**Mike** (who runs the ad agency Neil Patel Digital) thinks he's "crazy" for reinvesting his
whole share. He caveats that he can only do this because he **avoids heavy personal expenses**,
and advises viewers who aren't there yet to simply **not spend much** so they get there faster.

## Key claims
As of 2018-03-17, Neil states:
- His **core strategy is globalization** — expand aggressively into **non-English regions**
  (Brazil, Latin America, Europe, Asia) that US and venture-funded companies ignore.
- **Salesforce's #2 revenue market is Japan** — cited as proof big money exists outside
  English-speaking markets.
- **Brazil**: currency exchange ~**3–3.5x** weaker (less revenue per transaction) **but
  little-to-no competition**, making rankings and social shares far easier; the **Brazil ad
  agency is growing faster than the US** and could "in the long run make more money."
- He runs **150+ employees** and makes **millions in profit**, but takes a **$0 personal
  salary**, reinvesting everything into **acquiring competitors, brand, and expansion**.
- Ambition to **build Moz / SEMrush / Hotjar competitors** (Ubersuggest thesis); works with
  **Nana (Inbound Dungeon)** on Product Hunt launches; business partner **Mike** runs **Neil
  Patel Digital**.
- He can reinvest fully because he keeps **low personal expenses**; advises viewers to **spend
  little** to reach that freedom faster — "you don't need a lot of money to start, you just
  need to be crazy."
