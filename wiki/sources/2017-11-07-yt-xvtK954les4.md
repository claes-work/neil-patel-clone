# Snapchat VS Instagram: Here's Which Social Media Platform You Should Use For Business

## Summary
Neil Patel answers the Snapchat-vs-Instagram-Stories question by refusing to pick: use both. Since you're already producing the content, cross-posting to the other takes seconds, and not doing so is just lazy; the audiences differ (Snapchat skews younger) so covering both costs little and captures more reach. He then gives three rules for doing Stories well: (1) make your stories align/tie together into a followable journey rather than jumping between unrelated topics; (2) build anticipation the way Apple pre-announces products — e.g., teasing "tomorrow I'll share 10 tips on doubling your SEO traffic" so interested people return; and (3) post throughout the whole day, not just once or twice — unlike feed posts, frequent Stories don't cost you followers or engagement, and because Stories disappear (like reality TV) people keep following along.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-07, Neil states:
- Snapchat came out before Instagram Stories, but Instagram Stories is more popular (as of 2017-11-07).
- You should use both Snapchat and Instagram Stories, not choose one — if you already create content for one, reusing it on the other takes almost no time, and not doing so is lazy (as of 2017-11-07).
- The audiences differ: Snapchat tends to have a younger audience than Instagram (with overlap), so covering both has "little to nothing" to lose (as of 2017-11-07).
- Rule 1 — Your stories should align with each other and form a followable journey/story; don't jump between unrelated topics (e.g., food then horseback riding), or people won't keep viewing (as of 2017-11-07).
- Rule 2 — Build anticipation the way Apple pre-announces new phones/products; even without a product, tease upcoming content (e.g., "tomorrow I'll give 10 tips on doubling your SEO traffic") so interested viewers return (as of 2017-11-07).
- Rule 3 — Post throughout the whole day, not just once or twice; unlike posting an image 20 times a day in the feed (which loses followers/likes), frequent Stories don't hurt you (as of 2017-11-07).
- Because Stories disappear once they're over, people are more likely to keep following daily — like reality TV, viewers can follow along whenever they want (as of 2017-11-07).
