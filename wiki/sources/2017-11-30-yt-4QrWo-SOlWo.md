# 7 Secrets to Making Millions of Dollars Online | 21st Century Wealth Secrets Revealed!

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out seven mindset/business "secrets" he credits for building wealth in the internet age: (1) **focus** — pick one idea, not twenty (cites the apocryphal Buffett/Gates "focus" story, and says most people are not Elon Musk and can't run multiple companies at once); (2) **solve a real problem** — you don't make money by wanting money, you make it by helping people; (3) make the solution **easy to use or affordable** (ideally both) because "everyone's lazy" and wants convenience (Uber, Postmates); (4) **learn from your mistakes** — the internet changes fast so you'll make many; track them and avoid repeating them, and after 3–4 years you'll know what not to do; (5) **execute fast** — speed beats perfection ("if you move at turtle speed you'll get trampled"); (6) **read constantly** — you don't need MIT/Harvard/Stanford, learn from blogs, podcasts, books, and other founders' stories; (7) **study your competitors** — every business has competitors (even Uber's competitor was taxicabs); read founder interviews where they reveal their numbers, stats, and mistakes, and replicate what worked. A biographical opener: he started his career "picking up trash and cleaning restrooms." This is a repackaging of Neil's recurring focus / execute-fast / learn-from-mistakes themes for a wealth-mindset framing.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-30, Neil states:
- The internet lets you make millions "without investing a ton of cash or being well connected or being born of privilege" — unlike his parents' generation who built wealth via land, banks, or factories (as of 2017-11-30).
- Secret 1 — **focus on one idea**: younger people want to run 20 businesses; you may have 20 ideas but pick one. He invokes the (apocryphal) story that Buffett and Gates both wrote down "focus" as their #1 key to success. Most people aren't Elon Musk and can't run multiple companies simultaneously (as of 2017-11-30).
- Secret 2 — **solve a problem**: wealth comes from helping people; "if you're not solving a problem then why are people going to give you money?" (as of 2017-11-30).
- Secret 3 — the solution must be **easy to use or affordable, ideally both**; "everyone's lazy these days" and optimizes for convenience (as of 2017-11-30).
- Secret 4 — **learn from mistakes**: the internet has no fixed franchise playbook (unlike opening a McDonald's), so you'll make many mistakes; track them and avoid repeating, and "after three or four years eventually you'll know what not to do" (as of 2017-11-30).
- Secret 5 — **execute fast**: "speed and execution is preferred over perfection"; there's always a 15-year-old moving faster than you (as of 2017-11-30).
- Secret 6 — **read/learn cheaply online** from blogs, podcasts, books, and other CEOs' stories; formal elite education isn't required (as of 2017-11-30).
- Secret 7 — **study competitors' founder interviews**, where founders "reveal all their numbers, their stats, their mistakes," and replicate what worked (as of 2017-11-30).
- Biographical: he "started my career by picking up trash and cleaning restrooms"; his parents "didn't have a ton of money" but he "grew up middle class America," so "I didn't really struggle that hard" (as of 2017-11-30).

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION: Here Neil says he did **not** grow up poor — "no I didn't grow up dirt poor I grew up in middle class America." In the same-week video [[2017-12-11-yt-wKFbxYKmMYI]] he says "I grew up dirt po[o]r." His childhood economic status is described inconsistently across sources; treat as self-reported and imprecise.
