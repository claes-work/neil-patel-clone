# Ubersuggest's New AI Features!? (Marketing School)

## Summary
A short co-hosted Marketing School episode in which Neil demos two new Ubersuggest features aimed at LLM/AI search. Because Ubersuggest is Neil's own product, this episode is Neil-heavy: he narrates the roadmap while Eric plays prompter/questioner and frames the competitive positioning. Feature one is **LLM-platform brand tracking** — enter your URL and Ubersuggest auto-suggests your brand name and category, generates a list of prompts (10/20/100+), runs each prompt multiple times, and reports how often you're mentioned versus competitors on platforms like ChatGPT, with intent and sentiment layered on and reports scheduled weekly/monthly. Feature two extends the **keyword research report** (Ubersuggest's core "enter a keyword, get SEO data" flow) with LLM data alongside traditional search and AI Overviews, adding fields like prompt intent, trend data, difficulty-to-get-mentioned, most-frequent brands, content gaps and rank recommendations. The strategic thread is a **freemium-disruption play**: Neil positions Ubersuggest against tools like Profound by giving much of the data away free (even non-logged-in), with Eric noting rivals charge hundreds of dollars and that Ubersuggest is "coming in lower." Not landmark-worthy — this is dated product news, not a durable principle — but it is a clear signal of Neil's move into AI-search/GEO measurement.

## Key claims
- Ubersuggest is being changed in two main ways; the tool is meant for a business or someone with a website. (Neil, 2025-07-30 — self-reported about his own tool)
- **LLM brand tracking (feature 1):** you enter your URL and Ubersuggest auto-suggests your brand name (e.g. ubersuggest.com → "Ubersuggest") and your category (e.g. marketing tools / SEO tools; Nike → apparel or shoes), both of which you can edit; it then generates prompts (10, 20, 100, and up) and reports how many times you're mentioned versus your competition on LLM platforms like ChatGPT. (Neil, 2025-07-30 — product description of his own tool)
- The brand-tracking feature adds intent and sentiment (positive/negative) to those prompts, and runs each prompt multiple times (e.g. 10–20 runs) because a single run varies — averaging across runs reveals which companies and competitors are really being mentioned. (Neil, 2025-07-30 — product description; caption speaker-break, see attribution notes)
- You can see how you stack up against competitors and which prompts they get mentions/notoriety in that you don't; the reports can run automatically on a weekly or monthly basis, and you can add or remove prompts. (Neil, 2025-07-30 — product description)
- **Keyword research report (feature 2):** Ubersuggest's main use is entering a keyword to get SEO data; that report is being updated to add data for LLMs and traditional search alongside the AI Overviews, because people are increasingly using AI versions of search (Google AI Mode, ChatGPT) even though Google is still the dominant player. (Neil, 2025-07-30 — product description)
- Planned/candidate fields for the LLM keyword report (team-provided; exact first-version set undecided): prompt intent (informational, transactional, etc.), trend data (whether the prompt is growing or declining), difficulty to get mentioned in that prompt, brands that appear most frequently, total number of unique brands mentioned within those prompts, "visible triggers," output-type breakdown (e.g. step-by-step guides or definitions), related prompts, content gaps, recommendations, and data on how you can rank — plus sentiment. (Neil, 2025-07-30 — product description; some fields flagged by Neil as not-yet-confirmed)
- **Positioning/pricing:** much of the data will be available free without creating an account; there is a free non-logged-in tier, more free usage/data/prompts if you log in, and a paid upgrade for even more data. Competing tools (e.g. Profound) charge hundreds of dollars. (Neil, 2025-07-30 — self-reported about his own tool; competitor price points partly from Eric, see notes)
