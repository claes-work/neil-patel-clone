# Here are the Types of Content Twitter Loves

## Summary
Neil Patel says he was an early, heavily-active Twitter user, citing 443,000+ followers (as of
2022-07-25 — consistent with the ~430K figure two days earlier in [[2022-07-23-yt-GiAmFByUz6M]]), and
that he prefers Twitter's algorithm because it doesn't suppress content the way he says Facebook and
other networks do. He breaks down four content types with specific engagement numbers from his own
account: (1) thought-provoking questions (his "2M followers vs. $1M" tweet: 107 retweets, 77 quote
tweets, 797 likes, 167K impressions); (2) live videos, lower engagement but higher revenue-per-post
(a CRO webinar: 84 retweets, 61 likes, 90,308 impressions, but 3 leads and 1 customer); (3) tweet
storms (a voice-search stat thread: 24,563 impressions, 146 likes, 70 retweets, 4 quote replies); and
(4) quotes, which he says matter less for Twitter engagement than for "offline" branding — he claims
to have seen his own quotes reused on elevator display monitors that pull from Twitter.

## Key claims
- Self-reports 443,000+ Twitter followers as of 2022-07-25 (consistent with the ~430K figure cited
  two days prior in [[2022-07-23-yt-GiAmFByUz6M]]).
- A thought-provoking-question tweet ("would you rather have 2M+ followers or $1M") generated 107
  retweets, 77 quote tweets, 797 likes, and 167,000 impressions (as of 2022-07-25, specific engagement
  figures for a named tweet).
- A live-video CRO webinar tweet generated far fewer impressions (90,308) than the question tweet but
  produced 3 leads and 1 customer — framed as lower engagement but higher revenue yield (as of
  2022-07-25).
- A 6-tweet "tweet storm" on voice search generated 24,563 impressions, 146 likes, 70 retweets, 4
  quote replies (as of 2022-07-25).
- Claims his quote-tweets get reused on elevator digital-signage displays that pull content from
  Twitter, framing quote content as valuable for offline/branding reach more than for on-platform
  engagement (as of 2022-07-25, self-reported, unverifiable).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> What I love about Twitter is that the algorithm doesn't try to suppress your content compared to what Facebook and some of the other social networks do.

> If you're going to ask people questions on Twitter, make sure that they're thought-provoking questions.

> I've seen my quotes appear literally in elevators.
