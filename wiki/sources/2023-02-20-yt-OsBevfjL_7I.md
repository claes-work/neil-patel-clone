# How to Optimize TikTok for SEO

## Summary
Argues TikTok is now an SEO surface, not just a social platform. Cites TikTok
generating $4.6 billion in revenue in 2021, a 142% YoY increase (as cited, dated
2023-02-20), and cites Google's own internal data / an unnamed Google SVP quote that
nearly 40% of Gen Z users prefer TikTok and Instagram over Google for search, with the
SVP quoted as saying new internet users "don't have the expectations and the mindset
that Google has become accustomed to." Four-step framework: (1) understand audience
search intent — names four intent types (informational, preferential, transactional,
navigational) and gives a food-content example; (2) select keywords via Ubersuggest
or AnswerThePublic, noting a keyword can be competitive on Google but far less
competitive on TikTok; (3) define a hashtag strategy — treat hashtags like keywords,
mixing a competitive main tag with easier long-tail supplemental tags; (4) review
performance analytics and iterate — double down on what performs, cut "duds," framed
as improving overall account ranking (the algorithm demotes accounts whose videos
underperform, not just individual videos).

## Key claims
- Cites TikTok's 2021 revenue as $4.6 billion, a 142% increase year-over-year (as
  cited, dated 2023-02-20).
- Cites Google internal data (via an unnamed Google SVP quote) that nearly 40% of Gen
  Z prefer TikTok/Instagram over Google for search (as cited, dated 2023-02-20).
- States TikTok's algorithm evaluates overall account performance, not just individual
  videos, so consistently underperforming videos ("duds") suppress ranking for the
  whole account (paraphrase, dated 2023-02-20).
