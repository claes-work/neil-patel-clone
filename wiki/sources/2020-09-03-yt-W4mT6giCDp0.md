# How to Rank For 1000s of Keywords Using Google Search Console

## Summary
A Google Search Console tutorial for a "keyword gap" tactic distinct from Ubersuggest-
based expansion: in GSC's Search Results report, sort pages by high impressions with
comparatively low clicks, then open that page's Queries tab (sorted by average
position) to find specific search phrases the page is *almost* ranking for but doesn't
explicitly contain in its text. Walks through a live example on his own "Instagram
followers" article — checking phrase-by-phrase whether variants ("how to increase
Instagram followers," "how to gain followers on Instagram," "IG followers") appear in
the copy, and treating each absent variant as a keyword to add. After editing the page,
instructs submitting the URL through GSC's "Request Indexing" tool to prompt a faster
Google recrawl rather than waiting for natural re-crawl (sets expectations: reindexing
can take from same-day to "a few weeks to a month" before ranking gains show).
Assigns a homework quantity: repeat this process for 20 of the viewer's own
traffic-earning pages.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-09-03, Neil's GSC keyword-gap method: target pages with high impressions
  but comparatively low clicks, and mine that page's own Queries tab (not a competitor's
  page) for near-miss keyword phrases absent from the copy.
- As of 2020-09-03, Neil stated that after editing a page, submitting it via GSC's
  Request Indexing tool prompts Google to recrawl sooner than passive waiting, though
  ranking changes from an edit can still take "a few weeks to a month" to materialize.
- As of 2020-09-03, Neil's stated batch-size recommendation: apply this keyword-gap
  edit across 20 of one's own pages, not just one.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Indexation is them crawling and caching and knowing what that page is about. So if you've modified your text, you have to wait for Google to see it."
