# How to Outsource Your Marketing to the Right Ad Agency

## Summary
Note: the source ledger title ("The 5 Industries Most Likely to Make You a Fortune as a
Digital Marketer") is mislabeled — the actual transcript has no industries-and-fortune
content; it is entirely about how to choose/hire the right ad agency. This is the same
title/content-mismatch pattern seen on other videos in this era (see the ledger note
below). The page below summarizes the actual spoken content.

Neil opens with biography: his first real company, at age 16, was the ad agency ACS,
which did not survive the 2008 crash; he later built NP Digital, which he currently
owns, and has been both an agency operator and a serial hirer of other agencies
(including outsourcing marketing for Kissmetrics, a venture-backed company that
ultimately "didn't work out") (as of 2022-05-09). He says NP Digital now generates more
revenue from upsells/retention than from new-client acquisition, which he frames as a
satisfaction signal. His core argument: reputation of the agency founder/brand (his own
included) does not guarantee results — what matters is which specific people are
actually staffed on your account, so buyers should vet the individual team members'
qualifications and track record, not just the agency's brand name. He gives five checks
for hiring an agency: (1) vet the specific people who will work on your account, not
just the agency's name; (2) require proven experience in your specific industry/vertical,
especially in regulated spaces like cannabis or crypto, since a generalist agency will
have to learn the space at your expense; (3) require the agency to do an upfront,
detailed diagnostic of everything wrong with your current marketing before you commit —
if they won't break it down in detail, that's a red flag; (4) ensure goal alignment,
illustrated by an anecdote about a startup that had raised $100M, had almost no sales
team, sold five/six-figure deals, and unrealistically wanted to reach $20M revenue in six
months — Neil says he turned that down because the goals didn't logically fit the
resources; (5) make sure time zones are compatible for regular communication, regardless
of where team members are nominally based.

## Key claims
- Neil's first real company, started at age 16, was the ad agency ACS, which did not
  survive the 2008 financial crash (self-reported, as of 2022-05-09).
- NP Digital, which Neil currently owns, generates more revenue from client upsells and
  retention than from acquiring new accounts (self-reported, as of 2022-05-09).
- Kissmetrics (venture-backed) outsourced some of its marketing to ad agencies and
  "didn't work out," per Neil's retrospective framing (self-reported, as of 2022-05-09).
- The reputation of the agency's named founder/leader (his own included) does not
  guarantee results for a client; what matters is the specific individuals staffed on
  the account and their qualifications/track record (as of 2022-05-09).
- Agencies should be required to show proven results in the client's specific industry,
  especially in regulated verticals like cannabis or crypto, where a generalist agency
  would otherwise learn the space at the client's expense (as of 2022-05-09).
- A trustworthy agency should provide an upfront, detailed diagnostic of everything
  wrong with a prospect's current marketing before being hired; unwillingness to do
  this in detail is a red flag (as of 2022-05-09).
- Anecdote: a startup that had raised $100M, had a two-person sales team, and sold
  five/six-figure deals wanted to reach $20M in revenue within six months; Neil says he
  turned the engagement down because the goal did not logically align with the
  company's resources (as of 2022-05-09).
- Compatible time zones between client and agency team members matter for workable
  communication, regardless of where team members are nominally located
  (as of 2022-05-09).
