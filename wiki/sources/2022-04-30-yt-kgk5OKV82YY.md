# If I Were Starting A YouTube Channel Today

## Summary
Neil Patel says he started his YouTube channel in 2017, made a lot of mistakes, wasted
time and money on videos that underperformed, and took over a year to figure out
consistent growth (as of 2022-04-30). He distills what he wishes he'd known into four
fundamentals rather than "flashy" advice. (1) The first 24 hours matter enormously:
unlike SEO/Google — where ranking is slow, cumulative, and age-dependent — YouTube
videos that perform well in the first 24 hours (ideally the first hour) tend to keep
performing well for years, so a new channel can rank without needing account age; he
recommends pushing every new video hard across social media, email lists, push
notifications, and chatbot lists immediately on publish. (2) Thumbnails matter as much
as or more than titles, since most viewers click the thumbnail rather than reading the
title, and weak thumbnails suppress click-through and suggestion. (3) Keywords matter:
put the target keyword in the title, first sentence/paragraph, pinned comment, and
spoken in the video itself (since YouTube auto-transcribes even without an uploaded SRT
file); he recommends using Ubersuggest to find related keywords to include. (4) Ask
questions on camera to prompt comments, which he says are more powerful ranking signals
than likes or shares; early on he asked yes/no questions, but learned over time that the
YouTube algorithm rewards multi-word comment responses more than simple yes/no replies,
so open-ended questions work better.

## Key claims
- Neil started his YouTube channel in 2017 and spent over a year making mistakes before
  finding consistent growth (self-reported, as of 2022-04-30).
- YouTube videos that perform well in the first 24 hours (ideally the first hour) tend to
  keep performing well long-term, unlike SEO where rankings build slowly and often
  depend on site/account age (as of 2022-04-30).
- Recommended tactic: push every new video hard in the first hour/24 hours via social
  media, email blasts, push notifications (e.g. via "Subscribers"), and chatbot lists
  (e.g. Manychat) (as of 2022-04-30).
- Thumbnails are as important as titles for click-through and being suggested, because
  many viewers click on the appealing thumbnail rather than reading the title
  (as of 2022-04-30).
- Keywords should appear in the title, the first sentence/paragraph, a pinned comment,
  and spoken aloud in the video, since YouTube auto-transcribes video audio even without
  an uploaded caption file (as of 2022-04-30).
- Ubersuggest can be used to find related keywords (question-based, comparison-based, or
  industry-specific) to include in a video's title, description, and tags
  (as of 2022-04-30).
- Comments are a stronger engagement/ranking signal than likes or shares; asking a
  question during the video is the most effective way to prompt comments
  (as of 2022-04-30).
- Early on, Neil asked yes/no questions in his videos, but found over time that the
  YouTube algorithm rewards substantive (sentence-length) comment responses more than
  yes/no replies, so he shifted to open-ended questions (as of 2022-04-30).
