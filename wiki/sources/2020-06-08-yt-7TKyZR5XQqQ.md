# How to Setup Google Search Console - Module 06 - Lesson 2 - SEO Unlocked

## Summary
SEO Unlocked module 6, lesson 2: a Google Search Console technical-SEO walkthrough,
described as one of Neil's favorite lessons. Crawl-errors report first: fix flagged
issues, then click "Validate fix" to prompt a re-crawl (validation can take up to two
weeks). Most crawl errors are 404s; he estimates most are harmless (bots/scrapers
hitting URLs that never existed) and only two sub-cases need action — a deleted page
that had inbound links (301-redirect it) or a misspelled/incoming link to the wrong
URL (301-redirect to the correct URL). Sitemap: generate one (Yoast SEO, RankMath, or
a free XML generator) and submit it in Search Console. Internal/external links: check
that your own top internally-linked pages match what pages other sites link to you
for (ties back to the earlier "musketeer" framework). Recommends breadcrumbs + schema
markup (plugin options: Breadcrumb NavXT, All-in-One Schema Rich Snippets, or
Yoast/RankMath's built-in support) and using the URL-submission bar to force a fast
re-crawl after changes. Recommends **AMP** (Accelerated Mobile Pages) — says it barely
moves traffic in the US but measurably helps mobile traffic in slower-connectivity
regions like Brazil and India — plus a **lazy-load plugin** for image-heavy pages.
Closes flagging the Security Issues report (a hacked/insecure site tanks rankings
quickly) and an action-item trio: schema markup, AMP, lazy load.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-06-08, Neil said most Search Console 404 errors are harmless bot/scraper
  noise, and only two sub-types need fixing: a deleted page that had real inbound
  links (301-redirect it) or a misspelled/incoming link pointing to a wrong URL
  (301-redirect to the correct page).
- As of 2020-06-08, Neil said AMP produces little traffic lift in the United States
  but a measurable mobile-traffic increase in regions with slower average internet
  connectivity, naming Brazil and India specifically.
- As of 2020-06-08, Neil said a hacked or security-flagged site will see rankings
  "quickly disappear or tank," making the Search Console security-issues report a
  priority check.
- As of 2020-06-08, Neil recommended the URL-inspection/submission bar in Search
  Console to force a fast re-crawl (often within the hour) after making on-page
  changes like adding schema or breadcrumbs, rather than waiting for a natural crawl.
