# The Advanced SEO Formula That Helped Me Rank For 477,000 Keywords

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out a repeatable, link-building-free SEO formula he credits with ranking his site for over 477,000 keywords and driving 2.2 million unique visitors per month even in what he calls the most competitive niche (SEO itself). The core argument: instead of writing more new content, systematically optimize existing revenue pages. The workflow is (1) prioritize pages that drive revenue (product, service, content pages) and set up conversion tracking; (2) mine Google Search Console for keywords with high impressions but sub-5% clickthrough and rankings in positions 7-10 (the biggest-upside spots, per ClickFlow research on 5M+ title tags); (3) fold those keywords into title tags, meta descriptions, and body copy, using click hacks like question marks in title tags; (4) run each keyword through Ubersuggest's Keyword Ideas report to harvest long-tail variations (favoring high-CPC terms that convert), then naturally work them into content; (5) force re-indexing of edited pages via Search Console; and (6) interlink relevant pages to pass "juice." Once a page ranks for a head term, Google will rank it for related long-tails within ~30 days if the content is relevant. Neil notes he personally writes only one blog article a week while his team adjusts 90 existing posts per month — arguing optimization beats new-content production.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said he ranks for over 477,000 keywords and gets over 2.2 million unique visitors per month, and that SEO is the most competitive niche because he competes against other SEOs who know what he knows.
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said the first step is to focus optimization on pages that drive revenue (product, service, content pages) and to set up conversion tracking rather than chasing traffic-only keywords that don't drive revenue.
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said to use Google Search Console to find keywords with high impressions but less than a 5% clickthrough rate, export them to CSV, and sort by impression count, click count, and ranking.
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said (citing ClickFlow research on over 5 million title tags) that moving keywords already in positions 7-10 up to position 2-3 yields a massive traffic gain, whereas nudging positions 1-3 does little — so target the 7-10 keywords.
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said to add those keywords into title tags, meta descriptions, and content, and cited ClickFlow that title tags containing question marks get more than a 10% increase in clicks versus those without.
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said to run each ranking keyword through Ubersuggest's Keyword Ideas report to find long-tail variations, using CPC data as a signal (higher-CPC keywords typically drive more conversions, sales, and leads).
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said keywords should only be added where they genuinely make sense for the user, not shoved in, and that once you rank for a head term (e.g. "dog food") Google will almost instantly rank you for long-tail variants within about 30 days if the page content is relevant.
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said to submit edited page URLs in Google Search Console for re-indexing so rankings update faster than waiting for a natural crawl.
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said to interlink relevant pages — especially those stuck on page two or three — so link "juice" flows and lifts all the pages' rankings, then loop the newly-higher keywords back through Ubersuggest for more long-tails.
- As of 2019-10-21, Neil said this strategy requires no link building and is more effective than writing new content; he personally writes one blog article a week while his team adjusts 90 blog posts per month.
