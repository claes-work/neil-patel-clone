# Why newsletters are making a comeback (and why you should care)

## Summary
Short co-hosted segment, a direct follow-on to [[2023-08-24-yt-PQPQYSmuFv4]] (explicitly
referenced: "we recently did an episode of me breaking down sending out... 255 million emails").
Neil restates that generic, non-personalized email blasts have lost effectiveness over the past
~2 years, while newsletters/emails with genuine personalization and segmentation are seeing open
and click rates "go through the roof" — driven by tools like ChatGPT/Bard making customized
per-segment copywriting cheap to produce at scale. He again describes segmenting neilpatel.com
subscribers automatically by the blog topic (SEO, affiliate marketing, paid ads, social media)
they came from, so sends can be tailored to inferred interest. The co-host frames this within a
broader "easy mode is off, hard mode is back on" thesis: with third-party tracking degrading,
first-party data collection (email, SMS) is now essential, and newsletters should be a default
part of the marketing stack. Neil re-shares the "Pattern"/Utah in-email-checkout tool anecdote
(previously logged 2023-08-24 as an unnamed anecdote) with more detail: a founder and two
co-founders built (in three months) technology letting recipients select product options (e.g.
t-shirt color/size) and complete checkout entirely inside an email, without visiting a website;
Neil predicts newsletter conversion rates will rise sharply once technologies like this see wider
adoption.

## Key claims
- Reaffirms that non-personalized email blasts have lost effectiveness over roughly the past two
  years while segmented/personalized sends are seeing markedly improved open and click rates,
  aided by AI tools (ChatGPT/Bard) lowering the cost of per-segment customization (self-reported
  opinion, dated 2023-09-04; corroborates 2023-08-24).
- Describes segmenting neilpatel.com email subscribers automatically by the blog topic/category
  they arrived from (SEO, affiliate, paid ads, social) to infer interest and tailor sends
  (self-reported, dated 2023-09-04).
- Predicts newsletter conversion rates will rise substantially once in-email transaction
  technology (checkout without leaving the email) sees broader adoption (self-reported forecast,
  dated 2023-09-04).
