# How to Rank Smaller Websites on Google in 2023 - FAST Method for Non-Techies

## Summary
(Title says "2023"; publish metadata place this as 2020-03-30 — same title-refresh
pattern seen on other videos in this era, e.g.
[[wiki/sources/2020-02-20-yt-ednS83_2Y04]].) Neil explains how small/new sites can rank
despite lacking backlinks or big-brand authority. He opens with Google's June 2019
"diversity update," which he says caps a single domain at two results per SERP
(excluding branded terms), creating room for smaller sites. Tactics: (1) exact-match
domain names — still work, especially for localized/long-tail terms though not head
terms; cites a Gaps.com study where a misspelled-term domain
("ShoferDriver.co.uk" for "chauffeur") with a one-page site and two backlinks reached
page two of Google, and a "near-me" domain ("ApartmentsNearMe.co.uk") with ~1,000 words
and no backlinks reached page one; (2) one-on-one customer engagement — Neil says he
personally responds to comments across NeilPatel.com, YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, and
Instagram as a way small players beat "faceless" big companies, generating social
signals that help rankings; (3) long-tail keywords — cites Search Engine Guide that
Amazon makes 57% of its sales from long-tail keywords, recommends Ubersuggest's
keyword-ideas report filtered for high volume, high CPC (proxy for commercial intent),
and SEO-difficulty ≤40; (4) niche specialty content — Google favors niche sites over
broad ones, per his retelling of About.com splitting into six vertical sites and
deleting ~900,000 pieces of content, after which traffic and revenue (+140%) grew; cites
Brian Dean/Backlinko.com as an example of a successfully niched personal brand ("I've
known him for years"); (5) mobile-friendliness — reiterates mobile-first indexing and
50%+ mobile search share, recommends Google's PageSpeed tool and Cloudflare. Closes with
an agency plug.
