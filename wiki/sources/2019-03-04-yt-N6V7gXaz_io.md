# 7 Things to Do Before Becoming an Entrepreneur

## Summary

Neil's prerequisites checklist before starting a company, opening with "most entrepreneurs fail
... unless you watch this." Seven things: (1) **Work for someone else first** — you won't learn
to communicate, build teams, hire, or fire by jumping straight into your own company; corporate
experience (often years, including a promotion) teaches how things work. (2) **Read books from
other entrepreneurs** — learn from others' mistakes (he cites an IBM CEO's book); he attributes
his own success not to being smart but to avoiding others' and his own repeated mistakes.
(3) **Learn to budget** — "it's harder to make money than to save it"; entrepreneurs are
strapped for cash, so practice living on a budget now (e.g. Mint). (4) **Learn to solve
problems** — entrepreneurial problems are tougher than corporate ones (small budgets, thin
staff, less experience); weak problem-solving means you won't survive being pushed against the
ropes. (5) **Figure out your passion** — you'll work 60–70 hour weeks and still trail bigger
competitors, so without love for the work you'll quit. (6) **Be efficient with your time** — only
24 hours a day; use RescueTime to find where you waste time (e.g. logging into Instagram 20+
times/day) and cut it. (7) **Find a problem you want to solve** — you make money by solving a
problem with a solution that's cheaper or more efficient (ideally both), not by wanting money.
