# 5 Quick Ways to Get More Instagram Likes

## Summary
Five Instagram engagement hacks, framed as learnings from running experiments across
his own @NeilPatel account plus "38 other accounts" in various industries. (1) Go live
twice a week — cites Earth Web (17.6% drop in users creating live content) and
Growthoid (live video expected to grow 15x from 2022 levels) as evidence of a supply
gap versus demand; going live builds followers, which then boosts like counts on
subsequent posts. (2) Get relevant influencers/friends to leave genuine, on-topic
comments within the first hour of posting, which signals engagement to the algorithm
and surfaces the post to non-followers — explicitly warns against writing the comment
for them ("I wouldn't type out that comment for the person"). (3) Reuse trending
meme/dance audio in videos even for off-niche accounts, timed to when a trend is newly
popping off. (4) Explicitly ask for engagement (tag a friend, double-tap, comment,
share, drop an emoji) — cites an internal test result of "roughly 38.45%" more likes on
average. (5) Use carousel posts instead of long captions, citing "49.22%" more
engagement on average; gives an example of a creator ("Codie") co-publishing a carousel
across her personal and business ("UnconventionalAcquisitions") accounts for added
reach. Closes with an NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- Cites Earth Web: "17.6%" decrease in users creating live content, and Growthoid: live
  video "expected to grow 15X" from 2022 levels (third-party stats as cited by Neil,
  dated 2022-11-05).
- States going live twice a week increases engagement/followers, which compounds into
  more likes on subsequent posts (self-reported tactical claim, dated 2022-11-05).
- Internal/agency-observed stat: explicitly asking for engagement (tag/double-tap/
  comment/share/emoji) produces "roughly 38.45% more likes" on average (self-reported,
  unaudited, dated 2022-11-05).
- Internal/agency-observed stat: carousel posts get "49.22% more engagement" on average
  versus single-image posts with long captions (self-reported, unaudited, dated
  2022-11-05).
- Recommends timing trending audio use to right when a trend "is popping off," and
  recommends account collaborations (dual-posting the same carousel) to extend reach.
