# The Shady Hack People Are Using to Get Crazy LinkedIn Growth

## Summary
Co-hosted Marketing School segment (main-channel republish, outro captioned "[Neil]", confirming the
recurring sign-off across this era belongs to Neil). Revisits and adds granular detail to the
LinkedIn engagement-manipulation tactic first flagged in [[2023-07-08-yt-JcjY_7p9PQc]]: buying
verified LinkedIn accounts with 500+ real (not random) connections from sites like playerup.com,
typically 20 at a time for $1,000–$2,000 (bulk discounts apply), then having someone log into each
account and leave substantive comments within four hours of a target post going live — most
effective on corporate/company pages, which get the most virality. Reports (from friends he says
he's tracking) that a given batch of purchased accounts works well for the first 1–3 months, but
needs refreshing after about 6 months. Neil explicitly disclaims the tactic ("Eric and I aren't
recommending this... neither him or I focus on this tactic or use it") while explaining it in detail
as "how it is." Pivots to a "white hat" alternative he says he does participate in: informal
engagement pods (WhatsApp groups of marketers who ping each other when they post, asking for genuine
engagement) — frames this as ethical because participants only engage with content they'd have liked
anyway, just timed to help it spread faster at launch; says these groups have organically turned into
real relationships he's happy to support. States his general framework for shady tactics: "look at
what people are doing in a shady way and figure out how you legitimize it" — the white-hat path takes
longer (short-term pain, long-term gain) versus black-hat's short-term gain, long-term pain, "which
we've learned the hard way."

## Key claims
- Adds granular tactical detail to the previously-flagged playerup.com LinkedIn tactic: verified
  accounts need 500+ real connections (not random ones), are typically bought ~20 at a time for
  $1,000–$2,000 with bulk discounts, work best on corporate/company-page posts, remain effective for
  1–3 months, and need refreshing after ~6 months (self-reported, dated 2023-07-24; see
  [[2023-07-08-yt-JcjY_7p9PQc]] for the original disclosure).
- Explicitly disclaims personal use of the playerup.com tactic while describing it in operational
  detail — states neither he nor Eric use or recommend it (self-reported, dated 2023-07-24).
- Discloses participation in "white hat" engagement pods (WhatsApp groups that alert members to new
  posts for prompt, genuine engagement), framing them as ethical because engagement is only
  encouraged for content members would have liked anyway — states these groups have organically
  produced real relationships (self-reported, dated 2023-07-24) — new material not previously logged.
- States his general heuristic for gray/black-hat tactics: study what shady operators do and find a
  legitimized version of it; frames white-hat effort as short-term pain for long-term gain versus
  black-hat's short-term gain for long-term pain, "which we've learned the hard way" (self-reported
  opinion, dated 2023-07-24).
