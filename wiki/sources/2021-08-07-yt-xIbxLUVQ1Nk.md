# When You Have Thousands of Website Visitors but Can't Sell More Than $100/Month

## Summary
Nine reasons for a traffic-without-revenue problem, most reinforcing existing doctrine
with a couple of fresh figures/metaphors. Reiterates the ~8-9M-visitors/<0.1%-conversion
self-critique (consistent with prior sources) and the traffic-vs-intent doctrine, using
Ubersuggest's CPC-filtered Keyword Ideas report to find higher-intent terms. Introduces
a **"sales is like dating" analogy** — proposing marriage to a stranger fails, but a
staged courtship (date → coffee → more dates → meet friends/parents → marriage) succeeds,
mapped onto the need for a staged sales funnel (plugs Click Funnels). Reinforces the
sales-over-marketing belief with a Salesforce/stock-market-companies framing and repeats
the "Winning by Design" book recommendation plus a second title, "The Little Red Book to
Selling." Revisits the traffic/product mismatch problem using the same Instagram-
marketing-content-vs-services-offered example seen in other sources. On authority/
credibility, uses NP Digital's own case-study need as the example (paraphrase: without
case studies for other clients, ranking his own site was the credibility proof).
Checkout-process advice: a specific **two-step checkout figure** — asking for name/email
first, then remaining fields, lifts conversions "somewhere around 8, 9, 10, 11%,"
especially for new brands — plus a general "don't overdo payment options" note. Closes
on split-testing/CTA testing via Crazy Egg heatmaps and mouse-movement recordings.

## Key claims
- Two-step checkout (name/email first, then the rest) increases conversions by roughly
  8-11%, especially for new brands (self-reported estimate, dated 2021-08) — analytics-
  cro. New, more specific figure than prior general checkout-optimization mentions.
- Frames sales funnels via a staged-courtship ("sales is like dating") analogy: a single
  ask converts poorly; staged trust-building converts well (paraphrase, dated 2021-08)
  — agency-entrepreneurship. New voice/rhetoric metaphor, not previously logged.
- Recommends "The Little Red Book to Selling" alongside the previously-cited "Winning By
  Design" as sales reading (paraphrase, dated 2021-08) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Reiterates <0.1% conversion rate on ~8-9M monthly neilpatel.com visitors as a
  cautionary self-critique of pure traffic-chasing (self-reported, dated 2021-08) — seo,
  analytics-cro. Consistent with prior sources' figures.
