# How to Get Backlinks From Any Website (Big or Small)

## Summary
A tactical link-building playbook structured around three pillars: value exchange,
finding the right contact, and personalized outreach. Value creation tactics: pitch
fixes for broken/outdated content on the target site, or offer infographics/guides/
original-research/data-studies as link-worthy assets (Neil says his own
highest-backlink NeilPatel.com articles are chart-driven, e.g. "17 charts" pieces);
also recommends "ultimate guides" (5,000–10,000+ words, no fluff) and
templates/checklists. Finding the right contact: ask for an introduction rather than
using generic contact forms; use LinkedIn to find content/marketing leads; check
author bios; use Chrome extensions (hunter.io, VoilaNorbert, Find That Email) to find
emails; leverage mutual connections for warm intros (higher conversion than cold
outreach). Outreach script structure: (1) attention-grabbing line noting a specific
site issue, (2) a stats-backed solution, (3) an ask to connect. Recommends Mailshake
for personalized mail-merge outreach and tracking a spreadsheet of prospects/responses/
follow-ups, framing link building as "sales... it's all about the follow up."

## Key claims
- As of 2020-04-13, Neil said his own best-performing NeilPatel.com articles for
  earning 100+ backlinks are chart/data-driven pieces (e.g. content-marketing or SEO
  trend charts).
- As of 2020-04-13, Neil recommended hunter.io, VoilaNorbert, and Find That Email as
  free/freemium Chrome extensions for finding prospect email addresses for outreach.
- As of 2020-04-13, Neil said warm introductions (via mutual connections) convert
  better than cold outreach for link-building asks.
- As of 2020-04-13, Neil's outreach-script framework: (1) note a specific issue on
  their site, (2) offer a stats-backed solution, (3) ask to connect — and recommended
  Mailshake for personalized mail-merge outreach at scale.
