# Should You Use Hashtags on Instagram? | How to Get More Instagram Followers

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that hashtags have largely lost their value on Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn — where people already post plenty of text — but remain worthwhile on Instagram because it is a visual network where a photo alone gives the platform little to categorize. On Instagram, users still click through hashtags to discover photos, making hashtags an easy discovery mechanism. His practical guidance: use only five to seven hashtags (dozens is too many), make them very specific to the photo so they stay relevant, and choose hashtags that generate real traffic. He suggests using a tool like Ubersuggest to enter a keyword related to the photo and get a large list of hashtag ideas derived from what people search on Google.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-03, Neil states:
- Hashtags were widely used years ago but are now essentially old-school and unused on Facebook and Twitter (as of 2017-11-03).
- Hashtags do not really matter anymore on Twitter, Facebook, or LinkedIn, and are not needed on LinkedIn (as of 2017-11-03).
- Hashtags still provide value on Instagram, where many people continue to click through them to find photos (as of 2017-11-03).
- Instagram is a visual social network, so the platform does not really know what a photo is about; a description helps but hashtags help significantly more (as of 2017-11-03).
- On Facebook, LinkedIn, and Twitter people post a lot of accompanying text, whereas on Instagram people often post just a photo and nothing else, which is why hashtags matter there (as of 2017-11-03).
- You should use roughly five, six, or seven hashtags; dozens of hashtags is too many (as of 2017-11-03).
- Hashtags should be very specific to your photo so they stay relevant and drive more traffic (as of 2017-11-03).
- You should pick hashtags that generate a lot of traffic (as of 2017-11-03).
- A tool like Ubersuggest lets you type in any keyword related to your photo and returns hundreds of hashtag/keyword ideas based on what people search for on Google, and using similar ones as Instagram hashtags will generate more traffic (as of 2017-11-03).
