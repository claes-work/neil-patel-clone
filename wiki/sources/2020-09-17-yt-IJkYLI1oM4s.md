# Build Your Instagram Profile The Right Way — Instagram Unlocked, Module 1, Lesson 2

## Summary
Second lesson of Neil's "Zero to 100,000 Followers" Instagram Unlocked course (six-week
series; course hub: `neilpatel.com/training`). Covers Instagram profile setup: the bio
link as "the main profit center" of a profile (recommends pointing it at a lead-capture
page/landing page over a bare homepage when possible); bio composition (name, brand,
short description, link); Stories as his favorite conversion driver after the bio link
(cites Will Smith's stories as an example he personally follows, and Dhar Mann's
Story Highlights — e.g. a highlight built from his proposal-to-wife story sequence —
as a model for categorized Highlights with custom cover art); hashtag strategy (mix
popular with community-relevant tags found via Ubersuggest; caps of 30 per post/10 per
story; stuffing extra tags into the comments to work around the cap); call-to-action
discipline ("if you don't ask, you'll never receive" — attributed to his mom); single-
theme page discipline (his own theme: "all things marketing related") with occasional
personal images for personal brands; and content-calendar planning (recommends Trello,
personally; also lists Sked Social, Preview, Later, Ripl, Social Blade, Link.bio,
Canva, Storyart, Grum as tools).

## Key claims
- As of 2020-09-17, Neil frames the Instagram bio link as the "main profit center" of
  a profile — a passive selling asset that doesn't require new content to keep
  converting.
- As of 2020-09-17, Instagram Stories are cited as used by "roughly over 500 million
  users daily" (self-reported figure, no source given).
- As of 2020-09-17, Neil's stated hashtag caps: up to 30 on a regular post, up to 10
  on a story; exceeding these blocks the post/story from publishing.
- As of 2020-09-17, Neil personally uses Trello for his own content calendar ("I love
  Trello for my own content calendar. It's one of the easiest and simplest things.").
- As of 2020-09-17, Neil's page-theme doctrine: keep one theme (his own: "all things
  marketing related"); too many themes on one page "ends up looking like a mess."
