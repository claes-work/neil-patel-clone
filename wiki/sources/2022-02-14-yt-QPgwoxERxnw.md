# How to Plan 1 Week of Social Media Content in 60 Minutes (Tools and Hacks)

## Summary
A six-step weekly social-content-planning process, framed as doable in ~60 minutes.
Pre-work: maintain a running monthly list of video concepts (he keeps 16 at a time,
covering ~5 weeks at his ~3x/week posting cadence) and a similar rolling list of image
concepts, both stocked as a buffer for low-motivation days. Planning steps: (1) list
every content type you could publish that week (blog post, live video, story, shorts,
podcast promo, AMA, testimonials, quiz/poll, meme, collabs, etc.); (2) match content
types to platforms — gives platform-specific guidance (Instagram: visual/image-based,
carousels, short video, stories; Facebook: similar to Instagram plus blog/podcast
links; LinkedIn: mix of long-form posts, carousels, short video capped near 10 minutes,
podcast cuts, quote posts with cited sources; Twitter: most format-permissive except
long-form); (3) assign days/platforms per content type, recommending blog posts at
least twice weekly and video content at least weekly; (4) rough-outline each post; (5)
write captions, urging personality over "technical, bland, boring" copy; (6) schedule
via tools like Hootsuite or Buffer.
