# Project Magi - Google's Making Big Changes to Search

## Summary
Co-hosted segment on Google's then-in-development "Project Magi" conversational-search
initiative. Neil frames the competitive context: a New York Times report that Samsung was
considering making Bing its default search engine (which he says would cost Google ~$3 billion,
citing the NYT figure, with a larger loss if Apple did the same), plus Bard's rocky initial
reception and an associated stock-price/market-cap hit, as reasons Google is investing heavily
in Project Magi — reportedly ~160 engineers — to make search more conversational (ChatGPT-like)
and, eventually, transactional (e.g. learning a user's shoe size/brand preference and enabling
in-Google checkout). He predicts this will meaningfully reduce direct website traffic for some
queries (citing Google's historical precedent of directly answering weather and simple
arithmetic queries instead of sending clicks to third-party sites) but argues Google has, per
Google's Danny Sullivan, continued increasing organic traffic to websites overall year over
year, and that increased usage from a better experience can partially counteract the traffic
lost to direct-answer features. He predicts significant change to paid advertising, forecasting
a shift from cost-per-click toward cost-per-acquisition-style pricing where advertisers pay for
outcomes regardless of whether the user visits the underlying website, and a parallel SEO shift
from optimizing for click-throughs toward optimizing for being surfaced/selected within
AI-driven conversational shopping results.

## Key claims
- States a New York Times report described Samsung considering Bing as its default search
  engine, which he estimates (per the NYT figure) would cost Google roughly $3 billion, more if
  Apple did the same (reported/registry-adjacent claim, sourced to NYT reporting per Neil, dated
  2023-09-07).
- States Google's Project Magi involves roughly 160 engineers building a more conversational,
  eventually transactional, search experience (self-reported claim, dated 2023-09-07).
- Predicts Project Magi-style AI search will reduce direct website traffic for some queries but
  that Google has historically continued growing overall organic traffic to websites even after
  adding direct-answer features (e.g. weather, arithmetic), per Google's Danny Sullivan
  (self-reported/attributed claim, dated 2023-09-07).
- Predicts paid search will shift from cost-per-click toward cost-per-acquisition-style pricing,
  and SEO will shift from optimizing for click-throughs toward optimizing for inclusion in
  AI-driven conversational/transactional results (self-reported forecast, dated 2023-09-07).
