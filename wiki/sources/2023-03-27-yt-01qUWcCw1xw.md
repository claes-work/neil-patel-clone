# How to Create User-Focused Content That Converts

## Summary
Real-estate-investor-audience SEO interview ("Eat, Sleep, and Invest"). Core SEO
philosophy: content quality/user value beats keyword optimization — illustrated
with a "same laptop, half the price" example of why the better offer outranks
better on-page SEO alone; links are "votes," and endorsements from
high-authority/relevant sites (his political analogy: an ex-president's
endorsement swaying more votes than an average citizen's) matter more than
volume; explicitly advises against link-package sellers and PBNs (Private Blog
Networks) as short-term-only tactics that hurt long-term rankings, and against
donating to causes (e.g. local youth groups) purely to earn backlinks. ★
Illustrates the donation point with a personal anecdote: buying a $100 bag of
popcorn from a Boy Scout at a hot Las Vegas grocery-store fundraiser, refusing
change, and explicitly not asking for a link or any reciprocity — framed as "the
purpose of donating is because it makes you happy... don't do it for links."
Real-estate-specific content advice: get very granular by sub-niche (flipping,
luxury coastal, multi-family, high-rise condos) rather than generic "get rich in
real estate" content; use first-person, conversational copywriting with
invented-but-plausible statistics as an illustration of technique (explicitly
tells the host he fabricated the "National Real Estate Association" stat and
average-days-on-market figure as a demonstration, alongside a real personal
anecdote that his own home sat on the market roughly six months when he sold
it). On AI content tools (ChatGPT/Jasper era): says they're a decent starting
point but don't yet produce great finished content. Business/company detail:
self-reports NP Digital at 750+ employees; bought a UK company in February 2023
for "~8.6 million" (matches the AnswerThePublic acquisition recorded elsewhere,
though the registry-verified acquisition date is 2022-05-31 — see note below);
Ubersuggest priced around $10-30/month; NP Digital adding Germany and soon
France/Italy/Spain/Portugal/Malaysia/Singapore offices. States he doesn't track
his own site's traffic anymore ("I stopped checking... it doesn't matter"),
preferring qualified-lead and opportunity-conversion metrics.
