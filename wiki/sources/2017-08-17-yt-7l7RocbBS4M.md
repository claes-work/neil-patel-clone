# 4 Steps to Increase Your Email Open Rates

## Summary
A short email-marketing video in which Neil Patel teaches four tactics to improve email
deliverability (getting into the inbox rather than the spam/Promotions folder), which he
frames as the driver of open rates and sales. The four steps: (1) warm up a new list
gradually — don't blast a 100,000-person list at once; ramp from ~1,000 to 3,000–4,000,
then 10k/15k/20k/40k/60k/100k over days so ISPs (Gmail, Hotmail, AOL) grow comfortable
and inbox your mail; (2) use casual, non-title-cased subject lines so recipients feel it
could be from a friend and open it; (3) scrub your list of non-openers — monthly if you
send more than four or five emails a month, otherwise after every four or five sends —
because ISPs treat mail to many non-openers as spam; and (4) send text-based (minimal
HTML) emails, since image-heavy templated emails signal "promotion" and land in Gmail's
Promotions tab.

> ⚠️ NOTE: The ledger/video title is "4 Steps to Increase Your Email Open Rates," but the
> content is about email **deliverability** (inbox placement). Neil treats deliverability
> as the precondition for opens — the two are linked, not identical.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-17, Neil states:
- Step 1 — Warm up a new list gradually. Don't fire off 100,000 emails at once; if a big
  send gets low opens, ISPs (Gmail, Hotmail, AOL) start routing your mail to spam. Ramp
  volume over days (~1,000 → 3,000–4,000 → 10k → 15k → 20k → 40k → 60k → 100k) to warm up
  the ISPs so they inbox your mail (as of 2017-08-17).
- Step 2 — Use casual subject lines (not every word capitalized, not overly proper); a
  casual line reads like it could be from a friend, prompting opens (as of 2017-08-17).
- Step 3 — Scrub your list of people who don't open. If you send more than four or five
  emails a month, scrub monthly; if you send fewer, scrub after every four or five sends.
  Continuing to email non-openers makes ISPs treat all your mail as spam (as of
  2017-08-17).
- Step 4 — Send text-based emails (with minimal HTML). Image-heavy templated promotional
  emails get recognized and routed to the Gmail Promotions tab; text-based emails read as
  friendly/personal and land in the inbox (as of 2017-08-17).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Every email provider out there, Gmail, Hotmail, AOL, what they do is, if you send a ton of emails and people don't open them up, your emails will automatically start going to people's spam box."

> "When you send someone an email with a casual subject line, they're like, 'Oh, this could be a friend, let me just open it.'"

> "If you take your emails and only send them to people who are opening, in other words, scrub your list."

> "When you send text-based emails, Google or Gmail is like, 'Oh, cool, this could be a friendly personal email, so let me put this within the inbox.'"
