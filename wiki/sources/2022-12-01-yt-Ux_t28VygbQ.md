# 5 Hacks for Small Businesses to Outrank Larger Ones

## Summary
David-vs-Goliath SEO framing: big brands have domain authority but are slow-moving
("not agile... a lot of politics"), so small businesses should avoid competing directly
on head keywords and instead exploit five angles. (1) UX: cites Google's former head of
web spam Matt Cutts on user-focused signals mattering more than link/anchor-text
optimization; checklist of credibility, design, clarity, transparent pricing. (2)
Social presence: invokes the "rule of seven" (roughly seven brand interactions to
create loyalists) and quotes ex-Google CEO Eric Schmidt ("Brands are the solution, not
the problem. Brands are how you sort out the cesspool.") — self-reports posting on
Facebook/Instagram/TikTok and always tying posts back to NP Digital's site, driving
"thousands" of monthly branded searches. (3) Use canonical tags / 301-redirect
duplicate/near-duplicate pages so Google isn't confused about which page to rank. (4)
Optimize local listings (accurate NAP data, images, hours, reviews) — notes seeing "a
ton of super small businesses" get this wrong via inconsistent naming/address. (5)
Write detailed content and refresh it often — explicitly warns against assuming longer
is always better ("Google prioritizes experience," not word count for its own sake);
notes NP Digital clients often get more traffic/ROI from refreshing old content than
publishing new. Closes with an NP Digital plug.
