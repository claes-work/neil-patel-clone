# Learn the Best Marketing Strategies and Marketing Techniques for Free

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out a three-step, free self-education path for learning online marketing. First, read marketing blogs daily to stay current with a fast-changing industry — he names neilpatel.com/blog, moz.com/blog, searchengineland.com, and Backlinko as four sites that will teach everything needed. Second, and in his view most important, implement what you learn — but not on your own or your employer's business at the start; instead spin up a free throwaway WordPress.com site and test tactics there until you see what works, then port the working tactics to a real business. Third, use free diagnostic tools to guide the fixes: his own neilpatel.com/seo-analyzer report, Google Search Console's error reporting, or paid tools like Moz. He frames the whole method around learning by doing, opening by admitting he is good but not the best at marketing and that one can always improve.

## Key claims
- The way to learn online marketing for free is a three-step loop: read marketing blogs, implement/test on a practice site, and use free diagnostic tools to guide fixes (as of 2017-06-19).
- Four blogs will teach everything needed to learn online marketing: neilpatel.com/blog, moz.com/blog, searchengineland.com, and Backlinko; they should be read daily to stay on top of industry trends (as of 2017-06-19).
- Marketers who fail to stay current become outdated because Google, Facebook, and other social and ad platforms constantly change (as of 2017-06-19).
- Beginners should not implement new tactics on their own or their employer's business first; they should create a free "fake" WordPress.com site, test tactics there, and only move a tactic to a real business once it shows signs of working (as of 2017-06-19).
- Reading alone does not teach marketing — Neil says he learned marketing through actually doing, and without implementation you never learn (as of 2017-06-19).
- Free diagnostic tools guide what to fix: neilpatel.com/seo-analyzer returns a report of what is wrong with a page, and Google Search Console lists the errors Google finds on your site; paid tools like Moz are an alternative (as of 2017-06-19).
- Fixing the errors these tools surface will naturally increase a site's rankings, though results take time (as of 2017-06-19).
