# Designing Your Customer Experience & Case Studies - Module 7 - Part 3 - SEO Unlocked

## Summary
**Final lesson of SEO Unlocked** (module 7, part 3) — Neil calls it "both a happy day
and a sad day." Topic: designing customer experience so customers become evangelists.
Advises planning a minimum of **five sequential experience touchpoints** (not every
one will land, but a series increases the odds of at least a few delighting/
surprising the customer) and hitting emotional touchpoints in copy/video (example:
Nike's "Just Do It" campaigns), not just functional ones. Centerpiece case study:
**Mike Repole and Vitamin Water** — identifying an unmet need in the ~$6B water/sports-
drink/energy-drink market, partnering with **50 Cent** (given money plus equity),
growing from $0 to $100M to $355M to $700M between 2005-2007, and Coca-Cola's **$4.1
billion** acquisition in 2007 — framed as proof of the power of solving a genuinely
unmet need. Pivots to a content-vs-paid-ads ROI argument: a $500K Google Ads spend
stops producing the moment spending stops, while a $500K content-marketing spend
keeps generating leads/sales after spending stops — his stated basis for content/SEO
having a much higher long-run ROI and being "the cheapest and most effective way to
build a brand." Restates the **rule of seven** (seven brand exposures build loyalty/
advocacy) and cites his own **8,200+ published blog posts** as an application of it —
framed explicitly as brand-building, not ego. States a self-critical counterfactual:
if starting over, he would **not** name his brand after himself, because "individual's
brand never makes as much money as a corporate brand" — citing Vitamin Water as the
kind of product-brand he'd build instead. Closes the entire SEO Unlocked course with a
"do the fundamentals over and over" message and thanks for the seven-week run.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-06-18, Neil cited the Vitamin Water case (Mike Repole founder, 50 Cent
  as an equity-partner investor) — self-reported growth from $0 to $100M to $355M to
  $700M revenue between 2005 and 2007, and a **$4.1 billion** Coca-Cola acquisition in
  2007 — as his model example of solving a genuinely unmet market need.
- As of 2020-06-18, Neil argued content marketing has a higher long-run ROI than paid
  ads because content keeps producing leads/sales after spend stops, using a $500K-vs-
  $500K Google-Ads-vs-content thought experiment.
- As of 2020-06-18, Neil restated the **rule of seven** (repeated brand exposure drives
  loyalty and advocacy) and cited his own **8,200+ published blog posts** as a direct
  application of that rule.
- As of 2020-06-18, Neil said if he were starting over he would **not** name his
  business after himself, because "individual's brand never makes as much money as a
  corporate brand" — an explicit restatement/reinforcement of his corporate-brand-
  over-personal-brand belief, this time paired with Vitamin Water as the model.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "If I had to do it over again, I wouldn't name my brand after me, Neil Patel... because individual's brand never makes as much money as a corporate brand."

> "That intersection is where amazing brands live." [referring to what you want to sell vs. what people want to buy]

> "It's the same reason why I published over 8,200 blog posts. It's because I want my brand to keep being out there, not because I want to have the biggest name or some ego."
