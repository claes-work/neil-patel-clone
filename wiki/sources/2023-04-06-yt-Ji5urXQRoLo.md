# Digital Marketing Tips for 2023

## Summary
Business-owner-audience interview mixing an alternate monetization origin story
with current NP Digital operating detail. ★ **Cold-calling origin variant**: in
response to "how did you get the dollars rolling in," he says he did not
initially monetize his own traffic — instead he cold-called companies bidding
on expensive Google ad terms in his space, offering free work paid only on
results ("I'll do your marketing for free. If you like the results, pay me"),
building the business through referrals from there. This is a different
monetization narrative than the community-college/Speech-101/first-client
account recorded elsewhere — presented here as "how I figured out the money
side" specifically, potentially describing an early client-acquisition tactic
rather than replacing the existing origin story (not reconciled, recorded
as-is). Repeats the omnichannel/"be everywhere eventually, start with 1-2
channels" strategy, and the same "8,000+ companies surveyed" 2023 AI-investment
statistic used in [[2023-04-03-yt-8NBFZE3EOBY]] this batch, plus the "first
inning of AI" analogy. On SEO tactics: recommends starting with a free
diagnostic (cites neilpatel.com/seo-analyzer), then prioritizing user value
over technical tricks; recommends Ubersuggest/AnswerThePublic's
"backlinks opportunity report" to find sites linking to 2-3 competitors (a
strong signal they'll link to you too). Platform recommendation: WordPress for
content/service sites, Shopify for e-commerce, calling Wix/Squarespace fine but
not his top picks. ★ **States NP Digital's client-acquisition mix is 76% from
employee referrals, client referrals, and word-of-mouth combined**, and that
big-brand clients (mentions Amazon/Viacom-scale relationships in the broader
conversation) come from the same three channels via patient, long-game
relationship-building rather than paid/organic marketing of the agency itself.
Names the current country footprint precisely: **US, Canada, Brazil, UK,
Germany, India, Australia, Singapore (8 currently)**, adding Spain and Portugal
next (→10), with Italy and France after that, plus active Latin America/EMEA/
APAC hiring. On COVID: says the initial shock forced layoffs, but the shift to
online work ultimately grew the business; the 2022 stock-market downturn hurt
more than COVID did, since falling client stock prices triggered
budget cancellations independent of actual marketing profitability. On culture:
reiterates hiring for shared values/culture fit as most critical in a company's
first 10-100 hires, delegating to those early hires to keep instilling it as
the company scales; on delegation, says founders shouldn't seek "more of
themselves" but people who are better at specific functions (creative,
process, sales) than the founder is. Says he personally has little fear of
failure because "you're going to be your harshest critic... most people won't
ever know or watch," but still has "grass is greener" impulses he's learned to
suppress via sustained focus over "the last four or five years." Money advice
repeated: "easier to save it than it's to make it" (consistent with existing
record, attributed to his mother elsewhere).
