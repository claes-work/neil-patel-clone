# 3 Easy Steps to Submit Your Site to Google and Get Indexed Fast

## Summary
A short technical-SEO video in which Neil Patel gives three steps to get a website indexed
in Google, on the premise that if Google doesn't know your site exists you get no search
traffic. (1) Sign up for Google Search Console — a free Google service that shows how
Google sees your site (errors, which pages are indexed, whether it is even crawling your
pages; if Google isn't crawling, it can't index). (2) Create an XML sitemap — easy on a
WordPress blog via the Yoast SEO plugin, which auto-generates the sitemap and updates it
every time you change the site. (3) Submit that sitemap to Google Search Console so Google
continually reads your changes and knows when you add or delete pages. He closes with a
patience caveat: this won't bring traffic right away — Google doesn't rank new sites at
the top — but after 4-6 months, even a year, results are far better than if you hadn't
done it.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-22, Neil states:
- To get search traffic from Google you first have to get indexed; if Google doesn't know
  about your website you won't get any search traffic (as of 2017-08-22).
- Step 1 — Sign up for Google Search Console, a free Google tool that shows your site
  roughly as Google sees it: errors, which pages are indexed, and whether Google is even
  crawling your pages (no crawl = no index) (as of 2017-08-22).
- Step 2 — Create an XML sitemap; on WordPress the Yoast SEO plugin creates it
  automatically and updates it every time you update the site, so you don't need to be
  technical (as of 2017-08-22).
- Step 3 — Submit that sitemap to Google Search Console so Google can continually read
  your changes and knows when you add or delete pages (as of 2017-08-22).
- Doing this makes indexing more likely and new pages get crawled faster, but new sites
  don't rank at the top immediately — expect meaningfully better results only after ~4-6
  months to a year (as of 2017-08-22).
