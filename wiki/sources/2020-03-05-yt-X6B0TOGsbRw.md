# The #1 Biggest SEO Mistake Nearly Everyone Makes | Avoid This At All Costs

## Summary
Neil identifies "targeting keywords that are too competitive" as the single biggest SEO
mistake, given a market of a billion+ websites. He recommends long-tail keywords over
competitive head terms, citing his own traffic history (organic traffic hit 173,336
visitors/month after he began targeting long-tail keywords; now "well over a million").
He cites Search Engine Guide data that Amazon makes 57% of sales from long-tail
keywords. Walks through a dog-food example: "dog food" has ~135,000 monthly searches
but a pay/SEO difficulty of 100/77 (too competitive for new sites), whereas "dog food
for allergies" has 9,900 monthly searches and SEO difficulty 38 — realistically
rankable. Recommends Ubersuggest's Keyword Ideas report (target SEO difficulty ≤40,
volume in "at least the few thousands") and Content Ideas report (surfaces top content
by social shares, backlinks, search traffic for a given keyword) to plan content that
wins traffic from Google, social, and referral links simultaneously.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-03-05, Neil said his organic search traffic reached 173,336 visitors/month
  after he began targeting long-tail keywords instead of competitive head terms, and
  that he now gets "well over a million."
- As of 2020-03-05, Neil cited Search Engine Guide data that Amazon makes 57% of its
  sales from long-tail keywords, attributing this to higher commercial intent on longer,
  more specific search terms.
- As of 2020-03-05, Neil's worked example: "dog food" has ~135,000 monthly searches with
  pay difficulty 100 / SEO difficulty 77 (too hard for a new site), while "dog food for
  allergies" has 9,900 monthly searches and SEO difficulty 38 (realistically rankable).
- As of 2020-03-05, Neil recommended targeting keywords with an Ubersuggest SEO
  difficulty score of 40 or under, with monthly volume of at least a few thousand.
