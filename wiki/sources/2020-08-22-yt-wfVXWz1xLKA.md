# Go Omni Channel With Your Content Marketing - Module 4 - Lesson 3 - Content Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
The final lesson of **Content Marketing Unlocked** ([[2020-07-27-yt-9_rULUbLqE4]]) —
Neil explicitly calls it "the last lesson," completing the course (5 weeks,
2020-07-27 → 2020-08-22). Opens with why content marketing has become omnichannel:
text, video, audio, and multiple publishing surfaces (own site, Medium, WordPress,
Facebook, YouTube, podcasts) all now rank in Google, which is "getting more and more
popular." Cites stats: 24% of customers first discover a brand via its blog (a study
Neil says he "hasn't updated in years"); blogs generate 62% of organic traffic;
translating/transcribing content into other languages drives outsized traffic gains
given "billion plus blogs" of English-language competition. Names email marketing and
remarketing as the two biggest blog-traffic-to-revenue converters. Quotes Eric Schmidt
("most people don't want Google to answer the questions, they want Google to tell them
what they should be doing next") as the rationale for direct-answer content. Lists
named high-revenue content-marketing sites to emulate (Investopedia $14M, Dr. Axe
$20M+, Healthline $100M+, Penny Hoarder $36M+, NerdWallet $100M+, Credit Karma
$1B+ — self-reported/pulled from public web searches, not independently verified).
Closes the course with an 8-trait "great marketer" list — ambition, audience
understanding, focus, creativity, hard work, teamwork, customer retention, passion —
illustrated with the recurring "picking up trash and cleaning restrooms" hard-work
anecdote and a Richard Branson "my team, not me" story from a shared-conference
encounter.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-08-22, Neil claimed (citing an unrefreshed prior study) that 24% of
  customers first find a brand via its blog, and that blogs generate 62% of organic
  traffic (self-reported/uncited study, not re-verified by Neil at time of filming).
- As of 2020-08-22, Neil's omnichannel content thesis: text-only content marketing is
  obsolete — video and podcasting are "booming"/"rising fast" and must be mixed in.
- As of 2020-08-22, Neil listed named case-study sites with self-sourced revenue
  figures (Credit Karma "over a billion dollars," NerdWallet "over a hundred [million]")
  as proof of content marketing's ceiling — pulled from public web searches, not primary
  filings.
- As of 2020-08-22, Neil's 8 traits of a great content marketer: ambitious, understands
  the audience, focused, creative/original ("you don't win by copying"), hard-working,
  team-oriented, keeps existing customers happy (cites Amazon), and loves the work.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "I always tell people, one of my favorite jobs I ever had was picking up trash and cleaning restrooms... it taught me what hard work is."

> "There's no I in team. Without your team, you won't be able to build a big business." [recounting a conference conversation with Richard Branson, who told him "It wasn't me. It was my team."]

> "If you don't love what you're doing, then you're just not going to win. You're going to give up, you're going to be burnt out."
