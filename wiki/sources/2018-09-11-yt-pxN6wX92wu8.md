# 5 Insider Tips: How to Go From Page 2 to Page 1 of Google

## Summary

Solo SEO tactical. Neil opens with his company's joke — "where do you go to bury dead
bodies? Page two of Google" (no one visits page 2) — then walks through how to push a page
from page 2 to page 1. **(1) Steal your competitors' links.** Put the URLs ranking ahead
of you into **Ahrefs** to see everyone linking to them; first make sure *your* page is
actually better than theirs (Google ranks the best page, so the tactic fails otherwise),
then email each linking site a customized outreach note ("Hey John, I noticed you linked
to X… I cover A/B/C they don't, feel free to check it out"). His generic template is a
starting point you must customize; expect ~4–5 links per 100 emails, and rankings "slowly
climb." **(2) Optimize your title tag for clicks.** Use **Google Search Console** to see
which keywords earn impressions vs clicks; if your main keyword isn't getting clicks,
rewrite the title to evoke curiosity ("The 7 benefits of green tea — number 5 will shock
you"). CTR is a ranking factor: if searchers keep clicking the #2 result over #1, Google
reads #2 as more valuable and eventually swaps them. **(3) Treat keywords as problems, not
words.** A query is usually a problem someone wants solved; use Search Console to find all
the keywords a URL ranks for, then expand the page to solve them all — better UX means
fewer people bounce back to the SERP (pogo-sticking tells Google they didn't find their
answer). **(4) Build your brand.** Brand is one of the biggest ranking factors — people
buy Nike/Adidas or use Amex/Visa from familiarity, and "Google loves ranking brands higher
than non-brands" because anyone can build links or do on-page SEO, but a brand signals
you're in it for the long haul. Since you can't afford TV/billboards/magazines, build
brand with tools: **PRServe.com** (performance-based press — pay only when they get you
mentions), **Subscribers.com** (browser push to bring people back), **HelloBar.com** (email
capture), **Crazy Egg** (user recordings to improve UX), and surveys via **Koala[ro] /
SurveyMonkey** — and above all, **put the user first** (you make money by solving problems,
not by chasing money). He closes on patience: pages jump quickly from page 1000 to page 8,
maybe to page 3–4, but Google deliberately slows the climb near the top to confirm you're
truly providing a better experience than those above you.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-09-11, get **competitor backlinks via Ahrefs** — verify your page is better,
  pull their linkers, and send customized outreach; expect ~4–5 links per 100 emails and a
  slow ranking climb.
- As of 2018-09-11, **rewrite title tags to raise CTR** (use Search Console
  impressions-vs-clicks; evoke curiosity). Higher CTR than the results above you can push
  your listing up, because Google reads clicks as a relevance/value signal.
- As of 2018-09-11, **treat keywords as problems**: expand a page to solve every relevant
  query it ranks for so visitors don't pogo-stick back to Google (a "didn't find the
  answer" signal).
- As of 2018-09-11, **brand is one of the biggest ranking factors** — "Google loves
  ranking brands higher than non-brands"; anyone can build links or do on-page SEO, but a
  brand signals longevity and trust.
- As of 2018-09-11, build brand cheaply with tools: PRServe.com (performance-based press),
  Subscribers.com (browser push), HelloBar.com (email capture), Crazy Egg (user
  recordings), Koala[ro]/SurveyMonkey (surveys) — and **put the user first**.
- As of 2018-09-11, **rankings rise fast at first but slow near the top** — Google
  deliberately takes its time to confirm you provide a better experience than incumbents.
