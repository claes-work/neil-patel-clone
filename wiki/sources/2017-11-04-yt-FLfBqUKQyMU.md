# How to Promote Your Blog Without Paid Ads | 5 Sneaky Ways to Explode Your Blog Traffic!

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that while paid ads deliver ROI and he uses them daily, they are not the only way to grow a business, and he lays out five free, "sneaky" tactics to drive blog traffic — all built on piggybacking off competitors' existing audiences. The five: (1) monitor social platforms for people discussing competitors and join the conversation to surface your comparable content; (2) leave genuine, non-negative comments on competitors' blog posts and casually link back to your related content; (3) use Ahrefs to find who links to a competitor and send casual, non-pushy outreach emails; (4) use BuzzSumo to find a competitor's most-shared posts, write a better version via Brian Dean's skyscraper technique, then ask the people who shared the original to share yours; and (5) find people complaining about competitors on social, help them, and convert those "haters" into evangelists. Neil frames the whole approach as building community and traffic without spending money.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-04, Neil states:
- Paid ads do provide ROI and are worth doing if the ROI is amazing; he still uses paid ads every day, but they are not the only way to grow a business (as of 2017-11-04).
- Tactic 1 — Search social platforms (e.g., Facebook, Twitter) for people talking about your competitors, positively or negatively, and engage in that conversation to point them to your similar content, which brings more traffic and blog awareness (as of 2017-11-04).
- His competitor Mixpanel used this social-engagement tactic against his company Kissmetrics, which is how he learned it (as of 2017-11-04).
- Tactic 2 — Comment on competitors' blog posts; keep it positive and non-competitive (e.g., "nice post"), add tips, then mention and link to your own related content for the reader to check out (as of 2017-11-04).
- Tactic 3 — Use Ahrefs to enter a competitor's blog URL, see every site linking to them, and send each linker a simple, casual email noting your similar-but-more-advanced post — without directly asking for a link — which makes people more likely to include and link to your post (as of 2017-11-04).
- Tactic 4 — Use BuzzSumo to enter a competitor's blog URL and see their most (and least) popular posts sorted by social shares (as of 2017-11-04).
- For those top posts, apply Brian Dean's skyscraper technique: write a more detailed, "one-upped" version (e.g., "101 advanced ways" versus their "20 amazing ways") (as of 2017-11-04).
- BuzzSumo's "view shares" link reveals who shared a competitor's content, and you should then ask each of those sharers to share your content too (as of 2017-11-04).
- Tactic 5 — Everyone has complaints, and people complain about your competitors daily; find those complainers on social, help them, tell them about your blog and solutions, and turn competitors' haters into your evangelists, building community and passion for your brand (as of 2017-11-04).
- Doing these five tactics will get more traffic to your blog without spending any money on paid ads (as of 2017-11-04).
