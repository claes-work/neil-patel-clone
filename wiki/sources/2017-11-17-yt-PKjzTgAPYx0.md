# PPC Calculator: How to Measure ROI For Pay Per Click Advertising

## Summary
Neil Patel warns that ad-platform conversion numbers are systematically inflated: Facebook Ads, Google AdWords, and even Google Analytics almost always report more conversions than you actually get. Because they over-report, optimizing your spend on those figures can lose you money — if a customer is worth $100 and Google claims 10 conversions when you really got 5, you'll misallocate budget. The fix is not a software product; it's checking your own database and bank account for real sales and lead numbers. He notes the discrepancy affects nearly everyone, so companies apply a rule-of-thumb discount (e.g., halving the reported number if their database shows conversions run at half) and optimize spend against true conversions rather than the dashboard's.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-17, Neil states:
- Facebook and Google try to tell you your ROI, but the numbers in your Facebook and AdWords accounts usually always show more conversions than you're really getting (as of 2017-11-17).
- AdWords, Facebook, and even Google Analytics likely show more conversions than your real ecommerce/lead numbers; you need to look in your own database (as of 2017-11-17).
- Over-reporting causes real loss: if a customer is worth $100 and Google says 10 customers when you only got 5, optimizing spend on that inflated figure can lose you money (as of 2017-11-17).
- The numbers are off for almost everyone, not just you; companies double-check metrics and apply a rule-of-thumb discount (e.g., if the database shows half, treat Facebook's reported 1,000 sales as ~500) and optimize spend on true conversions (as of 2017-11-17).
- There is no quick software fix; the real method is checking your database and bank account for money actually coming in, rather than trusting a software solution's numbers (as of 2017-11-17).
