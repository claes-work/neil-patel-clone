# 3 DISASTROUS Business Mistakes All Entrepreneurs Make

## Summary

A Neil-fronted monologue on three mistakes entrepreneurs make when launching a product. (1) **Don't build the product first — validate with a landing page and take payments.** Rather than sinking time/money into building, create a landing page (Leadpages, Unbounce, ClickFunnels), promote the idea, and take payments; if you get traction, build it — if it takes too long, refund people (nothing wrong with that). He cites Kickstarter as proof: idea → landing page → promote → build only after results. (2) **MVP your product** — even after people are paying, don't build a "fully-bloated" feature set; read Eric Ries's *The Lean Startup* and build a minimum viable product with only what people actually need, then keep iterating, to avoid discovering "80% of the features we built are useless" after spending on development. (3) **Build up hype** — don't just quietly release; like Apple, create events, press, sneak previews, and a waiting list/signup so demand queues up; sell out, then close it off and keep building hype so the next launch has an even bigger line. He closes with his recurring "I'm not looking for anything, I just want to help — leave a comment" sign-off.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-05-22, Neil advises validating a product idea with a landing page that takes real payments (via Leadpages/Unbounce/ClickFunnels) before building, and refunding buyers if the build takes too long — likening it to Kickstarter's promote-then-build model.
- As of 2018-05-22, Neil recommends building a minimum viable product (citing Eric Ries's *The Lean Startup*) rather than a bloated feature set, then iterating, to avoid wasting development money on unwanted features.
- As of 2018-05-22, Neil argues that building hype pre-launch — events, press, sneak previews, and a signup/waiting list, as Apple does — lets you sell out fast; after selling out, close the offer and keep building hype for the next launch.
