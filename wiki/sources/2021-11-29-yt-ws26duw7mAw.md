# How Much Time Should You Spend on SEO in Order to Rank #1?

## Summary
Prescribes weekly/monthly SEO time budgets for two growth modes: "slow and steady"
(3-6 hrs/wk research+content, 4 hrs/mo competitive research, 4 hrs/mo technical SEO via
Ubersuggest Site Audit) vs. "aggressive" (3 hrs/day research+content, 1 hr/day
link-building/promotion, 3 hrs/wk email writing/scheduling, 1 hr/wk lead-magnet
creation, 3 hrs/wk social content). Then discloses his own personal routine on an
established site (neilpatel.com): weekly Ubersuggest competitive-domains check
(keyword overlap = shared industry) to find content gaps; a Google Search Console
routine looking for pages with declining quarter-over-quarter/year-over-year traffic,
diagnosed against keyword relevance; keeping on-page/technical SEO "buttoned up" via
Ubersuggest's automatic weekly site audits, fixed in the tool's presented priority
order; and — his stated single biggest time investment — email list collection via
lead magnets and gated content, justified by "the rule of seven" (people need ~7 brand
touches before evangelizing/buying) and a claim that built brands rank higher because
"Google trusts brands" and wants to protect users from unverified sources. Discloses a
delegation detail: a team of six to seven people continuously refreshes/prunes old
content for relevance (not word count), which he credits with sustaining SEO traffic.
