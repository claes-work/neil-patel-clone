# The Marketing Genius Behind Elon Musk

## Summary
Neil frames Elon Musk's marketing as deliberately unconventional stunts that generate
outsized buzz (as of 2022-05-30): the Cybertruck-vs-Ford-F150 tug-of-war, the
"unbreakable" armored-glass demo where the window broke on camera twice, naming a
child after a password-reset default, flamethrower sales (20,000+), launching a Tesla
Roadster into space, and a brain-chip pig demo. Cites a stat that even the failed
window-glass demo generated 661,000+ search results for "Cybertruck window" and, per
Notebook Check, over $80 billion in Cybertruck presales — his point being that
"failures" still generate revenue if they generate attention. He then reframes his own
Ubersuggest strategy explicitly as his version of Elon's "think outside the box"
playbook: giving away 90%+ of what competitors (SEMrush, Ahrefs, Moz) charge for, for
free. States Ubersuggest is his site's most popular page (20,000+ referring backlinks)
and is now NP Digital's number-one sales-generating channel, calling it more effective
than buying Google/Facebook ads while noting he isn't against paid ads when they're
profitable. Repeats the Inc. Magazine 21st-fastest-growing-company figure (a
recurrence).

## Key claims
- Neil frames giving Ubersuggest away for free (vs. competitors like SEMrush/Ahrefs/
