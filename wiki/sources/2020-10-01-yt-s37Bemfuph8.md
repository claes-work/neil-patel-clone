# Why You Should Write Content For Google and Not Social Media

## Summary
A live real-time-Google-Analytics demo of neilpatel.com's traffic mix, used to argue
for prioritizing search-intent content over social-shareable content. Recorded on a
Saturday: Neil shows ~1,000 real-time visitors on neilpatel.com, of which ~416 (he
calculates 41.24%) are organic-Google, versus only 4-5 visitors from social sources
(YouTube/Facebook/Reddit combined) — framed as "roughly 100 times more traffic coming
from SEO than I am getting for social media" (rechecked at video's end: 397 organic vs.
5 social). He then uses Ubersuggest's "top pages" and "social media content ideas"
tooling on his own domain and on the "digital marketing" keyword to show an inverse
relationship: articles with the highest social-share counts often get the least
organic search traffic, while lower-social-share, "how to"/definitional articles (his
example: "how to tie a tie," 450,000 monthly searches, which he says "no one" would
share virally) drive far more consistent organic volume. His conclusion/framework: (1)
first write the content people search for on Google (found via Ubersuggest keyword
research), written for humans not algorithms; (2) only then layer in more
advanced/opinionated content designed for social shares and backlinks (his tie-color
symbolism example). Names this baseline organic-search traffic "evergreen traffic."

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-01, Neil's live-data claim for neilpatel.com: roughly 100x more
  real-time traffic from organic Google search than from social media referrals
  (measured in a single Saturday snapshot: ~416 organic vs. ~4 social, then ~397
  organic vs. ~5 social).
- As of 2020-10-01, Neil's stated pattern across his own and third-party content
  analysis: high-social-share articles tend to have low organic search traffic, and
  vice versa — social virality and search-intent volume are largely uncorrelated in
  his observation.
- As of 2020-10-01, Neil's content-sequencing doctrine: write search-intent content
  first (via Ubersuggest keyword research), then add advanced/opinion content aimed at
  social shares/backlinks second — not the reverse.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "That's pretty much 100 times more traffic coming from SEO than I am getting for social media."

> "No one really cares to link to an article on how to tie a tie... but that gets a lot of traffic."

> "Write it for humans, of course, but... write content that people search for on Google."
