# Skyrocket your eCommerce with Shopping/Product Campaigns - Module 4 - Part 1 - eCommerce Unlocked

## Summary
eCommerce Unlocked lesson walking step-by-step through setting up Google Shopping Ads
(Merchant Center account → Google Ads PLA campaign → standard shopping campaign,
disable enhanced CPC, $10 starter budget, exclude non-shipped regions), using
Ubersuggest to find product-naming keywords and analyze competitor keywords via the
"traffic analyzer." Covers importing the Google Merchant feed into Bing Ads for a
head start, and third-party feed-management apps (paid and free) for
Google/Bing/Facebook/Instagram feeds. Recommends updating product feeds at least every
30 days (Neil does his weekly). Covers Facebook/Instagram dynamic product ads setup
(Business Manager → catalogs → product feed) and notes Instagram often outperforms
Facebook for his ecommerce clients, though it varies by business. Closes on
profitability tactics: live chat, heatmaps/A-B testing via Crazy Egg, and abandoned-cart
email/push-notification sequences (cites a ~10% sales lift from optimizing these).

## Key claims
- As of 2020-07-13, Neil recommended starting Google Shopping campaigns paused with a
  low ($10) test budget and low per-click bids, tweaking up only after data comes in —
  framed as a "get off to the races" starter approach, not an optimized end-state.
- As of 2020-07-13, Neil said that on the ecommerce accounts he's seen, Instagram
  typically drives more sales than Facebook, though he caveats "it does vary from
  business to business."
- As of 2020-07-13, Neil recommended updating product feeds "at least every 30 days,"
  stating his own practice is weekly.
- As of 2020-07-13, Neil said optimizing abandoned-cart email/push-notification
  sequences "in general should be able to increase your sales by 10%" (unsourced
  rule-of-thumb figure).
- As of 2020-07-13, Neil positioned remarketing campaigns (targeting site visitors,
  especially those who reached the checkout page but didn't convert) as one of his
  favorite, easiest-to-set-up, highest-ROI ad types.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "We're seeing on our end more sales from ecommerce, typically through Instagram, then we are from Facebook. Now it does vary from business to business."

> "One of my favorite approaches and this is one of the easiest ones is just setting up a remarketing campaign... it tends to have one of the highest ROI so I definitely recommend setting that up."

> "You don't want to start too high, you want to start low and then increase, don't go the other way around."
