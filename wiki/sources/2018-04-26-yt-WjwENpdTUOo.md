# Exactly How Many Links Can I Build Per Day for Natural SEO Ranking?

## Summary

A "Q&A Thursday" episode co-hosted with Adam from Viewership, answering a viewer (Vishvendra) asking whether there is a safe daily link-building limit. Neil's answer: there is no universal number — it depends on the site — but he teaches a ramp. A brand-new site with no links should start at roughly one link/day (~30 in month one), then ramp to ~60, then ~90; once a few hundred links are indexed by Ahrefs you can build in larger, irregular bursts (100 one month, 0 the next, 300 later) as long as they are natural. He stresses that a sudden influx of links to a linkless site looks manipulative to Google, that "slow and steady wins the race," and that links take roughly a year (sometimes 1.5–2 years) to fully kick in. Quality trumps quantity: one relevant, in-content, high-domain-authority link beats ten garbage links, and the ideal link is contextual/in-text on a highly relevant high-DA page. He and Adam warn against buying links (Neil recounts buying ~1,000 Fiverr links on his first site as "the worst decision of my life"), note that .edu/.gov/.mil links are not inherently better in his testing, and describe stopping their own scholarship-giveaway link tactic because too many people were abusing it.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-04-26, Neil says there is no fixed number of links you can safely build per day — it varies by website — but you can estimate a target by using Ahrefs to compare your backlink count against competitors ranking above you.
- As of 2018-04-26, Neil advises a brand-new site start at ~1 link/day (~30 in month one), then ramp to ~2/day (~60), then ~90/month; after a few hundred Ahrefs-indexed links you can build in larger, irregular amounts as long as they are natural.
- As of 2018-04-26, Neil says a sudden influx of links to a site with none looks unnatural to Google and risks appearing manipulative; slow and steady is safer.
- As of 2018-04-26, Neil claims links take roughly a year — sometimes 1.5 to 2 years — to reach their maximum ranking strength, though Google can pick up an individual link anywhere from a day to a month.
- As of 2018-04-26, Neil says link quality beats quantity: one relevant, in-content (contextual, in-text), high-domain-authority link is worth more than ten low-quality links from resource pages, sidebars, or footers.
- As of 2018-04-26, Neil states that in his testing .edu, .gov, and .mil links are not inherently better than normal links; a relevant in-text high-DA link from a site in your industry outperforms regardless of TLD.
- As of 2018-04-26, Neil (and Adam) warn never to buy links (e.g., from Fiverr) as it usually gets you banned or penalized.
