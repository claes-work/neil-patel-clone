# Biography research dossier — Neil Patel (Phase 2, 2026-07-14)

Deep-dive biographical research on **Neil Patel, the digital-marketing / SEO entrepreneur**
(co-founder of NP Digital, Crazy Egg, KISSmetrics, Hello Bar; owner of Ubersuggest and Quick
Sprout; co-host of the daily "Marketing School" podcast). Compiled from web sources on
2026-07-14. Every claim carries a confidence marker:

- **✅ registry / primary document** — official registry, court filing, or primary institutional record.
- **📰 reported** — independent third-party journalism or an institution.
- **🗣️ self-reported** — the subject's own accounts (his blog, "about" page, podcast, interviews).

> ⚠️ **DISAMBIGUATION.** This is NOT the actor **Neel/Neil Patel**, NOT the political
> aide/Trump-administration official Neil Patel (co-founder of *The Daily Caller*), and NOT
> the aerospace figure. This dossier is strictly the **marketer** born 1985, based in
> California, who runs neilpatel.com and NP Digital.

A recurring caveat threads this whole file: Neil Patel is a professional marketer whose entire
business is *ranking his own name and narrative*. Much of his "biography" is **self-reported**
and repeated verbatim by SEO-optimized aggregator blogs that quote his "about" page. Aggregator
echo is NOT independent corroboration — treat the chorus of biography-farm sites (namesbiography,
wikibioage, thecityceleb, etc.) as downstream of his own copy, not as press. Registry and court
records below are the load-bearing verified facts.

## 5. Crazy Egg (2005/2006–)

- **Crazy Egg** — website-optimization SaaS producing **heatmaps, scrollmaps and click
  tracking** to visualize how visitors interact with a page. Co-founded by **Neil Patel and
  Hiten Shah**. 📰 (https://www.crazyegg.com/about-us;
  https://mixergy.com/interviews/crazyegg-neil-patel-interview/)
- **Founding year — CONTRADICTION:**
  > ⚠️ CONTRADICTION: **2005** ("got lucky with Crazy Egg in 2005" — Growth Ramp / Hiten Shah
  > accounts, https://www.growthramp.io/articles/hiten-shah) vs **2006** (widely repeated
  > "co-founded Crazy Egg in 2006", https://www.yourtechstory.com/...). Reconcilable as
  > *conceived/started 2005, launched 2006*. Some bios also loosely call it "his first company
  > in 2008," which is wrong (conflates it with KISSmetrics). Treat founding as **~2005–2006**.
- Bootstrapped/slow-build; struggled to raise VC early, eventually profitable. 🗣️/📰
  (Mixergy: "CrazyEgg: The Slow-Build Bootstrap").
- Crazy Egg is reported to still operate (getlatka lists ~$4.5M revenue, ~4K customers, 2025).
  📰 (https://getlatka.com/companies/crazyegg) — see [[crazy-egg]].

## 6. KISSmetrics (2008–~2018)

- **KISSmetrics** — customer/behavioral **analytics and attribution** platform (tracking user
  journeys across channels), a "bigger problem" than Crazy Egg. Co-founded by **Neil Patel and
  Hiten Shah**, **2008**, funded by **True Ventures**. 🗣️/📰
  (https://neilpatel.com/about/; https://grokipedia.com/page/Neil_Patel)
- The **KISSmetrics blog** became a landmark SaaS content-marketing operation (a case study in
  its own right). 📰 (https://saasclub.io/podcast/hiten-shah-kissmetrics-crazyegg/)
- **CONTROVERSY — "supercookies" (2011):** ✅ court-documented.
  > ⚠️ CONTROVERSY: In **August 2011**, after **UC Berkeley researchers** published a report,
  > a class action (**Kim & Schutzman v. KISSmetrics**) alleged KISSmetrics used **ETags /
  > "supercookies"** to **re-spawn deleted cookies** and covertly track users. KISSmetrics
  > **settled** (finalized ~2012): it paid ~**$500,000** in plaintiffs' attorney fees, **$2,500
  > each** to the two named plaintiffs, and agreed **not to use ETags/Flash "supercookies"
  > without notice/opt-out.** A related suit tied to Hulu "zombie cookies" also named it.
  > ✅ (https://topclassactions.com/lawsuit-settlements/lawsuit-news/kissmetrics-settles-etags-tracking-class-action-lawsuit/;
  > https://news.bloomberglaw.com/tech-and-telecom-law/kissmetrics-settles-claims-over-user-data...;
  > https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evercookie)
  Note: the settlement was corporate; Neil is not personally named in most coverage, but as
  co-founder this is part of his record.
- KISSmetrics later pivoted/was sold; the brand was acquired and folded into other analytics
  tooling (~2018). 📰 (aggregator/Crunchbase-level) — see [[kissmetrics]].

## 9. Ubersuggest (acquired 2017–)

  party). **Neil Patel acquired it on 2017-02-13 for $120,000.** At acquisition it had ~**38,000
  backlinks, ~8,400 referring domains, ~110,000 monthly visitors.** ✅/📰
  (https://startupsnofilter.com/ubersuggest-acquired-neil-patel/;
  https://neilpatel.com/blog/ubersuggest-update/ — his own write-up 🗣️)

  https://carminemastropierro.com/ubersuggest-4/) — see [[ubersuggest]].

## 14. Controversies

- **FTX $55M clawback lawsuit (Nov 2024–).** ✅ court-documented.
  > ⚠️ CONTROVERSY: FTX's bankruptcy estate sued Neil Patel and his entities in the **U.S.
  > Bankruptcy Court, District of Delaware (Nov 2024)**, seeking to claw back **~$30.8M** paid
  > **Oct 2021–Oct 2022** to three Patel-controlled entities — **"I'm Kind of a Big Deal LLC"
  > (~$14.8M)** and **Neil Patel Digital LLC (~$16M+)** among them — headlined as a **"$55M"**
  > suit. FTX alleged **duplicative, exaggerated, undelivered** marketing services ("sooo
  > sloppy," "terrible performance") and that NP Digital charged FTX **~12×** its normal rate
  > ($200k–$500k/yr for comparable clients). ✅
  > (https://www.seroundtable.com/ftx-sues-neil-patel-for-55-million-39539.html;
  > https://ppc.land/ftx-debtors-sue-marketing-executive-for-millions-in-clawback-lawsuit/;
  > https://restructuring.ra.kroll.com/FTX/ — Kroll docket)
  > **Patel's response:** "We aren't being sued for $55 million," noting FTX "sued 70-plus other
  > companies the same week … to try and claw back money" (i.e., a standard bankruptcy
  > preference/clawback action against many vendors, not a fraud finding against him). 🗣️
  Status/outcome not resolved in this pass — a clawback claim is not a finding of wrongdoing.
- **KISSmetrics supercookie settlement (2011–12)** — see §6. ✅
- **"Bait-and-upsell" / agency-quality criticism.** Recurring third-party criticism (Reddit,
  review sites, a 2024 **BBB complaint** alleging a $15k SEO campaign with no reports/results
  and a refused refund) frames his **free tools (Ubersuggest, blog) as lead-bait** into
  high-priced NP Digital retainers ($5k–$20k+/mo). 📰-ish (diffuse; single complaints, not
  organized investigative press). (https://www.bbb.org/us/nv/las-vegas/profile/seo-services/neil-patel-1086-90046982/complaints)
  > LOW confidence on scope — these are scattered complaints and opinion pieces, not a landmark
  > exposé. Do not overstate.
- **Reputation-management / "parasite SEO" criticism.** Commentators (e.g. a 2025 Medium/ILLUMINATION
  piece; a Search Engine Journal note) allege he **buries negative press** by flooding SERPs with
  his own content so "Neil Patel scam" queries surface his own posts. 📰-ish (opinion/analysis).
  Fitting, given he is an SEO who literally teaches this — but treat as **commentary, not
  established fact.**
