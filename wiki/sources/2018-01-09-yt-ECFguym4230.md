# 7 Proven Ways to Grow eCommerce Sales By 50% or More | Increase eCommerce Sales

## Summary
A tight, tactical solo video: Neil gives seven ways to lift e-commerce sales by 50%+.
(1) **Focus on one product** that solves a real pain point — you don't need Amazon-scale
catalogs; his example is a woman selling insoles that make designer high heels comfortable,
"well into the seven figures per year" on a single product (Oberlo/Shopify to dodge
logistics). (2) **Upsell and downsell** — it's easier to get an existing buyer to spend
more than to acquire a new one; a pack-of-three raises lifetime value. (3) **Advertise** —
Facebook ads for **discovery/unaware demand** (target interests) vs Google AdWords for
**active search intent**. (4) **Leverage subscriptions** — recurring products (makeup,
razors); Dollar Shave Club "sold for a billion plus" on razor subscriptions; use ReCharge
on Shopify. (5) **Recover abandoned carts** — "roughly 75.6%" of carts were abandoned in
2017; capture name/email early and email non-completers; exit-intent offers via Hello Bar.
(6) **Optimize checkout** — cites a Baymard study, remove unnecessary fields, and use a
**two-step checkout** (email/account first, then card/shipping) for ~10% more sales.
(7) **Referral program** — via ReferralCandy, and crucially **incentivize both** the
referrer and the referee (Uber's model). A concentrated CRO/e-commerce playbook; several
tactics (Hello Bar exit-intent, two-step forms, LTV-via-upsell) echo Neil's broader CRO
themes.

## Key claims
As of 2018-01-09, Neil states:
- **Focus on one product** that solves a real pain point; you don't need a huge catalog.
  Example: a single-product insole business "well into the seven figures per year." Use
  Oberlo on Shopify to remove logistics friction (as of 2018-01-09).
- A product that's "cool" but solves no pain point can produce a **50% *decrease***, not
  increase (as of 2018-01-09).
- **Upsell/downsell** to raise lifetime value — "it's easier to get someone who's bought
  from you once to spend more money … than it is to get new people to buy" (e.g. pack of
  three vs one) (as of 2018-01-09).
- **Channel fit**: Facebook advertising is best when buyers **don't yet know** the
  product (target interest, e.g. beauty); **Google AdWords** captures people **already
  searching** (e.g. "makeup brush") (as of 2018-01-09).
- **Subscriptions** drive recurring revenue for consumable products; "why do you think
  Dollar Shave Club sold for a billion plus dollars — razor subscription"; use ReCharge
  on Shopify (as of 2018-01-09).
- **~75.6% of shopping carts were abandoned in 2017**; collect name/email before the card
  step and email non-completers; use exit-intent offers (Hello Bar) with a discount/free
  shipping (as of 2018-01-09).
- **Checkout optimization**: cites Baymard research that optimized checkout pages recovered
  "an extra billion … dollars" across companies; remove unnecessary/duplicate fields; make
  checkout **two-step** (email/account first, remaining fields second) to lift sales
  ~10% (as of 2018-01-09).
- **Referral programs** (via ReferralCandy) must **reward both sides** — the referrer and
  the person accepting the referral — "look at Uber … both parties … get compensated,"
  or it won't work (as of 2018-01-09).
