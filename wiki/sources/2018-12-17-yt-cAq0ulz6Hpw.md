# Is Growth Hacking Dead? | Neil Patel

## Summary

A monologue arguing that growth hacking isn't dead — it has just changed, the same way SEO-only and day-one-profitable paid ads no longer work as they once did. Old distribution hacks (Facebook's address-book invite system, Dropbox's refer-for-space) are played out; the mechanics still work but not as well, so you must get more creative. Neil gives five points: (1) growth hacking still works but you can't reuse the old school tactics everyone already used; (2) people must genuinely love your product — measure with **NPS**, aim for 60+, because referral hacks only work on a product people love; (3) get more creative than ever — his flagship example is a **video remarketing** play targeting cart-abandoners and half-finished leads with a video explaining what happens after they buy/convert, run on YouTube and Facebook, which converts far better than "basic" remarketing; (4) look for new, unsaturated channels early (Reddit ads, Quora ads) — early adopters outperform bandwagon-jumpers; and (5) **partnerships** — find non-competitors who share your audience, make them affiliates on a CPA/ad/promo deal, or cross-promote between two popular sites in your space (his example: he sells consulting, a partner sells a tool, and each pitches the other's offer to their signups).

## Key claims

- As of 2018-12-17, Neil said growth hacking is not dead — it has changed; what used to work doesn't work anymore, but growth hacking still works if you drop the played-out old-school tactics.
- As of 2018-12-17, Neil said old distribution hacks (Facebook's address-book invite system, Dropbox's refer-a-friend-for-space) no longer work as well because they're played out — you have to get more creative.
- As of 2018-12-17, Neil said people must love your product for referral/growth hacks to work; measure sentiment with Net Promoter Score (NPS) and aim for 60+, since asking someone who hates your product to share it won't work.
- As of 2018-12-17, Neil shared an underused growth tactic: for visitors who add to cart or start a lead form but don't complete, create a video showing what buying/converting is like and what the next steps are, then run it as a YouTube + Facebook remarketing campaign targeting only those people — it converts far better than basic remarketing.
- As of 2018-12-17, Neil said to look for new, unsaturated channels early (he cited Reddit ads and Quora ads as underused), because early adopters do better than those who wait and jump on the bandwagon.
- As of 2018-12-17, Neil said partnerships are a growth lever: find non-competitors who share your audience, make them affiliates on a CPA/advertising/promotion deal per customer, or cross-promote between two popular sites (e.g. he sells consulting and a partner sells a tool, and each pitches the other's offer to their own signups).
