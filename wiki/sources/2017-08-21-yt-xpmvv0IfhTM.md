# 4 Ways to Build Links for Local Businesses to Boost Your SEO Ranking

## Summary
A short local-SEO video in which Neil Patel gives four tactics for building the localized
links that local businesses need to rank (framing link building as one of the most
important ranking factors — more links, higher rankings). (1) Use Moz Local to find
localized directories worldwide, including local sections of national/global directories
like Yelp for your city. (2) Get a link from your local chamber of commerce — most US
cities have one that promotes area businesses and will usually say yes when asked. (3)
Network and cross-promote with non-competing local businesses (his example: a nail salon
and a hair stylist link to each other because their customers overlap). (4) Put competitor
URLs into Ahrefs (caption-garbled as "hrefs") to see who links to them, then reach out —
call them, network — and pitch your similar-but-differentiated local business as also
worth linking to. He signs off with a sample outreach script.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-21, Neil states:
- Link building is one of the most important factors for search rankings — the more links
  you have, the higher you rank — but local businesses specifically need more localized
  links (as of 2017-08-21).
- Tactic 1 — Use Moz Local, which lists localized directories worldwide, to get more local
  links; national/global directories (even Yelp) have localized city sections (e.g. Los
  Angeles) you can appear in (as of 2017-08-21).
- Tactic 2 — Ask your local chamber of commerce for a link; most US cities have one that
  promotes area businesses, and since you pay taxes in the city they'll usually say yes —
  no harm in asking (as of 2017-08-21).
- Tactic 3 — Network and cross-promote with non-competing local businesses and link to
  each other; his example is a nail salon and a hair-styling business, whose customers
  overlap (as of 2017-08-21).
- Tactic 4 — Enter competitor URLs into Ahrefs to see who links to them, then contact
  those sites (call them, get them on the phone, network) and pitch your similar local
  business — noting extra services the competitor doesn't offer — as also worth a link (as
  of 2017-08-21).
