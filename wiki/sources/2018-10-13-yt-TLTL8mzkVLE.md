# How To Undercut Your Competitors Price (And Still Make Money)

## Summary

Solo tactical/strategy. Neil's thesis: **undercutting on price is one of the easiest, highest-
leverage ways to outgrow competitors** — "pricing has such a huge impact on your business's
growth rate." His extreme example is **Ubersuggest**: he took competitors' paid features and
released them **for free**; despite downtime from high usage, users were so appreciative that
adoption "exploded" — they'd rather use it than pay $50–100/mo elsewhere. Counter-example on
the danger of relying on price protection: a **gun-safe company** with an ~**85% markup**
protected by a patent went from **>$100M/year in revenue to ~$5M** once the patent expired
and competitors shipped similar products at much lower prices — because **even a slightly-
worse product at a drastically lower price wins** most buyers. The ideal is **equal-or-greater
value at a lower price**, but simply undercutting is often enough to take market share. He
notes big corporations take it to the extreme as a **loss-leader**: undercut so aggressively
they lose money per transaction until competitors die off, then raise prices once they control
the market. Key caveat: **luxury/high-end goods are the exception** — undercutting diamonds by
3× signals *lower quality* and can backfire; for non-luxury goods, cutting price is the
easiest way to overtake competitors.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-10-13, **price undercutting is one of the easiest ways to grow** and take market
  share; pricing strongly affects growth rate.
- As of 2018-10-13, **Ubersuggest** freeing competitors' paid features drove explosive
  adoption despite downtime — buyers preferred free over paying $50–100/mo.
- As of 2018-10-13, a **gun-safe company** fell from **>$100M to ~$5M/yr** when its patent
  (~85% markup) expired and cheaper near-substitutes appeared — a **slightly-worse, much-
  cheaper product wins** most buyers.
- As of 2018-10-13, the ideal is **equal/greater value at a lower price**; corporations use
  **loss-leader** undercutting to kill competitors, then raise prices once dominant.
- As of 2018-10-13, **luxury/high-end goods are the exception** — deep undercutting signals
  low quality (e.g. diamonds) and can hurt you.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "one of the best strategies to beat your competition is… to undercut their price. I kid you not, pricing has such a huge impact on your business's growth rate."

> "even if the product isn't as good but it's close and the price point is drastically less, people will be okay just because the cost is so much lower."

> "if you can take the philosophy of having a product that's equal or greater in value and charging less, you'll definitely win."

> "when it comes to luxury goods, undercutting people with pricing sometimes can hurt you."
