# How to Instantly Find Dozens of Content Ideas (That Your Audience Will LOVE)

## Summary
Short, single-workflow tool tutorial answering the step after keyword research: given a
keyword, what content should you actually create? His principle is to let the market decide
retroactively — look at what people are **already sharing and linking to**, because shares
prove interest and links prove perceived value, so produce more of that and less of what
nobody links to. The workflow, entirely inside **Ubersuggest** (his own tool, which he says
he built to make his own life easier): enter a keyword of at least two words (not "dog" or
"marketing", but not too specific either), open **Content Ideas** under Keyword Overview,
and filter the popular articles by a **three-signal validation rule** — thousands of social
shares **and** at least 100 estimated visits **and** at least five backlinks. His reasoning
is that any one signal can mislead (social shares can be manipulated) but the combination
proves people like it, Google ranks it, and others find it valuable enough to cite. He then
covers the harder problem — generating enough keywords to keep feeding ideation — via the
**Keyword Ideas** report's questions, prepositions and comparisons tabs, singling out
**comparison keywords** ("ConvertKit versus Mailchimp") as high buying intent: the searcher
has decided to buy and is only choosing a solution, so those terms maximize revenue, leads
and sales. Finally, the **Top Pages** report on a competitor URL surfaces the pages driving
their traffic (mostly content pages); replicate those clearing >100 visits, >5 backlinks and
ideally thousands of shares — and, inversely, page deeper into the report to spot the "duds"
so you know what to avoid. Closes with an agency pitch.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil framed the problem as post-keyword-research: pick the wrong content type for a keyword and nobody reads, links or shares it, so it won't rank on Google.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil's ideation principle was to mine what people already share and link to — sharing proves ongoing demand, linking proves the content was found valuable — and to produce less of what earns no links.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil said he **developed Ubersuggest's content-ideas workflow to make his own life easier** and released it so it does the same for others.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil advised entering a keyword of **at least two words** — not single generic words like "dog" or "marketing" — while still keeping it somewhat generic rather than hyper-specific.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil's validation threshold for a content idea was all three signals together: **thousands of social shares + at least 100 estimated visits + at least five backlinks**.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil justified the three-signal rule by noting **social shares alone can be manipulated**, whereas estimated visits prove Google ranks it and backlinks prove others found it valuable.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil said the hard part is not finding ideas but continually generating keywords to feed the ideation reports.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil pointed at the Keyword Ideas report's **questions, prepositions and comparisons** tabs as sources of blog topics and of more intent-driven keywords.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil singled out **comparison keywords** (his example: "ConvertKit versus Mailchimp") as signalling someone who is ready to buy and only choosing between solutions — so targeting them maximizes revenue, leads and sales.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil said the **Top Pages** report on a competitor URL reveals the pages actually driving their traffic, and that these are usually content pages.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil's replication rule for competitor pages was >100 estimated visits and >5 backlinks, ideally with thousands of social shares — but if shares are missing, prioritize the pages with the highest visit counts (ideally in the thousands) and more backlinks.
- As of 2020-01-09, Neil said to page deeper into a competitor's top-pages report specifically to identify their **duds** — the unpopular pages — and avoid those topics.
