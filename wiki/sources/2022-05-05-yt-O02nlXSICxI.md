# 5 Marketing SKILLS that are HARD to learn but will pay off FOREVER!

## Summary
Neil frames five hard-to-learn but durable marketing skills, opening with a biographical
note: early in his career he says he only knew two skills well — writing and driving
organic (Google) traffic — and relied on others for everything else, later improving
other skills over time by working alongside people who were better at them
(as of 2022-05-05). (1) Copywriting: always valuable; AI tools can write copy but aren't
as good as a skilled human; he cites Ogilvy's line that "80 cents of the dollar is spent
on the headline" and says skilled copywriters have doubled or tripled conversions on his
own campaigns; recommends Ogilvy's books to learn it. (2) Analytical skills: interpreting
marketing data in an actionable, results-oriented way is rare — most people look at a
Google Analytics report and guess at actions without verifying whether those actions
actually move conversions/sales/revenue. (3) Storytelling: an ancient technique; good
stories have emotional highs and lows ("like a rollercoaster") that hook audiences; he
says he personally learned it by practicing telling true stories about his own past to
friends and family and observing which parts caused people to react, then emphasizing
those parts. (4) Funnels: as ad costs keep rising (he cites Google Ads costs rising
through COVID and even through the 2008 recession), you need upsells and downsells to
extract more value per customer; mentions ClickFunnels as a tool. (5) Good UX (user
experience, not visual design): understanding what a searcher actually wants when they
land on a page and delivering it as clearly and quickly as possible — otherwise rankings
suffer regardless of backlinks or word count.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> As Ogilvy said, "80 cents of the dollar, is spent on the headline." I've seen skilled copywriters, turn some of my campaigns around, realizing double, even triple the conversions and boosting my profits.

> Everyone looks at Google analytics reports and like, "oh yeah, we should do X, Y, and Z." But does really doing X, Y and Z help improve the conversions, the sales, the revenue?

> Good stories, have the ups, the downs, the scary moments, the happy moments. That's how you hook people in... I'm talking about stuff you did in the past. Because you can see what parts of your story cause people to engage and react.

> If you look at what happened during COVID, Google Ads skyrocketed cost. If you look at what happened in the recession in 2008 in America, Google ads still kept going up.

> When someone searches on Google and lands on your page, what are they looking for? Are you giving that to the them? If not, you're going to struggle. Your rankings are going to drop no matter how many backlinks you build or how many words you write in your text.
