# Breaking Down Content Creation For A New Website

## Summary
Solo consulting-style interview: the host runs "adclients.com," a coaching-education business
(YouTube ads, webinars, high-ticket coaching for coaches) doing roughly $500K/month via paid
advertising, with effectively no prior SEO investment, and is picking Neil's brain on how to start.
Content-length guidance: article length should match user intent, not a fixed word count (a
"how to tie a tie" piece needs far less than a from-scratch engine-build guide). On AI-generated
content: usable as a starting point but needs more than a "10%" human modification pass, because
E-E-A-T-quality content isn't something ChatGPT alone can produce, and this is a limitation Neil
expects to persist for a long time (concedes he can't predict exactly how model architecture would
need to change to output truly unique content, joking that's "a better question for Sam Altman or
Elon Musk"). On AI in his own business: primarily analytics — analyzing ad spend for wastage in
real time via tools like Google Data Studio/Tableau, implemented by engineers rather than himself;
also names Midjourney as a useful standalone AI tool for images. On niche strategy: recommends
starting in a tight mini-niche (e.g. "YouTube ads for coaches" rather than "YouTube ads" broadly)
to win traction fast, then expanding outward into the broader niche and eventually the broad market
— cites keyword examples like "how to run ads for coaching" / "how to sell coaching online." On
domain/brand strategy: firmly recommends reusing the existing, already-known "adclients.com" domain
rather than starting fresh or buying a new one, even for content aimed at a future standalone sale
— "why build a brand from scratch when you already have one." Debunks a WordPress-ranking myth
explicitly: platform choice (WordPress vs. any other CMS) has no inherent SEO advantage; what
matters is site optimization, load time, and content quality, full stop. Repeats the Wikipedia
content-freshness example (three-year-old content is like a three-year-old apple — nobody wants it)
as the reason continual updates outperform static "authority" from age. Sets timeline expectations:
early traffic increases within a few months, but the real ramp starts after roughly a year, with
compounding growth typically over years one to three; recommends benchmarking early progress via
Google Search Console impressions rather than expecting fast wins.

## Key claims
- Recommends matching content length to the informational need of the topic rather than a fixed
  word-count target (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-07-27).
- States AI (ChatGPT-class) content requires substantially more than a 10% human-editing pass to
  meet E-E-A-T quality bars, and expects this human-modification requirement to persist for a long
  time (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-07-27) — consistent with earlier E-E-A-T material logged
  elsewhere in the corpus.
- Names real-time ad-spend analytics (via Data Studio/Tableau, engineer-implemented) as the primary
  AI application in his own business, plus Midjourney for image generation as a standalone tool he
  personally finds useful (self-reported, dated 2023-07-27).
- Advocates starting SEO/content efforts in a tight mini-niche before expanding to the broader
  market and then the broad niche, illustrated with a "YouTube ads for coaches" example
  (self-reported framework, dated 2023-07-27).
- States platform choice (e.g. WordPress vs. any other CMS/site builder) has no inherent SEO
  advantage — what matters is technical optimization, load time, and content quality — explicitly
  calling the "WordPress ranks better" belief "a misconception" (self-reported opinion, dated
  2023-07-27).
- Sets a timeline expectation: some traffic increase within the first few months, with the real
  growth ramp beginning after about a year and compounding through roughly years one to three
  (self-reported estimate, dated 2023-07-27) — consistent with the ~1-year SEO payoff timeline
  logged elsewhere in the corpus (see [[2023-07-31-yt-iFynPlXb7k8]]).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Don't think about them. Do whatever's best for the user. It's like everyone's like, oh, I need a
> 5,000 word article... if your article is on how to build a car motor engine from scratch, I don't
> think people want a thousand words.

> You can use the AI but you may have to modify it way more than 10%... your content needs to have
> the double E, A, T. You're not going to get that from ChatGPT.

> People overestimate what AI can do for them at least in the short run. They believe it's an end
> all be all and they can do everything. I also think people underestimate what it can do for you in
> the long run.

> No, that's a misconception... No authority on the internet is how many people know your brand,
> talk about it, linked to it, et cetera. WordPress is just a platform.

> The reason Wikipedia ranks for almost everything is they update content. It's like, if I give you
> an Apple that's three years old, would you want to eat it?

> If it was that easy everyone would do it. That's why you guys spend probably millions of dollars a
> year on ads, it's easier. Doesn't mean ads are bad. It just means why not do both.
