# Why Some SEO Gurus Have Been LYING To Your Face This Whole Time

## Summary
Argues that tactical SEO advice (including his own) only takes a business so far; the
biggest SEO winners dominate because of product/brand strength, not tactics alone.
Three-part doctrine: (1) build an amazing, habit-forming product/service — cites Larry
Page's "billion-dollar business if people use your product multiple times a day" line;
(2) build a recognizable brand, illustrated with a "shoes" vs. "Nike" search-volume
comparison (Nike gets several million monthly searches vs. ~1M+ for the generic term
"shoes"), because Google favors brand queries as a trust/anti-spam signal; (3) keep
experimenting, since what works today may stop working — retells the sign-in-with-Google
case study (previously logged) where forcing Google-only login boosted conversions
initially, then reversed as consumer privacy concerns grew, resolved by offering both
login options. Recommends an omnichannel approach (SEO, content, paid, even TV ads via
NP Digital) for brand-building.
