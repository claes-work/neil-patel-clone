A seven-trend 2021 content-marketing predictions video, opening on the over-a-billion-
blogs saturation problem. Trend 1: you can't rely on SEO or social alone anymore — push
notifications (via a tool called "Subscribers") are pitched as a high-performing new
promotion channel, with self-reported per-post click counts in the 16k-89k range; also
recommends Messenger bots, email, remarketing, and cross-format repurposing. Trend 2:
translate content — repeats the Google-employee-sourced lesson (already logged) that
most non-English searchers don't search in English, with a self-reported traffic mix
of ~20% US / 80%+ international. Trend 3: spend more time updating over creating — a
specific, previously-logged-elsewhere ratio restated here as 1 new post/week to 21
updates/week. Trend 4: companies will hire for CTR optimization specifically; gives a
concrete "10 hacks" CTR-lift list (question titles, 15-40 character length +8.6%, exact-
match URL keyword +45%, power words +13.9%, emotional titles +7.3%, meta description
present +5.8%, years in title +4.9%, curiosity-gap +5.9%, "education"-styled titles
+10.4%) plus the Rand Fishkin click-manipulation SEO experiment (clicking result 4
instead of 1 moved it to position 1). Trend 5: YouTube/video SEO as a distinct
discipline — promote hard in the first 24 hours, use Ubersuggest for
titles/descriptions/tags, and use "the Hot Script by Sunny" as a hook technique. Trend
6: more CRO investment specifically for content (product mentions in-content, sidebar
ads, exit pop-ups). Closing trend: free tools (Ubersuggest) beat content marketing on
cost-effectiveness — self-reports Ubersuggest generating 200,000+ leads/month for his
sales team.
