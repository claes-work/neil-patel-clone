# How to Stop Being Just a Content Writer and Become a Marketer

## Summary
Draws a distinction between a "writer" (paid per word, focused on grammar/clarity/
research) and a "marketer" (focused on buyer pain points, keyword demand, competitive
research, headlines, and conversion), arguing the marketer role commands a premium.
Gives a practical starter checklist: research keywords in Ubersuggest and integrate
related terms; link content to owned products/services to drive revenue; use
Ubersuggest's content-ideas report to find who ranks for target keywords, then find
who links to those competitor pages and pitch them a value-driven outreach email (a
concrete before/after email example is given: don't say "link to me," instead point out
their existing link is outdated and offer an updated resource/infographic as the
replacement). Emphasizes internal linking, headline quality (repeats the 8/10-read vs.
2/10-click ratio), and infographics (via Canva) as ways to convert research into
traffic.

## Key claims
- Explicit writer-vs-marketer distinction: a writer optimizes for volume/grammar; a
  marketer optimizes for buyer-journey pain points, keyword demand, and conversion —
  framed as a way to charge a premium for the same content work (paraphrase, dated
  2021-11-11) — content-marketing, agency-entrepreneurship.
- Gives a concrete, reusable link-outreach email template: lead with the fact the
  recipient's existing outbound link is outdated/superseded, then offer a specific
  replacement resource (e.g. an infographic version) — reframes the pitch as helping
  their audience, not asking for a favor (verbatim example provided, dated 2021-11-11)
  — content-marketing, seo. New concrete script/wording not previously logged verbatim.
- Repeats the 8-out-of-10-read-headlines vs. 2-out-of-10-click-through ratio (already
