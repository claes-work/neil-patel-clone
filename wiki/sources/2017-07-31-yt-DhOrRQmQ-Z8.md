# How to Leverage Influencer Marketing to EXPLODE Your Business

## Summary
Neil teaches influencer marketing using Beats by Dre as the flagship example of a brand built on it. He names three main networks — Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat — and if forced to pick two, Facebook and Instagram (Snapchat underperforms them). His central rule is **relevance over reach**: irrelevant followers won't convert. He confesses his early mistake of chasing anyone with millions of followers, illustrated by a quote from soccer star Neymar — $60,000 to his charity to collaborate — which Neil passed on because Neymar's audience doesn't match his B2B business. He contrasts with FitTea, which works on Instagram because fitness people posting about it are relevant. Second rule: consistency — treat it as branding and run it 6+ months (Beats didn't blow up overnight); some awareness/sales come within a few months, but it really takes off around six months. Third rule: ideally don't pay — trade product/services if you can; if you must pay, negotiate directly (not through agencies, which mark up 2–3×), lock longer-term arrangements to drive rates down, and negotiate cross-posting across all their networks. He recommends nearly every B2C company try it; B2B can too, but relevant/authoritative influencers are harder to find.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-31, Neil states:
- Beats by Dre became a big company because of influencer marketing (as of 2017-07-31).
- Use Facebook, Instagram, and Snapchat for influencer marketing; if picking two, choose Facebook and Instagram — Snapchat isn't doing as well by comparison (as of 2017-07-31).
- **Relevance beats follower count**: if the influencer's fans aren't relevant, it won't work. His own mistake was chasing anyone with millions of followers (as of 2017-07-31).
- Anecdote: he got a quote from Neymar — donate **$60,000** to Neymar's non-profit charity to work with him — but declined because Neymar plays soccer while Neil sells to a B2B audience (as of 2017-07-31; self-reported).
- FitTea does well on Instagram because relevant fitness people post about getting fit / losing weight / more energy from it (as of 2017-07-31).
- Treat influencer marketing as branding: post consistently for 6+ months. Expect some brand awareness and sales within a few months, but it "really starts taking off" around six months (as of 2017-07-31).
- Ideally don't pay — get influencers to post in exchange for products/services. If you must pay, negotiate; don't go through agencies (they mark up by double or triple) — go direct (as of 2017-07-31).
- Negotiate longer-term arrangements (e.g. post once a week for months) to bring rates down substantially, and have them cross-post the same content across all their social networks (little extra effort) (as of 2017-07-31).
- Recommends almost every B2C company try influencer marketing; B2B can too, but it's harder to find influencers and you need authoritative ones (as of 2017-07-31).
