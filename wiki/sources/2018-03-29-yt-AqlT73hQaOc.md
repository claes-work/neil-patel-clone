# #1 Hack to Beat the New Facebook Algorithm Changes (2018)

_Q&A Thursday: Neil with co-host **Adam** (from Viewership). Only Neil-attributed
material is persona data; Adam's contributions are marked below._

## Summary
Neil reacts to Facebook's early-2018 News Feed shift away from links toward
"meaningful conversation": pages that just share links get downgraded, so link posts
reach fewer people. His counter-play is to **raise the page's overall engagement score
first** with conversational, video, and question-based content, and only then mix in
links — which then perform better because the page is "healthy." Adam adds tactics on
eliciting longer comments (ask one level deeper) and Neil stresses replying to every
comment. Neil recommends ~70%+ of Facebook content be conversational/video/question,
and using cheap "text-only question" ad promotions to fans to re-engage a base before
posting links organically.

## Key claims
As of 2018-03-29, Neil states:
- **Facebook is downgrading link-sharing pages** in favor of conversation; pages that
  post links day after day see reach fall even on posts that go viral elsewhere.
- Facebook scores a page on whether **fans engage**; a low-engagement history suppresses
  even great new content ("your users don't like it").
- **Engagement compounds**: posting engaging (esp. video) content daily lifts overall
  page reach, so subsequent **link posts also do better**. When he only shared links,
  reach dropped.
- **Reply to every comment** — Facebook A/B-style testing showed replying yields **~20%+
  more comments** (excluding his own replies); not replying makes a page look "dead."
- When posting a link, don't just drop the link + thumbnail — **write an engaging or
  controversial sentence/paragraph above it**; schedule posts in advance so there's no
  excuse to skip this.
- Keep **≥70% of Facebook content conversational** (questions, video, audio-with-still-
  image for podcast-style listeners); can't share links all the time.
- It's **not about fan count but engaged fans** — a small, highly engaged base wins.
- His other companies **spend ~$5–10K/month on Facebook ads**; a cheap tactic is to
  **promote a text-only question post to existing fans** to spike engagement.

### Adam (co-host, NOT persona data)
- To get long comments, treat online conversations like offline ones — ask **one surface
  level deeper** to get "5–10 minute" thoughtful replies instead of "how's your day → good."
- Strategy of **promoting content only to existing fans** so a higher % re-engages, so
  organic posts then reach more of the base.
