# How to Rank for Thousands of Keywords Without Building Links

## Summary

Neil gives three strategies to rank for many keywords **without link building**.
(1) **"Land and expand"** — pull the pages you already rank for from **Google Search
Console**, see every query each page draws impressions/clicks for, and add the terms
you're ranking for but haven't actually written into the page copy (naturally, not
stuffed). Then take your top head terms, run them through **Ubersuggest** to surface
related long-tail phrases, and fold those into the article too — ranking for a
competitive head term makes the easier long-tails easy to also capture. (2) **Content
clusters** — build a thorough "master"/parent page on a broad topic (e.g. SEO), use
**AnswerThePublic** to find the long-tail questions/comparisons/prepositions around it,
create a page per popular one, and internal-link them from the master page so they all
lift together. (3) **Content gaps** — put a competitor's URL into Ubersuggest, open
**Top Pages**, export the data, isolate the topics they rank for that you don't, write
a better page on each, then email the people who linked to the competitor's article
(Ubersuggest's top-pages report shows them) — not begging for a link, just flagging your
better/newer version for a share. Advanced move: combine them (run land-and-expand on
whichever cluster pages start ranking).

## Key claims

- As of 2019-08-26, Neil's **"land and expand"** tactic: mine Google Search Console for
  queries a page already ranks for, then add those exact terms (plus Ubersuggest
  long-tails) into the page copy to rank higher and wider.
- As of 2019-08-26, Neil said if you rank for terms whose words aren't in your copy,
  that signals Google to rank you lower — adding them naturally raises the page.
- As of 2019-08-26, Neil said ranking for a **head term makes long-tail phrases easy**,
  because head terms are more competitive than long-tail.
- As of 2019-08-26, Neil's **content-cluster** architecture: one thorough master page
  per broad topic, satellite pages for AnswerThePublic long-tails, internal-linked back
  to the master so the whole cluster rises.
- As of 2019-08-26, Neil's **content-gap** tactic: use Ubersuggest Top Pages to find
  competitors' ranking topics you lack, write better pages, and email the competitor's
  existing linkers to earn shares (not link-begging).

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The first strategy is what I like calling the land and expand strategy. In other words, you already have keywords and pages that rank on Google... they rank for something."

> "If you're ranking for some terms and you don't have those words in your website copy, in the text, what do you think that tells Google? Hey, yeah, we should rank them, but maybe not as high. If you add those keywords within your copy, you're much more likely to rank higher."

> "The more you hit up people and ask them to link to you, sure, a portion will link to you, but the majority will not. But instead, I want you to shoot them a simple email... If you like it feel free and share it."
