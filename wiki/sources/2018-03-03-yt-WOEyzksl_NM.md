# Creative Problem Solving | You Can't Always Be In Control (TRUE STORY: OUR MICS DIED)

_Shot on an iPhone after the studio microphones failed; **Adam Lodolce** (viewership.com) is
on camera with Neil. Only Neil-attributed material is persona data._

## Summary
A meta-example filmed after the studio mics died: Neil argues that in business **you can't
control everything** (revenue, sales, marketing, team members, recessions), and the winning
response is **creativity + action** rather than worry. The video itself is the demo — mics
failed, so they **grabbed an iPhone and kept recording**. He gives business analogues:
**lose a big customer** → cut costs, or get creative by going to **LinkedIn**, finding that
customer's **competitors**, and pitching them with the results you produced. On **brand
reputation**, he uses Adam's **controversial video** (annoyed ~90% of Adam's audience, drew
haters, but earned **2M+ views** and sparked conversation) to make the point that you'll take
hits, but **people forgive and forget** — learn from mistakes and keep moving forward.
Production takeaway: **you don't need a fancy studio** — **Tai Lopez** shoots many videos on
his iPhone and gets millions of views — so just take out your phone and start.

## Key claims
As of 2018-03-03, Neil states:
- **You can't control everything in business** — revenue, sales, marketing, team members, or
  macro conditions (a possible recession). That's normal.
- **Respond with creativity and action, not worry**: "there's always another option," you
  just have to think outside the box and take action. (Live demo: mics died → shot on iPhone.)
- **Losing a big customer** — creative response: cut costs, and/or go to **LinkedIn**, find the
  lost customer's **competitors**, show them your results, and try to close them.
- **Brand hits are survivable**: what you do in the past affects the future, but **people
  forgive and forget** — learn from mistakes and push forward (he says he's taken hits on his
  own brand). Adam's controversial video annoyed ~90% of his audience yet earned **2M+ views**
  and created conversation.
- **You don't need a studio or money to make video** — **Tai Lopez** shoots on his iPhone and
  gets millions of views; just take out your phone and start.
