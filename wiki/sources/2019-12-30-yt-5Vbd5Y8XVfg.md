# How Digital Marketing Will Change in 2020 (You're Not Going to Like It)

## Summary
Solo trend-forecast video for **2020** (the YouTube title was later re-dated to "2023" —
the captions say 2020 throughout). Seven predicted shifts plus a countermeasure for each.
(1) **Video reach drops** because everyone now shoots one video and slices it for LinkedIn,
Instagram, Facebook and YouTube; the answer is to *multicast* deliberately — make one video
that works on all platforms — and to **go live at least once a month**, because platforms
chasing television (Facebook holds contracts with sports networks, as do "the YouTubes of
the world") push live video to all your subscribers, and the extra engagement also lifts
your later recorded uploads. (2) **Bite-sized educational, interactive content is rising
fast** — the long infographic is being displaced by swipeable image sets; he points at his
own Instagram, where each swipe card gives a one-sentence tactic ("how to get more traffic
on YouTube or Instagram"), and claims these earn roughly 50% shares relative to likes
(2,000 likes → another 1,000 shares), telling viewers outright to copy it. (3) **Mobile
search traffic will fall** as snippets proliferate: he cites **Merkle** (organic visits −6%
YoY in Q2 2019; mobile visitor growth down from 13% in Q1 2019 to 5% in Q2 2019) and opens
with a **Comscore** claim that half of 2020 searches will be voice — the countermeasure is
structured markup to win rich snippets and therefore voice traffic. (4) **Google keeps
consolidating markets**, going head-to-head with Amazon via the Buy-on-Google button and
on-Google lead generation (his example: mortgage leads) — framed as an opportunity, not a
threat, given **Jumpshot**'s 49%-of-searches-end-in-no-click figure. (5) **Influencer
budgets will shrink** because engagement is near all-time lows per **InfluencerDB**; his
response is to pay each influencer less and shift to *quantity* of influencers. (6) **Stories
get harder to win on** — everyone posts them and people unfollow stories they don't care
about — so use Instagram **Close Friends**, enticing people in with exclusive content to
recover engagement. (7) **Shoppable experiences expand** — Buy on Google, Pinterest Shop-the-
Look pins, and eventually VR shopping with friends in a virtual mall — adapt or get passed by.
Closes with an agency pitch (Neil Patel Digital).

## Key claims
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil cited **Comscore** for the claim that half of all searches in 2020 would be voice search, and said Facebook, Instagram and LinkedIn reach were all declining.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil predicted **video reach will drop dramatically** because everyone shoots one video and cuts it into bite-size pieces for LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook and YouTube — video's effectiveness is exactly why it is saturating.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil's countermeasure to video saturation was **multicasting**: create one video that works for all platforms rather than platform-specific shoots.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil said platforms push live video to all of your subscribers because they want to compete with television — noting Facebook has contracts with sports networks, as do "the YouTubes of the world."
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil claimed that going live at least once a month makes your **recorded, non-live** videos get "more love and more spread" afterwards.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil said bite-sized educational/interactive content is displacing the long scrolling infographic, and pointed to his own Instagram swipe-through image sets — one sentence of tactic per image — as the format that works.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil claimed each of those Instagram bite-size educational images gets roughly **50% shares** relative to likes — 2,000 likes producing another 1,000 shares — and told viewers to copy the format.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil cited **Merkle**: total visits from organic search fell **6% year-over-year in Q2 2019**, with mobile hit hardest — visitor growth dropping from **13% in Q1 2019 to 5% in Q2 2019**.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil's fix for shrinking mobile organic traffic was **structured markup → rich snippets → voice-search traffic**, since voice traffic is increasing.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil said Google is consolidating markets and going head-to-head with **Amazon** via the "Buy Now on Google" button, letting people purchase without visiting the e-commerce site — and that Google will keep testing ways to keep people on Google.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil argued this is an opportunity rather than a threat: you can generate leads directly on Google without sending people to your site (his example: the mortgage industry) and sell products on Google, increasing revenue.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil cited **Jumpshot**: 49% of all Google searches result in **no click** — so failing to adapt costs sales and leads.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil predicted **influencer-marketing budgets will decrease**, citing **InfluencerDB** that influencer engagement rates were nearing all-time lows; less engagement means less ROI means influencers can't charge as much.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil's influencer countermeasure was to pay individual influencers less and go after **quantity** — more influencers — to keep generating sales.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil predicted social-media **stories** would get less popular and harder to win on (people unfollow stories they don't engage with), and recommended Instagram **Close Friends** with exclusive content as the entice-and-engage workaround.
- As of 2019-12-30, Neil predicted **shoppable experiences** would keep expanding — Buy on Google, Pinterest Shop-the-Look pins, and eventually virtual-reality group shopping in a mall/store setting — and warned that dismissing VR means being passed by.
