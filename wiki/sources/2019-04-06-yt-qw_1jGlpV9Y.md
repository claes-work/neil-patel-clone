# 7 social media predictions that will happen by the end of 2019

## Summary

Neil's seven 2019 social-media predictions: (1) all networks will **prefer video** to take share
from TV — start filming on your phone; (2) **brands gain weight** as platforms (and Google) fight
fake news, favoring branded sources — build brand via omnichannel/offline events and speaking;
(3) **likes and shares matter less; comments matter more** — in-depth comments especially, so
prompt for them; (4) **advertising gets tougher** as costs rise and big-brand dollars (e.g.
Coca-Cola brand campaigns) crowd out affiliate ROI ad buyers; (5) **algorithms converge** and
networks make it harder to send traffic off-platform (keeping users on-site) — you'll have to
pay for that traffic; (6) **users consolidate onto fewer networks** (e.g. Snapchat down,
Instagram up), so tailor content per network rather than regurgitating; (7) **few newcomers
survive** because incumbents copy their features and crush them.

> Note: these are time-bound 2019 predictions — record as dated forecasts, not standing beliefs.
> Several (video-first, brand signals, comments-as-engagement, per-network tailoring, pay-to-reach)
> echo his durable positions; treat the specific "by end of 2019" framing as of-its-moment.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-04-06, Neil predicted every network would prioritize **video** in 2019 to compete
  with TV (citing LinkedIn Live, YouTube Live, Facebook Live, Facebook chasing NFL broadcasts),
  and that phone-shot video is enough.
- As of 2019-04-06, Neil predicted **brands would carry more weight** because platforms and Google
  are fighting fake news and trust branded sources; build brand via an omnichannel approach
  (offline events, conferences, speaking).
- As of 2019-04-06, Neil predicted engagement would shift from **likes/shares to comments** —
  especially in-depth comments — so creators must actively prompt for them; worth "double, triple"
  the views.
- As of 2019-04-06, Neil predicted **advertising gets tougher**: costs rise and big-brand
  ("branded") ad dollars (e.g. Coca-Cola optimizing for likes on its polar bear) outspend and
  squeeze out ROI-focused affiliate advertisers.
- As of 2019-04-06, Neil predicted social platforms would adopt **similar algorithms** and make
  off-platform traffic harder, to keep users (and ad revenue) on-site — so you'll pay ads to send
  traffic to your own site.
- As of 2019-04-06, Neil predicted **user consolidation** onto favored networks (Snapchat losing
  to Instagram), requiring per-network campaigns since content isn't portable across platforms.
- As of 2019-04-06, Neil predicted **few newcomer networks** would survive, as incumbents copy
  their features and crush them.
