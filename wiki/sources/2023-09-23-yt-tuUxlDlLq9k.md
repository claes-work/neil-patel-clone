# How to Make Your Content Stand Out When Everyone is Using AI

## Summary
Marketing School (co-hosted) episode on content differentiation in an AI-saturated
landscape. Neil's core argument: shared data/studies get replicated quickly by
competitors and, increasingly, by AI itself, but personal, first-hand experience is hard
for AI to fabricate convincingly — he illustrates with an anecdote about a friend's
detailed personal cancer-journey content that only felt authentic because she had
actually lived it. He also argues creators should target topics that haven't been
"beaten to death" (using Google's then-new Bard rollout as an example of a fresh,
timely angle) and should integrate influencer/personality partnerships (from major names
like Tucker Carlson, Tony Robbins, Gary Vaynerchuk, LeBron James down to accessible
micro-influencers) to stand out from generic, AI-assisted competitors.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Shared data-driven content loses differentiation quickly because competitors (and
  increasingly AI) replicate the same studies/data; personal experience-based content is
  harder for AI to replicate and therefore more durable as a differentiator. (Neil,
  2023-09-23) — content-marketing / ai-marketing.
- Illustrates the personal-experience point with an anecdote about a friend who wrote
  candidly about her cancer journey — Neil says the level of detail was only possible
  because she had actually been through it, and that this kind of first-hand detail is
  what makes content stand out. (Neil, 2023-09-23) — content-marketing.
- Recommends targeting topics that haven't been over-covered; cites the (then-recent)
  public rollout of Google Bard and SEO adaptation to AI-driven search as a timely,
  under-covered angle he was pursuing with his own data. (Neil, 2023-09-23) —
  content-marketing / ai-marketing / seo.
- Recommends integrating influencers/personalities (from A-list figures like Tucker
  Carlson, Tony Robbins, Gary Vaynerchuk, or LeBron James down to accessible
  micro-influencers) into content/brand strategy as a differentiation lever, framing this
  as "personal brand," not just corporate brand. (Neil, 2023-09-23) — content-marketing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
