# The Most On Demand Digital Marketing Skills in 2019 (High-Income Skills to Master)

## Summary

Neil lists **seven digital-marketing skills to master in 2019** (the on-screen title's "2023"
is a later re-title; Neil says "2019" throughout). (1) **Data science / analytics** — you don't
need to be a data scientist, but you must read **Google Analytics** beyond visit counts:
sources, conversion rate, ROI, funnel drop-off. (2) **Content strategy** — writing no longer
guarantees traffic ("one blog for every seven people"); he reiterates his **80/20 split — 20%
writing, 80% promoting**. (3) **Video** — go live and publish evergreen video across YouTube /
LinkedIn / Facebook; it builds a more engaged community than almost any channel. (4)
**Storytelling** — underused, still as effective as ever; it lifts conversion as ad costs rise.
(5) **Email marketing / marketing automation** — not blast emails but behavior-based flows
(engaged users get sell emails, unengaged get nurture first). (6) **Funnels** — tools like
**ClickFunnels**; learn upsells/downsells or you won't be able to afford rising paid-ad costs.
(7) **Podcasting** — growing fast, listeners are engaged and higher-income; get in now because
it will be far more competitive in 3–4 years and takes years to pay off.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-06-08, Neil named seven high-value digital-marketing skills for 2019: **data
  science/analytics, content strategy, video, storytelling, email marketing/automation, funnels,
  and podcasting**.
- As of 2019-06-08, Neil reiterated the **80/20 rule**: he spends ~20% of his time writing an
  article and ~80% promoting it, because content is saturated ("one blog for every seven people").
- As of 2019-06-08, Neil said **marketing automation** means behavior-triggered emails —
  sell to the engaged, nurture the unengaged first — not sending everyone the same email.
- As of 2019-06-08, Neil said learning **funnels / upsells & downsells** (e.g. via ClickFunnels)
  is necessary to stay profitable as **ad costs keep rising**.
- As of 2019-06-08, Neil said **podcasting** is growing rapidly with an engaged, higher-income
  audience; start now because it takes years to bear fruit and will be more competitive within
  3–4 years. He noted podcasting had taken off more in Asia (e.g. China) than the U.S. at the time.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "There's over a billion blogs on the web. There's roughly seven billion people, so that means roughly one blog for every seven people. That's too much content."

> "I spend twenty percent of my time writing the article, eighty percent promoting it."

> "You don't want to send everyone the same emails. When someone's super engaged, you want to send them emails that mainly sell products and services. When someone's not engaged… you want to get them engaged first, and then sell them."

> "When you do podcasting, don't expect to get results right away. It'll take years before you see the fruit from your labor."
