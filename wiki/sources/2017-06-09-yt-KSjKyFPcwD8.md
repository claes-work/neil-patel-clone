# 1 Simple Hack to Increase Website Conversions

## Summary
Neil Patel teaches a single conversion hack: adding sound to a website to draw attention to the area you want visitors to focus on. On neilpatel.com/blog, first-time visitors see a bell appear in the bottom-right corner after roughly 20–30 seconds; the bell not only moves but also makes a ringing noise, pulling the visitor's attention to that spot so they click and complete whatever offer is being pitched. He states this is his current number-one converting area on the site, and that it works specifically because of the sound — he tested the same bell/alert without sound and it did not perform well. His reasoning is that almost no one uses sound on websites today (unlike the '90s music-and-sound era), so adding it now stands out and draws attention. He cautions not to put music/sound just anywhere, but only into the areas you want people to focus on. He implemented it as a bell using the free tool hellobar.com (Hello Bar), which lets you create a free alert notification, pick the bell's color, choose what shows when someone clicks it, and place call-to-actions/conversions inside; it only shows on a visitor's first visit so returning visitors aren't repeatedly annoyed by the noise. He frames it as a quick, easy, free win for a blog and claims it accounts for most of his conversions compared to anything else he has tested — noting other elements may collect more emails or visitors, but this converts more visitors into customers than anything else on his blog.
