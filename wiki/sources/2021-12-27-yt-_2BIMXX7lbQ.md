# Why You Should Stop Studying Digital Marketing (What To Do Instead While Getting 10x The Results)

## Summary
Argues people should stop pure studying (6+ months of learning with no implemented
campaigns, or abandoning a channel after one failed attempt) and instead: (1) pick one
marketing discipline to focus on; (2) find a client/product in that niche willing to let
them work free or cheap, favoring high-ticket or high-retention products/services in a
large total-addressable-market (TAM) — explicitly argues capturing 1% of a $100B market
beats 100% of a $1B market, and dismisses "the riches are in the niches" as not how big
companies get built; (3) reverse-engineer successful competitors' offers, pricing,
guarantees, and traffic channels (using Ubersuggest for organic and Facebook's Ad
Library for paid) to inform a minimal-viable-campaign; (4) run that MVP on a single
channel, test offer/ad/segment variations, and double down on the winning angle; (5)
then repeat the same playbook for a second, non-competing client/niche to broaden
experience. Closes with a "learning is great but you must implement" reflection,
crediting his mother (a teacher) with instilling that continuous learning is more
valuable than money, while cautioning that knowledge without implementation misses
intricacies "not taught through reading."
