# My Favorite Places to Eat and Explore

## Summary

A short personal/lifestyle Q&A (an off-camera interviewer, plus his friend "Adam,"
prompt Neil about travel and food). Almost no marketing content — this is biography and
voice material. Neil describes himself repeatedly as **"a creature of habit"**: he eats
at the same handful of restaurants over and over and never tires of it, because
minimizing trivial decisions frees him to focus on what he truly cares about. He names
his regular spots (Hollywood/LA: Genwa Korean BBQ, Spice Affair (Indian), Katana, Red O
(Mexican); Javier's in Orange County; Sushi Samba in Vegas) and notes **he doesn't eat
beef** (relevant when he travels to Brazil, where he stays near an Indian restaurant
called Tulsi in São Paulo for convenience). On travel he prefers **convenient** places —
loves New York, London, most of Europe, and Brazil (says the people there are amazing);
Asia's time zone is hard on work. He frames travel choices around convenience and
optimizing time because constant speaking/travel burns him out ("I'm not a machine, I'm
human"). He explicitly says he's at a stage of life where he wants to **spend more time
with family** — mentions hanging out with his dad, working while the TV's on and his dad
asks him things, walking the park. Closes with his standard sign-off offering free help
to viewers who comment.

## Key claims
