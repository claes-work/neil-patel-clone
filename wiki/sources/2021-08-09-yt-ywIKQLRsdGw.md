# Sometimes the Best SEO Strategy Is To ABANDON Some Keywords

## Summary
Reinforces the intent-over-traffic doctrine using neilpatel.com's own evolution as the
example: early on he chased any high-traffic keyword regardless of buyer intent
(Instagram followers, TikTok — topics unrelated to NP Digital's actual services);
now he says he no longer cares about raw visitor counts (8-10M/month, varying by
season) and instead prioritizes conversion-tracked traffic sources via Google
Analytics goals. Recommends reverse-engineering competitors' paid-ad keyword targeting
(via Ubersuggest) as a signal of commercial intent — sustained ad spend on a keyword
implies it drives revenue. Reiterates partnerships/affiliate distribution as a
non-keyword growth channel, using the standard Nike-Footlocker retail-partnership
analogy. Closes with the Ubersuggest logged-in "keywords I can rank for" filter,
recommending checking direct competitors' (not tangential ones like HubSpot) paid-ad
behavior on a candidate keyword as a go/no-go signal.

## Key claims
- neilpatel.com receives roughly 8-9M monthly visitors, ranging up to 10M and down to 7M
  seasonally (self-reported, dated 2021-08) — seo. Consistent with adjacent sources'
  traffic figures.
- Recommends checking whether a *direct* competitor (not a tangential one) is paying for
  a keyword on Google Ads as a proxy for that keyword's revenue potential before
  targeting it in SEO (paraphrase, dated 2021-08) — seo. Reinforces existing intent-over-
  traffic doctrine with a specific verification method.
- Early neilpatel.com content strategy chased any high-traffic keyword (e.g., Instagram/
  TikTok growth topics) regardless of relevance to NP Digital's actual service lineup —
  a mismatch he says he has since corrected (self-reported, dated 2021-08) — content-
  marketing, agency-entrepreneurship. Consistent with the traffic/product mismatch theme
  in adjacent sources this batch.
