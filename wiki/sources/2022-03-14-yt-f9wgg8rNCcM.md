## Summary
A six-step path to becoming a social media manager, framed with a personal aside that
he himself started out "just posting Instagram images" and learned the rest — driving
traffic, converting it to sales/leads, then retention/upsell/referral — through trial
and error. Cites a We Are Social/Hootsuite figure that 57.6% of the world's population
(~4.55 billion people) is on social media. Steps: (1) get educated primarily by
studying your target audience directly rather than via a formal degree — plugs his own
free "Unlocked" courses (Instagram/LinkedIn/Facebook Unlocked, Facebook's own social-
marketing certificate); (2) get test clients by picking a niche, doing competitive
research, and being willing to work at reduced or no rate initially to build a
foothold; (3) build a results-driven portfolio through aggressive (but ethical) testing
of content formats/topics, with analytics fluency as a core required skill; (4)
establish and document repeatable processes (client onboarding, delivery, reporting,
communications) as volume grows; (5) build a client-acquisition funnel by actively
self-promoting on social media, developing productized offers rather than fully custom
scopes each time; (6) hire and scale with people who have proven multi-time track
records, since first-timers require training investment.
