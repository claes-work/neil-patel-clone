# How to Maximize Your Customer Lifetime Value with Email Marketing - Email Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week Two, Lesson One of **Email Marketing Unlocked**, explicitly referenced in-video as
the start of "week two" (following Week One, Lesson Two:
[[wiki/sources/2021-01-30-yt-S4HcIG1lv2w]]). Frames the lesson around lifetime value
(LTV), citing Amazon as the model of a company maximizing email-driven LTV through
continual upsells/downsells. Walks through an email-type taxonomy by funnel stage:
welcome emails (education, cross-sell awareness), notification/transactional emails
(highest open/click rates of any type — demoed with an Ubersuggest ranking-drop alert
example that ends in a call-to-action to book a sales call), high-value newsletters
(states a personal 98%-educational / 2%-promotional email ratio, sharper than the
80/20 rule he says he "goes above and beyond"), review/feedback emails, cart-
abandonment sequences (states roughly half of people who start checkout never
complete it), purchase-receipt emails, and upsell mechanics (presale upsells,
one-click upsells, bundles, cross-sells — recommends presenting the next offer
immediately post-purchase while intent is highest). Covers list hygiene again (removing
"cold subscribers," a term reused from the previous lesson) and channel consistency
(design/tone/layout should stay recognizable). Closes on mobile-first design (50%+ of
opens are mobile, B2C reaching 70-80%; B2B "creeping up" past 50%) and states a
personal preference for plain text-based emails over image-heavy HTML, citing better
deliverability, a more personal feel, and higher click rates in his own experience.

## Key claims
- As of 2021-02-01, self-reports a personal email ratio of roughly 98% educational
  content to 2% promotional/selling content — a specific, previously-unlogged ratio
  sharper than the standard 80/20 rule he cites as the industry norm (paraphrase,
  self-reported).
- States roughly 50% of people who begin a checkout process do not complete it — a
  previously-unlogged cart-abandonment baseline figure (paraphrase, self-reported/
  industry statistic, not independently re-verified).
- States a personal preference for plain-text over image-heavy HTML emails, citing
  better deliverability, a more personal feel, and higher click rates in his own
  experience — a specific, previously-unlogged stylistic/tactical preference
  (paraphrase, self-reported).
- Cites mobile email-open shares by segment: 50%+ overall, 70-80% in B2C, "creeping up"
  past 50% in B2B — more granular than prior general mobile-majority claims already
  logged (paraphrase).
