# 7 Landing Page Flaws That'll Kill Your Conversions

## Summary

Neil lists seven landing-page mistakes that tank conversions. (1) **Load time** — backed by a
**Crazy Egg** stat: every one-second improvement in load time raised their conversions ~6%;
critical on mobile where reception varies. (2) **Generic landing pages** — even selling one
product, tailor the page to each **traffic source**: Google searchers want the solution hit
hard and fast; Facebook browsers need a broader, story-led pitch. He recalls doing marketing
for **TechCrunch** and serving its (startup/VC) readers a different message than
Entrepreneur-Magazine (newbie-entrepreneur) readers. (3) **Distractions** — cut excess
navigation, and beware over-sensitive exit popups that fire while people are still reading.
(4) **Visual disconnect** — the page's copy AND images must match the ad/source that drove
the click (his example: bidding on "Apple MacBook" but showing no MacBook text or image tanks
conversions). (5) **Bad mobile experience** — most Google searches are now on mobile; make
pages **responsive** rather than maintaining a separate mobile page. (6) **Not addressing
buyer concerns** — answer objections (guarantee, free trial, reviews, video testimonials/case
studies) on the page; use **HotJar/SurveyMonkey** to discover them; a landing page isn't just
"as short as possible." (7) **Too complicated a process** — too many form fields kills
lead/checkout conversion; use auto-fill and social login (Google/Facebook), especially on
mobile.
