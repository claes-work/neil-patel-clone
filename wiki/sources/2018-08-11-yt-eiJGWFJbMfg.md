# My #1 SEO Trick (It's Not What You Think)

## Summary

Solo video, and a strong statement of belief. People expect Neil's "biggest SEO trick" to
be a link-building or content tactic (he does rank page one for terms like "SEO," "online
marketing," "internet marketing"). His actual answer: **his #1 SEO hack isn't SEO — it's
building a brand.** Everything he does — YouTube videos, blog posts, the Marketing School
podcast — is brand-building, and "the one who has the biggest brand tends to win in the
long run." He illustrates with a friend who'd reflexively buy American Express, Tesla, and
Nike **without googling**, because those brands are built. You can't afford TV/billboards
like big corporations, but you can build a brand by **giving value**: record phone videos
for social, start a podcast, write blog posts. The engine underneath is **genuinely
caring/helping people** — he responds to comments (mostly from people who can't afford his
products) not out of obligation but because he genuinely enjoys helping; he credits this to
his **mom being a teacher**. He realized only later that helping was building his brand
(paid speaking gigs followed), and admits he'd once assumed Nike/Amex/Coca-Cola were
"wasting money" on branding vs. direct-ROI Google ads. Payoff to SEO: as your brand grows,
**more people Google your name**, and rising **brand queries** correlate with rising search
traffic — he calls this the future of SEO, paraphrases Eric Schmidt (brands help sort the
low-quality web), cites his own data, and points to his neilpatel.com post "The Future of SEO."

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-11, Neil's stated #1 SEO "trick" is building a brand, not any on-page or
