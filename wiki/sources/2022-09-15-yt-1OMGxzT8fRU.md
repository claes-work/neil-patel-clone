# How I Grew My SEO Traffic by 300% Through Translations and Patience

## Summary
A concrete traffic-geography breakdown and a 5-step international-SEO playbook. States
his own traffic mix: 14.53% United States, 28.07% Brazil, 13.52% India, 3.25% UK, 2.63%
Germany "and the list goes on." Justifies the non-US focus by citing large non-US
companies (NuBank ~$18B NYSE valuation; Walmart's 2018 $16B majority stake in Flipkart)
and states NP Digital is "the sixth fastest growing ad agency in the world" per Ad Week.
The playbook: (1) use hreflang tags so search engines serve the right page to the right
language/country audience; (2) pick target regions based on Google ad-spend levels (a
proxy for client budget, since NP Digital is a marketing agency) plus population/GDP,
prioritized in order; (3) translate/transcribe target keywords via Google Translate, then
run them through Ubersuggest (with the correct language/country selected) to find
localized keyword opportunities — hire local-language writers via Upwork rather than
requiring fluency personally, giving them a keyword list without forcing unnatural
keyword stuffing; (4) publish with correct hreflang tags; (5) promote via local-language
link outreach (using Ubersuggest's backlink report on regional competitors, hunter.io for
contact emails) and via regional Facebook/LinkedIn/Google ad spend (cites "a hundred
dollars" as going a long way).
