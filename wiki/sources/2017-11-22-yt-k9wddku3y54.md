# 1 Simple Hack to Build Hundreds of Backlinks: The Competitor Infographic Tactic

## Summary

In this solo video, Neil Patel presents a single tactic for generating large numbers of backlinks: recreate a competitor's most-linked informational content as an infographic, then pitch it to the sites that already link to that competitor. He opens by acknowledging that in saturated niches — where established players have built links for years — new content struggles to earn links naturally, and without backlinks a site won't rank well. His fix is to use Ahrefs to find which sites link to a competitor's most popular step-by-step or how-to articles, rebuild that content as an attractive infographic (citing the original source and adding your own logo), and then approach each of those linking sites to ask for a link. He stresses that infographics must be beautiful, simple, and visual to get embedded, that the hosting page needs surrounding text so Google can rank it, and that even if the infographic page itself doesn't rank highly, the accumulated backlinks lift the whole site's domain authority.

## Key claims

- As of 2017-11-22, Neil argues that because high-quality informational content already exists on nearly every topic, people are unlikely to link to a newcomer's content on its own merits.
- He states that competitors who have been publishing for 5–10 years generate far more backlinks simply because they have been around longer.
- As of 2017-11-22, he asserts that without backlinks you will not get strong rankings.
- The core tactic: put competitors' URLs into Ahrefs to see who links to their most popular articles, focusing on informational, step-by-step, list, and how-to content.
- Turn those articles into infographics using tools such as Infogram or Canva.
- When building the infographic from a competitor's tips, cite them at the bottom (e.g., "data and source provided by X, Y, Z site") and place your own logo at the bottom.
- Then contact each website that links to the competitor and ask them for a link to your infographic.
- Doing this across all of your competitors' most popular blog posts can generate a large volume of backlinks and improve rankings.
- On the page hosting the infographic, include a paragraph of text above and below the image so Google has text to rank the page.
- Infographics must be as beautiful as possible; ugly ones will not get embedded on other sites.
- Design guidance: avoid crazy neon colors, keep it simple, make the story easy to follow, and make it more visual than text-based.
- As of 2017-11-22, Neil notes the infographic pages themselves may not rank as high as desired, but the whole site ranks higher because the tactic builds up domain authority.
