# The 6 Time Wasters of SEO — STOP Doing These Activities ★

## Summary
Solo video on where SEO effort is wasted, opening with the honest premise that SEO takes
"dozens and dozens of hours per week for a really long period of time." (1) **Checking
rankings daily.** He discloses his own cadence: **rankings once a week**, Google Analytics
traffic roughly **once a day to once every two or three days** — and the analytics check
exists only to catch an algorithm hit or a traffic tank, not to inform work. "Focus on
execution and stop wasting your time continually looking at reports"; delegate the
watching to Ubersuggest's free weekly rank-tracking email. (2) **Obsessing over keyword
density.** He says flatly that when he writes content he does **none** of the H1/title/meta
keyword placement ritual — he writes for the user and for the most clickable title, "just
like an author would do when they're writing a book or just like a journalist." Keywords
appear naturally in a piece about dog food; forced ones make copy that doesn't flow,
readers hit back, and bad user metrics hurt rankings anyway. (3) **Building excessive
backlinks to raise domain authority.** From agency demand ("I want more links and I want to
increase my domain authority") he pushes back with his own results: he has **outranked
sites with DA 80–90 using a site with DA 40–50**. He notes DA is **Moz's invention**,
Ubersuggest's equivalent is **domain score**, everyone has their own, and **Google uses none
of them** — directionally useful, never a guarantee. His illustration is unusually pointed:
Entrepreneur, Forbes and Huffington Post have "all these guest posters creating shit
content" that doesn't rank everywhere, while a mom-and-pop's genuinely good article beats
it. (4) **Only chasing head terms.** Head keywords drive traffic but convert far worse than
long tail; use Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas prepositions, comparisons and suggestions. His
case study is second-hand but named: his friend **Mike, who runs marketing at Dr. Axe** —
the site lost SEO traffic to algorithm updates, but revenue was not crushed, because
someone searching "nutrition of a banana" was never going to buy supplements. (5)
**Desktop over mobile.** **Over 50% of Google searches are mobile**, there is a mobile-first
index, and the recent **BERT** update shows Google's focus on content and web design for
mobile — so use structured markup and mobile-optimized themes. (6) **Unreasonable
expectations.** Doubling or tripling hours doesn't move Google's timetable: "You don't
control when Google really wants to shift rankings." Which leads to the close, credited to
an online interview with **Jeff Bezos**: Bezos obsesses about the customer — not Google,
not competitors — and if you do what's best for your customers, "in the long run Google
should place you where you belong."
