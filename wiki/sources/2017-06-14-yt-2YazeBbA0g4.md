# 2 Simple Ways to Find New Blog Topics | Find Popular Blog Ideas!

## Summary
In this short solo tutorial, Neil Patel addresses the problem bloggers face once they publish consistently: although regular blogging drives more traffic, writers eventually exhaust their supply of topics and hit writer's block. He offers two research tactics for continuously generating blog ideas. First, use BuzzSumo by entering an industry keyword to surface the most socially-shared posts (filterable by the last six months or year), then write more detailed and thorough versions of what already performs — for example, expanding a "10 ways" post into "101 ways," or broadening a narrow how-to to cover more cases. Second, use Quora to find the common questions people ask within a niche and write blog posts that answer those questions better than anyone else. Neil adds a repurposing tactic: after answering a Quora-sourced question on his blog, he copies that blog content back into a Quora answer, which he says generates hundreds of thousands of views on his Quora profile. He frames the video as teaching both idea generation and an extra Quora traffic channel.

## Key claims
- Blogging consistently increases traffic but eventually leads to running out of topics and writer's block (as of 2017-06-14).
- Entering an industry keyword (e.g., "marketing" or "online marketing") into BuzzSumo surfaces the most popular posts in a space, sortable by recency (last six months or year) and by social shares (as of 2017-06-14).
- Posts with the most social shares are the ones audiences love most, so studying and writing better versions of them is a reliable way to generate ideas (as of 2017-06-14).
- Writing a more detailed or broader version of a proven post (e.g., "101 ways" instead of "10 ways," or covering additional cases) is an effective way to produce new content people still want (as of 2017-06-14).
- Content that performs well on someone else's site is likely to perform well on yours too, provided you are in the same industry (as of 2017-06-14).
- Typing a keyword into Quora reveals the questions people ask, and the most popular ones are common questions worth turning into educational blog posts (as of 2017-06-14).
- Answering those Quora questions on your blog better than anyone else yields a large supply of content and largely eliminates writer's block (as of 2017-06-14).
- Repurposing blog content as Quora answers — including replying to the original question and others who responded — generates traffic; Neil says he does this constantly and generates hundreds of thousands of views on his Quora profile without writing Quora-unique content (as of 2017-06-14).
