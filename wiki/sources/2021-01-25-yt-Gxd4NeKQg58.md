# How To Go From Zero to 50,000 Email Subscribers - With Email Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
This is the introductory lesson of Neil Patel's free "Email Marketing Unlocked" course, framing the promise of taking someone from zero to 50,000 email subscribers across a six-part program. Neil opens with credibility (over 800 million visitors driven to client websites, work with large brands, companies founded, awards from President Obama), arguing that because his strategies worked for big brands they will work for the viewer too. He grounds the case for email in scale and behavior data: 3.9 billion email users in 2019 growing toward 4.48 billion by 2024 (about half the world's population), 66% of online consumers having purchased as a direct result of email, and 72% preferring email as their channel of communication with businesses. He calls email the highest-converting marketing channel and says that if it isn't for you, you're doing something wrong — with the caveat that emails must not just be opened but acted on. The core of the lesson is a set of email workflows keyed to business type: abandoned-cart sequences for e-commerce/digital goods (send at 4 hours, 16 hours, then 2 days), flash-sale sequences (emails on days 1, 2, skip 3, then 4 and 5), nurture sequences for new leads/customers (immediately, then +2 days, then +4 days), and win-back campaigns with coupons for lapsed buyers. He shares best-practice stats (personalized subject lines lift open rates ~50%, three abandoned-cart emails drive ~69% more orders than one, video in email can raise click rates ~300%) and a contrarian send-timing tip: skip the crowded Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday window and send on Mondays, Fridays, and Sundays. He closes with the Semantics FM case study ($4.5M over 10 launches; email alone $1.2M of revenue in a 12-month span, 29% of total), the John Reese "digital ATM" anecdote, and course logistics at neilpatel.com/training.

## Key claims
- The course promises to take someone from zero to 50,000 email subscribers and is structured as a six-part program (as of 2021-01-25).
- Neil establishes credibility by citing over 800 million visitors driven to client websites, work with large brands, companies he has built, and awards from President Obama — framed as proof his strategies transfer to the viewer, not as bragging (as of 2021-01-25).
- Email had 3.9 billion users in 2019, projected to grow to 4.48 billion by 2024 — about half the world's population (as of 2021-01-25).
- 66% of online consumers have made a purchase as a direct result of email, and 72% of customers say email is their favorite channel of communication with companies they do business with (as of 2021-01-25).
- 61% of people say they like to receive promotional emails weekly and 28% want them even more frequently; 72% of US customers prefer email marketing over other types of promotion (as of 2021-01-25).
- Neil personally sends two to three emails per week and finds it super effective (as of 2021-01-25).
- Email is the highest-converting marketing channel Neil has seen; if it isn't the highest converter for you, it means you're doing something wrong (as of 2021-01-25).
- Opens and reads alone don't matter — what matters is that recipients act on the emails (as of 2021-01-25).
- Abandoned-cart workflow (e-commerce or digital goods): email at 4 hours, then 16 hours, then 2 days later to recover the sale; the email can be image-rich or text-rich, either works (as of 2021-01-25).
- Flash-sale workflow (e.g., Black Friday or Christmas offers): send four emails — day 1, day 2, skip day 3, day 4, and day 5 (as of 2021-01-25).
- Nurture workflow for a new lead or customer: email immediately to build rapport, then again 2 days later, then again 4 days later, focused on helping and guiding rather than only selling (as of 2021-01-25).
- Win-back workflow: for buyers who haven't purchased in a few months, send an email with a coupon or discount code, or news about what's new with the business/products/services, to win them back (as of 2021-01-25).
- Personalized subject lines generate roughly 50% higher open rates (as of 2021-01-25).
- Sending three abandoned-cart emails results in roughly 69% more orders than a single email (as of 2021-01-25).
- Adding video to an email can increase click rates by roughly 300% (as of 2021-01-25).
- Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Thursdays are the most popular send days, but because inboxes get bombarded then, Neil recommends sending on Mondays and Fridays for the most opens; Sundays have also performed well for him (as of 2021-01-25).
- You can keep following up with leads a week, a month, or even years later, and do well as long as you keep educating, helping, and building value (as of 2021-01-25).
- Case study — Semantics FM, a site selling mostly music-related courses/products, targeted Generation Z via an email funnel and generated $4.5 million over 10 launches; their "Dubstep, Start to Finish" course email alone generated over $100,000, email accounted for $1.2 million of revenue in a 12-month period, a record March of $125,000, and 29% of their revenue comes from email marketing (as of 2021-01-25).
- Course logistics: cheat sheets, worksheets, a goals worksheet, and a course itinerary are available at neilpatel.com/training under "Email Marketing Unlocked"; by the end of the course the viewer will know how to market their business with email and generate more sales (as of 2021-01-25).
