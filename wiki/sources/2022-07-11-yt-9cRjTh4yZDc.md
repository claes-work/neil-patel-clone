# My Favorite Business Hack ★

## Summary
Neil Patel describes what he calls a cornerstone of his own marketing: give away, for free, something
that people are normally used to paying for, in order to generate cheap demand, then sell that audience
something much bigger and more profitable. His central example is Ubersuggest itself — he says over 95%
of the product is free even though direct competitors charge roughly $99/month for equivalent tools,
because — in his framing — there is more money in marketing *services* than in marketing *software*. He
supports this with a company-scale comparison: HubSpot, "probably the biggest marketing tool out there,"
does "a bit over a billion dollars a year in revenue," while marketing-service holding companies (Dentsu,
WPP, Omnicom) each generate 10-plus billion dollars in revenue despite being less well-known consumer
brands. His logic: giving away the free tool generates "thousands and thousands of leads per month,"
which he then converts into NP Digital service clients — a funnel that's cheaper than paying for
marketing directly, especially since he says marketing costs "continually get more expensive" year over
year. He gives a rule for choosing what to give away: it must be genuinely valuable (something competitors
charge a lot for) and must be *related* to the higher-ticket offer (his counterexample: you can't give away
free payroll software and upsell marketing services, because payroll buyers aren't a marketing-services
audience). He cites Orabrush as a non-marketing-industry example: giving away a free tongue cleaner to
build demand, then upselling teeth whitening/tooth care/toothbrushes, eventually selling the business
successfully. Closes with the standard NP Digital pitch.
