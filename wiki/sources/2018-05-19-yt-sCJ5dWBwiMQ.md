# Why You Should GET OFF Social Media and Start Living Your Life

## Summary

A short Neil-fronted monologue (mid-conversation in tone) arguing that constant social-media capture is displacing both real experience and real productivity, especially for younger people. He observes that kids are bombarded with marketing and stories (Snapchat, Instagram) and are "trying to capture the moment" instead of living it. He recounts growing up before smartphones and notes he himself uses "one of those cheap free" iPhones. His two-part argument: (1) letting social media control your life keeps you from enjoying the moments you're in; (2) time spent taking "10 and 20 pictures a day" is time not spent building a company, learning, or growing website traffic. He is careful not to say quit social media entirely — instead **create balance and cap it at ~1 hour/day** — and to redirect energy toward goals. He argues it is statistically more realistic to build a company worth millions than to become a Jake Paul, Logan Paul, or Kardashian-level success, which is a "0.001%" outcome, not even the 1%.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-05-19, Neil argues younger people increasingly focus on capturing moments (Instagram/Snapchat stories) rather than living them, and that this displaces real experience.
- As of 2018-05-19, Neil says he grew up before smartphones and uses a cheap/"free" iPhone rather than a nice phone.
- As of 2018-05-19, Neil argues that hours spent taking many photos a day are hours not spent building a company, learning, or growing website traffic.
- As of 2018-05-19, Neil does not advocate quitting social media — he recommends creating balance and spending no more than ~1 hour/day on it, then focusing on goals.
- As of 2018-05-19, Neil argues that becoming "insta-famous" like Jake Paul, Logan Paul, or the Kardashians is a ~0.001% outcome, and that building a company worth millions is a more realistic path to success/income.
