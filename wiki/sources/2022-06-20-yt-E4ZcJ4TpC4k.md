# 5 Free Tools that Will Help You Rank #1 on Google

## Summary
A tool-roundup video listing five free tools for SEO/content ranking. (1) Ubersuggest — traffic and
backlink estimates for any URL, keyword suggestions, a "rank number one potential" keyword-research
feature that flags page-one-winnable keywords for your own domain, a site-wide SEO analyzer that
prioritizes fixes by impact, and free project creation with weekly to-do recommendations. (2)
"Subscribers" — a browser push-notification tool Neil frames as a branding/return-visits play: since
Google favors brand-like sites (contrasting New York Times/IRS-level trust against "Joe the
Plumber"), and many users won't give an email, push notifications let a site re-engage visitors
(sent on every new post) without requiring one, driving traffic spikes, social shares, and
occasional new backlinks. (3) Google Search Console — used two ways: to see which keywords already
drive traffic to a page (to decide whether to add missing high-impression/low-click keywords to
title/meta/body), and to compare current traffic against a year prior, prioritizing pages that *lost*
traffic (Google the lost keywords, see who now outranks the page, and rework the page to beat theirs).
(4) Answer The Public — visualizes question-based keyword opportunities, framed as a way to capture
question-driven search volume (Neil compares the payoff to Quora's traffic scale). (5) BuzzSumo — shows
what's popular on Facebook/Pinterest/other social networks, usable on competitor domains too, to focus
content effort on formats proven to get shares rather than guessing.

## Key claims
- Ubersuggest's "rank number one potential" feature flags keywords a domain has a realistic shot at
  ranking page-one for, based on the site's own keyword-research input (as of 2022-06-20).
- Neil states Google explicitly favors brand-like sites over unknown ones (contrasting New York
  Times/IRS-tier sites against "Joe the Plumber"), reasoning that brands are less likely to publish
  fake, harmful financial, or harmful health information — used as the rationale for the
  "Subscribers" push-notification tool as a branding/repeat-visit mechanism (as of 2022-06-20).
- Recommended Google Search Console workflow #1: for any page, check which keywords already drive
  traffic and compare against keywords used in the page's title/meta/body; add missing high-impression,
  low-click keywords to lift CTR (as of 2022-06-20).
- Recommended Google Search Console workflow #2: prioritize pages that lost traffic year-over-year
  (not pages that gained it) by identifying the specific keywords that dropped, checking who now
  outranks the page, and reworking the page to beat the new top results (as of 2022-06-20).
- Answer The Public recommended specifically for surfacing question-phrased search queries, since "a
  lot of the searches that people use on Google are questions" (as of 2022-06-20).
- BuzzSumo recommended for identifying proven-popular content formats on Facebook/Pinterest, including
  by entering a competitor's domain to see their top-shared pages (as of 2022-06-20).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Google wants to rank brands. And they've even said, they look at sites like New York Times, or IRS on one side. And then they look at sites like Joe the Plumber on another side... they prefer brands, because they know brands are going to be less likely to create fake information.

> Another thing I like doing within Google Search Console is I like looking at all the pages I'm getting traffic for right now versus what the traffic used to be a year ago. What are the pages that lost traffic? Forget what gained traffic, I need to fix the ones that lost traffic.

> Don't do the stuff that isn't working, do the stuff that is working. Even for your competitors, you can put in their domain name, see all their popular pages and use that to figure out what you should be doing as well.
