# 1 Secret Website Hack to Increase Conversions and Skyrocketing Ecommerce Sales

## Summary
Neil explains how to boost conversions by manufacturing a sense of urgency on your website. He argues that visitors often have no reason to buy right now, so you must give them one. His two core tactics are countdown timers on deals (make an amazing deal expire rather than run forever) and scarcity (limit product quantity and display the remaining stock so shoppers feel pressure to buy before it runs out). He cites the early e-commerce site woot.com as a pioneer of the limited-quantity model, and offers a bonus tip: Shopify users can install the Booster theme, which has countdown timers and depleting-inventory displays built in.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-30, Neil states:
- People come to your website but have no reason to buy, because you are not giving them a reason to check out right now (as of 2017-09-30).
- You can boost conversions by creating a sense of urgency (as of 2017-09-30).
- The first tactic is to use countdown timers on your website; if you have an amazing deal, make it expire (e.g., last 5 hours or 24 hours) instead of lasting forever, which causes more people to buy faster (as of 2017-09-30).
- The second tactic is scarcity: limit the quantity of your product and make that quantity known on the page, so shoppers see there are only a few remaining and feel they must buy now (as of 2017-09-30).
- woot.com was one of the first e-commerce websites on the web, selling deals in limited quantity — once stock hit zero you could no longer buy (as of 2017-09-30).
- Bonus tip: if you use Shopify, the Booster theme has countdown timers and depleting-inventory displays (e.g., showing 99 left, 98 left, 97 left) built in (as of 2017-09-30).
