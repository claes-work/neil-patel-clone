# How To Turn Ice Cold Visitors Into Loyal Customers

## Summary
Neil argues that because 98-99% of visitors are "ice-cold" — they don't know or trust the brand — pitching them directly fails, and the fix is a relationship-building sequence that delivers value before asking for a sale. He lays out five tactics: (1) offer value first via educational content (citing the ad trend of driving traffic to content before selling because it's cheaper per click and converts better), and pixel your site to build Facebook/Google lookalike audiences from buyers; (2) trade a little extra value for a small commitment using quizzes, ebooks, crash courses, assessments, and webinars — plus Messenger and push opt-ins — rather than a bare newsletter signup; (3) follow up and nurture through email sequences (ConvertKit, Mailchimp), remarketing with video and text ads across Facebook, Google, and YouTube, push notifications, Facebook Messenger blasts, and free webinars; (4) build fast connections with human-staffed live chat (e.g. Intercom); and (5) get social by pulling people into a private Facebook group or Slack community to deepen the relationship and drive word-of-mouth. He closes by pitching his agency, Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said 98-99% of your visitors never convert because they are "ice-cold" — they don't know or trust you.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said you must offer value first and build relationships before pitching, because most brand-new visitors won't buy without value added first (Amazon can skip this only because everyone already knows Amazon).
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said there is a big trend of Facebook and Google ads first driving people to educational content and then selling, because it's cheaper per click and those visitors are more likely to convert since they get to know you first.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said to put a pixel on your site so you can build lookalike audiences on Facebook and Google to show ads to people similar to those who already purchased.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said to give a little extra in exchange for a small commitment (name, email, phone) via quizzes, ebooks, crash courses, assessments, and webinars — the small commitment signals interest and builds rapport that makes later selling easier.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said a bare "subscribe to my newsletter" opt-in gives too little value, whereas offering something tangible in exchange for an email converts really well.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil recommended Facebook Messenger opt-ins and push-notification opt-ins (e.g. a free ebook in exchange) as low-cost ways to grow subscribers and followers.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said to follow up and nurture with email sequences (naming ConvertKit and Mailchimp), and to run remarketing sequences with video and text ads across Facebook, Google, and the YouTube network — warming up even visitors who never opted in.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said he also nurtures via regular push notifications, Facebook Messenger blasts on every new blog post, and free webinars with strong content, all to show value before selling.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said live chat with a real human (naming Intercom) can build trust and convert someone within about five minutes, and that automated/bot chat is not the same as having a human there.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said his friend Yaniv from Nextiva told him that one third of Nextiva's sales come from people on the site, one third from phone calls, and one third from Intercom.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil said to get social by bringing people into a private Facebook group or Slack community, because deepening the relationship makes them both more likely to convert and more likely to tell others, increasing revenue long-term.
- As of 2019-10-07, Neil pitched his ad agency Neil Patel Digital for help growing traffic and sales.
