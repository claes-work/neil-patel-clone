# Top-Performing Content Types for YouTube (The MASTER LIST) - Module 1 - Lesson 3 - YouTube Unlocked

## Summary
Lesson 3 of "YouTube Unlocked" (follows
[[wiki/sources/2020-10-08-yt-oe5SPPHe6xs]]), a rapid survey of YouTube content
formats intended to eliminate "content block." Neil runs through roughly 20 formats —
trend/challenge videos, social experiments, animal content, comparisons, gaming
walkthroughs, "best of" compilations, unboxing, vlogs, comedy sketches, Q&A, live
streams, webinars, how-to/tutorials, travel and lifestyle, and celebrity
collaborations — illustrating several with named examples (Logan and Jake Paul for
vlogs; PewDiePie for gaming; MailChimp-vs-ConvertKit as a comparison-format example;
his own weekly Q&A-video habit and Bounce Exchange interview). He frames unboxing
videos as under-indexed on views but disproportionately strong for driving
conversions, and recommends giving away product for free to seed them. The lesson's
operating instruction is not to chase every format: pick a handful that fit an
audience and personal fit, test them, and double down on whichever produces the most
views and subscribers.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-10, Neil claims unboxing videos, while not the highest-view format,
  drive disproportionately strong purchase conversions ("from what we've seen"),
  and recommends seeding them by giving away product for free to creators.
- As of 2020-10-10, Neil states no single content type outperforms the others
  universally — the right formats depend on the specific audience, and creators
  should test a handful rather than all of them before doubling down.
- As of 2020-10-10, Neil discloses he personally used to run Q&A videos on a weekly
  cadence (past tense, self-reported).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Mastering the different types of content that you can create will help you

> one that does better than the other. You got to figure out what does well for
