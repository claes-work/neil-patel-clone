# Do THIS if Your eCommerce Product Doesn't Sell (And Watch Your Conversions Skyrocket)

## Summary

Neil's fix for e-commerce products that won't sell is a **survey → change → A/B test →
re-survey** loop, built on the premise that "opinions don't matter" — only customer
feedback does. Step 1: **survey your audience** with tools like **Hotjar** and
**SurveyMonkey**, asking non-buyers "Why didn't you buy this product?" and "What else would
you like to see on this page?"; gather 30–50 responses over several days and look for
**patterns**, not one-off complaints. Step 2: make the indicated changes but validate them
with an **A/B test** (e.g. **Crazy Egg**, via a no-code WYSIWYG editor), because a change
can fail to lift — or even hurt — sales. Step 3: **re-survey** after the test to find the
next round of changes, iterating continuously. Step 4: don't survey only the product/home
page — **survey the checkout flow too** (trigger a survey when someone idles 40–60s), since
many e-commerce stores see 70–90% drop-off at checkout; fix based on feedback and A/B test
again. He notes 99% of traffic not converting is usually unanswered objections, which also
explains paid ads that "spend more than they make."

## Key claims

- As of 2019-04-25, Neil said **opinions (yours or his) don't matter** — only surveyed
  customer feedback should drive changes; survey via **Hotjar** and **SurveyMonkey**.
- As of 2019-04-25, Neil said to collect **30–50 responses over four to seven days** and act
  on **patterns**, ignoring single outlier complaints (e.g. "I don't like the color").
- As of 2019-04-25, Neil said to validate every change with an **A/B test** (**Crazy Egg**,
  no-code WYSIWYG) because a change can fail to help — or hurt — sales.
- As of 2019-04-25, Neil said to also **survey the checkout page** (trigger when a user idles
  ~40–60 seconds), because e-commerce checkouts often see **70–90% drop-off**.
- As of 2019-04-25, Neil attributed ~99% non-conversion of traffic, and losing paid-ad spend,
  largely to **unanswered customer objections**.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Opinions don't matter. All that matters is what your potential customers are telling you."

> "In many cases, a lot of e-commerce stores are seeing 70, 80, 90 percent drop-off just on their checkout page."

> "When you get quantity, you'll start seeing patterns, and you want to focus on the patterns. You don't want to focus on that one person who tells you 'I don't like the color of your site.'"
