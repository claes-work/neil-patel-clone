# How to Get More Instagram Followers Fast (and Be Instafamous)

## Summary

In this 2017 solo video, Neil Patel lays out a tactical checklist for growing an Instagram following quickly. He covers profile setup, image quality, consistent posting cadence, keyword- and hashtag-rich captions, a manual follow strategy, and engagement tactics like replying to comments and tagging friends. The advice reflects 2017-era Instagram mechanics (manual follow-for-follow, search-driven caption discovery).

## Key claims

As stated by Neil Patel on 2017-05-28 (platform tactics from this era may be dated):

- Start with a strong, professional profile image; a profile picture alone isn't enough — also fill in your name and a catchy description.
- Write a description that gives people a concrete reason to follow (his example uses credential-style proof points) and that matches the type of content you'll post, so followers know who and why they're following.
- Post visually striking, high-quality images; no marketing gimmick compensates for poor photos, and the more visual the photo the better it performs.
- Research what already works by searching hashtags or keywords in your niche to see which photos have historically done well.
- Posting once a week gets more likes per photo, but posting once or twice a day grows the follower count faster.
- Keep a consistent posting schedule; he suggests two daily time slots that work well — 10:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m.
- Use a long, thorough caption on each photo — richer text makes the photo more discoverable when people search on Instagram.
- Add a handful of hashtags, roughly five to ten per photo; 30 hashtags is excessive and tacky, but any hashtags beat zero.
- Manually follow about 30 people per hour within your niche or people who interest you; following more than that gets spammy and risks triggering Instagram's filters.
- Many of the people you follow will follow back, which leads to more likes and comments as you keep posting.
- Respond to comments and engage with people — engagement makes them more likely to share your photos with others.
- Once you have more followers, tag friends in relevant photos (e.g., tag a friend in a destination photo), which prompts others to tag more people and expands reach.
