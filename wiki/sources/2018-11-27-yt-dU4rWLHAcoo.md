# How to Create a Digital Product That Generates (AT LEAST) $100,000 Per Month

## Summary

In this conference talk to an affiliate-marketing audience (Affiliate World Asia), Neil Patel lays out his end-to-end playbook for building and selling a $1,000+ digital info-product that nets at least $100K/month. He argues the model has ~100% margins (cost is basically hosting), and walks the room through: picking a broad, non-marketing niche you are (or can partner with) an expert in; surveying at least 30 prospects to validate the product; building a proud, "put-your-face-on-it" membership product (videos/audio/worksheets over text) completable in one-to-three months; pricing at a minimum of $1,000 and under $3,000 (above that needs a sales team); and selling primarily through an automated webinar funnel (WebinarJam) reinforced by a multi-day InfusionSoft email sequence with countdown-timer urgency. He dissects his own webinar script slide by slide (goal → credibility → testimonials → three secrets → offer stack → bonuses → guarantee → buy button at $997), claims a 3.6 sales per 100 webinar registrations conversion (half from the live webinar, half from follow-up), and shares a battery of tactics: 15-minute recurring webinars for ~70% show-up rates, PicSnippets handwritten-image emails, TurboDial next-day SMS, 24/7 Philippines chat staff, and phone-based sales closing. He fields Q&A on pricing nerve, public-speaking avoidance (hire a voice/face), fear-and-vulnerability as selling emotions, and how a fitness or WordPress-plugin seller should ladder low-ticket into high-ticket. Throughout he repeatedly warns against fake testimonials (the recurring "Dr. Oz"/"Rob Patel" running joke) and insists sellers provide genuine value.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said a promoted digital product has "pretty much 100 percent margins" because after creation the only cost is hosting.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said he sells his digital product for $1,000 a pop (that figure being after refunds), and on one Saturday sold ~36 units having spent only ~$3,000 on advertising, supplemented by heavy organic traffic.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said his biggest niche-selection mistake was picking digital marketing — it was "too late" and too competitive; a broader niche like nutrition would have sold far more ebooks and courses.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said to gauge niche demand with Google Trends and to pick a broad, less-competitive niche; he cited Wellness Mama (not his blog) getting ~10 million monthly unique visitors with ~757 linking root domains, versus his QuickSprout at ~1 million visitors despite over 1,815 linking root domains.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said you must be enough of an expert to teach and deliver value, OR partner with / hire an expert to be the face; he cited a Brazil yoga/back-pain/pregnancy info-product run by non-experts netting ~$400K–$500K/month from Facebook ads (spending ~$20K–$25K USD/month at roughly 3-point-something to 1).
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said to survey at least 30 people (for statistical significance) — readers, or targets found via Facebook, Craigslist, phone — asking their biggest problem/challenge, using tools like Koalaroo (Qualaroo) and SurveyMonkey.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said a customer should be able to finish the product in one-to-three months: faster than one month causes too many refunds; longer than three months feels like too much work and kills follow-through.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said videos, audio files, and worksheets carry more perceived value than text documents; keep it actionable, don't overwhelm, and build a membership portal with simple tools like WP-Member — his own portal (built by a contractor "Vovo") cost about $1,000.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said do not charge less than $1,000; anything over $3,000 doesn't sell well without a sales team, so stay under $3,000.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil gave Facebook-ad return benchmarks: if you're bad at Facebook ads you should get ~$2 revenue per $1 spent, good is ~$3, and the best he'd seen was ~$5 per $1 at scale (some spending $20K–$30K/day) — and it works with a single Facebook ad account, no churn-and-burn.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil cited Ramit Sethi (I Will Teach You To Be Rich) making $5M in a week, illustrating that a $5 product would need ~100M visitors while a $2,000 product needs only ~250K visitors — argue for the higher price point.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said his webinar (WebinarJam) should run ~1.5 hours: ~40 minutes educating, ~20 minutes pitch, then Q&A, with a buy button shown at the right price.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said email is his single highest-converting and highest-revenue channel — he cited ~103,000 unique visitors from email and said email drives ~40-something percent more revenue than anything else despite not being the majority of traffic.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said his webinar converts at 3.6 sales per 100 registrations (even though not all 100 show up), split roughly 1.8 from the live webinar and 1.8 from the email follow-up sequence.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said he runs the live webinar every 15 minutes with an on-page countdown timer, achieving an almost 70% show-up rate.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said his offer stacks a ~$22,000 total value, anchors down through $5,000 and $3,000, then reveals a live buy price of $997 which "converts really well," backed by bonuses (implementation plan, a "get a million visitors" video lesson, a Facebook group, and his vendor "rolodex").
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil described his email stack: a seven-day InfusionSoft sequence whose last two days use a "Plus This" countdown timer that expires links to create urgency, plus list segmentation (e.g. separate buckets for people who clicked the buy button but didn't buy, or watched only the first five minutes).
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil recommended PicSnippets ($47, which he doesn't own) to send handwritten-style images with the recipient's name dynamically inserted to non-attendees, and TurboDial to auto-SMS non-buyers the NEXT day (never same-day — too pushy), handing warm responders to a sales rep to close by phone.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said he staffs the webinar chat 24/7 with well-paid Philippines workers (~$1,200/month, plus an extra $1/hour after they complete the program free) who proactively offer live feedback on attendees' website URLs to drive engagement and sales.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said pricing under $1,000 is a flat "no" — he tested $200/$500/$1,000 and kept making more money as he raised price, with revenue only dipping once he pushed to $2,000–$3,000.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said fear is a powerful selling emotion (citing Agora Financial making "over 400 million dollars a year" on survival/doom-themed offers), but vulnerability and relatability also convert — he cited his own story of chasing traffic and making zero for the first ~five years of business.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said low-fidelity phone video often converts BETTER than professionally produced video because it feels realistic and relatable, citing Tai Lopez's "Here in my garage" ~$66 product making millions.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said for niches where you can cast a wide net (e.g. fitness), start with a low-ticket product ($100–$200) and ladder up through supplements, ~$100/month tracking/coaching, and $1,000–$2,000 personal-trainer/Skype offers (arbitraging trainers from Craigslist); citing Beachbody P90X making money on the back end and Muscle for Life charging a few thousand for Skype training.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil advised a WordPress theme/plugin seller to raise prices ~10x by bundling into one "master plugin," to nofollow footer links on free themes (Google penalizes them), and to invest ~$10K in premium designers to get 10–20x more downloads.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil repeatedly warned against fake testimonials (fabricated Oprah/Dr. Oz endorsements) even though webinar content isn't indexable — use realistic, believable testimonials.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil said on checkout you can double-to-triple revenue on a low-end product by offering three upsells/downsells.
- As of 2018-11-27, Neil claimed friends running this model net roughly $100K–$200K/month (the weaker ones) up to ~$1M/month in profit (the good ones).
