# How to Run Split Tests and Optimize Your Website for Conversions

## Summary
Neil lays out a three-step process for deciding what to test on a website and then testing it. First, install Crazy Egg — a visual analytics tool — to see what visitors are and aren't doing. Its reports (scroll maps showing how far people scroll, heat maps showing where they click, and video recordings showing mouse movement and navigation) surface the issues and bottlenecks visitors face. Second, use Crazy Egg's WYSIWYG editor to make changes that address those problems. Third, run an A/B test (through Crazy Egg or on your own) to see which variant performs and which doesn't. He stresses that without testing you can't know whether your changes help or hurt, and that everyone — himself included — makes mistakes, which is why he tests everything.
