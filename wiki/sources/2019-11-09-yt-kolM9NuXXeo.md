# 7 Skills Every Marketer Must Master (Or Else Get Crushed by The Competition)

## Summary
In this solo talking-head video, Neil Patel lays out seven skills he argues every digital marketer must master to avoid being outcompeted. The seven are: (1) media buying — going beyond Facebook/Google/Bing to channels like Taboola, and pairing traffic with educational copy that sells (e.g., driving to a webinar that then pitches services); (2) SEO/organic traffic generation — hedge against ever-rising paid-ad costs (he notes Google didn't have a down year in the last recession because profitable ad spend survives cuts), even though organic converts worse than paid but carries high ROI since you don't pay per click; he plugs Ubersuggest's site audit for prioritized fixes; (3) copywriting and persuasion — citing David Ogilvy that 80 cents on the dollar goes to the headline, and that ~8 of 10 read the headline but only ~2 of 10 read on; persuasion is needed to fund rising ad costs via upsells/downsells; (4) channel partnerships / influencer marketing — focus on micro-influencers and channel partners sharing your exact customer base to leverage audiences cheaply (example: Hex Ties going from zero to millions via fashion-account Instagram posts, not just mega-accounts); (5) analytics — go beyond basic Google Analytics to goal tracking, cohort reports, and repeat-usage/session analysis to make data-driven rather than gut decisions; (6) social media marketing — emphasis on video and going live, low-fi phone-recorded videos like GaryVee or Tai Lopez rather than studio production; (7) research — competitive research on what competitors do/don't do, using podcasts like Nathan Latka's or Growth Everywhere to learn what's working and adapt. He closes by plugging his agency Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said the seven skills every marketer must master are media buying, SEO traffic generation, copywriting/persuasion, channel partnerships/influencer marketing, analytics, social media marketing, and research.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said media buying is broader than Facebook, Google, or Bing ads — he named Taboola as providing "a huge ROI" when used correctly.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said media buying requires generating copy that educates people and, through that education, pushes them toward the product/service — educating and selling simultaneously, rather than just driving to a lead-capture or direct-sale page. He gave his own example of driving traffic to a webinar landing page and selling his services within the webinar.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said paid ads will become more expensive over time, so marketers must learn organic/SEO traffic.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil claimed Google did not have a down year in the last recession — it still made more money — because advertisers who spend a dollar to make two, three, or four dollars won't cut profitable spend, and instead cut things like television ads.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said organic traffic doesn't convert as well as paid traffic, but still drives very high ROI because you don't keep paying per click.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil recommended (for getting started with SEO) going to Ubersuggest, entering your URL, and running the site audit report, which prioritizes fixes by traffic impact.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil cited David Ogilvy that "80 cents on the dollar is spent on the headline" and said copywriting is "all about the headline."
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil stated that eight of ten people read your headline but only two of ten click through to read the rest of the content.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said copywriting/persuasion matters because rising ad costs require adding upsells and downsells; without persuasion you won't make enough front-end revenue to keep affording advertising.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said influencer marketing is on the rise, and the key is finding micro-influencers or channel partners with the exact same customer base as you — a cheaper way to leverage someone else's audience by cutting out the middleman (Google/Facebook).
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said paying the Kardashians to promote a B2B product won't work, but having them promote a lipstick line they genuinely like and use can.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said a company he worked with called Hex Ties went from zero to millions in revenue through influencer marketing on Instagram — not via million-follower accounts, but by getting fashion accounts to post about them.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said good analytics means going beyond basic Google Analytics to use goal tracking, cohort reports (who comes back repeatedly), and analysis of which pages cause more sessions and repeat usage, so you optimize on data instead of gut.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said social media marketing comes down to video and going live; you don't need studio-quality production and can record quick phone videos like GaryVee or Tai Lopez, which is more relatable and cheaper.
- As of 2019-11-09, Neil said research means competitive research (what competitors do and don't do, how to do better), and he named podcasts Nathan Latka and Growth Everywhere as places competitors "spill their beans," giving an edge and letting you see what works in similar industries before testing on your own site.
