# What is Growth Hacking? (Drop Box Example)

## Summary
Neil defines growth hacking as a rapid-experimentation process that changes elements of a business's marketing to grow faster, framing it as the answer for companies that want to market but lack the budget of their competitors. He stresses that growth hacking spans the whole organization — not just marketing, but product, engineering, and sometimes sales — all working together rather than the traditional hand-off model where marketing buys traffic and passes it downstream. Using Dropbox as his central example, he shows how a failing paid-acquisition model was replaced by a referral program (more invited friends = more free storage) that built a massive user base and, through upgrades, a multi-billion-dollar company. He urges weekly rapid experimentation across traffic sources, onboarding, conversion optimization, and upsells, and recommends tracking every test with a tool like Crazy Egg to see whether changes lift or hurt leads, sales, and revenue — accepting that not every experiment succeeds.
