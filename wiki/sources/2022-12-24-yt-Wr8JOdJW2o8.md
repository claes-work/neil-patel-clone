# Why Your Social Media Strategy Doesn't Get Any Engagement

## Summary
Seven reasons a social strategy underperforms, framed around the view that "social
media isn't a roulette table, it's a game of strategy." (1) Content isn't optimized
per platform — cites carousel ads driving up to 10x more traffic and a 72% higher
CTR than standard sponsored posts on Facebook/Instagram; states per-platform
practices he uses: LinkedIn content that doesn't link off-platform, Instagram live
video, TikTok's native format, Twitter "tweet storms," YouTube videos built to keep
viewers watching subsequent videos (even others' videos), and Facebook videos over
five minutes outperforming shorter ones. (2) The content calendar is stale — cites
Sprout Social Index that 74% of consumers think one to two posts a day is ideal for
brands, contrasted with brands posting only a few times a week; recommends at least
daily posting, 1-2x/day as the sweet spot, more than 3-4x/day as "overboard" absent
exceptional content — reasoning that algorithms only surface posts to a portion of
followers per post. (3) No cross-channel approach — cites the average social user is
active on six platforms and spends 2.5 hours/day on social; recommends being present
on the six platforms an "ideal customer" actually uses, treating new platforms (cites
Clubhouse, TikTok as examples) as fine for early adoption but only once meaningfully
popular. (4) No call to action — cites average social-ad CTR of only 1.3%; the CTA
should be topically matched to the content (his example: a social-media video's CTA
should reference social-media help, not SEO help). (5) All-text posts — cites 68% of
social users prefer images to text; states his own view that "a video says 10,000
words." (6) Wrong posting time — cites a Hootsuite analysis of 30,000+ posts finding
10am Tuesday-Thursday as most effective on average, caveated as a starting point to
tweak, adjusted to the audience's own time zone. (7) Weak customer service — states
poor customer service is the primary reason consumers unfollow brands on social,
and frames responsiveness as caring, not just conversion-driven. Notes NP Digital was
"voted agency of the year" (self-reported, unspecified body). Closes with an NP
Digital plug.
