# My Favorite Customer Service Tip

## Summary
Argues the top customer-service/marketing tip is to "speak as your customers do" —
casual, conversational tone rather than corporate/professionalized language. Cites
HubSpot: 68% of customers will pay more for quality customer-service experiences (as
cited, dated 2023-02-18). Five practices: (1) casual email subject lines — cites
Barilliance: 64% of open decisions are based on subject lines, and self-reports he's
more likely to open casual, friend-like subject lines than all-caps/title-case
professional ones; (2) conversational content tone — "a true thought leader doesn't
talk down to their audience"; (3) improve support chat — cites 52% of consumers are
more likely to repeat-purchase if live chat is offered, and relays a named example:
"my buddy Yaniv," co-founder of Nextiva (a phone-service company that bootstrapped
then raised at a $2B+ valuation from Goldman Sachs), told him chat drives roughly 33%
of Nextiva's sales; (4) easy website navigation — cites 56% of customers expect to
find what they want within three clicks, recommends tools like Crazy Egg (his own
earlier company) for user recordings/engagement mapping; (5) how-to tutorial videos —
cites users are 3x more likely to prefer a YouTube tutorial over reading instructions,
with the "how to tie a tie" example. Closes reiterating "talk to them as if you're one
of them."

## Key claims
- Cites HubSpot: 68% of customers say they'll pay more for quality customer-service
  experiences (as cited, dated 2023-02-18).
- Cites Barilliance: 64% of customers decide to open an email based on the subject
  line (as cited, dated 2023-02-18).
- Cites a stat: 52% of consumers are more likely to make a repeat purchase if a
  company offers live-chat support (as cited, dated 2023-02-18); relays a
  third-party self-report from "my buddy Yaniv" (Nextiva co-founder) that chat drives
  ~33% of Nextiva's sales — third-party claim, not independently verified.
- Cites a stat: 56% of customers expect to find what they want on a company website
  within three clicks or less (as cited, dated 2023-02-18).
- Cites a stat: users are 3x more likely to prefer a YouTube tutorial over reading a
  product's written instructions (as cited, dated 2023-02-18).
