# Marketing on Etsy, Walmart, eBay & Facebook Commerce - Module 3 - Part 2 - eCommerce Unlocked

## Summary
eCommerce Unlocked lesson extending the Amazon playbook (keyword research via
Ubersuggest, listing optimization, HD images, reviews) to other marketplaces. **Etsy**:
323M monthly visits, 2.5M sellers; tag guidance (vintage/custom/handmade/gift-related
keywords) and a distinctive positioning note — "describe pretty much less about the
product... more about how it feels," since Etsy buyers shop on emotional/handmade
connection rather than spec sheets. **Walmart**: cites $1T+/year in sales and ~30%
annual e-commerce growth; notes most Amazon sellers push under 10% of their listings to
Walmart, framing this as a **low-competition opportunity**; win-conditions are
competitive/lowest pricing, free shipping, and the "Buy Box." **eBay**: 10B+ in
revenue, ~1.5B live marketplace listings, 174M active buyers; win-tactics: start listings
at a low price and let bidding build (auction dynamic), free shipping + 30-day
money-back guarantees, encourage positive feedback. Briefly surveys other marketplaces
(Facebook Marketplace, Google Express, Alibaba, Rakuten, Allegro, Wish, Wayfair) and
sourcing sites (Alibaba, Pocket, Delegate, Wholesale Big Buy). Closes on **voice search**:
cites that over 50% of U.S. searches are now voice-based (Alexa/Google Home/phone),
recommends targeting "buy"-intent keywords via Ubersuggest for voice-search
optimization, and shares a personal anecdote about test-buying products via his own
Alexa device "just to test it out."

## Key claims
- As of 2020-07-09, Neil said under 10% of Amazon sellers also list on Walmart,
  framing Walmart as a comparatively low-competition, high-volume ($1T+/year, ~30%
  annual growth) channel worth prioritizing.
- As of 2020-07-09, Neil's Etsy-specific positioning advice diverges from his general
  spec-forward product-page doctrine: describe emotional/experiential qualities
  ("how it feels") over technical product details, because Etsy buyers shop for
  handmade/emotional connection.
- As of 2020-07-09, Neil cited that over 50% of U.S. searches are now voice-based
  (unsourced figure) and recommended targeting "buy"-intent keyword phrases via
  Ubersuggest specifically for voice-search optimization.
- As of 2020-07-09, Neil shared a personal anecdote: when he first got his Alexa
  device, he test-bought products from Amazon via voice just to try the feature,
  "probably didn't need that stuff right away, but it was a fun experience" (minor
  self-reported voice/behavior datapoint).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Etsy is that site where people come for like handmade goods, that special emotional connection, that feeling, and that's what you want to do if you want to win on Etsy."

> "Everyone thinks about Amazon. But Walmart generates over a trillion dollars a year in sales... that's good news for you because that means there's less competition."

> "The amount of times I bought from my Alexa is huge. I buy so many products from Amazon just from my Alexa device."
