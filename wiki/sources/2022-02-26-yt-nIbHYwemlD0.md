# The Best Sales Funnel for e-Commerce

## Summary
A tactical breakdown of a two-step, paid-traffic e-commerce sales funnel, contrasted
against the "ad → generic product page → cart" funnel he calls typical and ineffective
for small/mid brands (framed against big-brand exceptions like Nike/Amazon that can
rely on brand loyalty instead). Recommends running ads to a single product per
campaign (one product, one message, one creative) rather than spreading across a
catalog; a dedicated landing page (not a standard product page) with strong social
proof (feature testimonials, star ratings, video testimonials of use — not just
unboxings), full product presentation (photos, 360° views, video), benefit-led copy,
persistent buy-now CTAs, and an FAQ section informed by actually asking non-buyers why
they didn't convert (phone calls or exit-intent surveys). Two post-add-to-cart
mechanisms: an "order bump" (a related, well-bundled, genuinely good-deal upsell
offered before checkout, claimed to rarely hurt conversion and typically add 4-6%
revenue) and post-purchase upsells (speed/automation/subscription framing, claimed to
add at least ~3% on a weak execution and 10%+ when well-tuned with two additional
upsells).

## Key claims
- Recommends single-product-per-campaign ad targeting as a frequently-violated best
  practice (self-reported guidance, dated 2022-02-26) — paid-ads, analytics-cro.
- Order bumps (pre-checkout related upsell) rarely decrease conversion and typically
  add roughly 4-6% revenue when the bundle is a genuinely good deal (self-reported
  figure, dated 2022-02-26) — analytics-cro.
- Post-purchase upsells (speed/automation framing) add at least ~3% revenue with weak
  execution, 10%+ when well-tuned with two additional upsells (self-reported figure,
  dated 2022-02-26) — analytics-cro.
- Recommends surfacing FAQs addressing non-purchase reasons discovered via direct
  customer calls or exit-intent popups, rather than guessing (self-reported guidance,
  dated 2022-02-26) — analytics-cro.
