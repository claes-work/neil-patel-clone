# The Three Unseen Laws of SEO (Ignore These and Ranking #1 on Google Won't Happen)

## Summary

★ L3-candidate — a named Neil framework: three "unseen laws" beyond the usual links/on-page advice
that he says determine long-run rankings. (1) **Brand queries** — as brands grow, Google favors
them; track your brand vs competitors in Google Trends and keep it "up and to the right." (2)
**User experience / user metrics** — dwell time, bounce/pogo-sticking, pages-per-visit; Google
compares your engagement to other rankers, so a great product/service beats pure optimization
(Dropbox, Slack, Airbnb won on UX, not SEO-first). (3) **Adapt to technology** — voice search and
ambient Google Home/appliance search are coming; keep up or get left behind. Includes a
self-reported anecdote that his own "online marketing" rankings jumped once his brand queries rose,
without new links or content changes.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-03-14, Neil framed three "unseen laws of SEO" that matter beyond standard links/on-page
  work: brand queries, user experience, and adapting to technology. [framework]
- As of 2019-03-14, Neil said **brand queries** are a rising Google signal (partly a response to fake
  news / low-quality content); the bigger your brand, the better your long-run rankings. Track brand
  search interest vs competitors in Google Trends, aiming for growth.
- As of 2019-03-14, Neil said that when his brand queries were low he struggled to rank for "online
  marketing," but as brand queries rose he shot to the top of page one **without** building extra
  links or changing content. [self-reported]
- As of 2019-03-14, Neil said Google uses **user metrics** — time on site, dwell time, back-button/
  pogo-sticking, pages-per-visit — compared against other rankers; poor UX loses rankings regardless
  of optimization, so you need a genuinely good product/service. Cited Dropbox, Slack, and Airbnb as
  companies that won on UX, not SEO-first (Airbnb's pro-photographer program lifted conversions).
- As of 2019-03-14, Neil said SEO keeps changing and you must **adapt**; cited a Comscore projection
  that half of searches would be voice search by 2020, and ambient search via Google Home / connected
  appliances as the direction of travel. [dated prediction — attributed to Comscore]
