# Why I Focus on Social Media Marketing Over SEO ★

## Summary
Explains why he currently emphasizes social media over SEO despite being "mostly known
for all things SEO" and running an agency built substantially on SEO. Core reasoning:
social gives near-instant feedback ("you can see results instantly... I don't have to
wait for an algorithm update") versus SEO's long lag, letting him pivot strategy
quickly. Cites stats: "74%" of social media users use social media to make buying
decisions; adults spend "95 minutes" per day across social platforms; Instagram Story
ads projected to generate "almost 16 billion" in worldwide sales in 2022 (cites
HootSuite). Cites a Google SVP (Prabhakar Raghavan, misspelled/mis-transcribed
"Pranhakar" in captions) reportedly stating at a 2022 conference that ~40% of young
people looking for lunch spots use TikTok/Instagram instead of Google Maps/Search —
used as evidence that even Google is worried about social displacing search.
Explicitly frames this as reallocation of emphasis, NOT abandoning SEO: "I still love
it," "you should use all channels" (omnichannel), reiterating the rule to use every
channel customers are on rather than picking one. Notes all platforms (search or
social) run algorithms nobody controls, and cites Google's direct weather-answer box as
a case of Google itself suppressing outbound clicks — paralleling the 2022-11-03
Quick-Read-Test source's same example. Closes with an NP Digital plug.
