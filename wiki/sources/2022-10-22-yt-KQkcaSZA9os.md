# You May Not Want Google to Rank Your Site in All Countries

## Summary
Opens from a Reddit thread where a user asked how to set country-targeting for a new
domain in Google Search Console; a named Google employee ("Gary Ellis" — likely a
caption garble of Google Search Advocate **Gary Illyes**, unverified spelling) is
paraphrased replying that Search Console geo-targeting is generally unnecessary since
Google infers geography from other signals, and pointing to how companies like Amazon
handle international SEO. Pivots to his own stat that "over 82%" of neilpatel.com/NP
Digital traffic comes from outside the US, framing international SEO as high-value but
warning against expanding into regions where the business can't actually serve
customers well — poor regional fit drives pogo-sticking (click, bounce, never return)
which he says actively hurts overall SEO. Gives a four-part international-SEO
playbook: (1) transcribe/adapt content per region rather than word-for-word
translation, since culture differs; (2) implement hreflang tags (recommends Aleyda
Solis's hreflang tool for generating the code); (3) build region-specific backlinks,
either via an Upwork-hired local link-builder or an agency (his own, NP Digital,
plugged with the self-description "awarded Agency of the Year"); (4) generate PR to
build brand-name search volume, illustrated with a Nike comparison (7M+ monthly US
searches for "Nike" vs. under 2M for "shoes," which he argues signals brand strength
to Google and earns higher rankings).
