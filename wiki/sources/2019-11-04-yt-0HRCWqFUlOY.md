# How to Find Hundreds of Thousands of SEO Keywords For Free

## Summary
In this solo tutorial Neil Patel walks through a free keyword-research workflow built entirely on his own tool, Ubersuggest. He argues you no longer need a handful of paid tools to find keywords or spy on competitors — one free tool does it. The method: (1) mine the keyword ideas report's suggestions, related, questions, comparisons, and prepositions tabs for high-intent, low-competition keywords; (2) find your own top-traffic pages via the traffic analyzer overview, pull the keywords driving them, and expand those into their question/comparison/preposition variants to integrate back into pages that already rank for head terms; (3) enter competitor URLs to see every keyword they rank for, examine their top pages and content, and use the content ideas report to find similar articles so you can write something better; (4) harvest the list of sites linking to competitors and do outreach for links; and (5) run the site audit report to fix duplicate titles/meta descriptions while working the new keywords in. His core SEO logic is that once a page ranks for a hard head term, ranking its long-tail variations is comparatively easy. He closes by citing his own site's traffic (5.5M+ monthly visitors, 16M+ page views) as proof and plugs his agency, Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said a few years ago you needed a handful of tools to find keywords and analyze competitors, but today you can do it all with one free tool — Ubersuggest, his own marketing tool.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said Ubersuggest is genuinely free and he makes no money off it; in fact more users cost him more in server costs.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said the keyword ideas report breaks keywords into suggestions, related keywords, questions, comparisons, and prepositions — and that questions, comparisons, and prepositions surface valuable keywords that few people are targeting.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said question keywords signal a searcher looking for an answer, and if your product/service is the answer you can expect to generate sales; comparison keywords signal a searcher ready to make a decision.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said to use the traffic analyzer overview on your own domain, scroll to top pages, click keywords to see what drives traffic to those pages, then run those keywords through Ubersuggest to find their question/comparison/preposition variants and integrate them into the top pages already ranking for the head terms.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said if you already rank for a head term it is really easy to rank for the long-tail, comparison, and preposition variations — e.g., ranking for "credit cards" makes it easy to also rank for "bad debt credit cards," "free credit cards," or "low interest rate credit cards" — because head terms are harder to rank for than their long-tail phrases.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said entering a competitor URL into Ubersuggest reveals all the keywords they rank for; their top pages reveal the type of content they create, the keywords each content piece ranks for, and who links to it.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said before creating a similar piece of content, take the main keywords from a competitor's article, run them through the content ideas report to find all similar blog articles, review them, and make sure what you write is better than everything ranking.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said you should hit up every site linking to your competitors, "beg them for a link," tell them your version provides more value, and be very specific about how they should link to you.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said to run the site audit report to find duplicate titles and meta descriptions, fix them, and work newly researched keywords into those titles/descriptions — because fixing them also helps increase rankings.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil said this keyword approach is how he grew neilpatel.com to over 5.5 million visitors a month and over 16 million page views.
- As of 2019-11-04, Neil promoted his ad agency Neil Patel Digital for people who need help growing traffic.
