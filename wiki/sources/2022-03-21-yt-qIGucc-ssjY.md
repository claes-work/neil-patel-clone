# I Spent $500,000 in TV Ads to Promote My Ad Agency… This is what happened

## Summary
A first-person case study of spending $500K on TV ads (stock/business channels like
CNBC and Bloomberg) in 2021 to promote NP Digital, despite being on record as a
"20+ years" digital-marketing enthusiast who generally distrusts interruption-based TV
advertising because it can't be measured like digital/SEO channels. His thesis: as
advertisers fled TV for digital, TV inventory became underpriced and COVID kept the
target audience (corporate executives compensated in stock, who watch stock-ticker
channels) at home watching. Targeted NP Digital's actual buyer persona — Fortune
50/500-level enterprise decision-makers — reasoning that executives paid in equity
watch financial-news channels. Ran a $100K test with no direct sales, then continued
to $500K total. Outcome: minimal website traffic and few qualified leads, but a
branding lift (more branded search) and, most importantly, a legitimacy/perception
shift — large prospective clients who already knew of NP Digital but assumed it was
small began including it in RFPs once they saw it advertising at that scale. Concludes
he would do it again as a one-off legitimacy play but not as a regular tactic, since
ROI was weak.
