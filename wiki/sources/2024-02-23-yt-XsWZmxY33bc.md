# Exposing Secrets From a Founder's Retreat

## Summary
Neil narrates a private "Founders Retreat" he's run annually since ~2018 (Wed–Sun, cost-split only, no business model) — this year in Phoenix, AZ (last year Turkey), ~8 regulars including ConvertKit founder Nathan Barry, ProfitWell founder Patrick Campbell, a quilting-company founder ("Al Don," ~$100M revenue) who buys entire towns, and friends "Sayed" and "Yaniv." Two Neil-attributed business takeaways dominate: (1) a **middle-of-funnel lead-nurture framework** — agency/company websites are optimized to "take orders" from the ~20% of visitors ready to buy now, ignore the 20% who'll never buy, but neglect the middle 60% where Patrick Campbell's ProfitWell reportedly generates most of its meetings via automated lead-enrichment + BDR outreach (6+ touches/month, 80+ calls/day per rep); Neil admits NP Digital and neilpatel.com under-invest here despite driving plenty of top-of-funnel traffic, and wrote his team a 6–7 page memo on fixing it; (2) a **procurement/negotiation tactic** ("Sayed's" method, which Neil says NP Digital already effectively practices) — have staff (not the founder) run vendor demo calls, request "best pricing" without disclosing a budget, don't reveal competing quotes, and issue a final 24-hour best-price ultimatum. A tangential aside on companies with inflated valuations built on debt rather than profit (unnamed Indian startup "worth $20B, now ~$1B") is Neil's own observed principle, not first-hand testimony.

## Key claims
- **Middle-of-funnel is where agencies leave money on the table.** For every 100 site visitors, ~20% are ready to buy now (sites are optimized for them) and ~20% will never buy; the middle 60% is under-served. Neil says he's strong at top- and bottom-of-funnel marketing but weak in the middle, and that NP Digital should be capturing far more from its existing traffic than it does. (Neil, 2024-02-23)
- Patrick Campbell's ProfitWell nurtures the middle 60% via automated triggers off gated downloads (e.g. "I noticed you downloaded this, what do you need help with?"), routed to BDRs; this reportedly produced the majority of their booked meetings — a workflow Neil says NP Digital doesn't do nearly as well despite the traffic volume it drives. (Neil, 2024-02-23)
- **NP Digital's website and neilpatel.com behave differently and can't be nurtured with the same sequence.** NP Digital's enterprise leads go through a more formal RFP-style process (limited follow-up cadence); neilpatel.com leads get heavier multi-channel (call/email/SMS) follow-up. (Neil, 2024-02-23)
- **Procurement negotiation tactic** (credited to friend "Sayed," which Neil says NP Digital effectively already does): don't have the founder/decision-maker join vendor demo calls — staff run the demos, ask vendors for "your best price" without disclosing budget, don't reveal competitors' quotes, and if a lower price is still wanted, issue a "match X price within 24 hours or we move on" ultimatum. (Neil, 2024-02-23)
- **Debt-financed "unicorns" are not what they look like.** Some people who appear to be billionaires are actually in debt; some high-valuation companies raise large corporate debt rather than generate positive cash flow, and much of their founders' net worth is illiquid private stock. Cited example (unnamed): an Indian startup once valued at ~$20B now worth roughly ~$1B. (Neil, 2024-02-23)
- Retreat logistics as a repeatable idea: doesn't have to be expensive or exclusive — Neil suggests anyone, even a 21-year-old, can organize a small one-day mastermind/retreat; value comes from peer learning "through osmosis." (Neil, 2024-02-23)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Our websites are optimized to take orders... for every hundred people maybe 20 of them are ready to buy now, our sites are optimized for the buy now, and 20% of people will never buy from you, and you have the middle 60% — and it's actually the middle 60% that matters.

> The key takeaway for me is the fortunes in the followup.

> Some people that you think are like billionaires or whatever — they're actually not, they're actually in debt, like a lot of money... a lot of people have made billions of dollars by stacking up debt.

> It doesn't matter what stage you're at in your career, even if you're like 21 years old, you can put your own little retreat together... these things just go a long way because people are learning through osmosis.
