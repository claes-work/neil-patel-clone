# How To Create A Compelling Brand - Module 7 - Part 2 - SEO Unlocked

## Summary
SEO Unlocked module 7, part 2: brand-creation methodology. Opens defining brand via
an **Elon Musk** framing — "a brand is just a perception, and perception will match
reality over time" (sometimes ahead, sometimes behind). Illustrates with Coca-Cola,
Nike, McDonald's, and his own name (marketing, sharing, growth, advice, SEO). A
brand's story is built from four elements: what the brand says about itself, what it
does in the world, what others believe/say about it, and how others interact with
it — reinforced by consistent messaging over time. Prescribes two worksheets (via
neilpatel.com/training): a Unique Selling Proposition / Unique Value Proposition
worksheet, and a "Bigger Contribution" worksheet. Competitive positioning research:
use Facebook's Ad Library or WhatRunsWhere to see competitors' running ads, sign up
with a dummy email to see their landing pages/email sequences, attend their sales
calls/webinars — explicitly instructing "buck the system," not copy competitors, but
use the research to find a differentiated position. On deployment: brand insights
should permeate everything (SEO, title tags, sales copy, website/product design,
sales calls, customer support), because "the moment it doesn't align, that's where
you're going to see things break." Frames every customer touchpoint (unboxing, a
sales letter, a video, an ad, a phone call) as part of the brand experience, and ties
experience quality directly to pricing power — a strong experience-driven brand can
charge premium prices with lower price resistance (his example: Apple customers still
buy despite price complaints). Closes pointing to three worksheets due before the
course's final lesson.
