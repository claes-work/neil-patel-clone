# My Favorite SEO Report - Uncover Your Competitors' SEO Strategy in a Few Clicks

## Summary
Solo Ubersuggest walkthrough of his self-declared "favorite SEO report": Similar Websites
→ Keywords Gap. Process: enter your domain (or a competitor's, if you're low-traffic),
use Similar Websites to build a manually-curated competitor list (demonstrates removing
false positives like Forbes — ranks for some of the same terms but isn't a true
competitor), then for each competitor click Keywords Gap → View All to page through every
keyword they rank for that neilpatel.com/NP Digital doesn't, discarding irrelevant hits
(e.g. "how to get your contacts", "emotional commercials in 2019") and keeping relevant
ones (e.g. "WooCommerce marketplace themes", "long scroll website examples", "Instagram
Stories", "Facebook Business Manager" — each tied back to an NP Digital service he can
pitch). Repeats the exercise against a second competitor (WordStream) to show the pattern
generalizes, then closes by recommending Googling the surviving keywords to reverse-
engineer what content ranks for them before writing.

## Key claims
- Names "Keywords Gap" (via Similar Websites → competitor selection) as his single
  favorite SEO report because it surfaces a competitor's ranking keywords that you don't
  yet rank for, generating a de facto content to-do list (self-reported, 2021-06-05) —
  seo.
- Demonstrates a manual relevance-filtering discipline on gap keywords — reject volume-only
  hits, keep only keywords tied to an actual NP Digital service line (SEO, PPC, CRO,
  Amazon marketing) — reinforcing the existing relevance-over-volume doctrine already in
  `wiki/topics/seo/` (self-reported workflow, 2021-06-05) — seo.
- Recommends, once a target keyword list is built, Googling each term to study the
  ranking SERP as the content-creation brief (self-reported workflow, 2021-06-05) — seo,
  content-marketing.
