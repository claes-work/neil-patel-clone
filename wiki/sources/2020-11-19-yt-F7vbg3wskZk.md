# Local SEO Signals (And How to Master Them) - Module 1 - Lesson 2 - Local SEO Unlocked

## Summary
Local SEO Unlocked, lesson 2. A deep pass through local-search ranking-signal
categories: business signals (category selection — ~4,000 Google My Business
categories as of 2020, pick as few and as specific as possible, describe the business
not its amenities), location signals (NAP consistency, real physical address, no
virtual offices), review signals (volume, diversity, recency, response rate; recommends
the tool Podium), off-page signals (aggregators/IYP directories), on-page signals
(keywords, SSL), link signals, and social/behavior signals (bounce rate, time on site,
GMB panel interactions). Closes with three named case studies: Yum Brands ($4B
single-quarter digital-sales record), KFC (75% of traffic from search; +60%
directions-clicks → +16% local-search revenue), and Whole Foods (per-store social
localization).

## Key claims
- As of 2020-11-19, recommends choosing the fewest, most specific Google My Business
  categories possible rather than broad or popular ones, illustrated with Wendy's/
  McDonald's (fast-food category, not "ice cream" or "dessert" despite offering
  desserts) — miscategorization is framed as actively harming rankings (paraphrase).
- As of 2020-11-19, recommends the tool **Podium** for increasing review volume via a
  low-friction, single-click review-request flow, and explicitly warns against
  incentivized reviews ("I'll give you a free dessert if you come to my restaurant in
  exchange for a review") as a terms-of-service violation on most platforms
  (paraphrase, tool endorsement + compliance caution).
- Cites three case studies as self-reported/aggregator statistics, not independently
  verified here: Yum Brands' digital sales rose over $1B YoY to a $4B single-quarter
  record; KFC drew 75% of traffic from search across ~920 locations (listings grew to
  ~50,000), with a 60% increase in directions-clicks producing a 16% increase in local-
  search revenue; Whole Foods runs per-store social media accounts with daily customer
  engagement.
- Speculates (2020-11-19, framed explicitly as a forward-looking guess, not a current
  claim) that the future of local SEO involves Google increasingly personalizing local
  results using social-graph and Android-device signals, contingent on user opt-in
  (paraphrase).

## Notable verbatim quotes
