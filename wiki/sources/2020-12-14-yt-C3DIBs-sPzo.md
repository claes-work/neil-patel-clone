# How To Protect & Grow Your Business With Digital Accessibility & SEO

## Summary
A co-presented webinar/sales-webinar with Joel from AudioEye (a digital-accessibility
vendor), framed around the 2021 Page Experience algorithm update. Neil's throughline:
digital accessibility (WCAG compliance, readable text, alt tags, navigable structure)
overlaps with SEO/UX signals Google already rewards, and — separately — accessibility
is a growing legal-compliance risk (ADA lawsuits, e.g. Domino's Supreme Court case)
and a large underserved market (Neil cites $8T in disposable income tied to people
with disabilities and their households). Neil supplies the SEO-relevant framework
(brand signals, click-through rate, user-experience-as-ranking-factor) and personal/
family anecdotes; Joel supplies the AudioEye product pitch and accessibility-industry
stats. The video is co-branded content (AudioEye is a paying/partner sponsor of this
webinar), so vendor claims about AudioEye's own product should be read as marketing.

## Key claims (Neil-attributed only; paraphrase unless quoted)
- As of 2020-12-14, frames the 2021 Page Experience update as Google formalizing that
  "if that page has a million links... but someone clicks on your listing and they're
  like, this page is crap, Google doesn't want to show it" — user experience as a
  ranking factor independent of backlink count (paraphrase + quote below).
- States alt tags and accessibility markup already function as SEO signals (Google
  uses alt tags to understand/rank images) and expects accessibility-adjacent signals
  to become a stronger ranking factor over time as the elderly population comes online
  and Page Experience rolls out — though he explicitly does not predict it will ever
  rival backlinks in ranking weight (paraphrase).
- Reinforces the standing brand-signal doctrine already in the corpus (citing Matt
  Cutts, ex-head of Google WebSpam): Google differentiates trusted, brand-searched
  sites (his example: New York Times, IRS) from low-trust sites, and the more people
  search/mention your brand by name, the higher your rankings trend over time
  (paraphrase, reinforcement).
- Personal/business self-reported stats: neilpatel.com generates roughly 10M visits/
  month (with seasonal B2B dips in Nov/Dec); his team publishes ~90 articles/month
  (mostly *updates* to existing content) versus his own ~1 article/week, and he
  frames continuous content freshness — not new-content volume alone — as the growth
  lever (self-reported).
- CTR-optimization guidance (paraphrase, reinforcement of standing doctrine): titles
  using words like "how to," "free," "you," and "tips" tend to raise click-through
  rate; recommends re-testing title tags in Search Console and expecting ranking
  movement within ~30 days.
- Anecdote-grounded accessibility framing: describes setting his mother (mid-60s, no
  significant vision impairment) up with iPhone accessibility settings (larger/bolder
  text) and her reaction, and his father (70+) abandoning sites with hard-to-read text
  — used to argue accessibility affects a broader population than commonly assumed,
  including older users without formal disabilities (self-reported family anecdotes).
- States his own site uses AudioEye's JavaScript (self-reported vendor endorsement;
  Neil is not an AudioEye employee — reads as a sponsored/affiliate-style
  recommendation, flagged as such rather than presented as neutral).

## Notable verbatim quotes (Neil only)
> If Google is ranking sites that aren't the best experience... they're talking about
> things like backlinks and keywords and internal links and external links, but at
> the end of the day, if you meet all those requirements, but someone clicks on your
> listing and they're like, this page is crap, Google doesn't want to show it.

> When you grow your brand awareness, your rankings go up. A big factor in Google's
> algorithm is brands... the more people that talk about you, the more people that
> mention your brand, the more people that are Googling your brand name, the higher
> you end up ranking in the long run.

## Joel (AudioEye, guest — context, NOT persona data)
- Product lead at AudioEye (joined the prior year to lead product); presents
  accessibility-industry stats: CDC report citing ~1-in-4 Americans with some form of
  disability; ~$8T aggregate disposable income tied to people with disabilities and
  their households (sourced to a Forrester report); ~26% of the population has some
  accessibility-relevant impairment; ADA-related lawsuit volume "climbing," citing the
  Domino's Supreme Court case. (Joel, 2020-12-14)
- Describes AudioEye's hybrid model (automated JavaScript-based fixes + human/IAAP-
  certified remediation experts), an "accessibility score" (0–100), and a "Sustainable
  Testing and Remediation Plan" document positioned as legal-defense documentation if
  served an ADA demand letter. Cites enterprise customers (FCC.gov, ADP, Social
  Security Administration). (Joel, 2020-12-14) — vendor marketing claims, not
  independently verified.
