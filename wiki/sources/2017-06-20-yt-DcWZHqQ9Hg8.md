# 5 Tools That'll Help You Drive Traffic to Your Website | Search Engine Optimization Tips

## Summary
Neil Patel walks through five tools he recommends for growing website traffic across SEO, social, and paid channels. (1) **Ahrefs** (ahrefs.com) — enter a competitor URL to see which pages and keywords drive their organic search traffic, plus the backlinks pointing to those pages, so you can create better content and email the linkers to earn links. (2) **BuzzSumo** (buzzsumo.com) — enter a topic to surface the most-socially-shared posts (Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest), then write a better version and use "view shares" (or a free Twitter URL search) to find and pitch the people who shared the original. (3) **Google Search Console** — after adding your site, it surfaces errors (404s, broken pages, link issues) to fix one by one for more traffic. (4) **SEMrush** — enter a competitor URL (e.g., amazon.com) to see the paid keywords they bid on, which signals high-converting, traffic-driving keywords worth pursuing organically or via paid. (5) **Ubersuggest** (ubersuggest.io) — enter a phrase to get a long list of related keywords pulled from Google Suggest autocomplete data to integrate into your content.

## Key claims
- Ahrefs is best used to increase Google (organic search) traffic; you enter a competitor URL and view their organic traffic to find their most popular pages and the keywords driving them (as of 2017-06-20).
- Writing a better post than a competitor's top-ranking page and then emailing everyone who links to that page (visible in Ahrefs' backlink data) earns more links and increases rankings (as of 2017-06-20).
- BuzzSumo is best used to increase social traffic, ranking popular posts by social shares across Facebook, Twitter, and Pinterest (as of 2017-06-20).
- Without a paid BuzzSumo account, you can still find sharers by pasting the original article URL into Twitter's search feature to see who tweeted it, then pitching them for a tweet (as of 2017-06-20).
- Google Search Console surfaces site errors (404s, broken pages, link issues), and fixing them one by one over time increases traffic (as of 2017-06-20).
- SEMrush shows the paid keywords a competitor bids on; because no one continually pays for keywords that lose money, those keywords are high-converting and worth targeting organically or via paid (as of 2017-06-20).
- Ubersuggest (at ubersuggest.io) generates a long list of related keywords by pulling from Google Suggest autocomplete data (as of 2017-06-20).
