# How to Develop a Social Media Strategy Step by Step

## Summary
A seven-step framework for building a social media strategy. (1) Set SMART goals first —
Neil gives his own goals as an example (become a digital-marketing thought leader,
build NP Digital's brand awareness, manage brand reputation, grow website traffic and
community engagement), and stresses goals must be Specific, Measurable, Attainable,
Relevant, and Time-bound rather than "pie in the sky" (e.g. a million followers in a
day). (2) Choose platforms that match the goal: he says Facebook still drives
conversions well while Instagram links are limited to stories/bio, whereas LinkedIn and
YouTube work better for thought leadership (long-form video on YouTube, written posts on
LinkedIn); recommends starting with one or two channels to avoid spreading thin, then
diversifying and repurposing content later. (3) Run a social media audit: follower
growth rate, days/times of notable gain or loss, top-engaging content by format
(video/image/carousel/written) and by message type, and audience demographics
(age/gender/location/language) to time posts correctly. (4) Build an audience profile
(age, interests, gender, cultural touchpoints) using Google Analytics, Facebook Page
Insights, Instagram Insights, Facebook groups/forums/Reddit, and Ubersuggest's keyword
ideas report to surface audience pain points/questions. (5) Do competitive research on
five accounts targeting a similar audience: study their promotion cadence, content
formats/messaging, cross-promotion across channels, which keywords/posts they rank or go
viral for, and which posts underperform. (6) Catalog which content types work best per
channel. (7) Build a content calendar (tools like Hootsuite or Post Planner) around the
chosen channels and content types.

## Key claims
- Neil's own social-media goals (as of 2022-05-02): become a digital-marketing thought
  leader, build brand awareness for NP Digital, manage brand reputation, grow website
  traffic, and improve community engagement.
- Social-media goals should follow the SMART framework: Specific, Measurable,
  Attainable, Relevant, Time-bound — not aspirational targets like "1,000,000 followers
  in a day" (as of 2022-05-02).
- Facebook is comparatively strong for driving conversions/sales; Instagram is weaker
  for click-throughs since links are largely limited to stories and bio
  (as of 2022-05-02).
- LinkedIn and YouTube are the more effective channels for building thought leadership
  in a field like digital marketing — long-form video on YouTube, written thought
  pieces on LinkedIn (as of 2022-05-02).
- Recommends starting with only one or two social channels to avoid spreading effort
  too thin, then diversifying and repurposing content over time (as of 2022-05-02).
- A social audit should check follower growth rate, notable gain/loss days and why,
  which content formats and message types drive the most engagement, and audience
  demographics to time posts appropriately (e.g. accounting for time zones)
  (as of 2022-05-02).
- Recommended audience-research sources: Google Analytics, Facebook Page Insights,
  Instagram Insights, Facebook groups, forums, Reddit, and Ubersuggest's keyword ideas
  report for surfacing common audience questions/pain points (as of 2022-05-02).
- Competitive research should study five accounts targeting a similar audience: posting
  frequency, content formats/messaging, cross-channel promotion, and which specific
  posts went viral or underperformed and why (as of 2022-05-02).
