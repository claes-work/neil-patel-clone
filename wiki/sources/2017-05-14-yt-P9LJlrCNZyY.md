# How Long Does Content Marketing Take to Work? Neil Patel's Digital Marketing Stats

## Summary
Neil Patel answers a single question — how long content marketing really takes to produce results — and his answer is roughly two years. He argues that text-based content (articles, blogs, news) ranks better than any other format on Google, but that a new blog post won't deliver rankings and traffic within a month or two. To prove the timeline he walks through his own NeilPatel.com organic search traffic: about 9,000 monthly Google visitors when he launched in January 2015, growing to 88,000 by January 2016 (nearly a 10x jump), then to more than 400,000 by January 2017. He attributes the slow climb not to publishing more content but to how long Google takes to rank a post — pages accrue social shares and backlinks and generally improve with age, so rankings and traffic compound over time. His practical takeaway: don't expect amazing results within a few months; occasional viral spikes on Facebook or Twitter aren't consistent, whereas search engines deliver the steady, dependable traffic that takes about two years to build.

## Key claims
- Content marketing takes roughly two years to produce consistent results; a blog post won't generate meaningful rankings and traffic within one or two months (as of 2017-05-14).
- Text-based content (articles, blogs, news sites) ranks better on Google than any other content format (as of 2017-05-14).
- NeilPatel.com launched in January 2015 and was getting about 9,000 monthly visitors from Google early on, after a few initial blog posts (as of 2017-05-14, self-reported).
- By January 2016 the site reached 88,000 monthly Google visitors — nearly a 10x increase (as of 2017-05-14, self-reported).
- By January 2017 search traffic exceeded 400,000 monthly visitors — a substantial (though not 10x) increase over the prior year (as of 2017-05-14, self-reported).
- The slow growth was not caused by producing more content each day; it reflects how long Google takes to rank a post (as of 2017-05-14).
- Google weighs factors like content depth, social shares, and whether a page earns more backlinks over time; these accumulate naturally and older content tends to perform better as those metrics improve (as of 2017-05-14).
- Viral spikes on Facebook or Twitter can produce sudden visitor jumps but are not consistent; search engines are the most consistent traffic source, and consistent Google traffic takes roughly two years (as of 2017-05-14).
