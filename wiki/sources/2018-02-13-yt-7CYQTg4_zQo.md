# How to STAND OUT on Social Media as a New Entrepreneur | Grow Your Brand with Social Media Marketing

_Solo Neil monologue._

## Summary
A solo talk on standing out on social media when you can't outspend rising Facebook/Google ad
costs. Neil's three-part answer: **pick the content type you're personally best at**, create
**long-form text for traffic**, and **participate consistently by helping others.** On format,
he refuses to prescribe video vs podcast vs text — **Gary Vaynerchuk** wins with video because
he loves it and is great at it; **Neil does text-based content** because he's better at it;
you'll do better at whatever you're naturally best at. But he draws a sharp distinction by goal:
if you want **street recognition/fame, video wins**; if you want **website traffic, long-form
text wins**, because (unlike videos and podcast files) it also ranks on **Google** — he cites
**Tim Ferriss** finding the same. Can't write? Hire from **jobs.problogger.net**. His distribution
engine is the familiar **BuzzSumo → "view shares" → skyscraper outreach**: find popular articles,
write a more in-depth version (101 ways vs 10 ways, so nobody bothers to copy you), then email
everyone who shared the original; he outsources sending those emails via **Upwork**, which grew his
**Twitter traffic ~17%**. The second pillar is **consistency as helpfulness** — not just posting,
but commenting in thorough paragraphs, joining groups, and genuinely helping, because "social is a
community; it's about other people." His honest expectation-setting: this is **not** fast — you
won't be "insta-famous," but clout compounds month over month; **Gary Vaynerchuk has done this 10+
years, Tim Ferriss put in years before *4-Hour Work Week*, and Neil says he's "been doing this for
over 16 years."**

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-13, Neil states:
- Ads keep getting more expensive; **social media can be free**, but it's noisy — you won't stand
  out without deliberate tactics.
- **Pick the content type you're best at.** He won't prescribe video/podcast/text; **Gary
  Vaynerchuk** does video because he loves/excels at it, **Neil does text** because he's better at
  it — you naturally do better at your strength.
- **By goal**: for **recognition/fame, video** helps most; for **website traffic, long-form text**
  works best — because videos and podcast files don't rank on Google, but long-form text does
  (cites **Tim Ferriss** finding the same).
- Can't write? Hire writers at **jobs.problogger.net**.
- **Distribution engine**: use **BuzzSumo** → target keyword → **"view shares"** to see who shared
  a popular article → write a **more in-depth "skyscraper" version** (101 ways vs 10 ways, so it's
  too much work for anyone to copy) → email each sharer with a personalized pitch to check
  out/link to your better article.
- He **outsources the outreach emails via Upwork** (people send on his behalf); this grew his
  **Twitter traffic by ~17%** and drove more followers/retweets (self-reported).
- **Consistency = helpfulness**: not just posting content consistently, but **commenting (in
  thorough paragraphs, not one-liners), joining groups, and helping others** consistently across
  Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, LinkedIn — "social is a community, it's about other people."
- **It takes years, not months.** You won't be "insta-famous"; clout builds month over month —
  **Gary Vaynerchuk 10+ years, Tim Ferriss years before *4-Hour Work Week***, and **Neil "over 16
  years."**
