# Google's search dominance drops to a 15-year low, media outlets hit by site reputation policy & more (Marketing School)

## Summary
A fast news-roundup Marketing School episode with no clear framework, covering five items: (1) a claim that Google's US search share hit a 15-year low (~77%), which the co-host immediately reality-checks against SimilarWeb data showing Google's absolute search traffic still growing over the long term; (2) Google's site reputation abuse policy torching the coupon/affiliate sub-sections of big media sites (CNN, USA Today, LA Times) as it cleans up SERPs; (3) a Search Engine Land read on Sundar Pichai signaling that links to websites will stay important; (4) Screaming Frog's new OpenAI integration to automate basic SEO at scale, with a human-double-check caveat and a peek at NP's own before/after-and-revert auto-optimization tooling; and (5) the Advon "AI content monster" exposé (Gannett/USA Today and Sports Illustrated fake AI bylines) used to reinforce the recurring mantra that pure AI content fails and AI must be paired with a human.
