# The Elon Musk 5-Minute Productivity System (Marketing School)

## Summary

A short co-hosted Marketing School episode on personal productivity systems. Almost the entire "system" content is **Eric's** — he introduces the topic, having researched it ("I looked this up… I read about this somewhere"), and lays out the framing: the Pomodoro Technique (25-min focus blocks + 5-min break), the claim that Elon Musk works in five-minute increments on the highest-leverage things, that Bill Gates worked similarly with an assistant planning everything (Gates being very detail-oriented and checking the schedule the night before), Parkinson's law (work expands to fill the time you give it, so tighter blocks force output), and a recalled anecdote that Elon's staff told someone each minute of his time is worth ~$22M. Eric says he started testing 15-minute blocks for half a day this week because he needs more focus. After Eric reads the Single Grain sponsor spot, he asks **Neil** how he does it; Neil describes his own method — his assistants set everything up in 15-minute increments and he works from a daily list of tasks, spending ~15 minutes on each. Eric marvels that this "is not normal," that Neil "has like an app in his head" and just focuses, whereas Eric gets distracted easily and needs an alarm. The episode closes on a light aside about one host performing best with a TV on in the background (speaker unclear). Low persona signal for Neil: the productivity theory is Eric's recalled/unverified material; Neil's only substantive contribution is his own 15-minute-task work method.
