# Conversion Hacks - CRO Unlocked

## Summary
CRO Unlocked Week 2, Lesson 1 ("Conversion Hacks"). Opens with Crazy Egg's user-session
recording feature (mouse-movement/video capture of real visitor behavior, ~1–2 minutes
to configure, sample 500 sessions or randomly rather than recording every session), then
contrasts **quantitative research** (structured, numeric — bounce rate, exit rate, CTR;
requires enough respondents to be statistically meaningful, e.g. 2–3 survey responses is
not enough) with **qualitative research** (opinion-based feedback — "I don't like the
colors," "I don't understand this page"), arguing both are needed together to maximize
conversions. On exit-intent: claims **conditional** exit popups (asking a yes/no question
first, before the "carrot" of email/purchase) consistently outperform popups that go
straight to the ask. Gives a landing-page checklist: high-quality images, limited choice,
customer testimonials, shorter scroll length (claims long-scrolling pages now underperform
given mobile usage), and video in place of long text blocks (claims better mobile
engagement than reading 12 pages of text on a phone). Reinforces friction-removal (limit
options to ≤3, citing decision-paralysis / "kid in a candy store" framing, and the common
3-tier SaaS pricing pattern) and "make customers love you" (Apple/Nike/Amazon brand-
loyalty framing) as under-discussed CRO levers. Cites a specific Amazon stat: 35% of
Amazon's revenue comes from dynamic recommendations. Closes on a worked A/B-test example
(100 visitors, 22 vs. 52 conversions) and a copywriting-best-practices checklist
(testimonials/social proof, simple site flow, dynamic recommendations, email, mobile
optimization, page-load speed via Ubersuggest's Site Audit report, loyalty-perk programs
that scale rewards with spend).

## Key claims
- States conditional exit-intent popups — asking a yes/no question before requesting an
  email or purchase — convert better than popups that go straight to the ask
  (paraphrase, 2021-05-03) — analytics-cro.
- Landing-page doctrine: use high-quality images, limit choices, include customer
  testimonials, keep scroll length short, and prefer video over long text blocks, arguing
  long-scrolling pages "don't work as well as they used to" given mobile usage patterns
  (paraphrase, 2021-05-03) — analytics-cro / content-marketing.
- Friction-reduction rule: limit choices to three or fewer options wherever possible
  (cites the common 3-tier SaaS pricing pattern as an example of applying this), framing
  more options as decision paralysis (paraphrase, 2021-05-03) — analytics-cro.
- States 35% of Amazon's total revenue comes from dynamic product recommendations
  (self-reported/uncited third-party statistic, 2021-05-03) — analytics-cro.
- Argues brand-loyalty ("making customers love you," citing Apple/Nike/Amazon) is an
  under-discussed CRO lever distinct from tactical page tweaks (paraphrase, 2021-05-03)
  — analytics-cro / content-marketing.
- Repeats existing wiki material recommending Ubersuggest's Site Audit report to check
  page-load speed, emphasizing mobile speed as more consequential than desktop
  (paraphrase, 2021-05-03) — analytics-cro / seo.
- Recommends "loyalty perks" programs that scale rewards with customer spend as a
  retention/CRO lever (paraphrase, 2021-05-03) — analytics-cro.
