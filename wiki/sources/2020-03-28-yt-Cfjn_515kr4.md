# How to Make Your Website SEO-Friendly When You Can't Code

## Summary
Neil addresses the fear that SEO requires coding by walking through no-code tools for
five technical-SEO tasks: (1) schema/structured data — argues it boosts CTR (his
example: a 5-star/500-review product listing beating a competitor with no review
schema) and points to Google's Structured Data Helper and a "Schema Markup Generator"
tool for people who can't hand-code markup; (2) XML sitemaps — explains sitemaps help
Google crawl/index more pages, recommends Screaming Frog for non-WordPress sites and
the Yoast SEO plugin for WordPress, and says to submit the sitemap via Google Search
Console; (3) robots.txt — explains it blocks Google from crawling sensitive or
in-progress/campaign-only pages, recommends Internet Marketing Ninjas' robots.txt
generator; (4) CDN for speed — reiterates that Google factors load time into rankings
and recommends Cloudflare's free plan; (5) on-page SEO/content optimization plugins —
recommends Yoast or Rank Math on WordPress for keyword density/placement, readability,
titles, slugs, and metadata without needing technical skill. Closes with an agency plug.
