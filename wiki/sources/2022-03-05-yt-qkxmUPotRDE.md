# How LONG Did It Take to Get My First 10,000 Subscribers on YouTube? ★

★ L3-candidate: concrete, dated first-person account of his own YouTube channel's
early growth history — two distinct, contrasting tactical phases with hard numbers,
directly useful for `persona/biography.md`'s content-strategy timeline.

## Summary
A first-person account of reaching his first 10,000 YouTube subscribers, framed as
taking roughly eight months and requiring two failed-then-refined tactics. Phase one:
paid YouTube ads, generating an average 323,000 views/month and 970,000 total views
from ads alone, but with poor retention (average only 50% of his short videos watched)
and a subscribe rate of just 0.2%; ad spend was "tens of thousands of dollars," and
views collapsed to near zero once ads stopped, with only ~1,500 subscribers gained in
that period — he frames it as producing short-term view volume without durable
channel growth. Phase two: switching to releasing one video per day, which produced
8,500 subscribers over four months despite lower raw view counts; he credits daily
output with both compounding subscriber growth and giving him fast signal on what the
audience wanted, letting him double down on winning formats. Closes with a repeatable
tactical playbook: keyword-optimize titles/descriptions via Ubersuggest, ask for the
subscribe within the first 30 seconds, hook viewers within the first 10 seconds, push
every new video via email/push-notification/other social channels (direct-messaging
existing fans rather than just posting a wall update), run this daily-upload regimen
for a 30-60 day data-gathering window, then taper to a lower cadence (he does three
times/week) focused on the highest-engagement formats identified from that data.
