# Should you raise money today?, 73% free trial conversion rate, Netflix ads tier & password crackdown

## Summary
Opens with the FanDuel liquidation-preference case (two key investors held a preference entitling
them to the first $559M of any acquisition; a $465M acquisition offer left founders/employees with
nothing) as a jumping-off point for whether founders should raise VC today. Neil states his
fundraising philosophy directly: raise if the capital funds something requiring massive scale (a
ChatGPT/OpenAI-style bet) — not his own style, since he rates himself as an operator/acquirer rather
than a breakthrough innovator; his rule of thumb is he'll take capital only if he's confident he can
turn $1 into $3-4 with a clear path, and he explicitly says he has no current scenario where he
could deploy $500M economically within NP Digital's acquisition model (buying small, still-growing
companies too small for PE) without overpaying and breaking the return math — he could deploy
$20-100M productively and recycle capital indefinitely instead. He references a Ray Dalio/Adam Grant-
style personality test he and Eric took (mediated by Eric), scoring himself very low on the
creativity axis but "maxed out" on both toughness/resilience and detail-orientation/deliberateness,
framing this as evidence for knowing your own strengths rather than chasing whatever the "moonshot"
crowd is doing; recalls raising money early in his career for KISSmetrics. He also tells a
conversion-rate story from his KISSmetrics days: switching signup flow to "Sign in with Google
only" produced a lift of nearly 100%, but conversions later declined as users grew privacy-averse
about Google-only login; restoring an email/password option alongside Google recovered the
conversion rate — drawing a direct parallel to modern checkout pages that add Apple Pay/Google Pay/
PayPal options. Separately, the episode discusses a ~73% free-to-paid conversion rate disclosed by
Chrisman Frank (CEO of the kids'-learning platform Synthesis) and the Netflix password-sharing
crackdown's reported 30M-paying-user 2023 addition (A/B-tested in Latin America between flat
per-household pricing and a per-added-user fee, per Bloomberg reporting).

## Key claims
- **Fundraising philosophy: raise capital only for genuinely capital-intensive, breakthrough bets;
  otherwise it's often a liability.** States he is "not the kind of entrepreneur who's innovating
  like crazy" and instead operates/acquires; his personal rule is to take money only when he's
  confident he can turn $1 into $3-4 with a clear deployment path. Says he currently has no scenario
  where he could productively deploy $500M within NP Digital's acquisition model (would take 5+
  years, or force overpaying for targets and break the return math), whereas he can deploy
  $20-100M and recycle it indefinitely without needing outside capital. (Neil, 2024-06-06;
  self-reported)
- **Personality test (referencing a Ray Dalio/Adam Grant-style framework) taken with Eric Siu:**
  Neil self-rates ~10/100 on creativity but "maxed out" on both toughness/resilience and on
  detail-orientation/deliberateness — used to argue that knowing your own strengths/weaknesses
  matters more than copying someone else's moonshot strategy. (Neil, 2024-06-06; self-reported,
  informal test)
- **Raised money early in his career for KISSmetrics** (and "a few other things"); the larger
  lesson he draws is that raising money makes most sense only for capital-intensive bets, given that
  today's cloud/AI tooling has made launching a startup cheaper but customer acquisition/traction
  more expensive due to competition. (Neil, 2024-06-06; self-reported)
- **KISSmetrics conversion-rate lesson: forcing "Sign in with Google" as the only signup option
  produced close to a 100% lift initially, but conversions declined over time as users grew
  privacy-averse to Google-only login;** restoring an email/password option alongside Google
  recovered signup rates — direct parallel drawn to modern checkout pages offering Apple Pay/Google
  Pay/PayPal alongside card entry. (Neil, 2024-06-06; self-reported, KISSmetrics-era)
- FanDuel case (context, not a claim about Neil): two key investors' liquidation preference
  entitled them to the first $559M of any acquisition; a $465M sale offer would have left founders
  with nothing. Used to frame a general "should you raise money" question rather than as commentary
  on FanDuel's actual outcome. (relayed third-party case, 2024-06-06)
- Chrisman Frank (Synthesis CEO) disclosed a ~73% free-trial-to-paid conversion rate, discussed on
  air as "extremely high" if credit-card-gated (unusual but plausible) and "unheard of" if not
  credit-card-gated. (third-party self-reported figure, relayed 2024-06-06, not independently
  verified)
- Netflix's password-sharing crackdown reportedly added ~30M paying users in 2023 (per a relayed
  Bloomberg-sourced Instagram post), A/B-tested in Latin America between flat per-household pricing
  (Reed Hastings' approach) and a per-added-user fee (Greg Peters' approach), with the per-user-fee
  method winning out. (third-party/relayed reporting, 2024-06-06, not independently verified)
