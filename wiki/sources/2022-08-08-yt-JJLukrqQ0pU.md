# SEO For Beginners - The Easiest Way to Build Links

## Summary
A beginner-tier link-building explainer. Neil frames off-page SEO (links) as the
hardest-to-manipulate ranking factor and lays out three link-acquisition tactics: (1)
create visual/animated infographics (sourced cheaply via designers on Dribbble) because
people naturally link to explanatory visuals; (2) release free tools — he cites
Ubersuggest (~25,446 backlinks) and AnswerThePublic (~24,390 backlinks) as proof that
free, normally-paid-for tools attract links passively, and suggests buying a cheap
white-label tool from CodeCanyon if building a custom one isn't feasible; (3) targeted
link-begging using Ubersuggest's Backlink Opportunity report — find sites linking to 2-3
named competitors but not you, then email them with a comparison case for why your page
deserves the link, only after confirming your page is actually better.

## Key claims
- Ubersuggest has ~25,446 unique backlinks and AnswerThePublic has ~24,390 unique
  backlinks, both attributed to being free tools people naturally link to (as of
  2022-08-08, self-reported/tool-reported figures).
- Free tools that substitute for something people normally pay for generate links far
  more easily than free tools duplicating existing free offerings (as of 2022-08-08).
- Ubersuggest's Backlink Opportunity report workflow: enter your URL + 2-3 competitor
  URLs to find sites linking to competitors but not you, then targeted outreach (as of
  2022-08-08).
- Explicit rule: don't send outreach emails claiming your page is better unless it
  actually is — improve the page first (as of 2022-08-08).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Links are the hardest factor to manipulate when it comes to SEO, and without them it's almost impossible to rank at the top of Google.

> When you release a free tool, over time, people just naturally link to it. You don't got to do outreach... literally, a lot of people just naturally link to it.

> Releasing tools or infographics or content isn't good enough. You have to beg for links. Not like ask. I'm talking about literally beg.
