# How to Become a Multi-Millionaire by LOSING Money

## Summary

Solo tactical/mindset. Neil's counter-intuitive thesis: **you grow fastest by optimizing
for lifetime value (LTV), not day-one profit** — being willing to lose money up front lets
you outbid competitors for customers. He frames it via a (self-reported) piece of advice from
a **billionaire** he met when younger: "the way you become a millionaire is by losing money."
His central model ranks companies by how they treat ad spend: **seven-figure companies** want
$1 in → $2 back immediately (instant profit); **eight-figure companies** are content to
**break even** up front, knowing repeat purchases make it profitable; **nine-figure companies**
will **lose money on the first transaction** because they optimize for LTV and trust the
customer keeps coming back. Because ad costs keep rising, the only way to "grab the majority of
the inventory" is to be willing to lose money short-term. Four enablers: (1) **amazing custom
tracking** — not just default Google Analytics; know conversions/goals/revenue drivers (he
cites **Mixpanel**) — because you can't know your allowable acquisition cost without knowing
LTV; (2) **provide unexpected, above-and-beyond service** to build loyalty (Amazon Prime: free
2-day shipping, Whole Foods discounts, grocery delivery, Netflix-style video); (3) **upsells and
downsells** to raise revenue per customer (his client **Skincare by Elena**: anti-aging cream →
vitamin C serum upsell → mud-mask downsell; analogy to McDonald's "would you like fries with
that / supersize / Happy Meal"); (4) **conversion optimization** (Crazy Egg A/B testing,
Hello Bar email capture on exit, SurveyMonkey/Qualaroo surveys) so you lose less per customer.
Anchor example throughout: **Amazon operates near net-zero margin** to gobble market share and
is now "worth almost a trillion dollars."

## Key claims

- As of 2018-10-16, businesses should **optimize for LTV**, not day-one revenue — a customer's
  first payment isn't all they'll spend (repeat purchases, upsells/downsells, referrals).
- As of 2018-10-16, Neil's company-size model: **7-figure = make $2 per $1 immediately;
  8-figure = break even up front; 9-figure = willing to lose money up front**, all trusting LTV.
- As of 2018-10-16, because ad costs continually rise, **willingness to lose money short-term is
  how you win the majority of ad inventory**.
- As of 2018-10-16, enabler #1 is **custom conversion tracking** (beyond default GA; e.g.
  Mixpanel) — without knowing LTV you can't know your allowable customer-acquisition cost.
- As of 2018-10-16, **unexpected above-and-beyond service** (Amazon Prime example) builds brand
  loyalty; **upsells/downsells** raise revenue per customer; **CRO** (Crazy Egg, Hello Bar,
  SurveyMonkey, Qualaroo) reduces short-term loss.
- As of 2018-10-16, **Amazon runs at ~net-zero margin** to capture market share; Neil predicts
  it will be **worth over a trillion dollars** (self-reported/forward-looking).
