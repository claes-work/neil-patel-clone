# Social Media Strategies To Boost Your SEO [title mismatch — content is a wide-ranging cold-outreach interview on influence, platforms, and success philosophy, 2021-06-17]

## Summary
Interview with an unnamed cold-outreach interviewer/small creator (explicitly describes
having cold-emailed Neil to land the interview; used by Neil mid-interview as a live
example of his "a no just means not right now" cold-outreach doctrine). Title doesn't
match content — another instance of the channel's recurring title/content mismatch
pattern. Substantively one of the more personal, philosophy-heavy interviews in the batch.

**Content/channel strategy**: advises playing to natural strengths rather than forcing a
format — cites Gary Vaynerchuk as strong on video/audio with a team doing his text,
himself as preferring text-based content despite doing video too; states podcasting is
currently underserved/high-growth; advocates omnichannel over single-channel focus,
repurposing one format into others via team members rather than personally doing formats
he dislikes.

**Engagement/algorithm doctrine**: names **comments** (not watch-time/session duration) as
the primary signal most platform algorithms optimize around, arguing comment-driven
engagement produces duration "naturally" as a downstream effect; tactic: ask questions in
videos to prompt comments. Ranks paid-attention priority for platforms right now as
**LinkedIn first, TikTok second, YouTube third**, with Google "further down the list" than
those three — a notable, dated platform-priority ranking. Restates rule-of-seven-adjacent
frequency advice (post 5–6x/day, go live often, sell during livestreams, co-stream with
other creators for cross-audience exposure) as an answer to "how to grow from 20–30K
subscribers."

**Success/contentment philosophy (biography/beliefs ★)**: defines success as being
*content* rather than *happy* ("happiness is a state of emotion you can't maintain 24/7"),
illustrated with a hypothetical of someone who loves cooking for a living over one who
chases money/status symbols. States his own ambition isn't about money or headcount but
"I just like winning," with a stated goal of building "one of the biggest marketing
technology companies." Names **Bill Gates** as an admired figure — not for Microsoft
success but for how he deploys his wealth toward global problems (COVID, malaria,
pollution), explicitly sidestepping vaccine-related controversy ("I'm not trying to tell
people what to do"). Restates and extends the "used to have a lot of websites... wanted to
make money... learned that focusing on making money is a bad use of time" arc (dates this
website-flipping period to "10, 15 years ago," i.e. roughly 2006–2011). Gives a family-life
detail: daily walks with his wife and daughter are now his substitute for one-on-one
mentoring ("my now one-on-ones are mentoring my daughter"), and explicitly contrasts his
own low-key self-presentation ("gym shorts and a white t-shirt that has ketchup stains on
it") against the flashy-lifestyle imagery common among influencers, tying this to his
existing "don't have to flash to do well" belief.
