# Marketing is Getting More Complex, But Paid Media is Getting EASIER.

## Summary
Short Marketing School episode debating a quote Neil says his own paid-media team
pushed back on: "marketing is getting harder, but paid media is getting easier." Neil
disagrees — he argues paid media has structurally gotten *harder* since Apple's iOS
privacy changes cut off ad-platform data (citing Facebook specifically), regardless of
AI tooling improvements or lower ad spend in a soft economy making ROI easier to hit
short-term. Both hosts converge on creative strategy, not automation, as the differentiator
now that machines increasingly handle bid/campaign optimization.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Disagrees with the claim that "paid media is getting easier": people saying this are
  usually pointing to AI campaign-management tools or to reduced ad spend/competition in
  a weak economy making ROI look easier, but the structural trend is the opposite —
  paid channels got much easier to generate ROI from when platforms like Facebook had
  full behavioral data, before iOS privacy changes cut that off; he expects Google to
  face similar data restrictions eventually. Generating a positive ROI on ad spend is
  "harder to do... now than it was before." (Neil, 2023-10-16) — paid-ads.
- Argues AI/automation in ad platforms is not neutral: he believes AI-driven campaign
  tools will be tuned to make the *platform* more money, not necessarily to maximize
  advertiser ROI, drawing an analogy to Google Ads account managers (compensated partly
  on ad spend growth) versus agencies/in-house teams — in his experience, advertisers
  get better results letting an agency or in-house team manage campaigns than delegating
  optimization to Google's own staff, because the incentives differ. (Neil, 2023-10-16)
  — paid-ads / ai-marketing.
- As machines take over bid/optimization work, he argues the remaining human
  differentiator is creative strategy — AI is good at generating creative *variations*
  but not at originating a genuinely novel campaign concept (his examples: Super Bowl and
  World Cup commercial-caliber creative), which still requires a dedicated team.
  (Neil, 2023-10-16) — paid-ads / ai-marketing.
- Pushes back lightly on a concern (raised from an NP Digital team visit to a Google
  conference) that Google will eventually try to cut agencies out of the ad revenue
  chain — argues the relationship is symbiotic since agencies drive substantial ad
  revenue for Google (and receive country-specific kickbacks in some markets), so he
  isn't worried about near-term disintermediation. (Neil, 2023-10-16) — paid-ads.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> The main reason I disagree with this... just look at Facebook, it was much easier to
> generate a ROI from Facebook ads when they had all that data until iOS blocked them...
> paid used to be much easier when these platforms had so much data.

> Your job from paid marketing is you spend a dollar and you need to make more. And it's
> harder to do that now than it was before.

> What AI sucks at is creating that amazing strategy... It's hard to do that kind of
> stuff through AI versus having a team really focus on doing something unique.

> [On Google managing agency clients' campaigns directly] Nine out of 10 times when you
> work with the agency... people get better results dealing with the agency or doing it
> in-house versus letting Google help them manage their campaigns. And it's because it's
> different incentives.
