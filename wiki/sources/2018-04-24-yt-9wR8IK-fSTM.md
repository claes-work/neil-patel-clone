# How I Got 100,000 Subscribers in Less Than a Year

## Summary

In this solo video, Neil Patel recounts how his YouTube channel surpassed 100,000 subscribers in under a year and lays out a six-tip playbook for replicating that growth. He frames marketing as such a small niche that "if I can do it, anyone can," and reports roughly 9,000 new organic subscribers per month without paid ads. His tips center on posting consistency (ideally three videos per week), on-platform YouTube SEO (attractive/animated thumbnails to lift click-through rate, keyword-optimized titles and tags, and videos longer than five minutes to boost watch time and rankings), showing emotion and high energy driven by genuine passion, manually transcribing every video and uploading the transcript for a stronger ranking boost than auto-captions (a tactic he credits to "Adam from viewership"), aggressively promoting each video within the first 24–48 hours because YouTube rewards early performance (the inverse of traditional SEO's slow compounding), and explicitly asking viewers for engagement (comments, likes, shares, subscribes) to build community and compounding subscriber growth.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-04-24, Neil states his YouTube channel has over 100,000 subscribers, gained in less than one year.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil claims he gains over 9,000 subscribers per month organically, without spending any money on paid ads.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil advises being consistent and shooting for ideally three videos per week, which he calls "the magic sweet spot" for consistent growth.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil says thumbnails must be attractive — not just a still image from the video, but animated/funny type images — because YouTube looks at click-through rate.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil advises optimizing videos via the title (with keywords), tags, and length; videos should ideally be more than five minutes because YouTube likes keeping people on the platform, and longer watch time raises rankings and suggestions.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil notes his early YouTube videos were one or two minutes long, and that most of his videos are now more than five minutes.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil recommends researching competitors' tags/videos by searching related keywords on YouTube and making better content than what they are doing.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil advises showcasing emotion, entertainment, high energy, and passion; he says if you don't love your topic you should make something else, because genuine passion produces natural high energy.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil advises transcribing every video and manually uploading the transcript to YouTube, which he says gives a bigger ranking boost than YouTube's automatic captions; he credits "Adam from viewership" for this tip and cites rev.com as a service that transcribes for about a dollar (stating he is not affiliated and gets no commission).
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil says transcripts should be uploaded from day one, not three months after publishing (though doing it late is still better than not at all).
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil claims YouTube is the opposite of traditional SEO: whereas traditional SEO rankings increase over time with updated content, YouTube judges a video mainly on its first 48 hours (and especially the first 24), so a strong launch tends to perform well for the video's lifespan while a launch that bombs is hard to recover.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil advises pushing each new video hard on release — sending it to your email list and via push notifications — so it gets early traction and keeps ranking.
- As of 2018-04-24, Neil's sixth tip is to ask for engagement within videos (prompting comments, likes, shares, and subscribes), which builds community and compounds subscriber growth.
