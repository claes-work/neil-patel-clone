# How To Find The Best AI Marketers To Work With

## Summary
Neil describes two sourcing tactics NP Digital uses to find strong AI-marketing
talent: (1) engaging people who already publicly talk about AI marketing (on blogs,
podcasts, Twitter/Instagram) in genuine conversation about their specific tactics
first, hiring conversations second, to both learn and identify who actually gets
results; and (2) scouring LinkedIn for employees at companies known for AI tools and
interviewing them about how they'd help a marketing org adopt/automate with AI —
noting some of the best hires for this purpose are toolbuilders/engineers, not
people with a "marketer" title. He also describes NP Digital's soft recruiting
approach on LinkedIn (asking "do you know anyone like you looking for a job?" rather
than a direct pitch) as a relationship-building tactic that avoids scaring off
strong passive candidates.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Tactic: identify people already publicly discussing AI marketing (blogs, podcasts,
  social) — full-time employees, freelancers, and hobbyists alike — and engage them
  in genuine tactical conversation ("what are you doing for automating keyword
  research / bid management?") rather than opening with a hiring pitch; this both
  teaches you and surfaces who actually gets results, who you then pursue as
  contractor, freelancer, or full-time hire. (Neil, 2023-11-13) — ai-marketing /
  agency-entrepreneurship.
- Tactic: scour LinkedIn for employees at companies that build well-known AI tools,
  and interview them on how they'd help a marketing organization streamline,
  automate, and adopt AI — noting some of the strongest hires for this purpose are
  toolbuilders/engineers rather than people with a marketing title, since AI
  adoption is fundamentally a technology-leverage problem. (Neil, 2023-11-13) —
  ai-marketing / agency-entrepreneurship [self-reported practice].
- ★ NP Digital recruiting practice: on LinkedIn, avoid a direct "we want to hire you"
  pitch — instead ask "do you know anyone like you who'd want a job?" as a
  lower-pressure opener that doesn't scare off strong passive candidates; relationship
  -building precedes closing. (Neil, 2023-11-13) — agency-entrepreneurship
  [self-reported].

## Notable verbatim quotes
