# Express bankruptcy: 100 stores closing amid investor rescue efforts, & Mark Zuckerberg's brand shift

## Summary
Opens with Eric telling a personal story (missed a job interview at Express in college
after eating an edible the night before) and a tangent on why neither host drinks
alcohol (Neil: developed headaches/allergic-type reaction after a long COVID-lockdown
break from drinking, decided to stop entirely). Moves to Express's Chapter 11 bankruptcy
and acquisition of its assets by WHP Global for a reported ~$35M commitment, contrasted
with the ~$25M Express paid for Bonobos from Walmart the prior year — framed as
"mismanagement" and a "best time to buy" distressed-asset environment. This leads into
an extended explanation of Neil's actual M&A due-diligence process at NP Digital: before
discussing valuation, he asks about founder intentions (stay on or exit), lead-generation
sources, management-team depth, client concentration, and "recurring" revenue quality —
illustrated with two anecdotes: (1) an agency claiming "recurring" revenue that turned
out to mean a client paid them 3 times in 7 years, and had $800K/year of personal
expenses run through the business, asking for a $10-12M valuation Neil calls
unrealistic; (2) a data/analytics-strong founder NP Digital tried to acquire who
declined, saying she wasn't interested in selling unless given $20M cash and 2 months
off/year for her team — which Neil finds contradictory with her earlier claim of not
caring about money. Neil also describes a self-devised interview question ("what are you
world-class at, not business-related") used to gauge how candidates communicate and
think, contrasted with a hire (David Shapiro) who correctly answered a spot-created
link-building scenario question about a life-insurance site and got hired on the spot.
Neil states his interview "no" rate is roughly 10-20% by the time candidates reach him
(most are pre-vetted), with a self-reported 90%+ success rate for executive hires
specifically, largely sourced via referrals and people who worked for competitors and
got promoted. Closes on Mark Zuckerberg's 2024 image/brand shift (chain, mustache,
Jiu-Jitsu, AI "llama" content, a viral Threads exchange with Priscilla Chan about a
backyard octagon) as a case study in personal-brand "warmth" and relatability.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Usually, someone comes to me and says like, Neil, you should check out our business...
> before we even get into valuation we ask some questions... how long have you been in
> business, how do you generate your leads, have you built out a management team, are
> you the CEO running it, are you looking to stay on board, what's your client
> concentration, what's your churn rate... we tend not to do deals where founders don't
> stay — that's a key lesson for everyone, agencies are so driven by the founder.

> A dangerous thing in business is believing... that you know everything and you can
> just get whatever you want done and you can do everything on your own.

> [interview question] Neil, what's something you're an expert at — so it could be like
> playing the French horn or poker, but not business related... I believe I'm an expert
> at marketing. Okay, tell me how do you become an expert in marketing. You become an
> expert in marketing by doing it for a long, long time and having hours of trial and
> error, failing tons of times, and figuring out how to fix all the problems that you
> created by doing things the wrong way.
