# If You Have ZERO Website Visitors, Do THIS First | Optimizing a New Website For Organic Traffic

## Summary
A three-step cold-start SEO framework: (1) identify winnable, intent-matched keywords
via Ubersuggest's "Keywords I Can Rank For" filter; (2) study competitors via the
Backlinks report and its Backlink Opportunity sub-report (sites linking to multiple
named competitors but not you — the best outreach targets), plus the Top Pages report
to find content gaps, while explicitly rejecting competitor pages outside your own
service scope (his example: not creating a "video editing" article just because
competitors rank for it, since neither he nor they actually sell video-editing
services); (3) go deep and narrow rather than broad. The niching argument is
illustrated with a **first-person narrative of neilpatel.com's own topical expansion
sequence** — starting purely as an SEO blog, then broadening in order to content
marketing, social media marketing, paid advertising, conversion optimization, and email
marketing, with a mention that he was, as of this recording, starting to write about TV
advertising (Hulu, Roku) as a further expansion. This sequenced expansion narrative is a
more explicit, ordered account of the site's content-scope growth than has been logged
before — useful for content-strategy/biography.
