# 6 Actionable Steps For Better Rankings With Small Websites

## Summary
A six-part ranking playbook aimed at small/new sites without backlinks or content
volume. (1) Local SEO — a five-step local checklist: claim Google My Business, add
photos, optimize the site (dedicated location/contact pages with address + phone +
schema markup), claim other profiles (Yelp, TripAdvisor, Facebook), and collect
testimonials/video reviews. (2) Video — invest in YouTube as "the second biggest search
engine after Google and Google Images," citing SimilarWeb data that YouTube is growing
in popularity while Facebook traffic has declined for two years. (3) Affordable quality
content — hire budget freelance writers via ProBlogger (cites $20–$200 per piece),
using Ubersuggest's content-ideas tool and detailed outlines (1,000–2,000 words) to
brief writers. (4) Target long-tail keywords big brands ignore — example: "what foods
are high in fiber" (SEO difficulty 15, 14,800 monthly searches); cites WordStream that
average long-tail conversion is 36% vs. 11.45% for the top 10% of landing pages overall.
(5) Target topics via content clusters — pillar pages + interlinked long-tail articles,
citing Moz's "Beginner's Guide to SEO" as an example. (6) In-depth, authoritative
content — cites an Ezoic study that small/medium sites competing with Alexa top-1000
sites saw a 34% average organic-traffic increase in 2018 while publishing ~1/8th as
much new content, adding 1,312+ new organic keywords; attributes part of this to
Google's 2019 diversity update capping any one domain at two SERP slots per keyword.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-04-27, Neil said YouTube is "the second biggest search engine after Google
  and Google Images," citing SimilarWeb data that Facebook traffic had been declining
  for the prior two years while YouTube's popularity grew.
- As of 2020-04-27, Neil cited WordStream: average long-tail-keyword conversion rate is
  36%, vs. 11.45% for the top 10% of landing pages generally — used to argue long-tail
  targeting is disproportionately effective for small sites.
- As of 2020-04-27, Neil cited an Ezoic study: in 2018, small/medium sites competing
  with Alexa top-1000 sites saw a 34% average increase in organic traffic while
  publishing about 1/8th as much new content, adding 1,312+ new organic keywords over
  the year.
- As of 2020-04-27, Neil attributed part of small-site opportunity to Google's 2019
  "diversity update," which caps a single domain at two SERP positions per keyword
  (excluding branded terms) — consistent with his framing of the same update in
  [[wiki/sources/2020-03-30-yt-totiNw9Rypc]].
