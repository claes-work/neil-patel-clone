# Drive More Traffic And Sales By Fixing This Website ERROR | The 404 Hack

## Summary
Neil explains that every website accumulates 404 error pages — caused by deleted pages or inbound links pointing to URLs that no longer exist — and that these errors grow as a site gets bigger. Rather than treating them as pure loss, he frames 404s as recoverable traffic. His method: open Google Search Console, review all 404 error pages, and 301 redirect each to the most relevant existing page (for example, an old deleted SEO post redirected to your latest SEO post). For unknown or random URLs that can't be sensibly redirected, he says to use the 404 page itself as a conversion surface — linking out to popular articles, product and service pages, and even prompting visitors to become email subscribers — so that "dead traffic" still produces traffic, sales, and revenue.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-19, Neil states:
- Every website has 404 error pages, and the bigger a site gets the more errors visitors will encounter (as of 2017-09-19).
- 404s occur because a page that once existed was deleted, or because someone linked to the wrong page or a page that doesn't exist (as of 2017-09-19).
- You should open Google Search Console, review all 404 error pages, and 301 redirect them to the most relevant page to keep capturing that traffic (as of 2017-09-19).
- A deleted post (e.g. an old SEO post people still visit) should be 301 redirected to your latest, new post on the same topic (as of 2017-09-19).
- For unknown/random URLs people type in that can't be redirected, the 404 page itself should link out to popular articles, products, and services that drive sales and conversions (as of 2017-09-19).
- You can even ask visitors to become an email subscriber on your 404 error page (as of 2017-09-19).
- Doing this lets you make money from otherwise "dead traffic" — more traffic and more sales (as of 2017-09-19).
