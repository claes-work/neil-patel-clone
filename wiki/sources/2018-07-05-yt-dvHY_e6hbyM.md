# Quora Hack: Secret Tips to Get More Visitors to Your Website

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode (Neil with interviewer Adam from Viewership) on generating traffic
from Quora. Neil opens with the autobiographical note that Quora **repeatedly banned and
reinstated him** (for alleged plagiarism of his own neilpatel.com content) before finally
leaving him in. His evolution of tactics: **(1)** Manually answer every industry-relevant
question to build authority — generated branding and some leads but not enough traffic, and
wasn't scalable. **(2)** Have his team repurpose relevant excerpts from his existing blog
posts into Quora answers with links back when it made sense — worked "amazingly well" (this
triggered the plagiarism bans). **(3, best)** Copy/paste blog content that already answers a
question, link back, AND add a few sentences of unique content just for the Quora audience,
done consistently. On upvotes: don't try to game them (they tried asking social followers to
upvote); instead find the most popular questions, give a more in-depth answer than the top
answer, link when relevant, and you'll naturally climb. He notes you won't make millions
from Quora, and you can alternatively run Quora ads (better for awareness than direct sales).
Adam adds a community-marketing coda: the more genuine value you give, the more you get; spam
gets blocked everywhere.
