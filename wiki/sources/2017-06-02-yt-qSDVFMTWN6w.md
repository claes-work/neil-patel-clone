# How to Increase Your Search Rankings Without Link Building

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that while link building is the primary lever for improving search rankings, it is not the only one, noting that Google's algorithm weighs over 200 factors (as characterized in 2017). He focuses on click-through rate (CTR) as a rankings factor driven by user signals: if searchers consistently click a lower listing over a higher one, Google will reorder results to keep users satisfied and prevent them from switching search engines. To exploit this without building links, he advises improving the organic title and description — controlled by the title tag and meta description — by using click-inducing words (get, now, try, fast, free) and time markers such as "updated in 2017," which he claims lifts his listings over a month or two above higher-ranked competitors. To find effective copy, he recommends Googling a target phrase and studying the top paid ads, reasoning that AdWords rewards high-CTR ad copy (via total expected revenue, not just bid) so the top-placed ads tend to have the most appealing titles and descriptions worth emulating in organic listings.

## Key claims
- Link building is the main way to increase search rankings but not the only way; Google's algorithm has over 200 factors (as of 2017-06-02).
- Click-through rate is one of the main factors affecting rankings; Google reorders listings based on user signals showing which result users prefer (as of 2017-06-02).
- If a lower-ranked result gets more clicks than the one above it, Google will "flip them around" to keep results relevant and avoid users defecting to another search engine (as of 2017-06-02).
- You can raise rankings without links by creating a more appealing title and description, set via the title tag and meta description (which a webmaster can change) (as of 2017-06-02).
- Effective titles/descriptions use click-driving words such as get, now, try, fast, and free (as of 2017-06-02).
- Adding a date phrase like "updated in 2017" earns Neil more clicks than the listings above him, and over a month or two his listings climb and outrank them (as of 2017-06-02).
- To find phrases that work, Google the target keyword and study the paid ads, because AdWords placement depends on ad-copy appeal (CTR), not just the bid (as of 2017-06-02).
- Google favors the ad with higher total revenue: a $2.50 bid getting 3x the clicks ($7.50 expected) is placed above a $5 bid ($5 expected), so top-placed ads typically have the most appealing copy (as of 2017-06-02).
- Mirroring the titles and descriptions of the top paid ads in your free (organic) listings helps you climb the organic rankings (as of 2017-06-02).
