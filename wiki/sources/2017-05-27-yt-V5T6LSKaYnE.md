# Here's How to Get Blog Readers to Buy Products and Services - How I Make Money Blogging

## Summary
Neil Patel explains how to convert blog readers into paying customers, framing it as a set of simple, repeatable tactics he has used for years (as of 2017). He argues that traffic alone is useless unless readers are turned into customers, and that the path there is building a connection with readers rather than just publishing content. His tactics: write in a conversational tone using the words "you" and "I" so the post feels like a dialogue rather than a lecture; make posts skimmable with subheadings, broken-up text, and paragraphs no longer than five or six lines; close each post with a conclusion that summarizes the takeaways and ends with a question to spark comments; respond to every comment to deepen the connection; and link to your own products and services within posts, including case-study posts that show the results people get from those products. He cites Derek Halpern of Social Triggers as someone who converts readers well by writing case studies. The through-line is that engagement and connection make readers far more likely to buy.

## Key claims
- Blog traffic and readers are worthless on their own; the goal is converting those readers into customers (as of 2017-05-27).
- Writing in a conversational tone using the words "you" and "I" makes a post feel like a conversation rather than talking at readers, which keeps them engaged (as of 2017-05-27).
- Posts should be made skimmable using subheadings and broken-up text, with no more than five or six lines per paragraph, because longer paragraphs become overwhelming and hard to read (as of 2017-05-27).
- Every post should end with a conclusion that summarizes what the reader should have learned, and that conclusion should close with a question to drive comments and engagement (as of 2017-05-27).
- Responding to reader comments builds a connection, and readers who feel connected to you are far more likely to become customers (as of 2017-05-27).
- You should link to your products and services within blog posts, and can write case-study posts showing the results people get from using those products and services, which generates more customers (as of 2017-05-27).
- Derek Halpern of Social Triggers converts readers into customers effectively by frequently writing case studies (as of 2017-05-27).
- Neil started a blog to generate customers after early jobs cleaning toilets and picking up trash at a theme park, having started with no money (biographical, as of 2017-05-27).
