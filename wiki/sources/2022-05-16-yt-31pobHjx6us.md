# 7 Beginner Mistakes That Are DESTROYING Your Social Media Engagement

## Summary
Neil lists seven engagement mistakes on social media (as of 2022-05-16), framing
engagement as the signal platforms use to decide whether to show content to
followers at all. (1) Not responding to DMs right away — cites Khoros data that ~75%
of users expect a private-message response on Twitter/Facebook, 68% expect a
public-comment response, and 73% expect a brand response on Instagram. (2) Not
responding to comments — frames social as reciprocation, recommends replying within
an hour where possible (up to half a day/a day is tolerable, less is better). (3) Not
learning from past successes/failures — track analytics, do more of what resonates,
less of what doesn't. (4) Self-serving content — keep self-promotion to roughly 5-10%
of output; says he personally promotes NP Digital at the end of some videos but that
it's a minority of content. (5) Too much selling — engage and build loyalty first,
since users don't go to social media to buy. (6) Not acknowledging story mentions —
reciprocate when followers tag/mention the brand. (7) Posting too much or not enough —
recommends finding the right cadence by watching engagement trends; suggests at least
once every other day as a floor and once a day as a starting recommendation.

## Key claims
- Social platforms use engagement to decide whether to distribute content to
  followers at all — posting alone doesn't guarantee reach (as of 2022-05-16).
- Per Khoros, ~75% of users expect a response to a private message on Twitter/
  Facebook, 68% expect a response to a public comment/tweet, and 73% expect a brand
  response on Instagram whether reached privately or publicly (cited stat, as of
  2022-05-16).
- Self-promotional content should be roughly 5-10% of total output; Neil says he
  personally keeps it to "a few percentage" of his own videos (self-reported, as of
  2022-05-16).
- Recommended baseline posting cadence: at least once every other day as a floor,
  once a day as a starting recommendation, adjusted up or down based on observed
  engagement (as of 2022-05-16).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Engagement is one of the key factors that pretty much all social media channels use to measure and determine whether or not they should show up your content to your followers.

> Not replying to comments can give people the impression that your brand just doesn't care for them.

> Make it about your audience, not about your own product and service... that should only be like five or 10% of the time.

> Users don't go to social media because they want to buy stuff... engage first, build that loyalty because then it's much easier to sell.
