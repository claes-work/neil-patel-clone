# The Surprising Truth About Email Marketing in 2018

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode with co-host **Adam (from Viewership)**, answering viewer Radoslav,
who doubts email marketing still works given spam filters and low engagement. Neil argues
email is NOT going away — businesses still run on email, and Gmail Ads revenue is growing —
so an email that reaches the inbox and gets read can generate sales. His playbook for
inbox placement: **(1) Only email people who want it.** Aggressive pop-ups that trick
people into a list hurt you; he praises GDPR's explicit opt-in tick box — conversions drop
but the people who tick it genuinely want your emails, raising open rates. **(2) Scrub
your list.** Above ~100,000 subscribers open rates start "tanking"; he cleans his list
monthly and cites just deleting 45,000 addresses (bounces/unsubscribes/non-buyers). Gmail
and Outlook weight engagement heavily, so removing non-openers raises deliverability and
pushes you from the Promotions tab to the inbox. He notes ConvertKit auto-cleans while (he
believes) MailChimp does not — implying MailChimp profits from billing inactive contacts.
**(3) Keep emails simple** — text-based, not template/image/video-heavy (which triggers
Promotions), max three links (he uses one), white-list via services like Return Path (AOL
white-lists; Gmail/Outlook don't), and use casual, friend-like subject lines. Adam adds:
always add value, don't spam. Neil notes his emails are all content (four a week max), and
recounts that a single monetized email (with help from **Ryan Deiss**) generated "well
into six figures / ~$100-something thousand" — after which he felt bad and reverted to
giving it away free.
