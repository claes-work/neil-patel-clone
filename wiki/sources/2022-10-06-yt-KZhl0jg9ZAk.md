# 7 Ways For Your Co-workers to Help You With Marketing

## Summary
A playbook for turning coworkers/employees into a marketing channel, framed around
needing continual reminders since one-off participation doesn't stick. Seven tactics:
(1) ask the team for social engagement on company posts — especially comments, which he
says weigh more on the algorithm than likes/shares — coordinated via Slack/Microsoft
Teams; (2) encourage employees to talk about the company on their own social profiles,
optionally via a referral-program incentive (framed as "another version of an affiliate
program"); (3) let employees use paid company time to become brand ambassadors at
conferences/events, gated on them actually speaking and promoting the company; (4)
recruit employee bloggers for higher-authority, expert-voice content, sourced via
"ProBlogger" (transcript renders "ProbLog") or "Upwork" (renders "Upper"), with a
part-time editor to polish it; (5) branded swag employees actually want to wear, framed
as a long-ROI "walking billboard"; (6) employee reviews on platforms that allow them,
citing a 95%-of-consumers-read-reviews stat (Findstack); (7) embed marketing
participation into company culture itself as the hardest-but-highest-leverage lever.
