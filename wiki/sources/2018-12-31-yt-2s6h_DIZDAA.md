# The Difference Between Ranking on Google vs YouTube | Neil Patel

## Summary

A comparison of Google SEO vs. YouTube SEO, arguing you can rank on both for far more
search traffic. Five differences: (1) **Speed** — YouTube videos typically rank in the
first 24–48 hours and how a video does on day one dictates its lifetime performance;
Google takes 3–6 months, usually over a year, and the first year sets long-term fate. (2)
**Authority** — both platforms weigh authority, but Google authority compounds as you
build links (steady climb year over year), whereas YouTube authority fluctuates
month-to-month: a run of dud videos tanks it and drags down all your videos, so you must
continually create amazing content and engagement to hold it. (3) **Ranking factors** —
Google ranks on links (find sites linking to competitors via Ahrefs' Link Intersect and
do outreach), while YouTube ranks on engagement and watch time, including session watch
time across other videos. (4) **Engagement type** — Google wants dwell/stickiness (people
staying and not bouncing back to results), while YouTube counts view time, comments,
likes, even dislikes, and rewards continued watching across the platform. (5) **Difficulty**
— YouTube is comparatively easy and "wide open" (results in ~30 days), Google is hard; a
brand-new site plus new YouTube channel would likely get more search traffic from YouTube
over the next two years.
