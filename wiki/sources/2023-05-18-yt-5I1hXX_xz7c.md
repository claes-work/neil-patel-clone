# My Secrets To Creating an SEO Friendly Website

## Summary
Formal-intro interview ("you are the marketer's marketer") opening on Q4/holiday sales
tactics, then pivoting into a full walkthrough of Neil's SEO-first growth philosophy. His
Q4 playbook: raise ad spend despite rising Q4 CPMs, but first add upsells/downsells/checkout
bumps to lift average order value, and cross-promote with complementary (non-competing)
creators/brands in the same audience. He then gives a **four-step SEO methodology**: (1) keyword
research via a free tool (Ubersuggest, his own product) to find a "laundry list" of relevant
terms, then publish content in a dedicated `/blog` folder; (2) distribute that content across
social channels (Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest, LinkedIn — explicitly **not** Instagram, which
he calls poor for content promotion); (3) build links by citing/linking out to other sites
in his own content, then emailing those sites to notify them (framed as reciprocity, not an
expectation of a link back) — he uses a Google-as-voting-system analogy (a link = a vote,
weighted by the linking site's relevance/authority, à la a political endorsement's weight
depending on the endorser); (4) fix technical/code issues so Google can crawl the site,
pointing to Ubersuggest's free Site Audit tool. He states a **concrete SEO results timeline**:
noticeable movement within 3 months, momentum at 6 months, real results within a year, and a
compounding "off to the races" snowball by year three — and frames the main reason businesses
quit SEO as impatience (giving up around the 6-month mark, just before payoff). He shares his
own numbers: **10M monthly organic visits** to neilpatel.com, of which **~180K/month** are
branded searches for his own name — a byproduct, he says, of years of non-branded SEO traffic
building the brand, not the starting point. Extends the "great equalizer" framing of SEO
(and, similarly, organic social) to small businesses/creators without ad budgets, and lists
his ranked picks for lower-cost paid-traffic alternatives to Google/Facebook: **Snapchat →
TikTok → Pinterest → LinkedIn** (LinkedIn priced highest but B2B-effective); says
Snapchat/TikTok/Pinterest skew B2C, while Taboola/Outbrain work across both B2B and B2C and
essentially any industry. Closes on what separates great marketers from average ones in a
saturated, omnichannel-required landscape: **creativity of concept**, not creative execution
— illustrated by his own decision to give away a paid-tool-equivalent product for free
(Ubersuggest) rather than pay an estimated $1.5-2M/month to bid on his own industry's
keywords.

  (excluding Instagram) → reciprocal outbound-linking/outreach → technical/crawlability fixes
  (self-reported methodology, dated 2023-05-18).
