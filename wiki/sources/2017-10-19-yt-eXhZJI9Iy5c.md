# How to Choose the Right Color Schemes for Your Brand or Website | Conversion Optimization Tips

## Summary
Neil walks through how to pick a color scheme for a website in this short solo tactical video. He opens by acknowledging that different colors carry different associations (green with nature and organic, red with urgency and stopping, black with luxury), but pushes back on the common advice that specific colors guarantee the most conversions and sales, calling that advice wrong. Instead he argues brands should choose colors that genuinely resemble the brand and product, because a mismatch will not flow, will fail to connect with people, and will show through in the web design. He then lays out three further principles: colors need contrast so call-to-action buttons stand out from the main design rather than blending in; once a main color is chosen, free color wheels on the web can take a primary color and suggest complementary colors to use throughout the design; and everything, especially call-to-action button colors, must be tested rather than assumed, so the choices that drive the most clicks and sales can be identified. He closes by recommending Crazy Egg's A/B testing feature to try different colors and see which ones most impact conversion rate.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-19, Neil states:
- Different colors carry different associations: green signals nature/organic/kind, red signals urgency and "stop," and black resembles luxury (as of 2017-10-19).
- The common advice that you must use specific colors to generate the most conversions and sales is wrong (as of 2017-10-19).
- You should pick whatever colors resemble your brand and product; colors that do not match the brand will not flow, will not connect with people, and that disconnect will come across in the web design (as of 2017-10-19).
- Colors need contrast so that call-to-action buttons stand out from the main design; if everything blends in, the site will not perform well (as of 2017-10-19).
- After choosing a main color, free color wheels available on the web let you input your primary color and get complementary colors to use throughout the design (as of 2017-10-19).
- Color choices, especially on call-to-action buttons, must be tested rather than assumed, since your personal color preference is not always what audiences respond to (as of 2017-10-19).
- Testing lets you pick the colors that cause the most clicks and sales (as of 2017-10-19).
- Crazy Egg's A/B testing feature can be used to test different colors and see which ones most impact conversion rate (as of 2017-10-19).
