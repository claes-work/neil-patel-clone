# 4 Daily SEO Tasks That You Need to Do (And That Produce Results)

## Summary
Solo screen-share tutorial describing Neil's own **personal daily SEO routine** — four
recurring tasks, demoed live in Ubersuggest and Google Search Console using his own
properties (NP Digital, neilpatel.com). Opens with the framing stat: Google makes over 8
algorithm changes per day on average, hence the need for continual (not one-time) SEO
maintenance. (1) Check rankings: sets up an NP Digital tracking project (keywords:
company name, "SEO company," "paid media agency," "advertising agency," "advertising
agency SEO"; competitor tracked: QuickSprout; daily + mobile rank tracking); reviews his
existing neilpatel.com rank history over a 30-day window; for any keyword trending down
(example: ranking #8 for "SEO company"), Googles the term live and studies the
higher-ranking pages (names seo.com — dismisses it as an exact-match-domain outlier —
Ignite Visibility, and Clutch as examples) to figure out what to improve. (2) Continual
keyword research: spends "two, three minutes" daily in Ubersuggest's keyword-overview
tool finding new keyword variants around seed terms (demoed with "digital marketing" →
"digital marketing company," "digital marketing firms," "digital marketing webinars"),
adding them to a saved list — states "SEO is all about keywords... the more keywords you
have, the better off you're going to do, assuming you actually take time to create
content around those." (3) Regular site audits via Ubersuggest's crawler: reviews
critical errors (e.g., slow-loading pages — routes those to his team since they're
server-related) versus warnings/recommendations he can fix himself (e.g., a page missing
an H1 tag). (4) Google Search Console review: works through enhancement reports
(breadcrumbs, AMP, FAQ) looking for errors, aiming to reduce "red" (errors) and "yellow"
(warnings) over time. Closes with the philosophy that no single SEO factor drives
rankings — it's the accumulation of many small, consistently-maintained things — and
plugs NP Digital.
