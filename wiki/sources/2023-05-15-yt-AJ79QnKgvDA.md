# Webinars for Sales Funnel: A Guide to Understanding the Ins and Outs

## Summary
Interview-format episode (opens mid-conversation, no formal intro — likely a topic-cut clip
from the same taped conversation as [[2023-05-18-yt-5I1hXX_xz7c]]) covering three threads:
whether founders should run marketing in-house or hire an agency, agency compensation
models, and the long-form-webinar sales formula. On in-house vs. agency: Neil says NP
Digital gets 60-70K inbound leads/month it follows up on (200K+ more it doesn't), and his
advice is to **hire cheap/contract and do it in-house while "figuring things out"** — agency
overhead only pays off once a business already has a working internal team and is trying to
double/triple scale, because agencies bring platform relationships and cross-account pattern
recognition that speed up growth. On compensation: no universal right model (retainer vs.
%-of-ad-spend vs. %-of-profit) — the test is whether the numbers pencil out to profitability
for the client, and %-of-spend should decrease as spend scales (managing $1M/month doesn't
cost 10x what managing $100K/month costs). On building an info-product funnel from scratch:
his stated first move is to study existing webinar formulas (names **Russell Brunson**,
**Russ Ruffino**, and **Sam Evans** as reference points, explicitly not endorsing the "biz
op" niche some of them operate in), build a webinar with cheap tools (Webinar Jam, PowerPoint
+ QuickTime recording), then mine **Facebook's Ad Library** to copy competitors' working ad
angles. He calls the long-form (often ~90-minute) webinar still the highest-converting format
for cold-traffic info-product sales specifically because it substitutes for a live salesperson
— building rapport and handling objections across multiple "closes" in one session — and says
so "sadly," since it takes far more effort than a landing page. On what separates viral
breakout creators from everyone else, he argues it's mostly **luck and timing**, not
replicable formula or authenticity, using Logan/Jake Paul and Kylie Jenner as examples of
being "early enough" to a platform/format; he pushes back on the idea that sustained fame
(the Kardashians) isn't a skill, calling multi-year attention-and-monetization retention
"brilliance" as a marketer/business person. On paid shout-out/follow-for-follow growth
tactics, he calls them actively harmful: Instagram's algorithm weights engagement rate, so a
follower spike from a giveaway (low ongoing engagement) signals "something's wrong with your
content" to the platform and suppresses future reach — the fix is **live video**, which
platforms push hard because it competes with linear TV, and which builds genuine rapport with
the *right* (engaged) followers rather than maximizing raw follower count.

## Key claims
- NP Digital handles 60-70K inbound leads/month it acts on plus 200K+ more it does not,
  used as evidence for his hire-in-house-first/agency-later sequencing advice (self-reported,
  dated 2023-05-15).
- Agency %-of-ad-spend pricing should decrease as spend scales, because managing a larger
  budget doesn't require proportionally more staff — cites NP Digital clients spending
  "80, a hundred million dollars a year" on Google ads as the top of that scale (self-reported,
  dated 2023-05-15).
- States the long-form webinar remains the highest-converting format for cold-traffic
  info-product sales because it functions as a compressed, one-sided sales conversation with
  multiple embedded objection-handling "closes" (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-05-15).
- Argues breakout social-media success is driven mainly by luck and timing rather than a
  replicable formula, and that copying an earlier breakout's exact tactics now (e.g. the Paul
  brothers' early content style) would no longer work due to changed algorithms and
  saturation (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-05-15).
- Calls follower-count-inflating tactics (giveaways, follow-swaps) counterproductive because
  Instagram's algorithm reads low post-giveaway engagement as a signal to suppress future
  reach; recommends multi-platform live video/co-streaming with peers as the better growth
  tactic instead (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-05-15).
