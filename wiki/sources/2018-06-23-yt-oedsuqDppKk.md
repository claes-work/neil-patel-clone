# Why You Should STOP Reading and START Doing

## Summary

A solo mindset monologue. Neil admits he doesn't read that often, then argues that
knowledge without application is worthless. He describes a class of people he calls
"learners" — always reading, attending meetings, listening to podcasts, quoting books
from Warren Buffett's annual meeting — whose businesses nonetheless underperform because
all they do is consume. His thesis: knowledge is priceless, but the **application** step
is the most important part; get up and execute on what you've read. He uses his friend
Eric (Eric Siu, his Marketing School co-host) as a gentle example — someone who does lots
of meetings, networking, books and dinners but needs to execute more on his business.
Neil contrasts this with one of his own businesses that reached **over $4M in annual
revenue in 7 months and 8 days**, crediting a culture that reads/learns (Friday lunch-and-
learns) but primarily EXECUTES. He closes by inviting viewers struggling to execute to
comment so he can help.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-06-23, Neil says he does not read often; when he does he reads The Economist,
  TechCrunch, crypto sites, and marketing sites like Search Engine Land.
- As of 2018-06-23, Neil argues that "learners" who only read/consume tend to have
  slower-growing businesses; knowledge must be APPLIED, and application is the most
  important part of learning.
- As of 2018-06-23, Neil claims one of his businesses hit **over $4 million in annual
  revenue within 7 months and 8 days**, attributing it to a team that executes (while
  also doing weekly lunch-and-learns).
- As of 2018-06-23, Neil references his friend Eric (Marketing School co-host Eric Siu) as
  someone who does a lot of meetings/networking/reading but needs to execute more on his
  business.
