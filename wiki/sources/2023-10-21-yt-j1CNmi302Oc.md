# How to measure the ROI of your content efforts in 2023

## Summary
Neil lays out his practical method for measuring content-marketing ROI in a post-GA4
world where organic keyword data is increasingly hidden: (1) UTM parameters as a
rough hack (acknowledging it's the "wrong" — i.e., not the textbook — use of UTMs),
(2) running CTA A/B tests on his highest-traffic pages and rolling winning CTAs out
site-wide, and (3) tracking which content topics/sources produce the most Salesforce
leads and closes, then doubling down on those topics rather than spreading into
overlapping "beginner's guide" variants that confuse Google's ranking signals.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Uses UTM parameters as a quick-and-dirty ROI hack, while noting this isn't the
  "correct" (i.e., intended-for-external-only) use of UTMs — a pragmatic workaround
  for teams without heavy analytics resources. (Neil, 2023-10-21) — analytics-cro.
- Primary ROI method: run A/B tests on his most-trafficked blog pages, testing
  different calls-to-action (service links, product links, sidebar/inline links),
  then roll the winning CTA out across all posts; only feasible on top posts given
  he has 6,000+ blog posts total. (Neil, 2023-10-21) — content-marketing /
  analytics-cro.
- Cross-references Salesforce lead data against the content piece a lead first read,
  to find not just which content drove leads but which drove closed deals — and
  produces more content on those specific topics rather than volume for its own
  sake. Considers this more important than ROI measurement itself. (Neil, 2023-10-21)
  — content-marketing.
- Explicit anti-cannibalization rule: if "beginner's tips to SEO" already drives
  revenue, he won't also create "newbies guide to SEO" or "dummies guide to SEO" —
  duplicative near-identical content confuses Google about which page should rank.
  Instead he creates adjacent-but-distinct content (e.g., SEO for global expansion,
  link building via infographics). (Neil, 2023-10-21) — seo / content-marketing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> The quickest way that I found to measure the ROI of my content marketing is A, UTM
> parameters... it's not the right way to do it, but for someone who doesn't have
> tons of resources, it's just a quick little hack.

> You got to figure out A, how do you convert those visitors into leads or sales...
> and B, you got to figure out, all right, what topics are driving the most revenue?
> And then you just start creating more content around those topics.

> I have over a 6,000 blog posts. There's no way I'm doing it on all 6,000.

> If I know SEO worked well, like the beginner's guide to SEO, then I may create
> content around how to get more traffic through SEO through global expansion or how
> to build links through infographics, because that's a subset of SEO.

## Eric Siu (co-host, context — NOT persona data)
[[wiki/entities/eric-siu]]
