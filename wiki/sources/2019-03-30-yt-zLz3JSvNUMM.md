# Why Offering Discounts Will Put You Out of Business | Discounting for Online and Offline Businesses

## Summary

Neil argues that discounting — especially in **B2B/enterprise** — destroys businesses, and that
tactics that work for Amazon/Macy's/Nordstrom don't transfer to small companies. Five reasons
not to discount: (1) it **sets a bad precedent** — discount-seekers keep asking for more scope
at the same price and are the wrong-fit customers; (2) it **ruins your brand** with full-price
customers who feel cheated; (3) discount buyers **churn faster and grumble more** — one of his
companies saw discounted signups churn **29% faster** while paying less and generating more
support load; (4) it **devalues the product** and makes you look cheap (you don't ask Goldman
Sachs for a discount); (5) it **attracts the wrong customers** who won't stick, upsell, or
downsell. His prescription: go upstream, optimize for **lifetime value** (upsells/downsells),
not short-term income. Reinforces his premium-positioning doctrine.
