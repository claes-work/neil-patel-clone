# How to Create a Marketing Strategy For a New eCommerce Website

## Summary
Solo seven-step playbook for a brand-new e-commerce store, opening on the failure mode of a
beautiful site with no traffic and only your mother ordering. (1) **Pick a niche** — never try
to be Amazon; big players beat you on price and shipping speed, while a niche lets you be the
expert with the best product, sell at a premium anyway, and run far more efficient ads.
(2) **Build a killer USP** — his example is a friend who sells custom insoles specifically for
high heels and makes over six figures a month in profit from Facebook ads, because her video
tells a specific story (loving luxury heels, hating the discomfort) rather than Dr. Scholl's
generic "comfortable for anyone." (3) **Create a fantastic user experience** — he recommends
Shopify for checkout flow, design and templates, and points to Dollar Shave Club's unboxing as
the standard for the post-purchase physical experience. (4) **Pick one traffic source and go
all in** — SEO is the long-term play; for e-commerce he'd start with Facebook ads (which
convert well for Shopify stores selling one or two products) and only then expand to Google
Ads, Google/Amazon product ads and lookalikes. (5) **Maximize value per visitor** — upsells and
downsells make ads and SEO profitable; Hello Bar for exit-intent email capture, subscribers.com
push notifications to recover checkout abandoners. (6) **Maximize average order value** —
free shipping raises AOV (his friend selling HDMI cables raised product prices, made shipping
free, kept the total identical, and sold more), plus checkout bumps (ClickFunnels as the model)
and long-running post-purchase upsells (Skin Care by Alana selling vitamin C cream after
wrinkle cream, for years). (7) **Use technology** — cart-abandonment, product recommendation,
win-back, contest, holiday, cross-sell and one-time-offer plugins on Shopify, Magento or
WooCommerce, plus live chat. Closes plugging Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil said a new store must focus on a niche or segment rather than selling everything like Amazon, because big companies beat you on pricing and shipping speed and you'd have no expertise advantage.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil said a niche store can charge more than competitors and still win because people know you as the expert in that category, and that ads to a niche store convert better than ads to a broad one.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil said a friend of his sells custom insoles made specifically for high heels (not the Dr. Scholl's brand) and makes over six figures a month in profit from Facebook ads to her e-commerce store, driven by a video telling her personal story of comfort in luxury heels.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil contrasted that specific USP with Dr. Scholl's generic "comfortable to walk for anyone" positioning, arguing a product that works for his Nikes may not work for his dress shoes.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil said he loves Shopify and recommends it for anyone in e-commerce, praising its checkout flow, designs and templates, and named Dollar Shave Club's unboxing as an example of an amazing experience.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil said to pick ONE traffic source and go all in, and specifically recommended starting with Facebook ads for e-commerce because they convert well for Shopify stores selling one or two products, expanding to Google Ads and other channels only after that works.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil recommended upsells and downsells to maximize value per visitor so that paid ads and SEO both become more profitable.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil recommended Hello Bar to capture email addresses as visitors are about to leave, then marketing to them with story, USP or a discount (e.g. $15 off), and subscribers.com push notifications to recover people who reached the checkout page without buying.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil said free shipping increases average order value, citing a friend selling HDMI cables who raised product prices, made shipping free, kept the final total the same, and got more sales purely because shipping read as free.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil pointed to ClickFunnels as the model for checkout bumps with an irresistible offer, and to Skin Care by Alana for post-purchase bumps that keep selling related products for years after the first purchase.
- As of 2019-12-19, Neil said plugins exist on Shopify, Magento and WooCommerce for cart-abandonment sequences, product recommendations, win-back sequences, contests, holiday sequences, cross-sells and one-time offers, plus live chat to answer objections and maximize sales.
