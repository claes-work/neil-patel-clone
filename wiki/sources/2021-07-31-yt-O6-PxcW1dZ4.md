# The Best Social Media Platforms For Advertising | Choosing the Right Ad Platform For Your Brand

## Summary
Opens with the identical line used in [[2021-07-26-yt-Tbja-0mTkjw]] ("I'm getting super
creative on how I do growth, and I always have been") — confirms these two videos are
clipped from the same interview session. Core content: a concrete **paid-platform
ranking by ROI-for-the-money** across non-Google/Facebook alternatives — (1) Taboola/
Outbrain (grouped together, "very similar"), (2) Snapchat, (3) TikTok, (4) Pinterest,
(5) LinkedIn (expensive but converts well for B2B) — with the reasoning that these
platforms convert at a fraction of Google/Facebook's cost (as little as 1/5–1/10) even
though they can't scale to the same absolute spend; his recommendation is to run
several of these smaller platforms in parallel ($2–10K/month each) before tackling the
big two. Extends the already-known "creativity is the top marketing skill" thesis with
a fresh, quantified example: building/giving away a free tool (i.e., Ubersuggest) is
framed as avoiding an estimated **$1.5–2 million/month** in Google-ad spend that would
otherwise be required to hit the same traffic level — a new dollar figure for the
free-tool-as-acquisition-channel doctrine (already ★-promoted in a different framing in
synthesis pass 6). Organic/SEO-for-brand-building doctrine reinforced with fresh
numbers: currently ~10M monthly visits without ads, ~180,000/month of which are
branded-search visits for "Neil Patel" specifically — starting from zero branded search
when he began. Closes on a text-vs-video-crawlability point: video content is
increasingly crawlable but still ranks worse than text in most Google searches, so
video should be repurposed into blog text for SEO purposes, not relied on alone.
