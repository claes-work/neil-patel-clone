# 4 Proven Local SEO Tips to Rank High in 'Near Me' Search

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode with co-host **Adam (from Viewership)**, answering viewer Karsen on
how to rank for "near me" searches with geographic relevance (example: "dog trainers near
me"). Neil's local-SEO tips: **(1) Get listed on the sites Google pulls from** — search
your target term plus "near me," see which localized directories/sites populate the results,
and make sure your business is listed on them; this raises your chance of appearing. **(2)
Add local geographic detail to your website** — list the suburbs/subsections, landmarks,
and town lines of the city you service (using Boston/Charlestown/Cambridge/Newton as the
example), plus "old-school" driving directions to your location. **(3) Use a local phone
number**, not an 800/888 number, so users trust you're genuinely local and in person (he
disdains fake national "near me" listings, using 1-800-Flowers as the anti-example — Adam
recounts having to go outside downtown Boston to find a legitimate local florist). **(4)
Get reviews** — customers check Yelp/Google to judge legitimacy; ask satisfied customers
for reviews. In Google's local pack of ~3 listings, if a lower-ranked listing accumulates
far more reviews (e.g., 50–100 vs. 7–10) and earns more clicks, Google will eventually push
it above the others. Adam adds that simply watching this content already sets a local
business apart, since many still rely only on word-of-mouth and flyers.
