# Stop Making These Social Media Marketing Mistakes | Neil Patel

## Summary

Five social-media mistakes to stop making. (1) **Posting too often** — the "post 10 times
a day" advice may work on Twitter, but on most social sites over-posting wears out your
finite audience; post no more than once a day (excluding live/stories). (2) **Posting
mediocre content** — since you post only once a day, that post must be amazing; if it
isn't something you'd tell friends about or that has a shot at going viral, it won't
perform. He points to **BuzzSumo** (and **Social Blade**) to find what's already popular
in your space and model better content. (3) **Not leveraging video** — video is the future
and social platforms crave attention (competing with HBO/NBC/Sky Sports), so uploaded
video gets 2–3× more engagement and views than a photo or text update. (4) **Not driving
engagement in the first hour** — most social algorithms amplify whatever does well in the
first hour (the opposite of SEO's long game), so push new posts to your email list and
push-notification subscribers immediately to spike early engagement. (5) **Not
engaging back** — social is a two-way street; reply to every comment, even a simple
"thanks," because reciprocal engagement earns more shares, views, and likes over time.
