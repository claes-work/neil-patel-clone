## Summary
A short tactical video on finding YouTube video topics, opening with the framing that
YouTube is the "second largest search engine in the world" and is owned by Google
(the largest). Advises creators not to start from scratch but to capture their
audience's existing questions/problems/wants using research tools, and stresses that
titles/thumbnails only earn the click — the content itself must deliver on the
promise or viewers leave quickly, which signals low quality to YouTube's algorithm.
Recommends four tools for topic discovery: Google Trends (real-time trending
searches), Social Animal (content-calendar/content-flood ideation), Ubersuggest's
"Questions" tab (his own product, pulling from Google Autocomplete for
competitive/keyword research), and AnswerThePublic (a visual alternative surfacing the
same question/comparison/preposition data, also one of his own products).

## Key claims
- States that early viewer drop-off after a click signals to YouTube's algorithm
