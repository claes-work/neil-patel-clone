# How to Optimize Your PPC Landing Pages for SEO

## Summary
Argues PPC landing pages should be built with SEO in mind rather than treated as a
purely paid, siloed asset, since every indexed page competes for organic ranking too
— and warns that Google's "helpful content" update can let a thin PPC landing page
drag down the SEO performance of an existing organic page if the two overlap. Cites
landing-page benchmark stats (9.7% average conversion rate; 7x more leads for sites
with 31-40 landing pages vs. 1-5, both attributed to HubSpot; a 4.42% conversion drop
per second of added load time; up to a 266% conversion drop from including more than
one offer on a page). Five tips: (1) publish landing pages to a sub-directory of the
main domain, not a separate domain; (2) do keyword research even for PPC pages,
favoring 3-5-word long-tail phrases — self-reports over 80% of NP Digital client-site
traffic comes from long-tail keywords, sourced via Ubersuggest and AnswerThePublic;
(3) don't default to short pages — length should follow how fast the page answers the
user's need, not the PPC-vs-SEO channel, and video content on a landing page can also
earn video-SEO traffic; (4) speed up load time (cites 47% of users expect load in
under 2 seconds, 40% abandon after 3 seconds) via smaller images, faster hosting, a
CDN, and removing unused plugins; (5) build backlinks to landing pages using
Ubersuggest's "Backlink opportunities" report against competitor URLs to find sites
willing to link into the space. Closes with an NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- Cites (HubSpot, as cited, dated 2022-12-17): average landing-page conversion rate
  is 9.7%; sites with 31-40 landing pages get 7x more leads than sites with 1-5.
- Cites: a 4.42% conversion-rate drop per additional second of landing-page load
  delay, and up to a 266% conversion-rate drop from presenting more than one offer on
  a page (sources unnamed, as cited, dated 2022-12-17).
- Self-reports over 80% of NP Digital client-site traffic typically comes from
  long-tail keywords (self-reported, dated 2022-12-17).
- Cites: 47% of users expect a page to load in under two seconds and 40% abandon a
  page after three seconds (source unnamed, as cited, dated 2022-12-17).
- States Google's search ranking prioritizes user experience/intent over raw backlink
  volume — "they don't look at like, oh this listing has a million back links. It
  should rank number one" (stated position, dated 2022-12-17).
