# Employees FIRST? (My #1 Tip for Business Owners)

## Summary

Solo, leadership/mindset. Neil's thesis: **no one builds a big company alone — it's your team.**
He anchors it in a brief conversation with **Richard Branson** at a Brazil conference called
**VTEX** (Neil stresses he doesn't know Branson well — a one-off exchange): Branson built the
Virgin Group with a great team, and figures like Branson, Bill Gates, and Elon Musk didn't build
multi-billion-dollar companies as lone founders. Practical guidance: **recruit people smarter than
you** — not smarter at everything, but **better than you at their specialty**; then **take care of
them**. You may not be able to **pay them what they're worth early on** (raise wages over time), so
show you care in **non-monetary ways** — gifts, taking them and their spouse to dinner, telling them
they're doing a good job. **Reframe "employees" as "team members"** — even at 100% ownership, give
them a say, because **you don't always know what's best**. **Listen to feedback**, especially when
**multiple people converge on the same advice within their area of expertise** (his analogy: take a
doctor's health advice but not their cybersecurity advice). Repeated closing: take care of your
people — "it's never you who's gonna build a successful company, it's your team."

## Key claims

- As of 2018-10-27, **a successful company is built by the team, not the founder alone** — cites
  Branson (Virgin), Gates, Musk as examples of team-built companies.
- As of 2018-10-27, **hire people smarter than you** — at least **better than you in their own
  specialty**.
- As of 2018-10-27, **take care of your team**; if you can't yet pay full market wages, use
  **non-monetary care** (recognition, gifts, dinners with spouses) and **raise wages over time**.
- As of 2018-10-27, **treat employees as "team members" with a say**, even at 100% ownership, because
  **the owner doesn't always know best**.
- As of 2018-10-27, **listen to feedback — especially when multiple experts converge** on the same
  advice within their domain (doctor health-advice vs. security-advice analogy).
