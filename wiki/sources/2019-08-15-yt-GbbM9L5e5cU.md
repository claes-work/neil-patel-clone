# How to Rank Your Old Content (And Start Ranking for Hundreds Of New Keywords)

## Summary

Neil argues that with ~1 blog per 7 people on the web, the smarter path to traffic is
**updating old content, not always creating new**. He discloses that on neilpatel.com his
team publishes **one new article per week but updates 90+ old/outdated articles per month**,
which he credits for roughly **4 million visitors a month**, and shares his formula. (1) Use
**Google Search Console + Ubersuggest** to find old articles **dipping** in traffic, then
refresh, fix, and **expand** them (put the ranking keywords into Ubersuggest for long-tails
and questions) — user-first. (2) Improve **click-through rate** on title/meta tags using
**ClickFlow**; a higher CTR than a higher-ranked competitor lifts you, and Google eventually
promotes the more-clicked listing. (3) **Delete or 301-redirect** irrelevant old articles
(his example: posts about **Vine**, which Twitter shut down) into the most relevant page to
pass link equity. (4) Push **internal links** from new content to old "gems," including
sidebar links to cornerstone pages. (5) **Embed multimedia** (video, podcasts, infographics)
to raise time-on-site and lower bounce — an indirect ranking signal he uses across his old
content. (6) **Adjust the last-updated date** when you make substantial changes and notify
Google — but don't be sneaky and change the date after a one-sentence edit. (7) **Promote old
content consistently** (find competitors' sharers via Twitter and ask for shares); rankings
climb gradually, rarely in one jump.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-08-15, Neil said neilpatel.com publishes **~1 new article/week but updates 90+
  old articles/month**, driving **~4 million visitors/month** (self-reported figures).
- As of 2019-08-15, Neil's refresh trigger: find **traffic-dipping old articles in Search
  Console**, then update AND expand them with Ubersuggest long-tails/questions, user-first.
- As of 2019-08-15, Neil said **CTR optimization on title/meta (via ClickFlow) can outrank a
  higher position** — Google promotes the more-clicked listing over time.
- As of 2019-08-15, Neil said delete stale, un-updatable content (e.g. **Vine** posts) and
  **301-redirect** it to the most relevant page to pass "juice."
- As of 2019-08-15, Neil said **embedding multimedia improves time-on-site / bounce rate**,
  which indirectly improves his rankings.
- As of 2019-08-15, Neil said **only change the last-updated date after substantial edits** —
  a one-sentence change + new date is something "Google's not going to like."
- As of 2019-08-15, Neil said rankings **climb gradually**; Google rarely jumps a page from
  bottom-of-page-three to top-of-page-one.

## Notable verbatim quotes
