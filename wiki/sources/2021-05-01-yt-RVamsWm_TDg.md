# How to Use Crazy Egg - CRO Unlocked

## Summary
This lesson from Neil Patel's free "CRO Unlocked" course (best practices, part two) walks through how to use Crazy Egg — the heatmap and conversion-rate-optimization tool Neil co-founded around 2005-06 — to understand and improve how visitors interact with a website. Speaking here as an instructor rather than owner, Neil frames Crazy Egg as a way to monitor visitor interactions by creating snapshots of a page, with advanced options for tracking specific device types (desktop, mobile, tablet), setting expiration times, and isolating specific time windows such as a Black Friday promotion. He then walks through Crazy Egg's core report types: heatmaps (a graphical representation of where people click, including dead clicks), scrollmaps (what percentage of users scroll where and where their attention concentrates), confetti and overlay reports (segmentable dot views and exact click counts/percentages per element, useful for spotting non-clickable "dead zones" users mistakenly click), and the list report (a tabulated breakdown of clicks by element, visible or not). Throughout, Neil ties the data back to concrete CRO actions — making sure calls to action are seen and clicked, ordering content by importance to users, tracking behavior behind pop-ups and forms, and not blindly placing CTAs above the fold since sometimes people must be educated first and engagement often peaks just below the fold. He connects user experience to SEO, arguing Google increasingly ranks sites for the best user experience, not just links. He closes by advocating continuous AB testing driven by data rather than guesses (cadence depending on niche and conversion volume, from one to two tests a week down to one or two a month), introduces "heuristic analysis" as the evolution of CRO beyond button-color tweaks toward improving the whole experience, and points to workbooks, a heat-mapping guide, and a checklist at neilpatel.com/training, plus his agency NP Digital for done-for-you CRO.

## Key claims
- Crazy Egg lets you monitor visitor interactions by creating a snapshot of a page and seeing how visitors interact with different variations as the page changes (as of 2021-05-01).
- When making a snapshot you can choose advanced options including which device type to monitor (desktop, mobile, or tablet) and an expiration time for when to stop tracking, enabling analysis of specific periods such as Mondays, a given week, or a promotion like Black Friday (as of 2021-05-01).
- A heatmap is a graphical representation of everywhere users clicked on a website — links, dead clicks, or clicking around while reading text — and helps evaluate how people engage with the site (as of 2021-05-01).
- Heatmap data can be used to redesign a site to decrease bounce rates, reduce drop-offs, and improve user experience to get more sales (as of 2021-05-01).
- SEO encompasses user experience; Google wants to rank sites with the best user experience, not just sites with more links and better on-page SEO (as of 2021-05-01).
- To analyze heatmaps: ensure calls to action are being seen and clicked (change placement if not), place content in order of importance to users, and track what happens behind pop-ups, forms, or login screens to optimize the whole experience (as of 2021-05-01).
- A scrollmap shows what percentage of users scroll where, and reveals that people sometimes view middle sections more than the top; it also tracks how much attention users give to elements, helping identify where to place the most important elements (as of 2021-05-01).
- Crazy Egg lets you segment traffic (e.g., paid ads, email, SEO) and see how different audiences interact with pages differently, so you can create different experiences for paid versus organic users (as of 2021-05-01).
- In a scroll-depth example, the most viewed section is just above the fold at roughly 550 pixels with 80% viewership, most visitors scroll before the page fully loads, and the area just above the fold is seen by nearly all visitors (as of 2021-05-01).
- CTAs should not automatically be placed above the fold, because sometimes you need to educate visitors and give more information before selling; engaged time peaks roughly just below the fold, so what sits right beneath the fold deserves attention (as of 2021-05-01).
- Pixels at the top are viewed for about four seconds, and viewing time increases to peak around 1,200 pixels down; people who scroll more tend to be more engaged (as of 2021-05-01).
- The confetti report shows colored dots representing different traffic sources, segmentable by day, time, weekend versus weekday, or Bing versus Google, to see how to improve conversion rates (as of 2021-05-01).
- The overlay report (accessed via the overlay button in the navigation) shows the exact number and percentage of clicks to every element via a plus marker; Neil's favorite part is seeing dead zones — non-clickable elements people click on that should be fixed — which almost every site has (as of 2021-05-01).
- The list report gives a tabulated list of every element showing what people click on and whether it is visible or not; clicking an element in the list report brings up the overlay report for it (as of 2021-05-01).
- Testing is a continuous, ideal method for assessing and iterating; tests should be based on what the data shows, not what you think will win (as of 2021-05-01).
- Test cadence depends on niche, industry, and conversion volume: with a lot of conversions (as Neil has) one can run one to two tests a week, otherwise perhaps only one or two a month — but the priority is simply to run them (as of 2021-05-01).
- Crazy Egg offers a free trial and a WYSIWYG onboarding that is easy to use for setting up overlay and confetti reports and running tests (as of 2021-05-01).
- Heuristic analysis — thinking about how people engage with the overall site and what to improve from a UX interaction-design standpoint — is the evolution of CRO, moving beyond questions like button color or text toward improving the whole experience (as of 2021-05-01).
- Neil offers a workbook, a heat-mapping guide, and a checklist under week one of CRO Unlocked at neilpatel.com/training, and his agency NP Digital (Neil Patel Digital) can do the CRO work for clients (as of 2021-05-01).
