# How to Create a Personal Brand and Get Noticed | Secrets Behind the 'Neil Patel' Personal Blog

## Summary
An interview in which Neil explains why he built the neilpatel.com **personal brand** and
how he grew it. His prior companies (Crazy Egg, KISSmetrics, Quick Sprout) were corporate
or investor-owned, so he wanted a **personal blog that stays with him for life** — noting
the irony that "Neil Patel" then became **NP Digital** (the agency), so he "lost" the
personal blog again. His rule: build a **corporate blog if you intend to sell the company**,
but a **personal blog if you're a serial entrepreneur** staying in one niche, because you
can keep marketing every new business to the same audience. He rejects lifestyle/personal
content because it's hard to monetize, and instead chases **big total addressable markets**
(digital advertising, "over probably 100 billion a year") — "if I got 1% of the digital
marketing industry … I'm a billionaire." His stated goal is not homes or cars but to build
**the largest marketing-technology company in the world** — "a scoreboard, and I want the
highest score" — acknowledging he's far from #1 (HubSpot multi-billions, Adobe, Google
$700B, Facebook $500B). He stresses **owning your own audience/email** so an algorithm
change can't cut you off, and recaps his traffic engine: **SEMrush** to find competitors'
top-traffic articles → **Ahrefs** sorted by fewest backlinks → write better versions
(skyscraper). Rich persona/biography material on Neil's motivations and goals.

## Key claims
As of 2018-01-18, Neil states:
- He built a **personal brand** because his earlier ventures were corporate/investor-owned
  (KISSmetrics VC-raised, Crazy Egg not personal); he wanted "a personal blog that sticks
  with me for life" — then it "turned into NP Digital … so now I don't have a personal blog
  anymore" (as of 2018-01-18). ★ persona/biography
- Rule of thumb: **corporate blog if you want to sell the company; personal blog if you're
  a serial entrepreneur** in the same niche, so you can keep marketing new businesses to a
  loyal audience — "a personal blog for serial entrepreneurs … is more important than a
  corporate blog" (as of 2018-01-18).
- He avoids lifestyle/personal content ("what you ate") because it's hard to monetize;
  instead he targets **big market caps / total addressable market** — global digital
  advertising is "multi-billions if not hundreds of plus billion," spend "over probably
  100 billion a year" (as of 2018-01-18).
- "If I got 1% of the digital marketing industry … I'm rich, I'm a billionaire" — you only
  ever capture a fraction, so market size matters most (as of 2018-01-18).
- His goal is **not** homes or cars but to "create the **largest marketing technology
  company in the world**" — "it's kind of like a sport … a scoreboard, and I want the
  highest score" (as of 2018-01-18). ★ persona/beliefs
- He is **not #1**: HubSpot is worth "multi-billions," and Adobe, Google ("700 billion"),
  Facebook ("500 billion") dwarf what he's built — "I have a long way to go" (as of 2018-01-18).
- Reason to build his own audience: **own your platform/email** — Facebook/Google/Instagram
  can change an algorithm and halve your traffic overnight; "with my own blog and my own
  audience, my own emails, I control it … no one can shut me down the next day" (as of 2018-01-18).
- How he grew traffic: "3 years of writing amazing content," then **SEMrush** → type in
  every competitor → take their most-trafficked articles → **Ahrefs**, sort by **least
  backlinks** → write better, more thorough, updated versions (skyscraper) (as of 2018-01-18).
- **Personal brand aids SEO**: it's easier to earn links/rankings for an individual than a
  faceless corporation ("less likely to connect with Coca-Cola than with … Tony Robbins"),
  and loyalty brings people back (as of 2018-01-18).
