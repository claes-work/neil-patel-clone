# How to Find Lucrative Keywords That Are Easy to Rank For

## Summary
Solo tactical Ubersuggest walkthrough on keyword research. Neil's method: type a
generic "head term" into Ubersuggest and check (1) whether desktop traffic exceeds
mobile traffic (desktop searches convert better, in his view), (2) whether searchers
click on results at all rather than getting a no-click ("weather"-style) answer
directly on the SERP, and (3) cost-per-click as a proxy for commercial intent — higher
CPC signals more lucrative traffic. He demonstrates filtering the "keyword ideas"
report by minimum CPC ($1+) and maximum SEO difficulty (under 40, shown in green) to
surface a target list, and recommends also mining the "related" tab (broader database
match) and "questions" tab (people-also-ask-style queries, useful for content
angles and near-me/comparison intent). Rule of thumb: if a head term has low SEO
difficulty and high CPC, its long-tail variations usually do too (and vice versa).

## Key claims
- As of 2020-07-20, Neil said desktop search traffic converts better than mobile
  traffic, and recommended prioritizing head terms where desktop volume exceeds
  mobile volume (unsourced, presented as established practice).
- As of 2020-07-20, Neil's standard Ubersuggest keyword-research filter for U.S.
  markets is minimum CPC of $1 and maximum SEO difficulty of 40, calling this "good
  volume, high CPC and low SEO difficulty" the recipe for a lucrative keyword.
  Recommends verifying the target audience is 18+ (assumes minors don't convert
  because "most of them don't have credit cards").
- As of 2020-07-20, Neil framed CPC as a proxy for keyword commercial value: "the
  higher the number, the more lucrative the keyword" and "if the head term is
  expensive cost per click, that usually means a long tail keywords are even more
  expensive."

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The reason I say you want more desktop searches is desktop searches tend to convert better than mobile searches."

> "You ideally want to go after... keywords that have a lot of volume, low SEO difficulty, ideally under 40, and good cost per click, the higher the number, the more lucrative the keyword."

> "If the head term isn't too hard to rank for, that usually means the long tail terms are going to be even easier."
