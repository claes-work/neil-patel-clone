# The Most Powerful Content Creation Strategy You're Not Using

## Summary
Opens by invoking Bill Gates's 1996 "Content is King" essay as prescient, plus HubSpot
figures (40% of marketers call content marketing central to strategy; 81% say their
company views content as a business strategy) and a stat that 45% of marketing
professionals struggle to create content that resonates. Prescribes the "hedgehog"
marketing concept (from the hedgehog-and-fox parable: the fox knows many things, the
hedgehog knows one thing well) — i.e., focus content on what you know deeply rather
than chasing broad reach ("you are much better off focusing on trying to meaningfully
reach a hundred people than you are trying to barely get through to a thousand").
Five implementation steps: (1) build a detailed buyer persona — cites personas making
websites 2-5x more effective at targeting; (2) segment the audience — cites a Milton
survey finding segmented email campaigns get 14% higher open rates, and describes his
own practice of sending different email sequences to first-time visitors, repeat
buyers, and cart-abandoners (the latter getting coupon codes, testimonials, or case
studies); (3) become a thought leader — cites a LinkedIn study (71% of professionals
say less than half of thought-leadership content is genuinely valuable; 65% say a
piece of thought leadership changed their perception of a company for the better; 64%
find it more trustworthy than marketing materials) and explicitly invokes Google's
E-A-T framework (expertise, authority, trust), arguing niche-focused sites outrank
attempts to be broad like Wikipedia/NYT; (4) make content more valuable/shareable —
cites 95.9% of bloggers promote on social and 69% use social share buttons, describing
his own practice of promoting every post across social channels plus email/push
notifications; (5) "find your engine" — focus on the single most profitable
product/service/idea rather than spreading across supplementary offerings, while still
allowing upsells/downsells as ancillary. Closes with an NP Digital plug.
