# How to DOUBLE Your Email Open Rates (4 Email Marketing Tips to Get Over 30% Email Open Rates)

## Summary
Neil explains four tactics for lifting email open rates from the sub-10% range most senders see to above 30%, the level he claims to hit on every email he sends. He argues that anything under a 20% open rate signals something is wrong, and offers four fixes: test the sender email/name because it strongly affects opens; write casual rather than professional subject lines so emails feel like they come from a friend; use lowercase subject lines instead of proper/uppercase capitalization to avoid reading as a corporate business email; and personalize the subject line with the recipient's name, which he says raises open rates by roughly five to seven percent.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-07, Neil states:
- Most people get less than 10% email open rates, which is poor performance (as of 2017-10-07).
- An open rate below 20% means something is being done wrong (as of 2017-10-07).
- He personally gets almost above 30% open rates on every email he sends (as of 2017-10-07).
- The sender email/name (e.g., a personal name, name@website, or company@website) drastically affects open rates and should be tested (as of 2017-10-07).
- Casual subject lines get opened more than professional ones because people open friends' emails more than business emails (as of 2017-10-07).
- Lowercase subject lines get more opens than uppercase/proper ones, which read as corporate business emails and get fewer click-throughs (as of 2017-10-07).
- Including the recipient's name in the subject line personalizes it and increases open rates, usually by five to seven percent (as of 2017-10-07).
