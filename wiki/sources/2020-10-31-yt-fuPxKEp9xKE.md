# How To Get B2B Leads & Clients On LinkedIn - Module 1 - Lesson 3 - LinkedIn Unlocked

## Summary
LinkedIn Unlocked Module 1 Lesson 3: a B2B-vs-B2C sales-model contrast followed by a
five-stage B2B sales-cycle framework (lead → discovery call → strategic plan/
presentation → decision meeting → close), with a goal and "homework" assignment defined
for both rep and prospect at each stage. Closes with LinkedIn Sales Navigator as the
prospecting tool: filtering by title/industry/interests/groups to build outreach lists,
paired with a short, low-friction connection-request template Neil says he uses himself.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-31, Neil contrasts B2C (lower price points, shorter sales cycles, fewer
  decision-makers, ~95% self-serve on-site) against B2B (wholesale/organizational
  selling, longer sales cycles, higher price points, >80% requiring a human
  salesperson) as the reason B2B needs a structured, multi-stage process (paraphrase).
- As of 2020-10-31, his five-stage B2B sales cycle: (1) lead → discovery call qualifying
  fit against an ideal-buyer profile and filtering fake/bad leads; (2) discovery call
  establishing the prospect's own problem understanding, homework = a questionnaire;
  (3) strategic plan/presentation demonstrating cost-benefit and urgency (framed as "if
  they don't move forward, they're losing time, they're losing money"), homework =
  recap email + contract; (4) decision meeting resolving remaining objections and
  confirming commitment; (5) onboarding/kickoff. Sales cycles cited as commonly 90-120
  days for B2B (paraphrase, tactical framework).
- As of 2020-10-31, recommends LinkedIn Sales Navigator for prospecting by title,
  industry, interests, and groups, feeding results into a CRM for outreach; offers his
  own low-friction connection-message template ("I'm looking to expand my network of
  marketing professionals...") as directly reusable copy (paraphrase + near-verbatim
  template).
- As of 2020-10-31, states repeat objections at the decision-meeting stage indicate the
  rep did not adequately explain the solution earlier and should change the
  explanation approach, not just repeat it (paraphrase, sales-coaching opinion).
