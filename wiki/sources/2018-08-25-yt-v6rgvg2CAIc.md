# Why I Do NOT Monetize Videos (My CRAZY YouTube Marketing Strategy)

## Summary

Solo values/strategy monologue. Neil explains that although you see him everywhere, it's
not because he's "popular" — it's because he **retargets** the same audience (subscribe and
you keep seeing his content). Then his core point: he **deliberately does not monetize** his
videos — no ads (Facebook/Google/YouTube), no CTAs to his site, and he **turns down paid
sponsorships/promotions** ("mention X and I'll pay you Y") as a rule. He'll mention a product
**only if he genuinely likes it and it provides value to the viewer — and he'll do it for
free**; most of the time he declines because he doesn't think it helps the audience. He says
he **barely mentions his own businesses** on his own videos because he doesn't want to. His
rationale is **indirect monetization**: if he does what's best for the viewer, "one way or
another" it comes back and he makes money — and he concedes it sounds crazy for someone as
analytical/data-driven as he is (he built multiple analytics companies). He notes he's
**tested affiliate pages** and could push them to $30–50k/month, but it's "just not me." He
frames the whole thing as **give-first selflessness and karma** — put people first, don't
be selfish, and it pays back long-term; and even if it didn't, "at least you and I had an
amazing time helping each other out." He explicitly tells viewers to do what *they're*
comfortable with — direct monetization is fine for others.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-25, Neil deliberately does **not** monetize his videos: no ad networks, no
  site CTAs, and he barely mentions his own businesses.
- As of 2018-08-25, he **refuses paid sponsorships/promotions** and affiliate commissions;
  he'll mention a product only if he genuinely likes it and it helps the viewer — and does
  so **for free**.
- As of 2018-08-25, his strategy is **indirect monetization / give-first**: doing what's
  best for the viewer comes back as revenue "one way or another," even for an analytical,
  data-driven person like him.
- As of 2018-08-25, he says (self-reported) he tested affiliate-commission pages and could
  scale them to ~$30–50k/month, but chooses not to because "it's just not me."
- As of 2018-08-25, he notes he **retargets** the same audience, which is why viewers see
  his content repeatedly across YouTube/Facebook.
- As of 2018-08-25, he explicitly permits others to monetize directly ("do what you're
  comfortable with") — the no-monetize stance is a personal values choice, not universal
  advice.
