# Top 5 Favorite SEO Hacks

## Summary
Five "outside the box" SEO tactics: (1) buy an existing, well-ranked blog in your niche
that isn't monetized (find candidates/traffic via Ubersuggest), merge its content into
your site with 301 redirects for near-instant rankings; (2) YouTube's algorithm rewards
videos that perform well in the first 24 hours, so he drives an immediate view spike via
email blast + push notification the moment a video goes live, rather than relying on
ads (too slow to ramp); (3) release free tools people are used to paying for — cites
calculator.net, an unglamorous site with 6.2 million backlinks purely from its free
calculators, as proof; tools can be custom-built or bought from CodeCanyon; (4)
responding thoroughly to blog comments functions as a lightweight, ongoing
content-freshness signal (echoing the Wikipedia-freshness analogy used elsewhere in the
corpus); (5) for App Store SEO specifically, using fewer (not more) keywords in the
title/description makes ranking easier, provided the chosen keyword(s) are high-volume.

## Key claims
- Recommends buying an unmonetized, well-ranked niche blog and merging it via 301
  redirects into your own site as a fast-track ranking tactic, findable/verifiable via
  Ubersuggest traffic data (as of 2022-08-29).
- States YouTube's ranking algorithm is front-loaded on first-24-hour performance,
  and that email/push-notification blasts (not ads, which ramp too slowly) are his
  method for engineering that early spike (as of 2022-08-29, self-reported).
- Cites calculator.net's 6.2 million backlinks as evidence that free, useful tools
  generate links without outreach (as of 2022-08-29).
- App Store SEO claim: using fewer keywords in title/description (vs. keyword-stuffing)
  makes ranking for those terms easier, provided the terms are high-volume (as of
  2022-08-29).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Buy websites... look for a blog in your space... that ranks really well within your space, has great content, but doesn't monetize.

> The fewer keywords that use in your title or description in the App Store, the easier it is to rank for those terms.
