# Do Yourself a Favor: STOP Trying to Rank #1 For Keywords on Google

## Summary

Neil argues that obsessing over the #1 spot is the wrong goal — he tracks **zero** of his own
rankings. Five reasons: (1) **rank tracking is inaccurate** — localized/personalized results
mean your "#1" isn't universal; (2) **#1 ≠ most traffic** — per Ahrefs, the #1 result gets the
most traffic **only 49% of the time**, so 51% of the time other positions win; (3) **you can
out-traffic #1 from lower positions** via **rich snippets / "ranking zero"** (he cites ranking
#3–4 for "SEO tips" yet getting more traffic than #1 by owning the snippet) — the trick is
structuring content (e.g. clearly numbered tips) so Google pulls it as a featured snippet;
(4) **not all keywords have intent** — high-volume head terms like "SEO," "online marketing,"
"internet marketing" bring info-seekers with no buying intent and little revenue, whereas an
intent term like "**SEO agency**" earns far more; (5) **rankings don't guarantee success** — his
recurring **Ahrefs "backlink checker"** case study: they did everything right but couldn't rank
until they served a **free skimmed-down tool (~100 links)** matching searcher intent, then shot
to page 1. Learn what users want with surveys (SurveyMonkey). Heavy overlap with the 2019-05-09
"new website ranking" and 2019-05-20 "8 things to unlearn" videos.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-05-12, Neil said he tries to rank #1 for **zero** keywords and **doesn't track his
  own rankings** — the goal is the best user experience, not position.
- As of 2019-05-12, Neil cited **Ahrefs: ranking #1 gets the most traffic only 49% of the time**
  (so 51% of the time other positions capture more). (Recurring stat across this era.)
- As of 2019-05-12, Neil said he ranks #3–4 for "SEO tips" but out-traffics #1 by owning the
  **rich snippet ("ranking zero")**; structuring content as clearly defined tips raises snippet
  odds.
- As of 2019-05-12, Neil said high-volume head terms ("SEO," "online marketing," "internet
  marketing") have **no buying intent** and drive little revenue; **intent-based** terms like
  "SEO agency" convert far better — chase intent, not volume.
- As of 2019-05-12, Neil re-told the **Ahrefs "backlink checker"** story: they couldn't rank
  despite perfect SEO until they gave away a **free ~100-link version** matching what searchers
  wanted, then jumped to page 1 — "you ignore the user, you're not going to rank."
