# Marketing On LinkedIn - Module 2 - Lesson 2 - LinkedIn Unlocked

## Summary
LinkedIn Unlocked Module 2 Lesson 2: a tactical grab-bag covering audience positioning,
content-posting cadence, scheduling-link outreach (Calendly), LinkedIn's Advanced Search
and Groups (with an explicit engagement quota and an 80/20 content-mix rule), and a
survey of LinkedIn Ads formats (sponsored content, sponsored messages, text ads, dynamic
ads) with his stated format preference and testing philosophy.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-11-05, Neil states that including a scheduling link (he names Calendly,
  Sales Navigator, Sidekick, Zoom, Skype as options, Calendly as his main tool) in
  outreach messages yields "40 plus percent more engaged people" who actually get on a
  call, versus leads who agree but never show up (self-reported, unaudited figure).
  ⚠️ Contrast note: this is a *scheduling-tool* engagement lift, not the same claim
  category as his automatic-scheduling/booking-rate figures elsewhere in the corpus —
  no direct contradiction, just a distinct datapoint.
- As of 2020-11-05, Groups quota: join at least 10 groups, interact with at least 5 per
  day, and apply an 80/20 content mix (80% sharing/engaging with others' content, 20%
  self-promotion) — framed as building authority/E-A-T-adjacent credibility as much as
  direct lead gen (paraphrase, tactical rule).
- As of 2020-11-05, LinkedIn Ads format survey: single-image, video, carousel
  (sponsored content); sponsored/conversation messages; text ads (sidebar, pay-per-
  click or CPM); dynamic ads (personalized sidebar units); his own stated favorite is
  content ads for ROI, with the tactical rule to sponsor only content that already
  performs organically, not purely self-promotional posts (paraphrase, tactical
  opinion).
- As of 2020-11-05, recommends LinkedIn's Advanced Search (1st/2nd/3rd-degree
  connection filtering, saved searches) for building targeted prospect lists, paired
  with joining Groups as a free way to build authority and "E-A-T" (his own SEO-
  authority-signal vocabulary applied to social platforms) (paraphrase).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Share 80% of interesting content, only 20% of self promotion. You don't want to
> continually self promote all the time.

> If you push out stuff that's self promotional, but no one cares to like it, comment
> on it organically, chances are it's not going to do well when you push it out for the
> ads as well.
