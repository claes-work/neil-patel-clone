# 10 Small Things On Your Website That Are Ruining Your Traffic (Remove These Today!)

## Summary
A 10-point technical-SEO checklist of traffic-killing site issues: (1) accidental
noindex tags (often toggled unintentionally via a CMS checkbox); (2) thin content —
reframed as a value/thoroughness problem, not a word-count problem; (3) excessive
same-anchor-text internal/external links, which reads as unnatural to Google; (4)
uncompressed high-resolution images, hurting Google Image Search traffic; (5) intrusive
pop-ups that don't offset their annoyance with value (he defends his own site's pop-ups
on this basis); (6) unfixed 400/500 error pages; (7) redirect loops from repeated 301
consolidation; (8) orphaned pages with no internal links (his team links every new
piece of content from three older, relevant pages); (9) internal links pointing at
redirected (not final) URLs; (10) accumulated internal broken links, cleaned quarterly
on his own team. Ubersuggest's site-audit report is named as the tool for finding
errors, broken links, and redirect issues.

## Key claims
- Reframes "thin content" explicitly as a value/thoroughness measure, not a word-count
  threshold: 500 words can be sufficient if thorough; 10,000 words can still be thin if
  it isn't (paraphrase, dated 2021-11-13) — seo. Sharper articulation of the existing
  depth-over-length doctrine already in the wiki.
- Concrete internal-linking practice disclosed: his team links every new content piece
  from three older, relevant pages (fewer or more when relevance requires it) to avoid
  orphan pages (self-reported team practice, dated 2021-11-13) — seo, content-marketing.
  New specific operational detail.
- Discloses a quarterly cadence for auditing/fixing internal broken links on his own
  team (self-reported, dated 2021-11-13) — seo. New maintenance-cadence detail.
- Defends his own site's use of pop-ups on a value-vs-intrusion cost/benefit basis
  ("if the pop-up provides value and they're getting more value than the intrusion
  effect... you're good to go") rather than avoiding pop-ups altogether (paraphrase,
  dated 2021-11-13) — seo, analytics-cro.
