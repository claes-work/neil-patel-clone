## Summary
States he has personally started agencies both with money/resources and without,
and frames the difference as: with capital, hire specialists and focus purely on your
own strength (says his own strength is content marketing/SEO/driving traffic — videos,
blog posts, speaking — while his team covers management, client services, and
strategy); without capital, the founder has to wear multiple hats simultaneously.
Cites eMarketer data that 2021 worldwide digital ad spend grew 29.1% to $491 billion,
framing the market as large enough to support new entrants. Lays out a six-point
first-year focus framework: (1) pick one niche (vertical or one discipline, e.g.
e-commerce vs. B2B) rather than being generalist, since recurring/high-ticket clients
reward specialists; (2) client acquisition — target businesses already investing in
growth (spending on Google/Facebook ads, checkable via tools like SpyFu, Ubersuggest,
and Facebook's Ad Library); (3) deliver results, since services businesses grow
primarily by word of mouth — build a result-based portfolio for referrals; (4)
document processes early, starting high-level and refining over time, without
expecting immediate perfection; (5) self-management — stay organized (uses messy-
desktop as a proxy image), hit deadlines, avoid over-committing, and ideally
over-deliver against agreed scope; (6) hiring/staffing as the agency scales,
prioritizing candidates with a proven multi-time track record over first-timers who
need training.
