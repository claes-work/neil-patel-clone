# How to BOOST Website Conversions By Personalizing Your Website and Marketing Message

## Summary
Neil explains how personalizing a website experience lifts conversion rates, starting from the premise that only about one or two out of a hundred visitors realistically buy. He argues that the more a site relates to the individual visitor, the more likely they are to convert, and walks through three concrete personalization tactics: using Hello Bar to show different messages based on visit frequency, referrer, or keyword; using geoIP (via MaxMind) to insert a visitor's city, state, or country into headline and ad copy; and tailoring the experience by device type, noting that responsive design alone is not enough and that mobile visitors may need many page elements stripped out because of small screens.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-06, Neil states:
- Realistically only about one or two visitors out of a hundred will buy something from a website (as of 2017-10-06).
- The more a website relates and personalizes to the individual visitor, the more likely they are to buy (as of 2017-10-06).
- Hello Bar lets you show different messages depending on how many times someone has visited your site, or based on the referrer or a specific keyword they came from (as of 2017-10-06).
- On his own site, first-time visitors see a top message asking if they want more traffic, and answering yes versus no triggers a different follow-up message (as of 2017-10-06).
- GeoIP identifies the city, state, and country a visitor is coming from, letting you insert their location into headline or web page copy (as of 2017-10-06).
- Using customized geoIP (via MaxMind) to put a person's city, state, or country into ad or web page copy increased his conversions by over double digits, and it was a simple change (as of 2017-10-06).
- You should customize the experience by device type, showing desktop and mobile users different things (as of 2017-10-06).
- Responsive design alone is not enough; you may need to exclude many desktop page elements for mobile visitors because mobile screens are small (as of 2017-10-06).
