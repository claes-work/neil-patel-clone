# My 5 Favorite PPC Strategies

## Summary
Five PPC strategies. (1) Remarketing/retargeting — cites familiar visitors are 70%
more likely to purchase than via a competitor (as cited, dated 2023-03-06);
explains the mechanic (site tells Google/Facebook a visitor viewed-but-didn't-buy
a product, so ads follow them), works for both B2C and B2B. (2) Cross-channel
approach — the value isn't running many platforms (Google, Facebook, Instagram,
TikTok, Snapchat, Reddit, Pinterest) per se but transferring learnings between
them to raise ROI across all. (3) Mobile-friendly landing pages — cites over 50%
of clicks happen on mobile (as cited); argues desktop-style content ports poorly
to mobile, recommends visual-first design (images/video/GIFs), responsive layouts
across screen sizes, and a CDN to speed load times globally. (4) Use his free
"Google Ads Creator" tool (neilpatel.com) to audit and fix Google Ads accounts.
(5) Choose campaign type deliberately (search, display, social, Shopping, Stories
ads) rather than defaulting to one type; illustrates with a Snapchat example where
he adapts landing-page copy directly into the Snap unit to keep users on-platform,
self-reporting this increased his conversion rates. Closes with an NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- Cites: familiar/remarketed visitors are 70% more likely to purchase than to buy
  from a competitor (as cited, dated 2023-03-06).
- Cites: more than 50% of ad clicks happen on mobile devices (as cited, dated
  2023-03-06).
- Self-reports adapting landing-page copy directly into in-platform ad formats
  (e.g. Snapchat) instead of driving off-platform increases his conversion rates
  (self-reported, dated 2023-03-06).
