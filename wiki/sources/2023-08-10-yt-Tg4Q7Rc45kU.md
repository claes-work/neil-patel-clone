# Meme-Driven Growth Strategies: Fueling Your Success Beyond Trends

## Summary
Short co-hosted segment on using memes to grow social accounts faster. Eric prompts Neil to define
a meme and share a favorite (Neil describes a joke meme about a permanently closed Thai
restaurant captioned "strong SEO game"). Neil's core framework: posting industry-relevant memes
does not directly generate revenue or customers — viewers of a meme are not, in that moment, buyers
— but memes are a top-of-funnel follower-growth lever; the follower base a meme strategy builds
then sees the account's "normal" (non-meme) content at higher engagement, and it is that normal
content, over time, that builds the relationships converting a small percentage of the new
followers into customers. The critical constraint, repeated twice for emphasis: memes must be
tightly relevant to the account's industry — posting random/off-topic memes, even if they go viral,
attracts followers who won't engage with the account's real content and can damage the account's
long-term performance. Eric adds that Elon Musk (self-described as a "meme dealer") exemplifies
leveraging popular culture this way.

## Key claims
- States memes don't convert viewers directly into customers, but function as a top-of-funnel
  follower-acquisition tactic; normal (non-meme) content, posted the majority of the time, is what
  actually builds the relationships that convert a small percentage of new followers into
  customers over time (self-reported framework, dated 2023-08-10).
- States memes must be tightly relevant to the account's industry — off-topic memes, even viral
  ones, attract followers who won't engage with real content and can hurt the account's long-term
  performance (self-reported framework, dated 2023-08-10, stated twice for emphasis).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> But here's the key to growing faster using memes. This is where most people get it wrong.

> The memes have to be related to your industry... you post memes to blow up your account. You
> also post majority of the time your normal content, and your normal content is what helps you
> build relationships with those new followers and then starts converting some of them into
> customers.

> Just be careful though, when you're using these memes and jabbing away, the memes need to be
> super super highly relevant to your industry... If you can't adapt it to your industry, don't
> post it.
