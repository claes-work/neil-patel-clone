# My 4 Step Morning Routine for Productivity and Success

## Summary

An interview-style clip: an unnamed interviewer notes that gurus like Tim Ferriss stress the importance of a morning routine and asks whether Neil follows one. Neil describes his own (which he calls "not the most entertaining"): he wakes around 5 a.m. without an alarm, has CNBC on for market/business news, checks and answers email from bed for 20–30 minutes, then either changes into gym clothes or showers, does a ~35-minute "really efficient" workout (one minute on a bike, one minute of CrossFit-style exercises) at a gym called Base Camp Fitness, showers, eats breakfast while starting work, and spends the rest of the day on meetings, calls, and batched email. He emphasizes batching email into chunks rather than doing it all day to stay efficient. The core lesson he draws is not to copy his routine but to run a **daily task list**: tasks small enough to finish within the day, sourced both from himself (set early in the morning) and from teammates who assign him tasks. Completing them daily creates a sense of accomplishment and a "snowball effect" of progress; missing them once leads to missing them repeatedly.
