# Are blog posts the best SEO strategy today?

## Summary
Reacting to a LinkedIn post (Peter Caputa, Databox CEO) arguing that publishing more blog
posts to win search traffic — his own standard 2010-era advice — no longer works, Neil
gives a domain-authority-tiered SEO strategy: low-authority sites should build free tools
to earn links naturally (citing Ubersuggest/AnswerThePublic's link volume); sites that
already have authority and traffic should prioritize refreshing/updating existing content
over publishing net-new posts, since search users and Google favor recency (Wikipedia as
the running example); new content still matters, but mainly to cover topics not yet
written about.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Disagrees with the blanket "publish more blog posts" advice he says he used to give in
  2010 — for low domain-authority sites (~20–50 DA) he'd instead build free tools to earn
  links organically, because tools (his examples: Ubersuggest, AnswerThePublic) generate
  far more links on a continual/residual basis than most content ever could, without the
  ongoing manual-outreach effort content requires. (Neil, 2023-10-12) — seo.
- For sites that already have domain authority and traffic, prioritizes refreshing
  existing content over new posts: pulls Google Search Console data comparing a page's
  traffic now vs. a year ago and vs. three months ago, and if traffic has dropped on
  either comparison, updates that page (checks what competitors ranking above it now
  include, adds video/images, improves tone) rather than only writing something new.
  Argues Wikipedia ranks well less because its content is the best available and more
  because it stays fresh and current. (Neil, 2023-10-12) — seo / content-marketing.
- Still recommends mixing in genuinely new content regardless of a site's authority
  level, since not every topic in an industry has already been covered. (Neil,
  2023-10-12) — content-marketing.
- On link-building economics: infographics used to reliably earn ~20-plus good/unique
  referring domains each (he recalls NP Digital once spending ~$30,000 on a single
  infographic) but says that approach no longer pays off today the way free tools do —
  Ubersuggest alone has roughly 21,000 referring domains, including from Google, Adobe,
  GitHub, Shopify, Wix, Reddit, PayPal, and Trustpilot. (Neil, 2023-10-12) — seo.
