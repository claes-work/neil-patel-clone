# Why I Use Instagram For B2B | Connecting Through Social Media

## Summary

A short monologue on channel selection and focus. Neil notes that although he's on every major social platform (Facebook, LinkedIn, YouTube, Twitter, Instagram), his Instagram profile has essentially one image — he barely posts, runs no stories, and doesn't "market" there. The reason: he's in B2B, where Instagram is a much harder place to get customers than SEO, Facebook, LinkedIn, or YouTube, and with limited time in the day he picks the channels that work best for his business rather than spreading equal energy across all of them (unless you hire a team). What he *does* use Instagram for is personal connection with his team members — following employees, viewing their photos and updates, and engaging — because Instagram tends to carry more personal content than Facebook. He extends this to family, and closes with two takeaways: (1) don't try to be on every social network — pick the one most relevant to you, and it's fine to skip the popular ones; and (2) don't treat social networks purely as promotion channels or vanity ("Insta-famous") — use them to connect with people, build relationships, and genuinely help.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-12-08, Neil said he is on all major social platforms but barely uses Instagram (one image, no stories) because he's in B2B, where Instagram is much harder for customer acquisition than SEO, Facebook, LinkedIn, or YouTube.
- As of 2018-12-08, Neil said he isn't calling Instagram useless — but there are only so many hours in the day, so you must pick the channels that work best for your business rather than leveraging all of them with equal energy (unless you hire staff).
- As of 2018-12-08, Neil said he uses Instagram mainly to connect with his team members (following employees, viewing/commenting on their posts) because people post more personal content there than on Facebook — and that working with people is about knowing them personally, not just a paycheck.
- As of 2018-12-08, Neil's two takeaways: don't focus on all social networks — pick the one most related to you (it's okay to skip the booming ones); and don't use social only to promote products or chase "social media famous" — also use it to connect, build relationships, and help people.
