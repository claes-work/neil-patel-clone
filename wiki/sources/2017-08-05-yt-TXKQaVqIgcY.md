# Should You Place Social Sharing Icons On Your Website?

## Summary
Neil argues that whether to place social sharing icons is a per-page-type decision, not a site-wide one. He recommends NOT placing sharing icons on pages nobody shares — the homepage, contact page, and product pages (e-commerce or service) — reasoning that if you wouldn't share a friend's contact page yourself, your visitors won't share yours either. He recommends placing icons on the pages people actually share: blog posts, informational, and humor/entertainment content, and specifically icons that scroll with the content (via tools like Sumo or AddThis) to lift the click rate, since he estimates you are "lucky if half a percent" of visitors click them. He also stresses matching the chosen networks to the audience: Facebook/Twitter/LinkedIn for B2B, and Facebook/Twitter/Pinterest (dropping LinkedIn) for consumer niches.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-05, Neil states:
- Whether to place social sharing icons has no simple yes/no answer; it depends on the page type — some pages should have them, others shouldn't (as of 2017-08-05).
- Do NOT place social sharing icons on homepages, contact pages, or product pages (e-commerce/service), because people don't share those pages (as of 2017-08-05).
- The mental model: people share content their friends will value; if you yourself wouldn't share a given page (e.g., a buddy's contact page), other people won't either (as of 2017-08-05).
- ★ DO place social sharing icons on blog pages, informational pages, and humor/entertaining content, because people love sharing information, education, and funny content (as of 2017-08-05).
- Use sharing icons that scroll with the content, because making them follow the visitor is the best way to get more clicks (as of 2017-08-05).
- You are "lucky if a half a percent" (~0.5%) of your visitors click on social sharing icons — self-reported benchmark (as of 2017-08-05).
- Sumo and AddThis are recommended social sharing plugins for implementing scroll-along share icons (as of 2017-08-05).
- ★ Match the displayed networks to your audience: for B2B, Facebook/Twitter/LinkedIn are the best three; for consumer niches, consider Pinterest and drop LinkedIn (since LinkedIn is a B2B network) (as of 2017-08-05).
- Picking the wrong networks for your audience means no one clicks or shares your content (as of 2017-08-05).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "There really isn't a simple answer to this. For certain pages you should and for others you shouldn't."
> "People want to put out valuable content that their friends like. So, if you aren't willing to share something... you're probably not going to share your buddy's contact page. If you're not willing to do it, other people probably aren't as well."
> "You're lucky if a half a percent of your visitors click on them. So the best way to get more clicks on these social icons is to make them scroll with the visitor."
> "If you're in the B2B niche, Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn are probably the best three to use. If you're in the consumer niche, you could even consider using Pinterest."
