# How much are CEOs actually worth?, $300k LinkedIn services, The end of inbound SDRs?, and more

## Summary
Three segments. (1) Prompted by Eric asking how much CEOs are "actually worth," a discussion of Snowflake CEO Frank Slootman's resignation announcement coinciding with a ~$15B market-cap drop (stock fell to $175, recovering to $186 within days); Neil argues Slootman is valuable but not "worth $15 billion" — that's a short-term market overreaction (citing "in the short run the market is a voting machine, but in the long run it's a weighing machine," misattributed on-air to Warren Buffett before being corrected to Benjamin Graham) — and that comparable CEO talent is hireable for $20-50M/year; contrasts with the counterfactual that if Microsoft's Satya Nadella had taken over Snowflake instead, the stock likely would have risen on reputation/recency bias. Snowflake's negative-net-revenue-retention business model (existing customers spend more even when some churn) is cited as a reason the company should recover regardless of CEO uncertainty. (2) A case study on a LinkedIn ghostwriting/lead-gen consultancy charging a CEO $300K/year ($25K/month, split $10K for content-story-extraction coaching + $15K for inbox management and booking 40-50 qualified LinkedIn meetings/month, ~$375-800/meeting) — reactions are mixed: skepticism the $10K content-coaching tier is worth it, but openness to the $15K meeting-booking tier if it's cost-effective versus the value of closed deals. (3) A discussion of whether AI is ending the inbound SDR (sales development rep) role, citing a LinkedIn post from a SaaS founder who shrank his inbound SDR team from 15 to 3 after finding diminishing returns on incremental SDR headcount despite 5,000 monthly free-product signups; the hosts argue AI will likely handle simple/high-volume sales interactions but human-in-the-loop will remain necessary for complex, custom sales cycles, and predict a future swing where "AI-enhanced human" combinations outperform either AI-only or human-only approaches as customers tire of pure-AI interactions. Ends with a personal anecdote about rejecting AI-drafted (ChatGPT) social-content scripts from a team member for being low-quality/"crap," and a broader belief that creative "taste" is too personal/unique to fully delegate, illustrated with the "storyworthy" story-bank exercise (a running log of daily personal anecdotes to mine for content) as the alternative to paying for expensive story-extraction coaching.

## Key claims
- **Snowflake CEO Frank Slootman's resignation coincided with a ~$15B stock-market-cap drop, but this is framed as a short-term overreaction, not an accurate valuation of his worth**; comparable elite CEO talent is estimated hireable for $20-50M/year, and the drop is partly attributed to market uncertainty about an unfamiliar successor. (Neil, 2024-03-22) `(opinion, dated)`
- **A counterfactual: if Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella had been announced as taking over Snowflake instead, the stock likely would have risen rather than fallen**, illustrating a recency-bias effect in how markets price CEO transitions. (Neil, 2024-03-22) `(opinion, dated)`
- **Snowflake's negative net revenue retention (existing customers spend enough more, over time, to offset churn) is framed as a structural reason the company should remain healthy despite a short-term CEO-transition stock hit**, alongside rising demand for data warehousing in an increasingly AI-driven world. (Neil, 2024-03-22) `(opinion, dated)`
- **Skeptical of a $300K/year LinkedIn ghostwriting/consultancy service's $10K/month "story-extraction coaching" tier ("that sounds a little expensive to me, I don't think I'd go for that") but more open to its $15K/month qualified-meeting-booking tier**, reasoning that if you're closing more in revenue than you're spending on meeting acquisition, "who cares — do that all day long"; suggests the client should try to scale meeting volume well beyond the initial 40-50/month. (Neil, 2024-03-22) `(opinion, dated)`
- **Believes AI will not fully eliminate inbound SDRs**: expects AI to take over "really easy" high-volume sales interactions but predicts complex/custom sales cycles will continue to need humans, and forecasts a future swing back toward "AI-enhanced human" hybrid sales interactions as customers grow fatigued with pure-AI communication and companies offering a "human touch" see conversion-rate advantages. (Neil, 2024-03-22) `(opinion, dated)`
- **Rejected a batch of ChatGPT-drafted social-content scripts from a team member as low-quality ("this content's crap... too wordy, it didn't flow well") after being asked to record them on short notice at 4am while jet-lagged**, and states creative "taste" is too personal and unique to fully hand off/delegate — direct quality control over content stays with the person whose brand it represents. (Neil, 2024-03-22) `(self-reported, dated)`
- **Recommends the "storyworthy" exercise — a running Google Sheet story bank updated daily with the most interesting thing that happened that day — as a free/DIY alternative to paying an agency thousands of dollars to extract personal stories for LinkedIn content.** (Neil, 2024-03-22) `(opinion, dated)`
