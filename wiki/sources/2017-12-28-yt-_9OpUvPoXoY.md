# How to Start a Successful Blog and Make Money Online (Over $1,000 per Month)

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that blogging income is really a **traffic problem, not a monetization problem** — "without people you're not going to generate any income" — then covers both halves. Traffic: (1) **long-form content** — 2,000+ word posts tend to rank on page one, and readers prefer the actionable depth; (2) **blog on topics you're genuinely passionate about**, because passion is what sustains the consistency that beats competitors (he says he'll publish even on Christmas); (3) use **BuzzSumo** to find the most-shared posts in your niche and go dramatically deeper — turn a rival's "10 ways to boost your traffic" into "101 ways" so no one can copy you and you become the authority (his skyscraper move). Monetization: he dismisses AdSense, affiliate marketing, and selling ebooks/courses as low-yield, and argues for **high-ticket offers** — because blog visitors convert worse than e-commerce visitors (you need ~100–300 visitors per $50 sale), it's far easier to collect a lead, call, and close a high-ticket sale. He discloses that his own B2B marketing blog brings in consulting clients paying **a minimum of $120,000/year, up into seven figures**, and claims his blog can make "roughly seven figures" in a given month. Reinforces Neil's long-form-content and high-ticket monetization themes with concrete numbers.

## Key claims
As of 2017-12-28, Neil states:
- Blogging income is fundamentally a **traffic problem**: "monetizing is how you make money [but] it's actually about how you generate traffic ... without people you're not going to generate any income" — regardless of whether the goal is $500 or $1M (as of 2017-12-28).
- **Long-form content ranks**: "content that's over 2000 plus words tends to rank on page one of Google," and readers get more actionable value from it than from a 300-word post (as of 2017-12-28).
- **Blog on passion topics**: he can't pick your topic for you; passion sustains the **consistency** that is the real reason "I do so well compared to my competitors ... it's not that I'm smarter, I'm just consistent" — he even publishes on Christmas (as of 2017-12-28).
- **BuzzSumo skyscraper**: search keywords in BuzzSumo to find the most socially-shared posts, then go far deeper — "I'm not going to write on 10 ways to boost your traffic, I'm going to write on 101 ways" — so competitors can't copy you and you become the authority (as of 2017-12-28).
- **Low-yield monetization to avoid**: AdSense, affiliate marketing, and ebooks/courses can make money but are not the path to meaningful income (as of 2017-12-28).
- **High-ticket is the key**: blog visitors convert worse than e-commerce visitors — you need ~100–300 blog visitors to sell one $50 item — so collect a lead, "pick up the phone and ... close them," and you can "charge them 10 times more" than a website-only sale (as of 2017-12-28).
- Self-reported economics: his B2B marketing/entrepreneurship blog gets consulting clients "paying me a minimum of $120,000 a year ... up to well into the seven figures"; and "in any given month I can make roughly seven figures from my blog" (as of 2017-12-28).
- Advice: skip AdSense/affiliate/ebooks and "go figure out a high-end ticket offer" — retreats, masterminds, coaching, consulting — for both B2B and B2C (a yoga blog can sell a $1–2k retreat) (as of 2017-12-28).
