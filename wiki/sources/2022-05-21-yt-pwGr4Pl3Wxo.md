## Summary
Neil retells the "email list is a digital ATM" framing he credits to **John Reese**
(income.com, described here with the same big-fish-tank/Lamborghini color as the
existing 2021-01-25 source), this time adding origin detail: Reese taught him this
"when I was much younger in my early 20s." Neil self-reports his age as 37 at the time
of filming, which is consistent with a 1985 birth year for a 2022-05-21 publish date.
He then argues emails are still read at scale (306 billion emails sent/received daily
per Statista; Mailchimp's ~$11B acquisition as evidence people still value email) and
gives nine tactical tips: (1) scrub the list of non-openers to protect deliverability/
avoid the spam folder (his own open rate is above 30% from doing this); (2) use casual,
not overly polished subject lines (self-reported +12% open rate from his own testing);
(3) use the email list to grow social followers, tying into the rule of seven; (4)
follow up on abandoned carts (self-reported up to 18% reduction in abandonment); (5)
keep emails under 200 words (self-reported 7-38% higher click-through); (6) send early
morning, ~6am in the subscriber's local time zone; (7) add value even inside sales
emails (Legion Athletics example: >10% of revenue via email, workout content bundled
with product sells); (8) resend to non-openers with a changed subject line (self-
reported +8% open rate); (9) add countdown timers only for genuinely time-limited
promos (self-reported ~19% sales increase).
