# How to Increase Your Facebook Ad CTR and Pay WAY Less Per Click

## Summary

Neil explains how to lower Facebook advertising costs by improving click-through rate rather than paying per click. He argues that pay-per-click gets expensive when ads earn few clicks, so he prefers to buy on a per-impression (CPM) basis and then optimize CTR himself. His three levers are: a scroll-stopping image (either a bright image that pops or one that blends with Facebook's color scheme so it doesn't look like an ad), emotional or shocking ad copy (curiosity-driven headlines like "What Happened Next Will SHOCK YOU" or "#6 Will Amaze You"), and rotating audiences and creative to avoid ad fatigue. Do these, he says, and paying per impression drives overall Facebook ad cost down.

## Key claims

As of 2017-09-04, Neil states:

- Facebook offers two buying options — pay per impression or pay per click; most people prefer pay-per-click because they don't want to pay when no one visits their site (as of 2017-09-04).
- If you aren't getting enough clicks, paying per click is very expensive; he has generally found it cheaper to pay per impression and then optimize for click-through rate yourself (as of 2017-09-04).
- A strong image is the first CTR lever: use a bright image that pops out and stands out, because an image that blends in with Facebook won't be seen or clicked (as of 2017-09-04).
- Alternatively, images that blend in with Facebook's color scheme also generate a lot of clicks because they don't look like ads (as of 2017-09-04).
- The second lever is optimizing the text: copy without emotion or shocking value gets fewer click-throughs, while curiosity- and emotion-driven headlines increase clicks (as of 2017-09-04).
- The third lever is rotating who you advertise to: targeting a small audience and repeatedly showing the same person the same ad causes ad fatigue, so you must regularly rotate ad images or pick new people to advertise to (as of 2017-09-04).
- Optimizing images and text yields more clicks, and choosing the pay-per-impression option while doing so will lower your Facebook ad cost (as of 2017-09-04).
