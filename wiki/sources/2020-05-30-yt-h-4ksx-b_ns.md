# Over 4 Million Backlinks Built With This Simple Process - Module 05 - Lesson 1 - SEO Unlocked

## Summary
Opening lesson of the SEO Unlocked link-building module, framing links as a "voting
system" rather than a raw-quantity game — citing Get Rich Slowly vs. The Penny Hoarder
as an example of a site with fewer links out-traffic-ing one with more, and an Obama-
vs-Neil-Patel political-endorsement analogy for topical authority (a link's weight
scales with the linking site's relevance/authority in that subject, not just its
overall size). Introduces the "Link Building Score Card" (downloadable resource) with
essential criteria — referring domains (unique linking sites, not repeat links from
one site), dofollow vs. nofollow (dofollow passes "search engine juice," but nofollow
sites can still drive referral traffic/revenue), domain/page score (0-100 scale;
Wikipedia/Google cited as near-100 examples), and anchor text (natural variation is
expected; all-keyword-match anchors signal manipulation) — and optional criteria: link
placement (in-content > footer/sidebar), relevancy (acceptable to have some irrelevant
but harmless links, e.g. social profiles; unacceptable to have wildly irrelevant links,
e.g. a space website linking to a dental site), and reverse-engineering competitors'
referring-domain strategy via Ubersuggest's backlinks report. States two tools for
sourcing/outreach: Ubersuggest to find link targets, Mailshake for outbound outreach.
Cites Moz research that it takes roughly 10 weeks for a new link to move rankings, and
gives his own team's observed timeline: ~3 months to ramp up a link-building campaign,
another ~3 months before links "kick in" — arguing against stopping after the first
three months. Closes with common link-building mistakes: too many exact-match anchor
texts, too few semantic/variant anchors, not enough "junk" anchors (bare URLs, "click
here"), missing brand-mention links, buying links, and manipulative reciprocal link
exchanges.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-05-30, Neil said his combined sites (neilpatel.com, QuickSprout, Crazy
  Egg) have built "millions and millions of links" over his career — a self-reported,
  round-number claim consistent with the video's title framing ("Over 4 Million
  Backlinks").
- As of 2020-05-30, Neil cited Moz research that a new link takes roughly 10 weeks to
  affect rankings, and said his own team's observed pattern is ~3 months to ramp up a
  link-building campaign followed by another ~3 months before the links' ranking
  effect "kicks in."
- As of 2020-05-30, Neil recommended Ubersuggest (for finding link targets/analyzing
  competitors' backlink profiles) and Mailshake (for outbound outreach) as his two-tool
  link-building stack.
- As of 2020-05-30, Neil said natural anchor-text distribution (brand name, bare URL,
  keyword variants, generic phrases) is expected and healthy; anchor text that is
  overwhelmingly exact-keyword-match signals manipulation to Google.
