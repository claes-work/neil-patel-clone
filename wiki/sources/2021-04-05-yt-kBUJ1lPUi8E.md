# How to Build a Global Brand (7 Shortcuts I'm Using to Build My Brand Internationally)

## Summary
Solo tactical video listing "seven facts" for building a brand — despite the title,
the content is a general personal/corporate-branding framework, not specifically about
international expansion (title/content mismatch, consistent with other titled-for-CTR
mismatches already flagged elsewhere in the corpus). Fact 1: blogging is the highest-ROI
brand channel — cites an Eric Schmidt quote ("brands are the solution not the problem"),
gives a marketing-spend/conversion-rate/ROI-by-channel breakdown favoring SEO/blogging,
and a concrete regimen (blog weekly, headline-first, educate-don't-sell, short
paragraphs, 80% promotion / 20% writing early on, shifting to 30/70 once an audience
exists). Fact 2: omni-channel presence lowers CPA even for already-known brands (cites
an old Expedia Canada anecdote), with his own traffic-source breakdown: 43% blog, 11%
Facebook, 9% LinkedIn, 9% YouTube, 4% Twitter, 24% other. Fact 3: be yourself, not a
copy of another creator — explicitly contrasts himself with Gary Vaynerchuk ("I'm not
Gary Vee... I don't try to create content like him because I'm not him"). Fact 4: video
is the fastest brand-building channel; gives a 10-point YouTube-growth checklist
(keyword research via Ubersuggest, catchy headlines, subscribe CTAs, 5–10 minute
videos, email distribution, SRT captions via rev.com, strong hooks, comment CTAs).
Fact 5: personal connection via transparency + in-person events (4+ conferences/year)
and frequent social live video. Fact 6: repurpose content across channels rather than
creating new content per channel. Fact 7: consistency/time investment — the "rule of
seven" (7 brand touches before recall), with Kobe Bryant/Michael Jordan effort anecdotes.

## Key claims
- States blogging drives his highest brand ROI, citing an internal channel breakdown
  where 43% of customer discovery traces to his blog vs. 11% Facebook, 9% LinkedIn, 9%
  YouTube, 4% Twitter (paraphrase, self-reported, dated to this recording, 2021-04-05).
- States early-stage content creators should spend roughly 20% of time writing and 80%
  promoting, shifting toward 30% promotion once an audience is established (paraphrase,
  self-reported operating advice).
- Explicitly distinguishes his own content style from Gary Vaynerchuk's, framing
  "be yourself" / play-to-your-strengths as the core personal-branding principle rather
  than copying a specific creator's playbook (paraphrase, self-comparison).
- Cites an Expedia Canada anecdote (unspecified "years and years ago") that running
  ads across all channels simultaneously (TV, radio, magazine, Google, Facebook) lowered
  CPA and raised ROI versus single-channel spend, despite Expedia being already
  well-known — attributed to Expedia contacts, not independently sourced (self-reported
  third-hand anecdote, no date given for the underlying event).
- Recommends a 10-point YouTube-growth checklist: keyword research (via Ubersuggest),
  Copyblogger-style headline formulas, subscribe CTAs, 5–10 minute video length, email
  distribution on publish, SRT captions (via rev.com), strong opening hooks, and
  comment-prompting outros (paraphrase, tactical checklist).
