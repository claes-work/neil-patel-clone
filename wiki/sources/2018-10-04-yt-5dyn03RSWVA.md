# How to Manage (Multiple) Social Media Accounts

## Summary

Q&A Thursday, co-hosted with **Adam Lodolce** (from Viewership). Viewer question (Xavier
Alcaraz): best way to manage 6–8 clients' social media? Neil's tactics: (1) Use scheduling
tools — Hootsuite, Buffer, but he **prefers MeetEdgar** because it **automatically re-shares
the same content over time** (most people share a post once, but most followers never see
it; re-sharing multiplies traffic). (2) **Exception for Facebook — post manually.** When you
push to Facebook through Hootsuite/Buffer, reach drops sharply because Facebook wants you on
their platform (that's how they earn ad revenue) — Neil says he saw **2–3x more reach** by
posting the same content natively (scheduled inside Facebook's own tool) vs via a third-party
tool ("over a hundred percent" reach loss). Caveat: he last tested this "a year or two" ago.
His generalization: **every platform is selfish and rewards native/manual posting.** For
agency ops: (3) build **standardized reporting** per network (follower growth, clicks,
notable comments/shares) so it's plug-and-play per client; (4) **report at month-end for
every client** (not per billing cycle) so all reports can be produced together; (5) tools
help — Canva (images), Social Blade / vidIQ (stats), instagram tools like Social Upgrade;
(6) **niching your clients into one industry** (e.g. Einstein Medical doing doctors/dentists/
plastic surgeons) makes management easier and margins higher; (7) **communicate regularly**
(weekly or biweekly calls/updates) — even if you're doing great work, silence makes clients
assume nothing's happening and question the bill. Adam adds (attributed to Adam) that his
agency uses **Basecamp** for weekly client updates, though both agree busy executives often
don't read updates and **email is usually the best channel**.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-10-04, Neil **prefers MeetEdgar** over Hootsuite/Buffer because it **auto-
  re-shares content over time**, capturing followers who missed the first post.
- As of 2018-10-04, **post to Facebook manually/natively** — third-party tools (Hootsuite/
  Buffer) cut Facebook reach sharply; Neil observed **~2–3x more reach** posting natively
  (last tested ~2016–17; a dated, untested-since claim).
- As of 2018-10-04, "**every platform is selfish**" and rewards behavior that keeps you on
  its own site (manual/native posting).
- As of 2018-10-04, agencies should build **standardized per-network reporting** and deliver
  it **at month-end for all clients** (not per billing cycle) for batch efficiency.
- As of 2018-10-04, **niching clients into one industry** (e.g. Einstein Medical: medical/
  dental/plastic surgery) eases management and raises margins.
- As of 2018-10-04, **regular communication is non-negotiable** — without visible updates,
  clients assume no work is happening and question the bill, "even if they're your best friend."
