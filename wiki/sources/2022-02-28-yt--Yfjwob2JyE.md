# 3 Things You Shouldn't be Doing in Marketing for 2023 | And What To Do Instead

## Summary
Three anti-patterns he tells marketers to drop. First, manual/repetitive research
tasks — recounts personally doing manual competitor/keyword research by hand before
switching to plugging URLs into Ubersuggest, plus a mention of using UiPath for
automating other manual marketing tasks. Second, chasing channels with no revenue
ROI: uses his own near-non-participation in Clubhouse as the example (no link-out
capability, no clear path to revenue), arguing brand-presence value still justifies a
minimal footprint on channels people expect to reach a business through (cites 84% of
consumers wanting to reach businesses via social media), but that ROI-negative
channels shouldn't consume disproportionate time — trim to "the right time" and
redirect saved hours to higher-ROI channels. Third, consuming his own
content/marketing education without implementing it — directly tells viewers to stop
reading/watching his material if they aren't applying it, framing execution (not more
learning) as what drives results.

## Key claims
- Personally used to do SEO competitor research manually (searching top-10 results,
  analyzing title tags/meta descriptions/backlinks one by one) before switching to
  Ubersuggest for the same workflow (self-reported, dated 2022-02-28) — seo.
- Barely participated in Clubhouse when it launched, citing no ability to link out to
  his site as the reason he judged it low-ROI for his business (self-reported, dated
  2022-02-28) — social-media, analytics-cro.
- Cites 84% of consumers wanting to be able to reach businesses through social media
  (third-party stat, dated 2022-02-28) — social-media.
- Explicitly tells his own audience to stop consuming his content if they aren't
  implementing it, framing execution over further learning as the actual driver of
  results (self-reported guidance, dated 2022-02-28) — agency-entrepreneurship.
