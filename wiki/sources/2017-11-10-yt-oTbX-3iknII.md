# How to 10X Your LinkedIn Traffic By Posting At These Times

## Summary
Neil Patel explains when to post on LinkedIn and how to maximize referral traffic from it. Because LinkedIn is a B2B network, most users are on it during normal working hours (9-to-5), adjusted for where your connections are (e.g., 9-to-5 Eastern differs from Pacific). His three steps: (1) find where most of your followers/connections are located; (2) post during their 9-to-5 window; and (3) test posting a few hours earlier — he finds 6, 7, or 8am effective because your post is one of the first things people see when they log in at the start of the workday. His final tip for driving blog traffic: only post the first few paragraphs of your blog post with a "click to continue reading" link, which he says yields 5–10x more traffic from LinkedIn than posting the whole thing.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-10, Neil states:
- LinkedIn is a B2B social network, so unlike Facebook people are rarely on it late at night or early morning — most are on between 9 and 5 (normal working hours) (as of 2017-11-10).
- Posting time must account for where your connections are, since 9-to-5 in New York (Eastern) differs from 9-to-5 in Los Angeles (Pacific) (as of 2017-11-10).
- Step 1 — Find where most of your followers/connections are located (as of 2017-11-10).
- Step 2 — Post during their 9-to-5 working-hours window in the appropriate time zone (as of 2017-11-10).
- Step 3 — Test posting a few hours before 9-to-5; he finds 6, 7, or 8am effective because your post is among the first things people see when they log in at the start of work (as of 2017-11-10).
- Traffic tip — When promoting a blog post on LinkedIn, only post the first few paragraphs plus a "click to continue reading" link (as of 2017-11-10).
- Doing that yields 5–10x more traffic from LinkedIn than posting the full content (as of 2017-11-10).
