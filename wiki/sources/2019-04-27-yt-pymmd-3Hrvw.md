# Why The Links You're Building Don't Bring You Any Traffic (Building High-Quality Backlinks)

## Summary

Neil's thesis: most link building fails because it chases links from **low-traffic sites**;
the fix is to target links that themselves **drive referral traffic**, using competitor
backlink data in **Ubersuggest**. He claims ~**300,000 referral visits/month** to his own
site. Process: (1) put competitor URLs into Ubersuggest to see their total backlinks,
who links to them, and top pages; (2) open **Top Pages** to find their most popular content
and check whether you have comparable pieces; (3) click **View All** on the backlink count
to list every linking site; (4) **email those linkers** with a short "I have a similar but
better article that also covers X, Y, Z" pitch (his "Cheers, Neil" template); (5) run your
top competitors through the **backlinks report** and look for **patterns** — sites linking
to three-to-five of your competitors have no bias against you and will likely link to you
too. Crucially, re-check each prospective linker's own **traffic overview** in Ubersuggest
and prioritize the high-traffic ones, because a link from a no-traffic site does little.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-04-27, Neil said he gets **over 300,000 referral visits per month**, and that
  competitors with high traffic usually have many backlinks whose linking sites also send
  traffic.
- As of 2019-04-27, Neil's link-prospecting method: **Ubersuggest → competitor URL → Top
  Pages → View All backlinks**, then outreach to each linking site with a "mine covers what
  theirs didn't" pitch.
- As of 2019-04-27, Neil said to run multiple competitors through the **backlinks report**
  and find **patterns** — a site linking to 3–5 competitors has no bias and will likely link
  to you as well.
- As of 2019-04-27, Neil said to **re-check each prospective linker's own traffic** in
  Ubersuggest and only pursue links from sites that actually have traffic, since low-traffic
  links "isn't going to do well for you."
