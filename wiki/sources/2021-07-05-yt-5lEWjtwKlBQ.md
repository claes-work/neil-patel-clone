# The Best Keywords Aren't Popular Keywords | The Best Keyword Strategy You've Seen

## Summary
Solo screen-share tutorial arguing that high-volume head terms underperform low-volume,
high-intent long-tail terms for revenue. Uses his own site as a live case study: his
neilpatel.com page targeting "SEO" (110,000 monthly US searches, $9.49 CPC) ranks but
generates roughly one new customer per year, while a separate page targeting "SEO
company" (18,000 monthly searches, lower rank) generates roughly one new customer every
other month — six per year, i.e. 6x the customer-generation rate off a page with ~84%
less search volume. Generalizes into a keyword-selection rule of thumb: favor 3+-word
phrases, prioritize high-CPC terms (proxy for buyer intent/conversion likelihood) over
high-volume terms, and target an Ubersuggest SEO-difficulty score of ≤50. Walks through
Ubersuggest's Related/Suggestions keyword views to surface these long-tail, high-CPC,
low-difficulty terms (demoed with "digital marketing agency," "digital marketing
consultant," "digital marketing agencies near me," "digital marketing agencies in Los
Angeles"). Mentions in passing that he "used to live in Los Angeles, but we have staff in
Los Angeles" — no longer based there.
