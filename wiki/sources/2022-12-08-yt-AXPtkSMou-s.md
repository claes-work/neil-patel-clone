# How to Get More Social Engagement When Reach is Declining

## Summary
Diagnoses declining organic reach across Facebook/Instagram/TikTok/YouTube as a
function of content-volume saturation and personalized feed algorithms that favor
friends' content over business pages (and indirectly favor paid ads). Cites social
referrals stagnant at "5% of all traffic," average Facebook post reaching only 5% of
followers, and Facebook seeing 510,000 comments and 293,000 status updates posted every
minute. Five counter-strategies: (1) post at algorithm-favorable times — cites a
Hootsuite study of 30,000+ posts giving specific windows (overall best 10am
Tue/Wed/Thu; Instagram 11am Wed; Twitter 8am Mon/Thu; LinkedIn 9am Tue/Wed; TikTok 7pm
Thu) while noting these must be adjusted to the audience's actual time zone; (2) show
your face — cites a Georgia Tech/Yahoo Labs study of 1.1M Instagram photos finding
face-containing posts get 38% more likes and 32% more comments, drawing the parallel
to Musk/Zuckerberg/Gates as visible brand faces; (3) target the right audience rather
than chase vanity-metric likes, recommending Answer the Public for interest discovery;
(4) add page integrations/customizations (custom tabs, email capture, podcasts, video
players, quizzes, polls, contest apps); (5) test posting frequency rather than assume
more is better — states engagement actually decreases from over-posting, recommending
one to two posts/day on Facebook/Instagram/LinkedIn for most brands. Closes with an NP
Digital plug.

## Key claims
- States social-media referral traffic has been "stagnant at 5% of all traffic for
  quite some time" and the average Facebook post reaches only 5% of followers
  (uncited figures, dated 2022-12-08).
- Cites a Georgia Institute of Technology / Yahoo Labs study of 1.1 million Instagram
  photos: posts with faces are 38% more likely to get likes and 32% more likely to get
  comments (as cited, dated 2022-12-08).
- Cites a Hootsuite study of 30,000+ social posts for optimal posting-time windows per
  platform (as cited, dated 2022-12-08).
- States engagement decreases when brands post "too often," recommending roughly one to
  two posts/day on Facebook/Instagram/LinkedIn as a general (not universal) guideline
  (self-reported working heuristic, dated 2022-12-08).
