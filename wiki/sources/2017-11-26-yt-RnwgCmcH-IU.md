# Top 3 Ways to Generate More Organic Search Traffic | Neil Patel's Content Marketing Secrets!

## Summary
Neil Patel pushes back on the idea that writing content alone generates traffic, then lays out a competitor-driven "skyscraper" workflow to earn organic search traffic. (1) Use Ahrefs to enter competitor URLs, see their most popular pages, and write better/more-thorough versions (e.g., turn their "10 ways to double your search traffic" into "101 ways"). (2) Pull the list of sites linking to that competitor page and email each with a short template offering your more thorough article — a two-step outreach (soft pitch, then send the link on reply) that earns links and lets you outrank competitors, which he attributes to Google's Hummingbird algorithm favoring more thorough content. (3) Repeat the same process in BuzzSumo to find their most socially popular articles and write better ones. He provides a verbatim outreach email template.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-26, Neil states:
- Writing content does not automatically generate traffic; if it did, everyone would succeed at SEO and content marketing (as of 2017-11-26).
- Step 1 — in Ahrefs, enter competitor URLs to see their most popular pages, then write better content than each (e.g., their "10 ways to double your search traffic" becomes your "101 ways") (as of 2017-11-26).
- Step 2 — see who links to the competitor's page and email each linker with a short template pitching your more thorough article; on a positive reply, email the link — this earns links and lets you rank for and outrank the same terms (as of 2017-11-26).
- Google's Hummingbird algorithm promotes more thorough content, which is why the better/longer version can outrank the competitor (as of 2017-11-26).
- Step 3 — repeat in BuzzSumo: enter the competitor's URL to find their most socially popular articles (Facebook/Twitter/LinkedIn traffic) and write far better versions (as of 2017-11-26).
