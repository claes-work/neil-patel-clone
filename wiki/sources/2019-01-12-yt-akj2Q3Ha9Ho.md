# How to Own the Front Page of Google Not Just Rank #1 | Neil Patel SEO Basics

## Summary

A strategy for **owning the whole first page of Google** for a term, not just ranking #1,
by getting multiple of your own pages onto page one. (1) **Interlink your content** —
cross-link an in-depth pillar (e.g. "Online Marketing Made Simple") with offshoot
articles (tools, how-to, advanced guide) to raise both pages' chances of ranking.
(2) **Create multiple pages on the same topic** — five, six, seven angles (beginner's
guide, tools, getting started, advanced guide) to occupy more than one listing.
(3) **Don't duplicate content** — regurgitating the same content across your own site
creates a bad user experience and Google won't rank duplicate pages; write fresh, unique
pages. (4) **Build links** — to every version of the post you want to dominate with,
using Ahrefs **Link Intersect** to find sites linking to 3–5 competitors but not you.
(5) **Promote on the social web** — Facebook/Twitter/LinkedIn, sharing each main and sub
article four-to-five times over six months (most fans miss a single share). (6) **Update
outdated content** once a year (e.g. swap a dead reference like Vine for Instagram) to
keep everything ranking. Extreme version: repeat across multiple sites.
