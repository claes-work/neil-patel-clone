# 5 Clever Hacks to SKYROCKET Your Mobile Organic Search Traffic

## Summary

A solo tactical monologue on optimizing for mobile organic search, framed by the stat
that 58% of Google searches happen on mobile (mobile outweighs desktop by ~28 billion
searches per year). Neil's hacks: **(1) Responsive design** — one design that adapts
across mobile/tablet/desktop rather than maintaining separate mobile and desktop sites.
**(2) Fast load speed** — critical because users on weak reception bounce (back-button)
if pages are slow, and bounces signal low quality to Google. **(3) Google AMP framework**
— boosts mobile friendliness and eligibility in Google's mobile results; Neil notes it
didn't move the needle much in the US (fast devices) but produced roughly a **30% mobile
traffic increase in countries like Brazil**. **(4) Small header image + readable content**
— large headers force scrolling on small screens; keep body text large and dark. **(5) No
intrusive mobile pop-ups** — full-screen pop-ups are hard to dismiss on mobile, raise
bounce, cut time-on-site, and can make rankings "plummet." Bonus: because of Google's
mobile-first index, show Google ALL your content (avoid "load more" hiding, and let it
crawl your CSS/HTML) so it can understand your mobile site architecture.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-06-26, Neil cites that **58% of Google searches happen on mobile**, and
  mobile searches exceed desktop by an extra **~28 billion per year**.
- As of 2018-06-26, Neil recommends a single responsive design over separate mobile and
  desktop sites, and says fast load speed is essential because slow pages cause
  back-button bounces that signal low quality to Google.
- As of 2018-06-26, Neil says leveraging Google AMP improved mobile traffic materially in
  markets like **Brazil (~30% increase)** but had little effect in the US (users there
  have fast devices).
- As of 2018-06-26, Neil warns that intrusive full-screen mobile pop-ups increase bounce
  rates, reduce time-on-site, and can cause rankings to plummet.
- As of 2018-06-26, Neil says that under Google's mobile-first index you must expose ALL
  content to Google (avoid "load more" concealment; allow crawling of CSS/HTML) so it can
  read your mobile site architecture and rank you correctly.
