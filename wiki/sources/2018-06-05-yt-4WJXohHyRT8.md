# 3 Lessons Learned From Spending Over $400k on Facebook Ads

## Summary

A solo tactical monologue where Neil shares three lessons from spending "over $400,000" on
Facebook ads. **(1) Don't buy subscribers/fans.** He built a fan page to over 900,000
fans, but algorithm changes throttle organic reach, so paying to accumulate fans (the
"well" model — build it once and keep drawing traffic) was "a huge mistake." Better to pay
per click to drive people to a product/service page and generate revenue; at his peak he
ran a 3:1 return (later stopped selling info products because he didn't enjoy it).
**(2) Video ads outperform banners** — most people default to banners because they're
easiest, but video costs less and converts higher. He advises adding on-screen
transcription/captions (Facebook video autoplays muted), conveying emotion, good length,
and a story hook. Optimize for ROI, not the cheapest click. **(3) Rapid creative
rotation** — Facebook's algorithm gives new ads a priority test window (first minutes/hour);
his team exploited this by launching ads and swapping creative/text (or stopping and
relaunching) within an hour, earning cheaper impressions and premium placements. He claims
this reduced spend by "over 30%" at peak. It didn't scale to their largest campaigns but
they still use it for many client campaigns.

  better than the "well" model of accumulating subscribers.
- As of 2018-06-05, Neil says at his peak he ran a 3:1 ROI on Facebook ads (≈$3 revenue per

  Facebook video autoplays without sound.
- As of 2018-06-05, Neil says to optimize for ROI, not lowest cost-per-click — "it's okay
  to be spending an extra dollar per click if you're making more money."
- As of 2018-06-05, Neil says rapidly rotating ad creative/text within the first hour
  exploits Facebook's new-ad priority window and cut his spend by "over 30%" at peak; it
  didn't scale to the biggest campaigns but is still used on many client campaigns.
