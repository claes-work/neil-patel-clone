# Shake Shack vs Chick-fil-A, TikTok ban strategies, Facebook's most important historic event, & more

## Summary
Opens reacting to a Shake Shack promotion targeting Chick-fil-A's Sunday closure (free
chicken sandwich with code). Covers Meta licensing its Quest headset OS to third-party
hardware makers (compared to Windows-style OS licensing), with both hosts skeptical VR
has reached mass-market usability (citing low usage of gifted Vision Pros) versus optimism
about Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses' more wearable form factor. On the looming TikTok
divest-or-ban law, Neil predicts a buyer inevitably emerges (nobody walks away from
~$100B of value) and frames it as a question of Chinese vs. American ownership rather
than a real ban, adding that potential acquirers (Google, Microsoft, Meta, Oracle) will
likely be blocked by antitrust concerns. Reads a widely-shared Peter Thiel-sourced
account of Facebook's board debating a 2006 ~$1B Yahoo acquisition offer at ~$40M
revenue/no profit — both hosts say they would have taken $1-2B at the time, while
crediting Zuckerberg's conviction (not needing the money, believing in unreleased
product roadmap) as the reason he didn't sell. This leads into Neil describing his own
posture toward private-equity acquisition interest: he always declines, prefers when PE
firms come to him for an in-person meeting/free meal rather than the reverse, and
self-describes as a "cheap date" (doesn't drink, doesn't eat much, prefers simple/free
meals) — paired with a broader stated preference for bootstrapping over VC (dislikes
board oversight and people-management), while noting VC still makes sense for
capital-intensive/technical bets like building an LLM. Closes on an Amazon Prime Video
history tangent (predecessor failed products "Amazon Video On Demand"/"Amazon Unbox",
folding video into the existing Prime subscription rather than charging separately) as
an example of a company "killing its own business" to reinvent, quoting Mark Cuban's
"how do I kick my own ass" framing.
