# How to Write Blog Posts That Are Better Than Any Other Content Piece On The Web (And Rank Page #1)

## Summary
A three-step tactical framework for out-writing competitors, reinforcing existing
doctrine with the same recurring Ubersuggest workflow: (1) competitive analysis on the
top-10 ranking results — checking content length (explicitly debunking the "2,200
words to rank" rule as format-dependent: a "how to tie a tie" article needs a video or
GIFs, not a wall of text), pulling recurring subheadings/gaps, and judging what value
the top results deliver to visitors (calculators, product pages, long-form depth) over
pure Google-pleasing; (2) own research via Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas report (mining
questions/prepositions/comparisons) plus Quora and Reddit for adjacent popular
subtopics; (3) raise production standards — add data visualizations/infographics,
image carousels, and tutorial video, and write headlines "amazing enough people will
click" while explicitly distinguishing this from clickbait (which disappoints on
click-through). Closes with the content-ideas report as an outreach-list generator:
find who linked to a competitor's article and pitch them the better version.

## Key claims
- Debunks a fixed word-count rule ("2,200 words to rank") as one-size-fits-all advice;
  content length should match user intent and format (paraphrase, dated 2021-09) —
  content-marketing. Reinforces existing quality-over-arbitrary-length doctrine.
- Frames "amazing headline" as distinct from clickbait: clickbait earns a click but
  disappoints on arrival; the goal is a headline that earns the click AND a page that
  delivers (paraphrase, dated 2021-09) — content-marketing.
- Reuses the Ubersuggest content-ideas-report → outreach workflow (find who linked to a
  competitor, pitch them the superior replacement) already documented from earlier
  sources — reinforcing, not new.
