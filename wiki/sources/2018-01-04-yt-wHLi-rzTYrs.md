# SEO Training: 3 Steps to Generating 100K Visitors Per Month in Organic Traffic [REAL LIFE EXAMPLE]

## Summary
A live coaching session: Neil Patel walks a real person ("Adam," starting a
marketing/education blog at viewership.com about growing YouTube traffic, agency
model, no paid ads) through how to reach 100K organic visitors a month. Neil first
**reframes the goal** — a traffic number is "totally arbitrary"; for an agency you
don't need 100K visitors, you can go straight to businesses that already have traffic
and revenue. He praises Adam for choosing the **agency model over info-products** and
for being willing to give away his "secret sauce" — because the people who can afford
to pay you have enough money that they'd rather hire you than DIY. His concrete play:
approach **influential blog owners who aren't yet doing video** (cites Legion Athletics
/ Muscle For Life in fitness, "8-figure plus," 1M+ visits/month) and help them add
video. For the organic-traffic build itself he gives his standard **content + links
grind**: write more in-depth guides than what ranks; use BuzzSumo to find the
most-shared articles and Ahrefs to find who links to them; email 100 people to earn
~5 links; link out to competitors with great content and ask them to share; then
outsource the outreach via Upwork (drafts prepared in Gmail, approved, then sent).
Timeline: impressions by ~3 months, some traffic by 6, real traffic at the 10–12
month mark — and only if you target mainstream keywords (30–40K+ searches/month,
checked in Google Keyword Planner / Ubersuggest). Reinforces Neil's agency-over-info-
products stance and his content-depth + outreach link-building playbook.

## Key claims
As of 2018-01-04, Neil states:
- A round traffic goal like "100K visitors/month" is usually **arbitrary**; interrogate
  the real business reason. For an agency, you don't need your own traffic — you can go
  directly to businesses that already have traffic and revenue and sell them a new
  channel (as of 2018-01-04).
- **Agency model beats info-products** for this case, and giving away your best tactics
  is correct: "the guys who can afford to pay you have enough money where they don't
  give a [damn] to do it on their own — they'd rather just pay you and get the work
  done" (as of 2018-01-04).
- First move: **work with influencers/business owners who have a big audience but aren't
  doing video** (e.g. high-ranked blogs) — they already get Google traffic and revenue,
  so adding video is an easy expansion sell (cites Legion Athletics / Muscle For Life,
  fitness, 1M+ visits/month, "over 10 million a year") (as of 2018-01-04).
- Foundation for organic traffic: **write more in-depth guides** than the existing
  ranking articles, then earn links to them (as of 2018-01-04).
- Outreach mechanics: BuzzSumo to find the most-shared articles (by social shares) →
  Ahrefs to list everyone linking to them → email/"beg" them for a link; **~100 emails
  yields ~5 links** — "it's a grind but it works" because "no one else is willing to"
  (as of 2018-01-04).
- Three-part outreach system: (1) link-building outreach, (2) social-share outreach via
  BuzzSumo, (3) **link out to others' great content — even competitors — then tell them
  you did**, and many will reciprocate by sharing (as of 2018-01-04).
- **Scale outreach by outsourcing**: hire cost-effective help on Upwork (mentions India),
  build a spreadsheet of targets, have them prepare emails as **drafts in Gmail** (using
  a company Gmail address at his own domain), approve in bulk, then send (as of 2018-01-04).
- Realistic SEO timeline: impressions in Google Search Console by ~3 months, some traffic
  by 6 months, and traffic "really picks up" at the **10–12 month** mark (as of 2018-01-04).
- Keyword selection gates the outcome: you must target **mainstream keywords** (roughly
  30,000–40,000+ searches/month, checked via Google Keyword Planner and Ubersuggest);
  low-volume keywords make 100K/month unrealistic (as of 2018-01-04).
- Summary formula: "create awesome content that's better than everything else that's out
  there, push it out and get links naturally … and use Upwork to outsource the muscle
  work"; most people simply won't do the grind (as of 2018-01-04).
