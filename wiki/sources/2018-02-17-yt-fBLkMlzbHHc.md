# How to Turn Passion into Profit | Tips to Turn Your Dreams into a Killer Career

_Conversational format with **Adam Lodolce** (viewership.com) interjecting; Neil is the
main speaker. Only Neil-attributed material is persona data._

## Summary
Neil's counter to "what if your passion doesn't make money": almost any passion can be
monetized if you drop the **conventional route** (which forces you to compete with everyone
else) and apply an **entrepreneurial mindset + creativity** instead. Worked examples: an
artist who takes a painting degree and waits for buyers won't earn — but one who paints
what's **trending** and sells it via **Facebook ads** (funded on a credit card / net-30
terms) can. His friend **Sasha** couldn't get hired after law school, so she went to
networking events, gave legal help "for pennies on the dollar," earned referrals, raised her
rates, and within ~2 years hit a **six-figure yearly income (~$80K profit)** — all without a
firm hiring her. A second creative play: approach an in-demand artist and offer to take the
**leads they're too busy for or who can't afford them**, serve those at one-tenth the price
(disclosing you're not the original), and pay the artist a **10% kickback** — free money to
them on leads they'd otherwise waste. The through-line: the traditional path fails, but
hard work + creativity applied to anything can make money.

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-17, Neil states:
- **Passion can almost always be monetized** — the reason most passions "don't make money"
  is people take the **conventional route** (e.g. a painting degree, or law school → job
  hunt) and end up competing with too many people.
- **Apply the entrepreneurial mindset to anything** and you can make money; take the
  traditional route and "of course you won't do well."
- **Artist example**: instead of waiting for buyers, paint **what's trending/"cool and hip,"**
  run **Facebook ads**, and sell — financeable on a credit card or **net-30** terms, so lack
  of capital is no excuse.
- **Sasha (law) example**: no firm would hire her; she networked, did legal work cheaply,
  earned referrals, raised prices, and within ~2 years reached a **six-figure yearly income**
  with **~$80K profit** (after expenses).
- **Overflow-leads arbitrage**: approach a busy/expensive artist, take the leads they can't
  serve or who can't afford them, charge **~1/10** the price (disclose you're not the
  original artist), and give the artist a **~10% kickback** on what you make — "free money to
  them" on leads they weren't using anyway.
