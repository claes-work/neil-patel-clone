# How To Increase Your eCommerce Revenue With Amazon - Module 3 - Part 1 - eCommerce Unlocked

## Summary
eCommerce Unlocked lesson on selling via Amazon specifically. Frames Amazon success as
internal promotion (product listing quality, keyword optimization, the "more details"
tab, customer support) plus external promotion (Amazon Product Ads, YouTube marketing,
Lightning Deals, other social channels). Cites a Jeff Bezos "not too big to fail" quote
on customer obsession. Product-page checklist mirrors general CRO doctrine (images/
video/bullets over paragraphs, detailed descriptions, keyword placement in title/
description/bullets via Ubersuggest keyword research) applied to Amazon's ranking
algorithm specifically: "a good product page means higher conversions... [Amazon]
rank[s] your product higher... when people are doing searches." Sets a **review
velocity target**: get to 15 reviews as fast as possible (then 30, 50, 100+),
explicitly warning against manipulated/non-organic reviews ("you don't want to
manipulate them... if they're not natural... it's going to cause your listing to go
down"). Introduces **ACOS** (advertisement cost of sale — ad spend ÷ sales) as the
core paid-Amazon-ads profitability metric, with a concrete campaign playbook: one auto
+ one manual PPC campaign, default bid ~$0.50-$1, "dynamic bids down only," bid toward
the second-place bid (not the top bid, to avoid overpaying), roll bids down monthly as
profitability improves. First-month checklist: rush 15 reviews, use auto PPC, bid
$1-2, $50/day max budget, minimum $5/day spend to start.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-07-06, Neil defined ACOS (advertisement cost of sale) as the core metric
  for Amazon PPC profitability — ad spend divided by resulting sales — and
  recommended running one auto campaign plus one manual campaign as the "bread and
  butter" PPC structure.
- As of 2020-07-06, Neil's stated Amazon bid strategy: "dynamic bids down only,"
  targeting roughly the second-place bid rather than the top bid to avoid overpaying,
  rolling bids down monthly once a listing becomes profitable.
- As of 2020-07-06, Neil set an explicit review-velocity target of 15 reviews as fast
  as possible (then 30/50/100+), and drew a hard ethical line against manipulated
  reviews: "you really do need natural five star reviews, because if they're not
  natural... it's going to cause your listing to go down."
- As of 2020-07-06, Neil cited Amazon at 2.51 billion visitors (unsourced, likely a
  monthly-traffic estimate) and a Jeff Bezos quote on customer obsession ("Amazon is
  not too big to fail... if we start to focus on ourselves, instead of focusing on our
  customers, that will be the beginning of the end").

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "It's not about gaming the system. It's about doing what's best for your customers."

> "You really do need natural five star reviews, because if they're not natural, chances are in the future, you're going to get more reviews, they're going to be terrible."

> "As long as it's profitable, and ideally, the more profitable, the better, you should keep spending."
