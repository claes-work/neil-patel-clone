# How to Find the (PERFECT) Niche for Your Business

_Q&A Thursdays; **Adam Lodolce** (viewership.com) hosts/asks and adds commentary, Neil answers.
Only **Neil-attributed** material trains the persona. Viewer question (J. Brooks, apparently
ex-military, ER nurse with a business/CS degree): how to pick a niche that isn't too crowded,
avoiding saturated spaces like internet marketing/SEO._

## Summary

Neil's core reframe: **"don't pick a niche based on income, pick a niche based off of passion,"**
and make sure it has a **large total addressable market (TAM)**. Chasing an "uncrowded" niche is
the wrong lens — if you're not passionate you won't out-work the space, and **a small market caps
your upside**. His memorable inversion: it's **easier to make $50K out of a $100B market than out
of a $1M market**, no matter how uncrowded the small one is. People don't make money by *wanting*
money; **they make money by solving a problem** — so find what you're passionate about, identify
the problems you and others face in that space, and solve them. If you don't know your passion,
**try new things every day** until you learn what you like and are good at (which usually coincide);
test a promising interest for a week, then a month, to see if it sticks. Two concrete **market-sizing
methods**: (1) **Google Trends** — compare your industry's keywords against "digital marketing" to
gauge relative size (aim for much larger); (2) **look at publicly traded companies** in the space —
big market caps (hundreds of millions to billions), attributing the relevant revenue line-item to
your target industry, signal a big niche. Then carve a specific "niche" within that large market and
solve a real problem. Adam adds the "**what problem am I uniquely qualified to solve?**" lens (using
his own YouTube-channel → Viewship pivot as an example) — marked as co-host commentary.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-10-25, **pick a niche by passion, not income**; passion drives the effort needed to win,
  and **money follows from solving a problem**, not from wanting money.
- As of 2018-10-25, **prioritize a large TAM** — "it's really easy to make fifty grand out of a
  hundred billion dollar market" and hard out of a $1M market; an uncrowded small niche caps upside.
- As of 2018-10-25, to **find your passion**, experiment daily and escalate promising interests
  (week → month); what you like and what you're good at usually align.
- As of 2018-10-25, **size a market two ways**: (1) **Google Trends**, comparing your keywords to
  "digital marketing"; (2) **public companies' market caps**, attributing the relevant revenue
  line-item to your target industry.
- As of 2018-10-25, then **carve a specific niche inside the large market and solve a problem** —
  "look for a bigger market, don't look for a small one."
- **(Adam Lodolce, co-host — not persona data):** ask "**what problem am I uniquely qualified to
  solve?**"; pair a big market with a unique angle (his YouTube-channel → Viewship pivot as example).
