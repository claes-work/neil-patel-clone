# 3 Powerful Tips to Explode Your Facebook Videos

## Summary

A "Q&A Thursday" episode with co-host Adam (from Viewership) answering a viewer question (from "Ruchi Singh") about ideal Facebook video length and whether to share YouTube videos on Facebook. Neil's core guidance: (1) **Never share/link YouTube videos on Facebook** — Facebook suppresses them because it doesn't want to grow YouTube; upload the video **natively** to Facebook (re-upload the same video), which he says yields not 2–3× but 20–50× more views. (2) **Longer native videos perform better** — ideally 5+ minutes (5–10 minutes is a good range; ~30 minutes starts to get too long) because Facebook is prioritizing video (Facebook Watch) and wants to keep people on-platform; he cites a study claiming ~90% of the Facebook news feed will be video "by next year." (3) Optimization tactics: upload a transcription if allowed, target the audience by language/region for higher engagement, write a compelling title and a long engaging description that asks questions at the end, and add **captions** (he admits he does captions on YouTube but is "lazy" about it on Facebook/LinkedIn) — friends report ~30–35% more views/engagement with captions. He adds: make content controversial/story-driven, use authentic body language and passion (don't fake it), shoot on an iPhone (no fancy studio needed), use relevant tags (Ubersuggest can help find keywords), and lean into whatever a platform is currently promoting — e.g., Facebook Live — to get free reach.
