# The Best Time to Post On Facebook | Facebook Marketing Tips!

## Summary
Neil Patel says there is no universal best time to post on Facebook because every audience is different. He recommends using Facebook Insights (available on a business/fan page — create one if you don't have it) to see when your followers are online, but warns that Insights only measures engagement and isn't a definitive answer. The only real way to know is to test: schedule posts across rough windows (5am, 8am, noon, 2pm, 4pm, 8pm), find which windows perform best, then post within an hour of those. He also stresses that weekdays and weekends behave differently — in his experience Monday–Friday perform the same, while Saturday and Sunday differ — so you should test each day separately.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-09, Neil states:
- There's no single best time to post on Facebook because every person, business, and profile — and their followers — is different (as of 2017-11-09).
- You should have a Facebook business/fan page; if you don't, create one (as of 2017-11-09).
- Facebook Insights shows when your audience is on Facebook and breaks it down by engagement (as of 2017-11-09).
- Audience geography matters: most of his customers/income are US-based, and someone based in Europe (≥8 hours ahead of his Pacific time) should not copy his posting times (as of 2017-11-09).
- Insights isn't a definitive answer because it measures engagement (which posts did well and when) rather than telling you exactly when to post (as of 2017-11-09).
- The only real way to know your best time is to test — schedule posts at rough windows: 5am, 8am, noon, 2pm, 4pm, 8pm (as of 2017-11-09).
- Once you see which windows perform best, post within an hour of those windows and refine (as of 2017-11-09).
- Weekdays and weekends differ, so test each day; in his experience Monday–Friday perform the same, but Saturdays and Sundays differ from weekdays (as of 2017-11-09).
- On his personal Facebook profile he posts at roughly the same time Monday–Friday, and a different consistent time on Saturdays/Sundays (as of 2017-11-09).
