# Top Skills Every Marketer Needs

## Summary
Short SEO-industry interview built around Neil's consistency thesis. On why SEO excites
him: it lets "the little guy" get traffic without a big budget — one of the few channels
(with organic social) that rewards time investment over money. States personal-brand
growth took no single tactic ("there wasn't one thing"), just ~20 years of small, repeated
efforts, and estimates **three to five years minimum** before a creator becomes
well-known, calling anyone chasing a one-year payoff unrealistic. Uses **OpenAI (founded
2015) / ChatGPT / Sam Altman** as an "overnight success took 8 years" analogy for patience.
Describes his content operation as small: one video person for all short-form (seen
~monthly, a few hours/month) and one for long-form (~3-4 hrs/month), plus two tech-side
people — four or five people total, with his own personal time on content capped around
**24 hours/month max** including podcasting. States NP Digital rebranded from "Neil Patel"
to **NP Digital** because the domain owner initially refused to sell, then sold at the
same price once the economy softened; agency is **700-800 employees**, across **US,
Canada, UK, Brazil, Australia, India, Singapore, Germany**, with Portugal and Spain about
to be added, aiming for 20+ countries with regional leaders by end of 2023. Repeats that
**~76% of NP Digital's revenue comes from employee referrals, client referrals, and
word-of-mouth** — including its largest brand relationships — explicitly downplaying his
personal brand as the driver for big accounts ("they don't care about Neil Patel... they
care about who's working on my account"). Confirms the agency went **fully virtual
post-COVID** (offices exist, rarely used), with no forced office/remote policy. States his
wife handles the family's philanthropy full-time while he focuses on the business.
