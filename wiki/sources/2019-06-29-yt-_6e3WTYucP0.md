# How to Increase Your eCommerce Sales by 10% With Email Marketing

## Summary

Neil says an eCommerce store can lift sales ~**10%** with email marketing, because most site
visitors won't buy on the first visit and email beats remarketing for winning them back. Five
tactics: (1) **collect emails** using incentives (a **$10 coupon**, free shipping) via tools
like Hello Bar — quick to set up; (2) **write relevant, segmented copy** — email a visitor who
browsed toilet paper about the bathroom, not televisions; segment or the campaign underperforms;
(3) do **partial checkouts** — capture name and email on the first checkout step (credit card on
a later page) so you can follow up with people who don't complete; (4) **segment buyers** into
their own list — after one purchase it's far easier to drive a second, third, or fourth, so keep
emailing them upsells and downsells for extra revenue with no added ad spend; (5) bonus — use
**subscribers** to collect browser push subscribers who can be pulled back to finish checkout.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-06-29, Neil said email marketing can increase eCommerce sales by roughly **10%**,
  and that collecting emails outperforms **remarketing** alone for non-buyers.
- As of 2019-06-29, Neil recommended **incentivized email collection** ($10 coupon, free
  shipping) via tools like **Hello Bar**.
- As of 2019-06-29, Neil stressed **relevance and segmentation** — email copy must match the
  product/page the visitor engaged with, not unrelated products.
- As of 2019-06-29, Neil recommended **partial checkouts** (capture name + email first) to
  enable cart-abandonment follow-up.
- As of 2019-06-29, Neil said to **segment existing buyers** and keep emailing upsells/downsells,
  since a second purchase is much easier than the first — plus a **push-notification** (subscribers)
  bonus for browser re-engagement.
