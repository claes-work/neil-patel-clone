# How to Find 1000 Blog Post Ideas in Less Than 1 Minute

## Summary
A tactical Ubersuggest walkthrough for sourcing blog-post ideas by mining competitors'
under-served content. Method: type a broad seed keyword (≥1,000 monthly searches
ideally) into Ubersuggest, scroll to the Content Ideas report, and sort by social
shares/estimated visits/backlinks — looking specifically for older, thin articles that
already earn some traffic and backlinks but are shallow or outdated. Demonstrates on a
2014 "10 essential tips for B2B marketing" article: critiques it as short, generic,
image-free, and stale, then shows how to out-produce it — more depth per point,
concrete platform links (WordPress/Wix/Shopify), updated data — to outrank it.
Instructs exporting the target article's ranking keywords (via Ubersuggest's Keywords
report) and its backlink sources, so the keywords can be woven into the new article and
the same linking sites can be pitched once the new piece is live. Closes with a
keyword-expansion step: run the target keyword through the Keyword Ideas report's
suggestions/related/questions/prepositions/comparisons tabs and fold in any
tangentially relevant terms as "extra traffic."

## Key claims
- As of 2020-08-27, Neil's content-gap method: find competitor articles with above-zero
  but middling traffic/backlinks/shares (not the top performers) as beatable targets,
  using Ubersuggest's Content Ideas report.
- As of 2020-08-27, Neil's stated heuristic for why older articles underperform:
  "Google likes ranking fresh content" — age alone, independent of a change in quality,
  can suppress traffic.
- As of 2020-08-27, Neil's outreach workflow: export a target article's backlink
  sources, then pitch those same linking sites once a more detailed replacement article
  is published.
- As of 2020-08-27, Neil advised including tangential/related keywords from
  Ubersuggest's suggestions even when "some of these keywords won't be related to the
  article" — for incremental traffic.
