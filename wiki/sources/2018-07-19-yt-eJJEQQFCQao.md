# Is Cryptocurrency REALLY Here to Stay?

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode (Neil with interviewer Adam) answering a viewer's question about his
views on cryptocurrency. Neil frames the space (Bitcoin, Ethereum/Ether, BTC, Ripple; points
to CoinMarketCap for prices/exchanges) as full of "hocus pocus" coins that will fail and lose
people money — the 2017 "put in $1,000, now worth a million" era is over, and 2018 is slower.
He draws a stock-market analogy: new markets are volatile (big gains and losses), then level
off as they saturate with more investors — less volatility means smaller losses but smaller
gains. His core opinion: **cryptocurrency is here to stay**, and he calls Bitcoin "the gold of
the internet" (crediting others for the phrase, not himself). His personal anecdote: a
conference in Asia struggled for over a month to wire him his $50,000 speaking fee (it failed
several times), then paid him in Bitcoin in under two minutes — which he says he'll pay taxes
on. He argues crypto's appeal is simplicity (peer-to-peer transfer without banks/bureaucracy/
wire fees and multi-day clearing) and a hard cap on supply, versus the US dollar the
government "keeps printing." He explicitly disclaims being an investor or giving financial
advice, says it's very speculative and not to put all your money in, but urges everyone to at
least learn about it. He'd accept crypto for SEO services "all day long," and half-jokingly
suggests storing crypto on a hardware device like a Trezor for security.
