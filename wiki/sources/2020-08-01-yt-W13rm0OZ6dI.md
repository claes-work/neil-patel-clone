# How To Rank Your Existing Content - Module 1 - Lesson 3 - Content Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week-one lesson 3 of **Content Marketing Unlocked** ([[2020-07-30-yt-tDvBdnLZLp4]]):
optimizing existing content instead of only publishing new posts. Neil states his own
team writes ~4-5 new blog posts monthly but updates ~90, framing content refresh as
the bigger traffic lever. Practice: delete outdated/irrelevant sections, 301-redirect
dead pages to the most relevant URL, strip dates to keep content evergreen, fix
technical SEO/dead links via Ubersuggest's Site Audit Report, merge near-duplicate
pages into fewer, deeper pages (cites Wikipedia's Abraham Lincoln page as an example
of thoroughness), and improve page experience/site speed (mentions Crazy Egg heat
maps — his own earlier company). Frames brand-name search volume as a compounding
signal: more branded searches correlate with more non-branded keyword traffic over
time. Gives a concrete optimization workflow: find pages via Google Analytics
landing pages / Search Console search results, target pages with page CTR <4-5% or
keyword CTR <5%, add target keywords to title/meta/headers/alt-text/first 100 words,
and use CTR-boosting title words ("how to," "free," "tips," "best," "tricks," a
year/date). Recommends monthly re-optimization cadence and ~30 days to see SEO
results after resubmitting via Search Console's URL Inspector.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-08-01, Neil stated his team publishes roughly 4-5 new blog posts a
  month on neilpatel.com but updates about 90 existing posts a month
  (self-reported operational figure).
- As of 2020-08-01, Neil's content-refresh checklist: delete/redirect irrelevant
  content, remove dates for evergreen framing, fix technical SEO errors via
  Ubersuggest Site Audit, merge duplicate/near-duplicate pages with 301 redirects,
  and improve page-experience/site speed.
- As of 2020-08-01, Neil's CTR-optimization thresholds: flag keywords with <5% CTR
  and pages with <4% CTR in Search Console as high-opportunity for title/meta rewrites.
- As of 2020-08-01, Neil claimed increasing brand-name search volume correlates with
  increased traffic for non-branded keywords over time (self-reported observation,
  no external data cited).
- As of 2020-08-01, Neil advised giving SEO changes roughly 30 days to show results
  after resubmitting a page via Search Console's URL Inspector, describing SEO gains
  as gradual rather than a "big boom."
