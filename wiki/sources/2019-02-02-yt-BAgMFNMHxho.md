# How to Work with Multi-Million Dollar Companies

## Summary

Part origin-story, part playbook for landing enterprise clients. Neil recounts two
formative deals. **Elpac Electronics** (a power-supply manufacturer, later acquired) was
his first big contract: he landed it after giving a Speech 101 talk titled roughly "how
Google works" at a community college **while still in high school** — a salesperson from
Elpac happened to be in the class and referred him. The gig paid Neil ~$5,000/month while
he drove the company well over $20M in leads (their sales team closed them). His **first
multi-million-dollar contract** was **PokerStrategy** — ~$100,000/month on a yearly deal
(≈$1.2M) — won not by pitching but by helping, for free, a non-native-English-speaking,
socially-shunned attendee at a London gambling conference, who then evangelized Neil to
every company there until one hired him. The lesson: big contracts don't come from
connections or Harvard; they come from **helping the "little people."** His four-part
strategy: (1) help people freely everywhere ("little is the new big" — help the unknown
many and decision-makers eventually hear about you); (2) speak at ≥4 conferences/year
(apply to all in your space, it's a numbers game); (3) help businesses locally (Chamber
of Commerce — locals prefer to hire local); (4) be seen as an expert by **interviewing
other experts** and having them share the interview.
