# How to Optimize Website Conversions by Improving Your CTA

## Summary
A short tactical CRO video in which Neil Patel gives four rules for optimizing website
call-to-action (CTA) buttons to lift conversions: (1) avoid generic CTA text like "Buy
Now" or "Try me" and instead write copy tied to the specific benefit the product/service
delivers; (2) direct the hero/main image toward the CTA — e.g. a person's eyes should
look down or toward the button, not out at the visitor — so attention flows to the CTA;
(3) add time-sensitive / urgency words ("Try now," "Free trial," "Grow your traffic
today") that are still benefit-relevant; and (4) place CTAs where they make contextual
sense and repeat them throughout the page (after the explainer video, after each feature
block) so visitors never have to scroll back to convert. He cites his own neilpatel.com
homepage as an example of benefit-aligned CTA text.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-12, Neil states:
- Generic CTA words like "Buy Now" or "Try me" are not effective; CTA text must be
  related to the product/service and to the benefit the consumer gets — benefit-aligned
  text makes visitors much more likely to convert (as of 2017-08-12).
- He points to the neilpatel.com homepage as an example where the CTA text is very
  relevant to the benefit the visitor will receive (as of 2017-08-12).
- The hero/main image should be composed to draw attention to the CTA; if the image
  shows a person, that person's eyes should look down or toward the CTA rather than at
  the visitor, so people subconsciously shift attention to the CTA (as of 2017-08-12).
- Add time-sensitive/urgency words ("Try now," "Free trial," "Grow your traffic today")
  that remain related to the product/service — this both signals the benefit and
  encourages people to start right away instead of delaying (as of 2017-08-12).
- Don't place the CTA only at the bottom or only above the fold; place it where it makes
  sense. After an above-the-fold explainer video, put a CTA underneath; after each
  feature/benefit block, add another CTA. Sprinkle CTA buttons throughout the page so
  visitors don't have to scroll up or down to click (as of 2017-08-12).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The call to action text needs to be related to your product and service."

> "If your text aligns with the benefit that the consumer is going to get, they're much more likely to convert."

> "So, for example, if you have an image of a person, that person's eyes should be looking down or to the call to action. It shouldn't be looking at you, the website visitor."

> "As you describe the benefits of your product and service, you should continually place your call to action buttons beneath them. Have them sprinkled throughout your page so then that way, people don't have to keep scrolling up or down to click on them."
