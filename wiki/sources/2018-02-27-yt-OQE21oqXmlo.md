# SEO Hacks to Skyrocket Your Google Rankings | 3 Tips to Grow Website Traffic

_Neil solo._

## Summary
Three on-page SEO tactics Neil says drive the majority of results. **(1) Google Search
Console (free)**: in **Search Analytics**, find pages with **high impressions but low CTR**,
then rewrite the **title tag and meta description** with enticing adjectives (his example:
"**10 effortless ways to double your search engine traffic**" beats "10 ways to…"). Keep it
**30 days** and re-check CTR; if your CTR beats the listing above you, Google treats your
result as more relevant and **swaps rankings**. He notes the **Yoast SEO plugin** for editing
these in WordPress. **(2) Site speed**: clean code isn't enough — you need a **fast server**
(he pays for **WP Engine**, not $5 hosting) and a **CDN like Akamai** so users load from a
nearby server (India example), plus **Google AMP** for mobile. He observes AMP boosted his
traffic **in regions like Brazil** (weaker mobile networks) but **not much in North America,
Canada, Australia, or the UK**. **(3) Theme your site**: after Google's **Hummingbird** update,
sites that are deep and thorough on **one topic** rank higher — so stay on one theme and break
a big guide into **subsections/pages** (like Moz's Beginner's Guide) to rank multiple pages for
one term, rather than writing **50 near-identical articles** (which confuses Google about which
to rank).

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-27, Neil states:
- **A few on-page tactics produce most of the results** — work smart, not hard.
- **Google Search Console → Search Analytics**: identify pages with **high impressions, low
  CTR**; an impression = your listing was seen on a search page, click or not.
- **Rewrite title tags + meta descriptions with enticing, keyword-inclusive adjectives**
  (e.g. "effortless") to lift CTR; keep humans in mind, don't just stuff keywords.
- **Measure after ~30 days**: a higher CTR than the result above you signals greater relevance,
  and Google will gradually **swap your rankings up** — "a grind," slow but effective.
- Use the **Yoast SEO plugin** to edit titles/descriptions on WordPress.
- **Site speed needs a fast server + CDN, not just clean code**: he uses **WP Engine**
  (expensive but fast), avoids **$5 hosting**, and recommends a **CDN like Akamai** to serve
  from servers near the visitor.
- **Google AMP** speeds mobile pages; in Neil's experience it lifted traffic **in regions like
  Brazil** (weaker networks) but **not materially in NA/Canada/Australia/UK**.
- **Theme your site (post-Hummingbird)**: stay on one topic; deep, thorough single-topic sites
  rank higher. Break a large guide into **subsections/pages** to rank multiple pages for one
  term (Moz's guide as the model).
- **Don't write ~50 articles on the same sub-topic** — it confuses Google about which to rank;
  instead make **one detailed article with subsections**.
