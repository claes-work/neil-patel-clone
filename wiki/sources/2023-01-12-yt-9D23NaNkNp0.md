# My 6 Favorite Productivity Hacks ★

## Summary
A personal-operating-system video that doubles as a biography/beliefs source:
self-reports NP Digital has grown to **roughly 750 people** (Jan 2023) and
that he personally works "60-plus hours a week" on the agency. Six hacks: (1)
shorter meetings — caps meetings at 30 minutes and calls at 15, arguing longer
slots invite rambling and less preparation; (2) delegating — states his goal
as "be more essential, but less involved," focusing his own time on strategic
direction, priorities, and feedback rather than being a bottleneck, explicitly
tied to the 750-person headcount ("things can't be reliant on me, or else
we're not going to perform well"); (3) the power of no — says he now declines
speaking engagements based on audience fit for the business rather than fee
size, a reversal from an earlier practice of "go and speak based on whoever
could pay me the most amount of money"; (4) tame the inbox — spends the first
30 minutes after waking on email, keeps the inbox near-empty during the day in
spare minutes (commutes, before meetings) so he isn't buried at day's end and
can spend evenings with family; (5) batch creation — records over a month's
worth of content in three—four hours, writes blog posts weekly (Sunday, or
Saturday) a week ahead of publication, and records 10 Marketing School
episodes with Eric Siu ([[wiki/entities/eric-siu]]) in under an hour; (6)
automate — uses Ubersuggest's AI tool and UiPath to automate mundane tasks.
Closes with an NP Digital plug.
