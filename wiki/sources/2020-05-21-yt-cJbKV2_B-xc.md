# Advanced Content Marketing Tactics - Content Marketing Part 1 - Lesson 2 - SEO Unlocked

## Summary
Introduces the "WHIPS" framework for matching content to buyer awareness stage:
Window-shopper (wants a category of product, undecided on vendor), Help-me (aware of
a problem, not a solution), Inform-me (aware of the solution, doesn't know where to
get it), Persuade-me (comparing named competitors — e.g. "MailChimp vs. ConvertKit"
comparison keywords), Show-me (knows the specific product, needs final proof/
testimonials). Ties each stage to a different Ubersuggest report (Keyword Ideas for
help-me/inform-me terms, the Comparisons tab for persuade-me terms). Warns against
perfectionism/"problem loop" (endlessly revising instead of publishing), citing British
cycling coach Dave Brailsford's "marginal gains" philosophy (credited with Team Sky's
six Tour de France wins) and Toyota's decades of incremental improvement as models for
compounding small content improvements rather than seeking one breakthrough piece.
Closes with a 20-point "2020 rulebook" for writing: put people first; teach; write
conversationally (use "you"/"I"); use your own natural voice; write to one reader;
dictate via Google Docs voice-to-text; prefer active over passive voice; short
paragraphs (2-3 sentences); simple words over jargon; back claims with research; read
inspirational writers (names Seth Godin, Guy Kawasaki); use visual media; guide the
reader to a next step/interlink; end posts with a conclusion (citing his own Crazy Egg
heatmap studies showing readers scroll to the conclusion first, then scroll back up if
hooked); open with a personal story; don't over-edit before publishing; end with a
question and reply to every comment; commit to a writing routine; outline first
(hook/opening/body/close); and let a draft rest a day before publishing.
