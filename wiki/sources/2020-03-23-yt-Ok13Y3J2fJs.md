# 4 Design Tweaks That Can Boost Your SEO Traffic

## Summary
Neil frames design/UX as an SEO ranking factor and gives four areas to fix: (1)
mobile-first design — since Google enabled mobile-first indexing by default in July
2019, and (citing Statista.com) 64% of Google searches happen on mobile, sites should
prioritize the mobile experience (readable fonts/spacing, clear heading hierarchy, rich
media, priority content above the fold), test with tools like BrowserStack, speed it up
with a CDN (recommends Cloudflare), and validate with Google's mobile-friendly test tool
and Search Console's fetch-and-render; (2) meaningful content organization — simplify
navigation to only the most important categories, use a single H1 with a proper
H2–H6 hierarchy (book/chapter/topic analogy), and add jump-to-section quick links for
long-form content, crediting "my buddy Brian Dean" for popularizing that last tactic;
(3) center pages on user intent — match page format (long-form article vs. short
product page vs. video) to the keyword's intent (transactional/navigational/
informational), using his "how to tie a tie" video-vs-text example, and use Ubersuggest
to see who already ranks for a target keyword plus their backlinks/domain
score/social shares as a benchmark of what's needed; (4) avoid intrusive mobile
interstitials — Google penalizes unsolicited pop-ups on page entry but exit-intent
pop-ups are fine if they add value ("win-win" framing). Closes with an agency plug.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-03-23, Neil said Google enabled mobile-first indexing by default starting
  July 2019, meaning Google evaluates the mobile version of a page first for relevance.
- As of 2020-03-23, Neil cited Statista.com that 64% of Google searches are done on
  mobile devices.
- As of 2020-03-23, Neil recommended Cloudflare's free CDN plan to improve mobile load
  times without needing developer resources.
- As of 2020-03-23, Neil credited "my buddy Brian Dean" for popularizing top-of-article
  quick-navigation links on long-form content.
- As of 2020-03-23, Neil said page format should match keyword intent (transactional /
  navigational / informational) — e.g. a transactional keyword like "buy an iPhone 12"
  warrants a short product page, not a long-form article.
- As of 2020-03-23, Neil said Google penalizes unsolicited interstitials shown
  immediately on page entry (mobile), but exit-intent pop-ups are acceptable if they
  provide user value.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "SEO isn't just about getting back links and working under technical SEO. For a long time, Google has been adjusting its algorithm to look at user experience to determine rankings as well."

> "Sometimes people may prefer a video. Let's say I'm creating article or web page on how to tie a tie. Would you like to see a text description with images? Or would you rather see a video that breaks in how to do it in less than a minute? You'll take the video."

> "Pop up gives value to the user, you should show it. If it doesn't, and it's just fulfilling your own prophecy of making more money then don't show it. It has to be a win win situation."
