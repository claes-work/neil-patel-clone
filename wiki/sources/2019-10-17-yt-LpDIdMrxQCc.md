# My 7 Top-Secret Strategies on How to Get More Facebook Likes

## Summary
In this solo video, Neil argues that Facebook's algorithm throttles organic reach, so growing Facebook likes and engagement requires deliberately feeding the algorithm signals it rewards. He lays out seven tactics: (1) post quality over quantity, leaning on Facebook Live and memes because they drive comments, which he treats as the engagement signal that matters most; (2) mine Facebook Insights to segment audiences by region/language/demographics and cultivate "top fans," so you don't "burn your whole page" by broadcasting content to people who won't engage; (3) engage with other brands and influencers in your niche, and when they ignore you, invite them to a live interview so they share it to their own page; (4) optimize posting timing, both for when your audience is online and for windows when competitors post little (he cites 6-7am Pacific and Saturdays); (5) use Facebook Messenger via MobileMonkey — not just to drive traffic off-platform but to push existing fans back to a just-published post to reignite engagement; (6) be consistent and test relentlessly, never repeating a format that failed; and (7) encourage engagement by asking specific questions (not "leave a comment," which the algorithm penalizes) and replying to every comment. Throughout, comments and a "network effect" from popular accounts are framed as the true drivers of viral reach and, in turn, more likes and shares.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said Facebook's algorithm restricts organic reach, so most of a page's friends, fans, and followers barely see any given post.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said the algorithm now punishes low-engagement posting: if fans barely engage with a post, the next post is shown to far fewer people — so you must post more quality content to keep the continually-engaging fan count high.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said Facebook Live videos get shown more often than posts that link out to a website, so struggling pages should use live video to revive engagement before sharing off-site links.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said memes and other engagement-bait content perform well, and that the engagement metric that matters is comments — not likes or shares — because comments make content more likely to go viral.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said marketers should constantly study Facebook Insights (audience type, best posting times, demographics) and segment posts by region and language so only likely-to-engage audiences see content, avoiding reach damage from unengaged segments — i.e., not "burning your whole page."
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said Facebook badges your "top fans," and you can encourage top-fan status by offering exclusives (an ebook, a course) to lift engagement in less-active audience segments.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said to engage with niche brands and influencers on their pages to draw their audiences back to yours, because when popular accounts like/comment it creates a network effect that reaches far more people than engagement from small accounts.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said the easiest way to win over influencers who ignore you is to invite them to a live-streamed interview, because they are much more likely to share it to their own page.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said timing is everything: he gets more engagement posting around 6-7am Pacific than in the afternoon, and posts on Saturdays because competitors post little then, so his content gets shown to more people despite lower overall traffic — a conclusion he draws from his own Facebook Insights data.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil cited TechCrunch saying 1.3 billion people use Messenger each month and App Annie ranking Facebook the #1 and Messenger the #2 most-downloaded app of all time.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil reported 88% open rates and 56% click-through rates using Facebook Messenger through MobileMonkey.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said his distinctive Messenger tactic is pushing fans back to a just-published fan-page post (not just off to his website), which reignites his fan base and signals to Facebook to surface the content to people who haven't engaged in 30/60/90 days.
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said to be consistent, post what the audience likes rather than what you like, treat every post as a test, and — while failing once is fine — never repeat a format that already failed ("don't fail twice").
- As of 2019-10-17, Neil said explicitly asking for a comment backfires because Facebook detects manipulation and dislikes yes/no replies; instead ask a specific, relevant question and reply to every comment, a tactic he says he has used since his blogging days and applies across YouTube, Facebook, and Instagram.
