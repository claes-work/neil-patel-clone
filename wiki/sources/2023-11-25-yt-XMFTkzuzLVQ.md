# Behind the Scenes Business Trends We Are Seeing

## Summary
Eric opens by relaying an anonymized anecdote from a mutual CEO friend who refuses to
hire from certain U.S. states (CA, NY, CO) because they're "litigious." Neil responds
that NP Digital takes the opposite approach: hire the best talent regardless of
location, without regard to state litigiousness. He then describes a specific hiring
tactic NP Digital uses on development/server projects: staffing three people across
different time zones on eight-hour shifts each, so the same project effectively gets
worked on 24 hours a day (five days a week, not seven) — framed as both a speed
advantage (constant progress) and a cost advantage (talent outside the U.S. is often
drastically cheaper). Separately, Neil lays out a downturn-investing framework: in
good economies with low rates, capital is hard to deploy well because valuations are
inflated (analogy: Berkshire Hathaway's large cash reserves during frothy markets);
in bad economies, even though revenue/profit may suffer, it's the best time to deploy
capital (via savings, bank loans, or capital providers like Capchase) because
acquisition targets are cheaper.
