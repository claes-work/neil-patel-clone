# 1 Simple Trick to Get Ranked High on Google FAST - HTML Internal Linking for SEO

## Summary
Neil argues that **internal linking** — not just external backlinks — drives rankings, using Mashable vs. TechCrunch as his proof point: TechCrunch has more backlinks/authority, but Mashable ranks for "almost everything" because it internally links better. He credits neilpatel.com's page-one ranking for "online marketing" mainly to internal links to his main guide, reinforced by sidebar links present on every page. Two rules: (1) link within your content to your most important pages, but keep anchor text natural rather than always exact-match; (2) put links to your key pages in the sidebar of every page. He warns against hiding links (tabs, tiny fonts, invisible text) as an early-2000s tactic that now risks a penalty.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-18, Neil states:
- Mashable.com ranks for almost everything on Google despite having less authority/fewer backlinks than TechCrunch, because it does a much better job of internal linking (as of 2017-07-18).
- neilpatel.com ranks page one for "online marketing" mainly through internal links, not external links from other sites (as of 2017-07-18).
- Mechanism: every post about online marketing links to his main online-marketing guide, and the sidebar on every page links to all his guides; concentrating internal links on a page signals to Google that he values it, so Google ranks it higher (as of 2017-07-18).
- Rule 1 — link within content, but don't always use the exact keyword as anchor text; over-using exact-match anchor text can actually make rankings drop, whereas natural anchors make rankings rise faster (as of 2017-07-18).
- Rule 2 — link to your most important pages from the sidebar of every page (as of 2017-07-18).
- The internal links must be **visible** — hiding them in tabs, tiny text, or invisible font "worked in the early 2000s" but can now hurt you and get you penalized (as of 2017-07-18).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "They don't have as much authority as TechCrunch.com, because TechCrunch has so many more back links, but ... Mashable does a much better job of internal linking, and that's why they rank so high."

> "By passing a lot of internal links to that page, it's telling Google that hey, I Neil Patel, value that page and feel it's important, and for that reason Google is like, okay, because you are putting so much emphasis on this page, we should rank it higher than your other pages."

> "Don't always use the exact keyword. Make it flow natural ... By putting too much anchor text in there, the anchor text being the keyword you want a rank for, sometimes you will notice that your rankings will actually drop."

> "You want to make sure the link is visible, and it's on every major page of your site."
