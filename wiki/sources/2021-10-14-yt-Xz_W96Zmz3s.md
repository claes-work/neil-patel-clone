# A Simple Strategy to Getting More Customers For Your Ad Agency

## Summary
Describes the zero-brand cold-acquisition strategy he says he used to grow his first
agency and says "still works today": target recently-venture-funded companies (via
Crunchbase) in the $2M–$10M raise range specifically — not mega-raises ($50M+, who
already have marketing departments) — because venture-funded companies must show fast
growth regardless of profitability. Build a detailed teardown report (SEO, paid, or
CRO issues plus fix recommendations and revenue upside, using Ubersuggest for the SEO
version), then email it not just to the founder/CEO (found via hunter.io) but CC the
company's investors (identified via Crunchbase's cap-table/board data), reasoning
investors will pressure the CEO to act on a credible, unsolicited teardown. Describes
an A/B-test variant: email the investor alone, name-dropping the portfolio company in
the subject line, asking them to forward it to the CEO — reasoning a forwarded email
from an investor gets read faster than a cold email. Adds a "bonus" enterprise-tier
tactic: cold-email a large company's CEO directly (Walmart/Target/eBay-scale, found via
a simple Google search) with the same teardown angle, asking them to forward it to
their CMO — a ~20–30% self-reported response rate — reasoning a CMO responds faster to
something forwarded by their own CEO.
