# The BEST Ways to Learn Marketing in 2025 (Marketing School)

## Summary
★ Landmark-worthy for later L3 promotion: Neil's evergreen "how to learn marketing" playbook — a two-step method with an AI twist. Step 1, build a foundation by learning from short-form creators, newsletters, paid communities, and podcasts/people-to-follow (skip books — outdated by the time they ship); pick the sub-discipline that fits you. Step 2, learn by doing: spin up your own WordPress/Shopify site on a topic you care about, try to get traffic/sales/leads, create content, promote, build links, and now lean on AI tools (HeyGen to translate/transcribe, ChatGPT to research better articles) — then track traffic week-over-week, learn from failures, and double down on what works. Having no money/resources is "actually even better."

A co-hosted Marketing School episode. Neil delivers the two-step learning method (learn from creators/newsletters/communities/podcasts → then experiment on your own website with AI tools, tracking week-over-week). Eric then recaps the two steps and adds a third point — the "eye of Sauron" single-channel focus (credited to "Caleb," who worked with Hormozi and Gary Vee): don't test too many things at once; focus one channel (e.g. SEO), and when it works, double down. Eric shares his own SEO/blogging origin story (torched lightshowvideos.com — no TAM; then how-to-get-rid-of-pimple.net, where he bought dirty links and spun up crappy articles, spiked traffic to ~50–60K/day, then lost it all to zero) as a lesson that you only learn by taking your licking. Eric reads the Agency Owners Association sponsor ad and closes.

## Key claims
- One of the best ways to learn marketing is to follow great short-form creators (Neil names Alex Garcia on Instagram), subscribe to newsletters (Marketing Brew, The Newsletter Operator), and pay to join learning communities (Slack communities with weekly workshops and guest speakers) — you learn from peers and build your network, especially valuable in your early 20s. (Neil, 2024-12-24)
- Books are not the way to learn marketing: by the time a book ships it's already outdated (Neil nods to Seth Godin as "the marketing master" but still favors current sources). (Neil, 2024-12-24)
- Better than books: listen to podcasts (e.g. Marketing Against the Grain) and follow practitioners sharing case studies on Twitter/LinkedIn ("marketing examined" on Twitter; Kyle Poyar on LinkedIn for account-based marketing). (Neil, 2024-12-24)
- Figure out what sub-discipline fits you first (brand marketing, short-form, creative, etc.), then go find the people who teach it. (Neil, 2024-12-24)
- Don't be the person who, asked in an interview how they get better at their job, says "I read blogs and listen to podcasts" but can't name a single one — have real, specific answers. (Neil, 2024-12-24)
- The best way to actually learn marketing (with an AI twist): create your own WordPress or Shopify website on a topic you're interested in and try to get traffic, sales, or leads — you must create content, promote it on the social web, and build links. (Neil, 2024-12-24)
- Use AI tools to help: HeyGen to translate/transcribe into multiple languages, or something as simple as ChatGPT to do research so you write better blog articles. (Neil, 2024-12-24)
- Track your traffic week-over-week and try to grow it; use tools like SEMrush, Ahrefs, or Ubersuggest (and lead-gen/ABM tools) to help. Having little money or resources is okay — it's "actually even better." (Neil, 2024-12-24)
- Some weeks will be great, most will be terrible — learn from your failures, adapt, and keep doubling down on what's working. (Neil, 2024-12-24)
