# A Simple Hack to Generating 93% More Ad Revenue

## Summary
A standalone tutorial aimed at website publishers monetizing via display ads. Neil
opens with skepticism toward AdSense used alone ("I'm not a huge fan of AdSense") but
says it works well when combined with other ad networks and rigorous testing. He
recounts personally spending six months to a year manually optimizing display ads on
a site he co-owned ("Real Cool Videos," with a partner named Ahmed) across variables
like placement, size, density, color, and network choice, before concluding manual
optimization doesn't scale. His prescribed solution is Ezoic, an AI/machine-learning
ad-optimization tool he calls his "secret weapon" for every newly acquired publishing
property — it optimizes per-visitor ad delivery using aggregated data across
billions of visitor interactions and many participating sites (rather than one
site's own traffic alone), removing ads automatically when they detect the
experience is being hurt, and citing a Small Business Trends study of 300+ Ezoic
sites showing an average 93% revenue increase over a year. He frames the deeper
mechanism as connecting ad experience quality to organic rankings: bad ad experiences
raise bounce rate and pogo-sticking, which he says Google reads as a negative
ranking signal.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-19, Neil states manually optimizing display-ad placement/size/
  density/color took him roughly six to nine months to a year to fully perfect on a
  site he co-owned, "Real Cool Videos" (with a partner, Ahmed) (self-reported,
  biographical detail — no prior wiki mention of "Real Cool Videos" found).
- As of 2020-10-19, Neil cites a Small Business Trends study: over 300 websites
  using Ezoic for one year saw an average 93% ad-revenue increase (third-party
  study, self-reported by Neil, not independently verified here).
- As of 2020-10-19, Neil's stated mechanism linking ad UX to SEO: bad ad experiences
  raise bounce rate, which Google reads as a negative ranking/quality signal for the
  page.
- As of 2020-10-19, Neil recommends never running a single ad network alone
  ("don't just use AdSense, you run multiple ad platforms") to maximize per-visitor
  revenue.
- As of 2020-10-19, Neil says A/B tests on ad variables should run for a month to
  two months rather than a few days, because advertiser bids re-adjust over
  six-to-eight-week cycles and short tests get distorted by early repeat-visitor
  effects (self-reported).
