# How to Write Engaging Blog Content For Boring Industries

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that any "boring" industry can produce engaging, shareable blog content, and that the constraint is ideation rather than the topic itself. He recounts a client, Lifeinsured.com, whose owner doubted anything about life insurance could go viral. Patel researched adjacent, emotionally resonant terms (rather than "life insurance" directly) on sites like Reddit — searching concepts such as "money" and "death" — and produced a list-style article, "19 Things You Didn't Know About Death," seeded with striking facts (e.g., that a person may remain conscious for 10–20 seconds after death, or that roughly 100 people a year die chewing a pen). His method: use Reddit, BuzzSumo, and Quora to surface popular, related angles, then create content that educates and informs rather than "sexy" content. He illustrates unmet demand with a personal anecdote about failing to find any useful article on installing a bidet. He closes with a skyscraper-style tactic: find an existing list post (e.g., 10–12 ways to do something) in your space and expand it far beyond (50 or 100 ways) so your piece outperforms even in a dull niche.

## Key claims
- Boring industries (e.g., auto insurance, life insurance) can still support amazing, shareable content; the topic being boring does not mean there is no content around it (as of 2017-05-29).
- For ideation, search terms and keywords related to your field on Reddit, BuzzSumo, and Quora to see what is already popular and to generate article ideas for any industry (as of 2017-05-29).
- Searching adjacent/emotional concepts rather than the literal niche term surfaces better ideas — for life insurance, Patel found little on "life insurance" directly but many ideas around "money" and "death" (as of 2017-05-29).
- Patel created "19 Things You Didn't Know About Death" for client Lifeinsured.com, taking a found concept and expanding on it (as of 2017-05-29).
- Content does not need to be "sexy" to perform; content that educates and teaches performs well because people value informational, educational material (as of 2017-05-29).
- There is unmet demand for practical/how-to content even in mundane topics — Patel could not find a single relevant article on how to install a bidet, and says a good one would have earned his read and social shares (as of 2017-05-29).
- Skyscraper tactic: when a competing list post breaks down 10–12 ways to do something in your space, expand on it to create one with 50 or 100 ways so your article outperforms, even in a boring industry (as of 2017-05-29).
