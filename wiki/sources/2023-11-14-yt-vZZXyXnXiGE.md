# How to Spend Your Marketing Budget Like a Genius (Ep. 2603)

## Summary
A short Q4-2023 Marketing School episode on how to allocate marketing budget in a downturn. Eric frames the macro shift — the "grow-at-all-cost / lose money to hit VC and valuation goals" era is over, and discipline plus efficiency are "back" (illustrated with his Warren Buffett / Charlie Munger desk statues and a longevity analogy). Neil's angle is a stance he says holds in ANY economy, good or bad: the best way to spend on marketing is slow, steady, and profitable — you cannot just "crank the knob" from $100/day to $1M/day (or 10k/mo to 200k/mo organic) without burning money for little ROI. Neil's distinctive claim is that the genuinely hard part of marketing is not running a profitable Google/Facebook ad or an SEO campaign, but scaling it (ranking for millions of keywords, spending $1–2M/month across countries) — scale is "what tends to break marketing," so ease in gradually regardless of how much cash or what valuation you have. Eric closes with a personal cautionary anecdote and the idea that a down economy lets disciplined marketers buy cheap attention and pull ahead of competitors.

## Key claims
- The best way to scale and spend on marketing is slow, steady, and profitable — and this is true in a good economy or a bad economy alike, not just a downturn. (Neil, 2023-11-14)
- You must be methodical with marketing spend; not everything has to be profitable (you should experiment on new, up-and-coming, less-competitive channels), but you cannot simply crank the knob and 10x/100x spend overnight. (Neil, 2023-11-14)
- All channels eventually get very competitive and saturated, so you have to keep finding new ones. (Neil, 2023-11-14)
- Jumping spend from $100/day to $1M/day — or organic effort from $10k/mo to $200k/mo — "just doesn't work that way"; you end up burning money on activity that produces little to no ROI. (Neil, 2023-11-14)
- Regardless of the economy, the hardest part of marketing is scaling UP; it is not hard to run a profitable Google ad, Facebook ad, or SEO campaign — it is hard to do it at scale (ranking for millions of keywords, spending $1–2M/month across many countries). (Neil, 2023-11-14)
- "Scale is what tends to break marketing"; you have to ease into it slowly, and this holds no matter your valuation or how much cash you have — slow ramp-up produces better results and burns fewer resources. (Neil, 2023-11-14)
- The macro context: as of recording (Q4 2023) it is a tough economic period; the old grow-fast, lose-money, chase-valuation-and-fundraising playbook is gone, and discipline / efficiency are "back," which is good for the long term. (Eric, 2023-11-14)
- If you spend a dollar and lose two, growth doesn't justify continually losing money — better to spend a dollar to make a dollar. (Eric, 2023-11-14)
- In 2020–2022 (pre-privacy-changes) marketing was "on easy mode"; roughly 50% of VC dollars flowed to Meta and Google ads [sic? figure stated loosely], and now marketers must be more creative and cut spend that isn't working. (Eric, 2023-11-14)
- A down economy lets you "buy" cheap attention and outspend/outmaneuver competitors if you're efficient. (Eric, 2023-11-14)
