# 7 Google Ads Hacks That'll Make Your Campaigns Scale Profitably

## Summary
Neil Patel walks through seven tactics for making Google Ads (referred to throughout as "AdWords") campaigns scale profitably, arguing the platform remains one of the best ways to grow a business because it reaches people at the moment they are actively searching for related keywords. His central theme is precision targeting: spend money on audiences most likely to convert rather than the broadest possible reach. The seven hacks are (1) build in-depth remarketing audiences, (2) focus on longtail keywords rather than high-volume generalized ones, (3) use exclusions to narrow to a hyper-focused audience, (4) test different locations instead of defaults, (5) add ad extensions to capture more SERP real estate, (6) implement UTM tracking to measure campaign performance, and (7) run ads on competitor keywords and brand names. He supports several points with specific figures (remarketing CTR ~10x higher, extensions lifting click-throughs ~10–15%, longtail keyword cost comparisons) and closes by pointing viewers to his ad agency, Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- Google Ads remains one of the best platforms to grow a business because it reaches the right people when they are actively searching for related keywords (as of 2020-04-16).
- Hack 1 — Create in-depth remarketing audiences: the click-through rate for remarketing is roughly ten times higher than traditional advertising, and CPCs cost roughly two to a hundred times less (as of 2020-04-16).
- Adding detailed targeting to remarketing efforts improves both spend and conversions; set up campaigns for people who have visited the site more than once, as they are already interested and more likely to convert (as of 2020-04-16).
- Hack 2 — Focus on longtail keywords: generalized keywords are highly competitive and high-volume but do not always drive the highest-quality traffic; "the money is in the longtail" (as of 2020-04-16).
- Worked example: the keyword "diet plan" has an SEO difficulty of 52, paid difficulty of 70, CPC of $4.35, and ~40,500 monthly searches; the longtail "diet plan that's low carb" has SEO difficulty 21, paid difficulty 42, CPC of $1.58, and ~12,100 monthly searches but converts better because the searcher wants a specific plan (as of 2020-04-16).
- Longtail phrases tend to generate more sales and often carry a lower cost per click even though they can make more money (as of 2020-04-16).
- Hack 3 — Use exclusions: exclusions find a hyper-focused, higher-converting audience (example: exclude high-income people if they are not your audience); the same applies to targeting elements in both Google Ads and Facebook Ads (as of 2020-04-16).
- Hack 4 — Test different locations: many advertisers just use default settings, which is a mistake; the three ways to target location are a location-based keyword, geotargeting to a radius, or both, and a mixture should be tested to maximize sales and weed out non-customers (as of 2020-04-16).
- Hack 5 — Add ad extensions: extensions are one of the most sure-fire ways to get more clicks because they take up more SERP real estate; per Google, adding extensions can increase click-throughs by roughly 10–15% (as of 2020-04-16).
- Hack 6 — Implement UTM tracking: tracking campaign performance is essential; Google's URL builder tool lets a non-developer create UTM codes per page in under 30 seconds and track performance in Google Analytics (as of 2020-04-16).
- Hack 7 — Run ads on competitor keywords: bidding on competitor keywords and brand names is a simple, practical way to get good ROI; Ubersuggest can reveal competitors' ranking keywords and a brand name's search volume (as of 2020-04-16).
- Trademark constraints may prevent using a competitor's name within ad copy, but advertisers can still bid on and drive sales from those searches because such searchers are highly targeted (as of 2020-04-16).
