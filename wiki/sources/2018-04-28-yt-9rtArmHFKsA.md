# Why I DON'T Share My Personal Life On Social Media (TRUE STORY)

## Summary

An interview-style clip (an unnamed interviewer, who says he has known Neil personally for ~six months, asks why Neil shares none of his personal life on social media). Neil gives two reasons. First, he is simply a private person who does not want the public inside his family life. Second — the "true story" — as his business has grown he gets sued frequently, sometimes over trivial things, and public personal details get used against him. He recounts being sued over mere referrals (referring someone to another company for no kickback), including paying roughly $10,000 via PayPal to make one complainant "get out of my hair," and receiving lawyer letters demanding repayment on a $150,000 contract a client signed with a third party, not him. He notes people assume he has money and should therefore pay. Beyond the legal risk, he rejects the always-capturing lifestyle: he uses an old phone, does not chase the latest iPhone, and is "a big believer in just living in the moment" rather than staging photos. He affirms that ~99.99% of his audience is wonderful and that he enjoys sharing occasional family photos (e.g., in Indian outfits), but keeps the deeply personal offline.
