# 3 Controversial Digital Marketing Campaigns That Made Me MILLIONS

## Summary

A Neil-fronted monologue opening with him acknowledging he has "a lot of haters" from past "aggressive marketing tactics," then sharing three bold tactics he says made him millions. (1) **The "Who is Neil Patel?" brand campaign** — he paid people (including, controversially, "half-naked girls," plus bodybuilders, fitness models, and makeup artists) to display his name to generate brand searches and buzz that lifted his Google rankings long-term; he says it led to business deals worth millions. He repeatedly and explicitly regrets the "half-naked" execution, says he "wasn't trying to degrade women," learned entrepreneurship from his mom, apologized to the community, and would not do it that way again — recommending fully-clothed participants and the "right audience" (ideally business people) instead. He notes this kind of "who is" campaign builds awareness (like Coca-Cola/Pepsi/Nike), not direct sales. (2) **Infographics** — early on at KISSmetrics he spent $500–$1,000 per infographic when nobody else was investing in them; they generated thousands of backlinks, helped push the KISSmetrics blog past a million visitors/month, and (with a WordPress embed-code generator) drove natural link-building and search traffic. (3) **Give everything away for free** — undercut competitors who charge (e.g., keyword tools): he bought Ubersuggest for "a bit more than a hundred thousand dollars," added the paid features, and released it fully free (no plans, no login, unlimited), growing its traffic ~50% to 400,000+ page views/month; the tool's leads feed his agency NP Digital, where a single lead/month covers its cost. He cites doing the same earlier at Quick Sprout with $30K+ "advanced guides" (e.g., the Beginner's Guide to Online Marketing) that he says generated in excess of a million dollars over time. Closes: get creative and "crazy," but — his hard-learned caveat — respect people and don't be sexist.
