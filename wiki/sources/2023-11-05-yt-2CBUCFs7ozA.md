# THE HARSH TRUTH: Will the Economy Get Better in 2024? | Ep. 2593 (Marketing School)

## Summary
Short co-hosted Marketing School daily episode (~2,500 words) on the 2023/2024 economic
outlook framed for marketers and agency owners. Both hosts repeatedly disclaim "we're not
economists," so nearly all macro content is opinion and self-reported. The economic-theory
scaffolding is **Eric's**: he narrates Howard Marks' (Oaktree Capital) "sea change" thesis —
three historical regime shifts (nifty50 → great moderation; rise of passive investing + the
2008 GFC; and now the end of easy money + rise of inflation from QE, COVID, and the Ukraine
war), plus the argument that fixed income/credit now yields near-equity returns. Eric also
carries the interest-rate forecast (Fed settling ~2–2.5% in three years, SOFR explainer,
mortgages staying ~5%+ but not 8%) and the closing "if we hit recession the money printer
restarts and the cycle repeats / US is the reserve currency" wrap. That whole macro-theory
thread is quarantined below.

**Neil's persona-relevant contribution** is the marketer/operator read: (1) the downturn
heuristic that marketing is the first budget businesses cut and the first they add back —
but they are **not** adding it back yet; (2) a real agency-data observation that ad-spend
interest is not converting to actual spend (pipeline of companies "interested" in spending
more never pulled through), so the slowdown is a long cycle, not a 3–6-month dip; (3) macro
signals he cites (SMB bankruptcies at a multi-decade high; nobody quitting jobs / everyone
wants security now vs. the 2021 "quit for 2× salary" era); and (4) the operator move — worst
time to sell a company (multiples low), best time to buy/expand, with NP Digital's M&A team
tasked to acquire ~$10M of EBITDA within ~12 months while it's cheapest. Solid, on-brand
Neil operator takes, but the marketing content mostly restates his known "marketing is
first-cut/first-added" position wrapped around borrowed macro commentary — **not** a new
framework, so **not** flagged ★ L3-candidate.

## Eric Siu (co-host, context — NOT persona data)
[[wiki/entities/eric-siu]]
- Narrated Howard Marks' (Oaktree Capital) "sea change" thesis from a ~24-minute talk: markets undergo rare regime shifts, and we're in the third of Marks' career. (2023-11-05)
- The three sea changes as Eric read them: (1) the early-1970s nifty50 blue-chip era ending and giving way to the "great moderation" of low inflation/rates; (2) the 2000s rise of passive investing / index funds culminating in the 2008 global financial crisis; (3) the current "end of easy money and rise of inflation," attributed to the Fed's quantitative easing, COVID-19, and the war in Ukraine. (2023-11-05)
- Marks' investing argument Eric relayed: you can now get near-equity returns without equities — ~5% just sitting in a savings account, and ~10–11% in some credit instruments / distressed-debt / lending, versus ~7–8%/yr in the S&P 500 — so capital shifts toward fixed income. (2023-11-05)
- Interest-rate mechanics: explained SOFR ("sulfur" in captions) as the market reference rate lenders price loans over (e.g. "3% plus SOFR"); forecast the Fed rate settling ~2–2.5% within ~three years and mortgages/business borrowing staying ~5%+ (not 8%), noting 5% mortgages were historically standard and 0% was the anomaly. (Eric — self-reported/unverified, 2023-11-05)
- Closing macro wrap: if the US actually enters recession, the "money printer" restarts and rates drop, repeating the cycle — possible next bull market — because the US holds the reserve currency and can print more (not endorsing it, not saying it lasts forever); meanwhile "aim for profitability, aim for efficiency." (Eric, 2023-11-05)
- Personal-finance aside: has "one too many homes" that go unused and would liquidate some at a loss to redeploy capital into his business ("I'll make a killing"). (Eric, 2023-11-05)
- Read the closing ad/CTA and plugged Neil's M&A interest ("Neil's shopping right now... NeilPatel.com, he responds well"). (2023-11-05)
