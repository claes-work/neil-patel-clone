# 1 Simple Facebook Marketing Tip to Engage With Your Fans!

## Summary
Neil explains that the Facebook cover photo is an underused but high-leverage way to connect with fans, and that many people get it wrong. He lays out three considerations for choosing one: make it relate to your audience (he cites his own repeated use of a Brazil-themed cover photo to bond with Brazilian fans and grow that audience), have it describe your business, product, or service (citing Gary Vaynerchuk's self-portrait plus signature as an example of connecting through personal brand), and, to go above and beyond, use a video as the cover to describe who you are and why your business is great. Doing this, he argues, makes fans more likely to engage, comment, share, like, and stay on your page.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-09, Neil states:
- The Facebook cover photo is a simple but frequently mishandled lever for connecting better with friends and fans (as of 2017-10-09).
- A cover photo should first relate to your audience; he repeatedly used a Brazil-themed cover to bond with Brazilian fans and says it helped him grow considerably in Brazil (as of 2017-10-09).
- A cover photo should also describe your business, product, or service — whatever you sell or help people with (as of 2017-10-09).
- Gary Vaynerchuk exemplifies this by showing an image of himself with his signature to connect through his personal brand (as of 2017-10-09).
- To go above and beyond, you should consider using a video as your cover photo to describe who you are and why your business is great (as of 2017-10-09).
- Following these tactics makes people more likely to engage, comment, share, and like, and to stay on your page (as of 2017-10-09).
