# How to Buy a New Business Using Leverage (Tips for Acquiring New Companies)

## Summary

Q&A Thursday (Neil with interviewer/co-host **Adam Lodolce** of Viewership). Question from
"Jason aka Mitchell": using **leverage (LBO)** and **earnouts** to buy businesses, including
internationally. Neil explains a leveraged buyout in plain terms — buying a business largely
with **debt/special financing and the target's own future profits**, so you don't pay it all
up front. He says (self-reported) he's done this and has a Seattle friend who does it "all
day long," and you don't necessarily need bank financing. His practical structure for
non-institutional buyers: **seller financing** — put a little cash down, negotiate to pay the
rest **out of the business's profits over ~3 years**, and add **earnout sweeteners** (extra
payments if the seller stays and helps hit growth milestones). Sellers often accept because
they're **burned out** and want out — though you'll usually pay a **premium** vs. an all-cash
price. (Big PE firms do the bank-financed debt version: portion down, bank finances the rest
because the business is profitable.) On **deal sourcing**: pick your target sector, do heavy
Google research using SEO tools (he names Ahrefs, SEMrush, **Ubersuggest**), then **cold-
email 50–100 owners** asking if they'd sell — most ignore you, some say yes. Or, if you're
lazy, pay a premium to **website brokers** (Google "website brokers"), or for smaller sites
use marketplaces like **Flippa** and **Empire Flippers**. He notes the **process is the same
domestically or internationally**. (Comic aside: Adam jokingly offers to buy neilpatel.com;
Neil says "a billion dollars," he owns 100%.)

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-30, an LBO means buying a business largely with debt/financing and the
  target's own future profits, minimizing cash out of pocket; bank financing isn't strictly
  required.
- As of 2018-08-30, the accessible structure for small buyers is **seller financing**: small
  down payment + pay the balance from the business's profits over ~3 years, usually at a
  premium to an all-cash price.
- As of 2018-08-30, add **earnout sweeteners** — extra payments tied to the seller staying on
  and hitting growth milestones.
- As of 2018-08-30, sellers often accept because they're burned out and want out of the
  business.
- As of 2018-08-30, large PE firms use debt finance (portion down, bank finances the rest on
  the strength of the business's profitability).
- As of 2018-08-30, source deals by choosing a sector, researching with SEO tools (names
  Ahrefs, SEMrush, Ubersuggest), and cold-emailing 50–100 owners; alternatively pay a premium
  to website brokers, or use Flippa / Empire Flippers for smaller sites.
- As of 2018-08-30, the acquisition process is the same for US or international targets.
- As of 2018-08-30, Neil states (self-reported) he has personally done leveraged acquisitions
  and knows someone in Seattle who does them routinely.
