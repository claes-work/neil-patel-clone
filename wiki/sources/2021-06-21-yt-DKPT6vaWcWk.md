# The Biggest Impact on SEO

## Summary
Interview/conversation with **Sujan Patel** (Neil's cousin — see
[[wiki/entities/sujan-patel]]), speaker-labeled in the captions. Restates and quantifies
Neil's existing **brand-queries-drive-rankings** thesis: cites (paraphrasing) ex-Google
CEO Eric Schmidt's "brands are the solution, not the problem" line, and describes his own
observed pattern — when PR spikes cause a temporary surge in people searching his own
name/domain ("Neil Patel SEO", "Neil Patel Ubersuggest", etc.), rankings across the site
jump and then hold near the new peak even after the brand-query surge subsides, regardless
of link-building activity. States you don't need to be the biggest brand overall — just a
recognizable brand *for the specific keywords you're targeting*. Restates the **rule of
seven** (seven exposures/interactions build loyal-follower likelihood) as his brand-
building framework, broken into concrete levers: email capture, push notifications, using
every social channel, responding to comments, and in-person/live interaction. Gives a
precise self-reported stat: **106,447 comments across 5,420 blog posts** ≈ 19.06 comments
per post. Names **free tools** as his single favorite brand-query lever ("I found that
that beats everything"), citing Ubersuggest's ~340,000 monthly visits from people
searching the Ubersuggest brand name directly, and Sujan's own tool Voila Norbert as a
parallel example of an unusual/non-obvious brand name still generating more searches than
its own top keywords. Closes reinforcing the existing "channels/tactics change, brand
compounds — start now" belief already in the wiki.
