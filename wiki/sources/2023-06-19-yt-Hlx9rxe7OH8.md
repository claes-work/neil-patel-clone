# Leveraging SEO in Your Personal Brand to Drive Business

## Summary
Solo interview centered on standing out in an AI-saturated content landscape. Extends Google's
**E-E-A-T** framework (experience, expertise, authority, trust — the added "experience" E vs. the
"EAT" framing used in other 2023 sources) into a "Where's Waldo" metaphor: when everyone uses the
same AI tools to generate content, everyone starts to look identical, and standing out requires
leaning into first-hand experience/expertise that AI-only competitors can't replicate; frames
this as more a time/effort problem than a difficulty problem — most people won't take out their
phone and start recording. Names **Alex Hormozi** and **Codie Sanchez** as creators currently
executing this well. Asked for his own biggest career mistakes: names (1) **lack of focus** —
chasing "the new shiny object" instead of concentrating on one business, says he is only now
(at "38," over the last five years) correcting this; and (2) **picking too small a TAM** —
explicitly reverses an earlier belief ("the riches are in the niches... that's so false... the
riches are in big massive markets"), paraphrasing an (uncredited, self-flagged-as-imprecise)
Google-founder line about building something people use daily. Book recommendations: *Art of the
Start* (Guy Kawasaki), *The Dip* (Seth Godin), *Principles* (Ray Dalio) — states no book in the
prior six months changed his thinking, and that he prefers learning via social platforms
(Instagram/Facebook/YouTube) over reading, including for topics outside his expertise (states you
don't need college for most professions, learn on YouTube instead, with a carve-out for
professions that legally require certification). On near-term AI opportunity: names **real-time
marketing analytics** (AI flagging budget waste/opportunity in real time instead of humans
reviewing weekly/monthly, citing NP Digital client cases getting 60%+ ROI improvement from a
single analytics review after going "two years without" catching the issue) and **ad creative
generation** (AI generating many creative variants for human-selected fine-tuning) as the two
biggest near-term AI disruption points in his own industry, plus AI-assisted SEO on-page fixes
(AI proposing code changes, human reviewing before upload). Praises **Gary Vaynerchuk's** content-
repurposing framework by name and describes his own team's practice: take one long-form
piece (e.g. this very podcast recording) and slice it into long-form clips, short-form clips,
transcribed blog posts, and mini podcast episodes, rather than separately producing each format
from scratch. Recommends **Social Blade** (competitor follower-growth tracking, manual
lookup, no leaderboard) and **Exploding Topics** (Brian Dean's trend-discovery tool) alongside the
recurring "~15% of Google searches have never been searched before" AnswerThePublic pitch. Lists
recurring audience-growth mistakes he sees: not using all channels, inconsistency, not analyzing
one's own/competitors' analytics, and not responding to community comments/DMs.
