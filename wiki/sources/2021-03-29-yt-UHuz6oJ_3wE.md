# How to Create The Ideal Blog Homepage

## Summary
Solo tactical video on blog-homepage layout, framed around the problem of rising blog
traffic not translating into revenue. Recommends a homepage structure: (1) above-the-
fold, a direct call-to-action/offer (his own example: a live neilpatel.com site-
analysis tool that captures a lead and demonstrates value; for e-commerce, a
product demo or promotion; for services, a free-consultation booking; cites Legion
Athletics as a good above-the-fold e-commerce example); (2) below the fold, an
"about"/credibility section (about the company, or "who you are" for a personal
blog); (3) a "getting started" pathway of 3-4 numbered steps guiding new visitors
into the content/product funnel (his own: take the quiz → check the tool suite → read
the blog → subscribe to the podcast). States his own homepage gets ~66K weekly page
views (~54K unique) and is typically a top-5-to-top-10 traffic page for any blog.
Explicitly advises against a homepage that is just a list of blog posts, and states he
deliberately keeps top navigation sparse so visitors scroll through the
pitch/funnel before reaching blog content.

## Key claims
- States his own blog homepage received 65,947 page views (54,000 unique) in the
  trailing 7 days, and characterizes the homepage as typically one of a blog's
  highest-traffic pages (top 5-10 at minimum) (paraphrase, self-reported dashboard
  read, dated to this recording).
- Recommends a three-part homepage structure: above-the-fold direct offer/CTA,
  a credibility/about section, then a numbered "getting started" pathway before the
  blog feed itself (paraphrase, tactical framework).
- States deliberately minimizing top-navigation options is a design choice to keep
  visitors scrolling through the conversion pathway before they reach the blog
  (paraphrase, design philosophy).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Don't make your homepage just a list of blog articles. That's not going to drive you
> leads and sales.

> Collect leads above the fold. Sell above the fold.
