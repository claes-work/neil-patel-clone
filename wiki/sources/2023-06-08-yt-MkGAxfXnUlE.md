# How Khan Academy's AI Shows How Businesses Will Level Up

## Summary
Short co-hosted daily segment sparked by Sal Khan's TED Talk on Khan Academy's AI tutor. Eric
opens optimistic that AI coaching will broadly lift productivity. Neil pushes back with a
"garbage-in-garbage-out" argument via an anecdote about a group trading text thread ("Alpha"):
a friend was told exactly what stock move to make (short a stock that then tanked ~40%) but
didn't know how to execute the trade and wouldn't take the time to find out — Neil's point being
that an AI coach helps only people willing to do the work of asking it good questions and
researching; people who just want the answer handed to them are "screwed" whether the source is
a human coach or an AI. He extends this to marketing: AI/coach models only help if (a) inputs are
accurate and (b) inputs are recent, which he flags as a real risk for marketing specifically
because platform algorithms change constantly — cites a since-superseded Search Engine Land
figure of roughly eight or nine Google algorithm updates per day — so AI trained on even a
few-year-old corpus can confidently coach people on tactics that are no longer correct. Eric
counters that newer models (cites ChatGPT-4 browsing) can pull from the live web, reducing
staleness, and reframes the opportunity as generational: the industrial-age "be a cog" education
model is being replaced by AI-assisted self-directed learning, which rewards people who think
for themselves. Neil agrees on the proactive-learner framing and closes with a company-specific
application: training an AI coach on a curated internal corpus (his and Eric's own blog output)
so it only surfaces answers reflecting the approaches they actually want their team using.

## Key claims
- Argues AI coaching only helps people who are willing to research and ask good questions
  themselves; people expecting to be spoon-fed the answer are not helped by either a human coach
  or an AI (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-06-08). (Neil)
- Flags marketing-specific AI risk: platform/algorithm updates happen so frequently (cites ~8-9
  Google algorithm updates/day, sourced to Search Engine Land) that AI coaching trained on stale
  data can confidently give outdated tactical advice (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-06-08).
  (Neil)
- Proposes training an AI coach on a curated internal blog/content corpus so team members get
  answers reflecting the specific approach the business wants used, rather than generic scraped
  advice (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-06-08). (Neil)
