# How to Share Your Brand's Story On Your 'About' Page

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that the About page is one of the most important pages on a website — not the most visited, but the one that determines whether a visitor sticks around and returns or leaves — because it is where people go when they don't yet know you or your business. He prescribes a five-part formula for an effective About page: (1) connect through storytelling by telling the story of who you are, what you teach, and how your mission helps the visitor; (2) focus on how you will help the visitor rather than only talking about your business ("it's not about you, it's about them"); (3) be impactful by using adjectives, emotion-conveying words, and power words instead of boring text; (4) keep it short — under 500 words or under a roughly two-minute video clip — to avoid a big drop-off; and (5) leave a clear action point that directs the reader to do something next (check out products/services, leave a comment) so they convert from visitor into customer. He cites his own NeilPatel.com/about page as proof, noting it uses a story and has hundreds of comments, including on translated/subtitled versions in other languages (e.g., Portuguese/Brazilian), which he attributes to the cross-language power of emotional storytelling. He connects loyalty from storytelling to word-of-mouth referrals and higher likelihood of buying, and analogizes brand loyalty to why people default to Google over Bing.

- Step three is to be impactful by using adjectives, emotion-conveying words, and power words rather than boring text (as of 2017-06-07).
- Step four is to keep it short: an About page over 500 words, or a video over roughly two minutes, is too long and causes a big drop-off (as of 2017-06-07).
