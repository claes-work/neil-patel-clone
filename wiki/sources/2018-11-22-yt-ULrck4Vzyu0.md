# How to Create a High Converting Affiliate Marketing Sales Funnel (Q&A Thursday)

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode co-hosted with **Adam [surname garbled in captions, "from ViewerShip"]**, answering a viewer question about whether you can build a free website to start affiliate marketing. Neil's core message: affiliate marketing does NOT require money — the misconception that you need a premium domain, expensive design, or paid Facebook/Google ad traffic is wrong. His zero-budget playbook: spin up a free WordPress.com site in a vertical you are genuinely passionate about (passion sustains the content volume), create content in whatever format is easiest (text/video/audio filmed on your phone), then use free tools (BuzzSumo, SEMrush free tier, Ubersuggest) to find proven titles/keywords and create more of what already works. Then promote via outreach: find people tweeting similar content and people you link to, email them (respecting GDPR in the EU — use a contact form if unsure) and ask them to share. Capture emails with Subscribers and Hello Bar, notify subscribers of new posts, and after ~6 months of community-building, monetize by recommending affiliate products inside your posts. Adam's addition: prioritize collecting email addresses from day one (a single opt-in landing page for all your YouTube traffic) so you can later promote an affiliate program or your own product to the list.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-11-22, Neil said affiliate marketing does not cost money to start and you do not need a registered/premium domain name or paid design — a free WordPress.com site is enough to begin and monetize.
- As of 2018-11-22, Neil said most affiliates do run paid Facebook/Google ads to a landing page pushing products, and that this is a valid model, but it is not the only way and not how he would start with little-to-no money.
- As of 2018-11-22, Neil said to pick a vertical you are passionate about, because without passion you won't produce enough content or put in the hours needed to succeed.
- As of 2018-11-22, Neil said to use free tools — BuzzSumo (free version), SEMrush (free), and Ubersuggest — to find titles and keywords that have already done well, then create more of that content to attract traffic.
- As of 2018-11-22, Neil said to promote by finding people tweeting similar content and people whose pages you link to, emailing them to say you linked to them and asking them to share — while following GDPR in the EU (use the site contact form if unsure whether you can cold-email).
- As of 2018-11-22, Neil said to collect subscribers with Subscribers and Hello Bar and notify them of every new post; after ~6 months you build a community/audience and can then push affiliate products inside your posts to earn income.
- As of 2018-11-22, Adam (co-host, not the subject) added that the highest-leverage move when starting is capturing email addresses immediately — e.g. a one-page opt-in you send all YouTube traffic to — so you can later promote an affiliate program or your own product to that list.
