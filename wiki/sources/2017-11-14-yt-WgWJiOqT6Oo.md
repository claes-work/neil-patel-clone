# 4 Easy Ways to Measure Content Marketing ROI | Is Your Blogging Worth The Effort?

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out four steps for measuring the ROI of content marketing: (1) sign up for Google Analytics (free) to see how much traffic you're getting; (2) set up goal/conversion tracking inside Analytics so you can attribute revenue per lead or sale and compare it against your spend; (3) accept that content marketing takes about a year to work — results start within six months but really kick in after a year, so be patient; and (4) use SEM rush to estimate what your traffic is worth in dollars (what others pay to buy the same keyword traffic). He cites his own neilpatel.com traffic being worth "in excess of half a million dollars a month," while clarifying that is the estimated buy-value of the traffic, not his income.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-14, Neil states:
- The first step to measuring content-marketing ROI is signing up for Google Analytics, a free Google tool that shows how much traffic you're getting (as of 2017-11-14).
- The second step is tracking conversions inside Google Analytics via goal tracking; it can tell you how much each lead or sale is worth and show revenue generated from content marketing so you can match it against spend (as of 2017-11-14).
- If you spend a lot and generate even more, you're fine; if you're spending $10,000/month but only generating $1,000/month, something is wrong (as of 2017-11-14).
- It takes a year before content marketing does well — you'll see results within six months, but things really start kicking in within a year or after (as of 2017-11-14).
- Expecting to be cash-flow positive in the first 12 months probably won't happen now; it was easier five-to-ten years ago, but rankings and social (Facebook) traffic are harder to get today, so give it time (as of 2017-11-14).
- The fourth step is checking SEM rush, which (like Analytics) estimates how much traffic you're generating and its dollar value, plus what others buying traffic for your keywords are spending (as of 2017-11-14).
- Neil's neilpatel.com traffic is worth "in excess of half a million dollars a month" by SEM rush's buy-it estimate — all from content marketing — but that is the traffic's purchase value, not his income (self-reported, as of 2017-11-14).
