# How to Respond to Negative Online Reviews | 2 CRITICAL Reputation Management Tips

## Summary
Neil argues that any business operating online will eventually get a negative review — not everyone will love what you do — so the question is how you handle it. He gives two tips. First, always respond, and always positively: if a customer dislikes your service, explain why your offering is strong and what you will do to fix the experience; if the reviewer is completely wrong, still thank them for the feedback, say what you will do to improve, and explain why you believe your product or service is decent — never respond by telling them they are wrong or don't know what they're talking about. Second, actually improve your business: take feedback to heart even when you don't think you're right, because the more you work to please people, the fewer negative reviews you'll get over time. He reassures that you won't please everyone and don't need a perfect rating — a 4.5 or 4.7 out of 5 is good enough, since no one is perfect.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-15, Neil states:
- As a business online, you can't please everyone and will eventually receive a negative review (as of 2017-10-15).
- When someone leaves a negative review, you should respond — explaining why your service is great and what you'll do to fix it, or, if they're completely wrong, acknowledging the feedback and stating how you'll improve the experience while explaining why you believe your offering is decent (as of 2017-10-15).
- Responses must always be positive; never tell the reviewer they're wrong or don't know what they're talking about — instead say you appreciate the feedback and explain why you disagree or what you'll do to fix it (as of 2017-10-15).
- You should improve your business even when you don't feel you're right — take feedback to heart and try to make the business better (as of 2017-10-15).
- The more you try to please people, the fewer negative reviews you'll get over time (as of 2017-10-15).
- A perfect rating isn't necessary; a 4.5 or 4.7 out of 5 is good enough because no one is perfect (as of 2017-10-15).
