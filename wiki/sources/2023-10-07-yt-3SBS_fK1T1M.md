# How to Deal With Creator's Block - Neil Patel

## Summary
Short Marketing School episode on beating "creator's block." Neil says ChatGPT-generated
title/concept ideas mostly don't solve real creative block; what works better is studying
adjacent (non-competing) creators/agencies for content ideas that transfer across similar
industries, running internal employee brainstorm incentives with cash prizes, and staying
in peer groups (in-person dinners, WhatsApp/chat groups, masterminds) with other creators
and business people to trade what's working.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- ChatGPT-generated title/concept ideas are common now but don't really solve most
  people's creator's block. (Neil, 2023-10-07) — content-marketing / ai-marketing.
- His preferred method: study "Analytics"/social profiles of adjacent-but-non-competing
  players in his space (e.g., as a performance-marketing agency owner, looking at what a
  web/app design creative shop is doing on social) — content ideas that work in similar
  industries tend to transfer. (Neil, 2023-10-07) — content-marketing.
- At NP Digital, runs internal employee brainstorming with cash prizes for ideas the
  company actually uses, plus a larger bonus ("kicker") if the resulting content performs
  exceptionally well. (Neil, 2023-10-07) — agency-entrepreneurship / content-marketing.
- Recommends staying in contact with other creators/entrepreneurs outside your direct
  competitive set — in person (dinners, masterminds — mentions being in one heading to
  Turkey) or via ongoing chat groups (WhatsApp, text) — as a steady source of content and
  business ideas; cross-references Eric's "Eric Does Dinners" event series as an example
  of a format that's improved with iteration. (Neil, 2023-10-07) — content-marketing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> You just got to try out new things. That's probably one of the biggest ways we've got
> around creator's block.

> The other thing that's really easy to do too, is you just go and you just start talking
> to other creators... you can ask them what's working, what's not, what are you testing?

> Take a look at your analytics, figure out what resonates with your audience, and maybe
> sometimes produce more of that. But also make sure you're having fun at the same time.
