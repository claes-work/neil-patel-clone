# What Should You Do When Someone Plagiarizes Your Content?

## Summary
In this short solo video, Neil Patel advises content creators on how to respond when others copy their work. He argues the response should be proportional and low-effort: if a site republishes content without linking back, a polite email to their contact page usually gets it removed; if a scraper site copies content but links back, no action is needed because Google recognizes scraper sites and it won't hurt rankings. He stresses that Google does not penalize duplicate content, that having the same content across multiple sites does not reduce rankings, and that people borrowing content while linking back is natural and can actually grow traffic. He recommends the free tool Copyscape to detect who has copied a page, discourages involving lawyers as not worth the ROI, and frames widespread content duplication as simply how the web works.

## Key claims
- If someone plagiarizes your content and does not link back to you, the best first step is to email them via their website's contact page and ask them to take it down; almost everyone complies (as of 2017-06-26).
- Scraper sites that copy your content but link back to you require no action, and Google recognizes these as scraper sites so they will not hurt your rankings (as of 2017-06-26).
- ★ Google does not penalize for duplicate content; the same piece of content on five different websites does not reduce your rankings (as of 2017-06-26).
- When others borrow parts of your content and link back / cite you as a reference, you should let them, because this is natural and helps grow your traffic (as of 2017-06-26).
- Even when people take your content and only give a small, hidden link, you should accept it, because the more people who do this the more your Google rankings will eventually benefit (as of 2017-06-26).
- Copyscape is a free tool where you enter your URL and it shows everyone who has taken or plagiarized your content (as of 2017-06-26).
- Involving lawyers over plagiarized content is not worth the ROI; an email is sufficient since almost everyone removes it (as of 2017-06-26).
- ★ Most content on the internet is duplicate, so widespread copying is simply how the web works (as of 2017-06-26).

## Notable verbatim quotes
