# How to Optimize Your Blog to Get Explosive Traffic Growth in 2020 (YouTube title later updated to "2023")

## Summary
Solo six-step blog-growth checklist for 2020, framed against a crowded field of over a billion
blogs. (1) **Technical SEO** — link building alone is not enough when hundreds of ranking
factors are in play; page speed, title tags, meta descriptions and an XML sitemap all matter,
and he routes viewers to Ubersuggest's Site Audit to crawl and prioritize the fixes.
(2) **Target keywords that convert** — head terms are being eaten by zero-click search (per
Jumpshot, roughly half of Google searches end in no click, because Google answers generic
questions like "what is the weather" directly), so mine Ubersuggest's questions, comparisons
and prepositions reports for lower-volume, high-intent long-tails; he ties this to broad core
updates and Google's move beyond keywords to parsing prepositions and verbs. (3) **Optimize
for mobile** — AMP plus responsive design; notably he reports AMP gives him little US lift but
a 10%+ mobile-search increase in regions like Brazil and India, and recommends Google's
Mobile-Friendly Testing Tool. (4) **Optimize for voice search** — citing Comscore that over
50% of searches are now voice, he prescribes structured markup (for rich snippets) plus mobile
friendliness. (5) **Focus on your audience, not the platforms** — Google, Facebook, Instagram
and LinkedIn all optimize for people, so answering real questions wins; use Ubersuggest's
content ideas report to see what's hot by social shares. (6) **Measure with Google Analytics** —
not just installing it but tracking goals and conversions, which content converts, which pages
drive bounces vs. time on site, entry vs. exit pages, and UTM tracking to validate campaigns —
so you stop producing content nobody cares about. Closes plugging Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil said there are already over a billion blogs on the internet and that sites are "not giving as much love as they used to," making 2020 blog growth harder.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil said Google has hundreds of ranking factors, so neglecting technical SEO (page speed, title tags, meta descriptions, XML sitemap) caps your results no matter how good your link building is.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil recommended Ubersuggest's Site Audit report to crawl a whole site, surface errors and prioritize the technical fixes.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil cited Jumpshot that roughly half of Google searches result in no click at all — neither ad nor organic — because Google answers generic questions itself.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil said long-tail, high-intent phrases still get clicked through, and attributed part of this to broad algorithm updates in which Google looks beyond keywords at prepositions, verbs and phrasing to understand intent.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil recommended mining Ubersuggest's keyword ideas report — questions, comparisons, prepositions — for lower-traffic but high-intent phrases that actually convert.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil said more people search Google on mobile than desktop, and that Google's AMP markup makes pages load in about a second.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil reported from his own experience that making pages AMP-compatible produced little US traffic increase, but typically a 10-plus-percent increase in mobile search traffic from regions like Brazil and India.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil said a non-mobile-friendly site loses visitors to the back button, raising bounce rate and degrading user metrics and therefore rankings, and pointed to Google's Mobile-Friendly Testing Tool to check.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil cited Comscore that over 50% of searches are now voice search, and said failing to optimize for Google Home and Alexa means struggling as the shift continues.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil's voice-search prescription was structured markup (to win rich snippets) plus mobile friendliness — two basics that should lift voice-search traffic.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil repeated his platform-agnostic principle: Google, Facebook, Instagram and LinkedIn all want what's best for people, so focusing on people rather than on the platform is what wins.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil recommended Ubersuggest's content ideas report to find what's hot by social shares in your space.
- As of 2019-12-14, Neil said everyone uses Google Analytics but few track goals and conversions, which content converts, bounce-causing pages, time on site, entry vs. exit pages, or UTM-tagged campaign performance — and that this data prevents creating content and campaigns nobody cares about.
