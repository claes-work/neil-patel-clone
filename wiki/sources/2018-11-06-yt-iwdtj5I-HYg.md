# Advanced Content Marketing Strategy (Step-By-Step) | A Look Into Neil Patel's Brain

_Recording of a live virtual "Advanced Content Marketing Summit" session. Hosts **Clayton Wood**
and co-host **Vasil** (caption "vasil azeroth" — surname uncertain) moderate/ask audience
questions; **Neil presents by sharing his screen** through his real analytics and tools. Only
**Neil-attributed** material trains the persona; host/moderator lines are context. A friend
**Ryan** (works at HubSpot) is cited second-hand._

## Summary

Neil walks his live analytics/tools stack to show his actual content process. Core doctrine:
**quantity wins, then double down on the natural hits.** Content is "a hit or miss game" — you
**can't predict** which posts win, so publish a lot at a good-enough quality bar, then **find the
naturally-winning pages in Google Search Console** (click **Pages**, not queries), pull the
**queries/impressions** each winner already ranks for, and **rewrite that post to be more thorough,
integrating those keywords** (into body + title tag + meta description, via Yoast) — not
keyword-stuffing, just more complete. His flagship example: **"13 secrets… Facebook organic reach"**
rewritten into **"20 secrets…"** grew ~13k→18k+ visits; a rewritten Instagram post spiked similarly.
After rewriting, **request indexing** in Search Console (Fetch as Google) so the change is picked up
fast. He reports traffic growth from **~695k → ~823k unique/month** in a month from ramping
production, with **~551 Google visitors per blog post** (722 English posts) and a goal of
**5–7M visits/month within two years**. Second engine: **competitor top-page mining** — put
competitor URLs into **Ahrefs** (top pages by Google traffic) and **BuzzSumo** (top by social
shares), then write a **more thorough version** of their best posts (Brian Dean's **skyscraper
technique**). Third: **tools beat content on ROI** — his **SEO Analyzer tool** is his #1 page and
HubSpot's email-signature generator (per Ryan) produces leads as good as content, cheaper.

Q&A tactics: keyword-density is matched to competitors by count, not stuffed (he uses SEO tools to
compare); to beat high-authority sites (WebMD) **go niche** — Google's **Hummingbird** update favors
focused niche sites (Penny Hoarder beating a bigger competitor); **outreach for shares** by emailing
everyone who tweeted a competing BuzzSumo article and everyone you link out to (20–30% say yes even
in industries where he's unknown); **email = ~$1/subscriber/month** rule of thumb; **stick to .com**
TLDs; feature snippets aren't chased (he often **loses** snippets when he rewrites, so he no longer
rewrites snippet-holding posts); marketing-automation stack = **Infusionsoft** (most features, poor
deliverability), **ConvertKit** (great deliverability, fewer features/buggy), **HubSpot** (sales
team's favorite). Time split at scale: **~60–70% creation / ~20% rewriting / ~10% distribution**;
when starting out, **50% writing / 50% promotion**. Find writers on **ProBlogger job board**;
he pays **~$100–1,000/post**. Single biggest lesson: **quantity usually wins** (HubSpot, Huffington
Post, Business Insider) — "some quality standard, then quantity."
