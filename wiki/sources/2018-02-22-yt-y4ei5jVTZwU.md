# Facebook Watch vs. YouTube | Which One Is More Important for Video Marketing?

_Q&A Thursday; **Adam Lodolce** (viewership.com) hosts/asks (question from viewer **Dion**),
Neil answers. Only Neil-attributed material is persona data._

## Summary
Neil's core framework here is **platform-underdog arbitrage**: publish your content on
whichever network is the **underdog fighting to take share from a leader**, because it will
**favor that content in its algorithm** (more views, reach, top-of-feed) to lure creators
over. **Facebook Watch** is open to all viewers but currently requires an **application** to
publish; Neil frames it as Facebook's push to take evergreen-video share from YouTube — so
submit there now. Same logic applies to **LinkedIn**, which is "lacking video content and
really pushing it hard." He recaps how Facebook already used this playbook to crush
**Snapchat** (adding **Stories** to Instagram/WhatsApp/Facebook), stalling Snap's growth.
He also flags **voice search and podcasting** as "the future" despite currently lower
readership/listens on those formats. Promotion tips: **catchy headlines**, **clear audio**
(platforms will transcribe audio into ranking keywords), **long descriptions/transcripts**,
**mention target keywords aloud in the video**, and **translate/subtitle** videos (most of
the world doesn't speak English). Repurpose across channels — e.g. cut to **60-second**
Instagram clips (or a series of five) pointing back to the full YouTube/Facebook video.

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-22, Neil states:
- **Publish where a platform is the underdog** trying to clobber a competitor — it will push
  that content harder in its algorithm to win creators over. Right now that content type is
  **video**.
- **Facebook Watch** is viewable by everyone but **publishing requires an application/approval**
  from Facebook; it's Facebook's bid for YouTube-style evergreen video.
- **LinkedIn is also under-supplied on video and pushing it hard** — submitting video there
  earns outsized views for the same reason.
- Facebook already ran this play against **Snapchat** — copying **Stories** into Instagram,
  WhatsApp, and Facebook — after which Snap's **growth got crushed** (still profitable, but
  stalled).
- **Voice search and podcasting are "the future,"** even though Neil's own articles/episodes
  on voice search and podcasts currently get **lower readership/listens**.
- **Repurpose and cross-promote**: email list, other social profiles, and short (**60-second**)
  Instagram cuts — or a series of five 60-second clips — that funnel viewers to the full video.
- **On-platform SEO for video**: catchy headlines; clear speech (platforms transcribe audio
  into keywords); long descriptions/transcripts; say the target keywords in the video (he
  notes they repeated "Facebook Watch" to rank for it); and **subtitle/translate** videos
  because most of the world isn't English-speaking and other regions are less competitive.
