A seven-trend predictions video for 2021 digital marketing, opening on the marginal-
gains framing (British cycling coach Dave Brailsford — already an established Neil
touchstone in the corpus) as the meta-lesson: no single "holy grail" hack wins a
saturated, COVID-accelerated market; small compounding edges do. The seven trends: (1)
page speed matters more than ever — a CDN-for-non-static-files hack he uses on
neilpatel.com is cited as good for up to +7% conversions; (2) text content length
matters less, but audio/video length should trend toward 10+ minutes (under-10-minute
video/audio underperforms, but the payoff plateaus past ~10 minutes — no added benefit
from 20-30 minutes); (3) marginal gains generally (the Brailsford framing applied to
things like voice-search compatibility); (4) top-rank position matters more as Google
takes more SERP real estate for its own monetization (flights, weather, calculators);
(5) repurposing/updating existing content becomes the norm over publishing new content
(cross-links to the standing "rank old content" doctrine); (6) backlink count matters
less relative to user signals/relevance (restates the standing user-signals-over-links
thesis); (7) brand power increases across all platforms as a defense against "fake
news" and platform distrust, tied again to the rule-of-seven touchpoint doctrine.
