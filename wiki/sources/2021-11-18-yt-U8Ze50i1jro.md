# 10 Free Tools That'll Help You Get Your First 10,000 Social Media Followers

## Summary
A 10-tool social-growth stack: MobileMonkey (automated Instagram DM/comment
interactions to reward mentions/story-tags); Social Blade (track competitor/peer growth
rates and "model after the winners" — names Backlinko, Ahrefs, and Eric Siu as
marketers he studies this way); Google Trends (ride upward-trending topics, avoid
declining ones); mention.com (brand/competitor mention tracking); Not Just Analytics
(Instagram competitor engagement/spike analysis); the Facebook Ad Library (see what ad
messaging competitors run, including messaging that's failing); Canva (fast templated
creative production); Agorapulse (centralized cross-channel engagement/response); Later
(cross-platform content scheduling, credited as key to his own consistency-driven
growth); and Restream (simultaneous multi-platform live streaming). Cites a Hootsuite
stat (2h24m/day average social media usage; ~57% of the global population uses social
media).

## Key claims
- Explicitly names Eric Siu (alongside Backlinko and Ahrefs) as a peer marketer he
  benchmarks via Social Blade — "just because I'm in marketing... it doesn't mean that I
  know everything. You always want to take that mentality of you can get better"
  (self-reported, dated 2021-11-18) — social-media, agency-entrepreneurship. Reinforces
  [[wiki/entities/eric-siu]] as a recurring named peer/collaborator (Marketing School
  co-host); a humility framing not previously logged this explicitly.
- Self-attributes his own social-media growth not to having "the best content" but to
  consistency, enabled by scheduling tools like Later (self-reported, dated 2021-11-18)
  — social-media. New explicit statement of the consistency-over-quality growth
  mechanism.
- Cites a Hootsuite statistic: average daily social media usage of 2 hours 24 minutes,
  and ~57% global-population social media penetration (third-party stat, dated
  2021-11-18) — social-media.
- Facebook Ad Library tactic: check which ad messages/topics competitors are running
  that are *not* performing well (still visible in the library despite being paid) as a
  signal to avoid those angles organically too (paraphrase, dated 2021-11-18) —
  social-media, paid-ads.
