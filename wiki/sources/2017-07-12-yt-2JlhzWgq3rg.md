# 7 Cool Hacks That'll Boost Your Conversion Rate

## Summary
Seven concrete conversion-rate tactics, each attached to a self-reported lift and a named tool. The set spans urgency (countdown clock), email capture (animated-GIF exit pop-ups), a low-commitment $1-trial offer emailed to non-buyers, geo-targeted personalization (city in the headline), personalized-image emails (PicSnippet), remarketing to cart/checkout abandoners, and lead-generating quizzes (LeadQuizzes). Notable for its stack of quantified before/after numbers from neilpatel.com.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-12, Neil states:
- A genuine countdown clock (accurately tied to when the offer ends) lifts conversions — he saw an 11% increase on neilpatel.com from that one tweak (as of 2017-07-12).
- Use exit pop-ups with an animated GIF background showing what the visitor will get (related to the exit offer) — one of his highest-converting email-capture elements; build it with Hello Bar (as of 2017-07-12).
- Offer a last-ditch low-commitment offer to non-buyers (the majority): a $1 trial or a 30-day free trial with credit card upfront, emailed separately so it doesn't affect front-end conversions; his $1 trial on informational products raised revenue by 15% (as of 2017-07-12).
- Personalize pages via geo-targeting — putting the visitor's city in the headline raised his conversions by over 20% because the offer feels made for them (as of 2017-07-12).
- Use PicSnippet to send personalized-image emails (e.g. Neil "holding a sign" with the recipient's name dynamically inserted) — he uses it to re-engage webinar no-shows, and it lifts clicks/conversions (as of 2017-07-12).
- Do remarketing — few people bother despite it being old; retarget the 95%+ who don't convert across Google, YouTube, and Facebook. His specific play: remarket checkout/credit-card-page abandoners with a Facebook/YouTube video showing what they'd get and answering objections (as of 2017-07-12).
- Use quizzes (he names LeadQuizzes) to engage and capture leads — he found ~60% of people complete a quiz and often convert to a lead; a "learn how to grow your traffic" quiz asks 5–8 qualifying questions then gates the results behind name+email (as of 2017-07-12).
