# How to Dominate Facebook Marketing Despite Its Algorithm Hating You

## Summary

Neil argues that Facebook organic reach, engagement, and clicks have been continually declining, but marketers should not give up or default to paid ads (which cost per click forever). His central move: **shift organic attention from posting on your page to Facebook groups**. He grounds this in Facebook's own strategic pivot — citing a Q2 2018 earnings call reporting **200 million members in "meaningful groups"** (active, growing, engaged), a number he says has more than doubled year over year, plus the **May 2019 F8 conference** where Facebook signaled a shift from public news feeds to private experiences amid privacy concerns. Tactics for groups: don't just drop links to your own site — **engage, respond, and help members** (in your own group and others'); create content that **causes comments** (controversial takes, questions, debates, polls) rather than relying on videos, which he says lost algorithmic favor as Facebook filled with video. The engagement mechanic: once a large share of your group already engages with you, they'll actually see your posts when you later link back to your site. He also pushes **Facebook Messenger as an email-list substitute** built via **MobileMonkey or ManyChat** (free plans), citing 1.3 billion monthly Messenger users (TechCrunch) and Messenger/Facebook as the #2 and #1 most-downloaded apps of all time (App Annie). He reports his own last-30-day Messenger stats of ~88% open rates and ~56% click-through rates. Closes on **consistency** — quality over quantity — and a plug for his agency, Neil Patel Digital.
