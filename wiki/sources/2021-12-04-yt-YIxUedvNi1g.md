# How to Reverse-Engineer The Strategy of The Most Successful Competitors in Digital Marketing ★

## Summary
A cross-channel competitor-research tool roster, organized by discipline. Social:
Social Blade (track what content/posting days drive competitor follower growth vs.
your own), "Not Just Analytics" (Instagram engagement analysis — what's working across
posts, Reels, IGTV, Stories, with a caution to test underperforming competitor formats
carefully rather than copy them outright), and Sociality.io (all-in-one cross-platform
competitor data). Paid ads: SpyFu (competitor ad keywords/spend), SEMrush and Ahrefs
(captioned as "Atrius" — likely caption garble), Ubersuggest, the Facebook Ad Library
(his stated favorite — reasoning: ads competitors pay for and still fail to get
engagement on are a signal that organic use of the same angle won't work either), and
BigSpy. SEO: Ubersuggest (competitor keyword gaps, backlink-opportunity outreach,
project-based recurring competitor reports). Content: Clearscope, Frase.io, SurferSEO
(keyword-driven content-quality guidance). Closes with a differentiation tactic: use
Google Trends to catch keywords trending upward before competitors do, arguing an early
mover on a trending term can outrank higher-authority competitors given strong user
metrics (time on site, page speed, low back-button rate) — and to deprioritize keywords
trending downward even if currently rankable.
