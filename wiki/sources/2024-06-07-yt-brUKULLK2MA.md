# Trump campaign raises $53M after verdict, 'Roaring Kitty' helped GameStop shares jump 30%, & more

## Summary
Discusses Donald Trump's reported $53M raised within 24 hours of his guilty verdict, framed
explicitly as a marketing/fundraising-mechanics discussion rather than a political endorsement;
Neil predicts the fundraising surge will both rally supporters and deepen political polarization,
and separately relays a YPO Beverly Hills chapter retreat conversation (a bus poll of ~10 members on
who would win the election) where 9 of 10 predicted a Trump win despite most members self-
identifying as Democratic-leaning — Neil places himself as "slightly on the right" within an
otherwise Centrist-leaning, mostly-Democratic-voting California social circle. The episode covers
"Roaring Kitty"'s return and the resulting GameStop stock volatility (a ~$116M position screenshot,
reported paper gains near $792M, a brief Robinhood trading pause) as a case study in using personal
influence to move a stock price, drawing a parallel to Elon Musk's crypto tweets and predicting SEC
scrutiny. A separate story: entrepreneur/TV personality Bethany (Skinny Girl vodka founder, ex-Real
Housewives) was denied appointment-less entry to a Chanel store despite her fame, sparking social-
media backlash — used to discuss the tension between retail scarcity/security and customer
experience (with a parallel drawn to Rodeo Drive stores requiring waitlists, e.g. Louis Vuitton).
Neil then recounts a friend (a hedge-fund manager who charters a Global 7500 jet) who collects
Patek Philippe watches he rarely wears purely for their appreciating, collateralizable value and
growing scarcity (waitlists now run ~6 months due to COVID-era supply constraints and rising
demand) — framing scarcity/exclusivity as a deliberate value driver, with a tie-back to Bryan
Johnson's "Don't Die" philosophy (unlimited life removes the stakes that make people take life
seriously, just as unlimited casino chips or video-game lives remove stakes). Neil closes by
reflecting on a recent interview with Marie Forleo: her ~20-year consistency running one group-
coaching program ("B-School") without reinventing her offer, estimating (via his own back-of-
envelope multiplication of her disclosed student counts and pricing, discounted for scholarships/
affiliates) that her lifetime course revenue is roughly $80M.

## Key claims
- **Places himself politically as "slightly on the right"** within his YPO Beverly Hills chapter,
  which he describes as majority-Democratic-voting despite most members self-identifying as
  Centrist; states he still votes (blue, given California's outcome is largely predetermined) even
  though "it doesn't matter." (Neil, 2024-06-07; self-reported)
- **On a YPO retreat bus, ~9 of 10 chapter members predicted a Trump election win despite most
  being registered/leaning Democratic** — cited as evidence that public political affiliation and
  private electoral predictions can diverge. (Neil relaying a group anecdote, 2024-06-07)
- **Negative political marketing is high-risk: "if you're going to make a bet on negative
  marketing, you better make sure you win — and win definitively."** Applied to the Trump-felon
  branding attempt by opponents, which Neil argues may backfire by galvanizing Trump's base and
  even converting undecided voters, given visible Democratic pushback against the prosecution as
  unfair. (Neil, 2024-06-07)
- **Watch-collecting friend anecdote:** a hedge-fund-manager friend (charters, doesn't own, a
  Global 7500 jet — implied ~$775M aircraft value) collects Patek Philippe watches he rarely wears;
  learned that watches worth over $5M can be collateralized/borrowed against. Notes that Patek
  prices and waitlists have both risen sharply (a watch he says he "picked up" a decade-plus ago for
  ~$70-80K is now worth $200K+) due to flat production, growing global wealth, and COVID-era supply
  disruption creating genuine scarcity — waitlists now run about six months versus walk-in
  availability and negotiability in the past. (Neil, 2024-06-07; friend's data self-reported/
  secondhand)
- **Scarcity/exclusivity as a deliberate value and engagement driver**, explicitly tied to Bryan
  Johnson's "Don't Die" philosophy: unlimited resources or lifespan remove the stakes that make
  people take something (money in a casino, lives in a video game, life itself) seriously. (Neil,
  2024-06-07)
- **Marie Forleo interview reflection:** praises her ~20-year consistency running one core group-
  coaching offer (B-School) without chasing new formats, and estimates (his own rough calculation
  from her disclosed student volume and pricing, discounted for scholarships/affiliate splits) that
  her lifetime course revenue totals roughly $80M. (Neil, 2024-06-07; estimate is Neil's own
  back-of-envelope math from Forleo's self-disclosed figures, not independently verified)
