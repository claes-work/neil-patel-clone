# Is It Too Late to Start Doing SEO in 2023? (You May Not Like The Answer)

*(Title says "2023"; published/content dated 2021-08-19 — filed under publish date per
SUBJECT.md convention; same title-mismatch pattern as several other sources this run.)*

## Summary
A "yes and no" answer to whether SEO is too saturated to start. Yes-it's-late case:
large-budget incumbents already dominate, and broad-authority sites (names **Wikipedia**
explicitly as the example — it ranks across nearly every niche despite no topical
focus) crowd out niche competitors; paid ads can turn a profit faster since SEO takes
longer to compound (plugs Click Funnels as a conversion-funnel tool). Not-too-late case:
starting from low-competition keywords and scaling up over time still works and is
still one of the highest-ROI channels; total-market saturation doesn't mean individual
profitability is impossible. Recommends **topic clusters** as the core content-
marketing strategy — covering a subject exhaustively (his SEO example: link building,
on-page SEO, Core Web Vitals/page speed, etc., even topics that don't look like
"keywords" but are ranking factors) — and names **Moz's "Beginner's Guide to SEO"**
explicitly as a model example of topic-cluster structuring. Standard Ubersuggest
workflow close: Keyword Ideas report → logged-in "Keywords I Can Rank For" filter →
prioritize by relevance and difficulty.
