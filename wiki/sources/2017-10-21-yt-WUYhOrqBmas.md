# 4 Steps to Effortlessly Boost Your SEO Traffic | Increase Your SERP Click Through Rate!

## Summary
Neil lays out a four-step method for raising SERP click-through rate without first improving rankings. He argues you should identify the keywords each page already ranks for via Google Search Console, place those keywords (ideally near the front) in the page's title tag and meta description, add an explicit benefit-driven promise to the title, and use adjectives such as "effortless" to make the listing more compelling. He frames higher CTR as itself a driver of rankings, claiming that as a page earns more clicks than competitors its rankings will eventually rise.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-21, Neil states:
- Google Search Console shows which keywords each of your pages is ranking for, and this is the starting point for improving SERP CTR (as of 2017-10-21).
- Placing your ranking keywords within the title and description — ideally toward the front — increases click-through rate (as of 2017-10-21).
- Including a benefit-driven promise in the title tag (e.g., "how to sell your home in the next 24 hours") makes more people click because they understand the benefit, provided you can actually deliver on the promise (as of 2017-10-21).
- Adding adjectives such as "effortless" to a title increases clicks versus the same title without them (as of 2017-10-21).
- Higher click-through rate than your competitors eventually causes your rankings to rise (as of 2017-10-21).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Who says you have to rank higher on Google to get more clicks?"
