# Gartner predicts search traffic will drop 26%, 40% of Americans use TikTok for search, and more

## Summary
Three segments, all heavily cross-talk/debate style and undiarized. (1) A debate over a Gartner prediction that traditional search-engine traffic (organic + paid) will drop ~25-26% by 2026 as users shift to AI chatbots/virtual agents. One host is more skeptical of the magnitude, arguing platforms fragment and adapt (citing Snapchat's continued growth — 46M DAU/800M MAU — despite "no one uses Snapchat anymore" perception, and that ~40% of Americans now search on TikTok) rather than one platform straightforwardly killing another (TikTok didn't kill Instagram, Instagram didn't kill YouTube); the other agrees search behavior/format will change substantially ("search as we know it") but expects overall search *volume* to keep growing, not drop 26%, because platforms like Google continuously adapt. Both agree they don't personally use TikTok much themselves despite the 40%-search-share stat, delegating content posting to a team. (2) A reaction to Morning Brew co-founder Austin Rief's claim that "there's no better time to have an engaged newsletter" — both hosts state a firm belief that newsletters are not standalone businesses (their own newsletters are marketing/lead-gen channels funneling to higher-LTV back-end services, not revenue centers themselves) and that paid newsletters are generally bad businesses due to a small addressable market, except in categories like finance (cited: a friend, Matt Paulson, running finance newsletters at ~$30M/year, mostly sponsorship-driven) where "the perception that it makes you money" converts unusually well. (3) A tangent on Bitcoin ETF inflows (disputed live on-air between $10B and $121B depending on assets-under-management vs. market-cap conflation, eventually reconciled), framed as "the marketing genius of Bitcoin" — a 15+-year-old product that "just works" and continues attracting capital through organic demand/hype rather than paid promotion.

## Key claims
- **Disputes a Gartner forecast of a 25-26% drop in search-engine traffic by 2026, predicting search volume will keep growing even as search behavior/format shifts substantially away from traditional "10 blue links."** Reasoning: platforms historically fragment attention (TikTok, Snapchat) without literally killing incumbents (Instagram, YouTube), and search engines continuously adapt their product to the platforms/behaviors that emerge. `(opinion, dated 2024-03-25; attribution: uncertain — undiarized, joint framing)`
- **40% of Americans reportedly search on TikTok**, cited as evidence of behavioral fragmentation across platforms rather than displacement of any one platform — both hosts state they personally barely use TikTok despite this, delegating posting to their teams while still creating some of the underlying content themselves. `(third-party stat, dated; attribution: uncertain — undiarized)`
- **Newsletters are not viable standalone businesses** except in a few categories (finance cited as the strongest exception, e.g. Agora-style "this will make you money" positioning); both hosts use their own newsletters purely as marketing/lead-gen channels driving to higher-LTV back-end services (webinars, sales calls), not as revenue centers, and both explicitly state they do not believe in paid/subscription newsletters as a business model. `(opinion, dated 2024-03-25; attribution: uncertain — undiarized, joint framing, consistent with Neil's established "content marketing not content business" framing elsewhere)`
- **Frames Bitcoin's sustained ETF demand as "the marketing genius of Bitcoin"** — a product with no traditional marketing budget that has simply "worked" since 2008 and continues attracting capital through word-of-mouth/hype-driven organic demand, contrasted with the constrained new-supply of ~900 new Bitcoin mined per day. `(opinion, dated 2024-03-25; attribution: uncertain — undiarized, joint framing)`
