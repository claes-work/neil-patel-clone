# The Beginner's Guide to Content Marketing in 2020 ★

## Summary
Solo nine-step beginner's guide (title now reads "2023"; captions say 2020). Framing:
content marketing is still the cheapest and most effective way to get targeted visitors, and
he cites **HubSpot** that marketers who prioritize blogging are **13x more likely to see
positive ROI**. (1) **Define your goals** — niche, content type, purpose; his contrarian
emphasis is that it isn't only about the audience: *you* have to be passionate about the
subject or the content won't come across as great. (2) **Research your audience** — build
personas around their questions and problems, so you write something you love *and* people
want, which also helps you rank; source the problems from Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas →
Questions report. (3) **Keyword research** — don't chase merely popular keywords, chase
commercially valuable ones: **high-CPC keywords tend to convert better** and generate more
income. (4) **Content ideation** — study competitors via Ubersuggest's top-pages report or
the content-ideas report (he notes BuzzSumo and **AnswerThePublic** as paid alternatives
while Ubersuggest is free); read the breakdown of social shares vs. search traffic vs.
backlinks, because a competitor page with lots of search traffic and no social shares shows
over-reliance on Google — you want pages strong in both so traffic is diversified. (5)
**Optimize for voice** — structured data markup → rich snippets → voice results, done *now*
rather than when voice is 70–80% of searches (again citing the Comscore ~50% figure and the
rise of Google Home and Alexa). (6) **Videos and visual content** — visual storytelling is
taking over from Instagram to Facebook to YouTube; alternatives are podcasts and
infographics. He discloses his own cadence: **four blog posts a month but over twelve videos
a month**, because video builds a deeper connection to the brand. (7) **Leverage social
media** — answering the common objection that reach is dying: the platforms are popular
regardless, the counter is going live constantly (he cites **Tai Lopez**, who sells directly
from live video and converts extremely well), and uploading native video still performs even
though links off-platform don't. (8) **Follow E-A-T** — the Medic Update means don't write
outside your credibility; he again disclaims health advice ("should you go to a hospital or
not… it can damage someone") and financial advice ("I'm not a financial planner… how to
maximize their 401k"), and prescribes an author bio on every page, links to social profiles,
guest posting, and even a Wikipedia page. (9) **Build backlinks** — Ubersuggest's backlinks
report on a competitor URL shows their most-linked pages, who links and the anchor text, so
you know what to replicate and who to pitch. Closes on an agency pitch.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil called content marketing still one of the cheapest and most effective ways to get targeted visitors, citing **HubSpot** that marketers prioritizing blogging are **13x more likely to see positive ROI**.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil said content strategy starts with the creator, not just the audience: if you don't write about something you're passionate about, it won't come across as great content and won't perform.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil said to build audience personas from the questions your audience asks and the problems you can solve — writing what you love *and* what people want to hear also helps you rank higher.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil's problem-discovery method was Ubersuggest → keyword ideas → **Questions** tab, treating the question keywords as the audience's actual problems.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil said not to chase popular keywords but commercially valuable ones — **high-CPC keywords tend to convert better and generate more income** for the site owner.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil's ideation workflow was competitor URL → Ubersuggest top-pages report, or keyword → content-ideas report, with **BuzzSumo** and **AnswerThePublic** named as paid equivalents to the free Ubersuggest.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil said to read ideation data as a diversification signal: heavy search traffic with no social shares means over-reliance on Google; the pages worth copying have both search traffic and social shares.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil repeated the **Comscore** estimate that roughly 50% of searches are voice, and urged optimizing with structured data markup now rather than waiting until voice hits 70–80% of searches.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil said rich snippets are the mechanism by which Google pulls your listing into voice answers.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil disclosed his own publishing cadence: **only four blog posts a month, but over twelve videos a month**, because he sees video becoming more popular and better for building a deeper audience connection.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil's answer to "my reach is dying, why be on social media?" was that the platforms are popular either way; **go live all the time**, citing **Tai Lopez** selling directly from live videos with extremely good conversion — and noting that native uploads still do well even though driving people off-platform gets less engagement.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil re-stated **E-A-T** and the **Medic Update**, disclaiming his own credibility in health ("I'm not an expert on that… it can damage someone") and in finance ("I'm not a financial planner… I should stick to what I know").
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil's E-A-T checklist was: author bio on every content page, links to social profiles, guest posting to build credibility, and possibly creating a **Wikipedia page**.
- As of 2020-01-06, Neil's beginner link-building method was Ubersuggest's backlinks report on a competitor URL — revealing their most-linked pages, the linking sites and the anchor text — to decide which pages to replicate and whom to pitch.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Everyone thinks it's just about your audience, but you're the one creating and writing it. If you don't write content that you're passionate about, that you love, it won't come across as great content and it won't do well."

> "You don't just want to go after popular keywords… Keywords that tend to have a high CPC tend to convert better and generate more income for the website owner."

> "If they're getting a ton of search traffic but no social shares, that means you're just too reliant on Google."

> "The last thing you want to do is see seventy, eighty percent of the searches being voice search and then you start optimizing for it. You want to do it now before it gets to that point."

> "I only create four blog posts a month, but I publish over twelve videos a month."

> "A lot of people tell me, Neil, my reach is continually dying, why shouldn't I be on social media? Either way, whether your reach is dying or not, these sites are super popular."
