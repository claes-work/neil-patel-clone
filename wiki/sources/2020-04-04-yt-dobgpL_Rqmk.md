# 96% Of Your Landing Page Visitors Will NEVER Convert (And How to Improve That)

## Summary
Neil breaks down three UX factors that hurt landing-page conversion, framed around
Unbounce's stat that the average landing-page conversion rate is 4.02% (so ~96% of
visitors never convert). (1) Page speed — cites a 50% of users expecting load times
under two seconds, and a Walmart datapoint that every one-second speed improvement
raised conversions 2%. (2) CTA placement relative to "the fold" — cites an NN/g figure
that content above vs. below the fold gets an 84% difference in usage, and an Unbounce
test showing a 41% conversion spike from moving a CTA above the fold; notes the
counter-case (less content above the fold can be used deliberately to push scrolling)
and recommends Crazy Egg heatmap/scrollmap testing to decide. Recommends multiple CTAs
on most pages (except single-button checkout pages). (3) Removing clutter — cites a
HubSpot test where removing navigational links raised free-trial page conversion 14%
and demo-page conversion 28%. Closes with an agency plug.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-04-04, Neil cited Unbounce that the average landing-page conversion rate
  is 4.02%, meaning ~96% of visitors don't convert.
- As of 2020-04-04, Neil said 50% of users expect a page to load in under two seconds,
  and cited a Walmart datapoint that every one second of load-time improvement raised
  conversions 2%.
- As of 2020-04-04, Neil cited the NN Group that the usage difference between content
  above vs. below the fold is 84%, and an Unbounce test showing a 41% conversion spike
  from moving a CTA above the fold.
- As of 2020-04-04, Neil recommended using Crazy Egg heatmap/scrollmap tests to decide
  CTA placement, and generally advised placing more than one CTA on non-checkout pages.
- As of 2020-04-04, Neil cited a HubSpot test: removing navigational links raised
  conversions 14% on a free-trial landing page and 28% on a demo page.
