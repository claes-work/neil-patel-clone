# Taking Your Business From Zero to $10 Million

## Summary
In this interview, Neil Patel (guest, responding to an unnamed host) breaks down how the founder/CEO role and the required skills change across revenue stages. He declines to speak to the $100M-to-$1B jump, saying he has never done it, and focuses on zero-to-$10M and $10M-to-$100M. He argues the main reason companies stall between zero and $10M is not weak processes or systems but choosing too small a niche; he advocates targeting a large total addressable market (TAM), using cars versus a novelty "croissant toast" product to illustrate how market size, not scrappiness, gates early growth. Scaling from $10M to $100M, he says, is about systems, processes and people — specifically hiring operators poached from competitors who have earned multiple promotions, plus traits like loyalty, industry relationships, the ability to bring a following, strong player-coach management, and a "work is life" ethic. He stresses culture starts at the top and leaders must model the hours. On personal routine, he claims to "barely work" yet effectively runs roughly 6am-to-9pm days nearly seven days a week while traveling weekly, framing it as play because he loves it. His stated 10-year goal is to build the largest digital ad agency in the world. He also details a lead-generation playbook: give away free software adjacent to your core service (Ubersuggest, AnswerThePublic for NP Digital) to capture leads that sales teams convert into higher-value services, noting services markets dwarf software markets. On build-versus-buy, he prefers acquiring an existing audience cheaply, otherwise building — increasingly with tools like ChatGPT plus developers to refine.

## Key claims
- The main reason most businesses struggle to grow from zero to $10M is not processes or systems but that they are stuck in too small a niche (as of 2023-07-03).
- Neil evaluates opportunities by TAM (total addressable market); the bigger the market, the easier it is to reach $5M-$10M because customers already spend heavily there (as of 2023-07-03).
- Selling high-ticket, high-demand products (e.g. cars at ~$50,000 each) makes reaching $10M in revenue relatively fast, whereas a novelty niche product with high production cost and low repeat demand does not (as of 2023-07-03).
- If you build a business in a big enough market, reaching $10M is "not that hard" (as of 2023-07-03).
- Scaling from $10M to $100M is largely about systems, processes and people (as of 2023-07-03).
- A key hiring tactic is to use LinkedIn to find people at competitors who have earned multiple promotions, since promotions signal the competitor valued them and they did a good job in your space (as of 2023-07-03).
- Beyond promotion history, Neil looks for loyalty (people who stay because real problems take years to fix), people who do NOT want to be entrepreneurs (entrepreneurs are hard to retain long-term), and people with long tenure in a vertical who hold relationships with banks, potential customers and key employees (as of 2023-07-03).
- He values hires who bring people with them when they switch companies, treating it as a signal of strong leadership (as of 2023-07-03).
- He looks for strong managers who understand systems and processes, and specifically "player coaches" who both develop others and get hands-on — NP Digital states this expectation in interviews and rejects candidates unwilling to do both (as of 2023-07-03).
- Neil favors people who believe "work is life" and will put in more than 50-60 hours a week, arguing a strict nine-to-five mindset will lose to competitors (as of 2023-07-03).
- He still endorses some work-life balance — not 70 hours every week without holidays or family time — with variable weeks (some 40-50 hours, some 80) and prizes flexibility (as of 2023-07-03).
- Culture around work ethic starts at the top; if owners and leaders will not put in the hours, they cannot expect employees to — lead by example (as of 2023-07-03).
- Neil claims he tells his team he barely works and that they only need to work a few hours a day, but in reality starts around 6am and finishes around 9pm nearly seven days a week while traveling weekly for work (as of 2023-07-03).
- He does not count activities like a dinner meeting to close a deal as "work" because he enjoys them; framing work as fun sustains high output (as of 2023-07-03).
- Money's main benefit for him is flexibility — e.g. attending his daughter's school events when in town — while he otherwise does "whatever it takes to win" (as of 2023-07-03).
- His stated company values include transparency, continual self-improvement and learning, and challenging themselves with goals that are not always obtainable (as of 2023-07-03).
- Neil frames business as "the greatest sport ever," motivated by an ever-harder scoreboard and competition (as of 2023-07-03).
- His 10-year goal (which he does not expect to hit in three years) is to become the largest ad agency in the world on the digital side, deliberately avoiding traditional/TV advertising as spend shifts to digital (as of 2023-07-03).
- Free-software lead-gen strategy: give away software adjacent to your core offering to attract leads/customers for the main thing you sell — e.g. a health-insurance seller giving free payroll software to companies, then selling a portion of them insurance (as of 2023-07-03).
- NP Digital sells marketing services and gives away free marketing tools like Ubersuggest and AnswerThePublic to capture leads, which sales teams then convert into services (as of 2023-07-03).
- Services markets are far larger than software markets: Neil estimates HubSpot at roughly $1.2-$1.4B (over $1B, under $1.5B) annual revenue, while large ad-agency holding companies like Omnicom and WPP do $10B+ (some over $15B) in revenue (as of 2023-07-03).
- Free software costs relatively little to run but yields many users and leads, a portion of whom buy the higher-margin services where customers spend more (as of 2023-07-03).
- On build-versus-buy: if you can buy someone who already has an audience but isn't monetizing well, that is the easier path; otherwise build it (as of 2023-07-03).
- Neil recommends starting tool-building with ChatGPT, then plugging in developers to fix and fine-tune, citing that ChatGPT reportedly built a working version of Pong in under 60 seconds (as of 2023-07-03).
