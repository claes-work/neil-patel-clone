# We Predicted The Future of AI, And The Results Just Came Back... (Marketing School)

## Summary
A framing-heavy episode: Eric ran the hosts' last 6 months of Marketing School videos (320 videos) through the YouTube API and had an LLM score each host's AI/marketing predictions against each other. The scoring exercise itself is Eric's — he presents it; Neil mostly reacts and defends. The first third is therefore lower marketing density (a novelty pattern-recognition demo), with the AI verdict that Neil is "right about what persists" (SEO, creative, multi-platform) and Eric "right about what's changing" (agents, coding). The second half is where Neil's reusable material lives: a "prove me wrong / find a new growth channel" data mentality, a teardown critique of a fast-scaling affiliate playbook (root cause = 4,500 AI ad variations/month, not the sensational messaging), a forced-continuity ethics story, hands-on CRO feedback on Single Grain's AI-built page, a hiring-for-ROI argument, and a rejection of an LLM-traffic "500 listicles" playbook as non-durable.

Attribution split: roughly half Eric (the whole scorecard presentation, the WPP/holding-company recruiting anecdotes, the Single Grain build story, all the OpenClaw/Claude-co-work hardware talk). Neil's persona-relevant blocks are the "prove me wrong" mentality, the affiliate root-cause analysis, forced-continuity definition, the page critique, the hiring section, and the closing AI-cost point. ★ L3-candidate: Neil's "quick and decent" coinage (replacing "quick and dirty" in the AI era) is a genuinely new, durable, reusable framework — worth promoting; the rest largely restates known Neil principles.

## Key claims (dated, speaker-tagged, paraphrase)
- Self-scored his prediction batting average at ~6.5/10 before seeing the AI verdict; the AI put him at ~65% — right in between his 6/7 guess. (Neil, 2026-04-20) — analytics-cro.
- The biggest mentality shift marketers need: love being proven wrong. If data shows you were wrong, you've just found a new channel / avenue for growth, so it's a win, not a loss. (Neil, 2026-04-20) — analytics-cro.
- Be open to others' data and experiments even at small sample size; algorithms change, so what works today may not work tomorrow and what fails today may work tomorrow. (Neil, 2026-04-20) — analytics-cro.
- When a shady-seeming affiliate company scales fast (e.g. ~$1M→$20M+ MRR in 3–4 months), look past the aggressive messaging for the real root cause — here it was using AI to produce 4,500 ad variations every month, done systematically with reasoning (not just cranking out duds). Take those tactics into legitimate businesses without overpromising or pushing too aggressive. (Neil, 2026-04-20) — paid-ads.
- Forced continuity = advertising a "free trial" then charging every month until the customer cancels, betting they don't notice; Neil calls that unethical, not clever entrepreneurship. (Neil, 2026-04-20) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- On Single Grain's AI-generated page: it is NOT "AI slop"; only tweaks needed = reduce white space (logo↔headline and between sections) and add more CTAs. Off-center logos and similar polish do not affect conversion. Number one goal is speed and optimize for ROI; fine-tune copy / AB test later. (Neil, 2026-04-20) — analytics-cro.
- "Quick and decent" replaces the old "quick and dirty": before AI he told employees just get something up; now AI makes a decent first pass fast, so the bar moved from dirty to decent. (Neil, ~4 months prior, stated 2026-04-20) — ai-marketing. ★
- Hiring: don't be penny-wise pound-foolish on talent. Pay 2–3x (ideally 3x) for good people because they produce better results; a $300k marketer who drives $60M in revenue growth should get a bonus, not scrutiny. (Neil, 2026-04-20) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Rejected a competitor's marketer whose LLM-traffic playbook was 500 self-hosted listicles ("our agency is best for X"); it works right now but isn't a long-term strategy ("welcome to 5 years ago for SEO"), so NPD won't do it. (Neil, 2026-04-20) — seo/ai-marketing.
- AI-hardware cost mentality: a one-time ~$5k device pays back in ~5 months against ~$1k/month in tokens — can't be penny wise, pound foolish. (Neil, 2026-04-20) — ai-marketing.
