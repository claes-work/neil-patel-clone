# The Money in Marketing AI is in the Ugly, Not the Sexy (Marketing School)

## Summary
★ Landmark-worthy for later L3 promotion: Neil's "the money in marketing AI is in the ugly, not the sexy" heuristic — the profit is in the unglamorous operational uses (AI analyzing data to find ad-spend wastage and scale-up opportunities, and AI running repeatable systems/processes), not in AI content/image/video generation that drives little traffic or conversion.

A co-hosted Marketing School episode recorded live at HubSpot Inbound 2024 in Boston. Neil argues the real money in marketing AI is in the "ugly": using AI to analyze spend/data across sources to find wastage or where to scale for better ROI, and using AI for systems and processes to save staff time — not the "sexy" AI content generation everyone chases at conferences. Eric runs a parallel thread on the agentic workflow they demoed on stage (AI agents doing boring jobs-to-be-done like consolidating Google Search Console data) and on "shiny object syndrome," using Google NotebookLM as the shiny-object example. Both extend the theme to boring "ugly" channels beating TikTok. Neil shares his acquisition data: his top new-customer channels are Google → YouTube → LinkedIn, but NP Digital's actual majority comes from RFPs/word-of-mouth, client referrals, and employee referrals at 1,000+ headcount ("companies follow good people"). Neil then goes deep on email as a top-ROI channel, the AMP-email tactic (via a tool he names "Zaymo") letting people buy inside Gmail, and an email-list-building play (buy engaged subscribers via Meta/Instagram, monetize with native ads/promotions). Eric closes with a Reddit-traffic stat (self-corrected on air), a Greg Eisenberg post, a community/in-person-events pitch, and a YPO "key associate" access loophole. Two sponsor ad reads (Agency Owners Association; offshore marketing hiring) are Eric's.

## Key claims
- In marketing AI, the money is in the "ugly," not the "sexy": using AI to create content/videos/images drives little traffic and few conversions, whereas the unglamorous uses are what pay off. (Neil, 2024-09-30)
- Using AI to analyze your data from many different sources — flagging where money is being wasted or where you should scale up faster because ROI is better — produces real savings and real profitability, but very few people actually do it. (Neil, 2024-09-30)
- Most teams say they look at their analytics/paid-ad data daily, but they really don't. (Neil, 2024-09-30)
- Companies chase the latest "sexy" AI feature demoed at conferences, but when you ask whether it will really generate meaningful traffic or revenue, the answer is usually "not really"; using AI for systems and processes is boring but saves time and expensive staff cost — that is the actually valuable ("sexy") use. (Neil, 2024-09-30)
- Neil's top channels for acquiring new customers: Google (search), then YouTube, then LinkedIn. (Neil, 2024-09-30) — he said "top two" but listed three; treated as top three.
- The majority of NP Digital's actual customers come from (1) RFPs / word-of-mouth from being known in the industry, (2) client referrals, and (3) employee referrals; once you're above ~1,000 headcount, people who worked with your staff seek them out — "companies follow good people." (Neil, 2024-09-30)
- Good people alone aren't enough — they need the right systems and processes so they can be efficient and not hit roadblocks. (Neil, 2024-09-30)
- Email is one of the highest-ROI channels. (Neil, 2024-09-30)
- The biggest email mistake isn't segmentation — it's sending the same static emails; with AMP email technology (Neil names the tool "Zaymo") recipients can select a product/variant/size and even click buy and purchase inside Gmail without ever visiting your website, which raises conversions. (Neil, 2024-09-30)
- Email-list growth play: if focusing on email, build a highly engaged list by buying subscribers via paid social (Meta/Instagram), drive them to that engaged list, then monetize with native ads and promotions for your own products; most people won't go the extra mile to build a genuinely good list. (Neil, 2024-09-30)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> The money's in the ugly, specifically when it comes to marketing.

> People always talk about AI, "Oh, I'm going to go use it to create content. I'm going to go use it to create this video or image." But look at the traffic that you're getting from that kind of stuff. Look at the conversions. It's not that much.

> when you use AI to analyze your data from all these different sources and telling you where there's wastage in money spent or where you should scale up faster cuz you're getting a better ROI, that's real savings or real profitability, but yet very few people want to actually do that kind of stuff.

> using AI for systems and processes, oh, that's boring. Well, that saves you time. You're paying staff a lot of money… In my eyes and I know in your eyes as well, that's the sexy stuff. But most people don't view it as sexy.

> For my top two channels for new customers is starts with Google, then it goes to YouTube, and then it goes to LinkedIn.

> number one is RFPs, just word of mouth of people knowing that we're in the industry. Uh number two is client referrals. Number three is employee referrals. Once you have enough head count, like we're above a thousand… companies follow good people.

> It's one of the highest ROI channels.

> The biggest thing that they're doing wrong with email — they're using it in the same way. I'm not talking about segmentation. I'm talking about the emails they're sending. And a lot of people don't realize with AMP technology, you can end up making it where your emails are powerful where someone can select a product… they can even click buy and purchase and they can do all this within Gmail without even going to your website. When you do things like that, conversions go up.

## Eric Siu (co-host, context — NOT persona data)
[[wiki/entities/eric-siu]]
- Recounted the agentic AI workflow they demoed in their Marketing School session at Inbound: AI agents automating jobs-to-be-done (e.g., log into Google Search Console, consolidate, find similar vectors); it looks boring and reads like "programmer speak," but a workflow that repeats over time makes a lot of money. (Eric, 2024-09-30)
- The biggest companies win by doing the same things over and over rather than trying to be perfect at everything; marketing suffers from "shiny object syndrome" — dial it back and keep doubling/tripling down on what actually works. (Eric, 2024-09-30)
- Google NotebookLM (turns a book into a podcast; "came out last week") is a shiny-object example — cool, but unclear it drives revenue; better to focus resources on what's already working, since the same marketing concepts can run for 10/20/30 years. (Eric, 2024-09-30)
- Boring "ugly" channels produce results; for most industries, spending more time there beats focusing on TikTok. (Eric, 2024-09-30)
- For his side ("us," i.e. Single Grain), acquisition is word-of-mouth first, then events/webinars and SEO — "the boring stuff"; from a joint Entrepreneur Magazine interview at the event, "the old stuff that works well" is emails and webinars, but above all it's whether you do good work. (Eric, 2024-09-30)
- Over-promoting an email list burns it out (cited an unnamed famous, highly-paid affiliate who only ever promoted). (Eric, 2024-09-30)
- Reddit traffic stat (self-corrected on air): he'd said on stage Reddit added ~1 billion monthly organic visitors; the real figure was ~700 million (from a low of ~1.6B to past ~2.3B monthly desktop+mobile visits) after Google's algorithm changes and Google's data deal with Reddit. Cited a Greg Eisenberg post — niche internet communities are in high demand, the value is the ideas/trends more than the traffic, and you can use AI to scrape Reddit pain points for startup ideas. (Eric, 2024-09-30)
- Community and in-person events are a big part of the future of marketing — "nothing beats in person"; at Inbound (~12–14,000 attendees) he made an intro/connection for Neil, with a possible M&A deal down the road. (Eric, 2024-09-30)
- YPO Global Marketing Summit access "loophole": non-YPO members can attend as a "key associate" if invited/signed up by a YPO member; Eric structured the event to allow key associates. (Eric, 2024-09-30)
- Read both sponsor ads: the Agency Owners Association peer group (marketingschool.io/agency) and an offshore marketing recruiting partner (marketingschool.io/hire). (Eric, 2024-09-30 — sponsor content, non-persona)
