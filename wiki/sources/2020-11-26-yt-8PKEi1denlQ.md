# The Easy Way to Rank Local Websites - Module 2 - Lesson 2 - Local SEO Unlocked

## Summary
Local SEO Unlocked, module 2 lesson 2, covering off-page local SEO. Opens with a
proximity/relevance/prominence ranking-factor triad, illustrated by a New York-vs-New
Jersey local-adaptation exercise (do keyword research and content localization
per-county/region, not as one national blob). Recommends dense location-based landing
pages (his example: Tiffany & Co.'s per-retailer local pages), local-news-tied content
(with a caveat against content-for-content's-sake), local-directory submissions,
readable keyword-rich URLs, internal linking (with natural, not keyword-stuffed, anchor
text), external-link outreach via Ubersuggest's backlinks report, HTTPS/site-speed,
schema markup (via Google's Structured Data Markup Helper), XML sitemaps through
Search Console, and voice-search optimization for "micro moments."

## Key claims
- As of 2020-11-26, his local-ranking-factor model is **proximity, relevance,
  prominence** — how close the business is to the searcher, how well the query matches
  the product/service, and what other customers say (paraphrase, standing framework).
- As of 2020-11-26, recommends dense per-location landing pages over a single generic
  locations list, citing Tiffany & Co. as a positive example of local-page depth
  driving local rankings and business (paraphrase, third-party case example, not
  independently verified here).
- Cites a voice-search purchase-volume figure attributed to "OCNC," self-described as
  imprecise in the moment ("I know it's well into the billions... either 20 something
  billion or 40 something billion") — flagged here as an explicitly uncertain,
  self-corrected statistic, not a reliable figure.
- Recommends natural (not always keyword-exact) internal-link anchor text, and frames
  external links as "votes" analogous to an election, consistent with the corpus's
  existing link-signals framing.
