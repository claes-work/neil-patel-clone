# A Unique Type of Content That Will Generate You 100,000 Visitors Per Month

## Summary
Neil breaks down the traffic engine behind neilpatel.com's free "training" section
(video courses like the ones later documented in the corpus as "Unlocked" courses):
he shows live analytics of ~65K–150K weekly page views to the training section
(~2–300K/month), attributes the bulk of it to referral/social sharing rather than
Google, and explains the production model — pay contributors on Upwork to build
PowerPoint decks, record 10–15 minutes of narration per module, and give away
courses that competitors sell. The differentiator he stresses isn't the video itself
but the downloadable assets (content calendars, worksheets, checklists) bundled with
each module, which he says drive thousands of downloads a day and are what actually
sustains traffic over time.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-11-30, reports the neilpatel.com training/course section generating
  roughly 2–300,000 page views/month, with individual weeks ranging ~65,000–150,000
  views (paraphrase, self-reported analytics, not independently verified).
- States most of that traffic comes from people sharing and linking to the training
  material (referral/social), not primarily from Google search (paraphrase).
- Reports averaging ~11,000–12,000 downloads/day across all training-material assets
  (worksheets, checklists, content calendars) (self-reported).
- Core tactic (paraphrase): give away free, high-production-value courses instead of
  charging for them (unlike most course creators); host them on your own site, not
  Udemy or similar platforms, to keep the traffic and leads.
- States each module takes him only "10, 15 minutes" of recording time because a team
  builds the PowerPoint deck first and he just narrates and gives feedback — a
  lower-effort production model than long-form blog writing, by his account.
- Claims the worksheets/assets — not the video lessons alone — are the actual traffic
  driver: "Releasing the videos is one thing, but once you release the worksheets,
  the cheat sheets, the handouts... that's when you'll start seeing that these
  training materials will start getting a lot of traffic over time" (paraphrase +
  quote below).
- Notes many of his older courses get MORE traffic today than when first published,
  attributing this to compounding referral/social distribution rather than SEO decay.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> If you want to do well, and you want more traffic and you want a leg up on your
> competition, consider creating courses. Don't upload them to Udemy or any of these
> sites, host them on your own website and give them out for free.

> Releasing the videos is one thing, but once you release the worksheets, the cheat
> sheets, the handouts that go along with each module, that's when you'll start
> seeing that these training materials will start getting a lot of traffic over time.
