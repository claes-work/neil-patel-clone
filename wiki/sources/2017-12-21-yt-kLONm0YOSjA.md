# How to Become a Social Media Influencer | Make Money Online and Become Internet Famous!

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out how to build a personal brand / social following, framing his own reach (2017: ~900k Facebook fans, a few hundred thousand Twitter followers, ~1M monthly blog readers) as the engine behind "millions of dollars." Four steps: (1) **be consistent for years** — not one post a week but years of output; he cites himself, Gary Vaynerchuk (who started with Wine Library and Conan appearances, not VaynerMedia), Seth Godin, Guy Kawasaki, and Tim Ferriss as brands built over ~10 years, none overnight; (2) **leverage multiple media types** (blog, podcast, video) because different people consume differently — this is where he narrates the **origin of the Marketing School podcast**: "my buddy Eric" [Siu] suggested they produce a podcast, and it now gets "over a half a million listens each and every single month"; (3) **engage your community** — he personally responds to comments, giving detailed replies to detailed questions because a short reply "shows I don't care"; (4) **meet people in person** — go to conferences, network, and speak (early on you won't be paid or accepted, but "it's a numbers game ... just like dating," keep applying). His timeline reality-check: a credible brand takes **5–10 years**, not six months. Reinforces Neil's personal-brand, multi-format, and community-engagement themes; the Marketing School origin story is a notable biographical datapoint.

## Key claims
As of 2017-12-21, Neil states:
- Self-reported reach: "over 900,000 Facebook fans," "a few hundred thousand Twitter followers," and "over a million people reading my blog every single month," which lets him "generate millions of dollars because of my personal brand" (as of 2017-12-21).
- **Consistency over years** is step one — not one article/video a week, but sustained output "for years and years"; he, Gary Vaynerchuk, Seth Godin, Guy Kawasaki, and Tim Ferriss all built brands over ~10 years, "it didn't happen overnight" (as of 2017-12-21).
- Gary Vaynerchuk's brand "started with Wine Library" and stunts like putting socks in Conan O'Brien's mouth on air — not with VaynerMedia (attributed to Gary, context only) (as of 2017-12-21).
- **Leverage multiple media types** (video, blog, podcast) because audiences differ — some read, some listen while driving, some watch (as of 2017-12-21).
- **Marketing School podcast origin**: "my buddy Eric [Siu] was like Neil we should produce a podcast and we started creating one called Marketing School and it gets over a half a million listens each and every single month" (as of 2017-12-21).
- **Engage the community**: he personally responds to comments; a short reply to a detailed question "shows I don't care," so he gives detailed responses — reciprocity builds a following (as of 2017-12-21).
- **Meet people in person**: attend conferences, network, and speak; early on you won't be paid or may be rejected, but "it's a numbers game ... just like dating," so keep applying and you'll improve (as of 2017-12-21).
- **Timeline**: a credible, respectable brand "takes five to ten years" — not overnight, not six months, though you'll see some results within a year (as of 2017-12-21).
