# Is SEO Still A Relevant Career in 2026? (Marketing School)

## Summary
★ Landmark-worthy for later L3 promotion: Neil's core career thesis — **SEO is more relevant than ever, but only for those who adapt.** He frames SEO as a discipline that has *always* evolved (yellow-pages name-stuffing → keyword spam on Excite/Lycos → content-for-humans + schema/de-duplication → today: content for humans "packaged for AI," where brand mentions matter as much as links). His two operating principles: *curiosity* and *adaptability*, with "no sacred cows." The people who complain on X that Google is being "unfair" are simply the ones who didn't adapt — "everyone wants to win, nobody wants to prepare."

A short co-hosted Marketing School episode answering "Is SEO still a relevant career in this new world?" Neil (the dominant voice) opens by calling it "a very booming career… as long as you adapt," then recounts a talk he gave at an event in Denver where an audience member asked whether AISO / GEO / SEO-for-ChatGPT is really the same thing as SEO. Neil's answer has two parts: (1) it *is* the same practice evolving — you can keep calling it SEO — because SEO has continuously reinvented itself over 15–20 years; (2) commercially it doesn't matter what it's called: if a client calls it AEO / AI SEO / GEO, adapt your vernacular to win the business (without deceiving them), because "you got to adapt with the customers." He draws an analogy to the "growth hacker" job-title era in corporate America (now just "growth marketer"), with Eric adding that people who didn't adopt the new title were less likely to get startup jobs — adapt or be displaced. Neil closes on the historical throughline (LLMs are now multimodal and looking at many signals) and the mindset conclusion: stay curious, stay adaptable, prepare, and you'll win. Neil + Eric only; no guest.

## Key claims
- SEO is a booming, more-relevant-than-ever career in the AI era — *as long as you adapt*. (Neil, 2025-10-18)
- Neil spoke at an event in Denver (a few days prior, on a Wednesday) about AISO / GEO / SEO — i.e., doing SEO for ChatGPT. (Neil, 2025-10-18)
- SEO has always evolved. ~15–20 years ago you could rank by stuffing junk keywords on a page, writing mediocre copy, and building links from high-PageRank sites. (Neil, 2025-10-18)
- It then evolved: you had to write good content for humans, keep content updated, stop spamming, avoid duplicate content (merge and prune pages on the same topic), and schema markup started to matter. (Neil, 2025-10-18)
- It's now evolving again: you create content for humans but *package it for AI*; links still matter, but brand mentions matter just as much — and you don't always need people linking to your site (in classic SEO, brand mentions didn't help you nearly as much). (Neil, 2025-10-18)
- Whatever you call it — SEO, AEO, AI SEO, GEO, AISO — it's the same discipline evolving over time, and you can still legitimately call it SEO. (Neil, 2025-10-18)
- Commercially, the label doesn't matter: if your customers call it something else and you refuse to adapt your vernacular, you probably won't get the business — so Neil will adopt whatever term the client uses to pitch them and generate revenue. This is done ethically: he won't deceive a client into thinking it's some brand-new thing, and may even tell them it's really just SEO adapting. (Neil, 2025-10-18)
- Analogy to job titles: when corporate America decided "we need a growth hacker," it was really just normal marketing / CMO work; nobody says "growth hacker" now — they say "growth marketer." Back then, people who didn't adapt to the new title were less likely to get a startup job. Adapt or be displaced. (Neil + Eric, 2025-10-18)
- Historical SEO throughline: yellow-pages "SEO" was gamed by adding A's to your business name ("AAA") to appear first; then keyword spamming on engines like Excite/Lycos, sometimes hiding keywords in background text; now it's far more sophisticated because LLMs are multimodal and looking at many signals. (Neil, 2025-10-18)
- There are "no sacred cows" in building a successful career; the two most important traits are curiosity and adaptability. (Neil, 2025-10-18)
- The SEOs who complain constantly (e.g., on X — "we should go after Google," "they're being unfair to us") are the ones who haven't adapted, no different from people who chronically complain about not getting what they want. "Everyone wants to win, nobody wants to prepare"; adapt + prepare + stay curious and you will win. (Neil, 2025-10-18)
