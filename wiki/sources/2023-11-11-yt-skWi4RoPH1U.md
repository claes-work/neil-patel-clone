# Every CEO is Desperate to Hire This Powerhouse Role (Ep. 2600)

## Summary
Short daily Marketing School episode built almost entirely around Eric Siu's case for hiring a **chief of staff** — a role Eric has recently hired for and is enthusiastically pitching to Neil. Eric supplies nearly all the substance (the "smoke jumper" framing, Matt Mochary's *The Great CEO Within*, and his three reasons to hire one). Neil plays the deliberate skeptic: he declines to hire one, argues he wants people who are *better* than him at what he's weak at rather than a "mini me," suspects his company may be too small, and closes with the episode's sharpest distinction — that a chief of staff is far more valuable for someone in a **day-to-day CEO/managing role (like Eric) than for someone like Neil who isn't**. Persona-relevant Neil content is therefore thin but pointed: hiring-to-fill-gaps and role-fit-depends-on-your-actual-job. (2023-11-11)

## Key claims
- Neil declines to hire a chief of staff for now, but leaves the door open: if Eric reports amazing results in ~three months, he says he might be "a little bit more open to it." (Neil, 2023-11-11)
- Neil's hiring philosophy: he does not want a "mini me" — he wants people who are *better than him at the stuff he sucks at*, hiring to fill in his gaps. (Neil, 2023-11-11)
- Neil speculates his own company may simply be too small to need a chief of staff, and that the need might appear at "double triple quadruple size." (Neil, 2023-11-11)
- Neil's key distinction (episode's closing point): a chief of staff is likely *much more valuable* to someone in a day-to-day managing role such as a CEO — Eric is a CEO of a company; Neil says he is not in that day-to-day managing role, so the role is less valuable to him. (Neil, 2023-11-11)
- Neil used to think a chief of staff / this kind of hire was overrated, needling Eric with his standing line "if you're so good as a coach, why are you a coach?" — noting some people just like it. (Neil, 2023-11-11) [attribution note: line 35-38 is Eric quoting/characterizing Neil's habitual view]
- Eric's core pitch (context): a chief of staff is a "smoke jumper" you can drop into any special situation; hire someone with a structured management-consulting background, or a high-upside junior you can eventually promote to run a department. (Eric, 2023-11-11)
- Eric's three reasons to hire one (context): (1) an extension/"mirror version" of yourself with high upside and new ideas; (2) a repeatable role that becomes a **business ops team / squad of chiefs of staff** tackling special projects without direction; (3) leverage — they attend nearly every meeting, see every decision, and learn your pattern recognition, doing things "their own spin but kind of my way." (Eric, 2023-11-11)
