# How to Start a Career in Digital Marketing

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out a hands-on, do-it-yourself path to starting a career in digital marketing, arguing that the best way to learn is to build and grow your own website. He walks through a sequence of steps: launch a WordPress (or Shopify) site in an industry you are passionate about, research competitors with Ubersuggest, produce content that beats the top-ranking pages, and promote it through link and social outreach. He then covers building a brand through email collection and push notifications, and stresses learning to sell as the way to master the full marketing suite. He frames the opportunity around the scale of digital ad spend and closes with a bonus tip on A/B testing conversion rates.

## Key claims
- The best way to learn marketing is to start your own website; building and growing a real site is the core learning method (as of 2019-04-22).
- The recommended first step is to create a WordPress blog (or a Shopify site for e-commerce) in an industry you are passionate about (as of 2019-04-22).
- Ubersuggest can reveal a competitor's traffic by region, their top pages, and the keywords driving their traffic, plus who links to them (as of 2019-04-22).
- The easiest and most valuable keywords to target first are those with high volume, high cost-per-click, and low search difficulty (as of 2019-04-22).
- To win rankings, don't just replicate a top competitor page — write a better version with better images, videos, and custom graphics that make your content stand out (as of 2019-04-22).
- Outreach at scale works: emailing hundreds of people who linked to competitors will win some links, boosting search traffic and generating referral traffic (as of 2019-04-22).
- Referral traffic is significant/valuable (as of 2019-04-22).
- Twitter search can be used to find everyone who shared a competitor's URL, so you can ask them to share your comparable content (as of 2019-04-22).
- You cannot build a brand without collecting emails; free tools like Hello Bar (with sliders and pop-ups) help capture email addresses (as of 2019-04-22).
- Getting people back to your site around seven times makes you much more likely to build a brand (as of 2019-04-22).
- The free tool Subscribers uses push notifications to let visitors subscribe in one click and be brought back through browser notifications; results are strong especially when combined with email (as of 2019-04-22).
- Learning to sell something — physical product, service, lead generation, or affiliate marketing — teaches the full suite of marketing, because marketing is not just driving traffic but converting visitors into customers (as of 2019-04-22).
- Bonus tip: run A/B tests (e.g., with Crazy Egg) to maximize conversion rate (as of 2019-04-22).
- Neil attributes the paraphrased sentiment "brands are the solution" to a Google executive, framed as being about which sites rank at the top of Google and combating fake news (as of 2019-04-22). [Neil's attribution to a Google exec; wording paraphrased]
