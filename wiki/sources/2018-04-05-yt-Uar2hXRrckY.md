# 6 BEST Digital Marketing Tools to Grow Your Business Online

## Summary

In this Q&A Thursday episode, Neil Patel (joined by co-host Adam from Viewership) answers a recurring viewer question about which marketing tools to use. His central framing is that tools are worthless without action: too many marketers download the latest "shiny" tool, tinker for an hour, and mistake that for productive work. He walks through six foundational tools he actually uses — Google Analytics, Ubersuggest, Google Search Console, Crazy Egg, Subscribers (push notifications), and HubSpot — repeatedly stressing that the point is not more traffic but relevant traffic that converts. A large middle section digresses into a content strategy he ties to Search Console: rather than endlessly writing new posts, go back and rewrite/expand underperforming existing content, and consolidate overlapping posts into one page per topic to avoid keyword cannibalization.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-04-05, Neil argues that tools are useless unless you take action on what they tell you; downloading the latest tool and tinkering for an hour is a waste of time, not real work.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil names six tools he recommends: (1) Google Analytics, (2) Ubersuggest, (3) Google Search Console, (4) Crazy Egg, (5) Subscribers (push notifications), and (6) HubSpot.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil says Google Analytics shows how visitors engage across a site and which traffic sources drive conversions — but only if you set up goal and conversion tracking; without that, there is no point using it. He notes Analytics will sample data at high traffic volumes unless you pay for a premium tier he pegs at roughly "$100 or $150 grand" per year [transcript garble: exact price/tier not specified].
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil advises optimizing for traffic that causes conversions, not traffic volume — citing an internal example where his team debated updating a post about a South by Southwest conference and he said to delete it because that traffic would not convert into customers.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil mentions he bought out KISSmetrics (which he co-founded) from its investors and planned to merge it into NeilPatel.com.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil recommends Ubersuggest for keyword research to grow search rankings, calling organic search the most consistent traffic source; he contrasts it with Facebook organic reach, which he says can swing from 300,000 visitors one day to 10,000 the next, whereas Google organic traffic is very consistent.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil describes his Google Search Console workflow: find posts with under a 5% click-through rate, pull the keywords those posts get impressions for, rewrite the content to encompass all relevant keywords (not just add them), and make it 2–5x more in-depth.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil claims that rewriting/expanding a Neil Patel blog post about growing Instagram followers took it from ~12,000 views a month to a bit over 30,000 views a month.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil says to cross-check Search Console CTR data against Google Analytics conversion data so you only invest in expanding blog posts that actually drive sales.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil warns that continually publishing new content (e.g. a new article every day for a year) causes topic overlap and keyword cannibalization, confusing Google about which of your 50 SEO or link-building posts should rank; consolidating to one page per topic and making it longer yields more traffic.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil cites Wikipedia and YouTube (the latter owned by Google) as examples of sites that rank for nearly everything by keeping one page per topic and lengthening articles, with no cannibalization.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil recommends "content clustering": a main pillar article (e.g. "What is SEO"/"SEO overview") linked to sub-articles covering specific subtopics (link building, on-page SEO, audits, competitive analysis) — but not multiple near-duplicate intro articles (beginner's guide vs. what is SEO vs. newbie's guide).
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil says an internal exercise where his team updated just four old blog posts grew one from ~1,000 uniques to ~5,000 uniques the next month.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil recommends Crazy Egg to see how visitors engage (scrolling, clicking) and to adjust pages and run A/B tests without needing a developer or designer, to maximize conversions.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil recommends Subscribers, a browser push-notification tool, to bring one-time visitors back via unobtrusive in-browser subscription prompts; he notes it works with or without HTTPS but urges switching to HTTPS (the SSL certificate).
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil predicts push notifications will not replace email marketing; email opens/engagement are declining, but the future is email combined with push and a few other channels.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil recommends HubSpot as a tool that integrates marketing with sales and tracks overall marketing and lead count, praising its many free and entrepreneur-friendly startup plans, which he attributes to it being a "billion dollar company" releasing features for free to crush competitors.
- As of 2018-04-05, Neil closes by reiterating that data and tools without action are useless — you would be better off saving the money and not using them at all.
