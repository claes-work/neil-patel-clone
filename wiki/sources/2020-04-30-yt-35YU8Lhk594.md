# Using Your Own Brand As Your SEO Secret Weapon

## Summary
Argues that building a personal/company brand is itself an SEO lever, using Kylie
Jenner as the headline example: she built an enormous social following before her
cosmetics line existed, then converted that audience into a website that ranks for
competitive beauty keywords (1.8M monthly visitors per SimilarWeb) without a
traditional link-building campaign. Neil pairs this with his own origin story — told
in more compressed form than in earlier bootstrap sources — as the second case study:
teenage jobs (theme-park trash/restroom cleaning) funded a failed job-board startup
(Advice Monkey) at 15, which forced him to self-teach digital marketing; a 2006 blog
became his real career catapult; Ubersuggest (framed here purely as a brand-building
free tool) now drives ~15M page views/month, worth "hundreds of thousands of dollars"
per month if bought as paid traffic. Introduces the "rule of seven" as the mechanism:
repeated exposure to a person/brand makes audiences more likely to search their name
directly, and those branded searches make it easier to outrank higher-domain-authority
competitors on other terms.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-04-30, Neil said Kylie Jenner's website gets over 1.8 million visitors per
  month (citing SimilarWeb) and now ranks for competitive commercial keywords like
  "eyeshadow palettes," attributing this to brand equity built before the SEO existed.
- As of 2020-04-30, Neil restated his origin story in brief: at 15 he started Advice
  Monkey (a job-search website, built because he "couldn't find a job"), funded by
  theme-park trash-picking/restroom-cleaning wages; it failed because "people just
  ripped me off," forcing him to self-teach digital marketing. Consistent with the
  fuller account in [[wiki/sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]].
- As of 2020-04-30, Neil said his 2006 blog was "the real catapult" for his career,
  generating conference-speaking invitations (he applied when not invited, "most would
  turn me down, but eventually more and more accepted me").
- As of 2020-04-30, Neil said Ubersuggest generates 15 million page views/month and
  roughly 3 million visits/month, framing the tool itself (not just its SEO output) as
  a brand-building asset that would cost "hundreds and thousands of dollars per month"
  to replicate via paid traffic.
- As of 2020-04-30, Neil names his "rule of seven": after seven exposures to a
  person/brand, audiences are much more likely to "relate to your brand," which
  increases branded search queries and makes it easier to rank for competitive terms
  against higher-domain-authority sites. This is the same "rule of seven" cited
  elsewhere in his tactical content (e.g. content-marketing frequency advice).

## Notable verbatim quotes
