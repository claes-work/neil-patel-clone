# Is Digital Marketing Going to Be Saturated in 2023?

## Summary
Title says "2023" but the spoken content is framed as "2022" predictions (title/date
mismatch, content itself dated 2021-12-20 per upload). Opens with ad-spend stats (global
digital ad spend ~$380B in 2021, ~70% YoY growth per eMarketer; US social ad spend ~$43B
in 2020, +20% YoY) to argue digital marketing will be "the most saturated environment
ever," then gives four differentiation levers: (1) fresh perspective in content — cites
~1 billion blogs / 1.8 billion websites and the recurring "auto insurance" example
(<200K monthly US searches vs. 7B+ indexed results) as evidence of oversupply; (2)
weight content-refresh over new content — discloses writing "one blog post a week"
(~4/month) himself while his team updates 100+ old articles/month, i.e. ~25x more
editing effort than his own new-writing output; (3) influencer marketing, citing
Nike/Michael Jordan, Kylie Jenner/Kylie Cosmetics, Apple/Beats-by-Dre/Dr. Dre, and —
notably, dated three years before its 2024 clawback lawsuit — Tom Brady's FTX
sponsorship and LeBron James/Tonal as current examples, plus Cameo as a low-budget way
to buy testimonial-style influencer content; (4) upsells/downsells to raise LTV as ad
and SEO costs rise, noting Marketing School's CRO-focused episodes are consistently
among the lowest-watched despite the topic's ROI; and (5) an omnichannel approach,
using the 2021 Apple iOS privacy change's damage to Facebook-ads-only ecommerce
businesses as the cautionary example.
