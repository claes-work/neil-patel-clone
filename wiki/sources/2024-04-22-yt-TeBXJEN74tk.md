# Experiments we are running and Tools we are using

## Summary
A "what's working right now" experiments/tools episode, breaking from the usual news format. Six items. (1) A competitor-content-to-infographic backlink-building tactic: auditing a competitor's most-linked articles, using ChatGPT/Gemini to summarize the data, converting it into an infographic via an AI visual-editor tool, then outreaching to the original article's linkers for a link to the new infographic — manual execution reportedly recovers ~15-20% of a source article's *real* (non-scraper) backlinks; AI-assisted execution achieves roughly half that success rate due to lower visual/output quality, though it's still considered efficient. (2) Cold-outreach email scaling via a tool called "SalesLead.ai" (compared to Outreach.io, valued over $1B), which rotates sending across multiple email domains/inboxes to avoid deliverability flags — cited via a secondhand data point of one operator sending 1-2K B2B emails/day at a ~1.6% conversion rate (32 conversions/day). (3) Auto-commenting on X/Twitter posts via "HypeFury," with a reported reliability problem (comments arriving 8-10 hours late rather than immediately, missing peak engagement) — still yielding 49 leads in 5 days across a small test, with an estimate that same-day posting could triple results, and that routing traffic to ebook/webinar pages converts better than a direct consulting-request page. (4) "Programmatic CRO": using an AI prompt ("you are the world's best conversion rate optimizer... analyze the content on this page and come up with the best possible call-to-action, no longer than six tokens") to generate page-specific, content-aware CTAs at scale across large sites (e.g., replacing generic "get a free consultation" with content-matched alternatives), reporting a 59% lift in button clicks. (5) A WhatsApp call-button test on non-US-market websites (used only outside the US, in B2B enterprise contexts), yielding a reported 12-13% lift in total leads despite cannibalization risk against existing contact-page conversions. (6) A tool-adoption retrospective: caution against tool sprawl (many small subscription charges accumulate — "death by a thousand paper cuts" — recommending quarterly/monthly subscription audits, citing a service like "Truebill" that analyzes and helps cancel/renegotiate subscriptions) alongside a disclosed investment in School.com (Sam Ovens' community platform, made ~2 years prior, now up in valuation), plans to route thank-you-page traffic into a free community there (~30% expected opt-in conversion based on past experience), and a test of an AI-dubbing tool ("MyGen," for Hindi/German/Spanish/French/Portuguese) that underperformed expectations — international audiences (Brazil specifically cited) reportedly preferred the original English audio with subtitles over dubbed versions, attributed partly to missing cultural nuance in AI dubbing.
