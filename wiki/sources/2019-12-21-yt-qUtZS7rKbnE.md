# One Tool To Make Your Website a Sales and Marketing Machine

## Summary
Solo tool-deep-dive — effectively a sponsored/partner review of **Keap Pro** (formerly
Infusionsoft), which Neil says he has used for years and been trained on by the Keap team,
whom he calls friends. The setup: online business owners are drowning in disconnected tools —
landing pages, hosting, email, automation, appointment setting, quoting, pipeline management,
invoicing, web forms, reporting — which is expensive and forces you to hire people. Product
sellers need landing pages, lifetime-value email sequences, behavioral automation and
reporting; service sellers need CRM/pipeline, automated prospect interaction, appointment
setting and invoicing. He then walks through how he personally uses Keap: landing pages and
web forms that auto-trigger email sequences based on which pages someone visited (an e-book
page plus the consulting page signals a far more qualified lead than the beginner's SEO guide)
and on open/click behavior, so he stops re-pitching someone who already clicked — a principle
he credits to copywriter **Frank Kern**: it isn't about having the best copy, it's about
sending the right email at the right time. He uses the CRM to check whether his sales reps are
actually following up and closing. His stated favorite use is **SMS**: a scripted opener
("Robert?" → "Who's this?" → "Hey, I'm so-and-so from Neil's team, I saw you filled out X, Y
and Z…") plus stage-specific reminders before calls and after unclosed pitches, which he says
raises the call and closing ratio and lets his sales team handle more prospects without new
hires. Also covered: self-service appointment scheduling straight into the Google calendar
(the same flow used to book the Neil Patel team), automated invoicing and billing so accounting
stays lean, trigger-based automation that treats a five-time repeat visitor differently from a
first-timer (sell vs. build trust), and reporting that shows which emails cause revenue rather
than mere opens plus end-of-month revenue projections — which he calls critical for SMBs so
you don't commit expenses against income you won't have. He offers to help viewers set up Keap
in the comments.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said he had been using Keap Pro for years, that Keap are friends of his, that he has received training from them, and that the software has helped grow his business tremendously.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil argued the core problem for online business owners is stitching together too many separate tools — landing pages, hosting, email, automation, appointment setting, quoting, pipeline, invoicing, forms, reporting — which is too expensive and forces over-hiring.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said product sellers need landing pages for paid ads, email sequences for lifetime value and seasonal/upsell opportunities, behavior-tracking automation to segment by funnel stage, and reporting; service sellers need pipeline/CRM, automated prospect interaction, appointment setting, forms and invoicing.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said he triggers different email sequences based on which pages a lead visited — someone who saw an e-book page and the consulting page is far more qualified than someone who read the beginner's guide to SEO — and stops pitching a link to someone who already clicked it.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil credited copywriter **Frank Kern** for the principle that success is not about having the best copy (most people are not amazing copywriters) but about great sequences that send the right emails at the right time.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said he uses the CRM/pipeline to verify whether his sales reps are actually following up with prospects and closing deals.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil named **SMS as his favorite use of Keap**, saying direct texting gets more people on the phone and improves his call ratio, and shared his team's exact opener script: text just the prospect's first name with a question mark, then, when they ask who it is, identify as Neil's team, reference the form they filled out, and offer a call.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said his team sends stage-specific texts — reminders before scheduled calls, and post-presentation follow-ups for unclosed pitches — which raises closing ratio and lets each rep handle more prospects without new hires.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said prospects can self-schedule appointments through his website straight into the Google calendar (the flow used to book the Neil Patel team), removing the "when are you free?" back-and-forth.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said Keap sends invoices and bills clients directly so his accounting team stays lean and only does bookkeeping instead of chasing every customer.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said a five-time repeat visitor should get selling emails while a one-time visitor who hasn't returned in 30 days needs educational trust-building emails — and that this applies equally to e-commerce, service businesses, consultants and coaches.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said that no matter how good you think you are at marketing, email or copywriting — himself included — it doesn't matter without good open, click and conversion rates; he uses reporting to kill emails that get opened but produce no sales.
- As of 2019-12-21, Neil said end-of-month revenue projections from the reporting are especially important for small and medium businesses (his own included) so you know how much you can spend to grow versus how much you should.
