# 3 Great Website Traffic Sources You're Probably Not Using

## Summary
Three underused traffic sources. First, high-domain-authority user-generated-content
sites (Quora, Wikipedia named as examples): participate genuinely in the community
and link back where relevant, even when the link carries no SEO value, purely for the
referral traffic — he mentions once having a person working full-time just answering
on Quora and linking to his site, calling it "that effective," with the traffic later
becoming residual without ongoing full-time effort. Second, paid content-syndication
platforms (Outbrain, Zemanta, ARC, Taboola, ZergNet, AllTop, 9Rules) that
algorithmically match sponsored content placements to contextually relevant articles,
likened to Facebook/Google Ads in targeting sophistication. Third, YouTube as an
underused traffic source for people who only consume it — states he received over
150,000 website visitors from YouTube in 2021 (~12,000/month) not counting the people
who visit after he verbally plugs NP Digital, and argues YouTube traffic converts
better than cold traffic because viewers already feel they know him; states Google is
his #1 conversion traffic source and YouTube #2. Also gives a YouTube-specific growth
tactic: because YouTube's ranking algorithm rewards strong first-24-hour performance
(unlike Google's slower-to-reward-consistency model), push hard on notifications/email
in the first day after publishing rather than over-polishing production quality.
