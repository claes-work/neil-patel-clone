# Advanced Facebook Ads Strategy: How to Retain Your Subscribers Forever

## Summary
A ManyChat (Facebook Messenger chatbot) tutorial framed as a fix for retargeting's
diminishing returns. Opens with a cost datapoint: Facebook ad CPC rose from roughly
$0.57 to $0.81 in one year (self-reported, no source cited), and argues retargeting
ads reach only a shrinking fraction of original ad-clickers. Walks through a 9-step
ManyChat setup: create account → define a valuable lead-magnet offer (free course,
webinar, case study, discount, checklist, quiz) → select a message-delivery flow
template → connect the Facebook ad account → build a "Click to Messenger" campaign with
a capped spend limit ("I usually put 100 dollars") → set location/age/gender/language
targeting plus interest-based *exclusions* for tighter targeting → write ad
text/creative → set the quick-reply button and select the ManyChat flow → preview and
launch. Claims his own and client Messenger campaigns see open rates up to 88% and
click-through rates up to 56% (self-reported, "I wish I was getting that through
email"). Pivots to SMS marketing as a second retention channel: cites an industry
projection of 48.7M US opt-ins and self-reported SMS benchmarks of ~98% open rate /
~36% CTR; names Gary Vaynerchuk and Grant Cardone as examples of marketers building
owned SMS lists to reduce platform dependency. Closes with an omnichannel-retention
doctrine: don't pick one channel (Messenger, email, SMS, push) — capture contact
info across all of them via the chatbot conversation and remarket cheaply instead of
re-paying for retargeting ads.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-08-24, Neil claimed Facebook ad CPC rose from ~$0.57 to ~$0.81 over one
  year (self-reported, no source cited).
- As of 2020-08-24, Neil claimed his/client Messenger (ManyChat) campaigns hit open
  rates as high as 88% and click-through rates as high as 56% (self-reported).
- As of 2020-08-24, Neil cited an SMS-marketing growth projection of 48.7M US
  opt-ins, with self-reported SMS benchmarks of ~98% open rate and ~36% CTR — both
  well above his cited email benchmarks.
- As of 2020-08-24, Neil's retention doctrine: capture email AND phone number AND
  Messenger opt-in from the same ad-click — remarketing to an owned list is "much
  cheaper than re-marketing ads" (paid retargeting).
- As of 2020-08-24, Neil named Gary Vaynerchuk and Grant Cardone as examples of
  marketers building owned SMS audiences specifically to reduce dependence on
  platforms like Facebook controlling their reach.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Most of the people that click on your ads do not convert, and this is why you want to get these data points so that way you can keep marketing to those people, which is much cheaper than re-marketing ads."

> "It's not that one is better than the other, you want to leverage all of them. If they can all help drive you sales, why not? Don't pick one, don't pick the other, pick all of them, and that's how you grow."
