# How to Get 4 Million Visits Per Month With One Simple Keyword Hack

## Summary
Neil Patel describes a single SEO tactic he credits for the majority of NeilPatel.com's four million monthly visits: instead of chasing brand-new keywords, expand the pages that already rank by targeting the long-tail variations of terms they rank for. He argues that once a page ranks for a difficult head term (e.g. "dog food"), Google will rank it easily for related long-tail variations. His workflow uses Ubersuggest's Top Pages report and Google Search Console to find existing rankings, then Ubersuggest's Keywords Ideas report to gather long-tail variations, which are folded back into the ranking page by rewriting and expanding the content. He claims this produces higher rankings across the long-tail variations within 30 days, and says he applied the hack to every one of his 2,000+ blog posts.

## Key claims
- Neil Patel says NeilPatel.com gets four million visits per month, of which two million are unique (as of 2019-06-10).
- He claims the majority of that traffic comes from one simple, easy hack that "no one's doing" and that you don't need to be an SEO to execute it (as of 2019-06-10).
- The core tactic: rather than finding new keywords to rank for, find keywords a page already ranks for (even small ones) and target their long-tail variations to generate more traffic (as of 2019-06-10).
- He asserts the head term is the hardest term to rank for; once you rank for it, Google will easily rank the same page for the long-tail variations (as of 2019-06-10).
- Individual long-tail variations may each contribute only one-fifth, one-tenth, or one-twentieth of a page's traffic, but combined they can add up to more traffic than the head term itself (as of 2019-06-10).
- Workflow Step 1: enter your domain in Ubersuggest to see the "Top Pages" report showing which pages drive traffic and which keywords drive rankings (as of 2019-06-10).
- Next step: log in to Google Search Console to see the terms you already rank for (as of 2019-06-10).
- Next step: enter those exact ranked phrases back into Ubersuggest to get the Keywords Ideas report listing long-tail variations of each term (as of 2019-06-10).
- Final step: take the ranking page, add all those long-tail terms, and rewrite/expand the content to be more valuable and comprehensive than any competing article (as of 2019-06-10).
- He claims that within 30 days the updated page will rank higher in Google for all the long-tail variations (as of 2019-06-10).
- He says he applied this hack to every single one of his 2,000+ articles/blog posts on NeilPatel.com (as of 2019-06-10).
