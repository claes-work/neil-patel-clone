# How to Convince Website Owners to Link Out to Your Website

## Summary
A link-building/outreach framework. Before pursuing a backlink, ask: does the content
solve a real problem for the target's audience; is it differentiated from the
oversaturated existing content on the topic (cites a stat: "7 billion" US search
results for "auto insurance" vs. ~300M+ US population); is it more up-to-date than
what's currently linked (mentions his team updates 100+ of his old blog posts every
month); and could it replace a broken link. Don'ts: don't beg, don't pretend you
stumbled on their content when you were actually searching for broken-link targets,
don't use unmodified outreach templates. Do's: target the actual decision-maker (not
just the article's byline author), use visual content (video/infographics — his
stated easiest way to earn links), back claims with data (ties to Google's E-A-T:
"Expertise, Authority, Trust"), build the relationship before asking, apply the
target's own advice/results before pitching them, address their audience's needs, and
follow up. Closes with a workflow using Ubersuggest's Backlinks Opportunity Report:
enter your domain plus 2-3 competitors to find sites linking to multiple competitors
but not you — high-probability targets.

## Key claims
- Cites a specific content-saturation stat: ~7 billion US search results for "auto
  insurance" against a US population of "300 and something million," as evidence that
  even boring topics are content-oversaturated (self-reported/third-party stat, dated
  2022-01-29) — seo.
- States his team updates "over 100 of my old blog posts each and every single
  month" as part of his content-freshness/traffic strategy (self-reported operational
  claim, dated 2022-01-29) — seo, content-marketing.
- Prescribes the Ubersuggest Backlinks Opportunity Report (own domain + 2-3
  competitors) to find link targets already linking to multiple competitors as a
  high-probability outreach list (self-reported tool workflow, dated 2022-01-29) —
  seo.
