# 22+ Marketing Channels For eCommerce - Module 1 - Part 3 - eCommerce Unlocked

## Summary
eCommerce Unlocked lesson surveying marketing channels, framed as "what I would do to
market my own e-commerce business if I owned one" — bolding a priority subset (content
marketing, SEO, social media, email marketing, PPC, package inserts, exit intent,
upsells/cross-sells) out of a longer list, with the advice to start with the priority
set and expand later. Opens with a KPI framework (unique visitors, conversion rate,
revenue per channel, profitability/margin) for comparing channel performance. Case
study: **Skincare by Alana** (Alana Mitchell), an "eight-figure" e-commerce business
built on educational content (skincare routines, acne/makeup tips) plus heavy live
video across Instagram/YouTube/social. Presents a quantified **upsell math example**:
at 500 orders/day and a $55 upsell, moving conversion from 5% to 15% take-rate turns
$1,375/day into $4,125/day (+$2,750/day) — illustrating the "two upsells at
checkout" rule (first upsell pricier, second ~2/3 price, complementary product). Cites
an average email-marketing ROI of "$30 for every $1 spent." Other tactics: product
inserts, quiz-based lead magnets (his own "Nutrition Secrets" supplement site used a
weight-loss quiz), exit-intent browser-push subscriptions via subscribers.com (~6%
CTR). Revenue benchmarks cited for American Eagle ($4.3B), Birchbox (~$200M,
subscription model), Dollar Shave Club (~$200M/yr, razors), Leesa ($95M, mattresses).
