# Why I Speak at Conferences for Free Now (Used to Charge $50k)

## Summary
Neil gives a dated, concrete account of a strategic pivot in his conference-speaking
business development: he used to charge $50,000 per one-hour US talk (plus travel) and
$100,000 internationally, doing 25-30 paid gigs/year pre-COVID, with demand still rising
today. He explains why he now speaks mostly for free (or minimal fee): top-tier events
that book Elon Musk and Bill Gates draw a higher-net-worth audience, and speaking at
those events (even unpaid, even without the premier stage) closes dramatically more
business than the high-fee circuit ever did. His stated lesson: he optimized for
short-run speaking revenue for over a decade and wishes he'd made the switch to
long-run/relationship-value events sooner. This is a earlier, more granular articulation
of the belief that later resurfaces (2026-07-17) as "conferences are a three-for-one"
being his single biggest lead-gen lever — this 2022 video is the origin/turning-point
story behind that later claim. ★ (belief-evolution link: bridges the 2019 $25-50k
speaking-fee datapoint [[2019-09-26-yt-hbgvd-wbBGU]] and the 2026 "conferences as the
#1 needle-mover" claim [[2026-07-17-yt-L03nbcDp8v8]].)

## Key claims
- Historical fee: $50,000/hour domestic (+ travel), $100,000/hour international, plus
  first-class travel/accommodation covered by the event; ~25-30 paid speaking gigs/year
  pre-COVID (self-reported, dated to before ~2020).
- Speaking-request volume has risen since COVID, not dropped, despite him now charging
  little/nothing for most gigs (as of 2022-08-11, self-reported).
- Strategic reasoning: events that book Elon Musk/Bill Gates-tier speakers draw a
  higher-net-worth audience; speaking there (even unpaid, without the premier stage)
  generates far more closed business than paid, lower-tier events did (as of
  2022-08-11).
- Explicit regret/lesson: he wishes he had made this shift "10-plus years" earlier;
  frames it as a "don't be shortsighted — optimize for the long run, not short-run fee
  income" principle (as of 2022-08-11).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> I used to charge $50,000 for a hour speech within the United States, literally only a one-hour speech.

> They can probably command half a million, a million dollars, but they're so rich they don't care for a half a million or a million dollars.

> The big lesson I learned is don't be shortsighted. Yes, there are many ways to make money, but don't focus on what's short run. Instead, focus on what's better for you in the long run.

> I wish I'd realized this 10 plus years ago when I started speaking and I started speaking for money, but it's too late to turn back the clock.
