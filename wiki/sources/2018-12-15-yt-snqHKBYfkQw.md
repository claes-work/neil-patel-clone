# Rank #1 on Google Using 7 Free SEO Tools | Neil Patel

## Summary

In this solo tutorial, Neil Patel walks through seven free tools he says can help a low-budget site owner rank number one on Google without hiring an agency or building links. The list runs Google Search Console (find your existing traffic-driving keywords, then rewrite title tags and meta descriptions to be magazine-headline appealing and lift click-through rate), Ubersuggest (mine long-tail variations of terms you already rank for and integrate them naturally into existing pages), the Yoast SEO plugin (WordPress on-page optimization plus XML sitemaps that ping Google), websiteresponsivetest.com (verify mobile-friendliness for Google's mobile-first index across many devices), Google Trends (measure and grow brand signals via an omni-channel "rule of seven" presence), Google PageSpeed Insights (diagnose and fix load time, which he asserts affects SEO), and the Keywords Everywhere Chrome extension (surface search volume and related keyword ideas while browsing Google normally). The through-line: leverage keywords you already rank for, optimize for clicks and mobile speed, and build brand strength.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-12-15, Neil frames the premise as: you want to rank #1 on Google, you don't have much money, you can't hire an agency — these seven free tools are the answer. He notes all seven are free.
- **Tool 1 — Google Search Console.** As of 2018-12-15, Neil says most people only use it to see which keywords they rank for, which he calls "not that effective." His preferred tactic: find the keywords driving the majority of your traffic, look at which pages they drive traffic to, then take that page's title tag and meta description, ensure the keyword is included, and optimize the title tag to be more appealing to win more clicks.
- As of 2018-12-15, Neil claims a click-through-rate ranking effect: if a thousand people search the same term and everyone clicks the second listing instead of the first, Google will move the second listing up to number one. So optimizing the title tag to be more appealing gets more clicks and better rankings.
- As of 2018-12-15, Neil recommends taking title-tag inspiration from magazine headlines (he cites made-up examples like "10 ways to lose weight in 30 days" and evoking curiosity like "The seven benefits of green tea, number five is going to shock you") to drive more clicks.
- **Tool 2 — Ubersuggest.** As of 2018-12-15, Neil says to plug your existing Search Console keywords into Ubersuggest to find all the long-tail variations. He claims if you already rank for the head terms, it is easy to rank for the long-tail terms — no link building and little on-page work required.
- As of 2018-12-15, Neil warns you cannot just add the keywords to a page; you must integrate them within your content, sometimes expanding or rewriting it so it flows and makes sense, or readers will bounce. He predicts that within 30 days of doing this you will get more search traffic to that page. He also suggests putting some of those keywords into the title tag and meta description.
- As of 2018-12-15, Neil says Ubersuggest ranks keywords by popularity and by how easy they are to rank for — a metric called Search Difficulty, shown as an "SD" number; higher = more competitive, lower = easier to rank.
- **Tool 3 — Yoast SEO plugin.** As of 2018-12-15, Neil says most viewers run WordPress and that this one free plugin makes on-page SEO much simpler — it helps optimize code, title tags, and meta descriptions, and even creates an XML sitemap you can plug into Google Search Console. He explains the sitemap pings Google when you release new content so Google can index, grab, and rank it.
- **Tool 4 — websiteresponsivetest.com.** As of 2018-12-15, Neil says to enter your URL there to check whether your site is responsive and mobile-friendly, because Google has a mobile-first index; more mobile-friendly sites rank higher. He stresses testing across many devices (iPhone, Android, etc.), not just your own phone.
- **Tool 5 — Google Trends.** As of 2018-12-15, Neil frames Trends as an SEO tool via brand signals, saying Google has valued brand signals for years but they are now more impactful than ever, and Google wants to rank brands higher than non-branded sites (partly to avoid ranking fake news / fake information). He says Google Trends shows how your brand compares to competitors — a higher line/graph is better.
- As of 2018-12-15, Neil says to grow your brand via an omni-channel approach (content marketing, SEO, social media marketing) and cites the "rule of seven": when someone sees your brand seven times they are more likely to resonate, keep coming back, and help your rankings long-term.
- **Tool 6 — Google PageSpeed Insights.** As of 2018-12-15, Neil asserts that PageSpeed does impact SEO. Because more people use Google on mobile than desktop and Google has a mobile-specific index, load speed matters; PageSpeed Insights tells you how Google views your site's load time and what to change to make it faster.
- **Tool 7 — Keywords Everywhere (Chrome extension).** As of 2018-12-15, Neil says this extension shows search volume and related keywords right on the Google results screen as you browse normally, giving you keyword ideas and reminding you which keywords are popular so you consider creating content around them.
- As of 2018-12-15, Neil closes by urging viewers to use all seven tools, and to subscribe/like/comment/share.
