# Why we Acquired Answer the Public

## Summary
In this short video Neil Patel explains the strategic rationale behind his acquisition of the keyword-research tool Answer The Public, and uses it as a teaching case for growth through acquisitions rather than marketing alone. He frames the story with scale context: he owns an ad agency called NP Digital, which he says is one of the fastest-growing companies per Inc Magazine at the 21st spot with over 700 employees globally, yet he calls it "really, really tiny" next to giants like Apple, Google, or ExxonMobil — companies that, he argues, grow largely through acquisitions (Google buying Nest and Waymo, Apple buying Beats by Dre, Facebook acquiring Instagram). He then walks through the specific economics that made Answer The Public attractive: it has roughly 60% of the traffic of his other tool Ubersuggest but generates less than 10% of the revenue, a gap he believes he can close and "4 to 5x" fairly easily. Crucially, he says he will not ruin the user experience or cut free features — instead he plans to give away more for free, reasoning that a better, more popular product will convert more users to a premium version over time. He identifies additional upside beyond direct revenue: Ubersuggest already drives roughly 30% of NP Digital's agency customers, and Answer The Public never tried selling to its own leads, opening a new stream (for example, consulting) that he promises to pursue without bombarding users with popups. He also notes the product had zero people working on it, so simply adding staff makes improvement easy. He closes by stating the whole point of the video is educational — encouraging viewers to consider acquiring other companies as a growth channel — while admitting it "can hurt" him as a marketer to concede that marketing is not the only path to growth.
