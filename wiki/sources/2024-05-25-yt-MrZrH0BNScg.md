## Key claims (Eric Siu + AOA members — context only, NOT persona data)
- Eric's thesis: over the next 5–10 years the agency of the future employs a small top-5% team of
  highly strategic/creative people, each directing tens-to-thousands of AI agents per client; he
  is deliberately delaying M&A roll-ups until this plays out so he doesn't have to cut headcount
  post-acquisition. (Eric, 2024-05-25)
- Sam Altman reportedly said in-person experiences will become more valuable as more of life goes
  AI-mediated — cited approvingly by both Eric and the AOA members as a case for continuing
  in-person/event-based business development. (Eric, reporting Altman, 2024-05-25)
- Member roundtable on SEO headwinds: one member's SEO revenue is up ~70% YoY (no headwind seen
  yet); another cites tailwinds (record enterprise-logo leads going to agencies that "actually know
  what they're doing"); a third cites B2B SaaS pullback (VC funding down, response rates down 50%,
  CAC up — cites a **Chris Walker** figure of ~$41,000 CAC for some paid-ads-plus-SDR B2B stacks).
  (AOA members David/Brendan, 2024-05-25)
- Google's AI Overviews appear to draw heavily on LinkedIn polls, Reddit, and Quora answers; one
  member notes Perplexity cited their own agency first when asked for "best digital marketing
  agencies." (AOA members, 2024-05-25)
- Eric's own prior software product generated ~$600K ARR but lost ~$100K/month; he shelved it to
  refocus on Single Grain, and is now building a narrower CRO-automation product instead, testing
  it internally before offering it to clients. (Eric, 2024-05-25)
- The "Home Run in the first 90 days?" question, asked of prospective clients, surfaces both their
  expectations and their sophistication — a cheap qualifying filter. (Eric, 2024-05-25)
