# How Many Links You Actually Need To Rank Page One On Google

## Summary
Live-screen breakdown using Ahrefs on the "web hosting" SERP: Neil pulls referring-
domain counts for each page-one result (ranging 474 to 53,000) and shows there's no
consistent correlation between raw link count and rank position — a page with 949
links outranks one with 53,000. His explanation: most large link counts are inflated
by scraper sites, RSS-feed mirrors, and other low-quality junk links that don't
represent real editorial votes, so the *effective* competitive bar is much lower than
raw counts suggest. He also connects the CTR/branding thesis already in the corpus
(informational/review content beats promotional pages) to link-building efficiency.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-12-03, demonstrates via Ahrefs that page-one ranking position does not
  correlate cleanly with total referring-domain count — the #1 result (949 links) had
  far fewer links than the #10 result (53,000 links) on the sampled SERP (paraphrase,
  single-SERP case study, not a controlled study).
- Rule of thumb (paraphrase): when sizing a link-building target, build "a third to a
  fourth, even a fifth" of the referring domains a top-ranking competitor has, and you
  can compete — because a large share of any site's reported links are scraper/RSS/
  spam links, not genuine editorial links.
- Advises building links "in a very, very slow pace" — deliberately not fast — because
  unnatural link-velocity spikes are detectable by search engines and can suppress
  rankings; reinforces this with a social-media-follower-count analogy (large follower
  counts with low engagement = fake followers, same signal problem as fast unnatural
  link growth) (paraphrase, standing belief).
- Reiterates (consistent with prior corpus material) that resource/review-style,
  educational content out-ranks direct self-promotional pages of similar link profile
  because it earns a higher click-through rate, and higher CTR is itself a ranking
  lever independent of link count.
- Practical guidance: don't over-index on domain rating/domain authority scores when
  vetting link targets; prioritize legitimate outreach over volume, and never pay for
  links or use black-hat tactics (paraphrase, standing belief, reinforcement).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Just because someone has 53,000 links doesn't mean you need 53,000 links.

> If you build too fast, what you'll find is you're going to have to build too many
> links... it's unnatural, the search engines know it, and you're not going to rank
> that highly. You need to slow it down. Less is actually better.
