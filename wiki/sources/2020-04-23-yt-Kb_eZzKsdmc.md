# Client says, 'Let Me Think About it.' and You say, '...'

## Summary
A sales-objection-handling framework for the "let me think about it" brush-off. Neil
opens with the "forgetting curve" — cites that people forget 50% of what they heard
within an hour, 75% within 24 hours, and 90% within 30 days, with the retained 10%
being "50% inaccurate" — to argue you must resolve objections in the moment rather than
let a prospect "think about it." His diagnostic: the objection means either genuine
disinterest or genuine uncertainty; ask "why aren't you interested?" or "why aren't you
sure? what's your biggest concern?" to find out which. Uncertainty typically reduces to
one of three causes: (1) fit — ask directly if it fits their needs; (2) functionality —
either commit to building missing features, argue they don't need them, or point to a
third-party integration that plugs the gap (his stated explanation for why tech products
integrate with each other); (3) financial — offer payment-term options (30/60/90 days),
adjust the feature set/price down, or find the actual budget-holder if you're talking to
the wrong person. General tactic: get multiple people within the buying organization
bought in, not just the primary contact, to raise the odds of a yes. Neil frames the
whole approach as logic-driven, not emotional manipulation: "sales is not just about
emotion."

## Key claims
- As of 2020-04-23, Neil said people forget 50% of what they're told within an hour,
  75% within 24 hours, and 90% within 30 days, with the retained portion also being
  about 50% inaccurate (the "forgetting curve").
- As of 2020-04-23, Neil's framework for "let me think about it": diagnose whether it's
  disinterest or uncertainty, then for uncertainty diagnose fit vs. functionality vs.
  financial concerns and address each with a specific tactic (feature roadmap/
  integration for functionality; payment terms or reduced feature set for financial).
- As of 2020-04-23, Neil advised getting buy-in from multiple people inside a
  prospect's organization, not just the primary decision-maker, to raise close odds.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Sales is not just about emotion. When you take people through those logical steps, it's easier to get them over that hurdle."

> "In just one hour, people have already forgotten 50% of what you said... In 30 days, the customer has forgotten 90% of what you said."
