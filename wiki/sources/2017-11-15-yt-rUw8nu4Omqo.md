# SEO Test 101: How to Measure Your Search Engine Optimization Efforts

## Summary
Neil Patel names the key metrics to track for an SEO campaign and reiterates his SEO timeline stance: results don't kick in for 6 months to a year, with the real skyrocket coming between year one and year two. The five metrics: (1) search traffic (via Google Analytics — up is good, down means change something); (2) impressions (via Google Search Console — you get impressions before clicks; a rising month-over-month impression count means you're doing something right); (3) backlinks (via Ahrefs — track both your own and competitors', and who's growing faster with natural, non-bought links); (4) content production volume (aim for three-plus articles/week minimum, ideally one-to-two a day; five-to-six a day is overboard); and (5) number of indexed pages (via Search Console — submit a sitemap; on WordPress use Yoast to auto-generate and ping Google). If the numbers rise, do more of what you're doing; if they fall, adjust.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-15, Neil states:
- SEO takes time: not 3 months, not even 6 months — results land somewhere between 6 months and a year, and within the first-to-second year is when rankings really skyrocket, regardless of industry (as of 2017-11-15).
- Getting results within a week means your site is already old with authority, or you're chasing easy keywords that won't generate income (as of 2017-11-15).
- Metric 1 — search traffic, seen in Google Analytics; rising is good, falling means you need to change something (as of 2017-11-15).
- Metric 2 — impressions, seen in Google Search Console (free); you get impressions (appearing in results without a click) before traffic, usually far more impressions than clicks, and starting at page 3–4; a continually climbing impression count month-over-month means you're doing something right (as of 2017-11-15).
- Metric 3 — backlinks, tracked with Ahrefs; track your own and competitors' link growth and who's growing faster; natural authoritative links (not bought) put you in a good place (as of 2017-11-15).
- Metric 4 — content production; four articles/week is good, one/week takes forever; ideally three-or-more per week at minimum, aiming for one-to-two a day; five-to-six a day is overboard. More content = more keywords Google can index you for (as of 2017-11-15).
- Metric 5 — number of indexed pages, shown in Google Search Console; submit a sitemap, and on WordPress use the Yoast SEO plugin, which auto-creates and updates the sitemap and tells Google about each new post (as of 2017-11-15).
