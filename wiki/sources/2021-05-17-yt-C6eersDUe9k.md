# The Untold Laws of Growth Hacking - Propelling Your Business to Exponential Growth

## Summary
Growth Hacking Unlocked Week 2, Lesson 1, introducing the three disciplines of growth
hacking (psychology, technology, marketing) after the funding-focused Week 1 lessons.
Opens with the thesis that growth hacking substitutes for expensive venture-funded
traditional marketing by being more capital-efficient, and that step one is always
having a product people actually want ("fine-tune your product... even if it's not
perfect, that's okay"). Devotes most of the lesson to psychology-driven growth tactics:
decoy pricing (the classic movie-theater popcorn small/medium/large example, where the
large is priced to look like the obviously good deal); social proof (used on the "Neil
Patel Digital" site); Intercom's personalized landing pages reportedly tripling
conversion; free trials (uses one on Ubersuggest, notes real drop-off but net-positive
revenue) and the "power of free" (Uber's free-ride giveaway at SXSW 2013 as
cheaper-than-advertising exposure); Cymatics (client of his agency, founder "Steven")
generating $4.5M+ across 8 product launches and 1.1M+ subscribers via competitions and
giveaways; product-demo/explainer videos (used on crazyegg.com years ago via a vendor
called "Demo Duck") with a stat bank (73% more buyers among video viewers, videos in
~14% of search results, 71% say video explains product better, 57% fewer surprises/
lower refunds, 58% trust companies with product video more); exclusivity/referral loops
(Gmail invite-only launch, Dropbox's 500MB-for-invites referral mechanic plus an
explainer video on its homepage); and "progression" as a video-game-style psychological
hook to keep users engaged. Also references an "advanced sales funnel" walkthrough
(landing page → lead capture → thank-you page → email → sales page → order page →
one-time upsell → follow-up email sequence) available in the paid course, citing "my
buddy Timothy Sykes" as growing to $15M/year (now bigger) using funnels. Closes previewing
the next lesson (technology) and plugging NP Digital.
