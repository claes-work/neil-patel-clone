# Simple Social Media Patterns You Need To Know to Grow Your Reach, Followers, and Engagement

## Summary
Interview with a longtime friend/collaborator referred to only as "Dan" (attribution:
uncertain — full name not stated in transcript; likely the same "Dan" credited with the
"Who is Neil Patel?" campaign in [[2021-07-29-yt-FbBVrn_zaUk]], recorded in the same
session/series). Opens on a counterintuitive link-building claim: **slower, fewer,
higher-quality links can outrank aggressive high-volume link building**, because Google
treats unnaturally fast link accumulation as a signal (paraphrase) — a sharper,
explicitly-comparative articulation than prior "quality over volume" link-building
notes in this corpus. References the incoming **2021 Page Experience algorithm update**
(mobile-first rollout, desktop uncertain at the time) as evidence Google is moving toward
holistic UX signals over keyword/link mechanics — reinforces the existing
RankBrain-as-UX-signals belief. The **"Who is Neil Patel?" brand campaign** is retold
here in a strikingly different tone than the 2018-05-29 apology source
([[2018-05-29-yt-w0AjQoLrKfw]], already in persona/biography.md): here it is framed
matter-of-factly as **smart arbitrage** — "Dan" is explicitly credited as the strategist,
spend is quantified at **$50–60K over a few months**, and the branded-search lift is
credited with saving **>$100K/month in PPC spend** by lifting organic rank for
"online/digital marketing" queries. **Tone note, not a factual contradiction**: the 2018
telling is an on-record apology for the tactics used (paying people, including "half-
naked girls," to display his name); this telling omits any apologetic framing and treats
the campaign as a straightforward growth-hacking success story. Both can be true (he
apologized for the *execution* while still standing behind the *SEO outcome*), but the
tonal gap is worth a synthesis-pass note if beliefs.md ever cites this campaign as a
clean case study. LinkedIn 3%-content-creator scarcity stat reinforced (already
documented). YouTube algorithm mechanism reinforced and extended: first-hour/first-24-
hour engagement drives ranking (already ★-promoted); new tactical detail here — actively
prompt viewers via email/push/text blasts immediately on publish to manufacture that
early-engagement spike (paraphrase, tactical, not previously this explicit).

## Key claims
- Building fewer, higher-quality, more slowly-accumulated backlinks can outrank
  aggressive high-volume link building, because Google treats abnormally fast link
  growth as a signal of manipulation (paraphrase, dated 2021-07) — seo.
- Google's 2021 Page Experience update (mobile-first rollout) is cited as evidence
  ranking is shifting toward holistic user-experience signals, not just keywords/links
  (paraphrase, dated 2021-07) — seo.
- "Who is Neil Patel?" campaign, quantified: ~$50–60K spend over a few months, credited
  with saving >$100K/month in PPC spend via branded-search-driven organic rank gains for
  "online marketing"/"digital marketing" (self-reported). Retold here without the
  apologetic framing present in the 2018-05-29 source — attribution/tone divergence
  flagged, not a factual contradiction — agency-entrepreneurship, seo.
- Only ~3% of LinkedIn users create content, per an article Neil cites (unverified
  currency) — social-media. Reinforces the existing LinkedIn-scarcity stat.
- Tactical YouTube-ranking play: on publish, actively drive views in the first hour via
  email blast, push notification, and text message to trigger the platform's early-
  engagement ranking boost (paraphrase, tactical) — social-media, seo.
- Recommends 5–10 minute (not 20–40 minute) YouTube videos as a practical sweet spot;
  notes production polish matters less than consistency (paraphrase) — social-media.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "If you actually build less links, focus on higher quality sites and you build it
