# 5 Quick Ways to Improve Your SEO Rankings

## Summary
Opens acknowledging SEO is "the long game" (cites NP Digital enterprise clients
staying "five plus years") but argues quicker wins exist. Five tactics: (1) find
"almost ranking" phrases via Google Search Console (Search Results → Average Position
→ Pages, filter for positions 11-20) and reinforce those exact terms in the title tag,
meta description, and body content to push page-two rankings to page one — notes
Google's NLP can already rank a page for terms not literally present, but reinforcing
them explicitly speeds the climb; flags that URL changes require a 301 redirect plus
updated internal links. (2) Add a free tool to the site to earn backlinks — cites his
own experience building Ubersuggest, which "increased my backlink count by 24,453
referring domains," and suggests non-developers buy a white-labelable tool from
CodeCanyon for "10, or even 15 or $20" instead of building custom. (3) Add internal
links from 3 relevant older articles pointing to each newly published article, to help
new content rank faster while it waits on external backlinks. (4) Optimize meta tags
using CTR-lift data (title/meta-description/URL formatting), citing a long list of
specific percentages (see Key claims). (5) "Content is king, so write it in half the
time" — points to Ubersuggest's AI writer (Lab section) as a starting-draft generator
(keyword → title/meta-description/heading suggestions), explicitly framed as a
head-start tool, not a publish-ready generator ("I wouldn't recommend publishing that
article... in its current state"). Closes with an NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- States NP Digital enterprise-division clients stay "five plus years," with an
  expectation of eventually retaining clients "over seven years" (self-reported,
  dated 2022-10-27, agency is described as "a newer agency").
- States adding the Ubersuggest tool to his own site "increased my backlink count by
  24,453 referring domains" (self-reported).
- CTR-lift stats for title-tag/meta/URL optimization (Ubersuggest-derived, self-
  reported, dated 2022-10-27): titles phrased as questions +14.1% CTR; titles of
  15-40 characters +8.6%; exact-query match in the URL up to +45%; "power words"
  (example: "effortless") +13.9%; titles conveying emotion +7.3%; having any meta
  description +5.8%; titles containing a year +4.9%; curiosity-gap titles (e.g. "the
  seven benefits of green tea, number six will shock you") +5.9%; education-styled
  titles +10.4%.
- Recommends checking paid-ads click data as a proxy signal for which organic titles
  will perform well (tactical prescription, self-reported).
- Describes Ubersuggest's "AI writer" (Lab section) workflow: keyword in → suggested
  title/meta description/headings out → draft article, explicitly positioned as a
  head-start/keyword-coverage tool rather than a finished, publishable piece.
