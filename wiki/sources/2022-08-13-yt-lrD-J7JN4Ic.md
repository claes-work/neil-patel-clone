# The Larger the TAM The Easier it is Market

## Summary
Another iteration of Neil's established TAM (total addressable market) doctrine, with a
concrete worked contrast: the global healthcare market (~$2.5 trillion) vs. the web
analytics market (~$10.7 billion, cited to Allied Market Research). He admits his
earlier businesses (implicitly Crazy Egg/KISSmetrics-era web-analytics products) were
capped by too-small a TAM — a lesson he says took "roughly 15 years" to fully internalize
despite investors repeatedly pressing him on market size. He credits NP Digital's growth
(named the 21st fastest-growing company in America per Inc. Magazine) to operating in
the much larger ad-agency TAM rather than the narrower web-analytics space, and reframes
the marketing-practitioner takeaway: focus less on search volume/CPC/conversion-rate
optimization and more on picking a large-TAM market in the first place.

## Key claims
- Named figures: global healthcare market ~$2.5 trillion; web analytics market ~$10.7
  billion "in 2026" per Allied Market Research (as of 2022-08-13, third-party-sourced;
  note the source cites a "2026" figure despite being an 2022 video — likely a
  market-research report's forward-looking estimate, not a caption/date error).
- Self-critical admission: his own past web-analytics businesses were constrained by too
  small a TAM relative to markets like health insurance; took ~15 years to fully grasp
  despite investor pushback on market size (as of 2022-08-13, self-reported).
- NP Digital credited as his fastest-growing company ever, ranked 21st fastest-growing
  company in America by Inc. Magazine — attributed to the ad-agency TAM being much
  larger than the web-analytics TAM (as of 2022-08-13, self-reported award citation).
- Practitioner reframe: marketers overweight search volume/CPC/conversion research and
  underweight TAM size when choosing what to market (as of 2022-08-13).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> The larger the TAM, the easier it is to market.

> I'm not really sure what industry I would've go after, but I definitely would've picked a bigger TAM because it's easier to market in bigger markets.

> That's what marketers forget about when they do the marketing. They focus too much on search volume, and conversion rates, and market research, versus just making sure the TAM is big enough.
