# What is Black Hat SEO Costing You? (How to Outrank Black Hat SEO)

## Summary

Neil argues that copying competitors who outrank you with **black hat SEO** is a losing
move: shady techniques rank in the **short run** but Google eventually "slaps" them.
He discloses that in his **early 20s** he ranked at the top for hyper-competitive terms
(web hosting, online casino, online poker, auto insurance) using black hat, but the
rankings lasted **less than a year** each — and says had he spent that time on legitimate
SEO he'd "be worth way more money." Rather than reporting competitors (a "waste of time"),
he gives four white-hat steps to beat them: (1) **survey your users** (SurveyMonkey, Hotjar)
on how to improve UX/content/product — Google ranks the best user experience (cites Ahrefs
finally ranking for "backlink checker" only after releasing a free limited backlink tool
users wanted); (2) **optimize title tags and meta descriptions** for click-through rate,
since user metrics/CTR can swap positions #1 and #2; (3) **be patient** and keep updating
content (Google penalizes stale content); and a bonus (4) **go global / translate your
content** into less-competitive markets (names Brazil — large population, decent GDP, little
link building) to dominate SERPs without competitors.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-06-20, Neil said **black hat SEO ranks only short-term** — Google adapts and
  penalizes it, usually within **under a year** — so long-run it loses to white hat.
- As of 2019-06-20, Neil **self-reported** having used black hat SEO in his **early 20s** to
  rank for web hosting, online casino, online poker, and auto insurance, and said he stopped
  because "it's terrible to do."
- As of 2019-06-20, Neil said the fix is to **survey users** (SurveyMonkey, Hotjar) and
  improve UX/content, because Google ranks the site that provides the best user experience —
  citing **Ahrefs** breaking through on "backlink checker" only after releasing a free,
  100-link-limited backlink tool.
- As of 2019-06-20, Neil said **optimizing title tags and meta descriptions for CTR** matters
  because click-through and user metrics can flip rankings between positions #1 and #2.
- As of 2019-06-20, Neil advised **patience** plus continually updating content (Google
  dislikes stale content), and — as a bonus — **going global / translating content** into
  less-competitive countries (e.g., Brazil) to dominate SERPs.
