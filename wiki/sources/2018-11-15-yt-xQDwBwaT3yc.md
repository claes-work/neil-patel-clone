# From Brick and Mortar to an Online Business in a Digital World

_Q&A Thursday; **Adam Lodolce** (viewership) co-hosts and adds his own "net content creation"
concept. Only **Neil-attributed** material trains the persona. Viewer question (Dr. Brian
Thomason): feels divided between running a brick-and-mortar business and building a digital product,
moving at "snail's pace" from distraction._

## Summary

Neil's answer runs on two tracks. First, a **reframe**: if you're trying to build a digital product,
it's usually because you **don't see the future in your brick-and-mortar** — but brick-and-mortar
"isn't ugly," it still works. He cites his buddy **Jeff** and **Everbowl** (açaí/juice) putting
healthy food in non-affluent areas — ~13 stores in ~1.5-2 years, profitable within ~3 months per
store — by "**giving away food for free**" (digital-marketing logic applied offline) to build
loyalty. If you believe in the brick-and-mortar, **expand it**; if you don't, **build the digital
product**. Second, and harder: **the "no time" excuse is really a priorities problem.** "You have
time — you're making time for TV, friends, drinking, eating out. If you really believe in something
you can figure out time. Sleep less." He grounds it in a **personal/family story**: when his
**parents immigrated to America**, his **mother** (a teacher whose foreign degree wasn't recognized)
**pushed him and his sister in a stroller several miles to a school and taught there for free for
six months**, then walked home and cooked; his **father did household chores and helped** without
pay. The lesson: if you truly want it, you make the time. If you have cash instead of time,
**hire help** (interns, experienced people). Tactics to bridge: **pre-sell the digital product** —
build a landing page, sell before it exists, and use that income to buy time away from the
brick-and-mortar (he **pre-sold >$500k of his book *Hustle* before launch**; you can start smaller).
Adam adds **"net content creation"** (net = no extra time): record content inside things you already
do (GoPro between appointments, phone voice notes, filming in the car). Use **RescueTime** to find
where time leaks.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-11-15, **brick-and-mortar still works** — if you believe in it, expand it; only build
  the digital product if you don't see its future.
- As of 2018-11-15, the "not enough time" complaint is a **priorities/willingness problem**, not a
  real time shortage — cut TV/leisure, sleep less, or **hire help** if you have cash not time.
- As of 2018-11-15, **pre-sell the digital product** via a landing page before it exists; the income
  buys time to build it and reduce reliance on the brick-and-mortar.
- As of 2018-11-15, **use RescueTime** to audit and reclaim wasted time.
- Example (**Jeff / Everbowl**, third-party): ~13 stores in ~1.5-2 yrs, profitable ~3 months/store,
  grown by **giving away free food** to build community loyalty.
- **"Net content creation"** (Adam Lodolce, **co-host — not persona data**): produce content inside
  activities you already do so it adds no extra time.
