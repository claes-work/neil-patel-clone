# The Hidden Benefits of A Short URL for Google SEO

_Q&A Thursday: Neil with co-host **Adam** (from Viewership). Only Neil-attributed
material is persona data._

## Summary
Answering a viewer (Arbaz) on URL shortening, Neil distinguishes link-shorteners (Bitly
— usable but he doesn't recommend leaning on such tools) from **short, keyword-rich site
URLs**, which he says rank better. Long URLs with many subfolders and stacked keywords
make Google read the page as being about that narrow combination; a **short, focused URL
(e.g. neilpatel.com/what-is-seo)** signals the page is about "SEO" broadly and ranks
page-one for it. Short URLs are also more **memorable and shareable** and less likely to
be misspelled/broken when linked. On domains: short, keyword-containing domains still
help — **exact-match domains** carry less weight than years ago but examples like
hotels.com and keywordtool.io still rank strongly. A keyword-in-domain also shapes the
**anchor text** people naturally use when linking, and builds **brand queries**, which
Google uses as a ranking tiebreaker (BMW vs. General Motors via Google Trends volume).

## Key claims
As of 2018-04-12, Neil states:
- **Short, keyword-rich URLs rank higher**; long URLs with many subfolders "pigeonhole"
  the page to a narrow keyword combination and rank less well.
- **neilpatel.com/what-is-seo** ranks page one for "seo" and related terms precisely
  because the URL is short and focused; a URL like ".../link-building-the-key-to-seo-in-
  2017" would only rank for those narrow/dated terms.
- Short URLs are **more memorable and shareable** (e.g. "wp-admin"/"wp-login") and reduce
  misspelled/broken links when others cite them.
- **Short keyword domains still help**: exact-match domains have **less pull than 3–5
  years ago** (Google changed the algorithm), but hotels.com and keywordtool.io still
  rank well; buying a keyword-matching domain for a "money keyword" is a valid hack, and
  it needn't be a .com/.org (.io works).
- A **keyword-in-domain shapes anchor text** — people link with the brand name, so a
  keyword-brand earns keyword anchors. His **Ubersuggest** page (neilpatel.com/ubersuggest)
  ranks (page 1–3) for "keyword tool" and should climb over time.
- **Brand queries are a major ranking factor**: with two equally-optimized, heavily-
  linked sites, Google favors the one searched more (BMW vs. GM); check relative demand
  in **Google Trends**. Neil ranks for terms like "online marketing" partly because many
  people search "neil patel" and variations.
