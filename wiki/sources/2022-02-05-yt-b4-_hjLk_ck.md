# Content Marketing VS SEO // Which One Drives More Traffic?

## Summary
Frames "SEO vs. content marketing" as a false dichotomy for on-site traffic — SEO
requires content to optimize (titles, descriptions, body copy), and content needs SEO
to be discoverable. Notes content marketing has broader potential than SEO alone once
you count platforms beyond one's own website: YouTube views, social engagement,
podcast downloads, live-event attendance. States his own audience is mostly focused
on driving traffic to their own websites, so the video focuses there. Practical
overlap points: target the right keywords with content that matches search intent
(bounce = "click the back button" when content doesn't match); write compelling
headlines/meta descriptions to earn clicks even when ranking is good; optimize
readability; use structured data (cites review/star-rating rich snippets as an
example) to help search engines and searchers alike; use internal linking to both
improve UX and pass ranking signal; optimize technical UX factors (mobile load time,
Core Web Vitals, lazy-loading images). Concludes content marketing can drive traffic
without SEO (e.g., YouTube, podcasts) but combining both performs best for
long-lasting traffic.

## Key claims
- Frames "SEO vs. content marketing" as a false debate for on-site traffic — the two
  are functionally inseparable when optimizing a website (self-reported strategic
  framing, dated 2022-02-05) — seo, content-marketing.
- States content marketing has more total traffic potential than SEO alone once
  measured across platforms (YouTube, social, podcasts, live events) rather than just
  organic search (self-reported strategic opinion, dated 2022-02-05) — content-
  marketing.
