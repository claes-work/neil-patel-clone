# How Passion Keeps Me Going (Grind Through)

## Summary

Solo motivational monologue, shot on a heavy production day. Neil opens by noting he's
shooting 20–25 videos in one day and, while he gets tired ("I am human ... not a machine"),
he refuses coffee/caffeine — people on set keep offering it and he declines. His thesis:
**the best form of energy is passion.** If you love what you're doing, you'll keep going
no matter how tired you are. He contrasts this with a friend who says he only works ~43
efficient hours because working 60 leaves the extra ~17 hours "really inefficient" — Neil
argues that if you're *truly* passionate you'll be efficient across all your hours and
grind through even when tired. He allows for vacations and breaks, but insists passion is
the best fuel for pushing through and hitting goals. The back half is career advice: if
you don't have passion for what you're doing, **stop doing it — life is too short and
you'll regret it.** You're not born knowing your calling (kids say "doctor/firefighter,"
then grow up lost — "a lot of people are 50 or 60 and still lost"); most people don't know
what they want and won't admit it. The way to find your true passion is to **try many
small things**; you'll eventually discover what you're good at, and what you're good at is
usually also what you're passionate about (they "go hand in hand"). Keep trying new things
until you find it — then you'll do well, won't need coffee, and will likely make more money
in the long run than if you'd stayed in something you didn't love.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-09-08, **passion, not caffeine, is the best energy source** — Neil shoots
  20–25 videos in a day and declines coffee, saying love of the work carries him through
  fatigue.
- As of 2018-09-08, **true passion makes all your working hours efficient** (his counter
  to a friend's "only 43 productive hours" view); breaks/vacations are fine but passion is
  what lets you grind through.
- As of 2018-09-08, **if you're not passionate about what you do, quit it** — "life is too
  short," you'll regret it.
- As of 2018-09-08, you discover your calling by **trying many small things**; what you're
  good at is typically also what you're passionate about, and the two "go hand in hand."
- As of 2018-09-08, doing work you love makes you better at it and likely earns you **more
  money in the long run** than staying in work you don't love.
