# 6 Breakthrough Email Marketing Tips to Grow Your Subscriber List FAST

## Summary

A "Q&A Thursday" episode co-hosted with Adam from Viewership, answering a viewer (Ashish) on how to convince people to sign up for an email list. Neil's core principle is to give something genuinely worthwhile in exchange for the email rather than just asking for it — nobody opts in to "put in your email," but they will opt in to "double your traffic in 30 days." Tactics, in his ranked order: pop-ups (via Hello Bar, cookied so they show once, ideally on exit) are the weakest; sidebar opt-ins are next; content upgrades are the best-converting — a small in-article opt-in offering a cheat sheet/bonus specific to that post ("that crushes everything," though it takes more work to make unique ones per post). He also runs an animated-GIF exit pop-up on neilpatel.com that shows what you'll get (e.g., a glimpse of how he ranks #1 for "online marketing"), which converts well because it demonstrates value and counters the fear of being spammed. For higher-quality (if fewer) leads he recommends webinars (evergreen via EverWebinar, enabling up-sells/down-sells/drip campaigns) and quizzes (via lead quizzes). Adam adds a quiz→webinar funnel he's been running (~15¢/lead quizzes feeding webinar sign-ups). Neil notes all of this can be done free with Hello Bar and, once monetization is set up, scaled with Facebook and YouTube ads.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-05-03, Neil says the key to list growth is offering something worthwhile (an e-book, course, or detailed resource) in exchange for the email, not merely asking people to "put in your email."
- As of 2018-05-03, Neil ranks opt-in placements: pop-ups (weakest, but usable via Hello Bar, cookied to show once and on exit) < sidebar opt-ins < content upgrades (best converting).
- As of 2018-05-03, Neil says content upgrades — a small in-article opt-in box (within the first ~3 paragraphs) offering a cheat sheet or bonus tips specific to that post — is his highest-converting offer, "crushes everything," but requires a unique upgrade per blog post.
- As of 2018-05-03, Neil describes an animated-GIF exit pop-up on neilpatel.com that shows what the subscriber will get (a glimpse of how he ranks #1 for "online marketing"), calling it one of his highest-converting opt-ins because showing (not just telling) value counters the fear of spam.
- As of 2018-05-03, Neil says webinars produce fewer but higher-quality emails that convert better into customers, especially as evergreen webinars (via EverWebinar) enabling up-sells, down-sells, and drip campaigns.
- As of 2018-05-03, Neil recommends quizzes (via lead quizzes) as high-converting lead capture, and says all of this can be done free with Hello Bar and scaled with Facebook/YouTube ads once monetization is set up.
