# How Long Does It Take to Boost Your Google SEO Rankings? | 1 Month, 12 Month, and 2 Year Timeline

## Summary
Neil Patel gives his SEO-results timeline, anchored to a specific neilpatel.com growth story: from ~9,000 search visitors/month at launch, to ~89,000/month after 12 months (roughly 10x), to over 450,000/month after 24 months. He caveats that new sites won't match his pace because neilpatel.com wasn't his first rodeo (10+ years of SEO experience). The generic timeline: first 3 months — impressions should rise (check Google Search Console search analytics); by 6 months — impression count up and Google Analytics showing non-brand keyword traffic; within a year — things start kicking in; within two years — the bulk of SEO growth, once authority and trust are built. Critical caveat: these timeframes assume you're actively building links, publishing content, and earning social signals — at minimum one blog post/week, five-to-ten links/month, and weekly content promotion on Facebook/Twitter/other socials with engagement. Without that, it takes even longer.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-20, Neil states:
- neilpatel.com went from ~9,000 search visitors/month at start to ~89,000/month within 12 months (nearly 10x), then to over 450,000/month at 24 months (self-reported, as of 2017-11-20). ★ concrete owned-site growth data point for synthesis
- New sites won't get the same results because when he started neilpatel.com it wasn't his first rodeo — he'd been doing SEO well over 10 years (self-reported, as of 2017-11-20).
- First 3 months — impressions should go up (check Google Search Console search analytics); if not, you're doing something off or you're in a competitive space (as of 2017-11-20).
- By 6 months — impression count should be up, and Google Analytics should show traffic from non-brand keywords (even if not a lot), beyond your existing brand-name traffic (as of 2017-11-20).
- Within a year — things should really start kicking in; within two years — you see the bulk of your SEO growth, having built authority and trust with Google, and start ranking for almost anything (as of 2017-11-20).
- Caveat: if you're not building links, writing a lot of content, or getting social signals, rankings take even longer (as of 2017-11-20).
- To hit these timeframes you need to publish at least one blog post/week, build at least 5–10 links/month, and promote your content every week on Facebook, Twitter, and other social profiles with engagement (as of 2017-11-20).
