# 3 LinkedIn Marketing Tips That Will Generate New Customers and Boost Engagement

## Summary
Neil frames LinkedIn as a powerful tool for corporate/B2B users — not just for finding jobs but for generating leads and customers — and walks through how often to post and what to post. His three tips: post daily (ideally two to three times a day), spread posts out with at least two hours between each one to maximize engagement per post, and test different post types (blog links, native content excerpts with a "continue reading" link back to the blog, and status updates such as inspirational/motivational quotes). He says varying post types builds up engagement and, combined with growing your network of influencers and connections, drives more traffic back to your site.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-27, Neil states:
- LinkedIn is valuable for people in the corporate world not only to find new jobs but to generate leads and customers (as of 2017-09-27).
- You should post on LinkedIn daily, ideally two to three times a day (as of 2017-09-27).
- Spread out your posts by at least two hours between each post; doing so gets more engagement per post (as of 2017-09-27).
- Test different post types on LinkedIn (as of 2017-09-27).
- One post type is plain links from your blog (as of 2017-09-27).
- Another post type is native content: putting the first few paragraphs of a blog post directly on LinkedIn, then adding a "click to continue reading" link that drives people back to your blog (as of 2017-09-27).
- Another post type is basic status updates, such as inspirational or motivational quotes — which Neil finds get the most engagement for him (as of 2017-09-27).
- Trying different post types helps build up your engagement, the number of influencers and friends you're connected to, and — when you post content related to your website — drives more traffic (as of 2017-09-27).
- Following these steps gives you a more popular LinkedIn page and generates more traffic to your site (as of 2017-09-27).
