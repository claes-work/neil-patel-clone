# How to Come Up With Content Ideas That Drive Traffic

## Summary
Solo tactical video demonstrating a competitor-driven content-ideation workflow using
Ubersuggest's "Similar Websites" + "Keyword Gaps" features: enter your own (or a
competitor's) domain, identify genuinely comparable sites (manually excluding
non-relevant ones, e.g. excludes Forbes as too broad), then pull the keyword gap
report to find terms competitors rank for that you don't. Walks through a live example
("podcast advertising") — checking whether he already has a post on the topic before
writing a new one, and prioritizing gap keywords whose top-ranking competitor pages
already pull meaningful estimated traffic (uses the Ubersuggest Chrome extension to
spot-check SERP traffic estimates), rather than assuming rank position alone predicts
traffic. States his own content-production time is split roughly: 10% advanced/
data-heavy content (optimized for social shares and backlinks over search traffic),
40% keyword-gap-driven search-intent content, 20% updating/refreshing old content, and
30% promotion.

## Key claims
- States his content-time allocation is roughly 10% advanced/stat-heavy content
  (optimized for shares/backlinks, not search traffic), 40% keyword-gap-driven search
  content, 20% refreshing old content, and 30% promotion (paraphrase, self-reported
  operating breakdown, dated to this recording).
- States advanced, data/stat-heavy content gets comparatively less SEO traffic but
  more social shares and backlinks per post, illustrated with his own and other blogs'
  data (paraphrase, self-reported, references an internally-cited post with supporting
  charts).
- Recommends checking your own site's existing content library for a topic before
  writing a "new" post targeting a keyword-gap term, to avoid duplicate/cannibalizing
  content (paraphrase, tactical process step).

## Notable verbatim quotes
