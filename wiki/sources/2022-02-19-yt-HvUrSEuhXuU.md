## Summary
A social-media-trends-for-2022 rundown: (1) videos keep getting shorter — cites HubSpot
that 64% of marketers planned to lean into more short-form video in 2022 (Shorts,
Reels, TikTok); (2) social ad creative must get more sophisticated as more advertisers
crowd in — cites Hootsuite that 51.4% of 18,000 surveyed marketers planned to increase
social ad spend in 2022; (3) companies will move from ad-hoc/committee-run social to
hired specialists; (4) platform diversity/omnichannel becomes necessary — cites HubSpot
that 64% of marketers already used 3-5 platforms, and includes a personal anecdote that
his team quietly launched and ran his TikTok account and surprised him with it; (5)
social commerce grows — cites Meta/Facebook research that 81% of people say Instagram
helps them research products, and Hootsuite's "We Are Social" 2021 report ranking
social networks as the second most important brand-research channel after search;
draws an explicit analogy to old TV home-shopping (QVC/Home Shopping Network, name-
checking his mother watching it) as the historical precedent for live-selling on
Facebook.
