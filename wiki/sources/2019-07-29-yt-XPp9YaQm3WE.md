# How to Market Yourself in the Most Competitive Industries

## Summary

Neil argues that the way to win in a hyper-competitive industry (he claims digital
marketing is the most competitive — he competes with every SEO, PPC, email, and blogging
specialist at once) is to **disrupt** rather than copy. His own example: everyone else
sold paid marketing tools, so he released **Ubersuggest for free**, losing "hundreds of
thousands"/"$200,000+ a month," to capture eyeballs — even if the product isn't as
feature-complete. His four rules for disruption: (1) you **don't need a lot of money** to
disrupt (his own spend is not a requirement for you); (2) **research customers** — survey
people to learn what the industry lacks and why they hate incumbents (Slack cited: beat
Skype by building a better product from customer feedback, ~20–25% of free users upgraded
to paid per Mary Meeker's report); (3) **go all in** — push hard, generate press, "scream
from the rooftops," don't fear haters; (4) **execute super fast** and learn from mistakes
because well-funded competitors will try to crush you. The payoff is **brand queries** —
people searching your name directly (Mailchimp and Apple cited as brand-driven winners
that don't rely on ranking for everything).
