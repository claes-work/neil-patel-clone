# This is What Your Marketing Mindset Should ALWAYS Be

## Summary
States that across 20+ years in digital marketing, the one constant through every
trend has been the customer, and that marketing should always be about offering
value to the audience, not just driving conversions. Frames the pandemic as having
shifted customer expectations toward flexibility, then gives a five-step playbook to
meet that: (1) leverage UGC (unboxing videos for physical products, testimonials/
reviews for services) — cites 60% of people saying customer-created content is the
most authentic; (2) offer a seamless online/service experience — cites 68% of
customers willing to pay more for good service, illustrated with a Comcast-to-Xfinity
rebrand-didn't-fix-bad-service anecdote and Netflix/Disney+ as the eventual
replacement; (3) invest in recurring customers — self-reports NP Digital's data that
acquiring a new customer is 5–25x more expensive than retaining one; (4) use
analytics (Crazy Egg named) to find and fix drop-off points; (5) survey the audience
(SurveyMonkey named) to find and fix problems, citing 80% of companies using
satisfaction scores.

## Key claims
- Self-reports 20+ years in digital marketing, framing the customer as the one
  constant across every trend (self-reported, dated 2023-01-23).
- Cites (source unnamed, as cited): 60% of people say customer-created content is the
  most authentic; 68% of customers will pay more for good service; 72% of companies
  believe analytics reports can improve customer experience; 80% of companies use
  satisfaction scores to analyze/improve customer experience.
- Self-reports NP Digital's internal data that acquiring a new customer costs
  "anywhere from five to almost 25 times more expensive" than retaining an existing
  one (self-reported, dated 2023-01-23).
- Uses a Comcast-rebranded-to-Xfinity anecdote as a cautionary tale that rebranding
  doesn't fix an underlying bad-service problem, self-reporting he was a Comcast
  customer for years before switching to Netflix/Disney+.

## Notable verbatim quotes
