# How to Convince Other Sites to Send You Free Traffic

## Summary
Neil argues that for a brand-new online business, the easiest source of traffic is other websites that already have it, because paid ads are expensive and SEO is slow to kick in. The core mechanism is a win-win offer: instead of asking a site to send traffic (which he says he ignores when it lands in his inbox) or paying up front for visitors, you propose affiliate marketing — pay the referring site a percentage only when they actually drive a sale, so unit traffic (100 or 1,000 visitors) costs nothing and only conversions cost money. He adds that big sites won't sign up first: you must prove the model works with smaller sites, then move up to medium sites, then large ones. Some sites will also link or send traffic for free because they like your content or want to cite it, but the sales-driving referrals come from sites that already have their visitors' trust and can tell them to buy from you.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-07, Neil states:
- The easiest way to get traffic for a starting online business is from other websites that already have it, since paid ads are expensive and SEO takes a long time to kick in (as of 2017-09-07).
- Getting a site to send you traffic has to be a win-win situation; a random ask to send traffic or link out gives them no reason to do it, and he ignores such emails (as of 2017-09-07).
- Paying up front for traffic is undesirable when you're just starting; a better structure is paying only for sales the referrer drives, which is affiliate marketing (as of 2017-09-07).
- Under an affiliate arrangement you pay nothing for the visitors sent (whether 100 or 1,000) and pay only a percentage on each sale they generate, so zero sales means zero cost (as of 2017-09-07).
- Big websites won't adopt the arrangement right away — they want proof it converts before risking their traffic — so you must first convince smaller sites, then medium-sized sites, then bigger sites (as of 2017-09-07).
- Some websites will drive you visitors for free because they want to promote content they like or cite information on your blog (as of 2017-09-07).
- Sites that already have trust with their visitors drive sales, because when a trusted site tells its audience to buy from you they are much more likely to act (as of 2017-09-07).
