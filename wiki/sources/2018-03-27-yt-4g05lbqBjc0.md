# 3 Marketing Mistakes You MUST Avoid | Marketing Tips and Tricks for Startups to Follow

_Neil solo, direct-to-camera. All persona data._

## Summary
Neil frames three startup marketing mistakes around his own **KISSmetrics** failure: he
**raised well over $10M** for it and **burned over $4M before earning the first dollar of
revenue**, spending years building the product before marketing it — only to hear "Neil, I
don't understand why I need this."

1. **Don't over-build before marketing/validating.** Test the market first — spend a few
   hundred/thousand dollars, or just **pick up the phone** and try to **pre-sell** (collect
   money even without a product, then **refund** — apologize, maybe send a Starbucks gift
   card). This tells you whether people will actually pay.
2. **Don't market to everyone.** Pick a **niche** — even mass-market products (laundry
   detergent) target specific segments (moms, babies, organic buyers). His example:
   **ConvertKit** failed trying to out-position Mailchimp as "better email marketing," then
   **niched to bloggers** and hit **$10M/year within two years**.
3. **Don't prioritize branding over customer acquisition (early).** Why build a brand with no
   customers? **Acquire customers first** — throw "spaghetti at the wall" across **Facebook
   ads, Google ads, SEO, content marketing, podcast advertising** and double down on positive
   ROI. Real **branding is a byproduct** of customers using and loving the product and telling
   others (Amazon and Google built brands via product experience and word-of-mouth, not early
   TV — "no one's talking about Yahoo these days").

## Key claims
As of 2018-03-27, Neil states:
- **KISSmetrics**: he **raised well over $10M** and **burned over $4M before first revenue**,
  having spent **years building** before marketing — a major financial mistake.
- **Mistake 1 — validate before building**: test the market cheaply or by phone, and **pre-sell
  / collect money** (refunding if needed) to confirm real demand before heavy build.
- **Mistake 2 — niche down**: don't market to everyone; even universal products pick a segment.
  **ConvertKit** only succeeded after niching to **bloggers**, reaching **$10M/year in two
  years**. Dominate a niche first, then expand.
- **Mistake 3 — acquire customers before brand-building**: spread spend across FB/Google/SEO/
  content/podcast ads ("spaghetti on the wall"), keep what has ROI; **brand is built by
  customers loving the product and word-of-mouth**, not logos or early TV (cites Amazon,
  Google vs. Yahoo/Bing/MSN).
