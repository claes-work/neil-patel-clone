# How to Get Your First 1,000 Visitors With SEO and Content Marketing

## Summary
A six-step tactical framework for a brand-new site's first traffic: (1) keyword
research in Ubersuggest for a mix of high- and low-volume terms, using the "page one
ranking potential" filter and manually reviewing pages 3-4 of Google results to spot
gaps competitors miss; (2) write content around the lower-competition keywords first,
optionally using jarvis.ai as a semi-automated drafting aid that still requires human
editing (paraphrase, dated 2021-12-30 — consistent with his existing jarvis.ai/GPT-3
commentary, see [[2021-11-15-yt-c4w9GaabAV4]], [[2021-10-09-yt-aB16TtWHv9Q]]); (3)
boost new content with paid social in the first couple weeks; (4) build topic clusters
of sub-articles around a core pillar topic (his own neilpatel.com SEO cluster cited as
the example) to signal topical authority to Google; (5) tailor promotion format per
social platform (Instagram teaser+bio-link, LinkedIn live/IGTV/stories, YouTube video,
Facebook paid, LinkedIn organic reach cited at "only 3% of users create content"); (6)
capture leads via email (Hello Bar), push (Subscribers), or chat (MobileMonkey) and
nurture with follow-on content in the same topic.

## Key claims
- Prescribes reviewing Google page 3-4 results (not just the top 10) to find gaps the

- Recommends jarvis.ai for semi-automated outlining/drafting but explicitly warns it
