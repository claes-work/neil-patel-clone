# Why You Should Never Buy Facebook Fans

## Summary
Neil confesses that he bought most of his ~900,000 Facebook fans — not shadily, but through Facebook advertising (fan-page ads plus post boosting) — spending roughly $400,000 in total, and says he wishes he never had them. The reason: as Facebook keeps tightening its algorithm, organic reach to fans collapses, so accumulated fans become worthless unless you keep paying to "boost" posts to reach them. He shows this happened to his own traffic. His rule: keep using Facebook advertising to sell products/services (it converts and makes direct money) and boost posts when useful, but never spend ad dollars buying fans/likes to a fan page — that money is "flushed down the toilet."

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-24, Neil states:
- His Facebook fan page had over 900,000 fans, and he bought most of them via Facebook advertising — a mix of fan-page ads and post boosting — spending roughly **$400,000** in total (as of 2017-07-24; self-reported).
- When he started buying fans he was getting well over **100,000 visitors/month** from Facebook; that traffic has since dropped **below 50,000** and keeps falling, because Facebook continually tightens its algorithm (as of 2017-07-24; self-reported).
- This decay is not unique to him — it hits anyone with a fan page; Facebook makes it harder and harder to grow a fan page and to get traffic from it, which pushes companies to advertise more (as of 2017-07-24).
- Facebook advertising itself is good — Neil still spends "a lot" on it because it converts and makes direct money — the mistake is spending ad dollars to acquire fans/likes rather than to sell (as of 2017-07-24).
- "Boosting" a post means paying Facebook so that all of a page's fans actually see that post; boosting is fine, buying fans is not (as of 2017-07-24).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The Neil Patel Facebook fan page has over 900,000 fans. That's a lot of fans. You know what, I wish I never had 900,000 fans."

> "Use Facebook advertising to sell your products and services because it works amazingly well. Just don't use your advertising dollars to send people to your fan page to like it. If you do that, you're just going to be throwing money down the drain."
