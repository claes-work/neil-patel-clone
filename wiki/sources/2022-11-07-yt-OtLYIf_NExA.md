# Here's What Facebook and Google Aren't Telling You About Your Ads

## Summary
Explains multi-channel attribution as the "issue" platforms don't surface: an
omnichannel marketing approach (invoking the "rule of seven" — a prospect needs ~7
brand touches before converting) makes it hard to know which channel actually drove a
conversion, because each ad platform (Google, Facebook, etc.) is incentivized to claim
credit for conversions it touched, even late in a multi-touch journey that started
elsewhere. Walks a hypothetical customer journey (LinkedIn → email → Google paid ad →
Twitter → purchase) to illustrate the problem, then runs through standard attribution
models: Last Click, Time Decay, Linear, Position-Based, First Click, and Data-Driven.
States he personally uses Position-Based (credit split between first and last touch)
because it answers "what are the main two drivers of the conversion." Gives a concrete
walkthrough of changing the attribution model in Google Analytics (Admin → Attribution
Settings → choose model). Closes with an NP Digital data-science-team plug.

## Key claims
- States the "rule of seven" (a customer needs ~7 brand interactions before converting)
  as the rationale for omnichannel marketing (industry framework, cited without a
  specific source, dated 2022-11-07).
- Claims ad platforms (Google, Facebook, "pretty much all paid platforms") default to
  attributing conversions to their own channel/last click even when other channels
  contributed earlier in the journey (Neil's stated observation/critique, dated
  2022-11-07).
- States his personal preference for the Position-Based attribution model, crediting
  first touch (discovery) and last touch (final conversion source) (self-reported
  practice, dated 2022-11-07).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> There's nothing wrong with this, but you ideally need to attribute your conversion to multiple channels to figure out what's accurately driving you revenue and what isn't.

> I typically use Position-Based myself which typically gives credit to the first touch and the last touch.

> Now you'll get a better idea of what is driving your revenue.
