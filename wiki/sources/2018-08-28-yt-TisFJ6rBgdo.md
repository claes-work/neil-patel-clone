# How To Build a Dream Marketing Team (From Scratch)

## Summary

Solo tactical on hiring your first marketing team, split by budget. **If you have money**:
hire someone who has **already done it for a competitor** and *proven* results — but
interrogate specifics ("what were *you* in charge of, what results did *you* produce"),
and in the interview make them **show, not tell** how they'd execute (e.g. "if you'll
blog: who writes, how do you find writers, how do you promote?") — the nitty-gritty reveals
whether they actually drove that company's growth and reduces the risk of wasting six
months. Do **reference checks with previous** employers (not the current competitor), and
ask the right questions — not "would you hire them?" but "what are their strengths,
weaknesses, and areas to improve?" **If you're scrappy/no money**: recruit **interns** from
local colleges (he's in Orange County / LA County — names UCLA, USC), paid minimum wage or
unpaid, and train them on free resources (his videos/articles, Moz, Backlinko). Screen for
people who **learn fast, are self-sufficient/independent, want to stick around, and are a
cultural fit** (shared values → more likely to convert intern → paid role). Key coaching
insight: don't just dump resources and say "go execute" — inexperienced people won't know
where to start; have them **research case studies** of competitors/similar businesses
(names GrowthHackers.com) and copy the step-by-step playbooks, then later fund conference
training (names SMX). Overall principle: **start with one person** regardless of budget —
with money, that hire builds the rest of the team; without, that one intern gets you to
revenue you can reinvest in expansion.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-28, if funded, hire the person who **produced proven results at a
  competitor** — dig into their specific responsibilities and make them demonstrate
  execution in the interview (show, don't tell) to de-risk the hire.
- As of 2018-08-28, do reference checks with **previous** employers (not the current
  competitor) and ask for strengths/weaknesses/areas-to-improve rather than "would you hire
  them again."
- As of 2018-08-28, if bootstrapped, recruit local-college **interns** (he names UCLA/USC in
  Orange County/LA), paid-minimum or unpaid, trained on free content (his blog/videos, Moz,
  Backlinko).
- As of 2018-08-28, screen interns/hires for fast learning, self-sufficiency, staying power,
  and **cultural fit** (shared values) — cultural fit predicts intern→paid-role conversion.
- As of 2018-08-28, don't just hand over resources and say "execute" — have inexperienced
  hires study **case studies** (names GrowthHackers.com) of competitors/similar businesses
  and copy the step-by-step, then fund conference training (names SMX) once revenue allows.
- As of 2018-08-28, **start with one person** either way: a funded senior hire builds the
  rest of the team; a single intern gets you to reinvestable revenue.
