# Should You Spend Time on Meta Tags?

## Summary
Neil's guidance on how much time to spend on meta tags, tiered by page maturity. The **meta keywords tag is useless** — Google ignored it years ago, confirmed by his own testing and other SEOs. For a brand-new page (which won't rank yet), spend no more than ~3 minutes on the title tag + meta description combined, optimizing for click-through appeal: short titles (~6–7 words), meta descriptions ~120–140 characters (Yoast SEO makes this easy in WordPress). Once a page is ~6–7 months old and getting traffic, invest 5–10 minutes: pull the keywords driving traffic from Google Search Console and work some of those phrases into the title and description to climb rankings over time. With experience the whole task takes seconds to a minute.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-22, Neil states:
- There are three meta tags: title, description, and meta keywords. The **meta keywords tag is useless** — Google said years ago it ignores it; his own extensive testing (and other marketers'/SEOs') confirms it doesn't help rankings one bit (as of 2017-07-22).
- A brand-new page won't really rank, so spend no more than a few minutes (~3 minutes total) creating its title tag and meta description; focus on making them appealing so people click through (as of 2017-07-22).
- Title tags: keep them short, ~6–7 words. Meta descriptions: ~120–140 characters; getting cut off sometimes is not a big deal, especially early (as of 2017-07-22).
- The Yoast SEO plugin gives a box to set a custom title tag and meta description per page on WordPress (as of 2017-07-22).
- Once a page is ~6–7 months old ("seasoned") and getting traffic, go into Google Search Console, find the pages getting the most clicks, and spend more time (~5–10 minutes max) modifying their title tag and meta description (as of 2017-07-22).
- Search Console shows the keywords driving traffic to a page; integrate some of those phrases into the title/description and rankings will climb over time (as of 2017-07-22).
- With experience (like him), you can create a title + meta description in seconds to a minute regardless of the page's age (as of 2017-07-22).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The meta keywords tag is useless. Do not use it. Reason being, Google has said years and years ago that they ignore meta keywords tags."

> "When you're first releasing a page, don't spend more than three minutes to create your meta tags. Once a page is over six months old, and it's getting traffic, you can spend anywhere from five to 10 minutes."

> "In Google search console, it will show you all the keywords that are driving traffic to that page. Consider integrating some of those phrases and words within the title tag and meta description."
