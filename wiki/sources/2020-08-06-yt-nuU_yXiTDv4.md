# Rank In Competitive Markets With Topical Clusters - Module 2 - Lesson 2 - Content Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week-two lesson 2 of **Content Marketing Unlocked** ([[2020-08-03-yt-U0ekBz_wWwI]]):
explains the topic-cluster/pillar-page model — one main "pillar" page on a broad
subject (e.g., SEO) linking out to detailed subpages on specific subtopics (e.g.,
link-building, on-page SEO), with subpages linking back to the pillar. Uses toy
examples (cats, dog collars) to illustrate. Frames clusters as a direct lever on
Google's **E-A-T** (expertise, authority, trust) signal, and claims his own ranking
for competitive SEO keywords is evidence the model works. Workflow: find cluster
candidates via Ubersuggest's Content Ideas report (by social shares, backlinks,
estimated organic visits), identify recurring subtopics among top-ranking
competitor content, and rewrite/expand into a structured cluster with internal
linking (natural anchor text) and standard on-page SEO (metadata, alt tags, schema).
Points viewers to his own neilpatel.com "what is SEO" article and "SEO Unlocked"
course as worked examples.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-08-06, Neil framed topic clusters as a direct lever for Google's E-A-T
  (expertise, authority, trust) signal, claiming his own high rankings for
  competitive SEO keywords demonstrate the model's effectiveness (self-reported,
  no independent ranking data cited in this video).
- As of 2020-08-06, Neil's recommended cluster-building workflow: use Ubersuggest's
  Content Ideas report to find high-performing competitor content, identify
  recurring subtopics, then build a pillar page linking to detailed subpages that
  link back to the pillar.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Topic clusters work... if it works for me and other people in some of the most competitive niches, like SEO, it will work for any other niche out there."

> "When you get very specific around that with your keywords, and you're focusing each subtopic... they're all more likely to rank higher because they're all feeding into one topic."
