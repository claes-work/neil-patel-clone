# How I Built a Website That Generates 4 Million Visitors a Month

## Summary
Neil Patel explains that the single lever behind his website's growth to four million monthly visitors is a deliberate focus on long-tail keywords rather than a constant hunt for brand-new head terms. He frames long-tail keywords as highly targeted search phrases (usually three or more words) that map closely to searcher intent, and that typically carry low search volume, low competition, and high conversion rates. His core method is to take keywords he already ranks for — including smaller ones — and find long-tail variations worth pursuing. He gives five reasons he favors this approach: long-tail terms are easier to rank for and communicate a clear customer need; they are less competitive, and his agency NP Digital (with its SMB division NPD Accelerate) sees results two to three times faster on them than on head terms; they are cheaper than pay-per-click advertising for Google Ads users; they are well suited to semantic voice search; and they make how-to content strategies easy to build. He closes with two free-plan tools for discovering long-tail keywords: AnswerThePublic, where a one- or two-word seed returns the questions people ask (pulled from Google Suggest), and Ubersuggest, where a seed keyword can surface up to a million related long-tail phrases. He positions this traffic as feeding meaningful leads and ROI for his agency.
