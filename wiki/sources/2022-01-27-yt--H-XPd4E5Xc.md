# How I Turn a Boring Product Into a Marketing Success Behind My Most Successful Campaigns

## Summary
Five tips for marketing a "boring" or unremarkable product/service: (1) create a
narrative around the problem that led to the solution — people respond to
story arcs, not features; (2) anchor the pitch to the customer's problems/needs/wants
and the emotional payoff (status, ease, relief), not the product itself; (3) make
abstract products (e.g. online courses) visual — show what's inside, walk through the
unboxing/using/results experience, since this measurably improves conversions; (4) use
humor even for boring categories, citing Old Spice's "man on a horse" campaign and
Dollar Shave Club's launch video as examples of humor turning commodity products
(deodorant, razors) into standout brands; (5) paint a picture of the status/emotional
elevation the solution provides — includes a personal aside about his grandmother
enjoying looking good to her friends, and people who own Ferraris privately enjoying
the feeling without posting about it publicly.
