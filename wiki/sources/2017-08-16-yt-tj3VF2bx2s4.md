# Why You Shouldn't Boost Your Facebook Posts

## Summary
A short paid-ads video in which Neil Patel argues against boosting Facebook posts for the
"99%" of advertisers who aren't expert copywriters. He gives three reasons: (1) boosted
traffic stops the moment you stop paying — there's no lasting return; (2) boosts usually
send people to a blog post, and blog posts (built to be funny, story-driven, or
educational) rarely convert visitors into customers; and (3) the spend is better used
driving traffic to a paid product, service, or a landing page built to convert, where
you at least have a higher chance of generating revenue on the spot. He is careful to
clarify he is NOT saying to avoid Facebook advertising altogether — Facebook advertising
that drives people to a product/service/landing page does work; it's specifically post
boosting that tends to get "flushed right down the drain." He is skeptical of the
"collect emails from boosted blog traffic then monetize the list" funnel, saying he
doesn't know one in 100 marketers who runs it correctly.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-16, Neil states:
- If you're an amazing marketer and great at copywriting you can boost posts, but for the
  ~99% who aren't amazing copywriters, don't boost posts (as of 2017-08-16).
- Reason 1 — Boosted posts give you traffic only while you pay; once you stop paying, the
  traffic stops (as of 2017-08-16).
- Reason 2 — Boosts typically send people to a blog post, and blog posts are built to be
  funny/story-driven/educational, not to convert visitors into customers, so most people
  won't see an ROI (as of 2017-08-16).
- The "collect emails from that traffic and monetize the list later" funnel is very hard
  to make work; Neil says he doesn't know even one in 100 marketers able to do it
  correctly (self-reported) (as of 2017-08-16).
- Reason 3 — Boosting costs money that would be better spent driving traffic to your paid
  product/service, where you have a higher chance of generating revenue right then and
  there (as of 2017-08-16).
- This is NOT a blanket "don't advertise on Facebook" message: Facebook advertising that
  drives people to a product, service, or a conversion-oriented landing page does work —
  it's boosting posts specifically that usually just gets wasted (as of 2017-08-16).
