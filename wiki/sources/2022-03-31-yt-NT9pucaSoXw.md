# Instagram Content Strategy 101 (How I Took My Instagram From 0 to 300,000 Followers)

## Summary
Neil Patel walks through how he grew his Instagram account from zero to 300,000 followers through purely organic growth — no paid ads and, he stresses, no buying likes, followers, or comments. He frames the journey as bumpy, with more downs than ups, but says the lessons he learned made the good moments count and drove significant follower gains. He argues the platform is still a viable opportunity despite rising competition, citing Instagram's roughly 1.393 billion global users (as of October 2021) and 20.3% year-over-year growth. His strategy is staged by follower milestone: early on he experimented by repurposing content from other channels (YouTube, Facebook) and testing image-based content until he found what worked; at 50,000 followers he layered in influencer partnerships within the same niche (going live together and reposting to cross-pollinate audiences), Twitter-quote screenshots, and image carousels built like infographics broken into five or six swipeable slides (which he notes can be made free in Canva). At 200,000 followers he shifted toward Reels — doubling down as the format was booming and learning by trial which topics resonated — while continuing partnerships with bigger influencers. He also describes an evolution in how he ran partnership lives: originally reciprocal (he goes live on their profile, they go live on his), but once his audience was large enough he would only appear on the other person's account without reciprocating, which brought in followers while sparing his own audience from live fatigue.

## Key claims
- Neil grew his Instagram from zero to 300,000 followers through purely organic growth, without ads and without buying likes, followers, or comments (as of 2022-03-31).
- The growth journey was uneven, with a lot of downs and a few ups, but the lessons learned made the ups high and drove a large follower gain (as of 2022-03-31).
- Instagram has at least 1.393 billion users worldwide (based on global advertising audience reach numbers as of October 2021), and grew 20.3% over 12 months — roughly 235 million new users (as of 2022-03-31).
- It is not too late to start on Instagram; new accounts grow from zero to thousands of followers every day, though reaching a million right away is unrealistic (as of 2022-03-31).
- In the earliest stage, Neil experimented by taking content from other channels like YouTube and Facebook and posting it on Instagram, and also tested image-based content, until he found the content types that worked best (as of 2022-03-31).
- At 50,000 followers, Neil began doing partnerships with influencers in the same niche (not a different niche): they went live together and reposted, so his followers would follow their profiles and their followers would follow his (as of 2022-03-31).
- Also around 50,000 followers, Neil added simpler tactics: Twitter screenshots (putting a quote on Twitter, screenshotting it, and posting it on Instagram) and image carousels — an infographic broken into five or six swipeable slides, which can be made free in Canva (as of 2022-03-31).
- At 200,000 followers, Neil started doing Reels and doubled down on them as the format was starting to boom, learning by trial which topics people love and which they don't (as of 2022-03-31).
- Neil is not sure Reels even existed when he first started on Instagram (as of 2022-03-31).
- At the later stage, Neil doubled down on partnerships that were working and pursued bigger influencers (as of 2022-03-31).
- Neil evolved his live-partnership approach: originally reciprocal cross-promotion (he goes live on their profile, they on his), but once his audience was large enough he would only go live on their profile and have them on his — appearing only on the other person's account without reciprocating (as of 2022-03-31).
- Appearing only on partners' accounts brought in a lot of followers and meant Neil didn't have to go live as often with his own audience, avoiding audience fatigue (as of 2022-03-31).
