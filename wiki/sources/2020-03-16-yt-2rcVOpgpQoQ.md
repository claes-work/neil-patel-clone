# Why I Stopped Doing Link Building (And Should You Too?)

## Summary
Neil says he stopped actively link-building almost three years before this video
(implying ~2017) because his site now earns backlinks passively while traffic kept
growing. He gives four reasons: (1) brand building — once a brand is established,
links accrue naturally; he invokes the omnichannel/"rule of seven" idea (seven brand
touches build bonding/trust) as the reason to be present across social, SEO, content,
email, push notifications, chatbots, and text; (2) a large evergreen content library
(hundreds/thousands of articles) that keeps earning links over years; (3) building a
free tool — Ubersuggest, which he says earns 25,000+ backlinks from 3,400+ referring
domains by giving away what competitors charge $100/mo–$1,200/yr for, and suggests
smaller businesses buy a cheap tool template from CodeCanyon ($10–20) to replicate the
effect; (4) original research/studies — he cites launching two industry studies the
prior year (a content-marketing study of 183 companies with 17 charts, and a social
-traffic study polling 483 companies) that together generated 97,000 backlinks from
119 referring domains. He then gives a decision framework: stop active link building
once you have social following, existing rankings, brand recognition, earned press/
speaking/podcast mentions, and linkable tools/content; keep building links if the site
is under two years old, has little organic traffic/backlinks, doesn't rank for
long-tail terms, and has no linkable assets yet. Established giants (Nike, Ford, BMW)
don't need to build links at all; a business can reach that status in its own niche.

  content-marketing study of 183 companies with 17 charts, and a social-traffic study

  interacts with a brand seven times they're far more likely to bond, convert, and
  link back — reasoning already recorded from earlier sources (cf.

- As of 2020-03-16, Neil gave an explicit decision framework for when to stop vs. keep
  building links, keyed to site age, existing rankings, brand recognition, and earned
  media/linkable assets.
- As of 2020-03-16, Neil said businesses without a big tool-development budget can buy
  a cheap interactive-tool template from CodeCanyon ($10–20) to build a linkable asset.
