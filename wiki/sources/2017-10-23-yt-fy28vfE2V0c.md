# 5 Tips For Increasing Your Google Adwords Quality Score | Save Money on Your PPC Ads

## Summary
Neil walks through five tactics for raising a Google AdWords Quality Score in order to lower cost per click on PPC ads. He frames Quality Score as tied to Google's own revenue optimization: a higher click-through rate signals relevance, which both raises Quality Score and reduces what advertisers pay. The five moves are targeting very specific keywords via exact match rather than broad match, adding negative keywords to filter irrelevant searches, grouping similar keywords so ad text stays relevant to each group, continuously testing ad text against competitors, and optimizing the landing page. He recommends spying on competitor ad copy directly in the SERP or via SEMrush's bidding history, and using Crazy Egg to see where visitors get stuck or bounce on a landing page.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-23, Neil states:
- Google is worth well over 500 or 600 billion dollars, largely because advertisers rely on Google AdWords ads (as of 2017-10-23).
- A higher AdWords Quality Score means a lower cost per click (as of 2017-10-23).
- AdWords charges only when someone clicks an ad; no click means no payment to Google (as of 2017-10-23).
- Targeting very specific keywords with exact match shows ads only for that keyword or phrase, while going too broad raises cost per click by showing ads to people who do not click (as of 2017-10-23).
- Negative keywords should be added to exclude irrelevant searches (e.g., a computer reseller bidding on "Apple" adding "food" as a negative keyword) to avoid wasted clicks, falling Quality Score, and rising ad costs (as of 2017-10-23).
- Grouping similar keywords lets advertisers write relevant ad text per group; putting all keywords in one campaign with the same ad text makes copy less relevant, lowering click-through rate and raising cost per click (as of 2017-10-23).
- Google optimizes for its own revenue, so a higher click-through rate makes ads cheaper and improves Quality Score (as of 2017-10-23).
- Continuously testing ad text so it out-clicks competitors lowers cost per click and raises Quality Score (as of 2017-10-23).
- Competitor ad text can be studied by searching a keyword and viewing top ads, or by entering a keyword in SEMrush to see the history of bidders and their ad copy (as of 2017-10-23).
- An excellent landing page raises Quality Score, while a page people bounce off of causes Google to ding the Quality Score (as of 2017-10-23).
- Placing the target keyword on the landing page (e.g., a "marketing" keyword matching a page that sells marketing) makes visitors more likely to stay and convert (as of 2017-10-23).
- Crazy Egg can be used to see where people get stuck on a landing page, how far they scroll, and what to improve for a better user experience (as of 2017-10-23).
