# This Social Network's Organic Reach Will Drop Dramatically in 2023 - Here's Why

## Summary
In this Neil-fronted solo video, Neil Patel argues that Instagram is the next social network whose organic reach will collapse the way Facebook's did after 2014, and he predicts a drastic drop starting in 2020 (the video's title says "2023," but the transcript only ever says 2020 — a title/caption mismatch). His thesis: Instagram is saturating (from 90M active users in Jan 2013 to over 1B by June 2018, with growth slowing from 13.1% in 2018 to a projected 4% in 2022), so competition for feed space intensifies while parent company Facebook wants to grow ad inventory (Instagram expected at ~25% of Facebook's total ad revenue in 2019, rising to ~30% globally by 2020) — squeezing organic reach to push advertisers to pay. He then gives a fix list: (1) use paid ads to boost content that's working and prime the algorithm; (2) go live every single day; (3) engage via comments with thorough replies; (4) constantly test which content formats work (his own small-image-plus-big-quote format was outperforming big images of himself); (5) respond to every direct message to build a loyal, engaged base; and (6) partner/co-produce content with similarly-sized Instagram influencers for multi-profile distribution. He closes by pitching his agency, Neil Patel Digital.
