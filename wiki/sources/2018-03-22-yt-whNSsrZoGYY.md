# 1 Simple Social Media Strategy for Getting Attention in a 'BORING' Niche

_Q&A Thursday; **Adam** (Viewership) hosts, question from viewer **Ben** (a UK mortgage
broker). Only Neil-attributed material is persona data; Adam's channel stats are attributed
to Adam._

## Summary
Neil answers how to do social media in a "boring" B2B industry. First principle: **don't chase
vanity views — chase the right audience** ("what's the point of millions of views and no
revenue?"). He recounts declining a viral-video pitch (someone offered to make him popular
with a "cooking with Hennessy" style video) because comedic virality wouldn't convert his
Fortune-1000 B2B audience. The tactical play for a dull niche (mortgages): create
**data/comparison infographics** — e.g. "most expensive cities to live in," home prices, cost
of food/transport, school districts, crime — which are inherently shareable "because everyone
has to live somewhere," even for cities you don't service. Distribution + SEO stack:
- Embed the infographic with the **EP Embed Code Generator** WordPress plugin so re-publishers
  **link back** to you (backlinks → Google rankings).
- No following yet? Use **BuzzSumo** → search related content → **View Shares** → **manually
  outreach** to sharers with a short pitch offering your (more visual) infographic.
- For backlinks, use **Ahrefs** to see who links to competing articles and pitch them the same
  way. Expect **~100 emails → ~5 links** ("it's a quantity game").

He frames combining the **social strategy + link strategy** as a win-win (social traffic +
Google traffic from the same asset), and closes that being in a boring industry is an
*advantage* because few competitors put themselves out there — which is exactly why he got
into video marketing himself.

## Key claims
As of 2018-03-22, Neil states:
- **Reject vanity metrics**: views without the right audience/revenue are pointless; a viral
  comedic video would not serve his **B2B / Fortune-1000** audience.
- **Boring-niche play = shareable data infographics** (most expensive/cheapest cities to live,
  home prices, school districts, crime) — shareable because the topic is universal.
- **Embed for backlinks**: use the **EP Embed Code Generator** (WordPress) so re-users link
  back, boosting Google rankings.
- **No audience yet → BuzzSumo + manual outreach**: find who shared competing content ("View
  Shares"), email them offering your more-visual asset; they'll often share it.
- **Backlinks via Ahrefs**: find sites linking to competing articles, pitch yours; **~100
  emails yields ~5 links** — a quantity game.
- **Combine social + SEO** from one content asset for compounding traffic.
- Being in a **boring industry is a differentiation advantage** — most competitors won't put
  themselves out there.
