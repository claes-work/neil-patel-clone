# This One Chrome Extension Will Boost Your Rankings

## Summary
A short product-tutorial video pitching the free Ubersuggest Chrome extension as a way to fold SEO into
daily browsing habits rather than a separate task people forget to do. Neil Patel notes Google sees
roughly 5.6 billion searches a day, so tying SEO tooling to searches you're already doing means you
never have to remember to "do SEO" separately. He walks through what the extension surfaces during a
normal Google search: search volume for the query plus related keyword ideas with their own volumes in
the sidebar; and, for any competitor result, that page's backlink count, social shares, and the keywords
it ranks for (primary and secondary), useful for building out content that targets the same keyword
cluster. He draws a distinction between obsessing over specific competitors versus staying aware of "the
competition" generally, saying he checks competitor sites periodically but doesn't fixate on them. Clicking
the extension's "U" button on any site pulls up that site's full traffic, technical issues, ranking
keywords, and backlinks — used to find new link targets, keyword gaps, and technical issues competitors
have that create an opening. Standard NP Digital agency pitch and like/share ask close the video.

## Key claims
- Google processes roughly 5.6 billion searches per day, the stated rationale for embedding SEO research
  into the search habit itself via a browser extension (as of 2022-06-25).
- The Ubersuggest Chrome extension is free and shows, inline on Google search results: query search
  volume, related keyword ideas with volumes, and per-result competitor data (backlinks, social shares,
  ranking keywords) (as of 2022-06-25).
- Neil says he checks competitor sites "every once in a while" but describes himself as focused on "the
  competition" broadly rather than obsessing over any one competitor (as of 2022-06-25, self-reported
  habit).
- Clicking the extension's button on any site surfaces that site's traffic, site issues, ranking
  keywords, and backlinks — usable to find outreach targets, keyword gaps, and technical weaknesses to
  exploit (as of 2022-06-25).
