# How to Stand Out From the Crowd in 2018 | 4 Secret Social Media Marketing Tips

## Summary
With Facebook/Google ad prices rising and social feeds crowded, Neil gives four ways to
stand out. (1) **Leverage new innovative channels and features** — platforms constantly
copy each other (Facebook Watch is Facebook chasing YouTube), and early on they hand out
free reach to pull creators over, so ride new features/platforms. (2) **Be transparent** —
audiences see through hype (leased-Ferrari flexing with disclaimers); he holds up **Pat
Flynn / Smart Passive Income** publishing real income reports (good months and bad) as
the model, and cites his own public journeys on the NP blog (0→100K→1M visitors/month)
and on his former site Nutrition Secrets (0→$100K/month) — sharing wins *and* failures
built loyalty. (3) **Use video** — people tell Neil his *video* content resonates most,
even though his blog gets far more traffic, because video builds a deeper personal
connection. (4) **Be a long-term thinker** — cites Gary Vaynerchuk's arc (Wine Library TV
→ agency → DailyVee) and his own 16 years; treat marketing as a **3+ year plan**, expect
traction after year one, growth after year two, "skyrocketing" after year three — but only
with consistency. Reinforces Neil's transparency, video-first, and long-game/consistency
themes.

## Key claims
As of 2018-01-13, Neil states:
- **Ride new channels and features early** — platforms copy each other (Facebook Watch =
  Facebook's YouTube play) and give **free reach** to creators who cross-post, to lure
  their content over; it doesn't have to be a new platform, just new features (as of 2018-01-13).
- **Be transparent** — people "see right through" hype and disclaimers (leased Ferrari,
  "results not typical"); lying about outcomes "is just going to make people hate you"
  (as of 2018-01-13).
- Transparency model: **Pat Flynn / Smart Passive Income** publishes real numbers,
  good months and bad, and it made him one of the most popular bloggers/podcasters
  (as of 2018-01-13).
- Neil applied the same on his own sites — sharing his journey **0 → 100K → 1M
  visitors/month** on the NP blog, and **0 → $100K/month** on his former site **Nutrition
  Secrets** (which he says he no longer owns), wins and losses alike (as of 2018-01-13). ★ persona/biography
- **Video wins connection**: people praise his video content over his blog or podcast
  even though the blog gets "way more traffic," because video "helps me build a deeper
  connection with you"; "it's all about videos" now, and phones are why video is so
  popular (as of 2018-01-13).
- **Think long term**: Gary Vaynerchuk wasn't overnight (Wine Library TV → agency →
  DailyVee); Neil has done this "over 16 years." Treat it as a **3+ year plan** — some
  traction after 6–12 months, growth after year two, "skyrocketing" after year three —
  *if* you push consistently every week (as of 2018-01-13).
