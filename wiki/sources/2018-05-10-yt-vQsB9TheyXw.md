# #1 Reason Your Website is Getting Traffic but No Sales (And How to Fix It)

## Summary

A "Q&A Thursday" episode co-hosted with Adam from Viewership, answering a viewer (an "Auntie Kate"/Ankita) who is getting Facebook, Instagram, and AdWords traffic to a month-old site but no sales. Neil's core diagnosis: early on, no sales usually means the wrong kind of traffic. New sites should not obsess over conversion-rate optimization from day one; they should first find channels that actually convert. He argues search intent is decisive — a Google Search-ad click on "fix my cracked iPhone screen" converts far better than a display/YouTube view of someone just watching videos — so social and YouTube traffic convert worse than paid Google Search. His fix: use SEM Rush to see which paid keywords competitors consistently bid on (a signal those keywords drive sales), test paid Google Search (and Bing) ads on them just to generate initial sales even unprofitably, then, once you know the converting keywords, invest in SEO and content (text, video, audio) around them for long-run organic traffic; only after a few hundred sales/leads should you move into CRO. The episode's second half is a YouTube-marketing tangent (largely Adam's channel experience): three levers — emotion (convey real feeling; Adam's video coach told him to show anger/excitement), optimization (clickable thumbnails with standout images + curiosity text; spend more time on the headline than the video — "8 out of 10 read the headline, 2 out of 10 click through"; keyword-stuffed tags/descriptions found via vidIQ), and using Google AdWords to boost a new channel (they took Adam's channel from <100K to ~400K views/month, using ads to build momentum that persists after spend stops because YouTube reads the watch signals).
