# My Biggest SEO Fail (Unbelievable Rookie Mistake)

## Summary

Solo confessional. Neil opens by acknowledging most people see him purely as "a SEO"
who succeeds at everything — and pushes back: he's had *many* huge SEO failures, and he
got good at SEO not because he's smarter than anyone but because he's **relentless and
obsessive** — he keeps experimenting and testing until something works. His biggest
failure was **black-hat SEO in his youth**: buying **expired domains** that carried EDU
and government backlinks (he names "national parks"–type domains), then turning them
into **casino sites** and ranking page-one for terms like "online casino." Google
eventually **penalized** him for being too aggressive and buying links he shouldn't
have. His takeaway is framed as *mindset*, not tactic: the real mistake was being
**shortsighted** — the shortcuts made quick money but the money vanished just as fast.
His lesson: with SEO, **think long-run** — legitimate rankings take a long time but they
stay, creating stability and reliable income. He closes on his enduring principle:
**Google's core purpose is to rank whatever is best for the user** (links, brand
signals, and on-page SEO are just proxies), so **doing what's best for the user** — not
for Google — is what ranks you highest and keeps you there long-term, even though it
won't win in the short term.
