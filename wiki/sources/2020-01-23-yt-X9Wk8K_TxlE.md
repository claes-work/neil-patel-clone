# The Best FREE Traffic Sources for New Websites

## Summary
Solo tactical rundown of six zero-budget traffic sources for a new site, opening with the
premise that paid traffic is the easy path if you have money. (1) **YouTube** — "the
second most used search engine"; he reports his own channel at **over 490,000
subscribers and almost one million monthly views in under three years, organically**. Two
mechanics: promote hard in the **first 24 hours** (friends, shares, likes, comments — not
ads) because YouTube's algorithm weighs first-day performance *and engagement*, not just
views, and bake comment-prompts into the recording ("leave a comment with yes/no"); and
**call out a URL** on your own site for a checklist or cheat sheet, which people will type
in manually. He caps the second one: don't do it on every video or "it'll get burned out"
— save it for genuinely good free offers. (2) **A free Udemy course** — per SimilarWeb,
Udemy gets **68M+ visitors a month** of people already in learning mode; give the course
away but host the PDFs, workbooks, checklists and downloads on your own site. Someone who
just watched 5–30 minutes of your teaching arrives with rapport and trust, so they convert
better. (3) **Podcast tours** — not starting a podcast, but pitching yourself onto the many
interview shows (findable in the iTunes store, and via Google, "because Google now indexes
podcasts"); during the interview name a URL with a free asset so listeners have a *reason*
to type it in rather than being "too lazy." (4) **Pinterest + Tailwind Tribes** — target
boards that already have ~20 pins each, make images on **Canva** pointing to your best
articles (relevance is mandatory: "I can't just post up dog images on Pinterest and say
hey, check out my marketing articles"), then use Tailwind's free trial → *Find a Tribe* →
join niche tribes and share other members' best content so they reciprocate. His target
volume is **30–50 pins a day**, scheduled through Tailwind. (5) **Virtual summits** — pick
a *narrow* niche (not "marketing," not even "SEO": link building alone, or Facebook organic
reach), package it with PDF action guides, templates, swipe files, checklists and cheat
sheets, choose a format (crash courses, interviews, slide decks, live Q&A, or a mix), and
recruit influencer guests who promote to their lists — a mutual-press trade where you also
inherit the master list. Files and recordings live on your site, and there must be a funnel
behind it: hours of engagement make the recorded version, or related products, an easy
upsell. The gate: **the summit's niche must match what you sell**. (6) **Facebook Groups**
— citing Facebook's Q2 earnings call, **200 million users are in "meaningful groups,"
roughly double the prior year**, as Facebook shifts from the public News Feed to private
group experiences for privacy reasons (announced May 2019 at its developer conference).
The protocol is earn-first: share others' content, comment, answer questions **for a few
weeks straight**, and only then post your own articles and videos. Because Facebook wants
people to stay on-platform, **video outperforms links** — so put the URL callout inside the
video. He closes against overwhelm: **pick one tactic and implement it**, don't do all six.
