# SEO Hacks You Need to Start Doing Today

## Summary
Brazilian-hosted interview (title is generic relative to actual content — roughly half SEO
tactics, half biography/wealth-philosophy). **SEO/tools segment**: names a five-tool starter
stack — Google Analytics (revenue-per-keyword, not just rankings), AnswerThePublic (long-tail
keyword discovery), Google Search Console (technical error diagnosis), vidIQ/TubeBuddy (YouTube
optimization), and Google Data Studio (cross-channel dashboarding); states paying for traffic to
a video/page doesn't directly help or hurt Google rankings but warns that traffic-then-cutoff
can look like a negative engagement signal once the paid boost stops. For a company starting SEO
from zero, recommends the "SEO Made Simple" guide plus Ubersuggest as the entry point, and says
the daily SEO routine should be: check/fix technical errors, publish new content, build links,
and get social shares — every single day. Defines "good content" via Google's E-A-T (expertise,
authority, trust): answer the reader's question thoroughly enough that they know what to do
next, make it easily digestible, and layer in personal experience/storytelling so it isn't a
rehash of what ten other people already wrote (uses the Nike-should-write-about-sports-not-
investing analogy, repeated elsewhere in the corpus). **Biography/wealth segment**: confirms
age as **37** at time of filming (2023-06-05) and headcount context of ~800 employees operating
in India, Brazil, and elsewhere (client examples: PwC, ESPN, CNN Brazil); discloses being the
21st-fastest-growing company in the US two years prior; states he has no plans to take the
company public ("I don't want the headache, I don't have investors") and prefers 2-3
acquisitions/year while scaling toward an eventual 5,000-10,000 employee headcount. Discloses
real-estate holdings: a Las Vegas house valued ~$18M and a Beverly Hills house under
construction valued ~$20M (~$38M combined). Describes a hedonic-adaptation realization during
COVID — after ramping private-jet spending (including a $200-250K one-way Vegas-to-São Paulo
charter) and once owning things like yachts, he and his wife concluded they were happier
directing money toward helping others than toward further consumption, and now prefers flying
commercial and donating the difference. States two children (ages 1 and 3 at time of filming),
describes his own goal-ladder as a kid (millionaire → 10 millionaire → 100 millionaire →
billionaire, "it doesn't stop") before catching himself. On childhood: says his family was
"middle class," his parents didn't have much money, and extended family (mom's and dad's
siblings) helped support the household; recalls his mother scolding him for laughing at a bad
"American Idol" singer ("never make fun of people... respect them for trying") as a formative
lesson against hating on others' success. Closes with three pieces of generic business advice:
(1) no one has all the answers — avoid repeating the same mistake rather than expecting to avoid
mistakes altogether; (2) hire and pay for already-successful talent rather than the cheapest
labor; (3) run at least one new marketing experiment per week because platform algorithms
(YouTube, TikTok, Google) constantly change, citing NP Digital's practice of cross-testing the
same experiment across its US/UK/India/Brazil client base to spot patterns faster.
