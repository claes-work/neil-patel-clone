# How to Find All the Keywords That Your Competitors Rank For (But That You Don't)

## Summary
A screen-recorded Ubersuggest tutorial demoed on neilpatel.com itself, narrated in
third person by an unnamed instructor voice ("Neil Patel" branding, but the on-screen
narration uses "I" throughout in a Neil-style walkthrough; treated as Neil-attributed
per the channel's standard format — see attribution note). Workflow: enter your (or a
competitor's) domain into Ubersuggest, open "Competing Domains" to see sites Google
associates with you by keyword overlap, and manually prune irrelevant matches (Forbes
and Entrepreneur excluded here as too broad-topic to be true competitors). For a
genuine competitor (demoed on HubSpot), click "View All" on the keyword-gap list —
every keyword the competitor ranks for that you don't — and manually filter for
relevance and commercial intent rather than mass-targeting everything (rejects
examples like "how to change messenger theme" or "Grand Canyon puns" as irrelevant,
keeps examples like "best real estate website designers" and "best company newsletter
examples" as relevant to an agency audience). Recommends exporting the full list to CSV
for large competitor sets and prioritizing keywords with high volume AND high CPC as
the most commercially lucrative targets (demoed: "online digital marketing diploma" at
$24.74 CPC despite modest volume). Closes with the same one-article-a-week compounding-
growth framing used elsewhere in the corpus (small monthly gains compounding
significantly over a year).

## Key claims
- As of 2021-01-23, recommends prioritizing keyword-gap targets by combined high
  volume AND high CPC rather than either alone, citing a $24.74-CPC example keyword as
  illustrative of a low-volume-but-lucrative target (paraphrase).
- Demonstrates manually excluding broad-topic "competing domains" (Forbes,
  Entrepreneur) from Ubersuggest's automatic competitor list because they cover
  unrelated topics despite keyword overlap — a workflow-hygiene step not previously
  logged in this level of detail (paraphrase).
- Restates the one-article-a-week compounding-traffic-growth framing already logged
  elsewhere in the corpus (reinforcement, not new).

## Notable verbatim quotes
