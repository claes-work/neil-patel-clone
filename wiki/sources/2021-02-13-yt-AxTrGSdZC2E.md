# How to Create The Optimal SaaS Pricing Page

## Summary
Tactical CRO walkthrough using Ubersuggest's own pricing page as the live example,
element-by-element, framed as the product of "tons and tons" of A/B tests (cross-
referencing his Crazy Egg co-founder background as the source of broader cross-industry
pricing-page data beyond just SaaS). Covers, top to bottom: headline with an optional
benefit statement (says this converts "not as well as it used to"), a badge (free
trial or money-back-guarantee) shown to boost conversions, a unique differentiator
bullet (measured an 8–9% conversion lift), a deliberately un-detailed plan overview
(too much upfront detail underperformed vs. a summary + expandable details), social
proof (found to help newer/less-established brands more than established ones, but
never to hurt), objection-based bullets (Ubersuggest's own objection: people want
hand-holding/training, so that's addressed on-page), a visual feature breakdown paired
with a comparison table (found the combination reduces support-ticket volume vs. a
table alone), a price-positioning/comparison section, an FAQ section (also framed as
reducing support load), embedded CTAs to avoid re-scrolling, and live chat (works even
without paid tools like Intercom/Drift). Credits "Gajon" of the CRO agency Spiralize
as the page's actual designer, and closes recommending multiple pricing tiers (raises
revenue-per-user even though most buyers pick the cheapest tier) and a two-step
checkout (registration page, then a separate payment page) as both conversion-tested.
Notably closes by recommending readers use Spiralize over his own agency for
pricing-page work, calling it more transparent than self-promoting.

## Key claims
- States a measured ~8–9% conversion lift from adding a bullet that explicitly
  differentiates the product from competitors/highlights a unique offer (e.g., a
  lifetime plan), on Ubersuggest's own pricing page (paraphrase, self-reported A/B
  test result).
- States that overly detailed plan-overview sections underperformed a brief summary
  with an expandable "details" option, in Ubersuggest's own testing (paraphrase,
  self-reported).
- States social proof helps newer/less-established companies more than established
  ones in pricing-page conversion tests, though it never measurably hurts either way
  (paraphrase, self-reported, previously-unlogged nuance).
- States combining a visual feature breakdown with a comparison table (rather than a
  table alone) reduced support-ticket volume and increased conversions on
  Ubersuggest's page (paraphrase, self-reported).
- Recommends a two-step checkout (separate registration step before the payment page)
  over a single all-at-once checkout page as conversion-tested to reduce buyer
  overwhelm (paraphrase, self-reported, extends prior two-step-checkout material with
  a SaaS-specific framing).
- Explicitly recommends a competing CRO agency (Spiralize, "Gajon['s]" company) over
  his own agency (NP Digital) for pricing-page work, framing it as a transparency
  choice (paraphrase, verbatim self-description below).
