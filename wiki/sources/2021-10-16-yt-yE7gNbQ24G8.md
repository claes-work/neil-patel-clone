# 10 Psychological Triggers to MAKE PEOPLE BUY From YOU!

## Summary
Lists 10 psychological triggers he says increase conversion rate: (1) availability/
scarcity — showing a low stock count, with an explicit ethical caveat against lying
about the real number; (2) novelty (Elon Musk's flamethrower); (3) authority (public
figures like Tony Robbins, Trump, Obama, Musk); (4) urgency (Amazon delivery-window
deadlines, Prime Day); (5) "reason why" — explaining the concrete problem the offer
solves (uses Ubersuggest's paid tier as his own example); (6) common enemy (a villain
concept or cause, e.g. a nonprofit funding anti-terrorism education work); (7)
anticipation (Apple product launches); (8) social proof (reviews/testimonials/star
ratings); (9) significance (does the purchase make the buyer look/feel better); (10)
community (cites his own past info-product business, where he built a buyer community).
Offers viewers a public guarantee: if they have traffic, implement all 10 tactics, and
still get no sales, he'll personally help for free via a comment reply.

## Key claims
- Frames a specific ethical line on the scarcity tactic: showing a real low-stock count
  lifts checkout completion, but displaying a fake low number when true stock is high
  ("we have 1,000 left, but let's only say we have five") is explicitly called
  unethical (self-reported test result + stated principle, dated 2021-10) — analytics-cro.
- Names the "common enemy" trigger with a cause-marketing example (anti-terrorism /
  women's-education nonprofit messaging) as a persuasion device distinct from the more
  commonly documented triggers (scarcity/urgency/social proof already established in the
  wiki) (paraphrase, dated 2021-10) — analytics-cro.
- Uses Ubersuggest's own paid-tier upsell (free version exists; people still pay for
  coaching/extra help) as a live "reason why" example (self-reported, dated 2021-10) —
  analytics-cro, agency-entrepreneurship.
