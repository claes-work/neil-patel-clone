# How to Build Backlinks Without Paying for Them

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that backlinks are necessary to rank on Google, but that paying for them is against Google's policy, risks getting a site banned, and is a short-term mistake. He lays out a three-step, no-cost process: use a backlink tool to find who links to competitors and which competitor pages are most linked, write a substantially better and more in-depth version of the target article, then send custom outreach emails to everyone linking to the competitor explaining why the new article is better. He stresses that the content-quality step is the most important part and that the tool used is interchangeable. Throughout he emphasizes that the method works but requires significant manual labor, which is why most people never build links. He positions this as the long-term, sustainable alternative to "shady gray hat tactics."

## Key claims
- Backlinks are required to rank on Google (as of 2019-07-15).
- Paying for backlinks is against Google's policy, is a good way to get a site banned, and is a short-term, ill-advised tactic (as of 2019-07-15).
- Most viewers likely do not build backlinks at all, and the main reason is that link building is time-consuming manual labor most people are unwilling to do (as of 2019-07-15).
- Step 1: Use a backlink tool (he offers a free one at neilpatel.com/backlinks) to enter a competitor's URL and see everyone linking to them; use the report to find the competitor's most popular / most-linked pages, and check backlink counts at both the whole-domain and specific-URL level (as of 2019-07-15).
- The "top pages" view in the sidebar of the backlink report shows the competitor's top pages, and its backlinks button reveals every site linking to a given article (as of 2019-07-15).
- Step 2 (the most important step): Write a better, more in-depth version of the competitor's article — e.g. expanding a 1,000-word piece to 5,000 words — but through genuinely thorough, high-quality content, not keyword stuffing or padding for length (as of 2019-07-15).
- Depth should mean covering every sub-detail an expert would (his pizza example: choosing organic vs. pesticide vegetables, draining water from cut vegetables, water quality's effect on dough), and enriching the piece with images, GIFs, videos, and custom-designed graphics (as of 2019-07-15).
- The specific tool used does not matter; any competitor's backlink tool works and content quality is what matters (as of 2019-07-15).
- Step 3: Email everyone who links to the competitor's article and explain why the new article is better, using custom (not templated) emails that add specific value before asking for the link (as of 2019-07-15).
- This custom-email approach takes more time but is more effective and yields a much better success ratio than generic outreach (as of 2019-07-15).
- Doing this consistently lets a site outpace competitors, rank higher, and capture their traffic; it is a better, long-term approach than gray hat tactics (as of 2019-07-15).
