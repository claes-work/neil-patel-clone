# How to Continually Find New Keyword Opportunities (That You'll Be Able to Rank For)

## Summary
Solo screen-share SEO tutorial on an ongoing keyword-discovery workflow using
Ubersuggest, demoed live on his own agency's use case ("NP Digital... an ad agency where
we help companies grow their traffic"). Method: type a seed term (e.g., "SEO company")
into Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas report, then select relevant variants — service-type
variants (company/agency/services), city/location modifiers (Texas, San Antonio,
Portland, Austin, Miami, Seattle, Atlanta, Orlando, NYC, Chicago), niche/industry
modifiers (e.g., discovers "SEO services for law firms" mid-demo and generalizes to
"for dentists, for doctors, for lawyers"), and adds them all to a saved keyword list
inside Ubersuggest (grouped by list, e.g., "SEO company" list, a separate "digital
marketing" list). Repeats the process for a second seed term ("digital marketing") to
show the pattern generalizes, surfacing an industry-specific keyword ("digital marketing
for healthcare") as another example of the niche-modifier pattern. Demonstrates going
one level deeper into long-tail territory (e.g., "SEO company Houston" → related
long-tail suggestions), noting that suggestion volume thins out the further down the
long tail you go. Shows the competitive-research payoff of saved lists: clicking into a
saved keyword (e.g., "SEO company Houston") to see who currently ranks top 10–30 for it,
naming the ranking competitors he pulls up live (Thrive Agency, a site by "Josh(ua)
Schwab Belland," N-16 Marketing, RegXSEO, and the Clutch directory) as pages to study and
then "create something better" than. Frames the overall discipline as continuous: keep
searching seed terms, keep saving/grouping keywords, remove ones no longer relevant, and
Ubersuggest auto-dedupes repeats — so the list compounds over time rather than being a
one-time exercise.

## Key claims
- Prescribes a repeatable keyword-discovery loop: seed term → keyword-ideas report →
  select service/location/niche-industry variants → save to a grouped list → repeat
  regularly, rather than a one-time keyword-research exercise (paraphrase, 2021-05-29) —
  seo.
- Identifies three keyword-modifier categories to systematically mine from a seed term:
  service-type (company/agency/services), geographic (city/state/metro), and
  niche-industry (e.g., "for law firms," "for healthcare") (paraphrase, 2021-05-29) —
  seo.
- Recommends studying the pages currently ranking top 10–30 for a saved keyword and
  building a page that improves on them, as the actionable next step after keyword
  discovery (paraphrase, 2021-05-29) — seo. Consistent with the competitive
  content-gap method described in the 2021-05-27 source in this same batch.
- Notes long-tail keyword-suggestion volume thins out the further down the specificity
  chain you drill (e.g., "SEO company Houston" → fewer further suggestions)
  (self-reported observation, 2021-05-29) — seo.
