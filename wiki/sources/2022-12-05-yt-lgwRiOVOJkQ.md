# The One Growth Strategy You Can't Overlook

## Summary
Pitches SMS/text marketing as his answer to "if you had to pick one marketing
strategy" — citing a self-stated 98% open rate and the best engagement rate of any
marketing medium. Cites Statista (75% YoY growth in SMS popularity in 2021), stats that
71% of people check phones within 10 minutes of waking (he claims to check his within
10 seconds) and that Americans average 2 hours 54 minutes of daily phone use. Six
strategies: (1) exclusive coupon codes tied to unique, per-subscriber codes and
payment integration; (2) drip campaigns with staged discount escalation (e.g., 5% at
signup → 10% after three weeks → 20% after two months) that stop once redeemed; (3)
collect subscriber information via polls/surveys to sharpen buyer personas; (4) run
contests with keyword-based opt-in (cites EZ Texting: half of consumers respond to a
text within three minutes); (5) transactional messaging — shipping and order
confirmations (cites G2: 75% like shipping updates, 65% like confirmations); (6) build
exclusivity/perceived-VIP treatment for SMS subscribers. Closes with an NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- Self-reports SMS marketing has a "98% open rate" and the best engagement rate of any
  marketing channel he uses (dated 2022-12-05; unaudited/self-reported figure).
- Cites Statista: SMS saw a 75% year-over-year increase in popularity among customers
  in 2021 (as cited, dated 2022-12-05).
- Describes a specific staged-discount drip sequence he recommends: 5% coupon at
  signup, 10% after three weeks, 20% after two months, stopping the sequence once a
  coupon is redeemed (self-reported tactic, dated 2022-12-05).
- Cites G2: 75% of customers like receiving shipping-update texts, 65% like
  purchase-confirmation texts (as cited, dated 2022-12-05).
