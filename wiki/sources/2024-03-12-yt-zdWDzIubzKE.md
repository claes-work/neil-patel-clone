# Being the CEO is the worst job ever

## Summary
Prompted by a tweet Neil posted (and Eric reposted) calling the CEO role "the worst job," the episode is mostly a first-person account of how Neil structures his own role at NP Digital: he holds the title of CMO, not CEO, deliberately — he describes himself as "a terrible manager" but an "amazing individual contributor and executor," has no direct reports, gives his VP of Marketing strategic input a few times a month, and channels most of his energy into creating his own content, recruiting/vetting for specific roles, watching the company's finances closely (checking the bank account multiple times a day), and staying deeply involved in company vision. He discusses NP Digital's 10-year goal of becoming a "global ad agency," notes the company has landed seven- and eight-figure annual contracts but not yet a nine-figure one (unlike some larger competitors), says NP Digital is self-funded with no outside investors, and describes a no-work-life-balance operating culture he applies to himself and expects (with encouragement toward "some balance") from his executives. Eric, in third person, corroborates and colors several of these traits (Neil's reputation for frugality/OCD-level expense-nitpicking, never caring about titles). A closing segment reacts to Elon Musk's newly filed lawsuit against OpenAI/Sam Altman and a tangential Tesla-as-technology-company vs. Tesla's uncomfortable seats discussion; this portion is undiarized banter and is quarantined per SUBJECT.md.

## Key claims
- **Holds CMO, not CEO, title at NP Digital by choice.** Neil states he has no direct reports, that his VP of Marketing runs day-to-day marketing while Neil gives strategic advice "two to three times a month," and that he creates a large volume of his own content so the VP doesn't have to. (Neil, 2024-03-12) `(self-reported)`
- **Self-assessment: "terrible manager," strong individual contributor.** Neil says he does well on small teams, considers himself "an amazing individual contributor and executor," and performs best working independently — a trait he once viewed as a weakness. He contrasts this with Mark Zuckerberg's stated strength as an individual contributor who nonetheless stays maximally involved and doesn't believe in heavy delegation, framing his own style as different (deep involvement only where he chooses). (Neil, 2024-03-12) `(self-reported)`
- **Works 60–70 hour weeks despite not holding the CEO title**, and frames his non-CEO role as deliberate rather than a lack of commitment to the company's vision, in response to people asking why he wouldn't just be CEO. (Neil, 2024-03-12) `(self-reported)`
- **Deeply involved in company vision and M&A, delegates execution.** Says he drives vision by talking constantly with NP Digital's executives, lets them execute even when he privately disagrees, admits when they were right ("I told you so... I should have listened"), and treats the business as "one big experiment bucket" he's comfortable spending money on even when experiments fail. Says he is "heavily involved" in M&A. (Neil, 2024-03-12) `(self-reported)`
- **NP Digital 10-year vision: become a "global ad agency."** States the company can currently land seven- and eight-figure annual contracts and manages ad spend well over $100M for some clients (pass-through, not fee revenue), but has never landed a nine-figure-per-year direct contract the way some larger competitors have — framed as a stated 10-year goal, not yet achieved. (Neil, 2024-03-12) `(self-reported)`
- **No work-life balance, self-funded company culture.** States plainly "I don't have work-life balance, I just work," that most of his executives share that culture, but that he coaches them to keep frustration ("bad economy... two steps backward") from following them home, and that NP Digital is self-funded with no outside investors. (Neil, 2024-03-12) `(self-reported)`
- **Leadership-composure belief, citing Frank Slootman.** Argues a leader can't visibly show anger or doubt even during a rough stretch, because visible panic spreads; cites ex-Snowflake/ServiceNow CEO Frank Slootman's "push the pace no matter what, every single day" philosophy approvingly, noting Snowflake's stock dropped ~20% around Slootman's exit. (Neil, 2024-03-12) `(opinion, dated)`
- **Checks the company bank account multiple times a day** via mobile-banking notifications (multi-factor-authenticated), corroborating an existing "watches money like a hawk" / OCD-adjacent trait Eric also names directly ("Neil's OCD, everyone"). (Neil, 2024-03-12; Eric's characterization noted separately) `(self-reported + co-host testimony)`
