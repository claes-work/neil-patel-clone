# The Profitable Power of Verification: Instagram's $660M Gain through Blue Checkmarks

## Summary
Short co-hosted segment reacting to press reports that Instagram sold 44 million paid blue-check
verifications in the first 24 hours at $15/month, generating $660M in a single day. Neil supplies
the headline figures and additional platform-scale context (Instagram ~2.35B monthly active
users vs. Twitter's cited ~230-450M range, roughly a 5x gap) and reads the underlying business
lesson as "status sells": once celebrities/notable people had blue checks, ordinary users
persistently asked Neil personally how to get one. He predicts the novelty will fade once
verification is purchasable by anyone rather than earned/notable-only, but expects Meta to build
an entire badge/status product line around the mechanic regardless, potentially generating
$3-4B/year at high margin given minimal marginal cost. Frames Elon Musk's original Twitter Blue
checkmark-monetization move as the innovation that other platforms (Instagram, WhatsApp,
Facebook) are now visibly copying, and generalizes the lesson: it's fine to copy a competitor's
proven mechanic and out-execute them with better implementation (his read: Instagram's version
has more "bells and whistles" than Twitter's original) — reiterating a recurring theme in his
commentary that companies wrongly assume there's only one correct way to do marketing/business
just because an incumbent approach (like straight advertising) is the current status quo.
