# How I Generated 37,186,336 Video Views Without Going Viral

## Summary
Solo tactical video arguing against chasing virality in favor of a "quality content in
quantity" compounding strategy. Cites his own Social Blade-reported YouTube total of
37,186,000 views, and states his true cross-platform total (YouTube + Instagram +
Facebook + LinkedIn + website embeds) is "probably safe to say over 50 million."
Demonstrates a keyword-ideation workflow using the Ubersuggest Chrome extension (shows
search volume inline while browsing YouTube) and neilpatel.com/ubersuggest's "Keywords
by Traffic" report on competitor domains (demoed live on his own domain and on
Copyblogger and Content Marketing Institute) to mine video-topic ideas from what
competitors already rank for on Google. States the core mechanism: individual videos
may get only 2,000–10,000 views/month each, but producing "hundreds and hundreds" of
videos compounds into millions of views over years — framed explicitly as a long game,
not a virality bet.

## Key claims
- States his YouTube channel had generated 37,186,000 views per Social Blade as of
  this recording, and estimates his true cross-platform video-view total (including
  Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, and website embeds) at "probably... over 50 million"
  (self-reported, dated 2021-04-12, partially third-party-sourced via Social Blade for
  the YouTube-only figure).
- States individual videos in his catalog typically get only 2,000–10,000 views/month

  of videos) compounding over years, not from any single viral hit (paraphrase,

- Demonstrates using Ubersuggest's "Keywords by Traffic" report on competitor domains
  (own domain plus named competitors Copyblogger and Content Marketing Institute) as a
  content-ideation source — mining what competitors rank for on Google to generate
  YouTube video topics (paraphrase, tactical workflow, tool-specific).
