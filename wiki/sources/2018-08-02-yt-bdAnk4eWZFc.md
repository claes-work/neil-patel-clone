# Growth Hacks for New Service Website (That Don't Cost a Fortune)

## Summary

Q&A Thursday (Neil with interviewer Adam). Reader "Bunty" runs a new interior-painting
service business and wants low-cost growth hacks. Neil's answer, service-business-specific:
**(1) Put your phone number top-right** — service businesses close more over the phone than
anything else. **(2) Lead forms on *every* page** — and don't just say "contact us";
offer value/advice ("let us help you determine which colors are right for your home"),
because people don't want to be sold to; educating and giving free help builds the
relationship that converts. **(3) Offer a free consultation** — say the exact words "free
consultation"; even repeating the same pitch multiple times still converts. **(4) Exit
pop-up** to catch leads on the way out (educate + offer the consult). **(5) Multi-step
forms** — ask name + email first, then phone and other fields on the next step; he claims
this yields ~10 more leads than asking everything at once. Then treat **marketing as
experimentation ("spaghetti")**: test five or six tactics, spend no more than ~$50 on
many of them, see what produces, and double down — there's no one universal growth hack;
it varies by business/region. Tactics that work for most: find who links to competitors
(Ahrefs, SEMrush) and ask for links; get local Chamber of Commerce links (helps rankings);
use BuzzSumo to find popular content and write something better, then ask the original's
sharers to share yours. He closes on relationships/referrals — how he started his own
agency was reaching out to other agencies not doing YouTube marketing, helping them, and
getting help back; "you don't have to go to Silicon Valley to build a multi-million-dollar
service business."

## Key claims

- As of 2018-08-02, for service businesses: put the phone number top-right (they close more
  by phone) and place a value-driven lead form on *every* page.
- As of 2018-08-02, offer a literal "free consultation" and lead with help/education rather
  than a pitch; the same pitch repeated still converts.
- As of 2018-08-02, a multi-step form (name + email first, phone/details next) yields
  roughly 10 more leads than asking all fields at once; add an exit pop-up to capture more.
- As of 2018-08-02, treat marketing as experimentation — test 5–6 tactics at ~≤$50 each,
  then double down on the winner; there is no universal growth hack (it varies by business).
- As of 2018-08-02, reliable low-cost tactics: competitor backlink outreach (Ahrefs/SEMrush),
  Chamber of Commerce links, and BuzzSumo-driven "make it better + ask the sharers."
- As of 2018-08-02, Neil says he grew his own agency largely through relationship-building
  and referrals, not technology or a clever tactic.
