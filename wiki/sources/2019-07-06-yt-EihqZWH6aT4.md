# How Many Blog Posts Should You Write Per Month?

## Summary

Neil answers "how much content?" by first disclosing his own cadence — he writes **four blog
posts a month** (~one a week, sometimes five in a five-week month) — then insists **what works
for him may not work for you**; the right number varies by blog and industry, driven by
competition. His method: (1) in **Ubersuggest**, enter each competitor's domain, open the
**top pages** report (with the keywords and backlinks per page), and compile a big list of all
competitors' ranking blog posts to see overlap and unique gaps — a read on how much content
exists to cover in the space; (2) check **how competitive** competitors are via **domain score**,
backlink count, and traffic. Decision rule: if competitors' **domain score is generally under
40**, you can outrank them by producing a lot of content (7/week, even 20/day) plus links and
internal linking; if it's **above 65**, don't chase volume — hunt **content gaps** (high-traffic
topics competitors haven't covered) and win there.
