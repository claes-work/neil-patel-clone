# How to Get More Organic Reach & Visibility on Facebook - Module 2 - Lesson 1 - Facebook Unlocked

## Summary
Facebook Unlocked Module 2, Lesson 1: how the News Feed algorithm decides visibility,
and how to maximize organic reach without ads. Core thesis: content must resonate with
your existing, smaller follower base first — if your core audience doesn't engage, the
algorithm won't extend reach to anyone else, regardless of broader appeal. Recommends
evergreen content (how-to guides, product reviews, tips, listicles) built with
Ubersuggest-sourced long-tail keywords, written for beginners (avoiding jargon), and
posted natively rather than linked off-platform (Facebook deprioritizes posts that send
users away, and Neil advises against third-party scheduling apps since Facebook wants
users posting directly on-platform to generate its own ad revenue). Covers testing post
frequency by industry (celebrity-gossip pages can post many times a day; B2B pages
posting 3-4x/day risks unfollows), comparing performance against other channels
(Google/YouTube ads, SEO, TV, radio), boosting reach without ad spend (asking network
contacts to engage, partnering with similar pages, cultivating brand evangelists via
testimonials-with-tips rather than raw self-promotion), and closes reinforcing the
"rule of seven" touchpoint doctrine and Facebook's stated preference for
authoritative/verified brands over unverified accounts (framed as an anti-fake-news
signal).

## Key claims
- As of 2021-01-04, articulates the News Feed algorithm mechanism: reach is gated
  first by whether your *existing* follower base engages, not by a post's broader
  appeal (paraphrase — a specific mechanism claim not previously logged this
  explicitly).
- Advises native posting over off-platform links and against third-party scheduling
  tools, reasoning Facebook favors behavior that keeps users generating ad revenue
  on-platform (paraphrase).
- Post-frequency guidance is industry-dependent — cites celebrity-gossip pages as a
  high-frequency-tolerant example vs. B2B pages where 3-4 posts/day causes unfollows
  (paraphrase).
- Restates the "rule of seven" touchpoint doctrine (already logged in the corpus) in a
  Facebook-specific context, adding Messenger, WhatsApp, and Instagram as the specific
  cross-platform touchpoints he uses to accumulate the seven interactions (paraphrase).
- Ties Facebook's brand/verification preference to combating "fake news," framing
  brand authority as a platform-trust signal, not just a marketing asset (paraphrase).
