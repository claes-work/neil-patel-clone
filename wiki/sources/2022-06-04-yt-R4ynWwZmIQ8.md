# One Quick Hack to Rank #1 of Google

## Summary
A short, single-tactic tutorial on earning backlinks by targeting sites that already link to
multiple competitors. Neil Patel opens with the observation that cold outreach to random sites
rarely earns a link, and frames links as "votes" analogous to a US presidential election — the
site with more (relevant) votes generally ranks higher, though you don't need the most, just
enough. His fix: instead of guessing who might link to you, find domains that already link to two
or three-plus competitors, since a site linking to multiple players in a niche is link-agnostic and
more likely to add one more. He walks through the mechanic using his own tool, NeilPatel.com/backlinks
— enter your URL to see domain authority, current backlinks, referring domains, and link growth over
time, then use the "Backlink Opportunities" feature to input two or three competitor domains plus
your own; it surfaces sites linking to those competitors' specific pages but not to you. The outreach
template is simple: identify a similar page on your own site and email the referring site noting it
covers additional ground ("X, Y, and Z") the competitor's page doesn't, asking for a link. He gives a
quantified expectation for this kind of outreach — emailing 100 such sites should yield 14+ links,
which he calls "a really good ratio." Closes with the standard NP Digital agency pitch and
like/subscribe/comment ask.

## Key claims
- Random, untargeted link-outreach ("hit up a website and ask them to link to you") mostly gets
  ignored; Neil frames links as votes — more (relevant) links generally means higher rankings, though
  not necessarily the most, just "enough" (as of 2022-06-04).
- Tactic: find sites that link to three-plus of your competitors but not to you — these sites are
  "agnostic" about which competitor they favor and are more likely to add a link to you too (as of
  2022-06-04).
- Tool/workflow: NeilPatel.com/backlinks → enter your URL for a Backlinks report (domain authority,
  link count, referring sites, link growth over time) → use the left-nav "Backlink opportunities"
  feature, enter two or three competitor domains + your own, to see who links to competitors' specific
  pages but not to you (as of 2022-06-04).
- Outreach template: contact each qualifying site, note that your page covers additional angles the
  competitor's page doesn't, and ask for the link (as of 2022-06-04).
- Quantified expectation: emailing 100 sites selected this way should produce 14+ links, which Neil
  calls "a really good ratio" (as of 2022-06-04).
- Standard agency pitch: NP Digital offered to do this tactic (and marketing generally) for clients
  (as of 2022-06-04).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Links are currency like votes. In the US when there's a presidential election the person with most votes generally wins... The same goes with the internet. The more links you have, generally the higher you're going to rank. You don't need the most to get at the top but you need enough.

> Well, focus on finding sites who link to multiple of your competitors is the key. If someone links to not one, not two, but three or more of your competitors, chances are they're willing to link to you because they're agnostic. They don't care about one competitor over another.

> If you email out a hundred, I know that may seem like a lot, you should get over 14 links. That's a really good ratio.
