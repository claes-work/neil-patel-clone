# How to Run a Profitable Social Media Contest | Go Viral And Make More Money Online!

## Summary
Neil lays out a four-step method for running a social media contest that actually makes money rather than just giving away free stuff. He recommends starting with contest software (Gleam.io) instead of paying a developer on Upwork, then uploading an email list to Facebook to build a 1% Lookalike Audience of similar prospects (or, absent a list, retargeting site visitors via a pixel). He stresses building in a sharing incentive so entrants who invite friends earn more points and better odds of winning, which drives viral spread. Finally, he argues the profit comes from the follow-up: emailing entrants afterward to upsell and downsell other products and services, without which the contest only incurs ad and software costs and loses money.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-25, Neil states:
- Companies keep running social media contests because they are profitable (as of 2017-10-25).
- Gleam.io is a simple software for running and tracking a contest and incentivizing participants, preferable to paying a developer on Upwork to build it (as of 2017-10-25).
- Uploading your email list to Facebook lets you create a Lookalike Audience, telling Facebook to find more people similar to your best qualified people (as of 2017-10-25).
- In general you want to use a 1% Lookalike Audience for similarity to the uploaded list (as of 2017-10-25).
- If you have no email list, you can place a pixel on your page to track and target website visitors, most of whom do not convert and should therefore be entered into your contest (as of 2017-10-25).
- Contests need a real incentive beyond free stuff; the more an entrant shares, the more likely they should be to win (as of 2017-10-25).
- With Gleam you can award more points the more people an entrant invites, so a popular entrant who invites friends can raise their odds toward one in 10 or one in 20 depending on effort (as of 2017-10-25).
- You must follow up with entrants by email and upsell and downsell them on other products or services; without this follow-up the contest loses money (as of 2017-10-25).
