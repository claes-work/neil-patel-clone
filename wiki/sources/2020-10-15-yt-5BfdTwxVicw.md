# Lessons From MrBeast, Logan Paul & Ed Sheeran - Module 2 - Lesson 2 - YouTube Unlocked

## Summary
Lesson 5 of "YouTube Unlocked" (Module 2, Lesson 2; follows
[[wiki/sources/2020-10-12-yt-sMbo87-nDwU]]). Opens with "seven lessons from the top
50 YouTube channels": stay focused on one topic/category; think of the channel as a
media company first (the underlying business is secondary to storytelling);
diversify income beyond ad revenue (products, services, affiliate, courses);
understand the audience's purchasing power before monetizing; play the long game
(YouTube as marathon, not sprint); become an active member of the platform's
community (comment, connect, learn from peers); and be patient — "tortoise beats
hare," consistency over virality. Neil then runs named case studies: Logan Paul (10M
subscribers in 340 days — the fastest in platform history at the time, per Neil;
20M+ subscribers and an estimated $1.8M+/year, though Neil personally guesses his
real earnings exceed $5M), MrBeast/Jimmy Donaldson (560 days and ~300 videos to reach
50K views and barely 1,000 subscribers, nearly quitting, before a "count to 10,000"
video broke through at 11M views; later ~$15M/year per Forbes), GoPro (grew via
user-generated unboxing/stunt content, ~6,000 GoPro videos posted daily), and Ed
Sheeran (45.6M subscribers, leveraging an existing music audience onto YouTube; ~$9M
estimated earnings from ~30M weekly views). The lesson pivots into YouTube ad
formats (TrueView for Reach, six-second bumper ads, masthead, discovery, sequencing)
and ad-creative best practices — design for sound-off/mobile viewing, keep viewers
engaged before the skip point, use retargeting/remarketing sequences — including a
step-by-step walkthrough for setting up sequential retargeting in Google Ads'
Audience Manager.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-15, Neil frames MrBeast's near-1,000-subscriber near-quit (560 days,
  ~300 videos, barely 50K views) as a persistence case study, with the breakout
  "count to 10,000" video reaching 11M views (self-reported/third-party-sourced,
  not independently verified in this source).
- As of 2020-10-15, Neil estimates (his own guess, explicitly flagged as a guess)
  that Logan Paul's actual YouTube earnings exceed the ~$1.8M/year Social Blade-style
  estimate, likely over $5M once all revenue streams are counted.
- As of 2020-10-15, Neil's stated preference among YouTube ad formats: discovery ads
  "work better for most companies" versus sequencing, which he calls "a little bit
  harder to do."
- As of 2020-10-15, Neil states roughly 6,000 GoPro videos are posted online daily,
  crediting GoPro's growth to user-generated content strategy (figure not
  independently sourced in this video).
- As of 2020-10-15, Neil's seven-lesson framework from top-50-channel analysis:
  focus/niche discipline, media-company-first identity, income diversification,
  audience purchasing-power awareness, long-game patience, community participation,
  and consistency over quick wins.

## Notable verbatim quotes
