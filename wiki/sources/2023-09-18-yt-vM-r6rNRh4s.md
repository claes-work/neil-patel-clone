# Keeping Your Customers Engaged - Creating A Mailing List

## Summary
Second segment of the same "Young and Profiting" (Hala Taha) interview as
[[2023-09-09-yt--47kDZOjCoc]], covering SEO philosophy, keyword research, email
marketing, creative lead generation, and 2023 marketing trends. Neil argues the real
long-run ranking factor is building a product/service good enough that users don't
bounce back to search — links and on-page signals matter but are downstream of user
satisfaction. He gives a politics/voting analogy for on-page vs. off-page SEO (a link
from an authoritative site is like an endorsement from a popular politician). He
discloses the February 2022 acquisition of AnswerThePublic for $8.6M, stating it then
accounted for roughly 40% of NP Digital's consulting revenue and had ~60% of
Ubersuggest's traffic, monetized only via a paid tool rather than services — the
strategic logic behind buying it. He details a free-tool lead-gen playbook (buying
white-label tools from CodeCanyon, gating them behind email capture — generates ~300K
new email addresses/month via AnswerThePublic + Ubersuggest), an email-list-scrubbing
practice via ConvertKit's cold-subscriber list, and a "meetings, not speaker fees" model
for conference talks (cites securing meetings with Michelin and a major Latin American
life-insurance company via a Brazil conference). Names podcasting and AI-driven
analytics as the two biggest 2023 marketing trends. ★ landmark — corroborates/refines
SUBJECT.md's AnswerThePublic acquisition entry with a specific price and revenue-share
figure; candidate for `persona/beliefs.md` (seo, email-marketing) and biography cross-
reference.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- The dominant SEO ranking factor in the long run is product/service quality, not
  backlinks or on-page code — if visitors bounce back to search results, rankings
  eventually fall regardless of technical SEO, because Google/Bing read user
  satisfaction signals. (Neil, 2023-09-18) — seo.
- On-page SEO = factors the site owner controls (load speed, keyword presence);
  off-page SEO = external signals (backlinks), analogized to a voting/endorsement
  system where a link from an authoritative, topically-relevant site (e.g. NYT, CNN)
  outweighs one from an unrelated small site. (Neil, 2023-09-18) — seo.
- Recommends using Ubersuggest or AnswerThePublic (both free-tier) for keyword research:
  search volume, competitiveness, and CPC (higher CPC signals a keyword drives more
  revenue). Confirms he owns Ubersuggest. (Neil, 2023-09-18) — seo.
- SEO vs. paid ads is not either/or — choose based on skillset/time/capital: paid ads if
  short on time or SEO skill, SEO if short on budget but have time, both if
  well-capitalized. (Neil, 2023-09-18) — seo / paid-ads.
- **AnswerThePublic acquisition detail**: bought in February 2022 for $8.6M; at
  acquisition it accounted for roughly 40% of NP Digital's consulting revenue and had
  about 60% of Ubersuggest's traffic, but was monetized only via tool subscriptions, not
  services. Rationale: marketing-software companies make far less than marketing-
  services companies (compares to WPP/Omnicom), so a free/cheap tool that generates
  service leads is more valuable than one that just charges for access. Self-reported,
  financial figures unaudited. (Neil, 2023-09-18) — seo / agency-entrepreneurship.
- Lead-gen playbook: buy white-label tools from codecanyon.net (~$10–50), gate repeat
  usage behind email registration; this strategy (via AnswerThePublic + Ubersuggest)
  generates close to 300,000 new email addresses per month. (Neil, 2023-09-18) —
  email-marketing / seo.
- Other lead-gen channels named: targeted LinkedIn Ads (company type/person/job title),
  webinars, podcast guesting, and — his favorite unconventional tactic — speaking at
  conferences in exchange for the organizer setting up meetings with sponsor/attendee
  companies instead of a speaker fee. Cites landing meetings with Michelin and a major
  Latin American life-insurance company via a Brazil conference, and with Whirlpool.
  Self-reported, unverified. (Neil, 2023-09-18) — agency-entrepreneurship / content-
  marketing.
- Email list hygiene: uses ConvertKit's "cold subscriber" tagging and deletes
  non-engaging subscribers monthly to protect deliverability; recommends against
  scraping/non-opt-in email lists on legal and effectiveness grounds. (Neil, 2023-09-18)
  — email-marketing.
- Email open-rate tactics: casual, lowercase subject lines (pattern disruption);
  keep the value-to-sell ratio at ~80–90% value / 10–20% sell; include a question to
  drive replies (helps deliverability and builds the relationship). Benchmarks: target
  30%+ open rates and 1–2%+ click rates; text-based emails outperform rich-media emails
  in his testing; 1–3 links per email is fine, more than ~10 is excessive, and
  no-link emails are sometimes appropriate. (Neil, 2023-09-18) — email-marketing.
- 2023 marketing trend #1 — podcasting: cites an internal survey of 8,000+ companies
  naming podcasting and AI as the top two trends; frames podcasting as under-saturated
  relative to blogging (>1B blogs vs. <10M podcasts) and notes podcast content gets
  repurposed into text and social clips. (Neil, 2023-09-18) — content-marketing.
- 2023 marketing trend #2 — AI, specifically AI-driven analytics/spend optimization
  (echoes [[2023-09-11-yt-b0YPBoMswZ4]]) over pure content generation; repeats the "AI
  is like a really good intern" framing (attributed to someone else, not original to
  Neil) as a way to describe giving every employee AI leverage. (Neil, 2023-09-18) —
  ai-marketing.
- Personal "secret to profiting in life": tries to stay even-keeled rather than chasing
  emotional highs, reminding himself others have it worse in hard times and better in
  good times, to preserve logical thinking and gratitude. (Neil, 2023-09-18) —
  biography / mindset.
