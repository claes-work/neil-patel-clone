# From Zero to 10M+ Views: The YouTube Shorts Secret (Ep. 2596)

## Summary
A co-hosted Marketing School episode (recorded "on the go") in which Eric Siu unpacks lessons from a Jay Clouse interview with 18-year-old Shorts creator Jenny Hoyos, and Neil Patel reacts and layers on his own framing. Eric drives the case study — the editing discipline ("shaving a second off can make a video explode"), Hoyos's hook-first storytelling process, her ~34-second target length, and her claim that retention matters less than most think — while Neil supplies the strategic overlay: the size of the idea (total addressable market) is what carries a video, mass-consumer topics beat niche ones, deliberately dragging out the answer keeps viewers engaged, and swipe-away rate is the metric that matters. Neil also flags the tension that bigger-TAM topics pull views but may convert worse, and points to funnel tactics (Blake Roacha driving Shorts/TikTok viewers to Instagram DMs to sell courses).

## Key claims
- Total addressable market (TAM) of the idea is the dominant driver of a video's reach; "it is the idea that will carry it at the end of the day" — bigger, mass-consumer topics (love, longevity to 200, "sugar will kill you") get far more views than niche ones. (Neil, 2023-11-07)
- Bigger-TAM topics pull large view counts but may not convert as well, so creators must weigh reach against conversion intent for a given piece. (Neil, 2023-11-07)
- Mass-consumer topics that apply to almost everyone, framed against conventional wisdom in a slightly click-baity way (headline/question framing), are what drives huge Shorts view counts. (Neil, 2023-11-07)
- When hooking viewers with an intro/headline, don't answer the question right away — drag out the payoff (while keeping the middle interesting) so people stay to the end, which increases views. (Neil, 2023-11-07)
- Retention percentage alone is not the key signal; swipe-away rate is what to watch — if >30–40% of viewers swipe away in the first milliseconds, the hook isn't strong enough. (Neil relaying Hoyos's view + his own framing, 2023-11-07)
- A storytelling short can be paired with a call-to-action funnel: e.g. Blake Roacha builds top-of-funnel with Shorts/TikToks, then sends viewers to Instagram to close course sales via DM. (Neil, 2023-11-07)
- Patty Galloway (who consults with MrBeast and other YouTubers) is cited as reinforcing that the idea is what ultimately carries a video. (Neil, context, 2023-11-07)
- Shaving even one second off a short can cause it to explode — "every single second matters," you can't waste a second. (Eric relaying Hoyos, 2023-11-07)
- Hoyos's process is hook-first: decide the hook and the closing statement before the story, then build the story around them, iterating over many edits (hours to days per idea). (Eric relaying Hoyos, 2023-11-07)
- Hoyos targets ~34 seconds per short and treats the YouTube algorithm as completely different from TikTok's; she shifted her focus from TikTok to YouTube Shorts. (Eric relaying Hoyos, 2023-11-07)
- Hoyos's "controversial opinion": retention doesn't matter as much as people think — she has seen 70%-retention videos outperform 90%-retention ones. (Eric relaying Hoyos, 2023-11-07)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> if you also look at what she's doing to succeed to get to 10 million views, she's doing mass consumer appealing topics that apply to everyone — not consumer topics that apply for a few people, but literally mass consumer topics that apply to almost everyone. And she's talking about stuff that's somewhat click-baity, she's going against conventional wisdom, the way she's posing questions doing her headlines, which is causing tons of people to view.

> the total addressable market for your idea matters a lot… it is the idea that will carry it at the end of the day.

> these are much bigger ideas that have a larger TAM… and they might not necessarily convert as well, so you have to take that into mind if that's what you're trying to do.

> when you hook people with your intro or your headline when you're doing shorts and you want a ton of views… don't answer it right away. So in your shorts, drag it on a little bit — the content needs to be interesting still at the beginning, middle and the end, but you don't want to give the answer to whatever you're answering until the end. That'll keep people engaged throughout the whole short, which will cause you to get way more views.

> when you look at your YouTube Studio you can see the percentage of people that swipe away… if greater than 30, 40% of people swipe away, it's just not good enough. You got to be able to hold their attention — the hook again, hook, story.

> he makes these shorts and these TikToks and then he funnels people at the very end of it… he gets the top of the funnel and he drives them to Instagram to close them through DM.
