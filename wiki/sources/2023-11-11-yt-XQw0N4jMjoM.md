# Our Thoughts On Selling Courses In Today's World

## Summary
Neil says no, he would not sell courses today, and gives a first-person account of
his own course-selling past (before NP Digital existed): peak course revenue around
$600K/month at 50-80% margin, which he calls objectively a very profitable business —
but argues it was still the *wrong* business relative to the opportunity cost, since
the same audience willing to pay $9.97-$1,000 for a course could instead be sold much
higher-ticket marketing services (NP Digital clients spending $10M+/year). He draws
an analogy to Google (ad spots > services) and HubSpot (recurring software + free
tools > services) choosing the higher-leverage business model available to them, and
recounts personally discontinuing a $97-course-plus-free-30-minute-call offer after
his now-co-founder Mike Kamo pointed out the free call wasn't improving conversions
(only 17-20% redemption) — removing it changed nothing, confirming it was pure margin
give-away. Conclusion: give courses away for free to build goodwill/content, monetize
via a higher-ticket vehicle instead.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Direct answer: would not sell courses in today's market. (Neil, 2023-11-11) —
  agency-entrepreneurship.
- ★ Self-reported financial history: stopped selling courses roughly seven years
  before this recording (before NP Digital existed); peak course revenue was
  "around like $600,000 a month," with $3-500K/month easily sustainable, and margins
  well above 50%, sometimes 60-80% profit. (Neil, 2023-11-11) —
  agency-entrepreneurship [self-reported].
- Core framework: a profitable course business can still be the *wrong* business —
  the same buyers willing to pay $9.97-$1,000 for a course could instead pay for a
  much higher-ticket offer (his example: NP Digital clients spending $10M+/year on
  services); the constraint is finding the higher-leverage "something else" for your
  specific market. (Neil, 2023-11-11) — agency-entrepreneurship / content-marketing.
- Analogy: Google chooses to sell ad inventory rather than services, and HubSpot
  chooses recurring software (with free tools) over services, because both are
  strictly better businesses than services when the platform/scale exists to
  support them — acknowledges he can't replicate either model exactly but adapted
  their "give away value, sell the bigger thing" playbook to the agency-services
  business. (Neil, 2023-11-11) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- ★ Biographical anecdote: used to sell a $97 course bundled with a free 30-minute
  phone call; only 17-20% of buyers redeemed the call, so his now-co-founder
  Mike Kamo (at the time, his separate ad-agency co-founder, pre-NP Digital)
  suggested removing the free-call perk — conversions were unchanged after removal,
  confirming the call added cost without adding sales. (Neil, 2023-11-11) —
  agency-entrepreneurship [self-reported].
- Recommends giving courses/eBooks away for free entirely, framing them as
  goodwill-building content rather than a revenue line, and monetizing via a
  separate, bigger offer. (Neil, 2023-11-11) — agency-entrepreneurship /
  content-marketing.
