# The Worst SEO Advice Ever! ("Content Is King")

## Summary

Neil names the worst SEO advice as the cliché **"content is king."** His counter: writing more/
better content no longer guarantees success ("go write 1,000 blog posts… traffic will go up, but
a very little amount," and few visitors convert). His own proof point: the Neil Patel blog
publishes **only one text article per week**, yet does ~**2M unique visitors/month** (returning
~2×/month ≈ **4M visits**, ≈ **6M page views**). Because there are already "a billion blogs… one
blog for every seven people," he spends the **80/20 the other way — 80% promoting content, 20%
writing it**. He then reframes his broader strategy: instead of competing in saturated content, he
invests in **less-competitive traffic channels** — free marketing tools (**Ubersuggest**),
marketing videos (this channel), and the **Marketing School** podcast. Verdict: content is still
good, but **promotion is king**, and there are more scalable, affordable traffic sources than
churning out articles. Reinforces the era's recurring content-saturation / promotion-first thesis.
