# An Entrepreneurs Guide to Growing Your Startup

## Summary
Long-form origin-story interview, unusually detailed even against the rest of the corpus.
**Teenage start**: says he began at 15.5 ("round up to say 16"), looking for a high-paying job on
Monster.com/CareerBuilder (pre-LinkedIn) but was blocked by certification and college-degree
requirements he didn't have; decided to build his own job-board site instead after noticing job-
board operators' stock valuations. Funded early contractor help by working minimum-wage jobs,
including cleaning restrooms at a theme park; the first site launched to zero traffic. Attributes
the decision to push through to "ignorance" — "I was young and I didn't know better... I didn't
know how the real world worked." Taught himself SEO after the site got traffic but no revenue,
reaching 100,000+ monthly visitors; pivoted to a performance-pay SEO pitch to businesses running
Google ads ("get you the traffic, pay me; if I don't, pay me zero"). **First real client**: a
power-supply manufacturer he says he drove ~$25M/year in revenue for via SEO; the client's owner
introduced him to his son, who ran an ad agency and brought in clients including Blue Cross and
Countrywide — four clients total generating ~$20K/month, at age 16. **2008 recession**: says the
ad-agency business began declining in the 2008 recession; around the same period he and a
co-founder (unnamed here) built **Crazy Egg** (conversion-optimization software) as a hedge,
which kept growing through the downturn, so they shut the ad agency and focused fully on Crazy
Egg, which he says "is still growing today," much larger than at founding. **College**: says his
parents forced him to attend despite his own reluctance, framing it as an "Indian kid" cultural
expectation ("no good Indian girl is going to marry you unless you get a college degree");
eventually his parents cared only that he marry, regardless of race, and he says his wife is
mixed-race ("a mutt"). Took 5.5 years to finish his degree, with the final 1.5 years spent on one
remaining class he had no urgency to finish because he was already making money; says college
"didn't do anything for me... it actually slowed me down." **NP Digital founding** (~5 years old
at time of filming, i.e. ~2018): started from nothing; first-year revenue ~$5M, second-year ~$18M,
grown to 700+ employees, #21 on the Inc. 500 its first eligible year; changed physical offices
three times in one year during the fastest growth (started at WeWork, sublet, then a bigger own
office), deliberately avoiding long (5-year) office leases. Co-founder is **Mike Kamo**, who runs
operations; states the company also now has a CEO, **Mike Gullaksen**, who runs the whole
company. **Self-assessed leadership style**: calls himself "a terrible leader" who is good at
ideas, vision, pitching, and sales but delegates people-management entirely; agrees with the
host's framing of him as more "artist" (loves the craft itself, indifferent to a fast financial
exit) than "entrepreneur" (serial, ROI/exit-focused) or "leader/manager" (builds and invests in
teams) — says Kamo is the "leader manager" of the two. Confirms age as **38** ("now") at time of
filming (10 days after stating 37 in [[2023-06-05-yt-Exw3t1MSHhE]] — see note below). States he
doesn't have work-life balance, travels almost every week (mostly international), but tries to be
fully present with his two kids (one daughter explicitly mentioned) when home, including
rearranging same-day podcast scheduling to pick his daughter up from school. States he doesn't
track headcount or revenue milestones closely, tracking instead "how many countries we're in,"
with a stated goal of reaching 50 countries. Confirms he does NOT keep track of company financial
benchmarks in the moment ("it's moving that fast... it's hard to even look at the benchmarks").
