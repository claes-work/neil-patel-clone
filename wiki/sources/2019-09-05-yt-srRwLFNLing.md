# How to Rank on Page 1 of Bing (Bing SEO)

## Summary

Neil argues Bing is under-served but still worth millions of visits/month, and gives
seven Bing-specific ranking tactics, several of which differ from Google. (1) **Exact-match
domains work better on Bing than on Google** — and if the .com is taken, a .org/.net/.co
exact-match still performs; if you have an existing site, get the **keyword into the URL**
(yourdomain.com/keyword). (2) **On-page keyword placement matters a lot** — H1, title tag,
meta description, first paragraph, and throughout the body. (3) **High-quality content** —
use the **Ubersuggest Content Ideas report** to see what's working, then do more of it,
fresh (don't copy). (4) **Social signals matter far more on Bing than Google** — Bing has
publicly stated it uses them; Neil notes Microsoft owns Bing and LinkedIn and was an early
Facebook investor, giving it social-data access. (5) **Use Bing Webmaster Tools** for
error/issue data. (6) **User experience** — pogo-sticking/bounce and brand queries feed
rankings; UX-poor sites won't hold Bing rankings. (7) Run the **NeilPatel SEO Analyzer**
(analyzes up to 150 pages free, optimized for Bing as well as Google).

## Key claims

- As of 2019-09-05, Neil said **exact-match domains perform better on Bing than on Google**,
  and non-.com exact-match extensions (.org/.net/.co) still rank well on Bing.
- As of 2019-09-05, Neil said **keyword-in-URL** is a strong Bing factor.
- As of 2019-09-05, Neil said **social signals are much more important for Bing** than
  Google, noting Bing publicly confirmed using them; he tied this to Microsoft owning Bing
  and LinkedIn and being an early Facebook investor.
- As of 2019-09-05, Neil said fast-acquired social shares make **Bing rankings climb
  quicker**.
- As of 2019-09-05, Neil said **user experience** (bounce/pogo-sticking, brand queries) is
  a growing Bing focus and that poor UX loses rankings over time.

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION (cross-engine nuance): Neil repeatedly says **social shares are not a
> direct Google ranking factor** but are a **direct Bing factor** — not a self-contradiction,
> but a distinction synthesis should preserve when writing any "do social shares help SEO"
> belief.
