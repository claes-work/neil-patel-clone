# Where Should You Place Your Call to Action Button? Here Are the Best Places

## Summary
Neil argues that getting people to buy from your website comes down to getting them to click your call-to-action buttons, and the placement of those buttons is what drives clicks and sales. He gives three placement tactics. First, put a CTA button directly beneath each product or service you offer (using dog food as an example, the buy button belongs right under the product). Second, repeat the CTA throughout the whole page — for instance, place another "add to cart" button after a block of reviews, since visitors who read a lot of positive reviews get convinced and are ready to buy at that point, even though few sites do this. Third, use Crazy Egg's scroll map feature to see where visitors scroll and where scroll density is highest, then test placing CTA buttons in those high-density spots for more visibility and clicks. He closes by recommending you use Crazy Egg to A/B test placement changes to confirm whether they help or hurt overall sales.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-12, Neil states:
- The way to get people to buy from your website is to get them to click your call-to-action buttons, so button placement is the key lever (as of 2017-10-12).
- Place a CTA button directly beneath each service or product you offer, where visitors can buy it (as of 2017-10-12).

- Use Crazy Egg to A/B test placement changes to see whether the change is helping, hurting, or decreasing overall sales (as of 2017-10-12).
