# What Is the Best Time to Tweet? | Here's My Twitter Schedule!

## Summary
Neil Patel argues the "best time to tweet" is not a specific hour but all day long. He shares his own numbers: when he tweeted about three times a day he never got more than 5,000 visitors/month from Twitter; ramping up to as many as 40 tweets a day pushed that to roughly 22,000 visitors over the last 30 days. Because there's always someone awake somewhere in the world, you should tweet from morning to night, ideally once per hour, seven days a week including holidays. To do this without staying up, he recommends scheduling tools like MeetEdgar (which recycles/promotes your content 24/7) and Buffer.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-08, Neil states:
- The best time to tweet is not a specific hour (like 8am or noon) — you should tweet throughout the day (as of 2017-11-08).
- When he used to tweet about three times a day, he never got more than ~5,000 visitors a month from Twitter (as of 2017-11-08).
- He now tweets up to 40 times a day, and over the last 30 days generated roughly 22,000 visitors a month from Twitter — up from ~5,000 (as of 2017-11-08).
- His Twitter stats vary month to month depending on what and how often he tweets and links out, and the type of articles (as of 2017-11-08).
- Rationale: people are awake around the world at all hours ("business never sleeps"), so tweet morning to night (as of 2017-11-08).
- Recommended cadence: multiple times a day, ideally once per hour, seven days a week — including Christmas, Thanksgiving, and other holidays (as of 2017-11-08).
- Use scheduling tools like MeetEdgar (which keeps promoting your content 24/7) or Buffer to tweet even while you sleep (as of 2017-11-08).
