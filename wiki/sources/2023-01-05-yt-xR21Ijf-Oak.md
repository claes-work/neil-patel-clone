# How We Boosted Our Revenue by 18% Using PayPal

## Summary
Sponsored-by-PayPal case study (disclosed upfront: "I would've created it
either way so you can copy my results") about adding PayPal as a checkout
option on Ubersuggest. Self-reports Ubersuggest's paying-customer geography
(~26% US, followed by UK, France, Germany, Japan) and that PayPal wasn't
offered initially because customers already had credit cards and requests for
it were rare. Co-founder Mike Kamo ([[wiki/entities/mike-kamo]]) told Neil that
his girlfriend's US-focused e-commerce site got ~40% of payments via PayPal,
prompting an experiment: adding PayPal to Ubersuggest's checkout. Within 30
days revenue grew ~16%, with 35%+ of customers choosing PayPal; by the time of
filming that revenue lift had grown to 18%. Self-reports that PayPal-paying
customers churn less (stay subscribed ~1 month longer than credit-card payers,
attributed to having both bank account and card linked to the service). Says
NP Digital now recommends adding PayPal to clients across verticals, with
lifts seen especially in retail, travel, insurance, and utilities. Extends with
general checkout-friction advice: optimize the full pre-checkout journey (page
load speed, click reduction), offer buy-now-pay-later (cites RBC: 20-30% lift
in retail conversions, 30-50% lift in ticket size), and use discount/promotion
partnerships like PayPal's Honey (cites Adobe Analytics: $8.9B spent online on
Black Friday; Honey's Chrome extension installed by 10M+ users) rather than
brand-cheapening price cuts.
