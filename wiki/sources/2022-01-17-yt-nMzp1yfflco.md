# The 6 Stages of Learning Digital Marketing - Which One Are You In? ★

## Summary
Presents a six-stage career-progression framework for learning digital marketing,
meant to be worked through in order: (1) **The Generalist Learner** — explore all
disciplines (social, paid, SEO, content, email) to map the field; (2) **Successes And
Failures** — test tactics on real (often pro-bono/cheap) clients or personal
projects, accepting failure as the learning mechanism; (3) **Niching Down** — pick one
discipline plus one client type once some disciplines prove easier/harder than
others (his own example: B2B was harder starting out but is now easier for him than
B2C after 20 years; gives "Facebook Ads for D2C e-commerce" as a model narrow niche,
citing Harmon Brothers/"Bacon & Eggs"-style creative as the craft ceiling); (4)
**The Portfolio** — document wins into a reusable "bag of tricks" playbook; (5)
**Scaling Up** — hire and automate repetitive work (name-checks UiPath for marketing-
process automation); (6) **Authority** — become a recognized voice via speaking,
content volume, and backlink/authority building. Frames skipping stages ("stage one
to stage six") as a common mistake.
