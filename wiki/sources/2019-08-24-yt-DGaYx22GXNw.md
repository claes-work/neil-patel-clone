# How to Leverage Content Marketing When You Have No Traffic (Small or New Websites)

## Summary

Neil gives three content-marketing tactics that work specifically when you start with **no
traffic**. (1) **Infographics** — in **Ubersuggest**, enter a keyword and open the
**content-ideas** report to find articles popular by social shares, backlinks, and search
traffic; pick one that's step-heavy or technical, rebuild its data as an infographic in
**Canva** (citing the source), then email everyone who **linked to the original article**
(Ubersuggest shows them) offering the easier-to-digest image. Infographics won't rank like
text but earn social shares and backlinks that lift the whole site's authority, helping your
text articles rank. (2) **Roundup lists** — build a "101 [X] resources you can't live
without" post that includes popular articles (with a paragraph or two describing each), then
email the people who linked to those articles asking for a link or share, and email the
article owners asking them to share your post. (3) **Q&A sites** — not by posting on Quora,
but by mining Quora for the popular questions/answers in your space, combining that with
**AnswerThePublic** question data, and running the URLs through Ubersuggest to see the (often
50–200) keywords each page ranks for; then write your own thorough, in-depth version covering
those keywords (thorough, not stuffed), and promote it with the outreach tactics above.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-08-24, Neil said **infographics work best for no-traffic sites**: rebuild a
  popular article's data in Canva, then outreach its **existing linkers** for shares/links.
- As of 2019-08-24, Neil said **infographics won't rank as well as text** but their social
  shares/backlinks **raise site authority**, helping text articles rank higher.
- As of 2019-08-24, Neil's **roundup-list** tactic: a "101 resources" post + a describing
  paragraph per item, then outreach both the linkers and the featured article owners.
- As of 2019-08-24, Neil said use **Quora for research, not posting** — mine its popular
  questions/answers, combine with AnswerThePublic, and check ranking keywords in Ubersuggest.
- As of 2019-08-24, Neil said write a **thorough, in-depth** version covering all the main
  keywords a page ranks for (50–200) — "it's not to stuff, it's to be thorough."

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If that article that's text-based was popular, the infographic version should be more popular... People prefer infographics more than text-based content."

> "Your infographics will start generating more social shares, more backlinks, which will boost the overall authority of your site, which then makes it easier for your text-based articles... to rank higher on Google."

> "The keywords that they're ranking for is the most important part because it's not just about the questions people are typing in. There's going to be in some cases 50, 100, 200 other keywords... When you create your own version... you want to cover all those main keywords. It's not to stuff, it's to be thorough."
