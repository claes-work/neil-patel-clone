# 7 Dead Simple Ways to Bring People Back to Your Site

## Summary

Neil says most sites see 90–95% of traffic as brand-new visitors and only 5–10%
returning, and gives seven ways to raise repeat visits. (1) **Remarketing** — pixel only
the high-intent pages (checkout, lead, key conversion pages), not all traffic, and make
the remarketing message the *opposite* of what failed the first time (if the landing page
sold emotionally, remarket logically). (2) **Push notifications** (Subscribers.com,
PushCrew) — one-click browser subscribe, no email needed; better open/click rates than
email. (3) **Intent-matched email** — send follow-up content matching why the person first
came (SEO reader → SEO email; CRO reader → CRO email); irrelevant blasts are why email
"fails." (4) **MobileMonkey** — a Facebook Messenger list with strong open/click rates,
free for now (Facebook will eventually charge). (5) **Exit pop-up** — but to gain a
**social follow** (FB/YouTube/IG/LinkedIn), not an email; higher conversion than the email
ask. (6) **Time-based events** — real webinars/contests/holiday promos with a 24–48h
expiry drive action. (7) **Consistency** — a fixed publishing cadence trains the audience
to return.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-08-29, Neil said typically only **5–10% of a site's visitors are returning**;
  ~90–95% are brand new.
- As of 2019-08-29, Neil said remarketing should target only **high-intent pages** and use
  the **opposite message** (emotional vs. logical) from the page that failed to convert.
- As of 2019-08-29, Neil said **push notifications and Facebook-Messenger (MobileMonkey)**
  beat email on open/click rates, and Messenger was, "for now," even beating push.
- As of 2019-08-29, Neil recommended an **exit pop-up for a social follow, not an email** —
  he'd found a higher conversion rate for the follow ask.
- As of 2019-08-29, Neil said his consistent cadence (blog every Tuesday, daily podcast,
  same three video days/week) drove **~40% repeat traffic** in the prior week.
