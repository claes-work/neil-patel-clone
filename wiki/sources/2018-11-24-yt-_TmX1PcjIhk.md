# When is the RIGHT Time to Hire a Business Lawyer?

## Summary

A short, personal monologue in which Neil frames one of his biggest entrepreneurial mistakes as not having proper legal counsel early on. His argument: startups get messy because founders skip legal documents ("I'll fix it once we're making money"), but doing well is precisely what triggers legal issues when the documentation isn't in place. He advises putting proper legal documents in place from the start — for VC raises (where lawyers are a given) but especially for informal deals with friends, where a handshake ruins friendships. The video is unusually biography-rich: Neil names his San Francisco lawyer **Joey Tran** (whom he praises as good, honest, and not the most expensive), recounts a lawyer's observation that most entrepreneurs mess up their first business's paperwork and only clean up after hitting problems, and lists the legal fights he has been through — FTC investigations, class-action lawsuits, and mediation with business partners. He also reveals that one of his business partners is his **brother-in-law**, and — contradicting the common "never do business with family" advice — says family partnerships have caused him the fewest problems because they're not petty about money and try to do what's best for the other person.
