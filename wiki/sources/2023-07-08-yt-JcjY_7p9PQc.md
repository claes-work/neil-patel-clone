# 7 Powerful Growth Hacks: Catapult Your Business to 10x Growth!

## Summary
Co-hosted "seven growth hacks" list segment. Hack #1 (Neil): use Hypefury or Typefully to auto-
repurpose a Twitter thread into a LinkedIn post and an Instagram carousel, and to get writer's-
block prompts. Hack #2 (Eric): partner with conferences (money, free speaking, or promotion help
in exchange) to get the conference organizer to broker one-on-one meetings with major attendees —
states they aim for ~8 meetings across 2 days per event and have closed large enterprise deals
this way. Hack #3 (Neil): feed Riverside.fm's AI transcription of a recorded podcast episode into
ChatGPT to auto-draft a 500-600 word blog post, which editors then expand with links/stats/trend
references — states this cuts blog-production time by 75-80%. Hack #4 (Neil): explicitly
self-labeled "black hatty" — buying verified LinkedIn accounts with 500+ connections (via
playerup.com) to seed early comments on a company's own posts for a virality boost; states NP
Digital does not do this itself and would not recommend it for a larger/established business, but
reports never having seen anyone banned for it. Hack #5 (Eric): retention.com — a
US-only-legal-in-practice (explicitly flagged as gray/black in GDPR jurisdictions) tool that
cross-references e-commerce site visitors against a third-party identity list to convert
anonymous traffic into named email leads at a claimed 20-30% match rate (vs. a typical 1-2%
organic opt-in rate), with an explicit expectation the loophole closes within a few years. Hack #6
(Eric, building on Neil's earlier free-tools framework): give away a free physical book, charging
only shipping, to build an email/sales funnel (name-drops ClickFunnels' book-funnel templates) —
frames this as one of the easiest ways to generate qualified leads/sales from a book. A tangent on
webinar funnel structure follows (Neil describes NP Digital's own historical webinar-to-landing-
page-to-upsell flow, noting they've since shifted mainly to enterprise lead-gen rather than
webinar-driven product sales); Eric adds a general webinar thank-you-page "MIFGE" (Most Incredible
Free Gift Ever, crediting the term to Dan Kennedy via Russell Brunson) framework.
