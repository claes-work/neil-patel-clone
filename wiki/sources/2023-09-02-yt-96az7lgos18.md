# 100 million views and $900 return!

## Summary
Short Marketing-School-style segment reacting to a viral case: a new YouTube channel (launched
April 15, niche: miniature katanas) hit ~109 million views with only ~$900 in estimated ad
revenue (per a public screenshot the creator, "Isaac," shared), because he'd forgotten to enable
the YouTube Partner Program and wasn't prioritizing ad revenue — he was instead driving katana
product sales. Neil's headline take: don't optimize for views/vanity metrics (subscriber counts,
view counts); optimize for revenue and conversions against a specific ideal-customer persona.
He argues mass-appeal, highly-viral content (like novelty product niches) usually converts
poorly because most viewers aren't qualified buyers, while narrow, specific niche content
(his example: content built for "an engineer... focused on cybersecurity") generates
disproportionately more revenue per view because it's tightly relevant to a real buyer's
problem, even though it won't rack up big view counts — illustrating with a hypothetical law
firm specializing in mesothelioma cases, which would never hit 100M views and wouldn't want to
given near-zero qualified-buyer overlap with a mass audience.

## Key claims
- States marketers should not optimize for views/subscriber counts (vanity metrics); the metric
  that matters is revenue/conversions against a defined ideal-customer persona (self-reported
  opinion, dated 2023-09-02).
- States highly viral, mass-appeal content (e.g. novelty-niche videos) tends to convert poorly
  because most viewers are not qualified buyers, whereas narrow/specific niche content converts
  better per-view despite lower view counts (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-09-02).
- Uses a hypothetical mesothelioma-law-firm example to illustrate that some niches should never
  target mass-viral reach, since near-zero of a 100M-view audience would be qualified buyers
  (self-reported illustrative example, dated 2023-09-02).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Don't focus on views, focus on conversions. Whatever you're doing, try to figure out who your
> ideal customer is. Create a persona and create content that helps them solve their problems.

> If you get tons of views and you're going viral, most of those people are not buying. But on
> the flip side, if you create really niche content... you're not going to get the views but you
> can generate more revenue.
