# SEO Techniques to Boost Organic Traffic

## Summary
Solo guest interview, likely from the same recording session as [[2023-08-12-yt-GS07GGY1sEw]]
(similar host voices/dynamic, published five days apart). Asked for the single biggest lever to
improve SEO, Neil gives a two-part answer. First (for the majority of sites): update your "money
pages" — the highest-traffic, highest-converting pages — on a quarterly basis, since content
freshness is why sites like Wikipedia dominate rankings, and stale content is the most common
neglected fix he sees at his own agency's clients. He illustrates search competition at scale live
using "auto insurance": ~301,000 monthly US searches against 1.18 billion results (~3,920
competing pages per searcher), noting only page 1 realistically captures traffic ("people hide dead
bodies on page two... that's page three"). Second (more creative): the free-tool-for-backlinks
strategy already logged from [[2023-07-31-yt-iFynPlXb7k8]] — build and give away a tool that would
otherwise be paywalled (his example: fitness-niche calculators), which organically accumulates
backlinks and domain authority over time better than infographic/content-only outreach. He notes
ChatGPT lowers the engineering cost of building such tools, though he assesses ChatGPT's software-
engineering quality as still "pretty shitty" as of this recording (while conceding it built a
functional "Pong" game in 60 seconds). He describes NP Digital's own scale ("nine-plus figure
revenue a year") and history: free tools historically drove 40%+ of agency leads, though at the
company's current maturity (5+ years old) most new business now comes from word-of-mouth,
referrals, and RFPs. He names two further growth strategies beyond building free tools: (1) buying
underutilized, traffic-heavy web properties and monetizing them — citing the February [2022]
acquisition of AnswerThePublic for $8.6M despite it being reported as doing "100 a month in
profit" pre-acquisition (a figure he deflates once employee costs are counted), and (2) buying a
former competitor's domain purely for residual traffic — describing personally offering roughly
$500K to buy the domain of his own former startup, KISSmetrics, after it folded, purely for its
~1M+/month visits, which fed leads to NP Digital. He also details a cross-vertical checkout-
partnership tactic (non-competing complementary products upselling/downselling each other at
checkout, e.g. toothbrush and toothpaste sellers). On NP Digital's global expansion: from 6
regions the prior year to an announced 16 (targeting 20) countries with local hired leaders,
localized/translated websites, and full-time recruiters who specifically target repeatedly-
promoted talent poached from larger competing agencies (WPP, Omnicom); the company is wholly
owned in most markets except partner arrangements in Brazil and India, which NP Digital intends to
eventually buy out.
