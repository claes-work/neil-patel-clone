# STOP Using Facebook Ads - The Best Alternative Ad Platforms

## Summary
Neil argues Facebook ads are getting more expensive/competitive, and (for advertisers
who haven't yet mastered Facebook) surveys five alternative paid-ad platforms with
stats for each: YouTube ads (same audience segmentation as Facebook; $0.10-0.30
cost-per-view; ~10-15% more expensive than Facebook but converts better); Instagram ads
(1B+ monthly active users, 50%+ visit daily, 80% of users outside the US, same
Facebook-level targeting granularity, photo/video/carousel/slideshow/story formats,
text-heavy ads penalized); Pinterest ads (93% of 300M active users use it to plan
purchases per Shopify, $50 average order value — higher than other major platforms);
Twitter ads (330M+ active users, 42% daily active per Pew Research, 24% of US adults
use Twitter); LinkedIn ads (660M+ users, 80% of B2B social leads come from LinkedIn
per Oktopost, higher cost but higher B2B ticket value/CAC justification).

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-29, Neil said YouTube ads run $0.10-$0.30 per view and are typically
  10-15% more expensive than Facebook ads but convert better, offsetting the cost gap.
- As of 2020-02-29, Neil said Instagram has 1B+ monthly active users, over half visit
  daily, and 80% of the platform's user base lives outside the United States.
- As of 2020-02-29, Neil cited Shopify data that 93% of Pinterest's 300M active users
  use the platform to plan purchases, and that Pinterest's average order value ($50)
  is higher than any other major social platform.
- As of 2020-02-29, Neil cited Pew Research that Twitter has 330M+ active users, 42%
  of whom are daily active, and that 24% of US adults use Twitter.
- As of 2020-02-29, Neil cited Oktopost data that 80% of all B2B social media leads are
  generated through LinkedIn, which has 660M+ users.
