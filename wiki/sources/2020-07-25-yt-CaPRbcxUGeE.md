# How to Outrank and Steal Traffic From Your Competition

## Summary
Solo tactical walkthrough of a competitor content-gap-and-outreach playbook, using
Moz.com as the worked example. Process: (1) run a competitor domain through
Ubersuggest's traffic analyzer, (2) open "top SEO pages" to find their most-visited
content pages, (3) pick a page that's outdated or thin (worked example: a Moz Google
My Business guide "updated in 2018" still pulling ~18K visits/month), (4) read it
closely and identify content gaps — his example: the Moz article is thorough but
generic, so his proposed improved version would segment tips by business type
(real estate vs. bakery vs. salon) so readers "get the answer to your question"
faster, (5) pull every keyword driving traffic to that competitor page via "View All
under estimated visits" and work them into the new article, (6) once the better page
is published, pull the competitor page's backlink list via Ubersuggest and email each
linking site pitching the updated version as a replacement link target. Neil frames
this as scalable: repeat for a competitor's top 20, 40, 60, 100 pages, and across
multiple competitors, to compound traffic gains. Notes that outreach conversion is low
per email but "a percentage of them will link back."

## Key claims
- As of 2020-07-25, Neil said Google favors updated/fresh content over older content
  even when the older page still ranks and drives significant traffic (worked example:
  an ~18K-visits/month Moz page last updated in 2018), framing content freshness as
  an exploitable ranking lever against complacent competitors.
- As of 2020-07-25, Neil's content-improvement heuristic is to identify what a
  ranking competitor page is missing — his example: segmenting generic advice by
  audience sub-type (business type) so readers reach their specific answer faster,
  rather than making the page longer for its own sake.
- As of 2020-07-25, Neil said most competitor-outreach emails asking sites to
  relink to an improved page get ignored, but "a percentage of them will link back,
  even if it's a small percentage" — framing volume/scale (top 20 → 40 → 60 → 100
  competitor pages, across multiple competitors) as the lever that makes the tactic
  worthwhile despite low per-email conversion.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Google rather would rank updated content that's new and fresh compared to older content... if I end up creating fresh new content... I could potentially rank higher than them."

> "As a business owner, you don't want to go through thousands and thousands of words of text. You just want to get the answer to your question."

> "Now most people will ignore me when I email them for outreach to convince them to link to me. But a percentage of them will link back, even if it's a small percentage."
