# 7 Rules of Money

## Summary

★ L3-candidate — Neil's wealth/money philosophy, a persona-relevant belief set distinct from
his usual tactical marketing. Seven rules: (1) **You don't make money by wanting it — you make
it by solving problems.** He cites Elon Musk (SpaceX for space, The Boring Company for traffic)
as someone who got rich by solving problems, not chasing riches; your solution must be more
efficient and cheaper than alternatives. (2) **You don't make it overnight** — unless you win
the lotto, wealth takes putting in your dues; no YouTube star or celebrity made it overnight.
(3) **Make your money work for you** — invest rather than buying fancy homes, cars, and
Ferraris; investments compound while status objects don't. (4) **It's easier to save money than
to make it** — once you have some, don't blow it; small savings (even using coupons at the
grocery store) add up and grow net worth. (5) **Learn from other successful investors** — read
how others (e.g. an IBM CEO's book) made and lost money to avoid repeating their mistakes.
(6) **It's okay to miss opportunities** — he missed the Airbnb investment and others; you won't
catch them all, so do your due diligence and skip anything that doesn't look right. (7) **It's
all about timing** — quoting Warren Buffett, "be fearful when others are greedy and greedy when
others are fearful"; be wary in a crypto mania, and buy when a market (e.g. real estate down 40%)
has crashed and others are fearful, holding for the long term.
