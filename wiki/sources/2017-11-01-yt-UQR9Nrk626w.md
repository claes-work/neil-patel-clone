# HOW TO HACK INSTAGRAM: How to Get Your Customers to Promote Your Products On Instagram

## Summary

In this short 2017 video, Neil Patel outlines three tactics for getting customers to promote products on Instagram: running monthly hashtag giveaways, inserting flyers/cards into shipped products that prompt buyers to post and tag the brand, and paying influencers to feature the product. He closes by warning that Instagram is a slow-payoff channel that should be run alongside SEO, content marketing, and paid ads rather than in isolation. The "hack" framing refers to marketing tactics (user-generated content and customer advocacy), not any technical exploit.

## Key claims

- Run a monthly giveaway where entrants must use a specific branded hashtag to be entered; this drives large volumes of participation, brand awareness, and sales. (Neil Patel, 2017-11-01)
- Use your existing email list to spur the giveaway: email subscribers each month with the giveaway details and contest rules, and they will go enter on Instagram. (2017-11-01)
- Include a physical flyer, pamphlet, brochure, or card in every product shipment inviting the buyer to photograph themselves using the product, post it on Instagram, and tag the brand — framed in a "join the club / join the crew" cool-and-fun tone. (2017-11-01)
- This insert tactic applies even to products sold on Amazon; put a flyer in the package to prompt Instagram promotion. Neil notes he is surprised more sellers do not already do this. (2017-11-01)
- Pay influencers with large followings to promote the product; top influencers will not enter a giveaway or do favors for free, but paid promotion works. (2017-11-01)
- Social proof from popular influencers triggers a bandwagon effect — when people see well-known figures using a product, they buy it too. (2017-11-01)
- Cited example: Beats by Dre grew largely through celebrities (NBA players wearing the headphones before games, the Kardashians posting photos), and was later acquired by Apple for billions. (Neil Patel's framing, 2017-11-01)
- Do not expect ROI right away from Instagram; Neil says it takes six months to a year to do well. (2017-11-01)
- Instagram should not be relied on as a standalone channel — combine it with SEO, content marketing, and pay-per-click; results come from the mixture, not any single channel. (2017-11-01)
