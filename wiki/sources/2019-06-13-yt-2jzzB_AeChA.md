# The Hard Truth About Marketing & What Will Stop Working In The Near Future

## Summary

Neil's "hard truth" for 2019: **marketing keeps getting harder** — ads more expensive, SEO
slower, organic social reach more throttled — because venture-funded companies pour raised
money mainly into marketing and sales, driving up costs ("the best marketer is the one who can
spend the most money on marketing"). His five adjustments: (1) **Put the user first** —
marketing "isn't about marketing anymore"; a great product wins, so survey (Hotjar,
SurveyMonkey), call, or meet customers and keep tweaking until you delight them. (2) **Go
omnichannel** — one channel no longer delivers old results; combine channels, collect emails,
use push notifications. (3) **Differentiate** — online businesses are "a dime a dozen"; find
what competitors miss (his recurring Apple ecosystem example). (4) **Rule of seven** — people
convert after ~7 brand touches, so use remarketing, push notifications, and email (cheap or
free) to keep re-engaging. (5) **Keep content fresh** — Google now favors fresh content and
fast load times among a billion blogs, so continually update content and improve speed or
rankings and sales dip.
