# My Biggest Advertising Mistake (and What I Learned) ★

## Summary
A self-described biggest professional mistake, framed as a cautionary/humility story
(opens by explicitly disclaiming that he has "always been at the level I'm at now" and
naming Elon Musk as more successful than him). Despite the title, the mistake is about
website messaging, not paid advertising: he says KISSmetrics (started 2008, an
analytics business co-founded with Hiten Shah per SUBJECT.md) had an unclear value
proposition on its site for "years," and he spent that period tinkering with the
headline without success. Once he shortened the headline, sharpened the value prop,
added social proof, and offered free trials, he says conversions jumped almost
instantly by 40% — meaning, by his own account, roughly three years of a ~40%
conversion shortfall. He quantifies the total cost of the mistake at **$1,250,391**.
Draws five lessons: (1) value prop is everything — cites Peep Laja that a weak/unclear
value prop is the number one issue limiting website growth, and recommends audience
surveys (SurveyMonkey, Crazy Egg, Hotjar) to source the audience's own language for
messaging; (2) social proof is essential — cites Finances Online that 93% of buyers
read reviews before purchasing, drawing an Amazon star-rating analogy; (3) offer free
trials — cites Slack converting 30% of freemium users, with some opt-out free-trial
models reaching closer to 60%; frames free/cheap trials as newly viable due to cloud
computing lowering costs versus "back in the day" when he started his first business;
(4) build trust — cites 46% of consumers say they'd pay more to buy from brands they
trust; (5) attention spans are short — states 80% of readers never make it past the
headline, so the value prop must be stated immediately. Closes with an NP Digital
plug.
