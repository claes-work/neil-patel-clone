# 7 Social Media Metrics You Need to Track

## Summary
A tactical rundown of eight metrics to track on social media instead of vanity
numbers ("4.5 billion users on social media" is explicitly dismissed as not
moving the needle). (1) Reach — the number of people who see content; check what
share is followers vs. non-followers and whether they fit the target
region/age; cites 75% of the world's population over 13 uses social media. (2)
Impressions — cites Meta's impressions grew 10% in 2021 as a benchmark for
judging whether a creator's own impressions are keeping pace with platform-wide
growth. (3) Engagement rate — cites Facebook (0.06%) and Instagram (0.68%)
benchmark rates, but recommends benchmarking against direct competitors instead
of platform-wide stats. (4) Amplification rate — shares-per-post as a ratio of
followers; credits Avinash Kaushik (digital marketer at Google) for coining the
term. (5) Virality rate — shares as a percentage of impressions rather than of
followers. (6) Audience growth rate — new followers as a percentage of total
audience; recommends TikTok for fastest growth, citing 105% US user growth over
"the last couple years." (7) Social referrals — cites HubSpot: consumers are
71% more likely to purchase based on social referrals, likened to Amazon
reviews. (8) Bonus/most important — ROI, tracked via Google Analytics goal/
conversion tracking plus post-purchase surveys asking how a customer found the
business. Closes with an NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- Cites (Meta, as cited, dated 2023-01-02): platform impressions grew 10% in

- Cites benchmark engagement rates (as cited, dated 2023-01-02): Facebook
  0.06%, Instagram 0.68% — but recommends benchmarking against direct
  competitors' engagement rate instead.
- Credits Avinash Kaushik (Google digital marketer/author) with coining

  (source unnamed, as cited, dated 2023-01-02).
