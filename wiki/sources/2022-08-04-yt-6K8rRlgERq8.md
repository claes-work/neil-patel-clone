# My Biggest Marketing Win

## Summary
Neil frames his single biggest marketing win not as one channel or tactic but as
**omnichannel marketing** — deliberately leveraging every available channel (SEO,
social, email, free-tool releases) rather than concentrating on one. His reasoning,
drawn from "20+ years" in marketing: platform algorithms inevitably shift and eventually
stop favoring any given business, so relying on one or two channels is fragile. He cites
Microsoft, Apple, IBM, Accenture, and Airbnb as companies that never source all traffic
from a single channel, and argues a channel doesn't need to be equally scalable or
profitable as others to be worth keeping — "something is better than nothing."

## Key claims
- Self-credits "biggest marketing win" to omnichannel strategy — SEO, social, email
  marketing, and free-tool releases combined, not any single channel (as of 2022-08-01,
  self-reported).
- Reasoning: over 20+ years in marketing he has observed that platform algorithms
  eventually stop favoring any given business/channel, making single-channel
  dependence risky (as of 2022-08-04).
- Cites Microsoft, Apple, IBM, Accenture, Airbnb as examples of companies that never
  source all their traffic/sales from one channel (as of 2022-08-04, unsourced
  assertion).
- Advises keeping a channel active as long as it generates any profitable traffic, even
  if it can't scale as well as others ("something is better than nothing") (as of
  2022-08-04).
