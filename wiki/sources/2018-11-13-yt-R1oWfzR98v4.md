# 7 SEO Experiments To SKYROCKET Your Traffic

_Recording of the final session of a live "Advanced SEO Summit"; host **Clayton** moderates and
runs audience Q&A. **Neil presents his "Demystifying SEO" deck by screen-share** through his real
analytics. Only **Neil-attributed** material trains the persona; moderator/audience lines are
context. Frameworks by others are credited (Brian Dean skyscraper; Rand Fishkin CTR test; Tyler,
who advised the URL change)._

## Summary

Neil presents **7 SEO experiments** run on his own sites, framed by a case: growing
**neilpatel.com organic search from ~9,029 → ~449,000 visits/month over Jan-2015→Jan-2017**.

1. **Does publishing frequency/quantity raise search traffic?** He tested 1x/wk up to 14x/wk,
   deleted low performers, translated into 4 languages (1,919 posts total). **Result: after a few
   hundred posts, traffic flattens** — more volume alone doesn't lift search traffic. The real
   payoff: volume reveals **unpredictable natural winners**; find them in Search Console (Pages →
   queries), then **skyscraper + build links** to those pages (his Facebook post went 4k→17k).
2. **Can social data speed up CTR optimization?** CTR raises rankings (Google swaps a higher-CTR #2
   above #1). The slow way is Search Console (30-day feedback loops). The fast way: **test title-tag
   variants on Twitter** (10+ tweets/day) to see which get the most engagement, then apply the
   winner — CTR gains lift rankings **gradually** over time. Title tactics: numbers, negative/curiosity
   words, "what is," ~**six-word** titles, powers/adjectives, the year.
3. **Does Google rank brands higher?** Yes — he **manufactured brand-query spikes**. Using Google
   Trends as the proxy for brand strength, he ran the **"Who is Neil Patel?"** campaign — paying
   models/influencers (via friends Dan & Brandon) to post his name **all on the same day each week**
   (e.g. Tuesdays). Concentrated same-day volume → search spikes → **~24% traffic lift**; takes
   months to kick in and must run consistently ~2 months. (Cites Shreds and a Gary Vaynerchuk
   phone-number-billboard stunt as parallels.)
4. **Is content really king — or are tools better?** He built the **SEO Analyzer tool** (~$20-30k);
   it climbed with **no link building** and became one of his top pages / top Search-Console
   sources — beating his homepage. **Tools = best ROI**, works B2B and B2C (HubSpot's tools per Ryan).
5. **Does bounce rate impact rankings?** He cut load time ~31%, compressed images, killed the
   **entry** pop-up (not exit), enlarged text, shortened paragraphs, pushed content above the fold.
   Biggest levers: **removing the entry pop-up + content above the fold**. US-only bounce
   **85.7%→72.9%**, traffic ~doubled. User-metric gains lift rankings **slowly** (Google guards
   against spam). Note: **mobile pop-ups showed no ranking penalty** in his data.
6. **Keyword density vs. thoroughness?** Adjusting **density did nothing**; making the page **more
   thorough** (covering the whole topic — his "how to start a blog" page covering every platform)
   moved it page-3→page-2. **Topical thoroughness > density.**
7. **Do URLs matter?** Removing dates from URLs (**/year/month/day/title → /blog/title**) grew search
   **~40% in one month** (283k→449k). Rationale: **semantic/topical relevance** — a marketing post
   isn't "about" a date. Requirements: schema **breadcrumbs**, **301** old→new, update **all internal
   links**, and **make every change at once** (done piecemeal ~6 times, it never sticks). URL change
   advised by **Tyler** (paid $20k CAD).

Long Q&A reinforces: rank new sites via **topical depth/relevance** (Abraham Lincoln Wikipedia,
pizza-shop example); **pick big low-competition niches** via Google Trends (nutrition/green tea over
marketing); favorite tools **SEMrush** (top paid keywords → target profitable organic terms) and
**Ahrefs** (top pages → replicate content), with SEMrush preferred for region coverage; sell SEO to
bosses using SEMrush traffic-cost estimates (and he believes real value is **3-4x** the reported
number); ideal local site structure = keyworded homepage, breadcrumbs, sidebar links to top pages.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-11-13, case metric: **neilpatel.com organic ~9,029 → ~449,000/month (Jan-2015→Jan-2017)**.
- **Exp 1 — frequency:** after a few hundred posts, **more volume alone flattens**; value is
  surfacing **unpredictable winners** to skyscraper + link-build (Facebook page 4k→17k).
- **Exp 2 — CTR:** CTR raises rankings; **test title tags on Twitter** for fast feedback vs Search
  Console's 30-day loop; gains lift rankings gradually. Title tactics: numbers, curiosity/negatives,
  "what is," ~6 words, year.
- **Exp 3 — brand:** Google favors brands; he **manufactured same-day brand-query spikes**
  ("Who is Neil Patel") for **~24% lift**; needs ~2 months, rotate influencers.
- **Exp 4 — tools > content:** SEO Analyzer (~$20-30k) ranked with **no links**, became a top page
  (beats homepage); **tools = best ROI**, B2B and B2C.
- **Exp 5 — bounce/user metrics:** removing the **entry pop-up** + **content above the fold** cut
  US bounce **85.7%→72.9%**, ~doubled traffic; rankings rise slowly. **Mobile pop-ups = no penalty**
  in his data.
- **Exp 6 — density vs thoroughness:** density adjustments did nothing; **thoroughness/topical
  coverage** moved rankings up.
- **Exp 7 — URLs:** dropping dates (**/y/m/d/title → /blog/title**) grew search **~40% in a month**
  (283k→449k); needs breadcrumbs+schema, 301s, internal-link updates, **all changes at once**.
- As of 2018-11-13, rank new sites by **topical depth/relevance**; **pick big low-competition
  niches** (Google Trends); favorite tools **SEMrush** + **Ahrefs**; he believes reported
  traffic/value is **~3-4x** understated.
