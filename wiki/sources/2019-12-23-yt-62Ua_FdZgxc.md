# The 5 BEST Digital Marketing Side Hustles ($20 to $195 Per Hour)

## Summary
Solo career/monetization video: five digital-marketing skills you can sell freelance,
each with a claimed going hourly rate, aimed at people who want to quit a nine-to-five.
(1) **Behavioral design (~$195/hr)** — not visual design; you decide placement and layout
and why, deliver wireframes, and move conversion, lifetime value, repeat usage and
engagement metrics (newsletter signups, add-to-cart, community joins, lead forms), which
Neil says also helps Google rankings by reducing bounce. (2) **Lead magnets ($20–100/hr)**
— most businesses struggle to generate leads; the work is picking the offer and shipping
it with free tools like **Hello Bar**, using **Ubersuggest** keyword data to avoid building
magnets nobody wants; the upsell is that the same client then hires you for the drip
sequence at another $20–100/hr, because a business that can't build a magnet can't build
the follow-up either. (3) **Paid social ads (~$145/hr)** — more creative flexibility than
Google (remarketing video vs. banners, image choice); because there are more advertisers
than inventory, a business owner who self-serves Facebook ads will usually lose money,
which is what creates the consulting demand. (4) **Conversion-rate optimization** —
everyone buys traffic (he cites Google's ~$100B+/yr ad income and Facebook's scale) and
pays designers and developers, but almost nobody optimizes conversion; the work spans copy
as well as design, plus checkout bumps and up/down-sells; tools named are **Crazy Egg** (his
own company) and **Hotjar**, and he points beginners at **Conversion XL** to learn it free.
Moving a 100k-visitor site from 3% to 4% is framed as large money for the client, and he
argues traffic only gets more expensive over time, so the skill compounds. (5) **Pricing
strategy consulting (~$115/hr)** — he says firms like **Price Intelligently** charge six
figures per pricing campaign and it's worth it because companies average a 15% new-revenue
increase; the craft is price elasticity, willingness to pay, maximizing LTV and structuring
pricing grids, and he tells viewers to learn it free from **ProfitWell**'s articles and
tutorials and then sell the same service.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil said behavioral-design experts were getting roughly **$195/hour**, the highest-paid of the five side hustles he listed.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil said behavioral design requires no visual-design tooling (no Photoshop or Canva) — you only need to know where elements should be placed in the layout and why, and can deliver wireframes to the client.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil framed behavioral-design outcomes as conversion rate, customer lifetime value, repeat usage and on-site behavior metrics — and said keeping people on the site instead of bouncing also helps the client's Google rankings.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil said lead-magnet creation pays **$20–$100/hour** depending on where in the world you are, and that many businesses struggle to generate leads at all.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil recommended **Hello Bar** (his own tool) to deploy the opt-in and **Ubersuggest** keyword data to decide which lead magnet to build so you don't waste time on offers nobody opts into.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil said the follow-up drip sequence is a second, separately billed engagement (another $20–$100/hour) because a client who cannot create a lead magnet also cannot create the follow-up sequence — and opting in does not mean converting.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil said paid ads on social media pay roughly **$145/hour**, and that social offers more flexibility than Google search (remarketing videos instead of banners, image/creative choice).
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil said a business owner who just "pops up ads" on Facebook will likely lose money because there are **more advertisers than inventory** — which is why they hire a paid-ads expert.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil said Google generates roughly **$100+ billion a year** in AdWords income and that Facebook became a multi-billion-dollar company on paid advertising — evidence that everyone is paying for traffic.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil argued the CRO arbitrage is that people pay for traffic, designers and developers but very few optimize conversion rates, and that CRO covers copy, checkout bumps, upsells and downsells — not just design elements.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil used the example that taking a site with 100,000 monthly visitors from a 3% to a 4% conversion rate is a large income gain for the client (assuming the product's ROI supports it).
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil said traffic is not getting cheaper over time — the trend is more expensive — which makes CRO a good skill to learn.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil said pricing-strategy consultants were getting roughly **$115/hour**, and that firms like **Price Intelligently** charge six figures per pricing campaign because companies average a **15% increase in new revenue**.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil described pricing consulting as CRO applied to price: elasticity, what people will pay and why, maximizing LTV, and structuring pricing grids.
- As of 2019-12-23, Neil pointed viewers to free education as the on-ramp for two of the five hustles — **Conversion XL** for CRO and **ProfitWell** for pricing — explicitly noting you can learn from ProfitWell's free material and then charge for the same services they sell.
