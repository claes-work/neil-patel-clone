# The Insane Canva Method for Creating Infographics (Ep. 2597)

## Summary
Short daily episode on using AI to generate infographics at scale. Eric drives the segment, describing a workflow he saw from the "Income Stream Surfers" YouTube channel: layer ChatGPT + a Canva integration + DALL-E (plugins) to prompt an infographic into existence for "pennies on the dollar," predicting every blog post will soon carry AI-made infographics. Neil is more measured and grounded in hands-on testing: his team has actually produced infographics this way, and the recurring failure isn't image quality but the *flow* — how the design elements and transitions tie together across points — which still requires a human to fix. Neil's practical takeaway is that AI is most useful for the data/research half of an infographic while humans still own the design polish, though both agree the human-in-the-loop requirement will shrink over time as the tools improve (ChatGPT now multimodal, and not even a year old at recording).

## Key claims
- You can create infographics by layering ChatGPT + Canva + DALL-E together via plugins; it has improved a lot versus the past. (Neil, 2023-11-08 — "I know that you can end up creating infographics with ChatGPT and DALL-E... it sounds like it's much better now than it was")
- Neil's team has actually tested creating many infographics through ChatGPT; the core problem was not image quality but the *flow* — how design elements and transitions tie together from one point to the next. (Neil, 2023-11-08)
- When the flow broke, the fix was to delete the sections that weren't working and have a human recreate and reintegrate just those sections — saves some time, but the AI output is hard to modify to flow well. (Neil, 2023-11-08)
- Neil's team last did an AI-driven full infographic ~1.5–2 months ago and repeats it "every few months"; increasingly they find it's quicker to just have a human do it. (Neil, 2023-11-08)
- Where AI is genuinely useful for infographics is data collection and research — they still use AI to source data points on a weekly basis (they produce infographics weekly for customers); the design element is where AI isn't good enough yet because they're "so picky on the design quality." (Neil, 2023-11-08)
- The space moves so fast that a test from 45 days ago is effectively "years" old in AI terms; ChatGPT is now multimodal (sketch a rough infographic, photograph it, have it work off that), so results vary and improve rapidly. (Neil, 2023-11-08)
- ChatGPT hadn't even been out for a year as of recording; this will keep getting better and content-creation lift will keep being taken off, but for now a human in the loop is still needed. (Neil, 2023-11-08)
- Neil predicts a future where you won't need a human in the loop — the infographic will still come out choppy, but you'll fix it through prompts rather than having a human "go in." (Neil, 2023-11-08)
