# My Marketing Plan Process — 6 Steps to Marketing Any Business

## Summary

Neil's diagnosis: tactics fail when you copy them without a plan. He gives a **6-step marketing
plan** framework: (1) **Define your goals** — traffic/brand awareness vs. leads vs. revenue vs.
e-commerce sales; wrong or no goals means working on tactics that don't serve the objective;
(2) **Understand your customer** — beyond the surface product need: why they'd choose you, what
problems/thoughts drive the decision; learn it by **talking to them** (surveys via SurveyMonkey,
phone calls, showing them your site/product/messaging), not just Google Analytics; (3) **Assess
key strengths** — know what you're good at (he's strong at SEO/content marketing, weak vs. Tai
Lopez at social) and lead with it rather than doing every channel at once; (4) **Pick one
marketing channel** — "super laser focus" on a single channel first (SEO, content, Facebook,
AdWords) to get results, then do competitive research (replicate what works for established
competitors, e.g. via **Ubersuggest** — enter a competitor URL to see traffic, top pages, links,
social shares, keywords); (5) [presented within step four's flow] competitive research; (6)
**Define how you'll make money** — traffic is only half the battle; design the monetization
(lead capture + sales process, or funnel with upsells/downsells) using tools like **Hello Bar**
(email capture), **Subscribers** (push notifications / return visits), and **Crazy Egg** (A/B
testing). Note: Neil verbally numbers "six steps" but competitive research is folded into the
channel step, so the on-screen count is loose.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-05-16, Neil said copied tactics fail without a **plan**: "just because you market
  something in a specific way that someone else teaches you, doesn't mean it's going to work."
- As of 2019-05-16, Neil's step 1 is **defining goals** (traffic/awareness vs. leads vs. revenue
  vs. e-commerce) — the "biggest mistake" is marketing without defined goals.
- As of 2019-05-16, Neil said **understand the customer deeply** by talking to and surveying them
  (SurveyMonkey, phone calls), not just reading analytics.
- As of 2019-05-16, Neil said **assess your key strengths and pick ONE channel first** with "super
  laser focus" before eventually going omnichannel; he named himself strong at SEO/content and
  Tai Lopez as stronger at social media.
- As of 2019-05-16, Neil recommended **Ubersuggest** for competitive research (competitor URL →
  traffic, top pages, links, social shares, keywords) and said to **replicate, not oppose**, what
  established, profitable competitors do.
- As of 2019-05-16, Neil's final step is **defining monetization** (lead capture + sales process,
  or a funnel with upsells/downsells), tooled with Hello Bar, Subscribers, and Crazy Egg.
