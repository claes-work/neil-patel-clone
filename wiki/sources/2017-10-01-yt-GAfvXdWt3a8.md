# 3 Easy Ways to Increase Your Online Revenue by 15% (or More) Without Acquiring New Customers

## Summary
Neil argues that you do not need new customers to grow revenue. He gives three tactics for extracting more revenue from your existing customer base. First, add upsells and downsells at checkout — the lever that makes them convert is speed and automation (an upsell that delivers the result faster and more automatically sells far better). Second, use marketing automation, especially email: collect emails on every page (he suggests Hello Bar) and set up automated sequences that pitch your product or service after the first two or three emails. Third, keep creating more products and services to sell to the same user base over time, since your best buyers are the people who have already bought from you. He cites Amazon as the example of a company that keeps winning by continually selling existing customers more products and services.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-01, Neil states:
- You can increase revenue without acquiring new customers, and it is "not that hard" (as of 2017-10-01).
- Adding upsells and downsells at checkout can increase revenue by another 10, 15, or 25 percent (as of 2017-10-01).
- The key with upsells and downsells is speed and automation; if the upsell gets the customer the result faster and in a more automated way, they are much more likely to buy it (as of 2017-10-01).
- If an upsell does not offer speed and automation, you usually see only about a 10 percent lift (as of 2017-10-01).
- The second lever is marketing automation, and email is one of the most powerful ways to sell people; every website should collect emails (he suggests Hello Bar / hellobar.com if you are not already) (as of 2017-10-01).
- With email automation, after two or three emails to someone who opens them, you can pitch them on buying your product or service, which raises conversions (as of 2017-10-01).
- The third lever is creating more products or services and selling them to your existing user base over time to generate more income (as of 2017-10-01).
- Your best buyers are the ones who already bought from you, so keep selling them more products and services over the upcoming years (as of 2017-10-01).
- Amazon continually does well because it keeps selling people more and more products and services (as of 2017-10-01).
