# Personal Branding: How to Go From Zero to Hero in No Time

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out a six-step playbook for building a personal brand from scratch, arguing that in a competitive landscape a successful business alone is not enough — a personal brand is what separates you from competitors. He advises beginners to concentrate on a single channel matched to their strength (spoken vs. written word) and where their target audience actually hangs out, then repurpose the same content across other platforms. He stresses documenting your journey and struggles rather than producing scripted content, researching topics your industry actually cares about, engaging with commenters, and building relationships through a podcast/interview tour — both appearing on others' shows and interviewing well-known authorities to borrow their credibility. Throughout, he emphasizes that channel choice should follow revenue relevance and buyer presence, not vanity metrics like likes and comments.

## Key claims
- A personal brand is the single thing that can separate you from competitors; having a successful business alone is not enough in today's competitive landscape (as of 2019-09-28).
- Beginners should focus on just one channel first, chosen by their strength — spoken word (video, podcasting) versus written word (blog, social media like Twitter, LinkedIn, Facebook) — because it is hard to deliver great content on many channels at once (as of 2019-09-28).
- Content created for one channel can be repurposed across others; Neil says he and his team put the same video on YouTube, Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn — spreading one asset across four places (as of 2019-09-28).
- Channel choice should follow where your audience, decision makers, and buyers are — not vanity metrics; not all channels are equal even if engagement is similar (as of 2019-09-28).
- Neil states he generates more revenue from LinkedIn than from any other social platform, likely because LinkedIn is a B2B network and he operates in the B2B space (as of 2019-09-28).
- Document your journey, struggles, processes, mindset, and relationship-building rather than producing scripted content; people are curious about your life, comparable to why "Keeping Up With The Kardashians" is globally known (as of 2019-09-28).
- You don't need a full production to document — a phone is enough; Gary Vaynerchuk succeeds by documenting his life with a camera crew, but budget-constrained creators can start with just a phone (as of 2019-09-28).
- Research topics people actually care about using Quora (popular questions), Facebook groups in your niche, and Ubersuggest's content ideas report (popular content by social shares, search traffic, backlinks) and keyword ideas report (popular keywords, questions, prepositions, comparisons) (as of 2019-09-28).
- Listen to comments and create content in response; if someone leaves a comment, respond — via a blog post, podcast episode, video, or direct reply — to build rapport and a long-run compounding effect (as of 2019-09-28).
- Reach out personally to the people who inspired your content to show appreciation; Neil cites a man named Khalil who encouraged him to write a blog post on FAQ Schema, and thanking him strengthened the relationship (as of 2019-09-28).
- Do a podcast and interview tour: search Google for podcast interviews with others in your niche, build a list, and pitch yourself with three specific topics you can discuss that benefit their audience, supported by stats and data (as of 2019-09-28).
- On podcast appearances, offer listeners something free on your website (master class, ebook, complimentary consulting call) to capture emails and grow your brand; also consider starting your own podcast and promoting it during other appearances (as of 2019-09-28).
- Interview other authority figures in your niche to associate yourself with already well-known people — the quickest way to build authority; then distribute the interview across Podcast platforms, YouTube, Facebook, and LinkedIn, and ask the guest to share it with their followers (as of 2019-09-28).
