# How to Outsource Your Digital Marketing Efforts With a Small Budget

## Summary
A short hiring/outsourcing video in which Neil Patel advises cash-strapped startups on how
to get marketing help cheaply. He rejects both interns (too long to train and ramp, may not
do a good job) and full-time hires (a startup shouldn't burn money) in favor of freelance
marketplaces: Fiverr and Upwork, where you can find marketing talent "for pennies on the
dollar." He concedes both sites have a lot of bad talent but insists there is genuinely
good talent for any marketing job — citing his own use of a writer on Upwork (Armand, for
conversion-focused sales letters and sales emails, not blog posts). His vetting rules:
require at least ~10 reviews that are positive and detailed; don't just pick the cheapest
person, favor more reviews (100 positive reviews likely = good work); and on Fiverr check
average response time (a ~3-hour average shows they're on it; a two-day responder means the
work will drag). Good communication predicts fast delivery.

- Instead use Fiverr and Upwork, where you can find marketing talent for "pennies on the

- Vetting: require at least ~10 reviews that are all positive and detailed; don't just pick

- On Fiverr, check average response time: a ~3-hour average shows they're on it; a two-day

> "Would I recommend interns? No, it's going to take too long to train them, ramp them up ... Would I recommend hiring a full-time person? No, you're a startup, the last thing you want to do is be burning money. Instead you should go to two websites, Fiverr ... and Upwork."
