# Do You Really Need High Domain Authority Links?

## Summary
Solo tactical video. Walks through Ubersuggest's backlinks report for several
page-one-ranking sites ("dog food" SERP: Petco, Amazon, Purina) and shows that in
each case the large majority of a ranking page's backlinks come from low/mid domain
authority (DA) domains, not high-DA ones — often well under 10% of links are DA 81-100.
Concludes Google itself does not use third-party DA metrics (Moz/Ubersuggest/Ahrefs/
SEMrush all compute their own) as a ranking factor; DA is only an indicator of general
site authority. Recommends focusing on link relevancy and volume of natural links over
chasing high-DA placements specifically.

## Key claims
- States the majority of backlinks pointing to top-ranking pages for a sample keyword
  are low-to-mid domain authority, not high-DA, based on live Ubersuggest pulls for
  Petco (<1% DA 81-100), Purina (~1-2%), and a partial exception in Amazon (already
  high overall DA) (paraphrase, demonstrated live, dated to this recording).
- States "Google doesn't use domain authority" as a ranking signal — DA is a
  third-party proxy metric, not a Google metric (paraphrase, reinforces the
  domain-authority-debunk belief already in the wiki, dated to this recording).
- Recommends: don't obsess over domain authority; focus on link relevancy and creating
  a good product/content, and natural links will follow (paraphrase, tactical advice).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Google doesn't use domain authority... it doesn't mean that you need tons of high
> domain authority links to rank.

> Don't obsess about the domain authority, focus on the sites linking to you and their
> relevancy over anything else.
