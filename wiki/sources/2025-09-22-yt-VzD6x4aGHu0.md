# China's Hustle - Learn from Train Food & Billionaires! (Marketing School)

## Summary

A short, light co-hosted Marketing School episode that is mostly business-culture/mindset banter rather than hard marketing tactics. Eric Siu (self-identified as Chinese) drives almost the entire episode: he argues the US should study China the way China studies the US, cites China moving faster, in-seat train-food ordering/delivery at each stop, ~$10k BYD electric vehicles, and 996 work culture ("all they think about is money"). He then goes down a wealth rabbit hole about a rich Chinese woman he follows on Instagram (handle rendered "Becca XBlue," name Rebecca, living in Atherton, CA) whose family built a China cloud-hosting company ("Camelot Systems") that hosted American firms, then made billions in Chinese real estate — leading into a claim that China has no recurring residential property tax and a brief US political aside about trying to eliminate property tax (attributed in-transcript to "the Santis" = Ron DeSantis). The single genuine marketing nugget is Neil's: he watches competitors constantly — the bigger ones to learn how they went upstream and gobbled up the enterprise market, and smaller/faster players for creative moves — and he relays a smaller agency's cold-outreach tactic: email big companies (X, Uber) a brand-new custom creative every week, so the pitch is "I just did it for you, here it is" instead of "can I do one for you." Marketing density is low; most substance is Eric's China commentary and is quarantined below.

- China has ~$10,000 electric vehicles from BYD. (Eric, 2025-09-22) — figure approximate/recalled.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> I look at my competitors all the time... Because they're bigger than me, and I want to figure out why they're bigger, why they're struggling right now, and what I can learn from to not struggle, and how I can overtake them.

> the smaller ones move faster. Like I recently was looking at this smaller agency. They've picked up some large enterprises... they constantly email people like companies like X or Uber or whatever it may be and they'll send them a new creative every single week that they could just use... that they made custom to them. The smaller players grow much faster and do more creative things. So, it's like how can we do what the smaller players are doing to grow faster and the larger players are doing to go more upstream... in essence the best of both worlds.
