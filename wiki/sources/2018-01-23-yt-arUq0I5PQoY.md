# How to Build Unique Links to Skyrocket SEO Rankings (2018) | Backlink Strategies to Rank on Google

## Summary
A step-by-step link-building tutorial. Neil opens with a Moz survey result — **links are
the #1 Google ranking factor** — and says he built "over 23,000 unique links to
neilpatel.com," a purely marketing site, so the same tactics work in any niche. His core
play is the **infographic link engine**: use BuzzSumo to find the most-shared, in-depth
list articles in your niche; turn that content into a **visual infographic** (add your own
data, cite the source); build it with Infogram/Canva or hire a designer on Dribbble
("doesn't cost more than 500 bucks"); make it genuinely visual and **flowing** (his
color-psychology infographic as the good example). Add **embed code** (WordPress plugin
"WP Embed Code Generator") so others can place it and link back. Drive traffic/links via
outreach: BuzzSumo's "view shares" to email everyone who shared the source article, and
**Ahrefs** to email everyone who linked to the competitor's article. He credits the
**skyscraper technique to Brian Dean** — write far more thorough versions ("101 ways" vs
"10 ways"), then do the same share/link outreach — creating a snowball. Finally, capture
emails with **Hello Bar** (exit pop-up) because email subscribers are your most loyal
sharers/linkers. A canonical Neil link-building source; reinforces his BuzzSumo→Ahrefs
outreach playbook.

## Key claims
As of 2018-01-23, Neil states:
- **Links are the #1 search-ranking factor** — cites a Moz study of SEO experts; "links
  are really hard to build," which is the whole challenge (as of 2018-01-23).
- He built "**over 23,000 unique links** to neilpatel.com," a purely marketing-related
  site — so if he can get marketers to link, the same tactics work in any niche
  (as of 2018-01-23).
- **Infographic tactic** (works "now and … five, six years ago"): BuzzSumo → keyword →
  take the popular, in-depth, list/step-by-step articles → build an **infographic** of
  that info (add your own data, source the origin site); most people would rather look at
  an image than read thousands of words (as of 2018-01-23).
- Production: use **Infogram or Canva**, or find a designer on **Dribbble** — "if you're
  gonna pay a designer it doesn't cost more than 500 bucks" (as of 2018-01-23).
- The infographic must be **highly visual and flow smoothly** point-to-point; too many
  clashing colors or choppy steps kills it (example: his **color-psychology** infographic)
  (as of 2018-01-23).
- Add **embed code** at the bottom so others can place it (each embed = a backlink); if
  you can't code it, use the WordPress plugin **"WP Embed Code Generator"** (as of 2018-01-23).
- Outreach for shares/links: in BuzzSumo click **"view shares"** to get everyone who
  shared the source article and email them; then use **Ahrefs** on the competitor's URL
  to get everyone who links to it and email them — "if they're already linked to the
  article … why wouldn't they embed the infographic" (as of 2018-01-23).
- **Skyscraper technique** — credits **Brian Dean** as the inventor — write more thorough
  articles ("101 ways" vs "10 ways to double your search engine traffic"), then repeat the
  view-shares + Ahrefs outreach to snowball traffic and links (as of 2018-01-23).
- **Capture emails with Hello Bar** (exit pop-up, as on neilpatel.com): email subscribers
  are "your most loyal readers," most likely to return, share, and link to new posts
  (as of 2018-01-23).
