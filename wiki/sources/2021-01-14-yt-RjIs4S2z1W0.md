A seven-trend 2021 SEO-predictions video. Trend 1: rankings will "seesaw" more with
each of Google's ~3,000+ yearly algorithm changes — expect fluctuation even when doing
everything right, but net gains over a year if fundamentals (user-first) are sound.
Trend 2: position one becomes disproportionately more valuable than positions two-four
as Google claims more SERP real estate for its own monetized features (mortgage rates,
weather, travel booking) and some page-one results no longer show a full ten organic
listings. Trend 3: "user experience is now SEO" — the Page Experience update rewards
overall mobile experience, not just page speed. Trend 4: "riches in the niches" — the
about.com case (splitting into niche sites, deleting most content, revenue and traffic
both rising) as the model over building a Wikipedia-style broad site. Trend 5: content
length matters less than getting to the point — animated GIFs/video can beat a forced
2,000-word article for simple queries. Trend 6: fewer, slower, more organic backlinks
outperform fast link-building — his team is seeing content that sits 3-4 months before
manual outreach begins rank faster than content aggressively linked from day one.
Trend 7: branding alone won't be enough — about.com's brand didn't save its rankings;
being a topical authority (reinforced by the rule-of-seven brand-touchpoint doctrine)
still wins. Bonus trend: global SEO from day one — his own traffic is now ~80%
non-US (up from an earlier figure), driven largely by translating/localizing content,
per his self-reported customer survey data.
