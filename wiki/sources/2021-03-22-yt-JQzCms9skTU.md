# How to Monetize Your AMP Traffic

## Summary
Short solo tactical video. Shows via Google Search Console (last 16 months, ~66M
clicks) and Google Analytics that a large share of his mobile traffic lands on
Accelerated Mobile Pages (AMP, URL slug `/amp`) — pages that historically carried
content only, with no monetization elements. Notes some major publishers (names "The
New York Times") abandoned AMP after finding it generated no ad revenue, but he kept
his AMP pages and instead had a developer add top + bottom sticky-bar CTAs linking to
his consulting page. Reports the change lifted overall conversions by roughly 6%
(caveats that desktop is still the majority of his traffic, so the absolute AMP
contribution is modest but "better than nothing").

## Key claims
- States roughly 25 of ~66M total clicks (about a third to two-fifths, per his rough
  on-screen math) over 16 months came from mobile, and that of his mobile traffic, the
  majority lands on AMP pages specifically (paraphrase, self-reported dashboard read,
  dated to this recording).
- States adding a sticky top-bar + bottom-bar CTA to his AMP pages (linking to his
  consulting page) lifted his overall site conversions by ~6% (paraphrase,
  self-reported, unaudited).
- Notes some large publishers dropped AMP after finding no ad-revenue benefit, but he
  chose to keep AMP and monetize it instead of abandoning it (paraphrase, positions
  him as making a different call than industry peers).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> I never ended up switching back to normal. I kept my accelerated mobile pages... just
> add sticky bars to the top and the bottom of your amp pages... and that's better
> than generating no conversions.
