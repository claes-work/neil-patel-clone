# How I Spend My Day as a Digital Marketer

## Summary
A detailed hour-by-hour daily schedule (2022 era), opening with the observation that
Google made "more than 4,500 algorithm updates" in 2020 (~12/day) and marketers can't
chase every one. The schedule: 5:00am wake (attributed to kids waking early), brush
teeth, ~15-30 min HIIT workout ("Joe, The Body Coach" on YouTube, no equipment, works
while traveling), done by 6:30am; 6:30-7:30am emails (states the company has "over 700
employees and 900 plus clients"); 7:30-8:00am reading industry/economic news (Google
stock, algorithm updates); 8:00-10:00am and 11:00am-noon phone calls (prefers calls
capped at 15 minutes, "hates" hour-long calls, calls mostly about marketing/growth
experiments); 10:00-11:00am studies other people's marketing across all verticals (using
Ubersuggest/SimilarWeb) for cross-industry ideas; noon-1:00pm first meal (practices
intermittent fasting, avoids sugar and coffee, often takes meetings during lunch);
1:00-2:30pm content creation — writes his own YouTube scripts (states ~8 hours/month to
script 16 videos, though filming itself takes only a few hours); 2:30-3:00pm plays with
his kids fully present, no phone; 3:00-5:00pm more calls, including at least one hour/day
talking to other marketers (peers/friends) about what's working for them; 5:00-6:00pm
reads industry and general news (BBC), noting world events affect marketing (cites 2020
COVID factory shutdowns driving e-commerce growth); 6:00-6:30pm family dinner ("majority
of the time," not daily); 6:30-7:00pm helps put kids to bed; 7:00-7:30pm dedicated time
with his wife (she asks for only 10 minutes of undivided attention/day, he gives at least
30); 7:30-8:30pm personal research time (space, history, new technologies, other
businesses — "can't turn my mind off"); 8:30pm+ final email clean-out before bed so he
doesn't wake up to hundreds of unread messages. Closes by telling viewers to adapt the
schedule, not copy it wholesale.
