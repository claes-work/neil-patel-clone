# Outreach Secrets: Winning Tactics to Engage Decision Makers

## Summary
A ~15-minute AOA coaching-call preview hosted by Eric Siu. Dave Fox (a branding/SEO/content agency
owner trying to move upmarket from solopreneurs to mid-market/SMB clients) asks how to get past
gatekeepers to reach decision-makers. Attendee Adam describes a "near-bound" LinkedIn tactic:
mapping a target company's marketing department via LinkedIn Sales Navigator, warming up peripheral
contacts (liking/commenting) before approaching the actual decision-maker. Another attendee argues
that modern "gatekeepers" are really digital-marketing managers who function as internal champions
once trust is established, rather than obstacles to route around; recommends near-daily LinkedIn
posting to build visible credibility. A third attendee (running a ~6-month-old interview podcast in
Austin) describes landing two client deals purely from guest relationships built through 30 minutes
of pre/post-interview conversation, though flags that senior executives increasingly gatekeep who
they'll appear with until a podcast hits a credibility threshold (e.g., 100 episodes). David
proposes adapting the podcast-authority tactic into shorter LinkedIn articles/TL;DR client-
interview content instead of full podcast episodes, to lower the audience's commitment cost. A
second attendee (John) asks a broader awareness-building question (not enough differentiation from
"a few doors down" competing agencies); Eric recommends a "foot in the door" approach (a low-
commitment AI chatbot offering as an entry point) over trying to sell the whole agency at once, plus
PR/trade-publication placements and leveraging existing client testimonials/referrals as the
highest-value awareness lever.
