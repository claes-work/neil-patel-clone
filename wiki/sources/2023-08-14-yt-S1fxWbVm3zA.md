# Insider starts to experiment with AI - Neil Patel

## Summary
Short co-hosted segment triggered by news that Insider (the media company) launched an internal
"Skunk Works"-style working group letting newsroom staff test AI across its journalism products.
Neil frames the underlying driver as cost-cutting: AI lets companies extract more output per
employee, and "if you can get your cost down and your production up, it's a win-win — more profit,
more traffic." He extends this beyond media into marketing agencies generally: a 20-person
analytics team doing manual data analysis could shrink to three with AI assistance, and a full
creative/design department producing social content could shrink to one or two people using tools
like Midjourney to produce roughly five times the prior output — margin-expanding for the
businesses adopting it. He predicts this compounds into startups needing dramatically less capital
to reach a proof of concept (cheap hosting, AI-assisted build), enabling bootstrapping without
raising money at all, which he expects will raise investor expectations for pre-funding traction.
He then discloses that Ubersuggest — hosted on AWS — was in active talks with Google Cloud about
migrating; the pitch that interested him wasn't primarily the cost discount but Google's offer to
help "slice and dice" Ubersuggest's data for AI-generated insights, which he contrasts with an
internal quote of roughly $550,000-580,000/year to staff a human team (naming an NP Digital
colleague, Carlos) to do the same manual data-analysis work for thought-leadership content.
