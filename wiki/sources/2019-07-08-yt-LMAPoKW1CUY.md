# Selling The Invisible: The 5 Best Ways To Sell Your Services

## Summary

Neil explains how to sell intangible **services** (harder than tangible objects) in five ways.
(1) **Describe the benefits** — spell out in plain English why to buy (e.g., NP Digital helps
drive more traffic and sales). (2) **Describe outcomes** — get specific and into the nitty-gritty
(5× more traffic, conversion rate per visitor, revenue, profit margin); specificity makes the
close easier. (3) **Value** — many businesses hire out because they're understaffed and short on
time and can't hire good talent, so a consultant/contractor who saves time and makes them money
is high value; find the client's issue and the value you provide. (4) **Anchoring** — since you
have competitors, show how you're more efficient, cheaper, better, or faster and lay out
everything the client gets with you that rivals don't. (5) **Pricing** — Neil says he **hates
offering discounts**; matching or slightly undercutting competitors can ease a close at equal
value, but he prefers the reverse: NP Digital costs more, and he shows why the premium delivers
results competitors don't ("if you want good stuff you got to pay a premium dollar").

## Key claims

- As of 2019-07-08, Neil framed selling services as **five levers**: benefits, outcomes, value,
  anchoring, and pricing.
- As of 2019-07-08, Neil said **specific outcomes** (traffic multiples, conversion rate, revenue,
  profit margin) close better than high-level pitches.
- As of 2019-07-08, Neil said businesses hire services mainly because they're **understaffed and
  time-poor** and can't hire good talent — so saving time is a core value proposition.
- As of 2019-07-08, Neil recommended **anchoring** against competitors by itemizing what the
  client gets with you that they don't get elsewhere.
- As of 2019-07-08, Neil **self-reported** that he **hates discounts** and prefers to justify NP
  Digital's **premium pricing** by demonstrating superior results rather than cutting price.
