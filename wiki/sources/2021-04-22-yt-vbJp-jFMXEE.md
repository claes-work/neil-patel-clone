# The SEO Checklist - How to Fix All of Your SEO Errors

## Summary
Solo tactical video demonstrating his free neilpatel.com/seo-analyzer tool as a
shortcut to Google's "200+ ranking factors" — runs it live against his own site,
walking through the resulting scorecard (on-page SEO score, monthly traffic, organic
keywords, backlinks, critical errors/warnings/recommendations, site speed) with a
green/yellow/orange/red severity system. Deep-dives two live examples of his own site's
real errors: (1) "28 pages with low word count" — explains Google's thin-content
threshold (states the average page-one-ranking article contains ~2,200 words, though
clarifies that isn't a literal target) and shows several genuinely-thin pages on his
own site (contact pages in multiple languages, blog pagination pages, an A/B-testing
calculator tool page) where thin content is acceptable versus pages that should be
beefed up (e.g. his Portuguese About page, given Brazil traffic); (2) "17 pages with
duplicate meta descriptions/titles" — shows English meta descriptions/titles wrongly
reused on German/Spanish/Italian pages, recommending either translation or
rel=canonical for legitimately duplicate templated pages (e.g. paginated blog listing
pages). States a scoring rule of thumb: 90+ on-page SEO score is excellent, 80+ is
decent, below that needs continual work. Recommends re-running the analyzer at least
monthly since Google's algorithm changes "roughly eight, nine plus times a day."

## Key claims
- States the average web page ranking on Google's page one contains roughly 2,200
  words, explicitly caveating this is not a literal word-count target (self-reported
  tool-derived statistic, dated 2021-04-22, source methodology not disclosed).
- States Google's algorithm changes "roughly eight, nine plus times a day," and
  recommends re-running an SEO audit at least once a month to catch newly-introduced
  errors as a site grows (paraphrase, self-reported operating cadence advice).
- Gives an on-page SEO score rule of thumb: 90+ is excellent, 80+ is acceptable, below
  80 needs continual improvement (self-reported scoring heuristic tied to his own
  SEO Analyzer tool, not an independent standard).
- Demonstrates, using his own multi-language site as a live example, that reused
  English meta descriptions/titles on translated (German/Spanish/Italian/Portuguese)
  pages is a common real error, distinct from legitimately-thin pages (contact forms,
  pagination, narrow-purpose tool pages) that don't need remediation (paraphrase,
  tactical/self-demonstrated, candid about his own site's existing errors).
