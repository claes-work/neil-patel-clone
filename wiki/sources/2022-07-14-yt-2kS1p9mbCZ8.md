# The Number 1 Hack to Collect More Emails

## Summary
Neil Patel frames email-list building as a "digital ATM," retelling a mentorship anecdote with
**John Reese** (early income.com creator) who told him "the money's in the list" — send an email
blast instead of drawing down savings. His tactic for this video is a gamified opt-in: a "spin the
wheel" widget (Wheel-of-Fortune style) placed on-site, offering prizes (free consultations, eBooks,
courses, coaching calls) in exchange for name + email. He cites a conversion benchmark of 2.3–3.8
unique emails per 100 visitors (net of people gaming the wheel with duplicate emails, which tools
like Privy try to block) and says his own team has closed "multiple six-figure deals" from wheel-
collected leads, while cautioning that wheel-sourced leads convert worse than a direct consulting
lead form — positioning spin-the-wheel as one tactic among several (lead forms, content
upgrades/blockers, exit popups, quizzes), not a replacement for them. Closes with the standard NP
Digital pitch.

## Key claims
- Retells being told by **John Reese** (years ago, "back in the day," income.com) that an email list
  is a "digital ATM" — unlike a real ATM (drawing from savings), an email blast generates fresh
  income (as of 2022-07-14, self-reported anecdote; John Reese already documented as a mentor figure
  elsewhere in the corpus, e.g. [[2018-02-20-yt-CkPZk9i4SSY]]-adjacent material).
- Recommends a "spin the wheel" gamified opt-in widget as an email-collection tactic, citing tools
  like Privy or a custom-built solution (as of 2022-07-14).
- Benchmark claim: for every 100 site visitors, a well-stocked spin-the-wheel yields 2.3–3.8 *unique*
  emails, after discounting visitors who submit multiple emails to get repeat spins (as of
  2022-07-14, self-reported/unaudited).
- Claims his team has closed "multiple six-figure deals" sourced from spin-the-wheel leads, while
  noting such leads convert worse than direct lead-form submissions (as of 2022-07-14, self-reported).
- Recommends combining spin-the-wheel with lead forms, content blockers/upgrades, exit popups, and
  quizzes rather than relying on one tactic alone (as of 2022-07-14).
