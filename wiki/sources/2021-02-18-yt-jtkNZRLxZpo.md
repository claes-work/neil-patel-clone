# How to Get More SEO Traffic Even When You Can't Rank #1

## Summary
Tactical video demoing the **Ubersuggest Chrome extension** as a SERP-overlay tool for
keyword-gap mining. Installs the extension live, then searches "marketing," showing
per-result overlay data (domain authority, estimated visits, social shares, backlink
count) directly on the Google results page. Uses a live case study: for "marketing,"
the #1 result (Investopedia) gets an estimated 116,000 visits while the #3 result gets
241,000 — explained by the #3 page ranking for a much wider spread of secondary
keywords (e.g., "what is marketing," "marketing definition in business") than the #1
page's dominant single term. Generalizes to his own site: he doesn't rank #1 for "SEO"
(Moz, Search Engine Land, Google, and Wikipedia all outrank him) but still gets more
estimated traffic than Wikipedia on that term, because the extension shows him ranking
for adjacent long-tail terms competitors above him don't cover — e.g., spotting that he
doesn't rank at all for "Google SEO" despite it getting good search volume, flagging it
as copy to add. Prescribes the workflow: click through competitors ranked above you,
skip their low-traffic terms, identify their high-traffic terms you don't rank for, and
rewrite/update your content to include them over time.

## Key claims
- Cites a live example where Investopedia's #1-ranked "marketing" result gets an
  estimated 116,000 monthly visits while the #3 result gets an estimated 241,000 —
  used to argue rank position alone doesn't determine traffic (paraphrase, Ubersuggest
  extension-estimated data, not independently re-verified).
- States he personally does not rank #1 for "SEO" (ranked behind Moz, Search Engine
  Land, Google, and Wikipedia per the live demo) but still gets more estimated traffic
  than Wikipedia on that term because he ranks for more adjacent long-tail keywords
  (paraphrase, self-reported, extension-estimated).
- States a specific, previously-unlogged workflow step: using the extension's per-
  competitor keyword breakdown to identify high-traffic terms a lower-but-still-above
  competitor ranks for that you don't (e.g., "SEO optimizing," "SEO optimizations"),
  then rewriting existing content to add them (paraphrase).
