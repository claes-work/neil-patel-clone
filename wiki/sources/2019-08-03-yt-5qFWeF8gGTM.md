# The Secret to Making Paid Advertising Work

## Summary

Neil's single "secret" to profitable paid advertising is **the funnel**, not the ad
optimization. He argues paid ads fail for most people because they drive traffic straight
to a product and expect it to convert; even with the right keywords (via Ubersuggest),
competitor ad research (Facebook Library), and a good landing page, you'll struggle to make
money without a funnel — because a funnel **optimizes for lifetime value (LTV)** rather than
front-end return. Amazon is his example: it loses money on the first transaction but wins on
LTV by emailing related products. He relays a framework someone told him distinguishing
company scale by ad economics: **seven-figure companies** demand a 2–3x return immediately;
**eight-figure companies** aim to break even on the front end and profit over time;
**nine-figure+ companies** are willing to lose money up front because LTV is so much
higher. Funnel mechanics that lift LTV: **checkout bumps, upsells, downsells, trigger-based
emails, and browser push notifications** for abandoned carts — each small, but together they
make campaigns profitable, especially combined with an amazing customer experience
(Zappos, Amazon, Apple cited). His closing point: ad costs keep rising, so a funnel is what
lets you keep spending more.
