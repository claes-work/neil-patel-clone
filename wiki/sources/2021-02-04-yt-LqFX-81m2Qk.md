# 3X Your Email Results By Applying These Frameworks & Workflows Hacks - Email Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week Two, Lesson Two of **Email Marketing Unlocked** (explicitly named in-video,
continuing directly from [[wiki/sources/2021-02-01-yt-nhfs2dyx9Cg]]'s "week two, lesson
one"). Framed around testing/tweaking email marketing via a five-step contact-iteration
process: understand product usage, gather customer feedback, segment by demographics/
psychographics/behavior/environment, keep customer profiles current, and re-survey
periodically. Covers relationship-building copy tactics (first-name personalization,
you/I language), technical list-hygiene housekeeping (diagnosing low open rates per
email type — welcome, activation, marketing, retention/reactivation — hard vs. soft
bounces, spam-complaint causes, and a rule of suppressing contacts after four
consecutive non-opens to protect sender reputation). Introduces persona-building for
segmentation and "predictive modeling" from list behavior, citing his own past-buyers
list converting at a rate that drives roughly 20% of new purchases. Cites an
industry open/click-through-rate benchmark for small companies, and closes on A/B
testing and his favorite tactic — email marketing retargeting (remarketing website/
banner ads, including Gmail inbox ads, to people who opened an email).

## Key claims
- States that roughly 20% of his new purchases come from re-emailing his past-buyers
  list, which he says he treats "like it's gold" (paraphrase, self-reported).
- Cites a benchmark for companies with 1–10 employees sending 16+ campaigns/month: a
  median open rate of 35% and click-through rate of 7% (paraphrase, cited as
  industry/report data, not independently re-verified; source of the benchmark not
  named in-video).
- States a personal rule: if a contact hasn't opened any of the last four emails sent
  to them, stop emailing them — continuing to do so degrades inbox-provider (Gmail/
  Outlook) deliverability for the whole list, not just that contact (paraphrase).
- Describes "email marketing retargeting" — remarketing banner ads (including ads
  shown inside the Gmail inbox itself) to people who opened an email — as his personal
  favorite email tactic, citing it as producing some of the highest conversion rates
  he's seen (paraphrase, self-reported, previously-unlogged tactic).
