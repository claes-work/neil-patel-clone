# How Did Amazon Get So Big? (The Marketing Secrets Behind Amazon's Growth)

## Summary
A case-study breakdown of Amazon's growth, framed as a lesson in user-experience-led
marketing rather than paid/SEO tactics. Origin: Jeff Bezos saw web usage growing 2300%/yr,
shortlisted 20 sellable products, picked books (low-cost, universally appealing
commodity), then expanded into other commoditized media and eventually general
retail. Marketing insights: (1) Amazon's 2008 vision statement centered "low prices,
convenience, wide selection"; (2) free shipping funded by Prime (Neil estimates ~54M
members × $99/yr ≈ $5.4B/yr revenue, against Neil's cited ~$1B/quarter shipping cost);
(3) open, unfiltered product reviews — Bezos's rationale ("we don't want people buying
bad products") — tied to the 80/20 rule that most future profit comes from a minority
of existing customers; (4) personalized recommendations/email via login data, contrasted
with generic marketing; (5) technological differentiation (Kindle — cites 2011 Kindle
sales outpacing paperbacks 115:100; Fire Stick; Echo/Alexa — cites ~118M US home
assistant devices and Echo's 61% market share); (6) partnerships (Fulfillment by Amazon,
UPS drop-off returns). Neil's closing thesis: Amazon's "secret" isn't AdWords, SEO, or
Facebook Ads — it's relentless user-experience investment driving word-of-mouth growth.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-04-06, Neil said Amazon's 2018 revenue exceeded $232B, growing ~20% YoY
  into the 2020s (self-cited figures, not independently verified in this source).
- As of 2020-04-06, Neil retold that Bezos chose books as Amazon's first product after
  seeing web usage grow 2300%/year and shortlisting 20 possible product categories.
- As of 2020-04-06, Neil said Amazon deliberately opened unfiltered product reviews
  early on, quoting Bezos's reasoning as wanting to discourage sales of bad products,
  and tied this to the principle that 80% of future profit comes from 20% of existing
  customers.
- As of 2020-04-06, Neil cited that in 2011 Amazon sold 115 Kindle books for every 100
  paperbacks, and that Amazon accounts for roughly 80% of e-book sales.
- As of 2020-04-06, Neil said Amazon Echo held 61% of the smart-speaker market by the
  end of the prior year, citing Voicebot.ai/Voicify data, with ~118M home-assistant
  devices in the US.
- As of 2020-04-06, Neil's thesis: Amazon's core marketing lever is user experience,
  not paid ads/SEO — "their secret to marketing isn't Google AdWords or SEO or Facebook
  Ads."

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Their secret to marketing isn't Google AdWords or SEO or Facebook Ads, everything that I mentioned is about making the experience for users better."

> "When you're thinking about your marketing, don't just think about how you're going to acquire more users, but think about how you can make the user experience better which allows you to grow through word of mouth, which is the best channel out there."
