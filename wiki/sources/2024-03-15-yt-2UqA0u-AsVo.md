# Smart examples of GPTs at work, Most valuable media channels, Brian Johnson sold for $400M, and more

## Summary
Three segments, well anchored by explicit first-person NP Digital references. (1) Reacting to a Lenny's Newsletter roundup of custom-GPT use cases, Neil describes NP Digital's own dedicated "AI working group" (4–6 full-time staff, including one named Kyle) whose job is to identify which client-facing tasks (SEO content, keyword research, link building, paid-ads work, CRO, etc.) can be automated, then redirect the *time saved* — not toward taking on more clients or padding margin, but toward doing more/better work for existing clients (more outreach, more strategy calls). He frames this as distinct from a pure cost-cutting AI use case and estimates the team splits roughly 50/50 to 70/30 between hands-on work and AI-efficiency projects, because staying embedded in day-to-day work is what makes them effective at finding automatable tasks. (2) Reading a Morning Brew co-founder's stack-ranking of "most valuable media channels" (email > YouTube > online community > podcast), Neil argues the ranking is the wrong frame — attention lives with individual creators/influencers, not channel types, and marketers should find whoever has the most engaged audience for their specific buyer (Sam Altman's email list for an AI product; Aaron Levie over MrBeast for enterprise software; Charli D'Amelio's 152M TikTok followers as why L'Oréal/Amazon/Prada want to partner with her; Kylie Jenner as an example of high engagement but wrong audience fit for marketing services; a hypothetical Marc Andreessen or Satya Nadella endorsement of NP Digital) — and predicts a coming wave of deepfaked executive endorsements. (3) A Bryan Johnson ("Don't Die," blood-transfusion life-extension experiments, ex-Braintree founder) discussion covering his two-year audience-building-before-monetizing playbook (olive oil, supplements, "biological age" positioning); Neil recounts almost buying Johnson's olive oil before friends talked him out of it, and states, as a father, discomfort with any life-extension approach that could risk his own children's wellbeing for his benefit.

## Key claims
- **NP Digital runs a dedicated, multi-person internal "AI working group."** Roughly 4–6 full-time employees (one named Kyle) audit where client-facing staff (SEO, paid ads, CRO, etc.) spend their hours, identify automatable tasks, and redirect time saved into doing more for the client rather than increasing margin or client load; the working-group staff split their own time roughly 50/50–70/30 between hands-on client work and AI-efficiency projects, deliberately, so they stay close enough to daily work to know what's worth automating. States the concept predates this episode by "probably more than a year." (Neil, 2024-03-15) `(self-reported)`
- **"Most valuable media channel" reframed as "who controls the most engaged attention," not a channel-type ranking.** Argues against treating email/YouTube/community/podcast as a fixed hierarchy; the right partner depends on audience fit for the specific product (Sam Altman's list for an AI product; Aaron Levie over MrBeast for enterprise software buyers; a hypothetical Satya Nadella or Marc Andreessen LinkedIn/X endorsement of NP Digital). Cites his own ~1M YouTube subscriber count and Gary Vaynerchuk's 4M+ as examples that raw subscriber counts don't guarantee views — YouTube surfaces content based on early engagement, "democratizing" reach for non-subscribers when a video performs.
