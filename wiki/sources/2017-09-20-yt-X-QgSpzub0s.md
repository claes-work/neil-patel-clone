# The RIGHT Way to Drive More Online Sales By Discounting Your Products

## Summary
Neil argues that offering sales and discounts is one of the quickest ways for an ecommerce business to drive more revenue, and though it can slightly hurt the business by conditioning some customers to only buy on promotion, total revenue and profit are typically larger as long as the discount does not push the product into negative margin. He advises timing promotions to holidays, events, or a business anniversary, and points to Legion Athletics (a fitness supplement/protein powder seller) as a company that runs a sale roughly once a month to reach over $10 million in sales — noting revenue dropped by over 20% in months they paused promotions. His core prescription is to never discount main product items outright but instead structure offers around bundles and spending thresholds (e.g., 20–30% off a combined bundle, or $10 off plus free shipping on orders over $100) so promotions incentivize higher spend without training existing customers to expect cheap single-item deals.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-20, Neil states:
- Offering sales and discounts is one of the quickest and easiest ways for an ecommerce business to drive more revenue (as of 2017-09-20).
- Heavy discounting can hurt the business because some customers become conditioned to only buy when there is a sale or discount, but not everyone behaves that way (as of 2017-09-20).
- Total revenue and total profit are typically larger when offering sales and discounts, provided the discount does not put the product into negative margin (as of 2017-09-20).
- Discounts should be timed to holidays and events (Father's Day, Mother's Day, Christmas, Thanksgiving, Halloween) or to the business's own yearly anniversary (as of 2017-09-20).
- Legion Athletics runs a sale or promotion roughly once a month and reached over $10 million in sales largely because of these promotions (as of 2017-09-20).
- In months when Legion paused promotions, revenue was substantially lower, typically by over 20% (as of 2017-09-20).
- Businesses should not discount their main product items directly (e.g., 50% off a single product), because that trains customers to expect cheap deals (as of 2017-09-20).
- Discounts should instead be structured around bundles — combining popular items with a 20% or 30% discount — which drastically increases sales (as of 2017-09-20).
- Spending-threshold offers work well, such as $10 off plus free shipping when a customer buys $100 or more of product (as of 2017-09-20).
- The key is to use sales and discounts to incentivize customers to spend more, via special bundled/packaged offers, so existing older customers are not alienated (as of 2017-09-20).
