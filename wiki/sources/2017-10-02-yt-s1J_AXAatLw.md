# 2 Simple Ways to Differentiate Your Product From Your Competitors (HUGE Competitive Advantage)

## Summary
Neil explains two simple tactics for differentiating a product or service from competitors who offer something similar. First, tell a story: because people connect with emotion and narrative, sharing why your company is different makes prospects more likely to connect with and buy from you, whereas a website without stories reads as a cold corporation. Second, craft a unique selling proposition (USP) that is distinct from competitors' positioning — for example, countering a large competitor's "biggest player" claim by positioning yourself as a boutique agency where clients work directly with the founders rather than a recently hired college graduate. Combining storytelling with a differentiated USP, Neil argues, sets a business apart and drives more purchases.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-02, Neil states:
- When people understand why your product or company differs from other players in the space, they are more likely to connect with you and buy from you (as of 2017-10-02).
- Storytelling is powerful because it goes back thousands of years and people connect with stories and emotion (as of 2017-10-02).
- A website with no stories on it makes people see the business as a cold corporation (as of 2017-10-02).
- Your unique selling proposition (USP) must be different from your competitors' (as of 2017-10-02).
- A USP can be built by positioning against a competitor's strength — e.g., against a "biggest player" competitor, position as a boutique agency where clients work directly with the founders instead of a recently hired college graduate (as of 2017-10-02).
- Combining storytelling with a differentiated USP lets you stand out and makes more people likely to buy from your site versus other players in the space (as of 2017-10-02).
