# Bonus episode: A sneak peek of our AOA hangout

## Summary
A ~10-15 minute preview clip of a coaching call from the Agency Owners Association (AOA), a
mastermind community Eric Siu co-runs with Neil Patel for agency owners. The entire call is
narrated and hosted by Eric Siu in the first person; Neil is referenced only in the third person
as a co-creator of the group and as an anecdotal example (e.g., "the first day Neil joined YPO, he
said it was a waste of time... then later he name-dropped being a YPO member himself," used as an
example of how EO/YPO-style entrepreneur networks change the tenor of business conversations).
Eric's coaching content: a case study of a Canadian marketing-holding company (~$400M CAD market
cap) that diversified into acquisitions rather than staying focused, contrasted with a
(third-party-described) bootstrapped agency doing ~$120M/year revenue and ~$30M/year profit by
staying focused on one thing — used to argue for the value of focus over diversification, citing
the Warren Buffett/Bill Gates "one word: focus" anecdote. Eric describes his own history joining
EO (Entrepreneurs' Organization) at 24-25 and later YPO (Young Presidents' Organization), an EO
member's hospitality during a Tokyo trip (two dinners, a 3:30am fish-market visit, ultimately
hiring the host's daughter as an intern), and frames AOA as "the unbundling of YPO" specifically
for agency owners. He answers audience Q&A on B2B organic content strategy (Twitter/X as a
low-penalty testing ground before scaling to LinkedIn; a personal habit of a 30-minute hourglass
writing block and a 500-words/day accountability-partner arrangement with Morning Brew co-founder
Alex Lieberman), cites Adam Robinson (Retention.com, bootstrapped to $22M ARR) and Chris Walker
(Refine Labs) as examples of agencies/companies built substantially via LinkedIn, and closes with
a Q&A response on Reddit's rising SEO/search visibility despite Google's public statements, citing
a Google-Reddit commercial licensing deal (~$60M/year cited) as context for skepticism toward
Google's stated neutrality.
