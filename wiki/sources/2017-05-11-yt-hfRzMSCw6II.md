# How to Get More Twitter Traffic (Fast): Viral Marketing Techniques

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out two tactics for driving more referral traffic from Twitter (the platform's name as of 2017). The first is for accounts that already have a sizeable following: sharply increase tweet frequency — going from one tweet a day to roughly 10 tweets a day, re-sharing your own blog posts (including old ones) on a queued schedule via a tool like Buffer so it's low-effort. He reports this raised his own monthly Twitter referral traffic from about 1,100 to over 4,700 visitors. The second tactic is for accounts without a following: write a thorough, actionable blog post (2,000+ words), use BuzzSumo to find who tweeted a competing high-share article on the same topic, then email each of those sharers a short pitch offering your (better/complementary) piece, and follow up on acceptance with a gentle ask to share. He estimates 20–30% reply positively. Together the two levers are framed as a fast way to grow Twitter-sourced traffic.

## Key claims
- Twitter is among the ~100 most popular sites on the web per Alexa.com, making it an underused traffic source for many marketers (as of 2017-05-11).
- If you already have a large following, tweeting ~10 times a day instead of once a day meaningfully increases Twitter referral traffic (as of 2017-05-11).
- You can re-share the same old blog posts repeatedly rather than needing constantly new content; queue them in order via a scheduling tool like Buffer to avoid daily manual posting (as of 2017-05-11).
- Neil's own Twitter referral traffic rose from roughly 1,100 visitors/month (one link/day) to over 4,700 visitors/month (~10 articles/day) (as of 2017-05-11).
- If you lack a following, the leverage comes from outreach: write a thorough 2,000+ word actionable post, then use BuzzSumo to identify everyone who tweeted a high-share competing article on the topic (as of 2017-05-11).
- Email each of those sharers a short, personalized pitch referencing the article they shared and offering your related/better piece, then follow up after they accept with a light request to share it (as of 2017-05-11).
- Roughly 20–30% of the people emailed will reply positively, and many of those will end up sharing your article (as of 2017-05-11).
- Neil reported having over 200,000 Twitter followers at the time (as of 2017-05-11).
