# How to Find a Remote Digital Marketing Job (Tips for 2023)

## Summary
Neil argues that finding a remote digital marketing job is not that hard in 2022 because
companies are struggling to find talent at scale and are willing to pay a premium for
skilled candidates (as of 2022-05-07). He splits remote work into full-time employment
versus part-time/contract work. For full-time roles: research target companies via job
boards, company sites, and social media (LinkedIn, Twitter), and check Glassdoor for
employee/CEO reviews and culture fit. He names specific job-search sites set to
"remote": Indeed.com (large database, plus salary/culture info), LinkedIn (remote
filter, shows what competing applicants' profiles look like), and Glassdoor (job
listings plus company insight), as well as remote-specific boards: goremotely.net,
remote.co, flexjobs.com, justremote.co, remotive.io, remoteok.io. For part-time/
freelance gigs, he advises specializing in a niche (e.g. SEO specifically, or just
Google Ads, or a vertical like Google/Facebook ads for e-commerce) to access
higher-quality opportunities, and lists freelance platforms: Upwork, workana.com,
peopleperhour.com, freelancer.com, toptal.com, guru.com, remote.com. He closes noting
NP Digital is hiring and that companies struggling to find candidates can outsource
hiring to NP Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2022-05-07, companies are struggling to find digital-marketing talent at scale
  and are willing to pay a premium for skilled candidates, making remote jobs relatively
  attainable for those with strong skills (self-reported).
- Glassdoor is useful both for researching target-company culture/CEO reputation and as
  a source of remote job listings itself (as of 2022-05-07).
- Recommended remote-job search sites (location set to "remote"): Indeed.com, LinkedIn,
  Glassdoor, plus dedicated remote-job boards goremotely.net, remote.co, flexjobs.com,
  justremote.co, remotive.io, and remoteok.io (as of 2022-05-07).
- For freelance/part-time work, niching into a technical specialty (e.g. SEO
  specifically, or only Google Ads, or a vertical like e-commerce Google/Facebook ads)
  yields higher-quality opportunities than generalizing (as of 2022-05-07).
- Recommended freelance platforms: Upwork, workana.com, peopleperhour.com,
  freelancer.com, toptal.com, guru.com, remote.com (as of 2022-05-07).
- NP Digital was hiring as of 2022-05-07, and Neil offers NP Digital as an outsourcing
  option for companies struggling to find marketing candidates themselves.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Finding a remote digital marketing job isn't that hard... for companies, it's become pretty hard to find talent at scale, and these companies... are looking for talent, and are willing to pay a premium.

> Glassdoor shows you employee reviews about the companies, reviews about the CEO, and their culture in general. You'll quickly find out which of these companies would or wouldn't be a good cultural fit for you.

> It's easier to find freelance gigs when you specialize in a niche, whether it's a technical specialty like SEO or just focusing on Google Ads... that's when you get higher quality opportunities.
