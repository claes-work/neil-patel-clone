# The Best Way to Invest In Branding

## Summary
Multi-host panel interview (same recurring show as the two prior August entries). On
agency-vs-in-house: Neil says most clients use a hybrid of both, and the choice comes down to
ROI ("you spend a dollar, you better be making more than a dollar... in the long run"), not
ideology. He says agencies can move faster and cheaper than building narrow in-house specialties
(his example: hiring dedicated TikTok-ads and TikTok-organic-content specialists is expensive
for a small business relative to sharing an agency specialist's time). On social content: he
posts to LinkedIn/Instagram/TikTok not because of platform choice but content-type choice —
avoiding generic viral-bait ("if I was 20 years old...") in favor of content aimed at closing
Fortune 1000 clients, then repurposing the same content across all platforms since it costs
little extra. On sales cycles: pre-downturn, NP Digital's enterprise sales cycle was 3-6 months;
in the current economy (2023) it's "lucky" at 3 months, up to a year in the slow case, sometimes
multi-year (cites a lead that first made contact 2+ years before eventually signing). On
branding vs. performance ROI: he distinguishes brand campaigns (evaluated on brand-building, not
tracked for ROI) from performance campaigns (tracked), and runs a live brand-recognition
exercise with the hosts (Ferrari for "a car," American Express for "credit card," Adidas for
"running shoes") to argue purchase decisions are often brand-driven by default, requiring both
brand and performance investment, especially as a company matures (20-30+ years old). He states
NP Digital works with many brands doing Super Bowl commercials and that "in almost all cases"
these lack positive ROI, are pursued purely for brand exposure, and are typically bundled with
expensive ancillary ad-buy commitments (citing an Olympics up-sell example) — he believes there
are more efficient ways to spend the same branding budget. He restates NP Digital's 2023 goal:
expanding into 20+ countries in a weak economy (cheaper to hire/expand when times are bad),
targeting 30-35 by the following year and eventually 50, via a "land and expand" strategy —
landing small (sub-six-figure) accounts inside huge global brands in lower-risk regions, then
expanding into larger markets/divisions once results prove out over a multi-year horizon (NP
Digital was five years old at the time). He closes reiterating that sustained multi-year,
omnichannel presence (not single-channel tactics) is now required for meaningful traffic growth,
since single-channel plays that used to work (pure SEO, viral tweet-and-share tactics, cold
email-list blasts) have become far less effective.

## Key claims
- States the agency-vs-in-house decision should be evaluated purely on ROI, not ideology; most
  clients use both (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-08-31).
- States NP Digital's enterprise sales cycle was 3-6 months before the 2023 economic downturn
  and is now typically 3 months at best, up to a year in the slow case, occasionally multi-year
  (self-reported, dated 2023-08-31).
- States NP Digital runs brand-building social campaigns without ROI tracking, distinct from
  performance campaigns which are tracked; both are necessary, especially as a company matures
  past ~20-30 years old (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-08-31).
- States NP Digital works with many brands running Super Bowl commercials and that, in almost
  all cases observed, these lack positive ROI and are pursued purely for brand exposure by
  companies that can afford it; believes the same budget could be spent more efficiently
  (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-08-31).
- Restates NP Digital's 2023 global-expansion goal (20+ countries in 2023, 30-35 by the
  following year, eventually 50) via a "land and expand" strategy of landing small accounts
  inside large global brands in lower-tier markets before expanding into larger
  markets/divisions (self-reported, dated 2023-08-31; corroborates prior logged instances).
- States single-channel marketing tactics that used to work (pure SEO, viral social tactics,
  cold email-list scraping/blasting) are now far less effective; sustained, multi-year
  omnichannel presence is required (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-08-31).
