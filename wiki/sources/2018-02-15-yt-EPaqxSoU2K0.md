# How to Find a Profitable Niche Market for Your Blog | Turn Your Passion into Profit

_Q&A Thursdays; **Adam Lodolce** (viewership.com) hosts/asks, Neil answers. Only
Neil-attributed material is persona data._

## Summary
Neil's framework for choosing a blog niche, using Adam (who blogged on relationships, then
pivoted to YouTube marketing/viewership.com) as the worked example. Three filters, in order:
**(1) long-run passion, (2) monetization, (3) search volume/trend.** Passion has to be
**long-run** — willingness to do it for **10–20 years** (or a team to carry it), because without
"can't-stop-thinking-about-it" passion you won't blog consistently; if you don't know your
passion, **try 10–20 things** and you'll at least learn fast what you *don't* like, which points
you toward what you do. But passion isn't enough: some topics **don't monetize well.** Dating
(Adam's first niche) monetizes only okay and **needs a lot of traffic** to make money — avoidable
if you'd checked **Google Keyword Planner** first for search volume and **cost-per-click** (high
CPC = a "money industry"). His tool stack for validation: **Ubersuggest** for related keywords +
volume + CPC, and **Google Trends** to confirm the topic is trending **up, not dying** (or you'll
have no audience in five years). Finally, on niche *breadth*: the **about.com "everything" model
is dead** — Google's **Hummingbird** update rewards **thematic, topically-authoritative niche
sites** (deep on one topic) over generalist sites; about.com had to pivot to individual niche
sites, and **Wikipedia** survives only because it's super-thorough on everything. Neil's aside:
Hummingbird "didn't really affect me… because I've only been blogging about marketing" and goes
super in-depth — a live illustration of the niche-authority principle.

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-15, Neil states:
- **Filter 1 — long-run passion**: pick a niche you'll want to do for **10–20 years** (or have a
  team to carry it); without obsessive passion you won't blog consistently.
- If you don't know your passion, **try 10–20 things** — you may not find your passion immediately
  but you'll quickly learn what you *don't* like, which steers you toward what you do.
- **Filter 2 — monetization**: passion alone fails if the topic doesn't make money ("if your
  passion never makes money you're out of luck"). **Dating** monetizes only okay and **needs a lot
  of traffic** to earn — a trap avoidable with upfront research.
- **Check monetization with Google Keyword Planner**: it shows keyword volume and **cost-per-click**;
  high CPC on high-click terms = a "money industry."
- **Filter 3 — volume & trend**: use **Ubersuggest** (related keywords, search volume, CPC) and
  **Google Trends** to confirm the topic is **trending up, not declining** — pick a dying topic and
  "in five years you're going to be out of a blog."
- **Niche breadth — the about.com generalist model is dead**: Google's **Hummingbird** update
  rewards **theme/topical authority** — a thorough, authoritative niche site beats a
  cover-everything site; about.com had to pivot to individual niche sites.
- **Wikipedia** is a rare generalist survivor only because it has **super-thorough content on
  everything**; thin content won't rank in any niche.
- Personal aside: **Hummingbird "didn't really affect me… because I've only been blogging about
  marketing"** and he goes super in-depth — a live example of the niche-authority principle.
