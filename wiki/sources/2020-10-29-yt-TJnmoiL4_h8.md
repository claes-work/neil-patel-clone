# Creating the Perfect LinkedIn Profile - Module 1 - Lesson 2 - LinkedIn Unlocked

## Summary
LinkedIn Unlocked Module 1 Lesson 2: a section-by-section profile-optimization checklist
— photo (recent, high-res, ~400x400, face ~60% of frame, solo, no selfie, plain/white
background, light expression, no five-year-old glamour shots), headline (~120 chars,
benefit- and keyword-rich), summary (~2,000 chars, authentic, keyword-rich, with a clear
CTA — he references his own summary as an example), experience (list companies/roles,
show promotions as a credibility signal), education, and the volunteer, skills/
endorsements, recommendations, and interests sections. Includes a personal aside: Neil
says he and his wife volunteer for causes they believe in, and that this has organically
led to business relationships and client discovery on LinkedIn without deliberate
networking intent.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-29, Neil's profile-photo checklist: looks like you *today* (not 5-10
  years ago or a future aspiration), ~400x400px minimum, face ~60% of the frame, solo
  (no other people in frame), plain background, taken by someone else (not a selfie),
  light/friendly expression — framed as a hiring/business-culture signal, not just
  aesthetics (paraphrase, tactical opinion).
- As of 2020-10-29, recommends listing job-title promotions within the same company
  explicitly (rather than one merged listing) as a credibility signal to recruiters and
  potential business partners (paraphrase).
- As of 2020-10-29, Neil states he has "gotten a lot of business from volunteering over
  the years" without pitching — he and his wife volunteer for causes they believe in,
  and business relationships followed organically, including people who found him via
  shared-cause LinkedIn connections rather than direct pitching (self-reported anecdote;
  wife referenced but not named, consistent with SUBJECT.md's not-public family-name
  policy).
- As of 2020-10-29, recommends up to 50 skills listed, prioritizing top-10 in-demand
  skills, and being selective/deliberate about giving and requesting recommendations
  (favor higher-seniority endorsers) rather than mass-soliciting.
