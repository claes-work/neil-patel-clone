# 3 GUARANTEED Ways Stand Out On Social Media

_Neil solo, direct-to-camera. All persona data._

## Summary
Neil gives three ways to cut through social-media noise. **(1) Storytelling** — the one
thing people always love, regardless of algorithm changes; can be delivered via text,
audio, video, or images. **(2) Be unique / make genuinely better content** — stop
blaming platform algorithms; if content isn't shared, the fault is yours, so study who's
going viral (even outside your niche) and adapt. He notes his own **video content does
"over a thousand percent better" than his text-based content** by being more
entertaining and showing personality, even in a B2B space. **(3) Be part of the
community and genuinely help people** — not for ROI but out of care ("I believe in
karma"); goodwill compounds. Illustrates with a **KISSmetrics-era** story: he helped a
Twitter user who was frustrated with competitor **Mixpanel**, without pitching his own
product, and the person later signed up as a paying customer. Closes on patience —
results come over a year, not in 30–60 days.

## Key claims
As of 2018-04-10, Neil states:
- **Storytelling is the durable differentiator** across all algorithm changes; format is
  flexible (text/audio/video/image). People remember stories going back millennia.
- **Content quality is what you control**, not the algorithm — if content isn't liking/
  sharing, blame yourself, study viral creators (in or out of your space) and adapt.
- His **video content performs "over a thousand percent better" than his text content**
  because it's more entertaining and shows personality; he's B2B but still shows himself.
- **Participate in and serve the community genuinely** — "not everything's about ROI";
  help people who can't pay you. He frames it as **karma** and goodwill that returns.
- **KISSmetrics story**: he answered a Twitter user unhappy with **Mixpanel**, helped for
  free without pitching, and the person — on learning Neil had a competing analytics
  product — **signed up as a paying customer**.
- **Be patient**: expect gradual week-over-week / month-over-month growth over ~a year,
  not results in 30–60 days.
