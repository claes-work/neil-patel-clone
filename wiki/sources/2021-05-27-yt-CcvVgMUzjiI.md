# How to Track and Improve Your Rankings

## Summary
Solo screen-share SEO tutorial on rank tracking and using rank-drop data to drive content
improvements, using Ubersuggest live. Walks through creating a new Ubersuggest tracking
project using **crazyegg.com** as the live demo site: choosing a location (US, since most
Crazy Egg signups come from there; notes city-level localization is available for local
businesses), selecting suggested/relevant keywords, adding competitors (names HubSpot,
neilpatel.com, and QuickSprout as Crazy Egg's tracked competitors), and choosing
daily + mobile rank tracking (recommended over weekly given mobile's majority share of
search). Shows his own neilpatel.com rank-tracking dashboard with real ranking
fluctuations over a 30-day window as a live example, explaining the workflow: look for
keywords with a red down-arrow (declining rank) as an actionable priority list. Uses
Crazy Egg's ranking for the keyword "heat map" (position 6, confirmed accurate against a
live Google search) as the worked example: reviews the pages ranking above Crazy Egg
(Wikipedia, heatmap.com, Hotjar, Strava, seaborn.pydata.org) and judges Hotjar's page as
the best competitor example (better UX/navigation, a progress bar, more thorough
coverage). His prescribed content-improvement method: once a page is already on page one,
on-page SEO and links get you "seen" but reaching the top requires auditing every
competitor ranking above you, improving usability/layout to match the best example, and
covering everything useful that competitors cover (never adding filler/fluff purely to
pad length) — then waiting weeks for rankings to climb gradually as a result. Closes with
a recap of the workflow and a plug for NP Digital.

- States on-page SEO and links get a page "seen" and to a reasonable position, but

  QuickSprout as tracked competitors for Crazy Egg (self-reported, 2021-05-27) —
  seo / biography (Crazy Egg operational detail, consistent with existing biography

- States ranking improvements from content changes climb gradually over "the next month
  or two," not immediately (self-reported, 2021-05-27) — seo.
