# SEO Mistakes to Avoid | 3 Black Hat Techniques That WILL Get You Banned from Google

_Neil solo. He narrates his own **early black-hat "war stories"** as cautionary tales —
biographical/self-reported; the tactics are told to warn against, not to recommend._

## Summary
Neil confesses three black-hat SEO tactics from his younger days and why each is a trap.
**(1) Manipulative theme/widget link building**: he ran a **web-hosting affiliate site** (he
never actually provided hosting), bought up popular **free WordPress themes** outright (e.g.
paying a creator ~$10K for the whole theme/business), and swapped the footer credit link to
point at his site — manufacturing **hundreds/thousands of links**. It ranked **#1 for "web
hosting"** (~6 months to page one, ~3 more to #1) and earned **$100K+/month** in affiliate
income against ~$50K spend — then Google **banned only him** (competitors #2/#3 used the same
trick unbanned; he thinks Google made an example once people realized it was his site).
**(2) Expired high-authority domains**: he bought an expired **civil-war-themed domain**
("battlefield bypass," loaded with **.edu links** and high PageRank, ~$10K+), swapped it to
**casino content**, and ranked **top-3 for "online casino"** — six figures/month until Google
caught on and banned it. **(3) Regurgitating the same content** (e.g. "top 10 SEO tools" for
2016, 2017, 2018) creates duplicate articles Google can't choose between, quietly **shrinking
traffic**. Fix: **update old content in place**, prune/delete dead posts (e.g. FeedBurner
mentions), and **consolidate weaker duplicates** by 301-redirecting them into the strongest
one. He cites a **blog he bought for ~$500K** whose "seasonal" traffic swings (1.2M vs 800K
uniques) were really caused by regurgitated + outdated content. Overarching lesson:
**think long-term**, measure year-over-year, and avoid short-term tricks.

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-20, Neil states (biographical claims are self-reported):
- **Theme/widget link building is bannable.** He bought popular free WordPress themes,
  switched their footer credit links to his web-hosting affiliate site, and built
  "hundreds and thousands of links."
- That site ranked **#1 for "web hosting"** and generated **$100K+/month** affiliate income on
  ~$50K spend over 6 months — then Google **banned only his site**, while two competitors
  using the identical tactic were not banned; he believes he was made an example of.
- **Expired-domain manipulation is bannable.** He bought an expired civil-war domain with
  **.edu links and high PageRank (~$10K+)**, replaced it with casino content, and ranked
  **top-3 for "casino"/"online casino"** at six figures/month until Google banned it.
- Back then **PageRank "mattered"; now it doesn't** — a dated aside on how ranking signals
  changed.
- **Regurgitated/duplicate content** (annual "top 10 tools" rewrites) doesn't necessarily
  trigger a ban but **drastically reduces traffic**, because Google can't decide which of
  several near-identical pages to rank.
- **Fix duplication by updating and pruning**: refresh old posts (change 2017→2018, add
  thorough current tools, delete outdated ones like FeedBurner), and **301-redirect the
  lowest-traffic duplicate into the strongest** — traffic goes up and becomes more consistent.
- He **bought a blog for ~$500K** whose traffic swings were misattributed to seasonality but
  were actually caused by regurgitated + outdated content.
- **Think long-term**: measured year-over-year, avoiding short-term tricks keeps rankings
  trending up; longevity beats widget/link hacks.
