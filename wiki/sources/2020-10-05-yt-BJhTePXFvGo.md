# How To Go From Zero to 100,000 Subscribers on YouTube - YouTube Unlocked - Module 1 - Lesson 1

## Summary
This is the introductory lesson of Neil Patel's free "YouTube Unlocked" course, framing the promise of taking a channel from zero to 100,000 subscribers over a two-week, six-video program. Neil opens by establishing his own credibility (co-founder of the NP Digital ad agency and the Ubersuggest keyword-research/SEO software) and states the non-negotiable foundation for YouTube success: creating quality content that viewers actually want, because without it the subscribers, traffic, and eventual leads/sales never materialize. He gives a brief history of YouTube (founded 2005, started as a dating site, first video "Me at the Zoo", Google's $1.65 billion acquisition) and cites its enormous scale — over 2 billion monthly users, a billion hours of video watched daily, and 90% of people saying they discover new brands or products there — to argue that any business, even a "boring" B2B one like his, should be on the platform. He then previews the course roadmap: build a marketing strategy, create the channel, produce videos, optimize videos for SEO (his favorite step, since SEO drives consistent traffic beyond suggested videos), understand analytics, and grow long-term. He closes with logistics — worksheets and downloadable assets at neilpatel.com/training under "YouTube Unlocked," videos roughly 10-15 minutes each, no expiry, fully free — and motivation via the Ryan's World case study (20M+ subscribers, ~$26M/year reviewing toys).

## Key claims
- The course promises to take a channel from zero to 100,000 subscribers, structured as a two-week program of six videos total (as of 2020-10-05).
- Quality content that users actually want is the foundation of YouTube success; without it you won't earn subscribers or long-term traffic, and views won't convert into leads or sales (as of 2020-10-05).
- YouTube was founded in February 2005, started as a dating website, then pivoted to a free video-hosting platform; the first video ever uploaded was titled "Me at the Zoo," and Nike was the first major brand to post there (as of 2020-10-05).
- Google acquired YouTube for $1.65 billion (as of 2020-10-05).
- YouTube has over 2 billion-plus users every month and is the second most popular social platform after Facebook (as of 2020-10-05).
- 90% of people say they discover new brands or products on YouTube (as of 2020-10-05).
- People watch roughly 1 billion hours of video on YouTube every day, generating billions of views (as of 2020-10-05).
- Being on YouTube lets a business tap into an entirely new audience and generate more sales, which is why any business should have a channel (as of 2020-10-05).
- Neil positions his own results as proof the method works even in an unglamorous niche: he gets over a million views a month in a "boring" B2B space and is approaching a million-plus subscribers — implying that if it works there, anyone can do it (as of 2020-10-05).
- The course is aimed at getting to the first 100,000 subscribers; channels already well past that (e.g., a million) are not a good fit (as of 2020-10-05).
- The YouTube growth roadmap has these stages: build a marketing strategy, create your channel, create videos, optimize videos for SEO, understand YouTube analytics, then grow for the long term (as of 2020-10-05).
- SEO is Neil's favorite step because most creators rely on suggested videos, whereas SEO can bring very consistent traffic (as of 2020-10-05).
- Course logistics: each week includes steps, worksheets, downloadable assets, and action items available at neilpatel.com/training under "YouTube Unlocked"; each video runs roughly 10-15 minutes; the course does not expire and is completely free (as of 2020-10-05).
- Motivational case study: Ryan's World has over 20 million-plus subscribers, and Forbes estimates the channel earns over $26 million a year reviewing toys (as of 2020-10-05).
