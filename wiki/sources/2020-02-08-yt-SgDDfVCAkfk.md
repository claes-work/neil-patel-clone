# Free Resources to Learn Marketing: Digital Marketing Courses and Certifications

## Summary
Neil Patel curates a list of free digital marketing courses and certification programs for beginners looking to jumpstart a marketing career. He frames the problem as one of information overload: trying to learn marketing by Googling produces conflicting advice from different sites. His antidote is a set of vetted, no-cost resources, each with a short note on what it teaches and who it suits. The seven named resources are Google's Online Marketing Challenge (a comprehensive, competition-flavored beginner course where students run real campaigns for nonprofits on a $10,000 budget), Constant Contact's Social Media 101 (step-by-step social presence building across platforms), HubSpot Academy (broad course catalog with certifications and templates), Google Skillshop (Google's own platform for Google Ads, Analytics, and My Business, with certification), SEMrush Academy (technical SEO, link building, keyword research and content marketing, beyond just the tool), Facebook Blueprint (official Facebook/Instagram ad training with certification), and Copyblogger's Internet Marketing for Smart People (blogging, content marketing, direct-response copywriting, and building an online business from scratch). Throughout, Patel emphasizes that certifications look good on a resume and help when applying for marketing jobs, and closes by stressing that all recommended resources are effective and require no spend.

## Key claims
- Marketing is a booming field and a marketer can earn a very good living as a professional (as of 2020-02-08).
- Trying to learn marketing by Googling produces confusing, conflicting advice from different websites, which drives beginners to give up (as of 2020-02-08).
- Google's Online Marketing Challenge is probably one of the most comprehensive marketing courses available for beginners; it combines academic modules taught by top academic panelists with real-world practice in which students build online advertising campaigns for nonprofit companies on a $10,000 budget and compete on their advertising skills (as of 2020-02-08).
- Constant Contact's Social Media 101 course teaches step-by-step how to build a social media presence across Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Instagram, Google My Business, Snapchat, and YouTube, with example lessons, how-to guides, and glossaries, and is completely free (as of 2020-02-08).
- HubSpot Academy offers digital marketing courses in nearly every subject (from advertising to growth marketing to website design), awards certifications on completion, and provides guides, webinars, and templates such as social media editorial calendar templates (as of 2020-02-08).
- Google Skillshop is Google's official platform for learning marketing with Google products (Google Ads, Waze, Google Analytics, Google My Business), covering paid ads across search, display network, and mobile, plus local-business marketing and measurement in Google Analytics, with courses from introductory to advanced and a Google certification (as of 2020-02-08).
- Very few marketers run ads on Gmail despite Gmail having a huge user base, versus the many who run ads on Google search — an underused opportunity Skillshop teaches (as of 2020-02-08).
- SEMrush Academy goes beyond basic tool usage, teaching technical SEO, link building, keyword research, and content marketing at both basic and advanced levels; it is free and offers certification, and does not merely promote the SEMrush tool (as of 2020-02-08).
- Facebook Blueprint is Facebook's official training covering marketing on Facebook and Instagram, from creating ads to managing an Instagram business account, suits both beginners and advanced marketers, and offers a certification useful for landing an ad-agency or corporate marketing job (as of 2020-02-08).
- Copyblogger's "Internet Marketing for Smart People" course covers blogging, content marketing, direct-response copywriting, and creating offers, and provides a framework to start an online business from scratch — techniques not necessarily covered in the other listed courses (as of 2020-02-08).
- All the recommended courses are among Patel's favorites, are effective, cost nothing, and are especially helpful for someone looking for a job in digital marketing (as of 2020-02-08).
