# X Turns To Google To Sell Ads

## Summary
Reacting to an Ad Age piece on X (formerly Twitter) partnering with Google to sell
programmatic ads after being dropped by Bing (April 2023) amid a steep ad-revenue
decline (Neil/Eric cite 40-50% down). Neil argues the move carries little
reputational risk for Google despite X's brand-safety issues, drawing a parallel to
Facebook's ad revenue being unaffected by past "ban Facebook" backlash, and predicts
X ad prices will rise over time as more advertisers compete for the newly-accessible
inventory.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Argues brand-safety controversy on X poses little real risk to Google's ad
  business — even if X breaks publisher-standard policies and Google draws
  criticism, businesses won't stop advertising on Google because of the ROI it
  drives, citing the precedent of Facebook's ad revenue being unaffected by past
  "ban Facebook" sentiment. (Neil, 2023-10-23) — paid-ads.
- Predicts X advertising will get *more expensive* over time as the Google
  partnership increases advertiser competition for the inventory, tempering
  Eric's "good move for both sides" framing. (Neil, 2023-10-23) — paid-ads.
- Recommends advertisers try X ads now, describing pricing as still cheaper than a
  year prior with strong observed ROI, i.e., before the predicted price rise plays
  out. (Neil, 2023-10-23) — paid-ads.
