# What's Going to CHANGE in Marketing in 2023 (The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly) [title mismatch, spoken=2022]

## Summary
> ⚠️ Title says "2023" but the spoken content is explicitly a **2022** predictions video
> ("Marketing is about to change in 2022... what's going to change in marketing in
> 2022") — a title/spoken-year mismatch of the kind already logged on
> [[wiki/sources/2021-10-21-yt-qjYGcWuZ2_U]] and [[wiki/sources/2021-11-15-yt-c4w9GaabAV4]].

A "good/bad/ugly" trends framework for 2022. Good: D2C e-commerce keeps growing
(cites eMarketer: US D2C e-commerce sales of $111.54B, 14% of total retail
e-commerce); voice search is rising (NP Digital client data shows a 6% increase during
the pandemic, sustained after); live video is booming as audiences tire of
Netflix/Disney+ catalogs not keeping pace with demand (cites 13% better performance
post-pandemic vs. pre-pandemic), recommending Restream to simulcast across
YouTube/Facebook/Twitter/LinkedIn/Instagram at once. Bad: competition increases in more
niches as more companies moved online post-lockdown, meaning slower, harder results
requiring more effort and patience. Ugly: the end of third-party cookies in browsers
like Chrome makes personalization harder for small businesses that can't buy data at
scale. Prescribes three responses: go omni-channel (single-channel growth, like
Facebook's old invite-flow era, "doesn't work anymore"); invest in CRO to get more out
of increasingly expensive paid traffic; and raise average order value via upsells/
downsells (ecommerce example: "Skincare by Elena" upselling a vitamin C serum against
an anti-wrinkle cream) or additional service attachments post-close for service
businesses.

## Key claims
- First-party client data disclosure: NP Digital observed a 6% increase in voice search
  during pandemic months that has persisted, with the growth concentrated in voice
  search *for commerce* specifically (self-reported agency data, dated 2021-11-25) —
  paid-ads, analytics-cro. New quantified voice-search figure not previously logged.
- Predicts the end of third-party cookies (naming Chrome specifically) as the "ugly"
  2022 trend, and explicitly counsels not worrying about platform-side targeting
  degradation (e.g. iOS/Facebook) since that's outside marketers' control — focus
  instead on omni-channel reach, CRO, and AOV (self-reported opinion, dated
  2021-11-25) — paid-ads, analytics-cro. A distinct three-point response doctrine to
  the privacy-era squeeze, not previously logged this explicitly.
- Cites eMarketer data: US D2C e-commerce (digitally-native + established brands)
  generated $111.54B, 14% of total US retail e-commerce sales (third-party stat, dated
  2021-11-25) — paid-ads.
- Recommends Restream specifically for simultaneous multi-platform live broadcast
  (YouTube, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram) to grow live-video audience — the
