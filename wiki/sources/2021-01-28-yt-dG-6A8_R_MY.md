# Lead Generation Tactics I Used To Acquired Over 2 Million Subscribers - Email Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week One, Lesson focused on building an email list — the second lesson of the free
**Email Marketing Unlocked** course (launched
[[wiki/sources/2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]; note the publish-date order in the ledger
runs 01-25 course-intro → 01-28 this lesson → 01-30 → 02-01, so this is the
list-building lesson that follows the course intro). Opens with a McKinsey stat
(employees spend ~13% of their working week in email) and frames lead generation as:
identify target audience → traffic source → collect the email via an irresistible
offer → personalize. Traffic sources named: SEO, Google Ads, YouTube ads (called out as
working "exceptionally well"), LinkedIn. Collection tactics, each with a self-reported
lift figure: Exit Intent popups (+50.3% emails collected, demoed on neilpatel.com and
Quicksprout), newsletter/sidebar opt-ins ("not bad"), and quizzes (highest performer —
the "Nutrition Secrets" case study, a site later acquired by another company, drove a
108% lift in email/lead capture via a simple "I'm a man / I'm a woman" branching quiz,
and a related funnel produced a 210% increase in sales from the resulting leads;
separately, a Quicksprout webinar funnel saw 42% registration and 8.5% close rate).
Login-friction reduction tactics: social login ("sign in with Google"/Facebook) lifted
capture by 59.4%; changing the CTA wording from "sign up" to "login" added another 22%.
Closes with campaign-setup basics (goal-setting, tone/brand voice, segmentation,
compelling subject lines — cites David Ogilvy's "80 cents of the dollar is spent on the
headline"), mobile-first email design (60%+ of email opens are on mobile), and points
to neilpatel.com/training for worksheets.
