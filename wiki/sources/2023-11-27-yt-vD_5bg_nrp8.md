# Here's What The Top YouTubers Taught Us About YouTube Marketing

## Summary
A co-hosted Marketing-School-style episode filed on the main @neilpatel channel. Eric Siu opens by relaying takeaways from other YouTubers he's spent time with — George Gammon's "you're an employee of YouTube" framing, and a conversation with Ali Abdal (~5M subs) about YouTube being a demanding "hamster wheel." Neil Patel supplies the durable persona content: YouTube rewards consistency, but more specifically **hit-rate consistency** — once a channel lands two or three hits it's easier to sustain momentum, but a stretch of mediocre content after early hits actively hurts the whole channel. He draws a broad-reach-vs-lead-quality tradeoff from his own agency's data: broad "hack" content (e.g., generic WordPress/Google-ranking tips) drives more views but fewer qualified leads, while narrow technical content (e.g., subdomain-vs-subdirectory SEO for 50+-language sites) drives fewer views but leads from large enterprise companies — which is where NP Digital actually sees YouTube revenue. Neil also gives a channel-vs-business diversification distinction: diversify marketing channels once you've found one that works, but stay laser-focused on one business and a big TAM.

## Key claims (dated, speaker-tagged, paraphrase)
- Consistency has two layers: (1) you must consistently produce content, but (2) more importantly, the content has to land as "hits" — once you get two or three consistent hits (assuming quality stays high), momentum is easy to maintain; but following hits with a few mediocre pieces "screws up the whole channel." (Neil, 2023-11-27) — content-marketing.
- Broad, widely-relevant "hack" content (e.g., a WordPress-ranking tip) gets far more views but generates a lot fewer qualified leads. (Neil, 2023-11-27) — content-marketing / analytics-cro.
- Narrow, technical content (e.g., subdomain vs. subdirectory SEO for a site in 50+ languages) gets far fewer views but drives leads — specifically leads from large enterprise companies — which is where NP Digital sees actual revenue from YouTube. (Neil, 2023-11-27) — seo / content-marketing.
- YouTube (alongside Facebook) is a social channel worth long-term attention — Neil expects it to stay extremely popular over the next 5–10 years — but he would not recommend putting all marketing eggs in one basket. On Facebook specifically, he sees the real money in running ads, not organic. (Neil, 2023-11-27) — social-media.
- It's fine to diversify marketing *channels* once you've figured one out; it is a mistake to diversify a *business* into too many unrelated businesses — businesses should stay laser-focused on one big TAM (total addressable market), which also makes marketing easier (broad appeal, or a smaller-but-high-spend audience, both make revenue easier to generate). (Neil, 2023-11-27) — agency-entrepreneurship.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> You got to make sure that you're consistently trying to produce hits. And what you'll find is more of your content will just go viral.

But then on the flip side, we generate a lot less qualified leads. But when we produce content that's not as much of a hit... it drives leads, and leads from large enterprise companies, and that's what we're seeing revenue from YouTube at least.
