# Here's Why I Don't Blog As Often as I Used To ★

## Summary
A detailed, quantified self-description of Neil Patel's current (2022) content production system,
contrasted against his earlier daily/4x-daily blogging habit. He states he now blogs only about once a
week (4–5 posts/month), but has shifted volume into other formats: 3 long-form videos/week (~5 min
each), 7 short-form videos/week (~30–60 sec each), a daily podcast episode (~5 min), 2–3 image-based
content pieces/week (made by his team), and a monthly webinar. He describes batching most of this
production — podcast episodes recorded 10 at a time, and both long- and short-form videos shot in one
sitting per month — rather than producing daily in real time. His stated rationale for de-emphasizing
blogging specifically is competitive saturation versus opportunity elsewhere: he self-reports his YouTube
channel gets roughly 640,000 views/month and his podcast generates "over $1 million a month," attributing
this less to marketing skill ("Am I the best marketer in the world? No") than to lower competition on
those channels, citing (via EarthWeb) only ~2 million podcasts existing worldwide versus a billion blogs.
His general advice: don't stop blogging, but shift a growing share of content effort to less-saturated
channels (YouTube, LinkedIn, Facebook/TikTok/Instagram) where audiences are "big enough" and competition
is much lower, enabling reach without heavy ad spend. Closes with the standard NP Digital pitch.

## Key claims
- Current output cadence (self-reported, as of 2022-07-02): ~1 blog post/week (4–5/month, down from a
  peak of ~4 posts/day); 3 long-form videos/week (~5 min each); 7 short-form videos/week (~30–60 sec
  each); 1 daily podcast episode (~5 min); 2–3 team-produced image posts/week; 1 webinar/month.
- Production is heavily batched: podcast episodes recorded 10 at a time; long-form and short-form videos
  each shot in a single monthly sitting, not produced daily in real time (as of 2022-07-02, self-reported
  workflow).
- Self-reported channel performance: YouTube channel gets ~640,000 views/month; podcast (Marketing
  School) generates "over $1 million a month" (self-reported/unaudited, as of 2022-07-02) — attributed to
  low channel competition, not superior marketing skill.
- Cited (via EarthWeb, unverified by Neil beyond citation) figure: only ~2 million podcasts exist
  worldwide, versus roughly a billion blogs — the basis for his channel-competitiveness argument (as of
  2022-07-02).
- Strategic advice: keep blogging, but shift growing content effort toward less-saturated channels
  (YouTube, LinkedIn, Facebook, TikTok, Instagram) where audience size is "big enough" and competition is
  far lower than in blogging or Google search (as of 2022-07-02).
