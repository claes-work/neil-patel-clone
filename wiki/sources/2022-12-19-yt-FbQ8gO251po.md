# How to Measure SEO Effectiveness

## Summary
Argues Google ranking position alone is not a sufficient SEO success metric — "if you
double, triple up your Google traffic and you don't generate any more revenue, does it
really matter?" — and lays out five metrics to track instead. (1) Organic traffic
over time (via Search Console/Analytics), framed as the signal for whether a site is
riding algorithm updates well or badly, noting Google runs roughly three core updates
a year among thousands of smaller changes; cites 25% of users go to Google's second
page and 90% of pages get zero organic traffic. (2) SERP visibility (Search Console)
as an early-stage improvement signal before rankings translate into clicks. (3)
Backlink count, framed as a quality signal; cites 94% of internet content never earns
a backlink, 60% of businesses outsource link building, an Ahrefs finding that 43.7% of
top-ranking pages contain reciprocal links (reciprocal linking itself dismissed as
"gaming the system" that doesn't help ranking), and that longer content earns 77.2%
more backlinks on average than shorter articles — while cautioning length should match
the topic (his tie-a-tie example: keep it short/visual). (4) Domain authority (Moz's
1-100 score), flagged explicitly as a third-party metric, not Google's own, to be used
only as a general rule of thumb. (5) Conversions and revenue, called "the most
important metric" — ties back to keyword research and cost-per-click as an early
revenue-intent signal (his stated heuristic: keywords with high CPC in Google Ads
tend to drive more sales), recommending Ubersuggest and AnswerThePublic for keyword
discovery. Closes with an NP Digital analytics-team plug.
