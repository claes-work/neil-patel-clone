# The best marketing channel, Greatest marketing myths, 1 person companies doing $300M+ and more

## Summary
Opens with a data-driven thesis, delivered by the researching host (self-identified as OCD about inbox-cleaning — matching Neil's OCD trait established in [[wiki/sources/2024-02-28-yt-6WXZPs-Ud4E]]): people spend the *least* daily time on search (~15.4 min) versus email (~2h52m), streaming (~2h37m), and social (~2h27m), but search still drives outsized ROI because it's **intention-based** — "Google is the greatest toll booth on the internet" — versus passive/entertainment time on other channels. Cites Meta's 2022 ad revenue (~$113B) against Google's roughly double (~$224B) as supporting evidence. The episode then reacts to a Ross Simmons tweet on "biggest marketing myths" (best-product-wins, build-it-and-they-will-come, more-creative-is-more-effective, good-marketing-equals-immediate-results, SEO-is-dead, email-is-dead, nobody-reads-long-form), during which Neil states his own first business was a job-listing website (built in reaction to frustration with Monster.com) that failed until he learned internet marketing — a "build it and they will come" myth he says he personally disproved — and retells a Ty Lopez anecdote where Lopez's team pushed for WhatsApp-based internal comms and Neil insisted email "will never die." Closes on a tweet thread of one-person companies generating $10M–$300M/year (Stardew Valley, Digital Inspiration, Plenty of Fish's early solo era, etc.).

## Key claims
- **Search converts disproportionately to its time-share because it's intention-based, not attention-based.** Daily time-spent: email ~2h52m, streaming ~2h37m, social media ~2h27m, search ~15.4 minutes (sourced from Statista, converted from monthly figures). Despite the smallest time-share, search intent (e.g., "cheap laptop," "credit card for bad credit") converts directly to purchase intent, making it "the greatest toll booth on the internet." (Neil, 2024-03-04)
- **Meta vs. Google 2022 ad revenue comparison** cited to reinforce the intention-based-ROI point: Meta ~$113B vs. Google ~roughly double, ~$224B, in the same period. (Neil, 2024-03-04) `(third-party financial figures, not independently verified in this source)`
- **"Build it and they will come" is a myth Neil says he personally learned the hard way.** His first business was a job-listing website, built out of frustration with Monster.com; it failed to attract users until he separately learned internet marketing. (Neil, 2024-03-04)
- **Email is not dead — reinforced by a Ty Lopez anecdote.** ~2–3 years prior, while Ty Lopez's team was acquiring companies (cited: Dress Barn, Pier 1), they proposed switching all internal company communication to WhatsApp; Neil pushed back that businesses still run on email, calling it a channel that "still works" and predicting it "will never die" because incoming Gen Z workers are forced to adopt it once they join a workforce run on it. (Neil, 2024-03-04)
- **More creative variants ≠ automatically better; fatigue is real but so is creative burnout from too few variants.** Running the same single ad creative repeatedly causes fatigue and hurts performance, but simply producing more creative doesn't help if the additional creative is low-quality — the real challenge is scaling creative *output* while maintaining quality. (Neil/joint, 2024-03-04) `(attribution: uncertain — undiarized exchange)`
- **Long-form content is not dead; Neil reads a lot of it, specifically analytical deep-dives** (cites The Economist and Patrick Campbell's long-form competitive-strategy analysis as an example) because long writing "incentivizes" writers to surface real data/insights not otherwise available from shorter formats or conversations. (Neil, 2024-03-04)

## Notable verbatim quotes
