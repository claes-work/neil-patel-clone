# How to Build an Amazing Marketing Team

## Summary
A hiring-methodology video in which Neil Patel frames his own success as a function of team-building
rather than personal marketing genius ("I'm not the best marketer in the world... I can always improve").
His recruiting method: identify competitors (via Ubersuggest, which surfaces competitor sites for a given
URL) that are bigger than you, then find their marketing employees on LinkedIn. He uses tenure and
promotion history as a proxy for real performance, since interview self-reports of "driving amazing
results" can't be independently verified — someone promoted multiple times at one company, and again at
a prior company, is treated as reliably good ("third time's a charm"), versus someone with no tenure or
promotion history who is unlikely to be loyal or proven. He stresses culture fit as an independent
gating factor regardless of track record. For outreach, he says directly asking someone to join
("I want to hire you") gets ignored, but asking them to refer someone with their skillset gets a much
higher response rate, often surfacing the person themselves as interested. His interview method leans on
specific, executional questions (e.g., "what's wrong with our website," "how would you fix our SEO," "what
team do we need") to separate people who can execute from people who are only "coaches" — framing his
ideal hire as a "player," not a coach. Closes with the standard NP Digital hiring/marketing-help pitch.

## Key claims
- Neil frames his business success as team-building-driven rather than personal marketing superiority,
  explicitly disclaiming being "the best marketer in the world" (as of 2022-07-09, self-reported stance;
  reinforces the same framing in the 2022-06-09 hiring video, [[2022-06-09-yt-bVw2v0KjFtM]]).
- Recruiting method: use Ubersuggest to identify larger competitors, then find their marketing staff on
  LinkedIn as a sourcing pool (as of 2022-07-09).
- Heuristic: tenure + repeated promotions at a company (and ideally at a prior company too) is used as a
  proxy for real, verifiable performance, since interview self-reports of past results can't be
  independently checked (as of 2022-07-09).
- Culture fit is treated as a hard, independent gate — a candidate can be highly competent and still be
  rejected for not fitting culturally (as of 2022-07-09).
- Outreach tactic: asking a target to refer someone with their skillset gets a much higher response rate
  than a direct hiring pitch, and often surfaces the target's own interest (as of 2022-07-09).
- Interview philosophy: ask specific, executional questions (e.g., "how would you fix our SEO") to filter
  for "players" (people who execute) over "coaches" (people who only advise) (as of 2022-07-09).
