# A Quick Hack That'll Help You Rank For More Keywords

## Summary
A step-by-step long-tail-keyword tactic using Google Search Console + Ubersuggest.
Walkthrough: open Search Console → Pages report → pick a page already ranking for a
head term (his own example: his "what is affiliate marketing" page, which ranks for
"affiliate marketing") → feed that head term into Ubersuggest's "view all keyword
ideas" to surface longer variations (e.g. "affiliate marketing for Amazon,"
"affiliate marketing programs," "affiliate marketing for beginners") → check which
variations the site does NOT already rank for (his live example: he checks "affiliate
marketing for Amazon" and finds he's absent from page one and two of Google) → cluster
the Ubersuggest keyword-idea list into groups of similar terms, writing one new
article per cluster → then edit the original head-term article to link out to each of
the new granular articles. His stated mechanism: this signals topical depth/relevance
to Google around the head-term page, which then lifts rankings for the long-tail
variations linked from it. Closes by noting long-tail terms individually carry less
volume than the head term but "in most cases" sum to more total traffic than the head
term alone.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-09-28, Neil's stated long-tail-SEO mechanism: linking a head-term-ranking
  page out to newly written, more granular articles on the same topic causes Google to
  read the site as more topically authoritative, lifting rankings for those long-tail
  articles (self-reported SEO theory, not independently verified in this source).
- As of 2020-09-28, Neil claims that, in most cases, the sum of long-tail keyword
  traffic exceeds the traffic from the head term alone.
- As of 2020-09-28, Neil demonstrates live that his own site was not ranking on
  Google's first two result pages for "affiliate marketing for Amazon" despite ranking
  for the head term "affiliate marketing" — used as the worked example driving the
  tactic.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "If you rank on Google for the head term... you can easily get traffic for longer variations of that term."

> "Ensure these longer-tail terms don't get as much traffic as the head terms, but when you add them all up, it actually generates more traffic in most cases than just the head term."
