# Is Instagram Marketing Really Worth it?

## Summary

Neil sets realistic expectations for Instagram ROI, split by B2B and B2C. For **B2B**,
don't expect direct sales (no one buys a $10K product off Instagram all day); it gives
**brand awareness** that indirectly helps, and the better play is publishing content other
successful B2B people follow rather than pitching products. He says he's personally
generated "millions of dollars in revenue" from Instagram while spending relatively little
— but stresses this is a **harder-to-replicate anomaly** and that his SEO and paid-ads ROI
are far greater, so you must focus your efforts. For **B2C**, single big-influencer buys
usually lose money — he cites a secondhand story of someone paying **Kris Jenner $85,000
for a post** and generating **less than $85,000 in sales** (worse with physical-goods
cost). What works is **many micro-influencers posting in a tight two-to-three-day window**
to create a concentrated splash (analogized to news cycles/trending topics); spreading it
over six months fails. Building your own popular profile helps a little (using his other
Instagram tactics). Overall he rates Instagram **less effective than Facebook, LinkedIn,
or YouTube** because it's hard to link out and drive purchases. He still uses it — partly
for decent revenue, but mainly as a personal network to see what his family is up to.
