# Why I HATE Adsense (And REFUSE To Use It On My Website)

_Q&A Thursday; **Adam Lodolce** (viewership) co-hosts and supplies a worked example from his own
YouTube channel. Only **Neil-attributed** material trains the persona. Viewer question (Albert
Santiago de la Cruz): what do you think of AdSense?_

## Summary

Neil's blunt position: **he refuses to run AdSense / display / banner ads on his own properties**
— while clarifying he **loves buying ads** (he's a heavy advertiser and gets paid by Google/Facebook
to promote their ad products). Three reasons: (1) **You keep only a fraction** — Google the
middleman takes an opaque cut, and "I don't think everything is crystal clear on how much you get
to keep." (2) **The advertiser is out-earning you on your own audience** — if someone keeps buying
ads on your site, they're making more than they pay Google (who they pay more than you get), which
means **you'd make more by selling your own product/service to that same traffic**. (3)
**Platform-dependency risk** — relying on AdSense makes "Google your boss"; they can demonetize or
shut you down overnight ("I've known YouTubers who make 50–60k a month from AdSense — you get
demonetized, you're out"). He uses **Adam's numbers as proof**: Adam's 1M+ view channel earned
~**$2,000–2,500/month** from AdSense, but when Adam sold his own info-products/ebooks to that same
audience (pre-website) he made **~10x that (~$20k/month)**. Recommendation if you must run ads:
**diversify** — direct ad sales + media partners, not AdSense alone — and always **disclose
sponsored** content (ethical, sometimes legally required).

## Key claims

- As of 2018-11-08, Neil **refuses to place AdSense/banner ads on his own sites** but **likes
  buying ads** — "be the person buying the ad, not the person at the bottom of the totem pole
  serving them."
- As of 2018-11-08, **you keep only a fraction** of AdSense revenue and the split is not transparent.
- As of 2018-11-08, if an advertiser repeatedly buys on your site they out-earn what they pay →
  **selling your own product/service to that traffic makes more money** than serving ads.
- As of 2018-11-08, **platform risk is the deeper problem** — AdSense/YouTube can demonetize or
  shut you down; single-source ad income = "Google is your boss."
- As of 2018-11-08, if you run ads: **diversify** across direct ad sales + multiple media/ad
  partners so income isn't AdSense-dependent.
- As of 2018-11-08, **always disclose sponsored** videos/blog posts — ethical, sometimes required.
- Worked example (Adam Lodolce, **co-host — not persona data**): ~$2,000–2,500/mo from AdSense on
  1M+ views vs ~10x (~$20k/mo) selling his own info-products to the same audience.
