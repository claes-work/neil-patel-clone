# 1 Simple Hack to Getting 1000 Likes on Facebook

## Summary
Neil Patel walks through a tactic for driving organic engagement (likes and comments) on Facebook without paid advertising. His core recommendation is MobileMonkey, a then-free chatbot tool he says he uses himself and has no affiliation with. He describes installing its chatbot on a website and connecting it to a Facebook fan page so visitors and messengers automatically subscribe to a Facebook Messenger list, which functions like an email list on Facebook. When publishing a new post, he sends a "blast" to that Messenger list asking subscribers to like and comment, arguing that comments are the biggest driver of the Facebook algorithm. He claims Messenger blasts deliver far higher open and click-through rates than email.

## Key claims
- Neil claims MobileMonkey is a free chatbot tool that he uses himself with no association to the company, and that his Facebook traffic "skyrocketed" after adopting it (as of 2019-04-13).
- He positions MobileMonkey as a free alternative to paid website chat tools like Intercom and Drift, which he describes as expensive (as of 2019-04-13).
- Installing the MobileMonkey chatbot on a website lets visitors chat with you and automatically subscribe to your Facebook Messenger list, building a base similar to an email list but on Facebook (as of 2019-04-13).
- He claims MobileMonkey handles Messenger subscriptions without GDPR issues and states, from his understanding, that the tool is GDPR compliant (as of 2019-04-13).
- Connecting MobileMonkey to a Facebook fan page means people who message or interact with you there also subscribe to the list (as of 2019-04-13).
- When you have a post you want traction for, you send a "blast" through MobileMonkey notifying subscribers, who then like the content (as of 2019-04-13).
- Neil claims the biggest key to the Facebook algorithm is comments, and that more comments make content go more viral, so subscribers should be asked to both like AND comment (as of 2019-04-13).
- He advises creating engaging posts (e.g., asking a question) to prompt comments, since subscribers reading the Messenger blast are already on Facebook and can act immediately (as of 2019-04-13).
- He claims MobileMonkey Messenger lists get open rates of 60% on the low end and typically 70–80%+, with click-through rates in many cases over 30%, which he says beats email (where he cites typical email open rates of 10–20%) (as of 2019-04-13).
- He claims applying this tactic should let you get over 1,000 likes on Facebook (as of 2019-04-13).
