# The BEST Marketing Strategy is Often The SIMPLEST - Here's Why

## Summary
Argues against running many marketing channels simultaneously when launching a new
product/service/website: says people who pitch him multi-channel plans (SEO + several
paid platforms + content, all at once) are unlikely to succeed, and prescribes doing
"one experiment at a time" on a single channel until it works, then scaling that channel
before adding another. States the primary early-stage focus should be validating the
offer itself (messaging, checkout flow, product presentation) before scaling marketing
spend — if an offer fails everywhere across iterations, don't scale the marketing, fix
the offer. Recommends paid ads as the best first channel for brand-new sites/products
specifically because they give fast conversion signal, contrasted against SEO on a new
site which he says will not show meaningful ranking movement for at least six months.
Explicitly says he is not recommending large ad budgets, just enough to get a directional
read.

## Key claims
- Prescribes "one channel at a time, then scale, then add the next" as the correct
  early-stage marketing sequencing, arguing simultaneous multi-channel launches fail
  more often (self-reported tactical framework, dated 2021-12-18) — content-marketing.
  Consistent with, and more explicit than, prior single-channel-focus advice already
  logged (e.g. "master one platform").
- States that offer/messaging/checkout validation must precede scaling marketing spend
  — if the offer fails across repeated iteration, iterate the offer rather than scale
  ads (self-reported belief, dated 2021-12-18) — analytics-cro, content-marketing.
- Recommends paid ads over SEO as the first channel for a brand-new site specifically
  because SEO on a new domain will show no meaningful ranking traction for roughly six
  months, whereas paid ads give near-immediate conversion signal (self-reported opinion,
  dated 2021-12-18) — paid-ads, seo.
