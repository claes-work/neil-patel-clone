# Boost Your Authority and Traffic With This SEO Strategy

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode (Neil with interviewer Adam) on infographic link building — Neil is
introduced as "the king of infographics" (he notes he used to do far more of them). Two
threads. **(1) Embed code vs. plain download**: always insist people use your embed code, not
just download and re-upload the image, because embed code is HTML that includes a link back to
your site — the same link juice as any link. If they self-upload, they may add a "courtesy of"
credit but forget to link. On WordPress he uses a WP embed-code generator plugin (older/
unsupported but still working) to create his embed codes. **(2) How to actually get embeds —
his highest-converting process**: don't just create stats and email random sites in your niche
hoping they link. Instead, find the most popular existing blog posts in your niche (search
BuzzSumo by keyword, sort by social shares), then run those URLs through Ahrefs or Majestic to
list everyone who already linked to those articles. Turn the popular article's topic into your
own infographic, then email each of those existing linkers ("hey John, I noticed you linked to
X, I turned it into an infographic, feel free to embed it") — plus everyone who *shared* the
article, asking for a share. He says this converts roughly triple vs. cold-asking your
industry, because these people have already demonstrated they link/share on the topic. Design
guidance: cite your data sources, add your own data, keep colors minimal (so it matches host
sites' color schemes and they're more likely to embed), and limit to five or six main points
that flow together and tell a story. On Pinterest: hit-or-miss for infographics — more visual/
design-and-marketing topics do better there, but he found the traffic converts poorly into
sales/shares/links, so he stopped focusing on it. Need one produced? He names 99designs.com or
Dribbble.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-07-26, always require embed code (not self-download) for your infographic —
  embed code is HTML with a backlink, giving the same link juice as any link; self-uploaders
  often forget to link.
- As of 2018-07-26, Neil uses a WordPress embed-code-generator plugin (older/unsupported but
  still functional) to make his infographic embed codes.
- As of 2018-07-26, his highest-converting embed strategy: find the most-shared posts in your
  niche via BuzzSumo, get their existing linkers via Ahrefs/Majestic, make an infographic on
  that topic, and email those linkers (and sharers) to embed/share it.
- As of 2018-07-26, this "existing linkers" approach converts ~3× better than cold-asking
  people in your industry.
- As of 2018-07-26, design rules: cite sources, add your own data, keep colors minimal (to
  match host color schemes), and limit to five or six connected main points that tell a story.
- As of 2018-07-26, Pinterest is hit-or-miss for infographics — more visual/design topics do
  better, but the traffic converts poorly, so he stopped focusing on it.
