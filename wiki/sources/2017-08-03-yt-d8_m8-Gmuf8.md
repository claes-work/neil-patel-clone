# Facebook Ads vs Google Ads: Which Paid Advertising Should You Use For Online Marketing

## Summary
Neil contrasts the two major paid advertising platforms and argues the choice comes down to intent versus audience. He teaches that Google AdWords is intent-based — people search for what they already want (e.g., "auto insurance," "cheap hotels in Las Vegas"), so ads convert well and can send traffic straight to a purchase or product page. Facebook, by contrast, has no purchase intent because people are there to connect with friends and browse photos and videos; direct "buy now" ads won't convert the same way. Instead, Neil recommends Facebook for reaching a broad, segmented audience (e.g., women interested in beauty, car enthusiasts, business owners) and warns against selling immediately. His prescribed Facebook approach is to nurture first — drive people to educational content such as a blog post or a webinar that explains why the product or service is valuable, and only then convert them into customers. He notes one exception: for a low-end / low-ticket product, you can drive Facebook traffic directly to a checkout or product page.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-03, Neil states:
- ★ Google AdWords is intent-based: users type in what they are looking for, so when someone searches "auto insurance" they are likely actually looking to buy auto insurance, which is what makes AdWords effective (as of 2017-08-03).
- ★ Facebook Ads have essentially no purchase intent because users are there to connect with friends and view photos, videos, and walls — so direct "buy now" ads (e.g., "Auto Insurance. Buy now.") won't convert the same way they do on Google (as of 2017-08-03).
- Google AdWords is the right choice for intent-based businesses selling a specific product or service to people actively searching for it (consulting help, accounting help, an iPhone, a dog leash, etc.) (as of 2017-08-03).
- Facebook is the right choice for reaching a broad, segmented audience defined by category or interest — beauty, automotive, car enthusiasts, business owners (as of 2017-08-03).
- ★ The ideal way to convert Facebook traffic is to educate first, not sell immediately: send people to a landing page with educational information (a blog post explaining why the product is valuable, or a webinar) and convert them into customers afterward — unlike Google, you do not send them straight to a buy-now page (as of 2017-08-03).
- Exception: if you are selling a low-end / low-ticket product on Facebook, you can drive traffic directly to a checkout page or product page first (as of 2017-08-03).
