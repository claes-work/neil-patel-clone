# Facebook Marketing Strategy: How to Build a Six Figure Business in Under 90 Days With Facebook Ads

## Summary
Neil Patel walks through an **evergreen automated webinar funnel** driven by Facebook video ads — a tactical sales system he says he has personally run (but no longer does, "I don't care for the money"). The build: (1) sign up for **WebinarJam** to run/sell on a webinar, plus **EverWebinar** to record it once and replay it as if a new session starts every 15 minutes; (2) structure the webinar as 30–40 minutes of genuine education, then **ask permission to pitch** ("do I have your permission?") before the offer, because asking permission makes people more likely to stay for the pitch; (3) sell a **discounted** offer, typically priced $500–$1,500, where the "original" price is justified by demonstrated value (you must *show* $5,000 of value to sell at $500) and a **countdown timer** adds time-scarcity; (4) drive traffic via a simple opt-in landing page and use **Twilio** SMS reminders; (5) **engage the chat** — an internal analysis found commenters are 3× more likely to buy; (6) **remarket** to non-buyers with a Facebook video ad showing what they'd have gotten; (7) offer a **$1 trial** to reclaim the concerned non-buyers (adds ~15% more sales). He stresses financing ads on net-30 credit cards, using subtitled video ads, and — crucially — that you can **sell before the product exists** (deliver within 2 weeks / 30 days) but must deliver real value and refund generously. He shares concrete funnel economics.

## Key claims
As of 2017-12-07, Neil states:
- You don't need money to start: **credit cards are net-30**, so you can run ads now and pay the bill from the revenue that comes in first (as of 2017-12-07).
- Use **WebinarJam** to run/sell on a webinar and **EverWebinar** to make one recording replay on a rolling ~15-minute schedule, so it "continually seems like you're holding a new one" — letting you make sales while asleep (as of 2017-12-07).
- Webinar structure: first **30–40 minutes education**, then explicitly **ask permission to pitch**; permission increases how likely people are to hear the offer (as of 2017-12-07).
- **Price the offer $500–$1,500, discounted from a higher anchor** — but you must *demonstrate* the higher value (show $5,000 worth to sell for $500), not just assert a fake original price; add a **countdown timer** for time-scarcity (as of 2017-12-07).
- Drive traffic with a **simple opt-in landing page** (photo of you + what to expect + opt-in) and **Twilio** SMS reminders (as of 2017-12-07).
- **Engagement drives sales**: an internal analysis found people who leave a chat comment are "more than three times likely to buy" than non-commenters (as of 2017-12-07).
- **Facebook ads should be video-based and subtitled** (many viewers watch without sound); include benefit copy (as of 2017-12-07).
- **Remarket to abandoners** with a Facebook video ad of what they'd get; a lot of sales come from this (as of 2017-12-07).
- A **$1 trial** for non-buyers recovers ~15% more sales; email them before the trial ends so they can cancel (as of 2017-12-07).
- **Funnel economics** (self-reported): selling a ~$1,000 product, per 100 webinar registrations he got ~3.6 sales with ~20% refunds, still netting "well over $2,000" (he estimates ~$2,500–$3,000 after refunds); registrations cost ~$6 each, so ~$600 ad spend produced >$2,000 (as of 2017-12-07).
- You can **sell before building the product** and deliver within 2 weeks / 30 days — but only sell something genuinely valuable; "don't put your name behind crap"; refund generously even past the guarantee window; protect against feeling "like a sleaze bag" (as of 2017-12-07).
