# How to Build An Organization of Top Performing Marketers

## Summary
Short co-hosted daily segment defining and hiring "top performing marketers." Eric opens the
frame (marketers who produce revenue, not just clicks/followers) and shares a self-deprecating
personal anecdote about an early job where he optimized Google Ads quality score to 10/10 while
driving zero conversions, and his boss correctly called it out. Neil reframes the goal for any
marketing team: results matter only insofar as they drive revenue, illustrated with an internal
example of his own team ranking for the keyword "Discord" and driving hundreds of thousands of
visitors over four months with zero conversions, after which the team deliberately let the
ranking/traffic drop since it wasn't converting. Neil then lays out a three-part hiring framework
for top performers: (1) look for channel specialists, not generalists; (2) once you land one top
performer, ask them for referrals to other top performers they've worked with, since top
performers dislike working alongside weak colleagues; (3) prioritize candidates who've worked at
multiple competitors in the space **and been repeatedly promoted there** — repeated promotion
across employers is a stronger signal of real ability than self-reported interview claims, since
it means other companies independently validated their value. Eric adds the inverse pattern from
his own hiring history: candidates who stayed 5+ years at one job without ever being promoted
tended to plateau at the original role and couldn't scale up when hired. Both close on retention:
once you have a genuine top performer, compensate with equity/stock options and give consistent
verbal recognition.
