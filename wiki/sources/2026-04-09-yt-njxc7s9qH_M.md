# Meta and Google Are Not the Best Place to Start Anymore (Marketing School)

## Summary
Short co-hosted Marketing School episode covering three riffs: (1) three "underpriced" Q2 2026 marketing channels, (2) how AI reshapes A/B-player performance and team adoption, and (3) five skills every marketer needs by 2027. **The attribution split is heavy toward Eric this episode.** The opening three-channels segment (smaller social networks, GitHub-as-channel, GEO) and the closing five-skills segment both carry Eric Siu's signatures (his open-sourced AI skills / "auto growth" ecosystem; "Neil's seeing it in his org") and are quarantined below — the transcript has no speaker labels, so this rests on inference. **Neil's confident, persona-trainable contribution is the middle segment on AI and team performance:** his definition of A- vs B-players, the "parallel tabs/agents" multiplier, quantified productivity lifts he sees in his org (~30-40% for A-players, ~50% for B-players), and NP Digital's AI-adoption philosophy (don't force everyone to master every new tool; let natural early-adopters build AI tooling/SOPs so the AI adapts to people). ★ L3-candidate: Neil's team-adoption framework ("build tech so AI adapts to your people, not everyone masters AI") is a durable, reusable operating model worth promoting later — flagged, not inline-promoted.

## Key claims (dated, speaker-tagged, paraphrase)
- The biggest AI multiplier is being able to talk to it and run multiple things in parallel — separate tabs/agents doing CRO, SEO, design, and shipping features simultaneously, without waiting. (Neil, 2026-04-09) — ai-marketing.
- A B-player is someone who needs to be told what to do; an A-player just does it. AI removes the resource constraint on B-players (via co-pilots) so a told-what-to-do B-player can produce much more — closer to A-player output, though not yet autonomous. (Neil, 2026-04-09) — ai-marketing / agency-entrepreneurship.
- Controversial view: AI keeps B-players as B-players and A-players as A-players; both do more, but the standard for what counts as "A" or "B" rises. (Neil, 2026-04-09) — ai-marketing.
- Money/finance decisions still need A-player judgement, not a B-player rollout. (Neil, 2026-04-09) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Observed lifts in his org: A-players ~30-40% better with AI, B-players ~50% better; bigger lifts overall for B- and C-players than for A-players. (Neil, 2026-04-09, self-reported) — ai-marketing.
- NP Digital's adoption approach: making everyone an A-player with AI is unrealistic (you can't re-onboard the whole team every 30 days as new tools drop); instead identify the elite/natural tech adopters who adapt on their own, and put them in charge of building the tech and SOPs that help each department — so the AI adapts to the people rather than forcing everyone to master AI. Low-fear, not heavy pressure. (Neil, 2026-04-09) — agency-entrepreneurship / ai-marketing. ★
- (Shared/hedged) Fewer than ~2% of a company reaches "transformative" AI use; well below 10%. (Neil + Eric, 2026-04-09, estimate) — agency-entrepreneurship.

## Eric Siu (co-host, context — NOT persona data)
[[wiki/entities/eric-siu]]
- Three underpriced Q2 2026 channels (opening segment, attributed to Eric): (1) the social networks "no one likes talking about" — Snap, Pinterest, X: ROI still massive, cheap, don't scale as much but drive more profit per spend; smaller orgs should start paid there before Meta/Google. (2) GitHub as a marketing channel — he open-sourced all his AI skills, got 250 stars overnight, keeps adding to the repo; used Garry Tan's scaffolding that asks users to share usage data, letting him see what users want; agents increasingly use GitHub. (3) GEO (generative engine optimization) — claims GEO traffic converts ~2.5x better than email/SMS and ~3x better than paid channels; results come faster than SEO (a few months); engines favor freshly-updated site content and reward brand-mention recency (not just velocity) — old articles (e.g. Coca-Cola's 10-15-year-old mentions) get pulled less than 2-3-month-old ones. (Eric, 2026-04-09)

Gave a "credit card conversion" example needing A-player judgement. (Eric, 2026-04-09)
- "Chief inspirer AND chief fearmonger" leadership take; the Anthropic-team-doesn't-hand-write-code anecdote (secondhand); trains staff via Slack Looms and a Gather.Town "gong"; "you are the product manager, the agents are your engineers." (Eric, 2026-04-09)
- The entire closing "five skills every marketer needs by 2027" list is Eric's: (1) adaptability/healthy relationship with change, (2) understanding data (AI's data breakdowns are often off; bad data breaks everything), (3) storytelling (hook → story → offer), (4) deep ICP understanding including cultural/country nuance, (5) working in parallel with AI across many agents/screens. (Eric, 2026-04-09)
