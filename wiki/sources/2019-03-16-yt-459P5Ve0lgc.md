# Sell Me This Pen — The Key to Selling Anything | Sales Techniques

## Summary

Neil's answer to the "sell me this pen" interview trope: don't lead with features/price — sell the
end solution. His four-step sales method: (1) understand the customer by asking discovery questions
about how they use the item and what value they get; (2) qualify — not everyone is your ideal
customer, so focus energy on qualified leads and ignore the rest; (3) use both logic and emotion in
the pitch (emotion: an expensive pen to commemorate business-changing contracts; logic: a pen that
lasts twice as long for only 10% more); (4) use scarcity/pressure to close faster ("only two
contracts left this month"). Ends by reinforcing the no-discount rule: pressure yes, discounts
never, or buyers walk all over you. Reinforces the "sell the outcome, not the product" theme also
in [[2019-03-09-yt-vwZeqbYZS_Q]].

## Key claims

- As of 2019-03-16, Neil said the key to selling anything is to sell the **end solution**, not the
  object or its features/price — "you're not selling a pen, you're selling the end solution."
- As of 2019-03-16, Neil's step 1 is discovery: ask the prospect questions (do you use pens? what
  for? pens or pencils? how often?) to understand their motive before pitching.
- As of 2019-03-16, Neil said step 2 is qualifying: not everyone is your ideal customer; a salesperson
  should focus time/energy on qualified leads and ignore the rest — it's okay not to sell everyone.
- As of 2019-03-16, Neil said step 3 is to combine **logic and emotion**: emotional framing (an
  expensive pen to remember business-changing contract signings) and logical framing (a pen that
  lasts twice as long for ~10% more = saving money).
- As of 2019-03-16, Neil said step 4 is **pressure/scarcity** — e.g., "I can only take on two more
  contracts this month" — to get prospects moving, while keeping communication open.
- As of 2019-03-16, Neil reiterated: never offer discounts, even under pressure ("the worst thing you
  can do") — discounting crushes your numbers and invites buyers to "walk all over you." [belief —
  pricing discipline; consistent with 2019-03-07 and 2019-03-09]

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You're not selling a pen, you're selling the end solution."

> "Being a sales person, it's not about going after a hundred or a thousand leads. It's about focusing your time and energy on all of the qualified ones and ignoring the rest."

> "When you're putting pressure, do not offer discounts. That's the worst thing you can do ... the moment you offer a discount, people will walk all over you and they'll ask you for more and more."
