# The Best Alternative to Google Ads (My #1 Growth Hack)

## Summary

Solo tactical. Premise: Google Ads costs keep rising; Neil's alternative that yields "much
better ROI in the long run" is **building a free product/service and leveraging growth
hacking** instead of pure paid acquisition. His anchor example is **Dropbox**: early on
they tried Google Ads but were spending ~$200–300 to acquire a customer worth ~$60/year
($5/mo) — unsustainable — so they switched to growth hacking (invite flows: invite users →
get more free space). A free product is "the carrot you dangle": it pulls in many users,
builds brand loyalty/rapport, and makes the eventual **upsell to paid products far easier**.
Neil's own proof: **Ubersuggest** costs him **~$150K/month** to give away features that
tools like BuzzSumo/SEMrush charge for — but he estimates the same visitor volume via
Google Ads would cost **~$600K/month**, so the free tool is far cheaper acquisition. He's
explicit it's not anti-Google-Ads ("ideally you should be doing both") — the key risk of
ads is that **the moment you stop paying, the traffic stops**. And free tools needn't be
costly: **HubSpot's free email-signature generator** cost only a few thousand dollars to
build, near-zero to maintain, and reportedly drives **seven figures of revenue per year**.
Tactic: add **invite/referral flows** (Dropbox-style "invite more users for more free
usage") to your free product.
