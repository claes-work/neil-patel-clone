# How To Grow Your Business Through Word-Of-Mouth Marketing

## Summary

Solo tactical/philosophy. Neil's thesis: **word-of-mouth is the best form of marketing**,
and it's earned not by chasing Google rankings or Instagram/Facebook popularity but by
**solving problems better than anyone else and taking care of customers**. Companies get big
primarily because they build an amazing product/service — not because of clever marketing.
Examples: **Slack** wasn't first and did little marketing (their animal-filled TV ads
confused people), yet grew into a multi-billion-dollar company on product love;
category-leading brands people default to (**Nike** shoes; **Visa/Mastercard/Amex**;
**Amazon**) are chosen largely because of ubiquitous positive word-of-mouth, not ads. His
signature case is **Zappos**: they moved from Silicon Valley to Las Vegas for cheaper
support operations and built the company around best-in-class service — famously **upgrading
ground orders to free overnight shipping at random** because they cared about the customer
and wanted people to tell friends. That word-of-mouth drove Zappos to a valuation "well over
a billion dollars" (acquired by Amazon). Takeaway: invest in product quality and customer
care as the true growth engine; the referrals follow.
