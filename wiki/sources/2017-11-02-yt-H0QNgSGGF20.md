# How to Drive Traffic to Your Blogs Posts Using LinkedIn (60,000 Website Visitors Per Month!)

## Summary
Neil lays out a four-part tactic for driving blog traffic from LinkedIn, which he calls one of the best social networks — especially for B2B, where it beats Facebook and Twitter because your ideal (business-owner/marketer) customers are already there. The core move: instead of relying on the LinkedIn share button on your blog, republish only the first few paragraphs of your post natively on LinkedIn with a "click to continue reading" link back to your site. He claims this shift took him from "not even more than a few thousand visitors" from LinkedIn to "over a thousand visitors a day." The video's title promises 60,000 visitors/month, but in the transcript Neil only substantiates the tactic with the "thousands per day / over a thousand a day" figure — the 60,000/month number is not directly stated or backed up in the talk. Supporting tactics: connect with people in your space (LinkedIn caps connections at 30,000), keep the LinkedIn share button on posts (helps little but doesn't hurt), and add a PS to your email blasts asking readers to share on LinkedIn.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-02, Neil states:
- LinkedIn is one of the best social networks for traffic, and better than Facebook and Twitter for B2B because your ideal customers are there (as of 2017-11-02).
- Blog visitors who aren't converting often aren't converting because you're not leveraging LinkedIn (as of 2017-11-02).
- Step one is to connect with other people in your space; his own connections are mostly business owners and marketers who are either potential customers or peers who can promote his content (as of 2017-11-02).
- LinkedIn caps you at 30,000 connections, and he wishes it were unlimited so he could get more traffic (as of 2017-11-02).
- Simply clicking the LinkedIn share icon on a blog post (e.g., in his sidebar) doesn't generate much traffic (as of 2017-11-02).
- The high-leverage tactic is to post only the first few paragraphs of your content natively on LinkedIn with a "click to continue reading" link driving people to your site (as of 2017-11-02).
- This teaser approach gets him thousands of visitors from LinkedIn per day / over a thousand visitors a day, versus not even a few thousand before (as of 2017-11-02).
- Keeping a LinkedIn social sharing button on blog posts doesn't help a lot but doesn't hurt — "something's better than nothing" (as of 2017-11-02).
- In your email blasts announcing a new post, add a PS asking subscribers to share it on LinkedIn (as of 2017-11-02).
- Combining all these strategies yields more LinkedIn traffic and, better, more leads and sales, since LinkedIn has some of the best traffic — especially in B2B (as of 2017-11-02).
