# How To EXPLODE Your Shopify Store with Content Marketing

_Q&A Thursday; **Adam Lodolce** (viewership.com) hosts/asks (viewer question about Shopify),
Neil answers. A team member, **David**, is referenced. Only Neil-attributed material is
persona data; Adam's stated video preference and David's story are context._

## Summary
Neil's Shopify content playbook has two pillars. **(1) Pick the content format you're
naturally best at** — not "video or text?" but what suits you (Adam prefers video; Neil says
he's better at **written content** after 10+ years, though he's improving at video). **(2)
Concentrate on ONE product** and build all content around it. His proof: a woman he met at
one of **Chris Winfield's dinners** does **six figures/month on Shopify with a single product**
— **comfort insoles for high heels** — because luxury brands (Gucci/Prada) won't make heels
comfortable (customers perceive discomfort as luxury), so she filled the gap with **Facebook
ads + one product**. Start with one product, add **upsells/downsells** later via a plugin
(**Zipify / One Click Upsell**). For content, create assets *around* the product — how-tos,
a health "study," and especially **infographics** (he cites an animated cheetah-speed
infographic as the viral shape). Find hot angles on **BuzzSumo**, commission infographics
cheaply on **Dribbble / 99designs** ($50–$500), then do **outreach**: use BuzzSumo "view
shares" to email people who shared the original text piece, and **Ahrefs** to email sites
that linked to it, offering your visual/infographic version for a share or embed
(**WP Embed Code Generator** plugin for embeds). Gauge market size with **Google Trends**
(bigger than "digital marketing" = big enough). A second one-product example: **David** sold
an **organic eczema solution** at **six figures/year**.

## Key claims
As of 2018-03-01, Neil states:
- **Content-format choice = what you're naturally best at / comfortable with** (video, audio,
  text, or visual/infographics), not a fixed "video vs text" rule. Neil says he's stronger at
  **written content** (10+ years) than video, though improving.
- **Start with ONE product** on Shopify — you don't need 20–30 products. A single product can
  do **six figures/month**.
- **Case (self-reported)**: a woman met at **Chris Winfield's dinner** does **six figures/month**
  with one product — **comfort insoles for high heels** — via **Facebook ads**, filling a gap
  luxury brands won't (women perceive uncomfortable high-end heels as luxurious).
- **Add upsells/downsells later** with a plugin — **Zipify / "One Click Upsell"** (e.g. "buy
  three at a discount").
- **Build content around the one product**: how-to guides, a health/data "study" (compile
  existing web data), and **infographics** — the shareable/viral format (animated
  cheetah-speed infographic cited as the model).
- **Ideation + production**: **BuzzSumo** to find hot topics; commission infographics on
  **Dribbble** or **99designs** for **~$50–$500** (price varies by country).
- **Link/share outreach**: BuzzSumo **"view shares"** → email people who shared the original
  text article; **Ahrefs** → email sites that **linked** to it — pitch your infographic for a
  re-share or **embed** (use the **WP Embed Code Generator** plugin on WordPress).
- **Google Trends** gauges market size — if the term's graph is **bigger than "digital
  marketing," it's big enough**.
- **Second one-product example (self-reported)**: team member **David** sold an **organic
  eczema solution** at **six figures/year** with one product.
