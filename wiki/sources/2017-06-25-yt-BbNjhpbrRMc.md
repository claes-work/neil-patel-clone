# 4 Easy Steps to Marketing Your Local Business Online

## Summary

In this solo how-to, Neil Patel lays out a four-step playbook for marketing a local business online, centered on ranking in Google's local search results. He advises claiming and completing a Google My Business listing with a consistent name, address, and phone number across the web; optimizing the site for local SEO with localized terms and NAP details on multiple pages, plus local links (e.g., from the Chamber of Commerce); improving click-through rate through more customer reviews and Schema markup; and not relying on Google alone by maintaining and managing reviews on other localized platforms such as Yelp and TripAdvisor.

## Key claims

- Google's local search results show a "local pack" consisting of a map plus exactly three business listings (never five or six), and appearing there is the goal (as of 2017-06-25).
- Setting up Google My Business and submitting corporate information makes a business much more likely to be placed in the local pack (as of 2017-06-25).
- Entering the proper name, address, and phone number in Google My Business and keeping that data consistent across the whole web (e.g., Yelp and your own website) makes a business much more likely to rank (as of 2017-06-25).
- Beneath the map listings are normal organic listings, and a business can rank there too, effectively taking up "double the spots" (as of 2017-06-25).
- Using localized terms on the website and placing the company name, address, and phone number on the site helps local rankings (as of 2017-06-25).
- NAP/local details should appear on multiple pages — not just one — including the contact page, location page, and any major page about the business such as menu pages, which raises rankings (as of 2017-06-25).
- Getting links from sites within your region — including asking the local Chamber of Commerce, which often promotes city businesses, to link to your site — raises rankings; the more regional links, the higher you rank (as of 2017-06-25).
- Seeing a business listing does not guarantee a click, so improving click-through rate is a distinct third step (as of 2017-06-25).
- Generating more customer reviews (by asking in-store customers to review) makes people more likely to click through (as of 2017-06-25).
- ★ Using Schema markup gives Google all the data about a localized business; Google publishes a page explaining how to use the code, and WordPress users can add it automatically via the Yoast SEO plug-in (as of 2017-06-25).
- Businesses should not rely solely on Google, because other localized sites matter — Yelp is popular in the US and TripAdvisor is popular around the world — and a business should be present on them (as of 2017-06-25).
- Encouraging and actively managing reviews, and fixing the underlying product or service when customers are unhappy, leads to continued new reviews; more reviews around the web mean more people show up, and more people showing up means more money (as of 2017-06-25).
