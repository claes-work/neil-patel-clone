# How Content Marketing Started - Module 1 - Lesson 2 - Content Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week-one lesson 2 of the **Content Marketing Unlocked** free course
([[2020-07-27-yt-9_rULUbLqE4]]), covering the history of content marketing and a
keyword-research walkthrough. Neil traces content marketing's origin to John Deere's
1895 farmer magazine *The Furrow*, the Michelin Guide (early 1900s, created to sell
more car travel), and radio/TV soap operas (named for their soap-company sponsors),
through to the digital age (Google, YouTube, Facebook). Quotes Sergey Brin on
Google's founding search philosophy. Walks through finding "valuable keywords" using
Ubersuggest's Keyword Ideas report (target SEO difficulty ≤40, high CPC, search
volume ideally 1,000+/worst-case 250+) plus Wikipedia, Reddit, Forums, Google Trends,
Quora, and Amazon Reviews as free keyword-idea sources, using a hypothetical Gillette
razorblade example. Advises grouping related keywords into one thorough page rather
than many thin pages. Cites case studies: Kraft Heinz claims 4x content-marketing ROI
vs. traditional ad spend; NerdWallet's founder built the company from $800 after
being laid off into a "half a billion dollar" business; neilpatel.com itself gets
"over 10 million visitors a month" from this approach (all self-reported/third-party
claims, not independently verified here).

  rather than many thin ones.
