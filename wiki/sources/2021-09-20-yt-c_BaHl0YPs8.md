# 7 SEO Secrets You Can Learn From Big Brands

## Summary
Seven traits shared by big-brand SEO, each reinforcing existing brand-is-the-moat/
content-at-scale doctrine with fresh concrete numbers: (1) brand search volume drives
organic traffic more than category keywords (Nike 5M+ monthly searches vs. "shoes"
1.2M); (2) big brands earn backlinks passively via PR/earnings/product releases rather
than outreach, self-citing neilpatel.com's own passive Ubersuggest-driven backlinks;
(3) PR is run for product-demand generation (Nike/Apple launch hype), not for SEO —
recommends performance-based PR agencies (names **PR Serve**); (4) content at scale —
cites indexed-page counts: Apple 171M, Microsoft 34.9M, IBM 9.93M, Nike 2.24M, vs. his
own "thousands"; (5) localization/translation into lower-competition languages
(Portuguese, Hindi, Swahili) — self-cites neilpatel.com content in German, Portuguese,
Spanish, Italian, Japanese, and Mandarin; (6) high domain authority tolerates SEO
mistakes because branded search intent is already resolved — reframed as a "put the
user first, not Google first" argument; (7) big companies are slow but data-driven,
recommending smaller businesses combine their own speed advantage with the same
data-driven discipline.
