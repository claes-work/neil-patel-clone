# My Best Marketing Strategies for [2018] | Creative Growth Plans for Business

★ **L3-candidate for next synthesis** — a rare, explicit statement of Neil's capital-allocation
/ "buy traffic instead of building it" strategy, with hard numbers and a lesson learned.

## Summary
Neil's 2018 marketing strategy in one line: **stop paying to create traffic; buy companies that
already have it.** He reveals that generating ~1M visitors/month on neilpatel.com — via video,
podcast, blogging, promotion, ads, and outreach-based link building — cost NP a bit **more than
$110,000/month averaged over 2017**, with no guarantee the spend produces traffic. So the new
plan is acquisition: he just bought a marketing-space blog for **$500,000** that already gets
**~800k (bad month) to 1.2–1.3M (good month) uniques** and is making **under $3k/month** — an
under-monetized asset he can plug into NP's monetization flow. He contrasts two past deals to
teach the core lesson: **Ubersuggest** (bought for ~$110–140k) was a *good* acquisition because
of its traffic; but a domain he bought for **$100k purely for its backlinks**, then 301-redirected,
did **nothing** for his traffic. Conclusion: **buy on traffic, never on links.** His acquisition
rules: ignore existing revenue (no revenue is "even better" — no contracts/conferences/ad deals
to inherit, as with Search Engine Journal, which he chased 2016–end-2017 without a deal); target
**under-monetized** sites; and require **payback in under 12 months** or he won't buy. He's also
using **creative earnout financing** — e.g. $1M up front plus $100k/month (or /year) out of the
acquired business's own profits — a structure he learned from his "buddy Edward," who acquired
mortgage companies the same way. **2018 goal: spend at least $5M acquiring businesses on traffic
alone.**

## Key claims
As of 2018-02-03, Neil states:
- Generating ~1M visitors/month on neilpatel.com **cost a bit more than $110k/month averaged
  over 2017** (video production, podcasting, blogging, promotion, ads, outreach link building) —
  with no guarantee the spend yields traffic (self-reported).
- **2018 strategy: buy blogs/sites based on traffic**, because "it's cheaper to just spend money
  acquiring people who already have the traffic" than to create it.
- He **bought a marketing-space blog for $500,000** that gets ~800k–1.3M uniques/month and makes
  **under $3k/month** — the low monetization is *why* it's a good deal to him (self-reported).
- **Ubersuggest** (~$110–140k; *actually 2017*, see contradiction) was a good acquisition because
  of its **traffic** — the lesson that shaped the strategy.
- Counter-lesson: he once spent **$100k on a domain purely for its backlinks**, 301-redirected it,
  and **nothing happened** to his traffic → **"who cares about links; don't buy anything for links,
  buy purely based off of traffic."**
- **Ignore existing revenue** when acquiring — no revenue is "even better," because you avoid
  inheriting conferences, ad contracts, and other baggage (his objection to **Search Engine
  Journal**, which he pursued 2016–end-2017 without a deal; also tried **Search Engine Watch**, PE-
  owned, via third parties so they wouldn't inflate the price).
- Acquisition math: plug the bought traffic into your **monetization flow** and require
  **payback in under 12 months** — "if I can't make my money back in less than 12 months I will
  not buy it."
- Target **under-monetized** sites (the seller hasn't optimized traffic or conversions), then
  optimize post-purchase to double revenue.
- **Creative earnout financing**: e.g. ~$1M up front plus ~$100k/month (or /year) paid out of the
  acquired business's own profits, so you don't tie up all your cash — a structure learned from
  his "buddy **Edward**," who acquired mortgage companies this way.
- **2018 goal: spend at least $5M** acquiring businesses purely on their traffic (self-reported).
