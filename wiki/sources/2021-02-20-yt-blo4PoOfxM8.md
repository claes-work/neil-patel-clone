# The New Form of Content Marketing

## Summary
Argues that **building and giving away a free tool beats scaling blog content** as a
traffic strategy, using neilpatel.com's own Google Search Console and WordPress data as
evidence. States his blog has 6,978 total posts (6,911 published) generating an
estimated 14 million clicks over the trailing three months per GSC. Walks a specific
example: "affiliate marketing" ranks #1 and earns 116,000 clicks over three months
despite only ~60,000 monthly US searches and 836 million competing results — used to
argue the "affiliate marketing" content space is saturated ("we don't need any more
content on affiliate marketing") and that maintaining rank there requires continual
updates rather than new articles. Contrasts this with the keyword "Ubersuggest" (his
own tool's brand name), which alone drives an estimated 1.421 million clicks over three
months — roughly 12x his own "Neil Patel" personal-brand term (118,000 clicks) — and
Ubersuggest's ~97,000 total backlinks from ~17,000 unique referring domains, which he
attributes entirely to word-of-mouth from giving the tool away free. Argues tools scale
more cheaply long-term than content (though recommends doing both, not one instead of
the other), with the free-giveaway model as the essential lever, and demos
CodeCanyon.net (codecanyon.net) as a way to buy inexpensive white-label SEO/industry
tools (e.g., ~$29) to rebrand and give away without building from scratch, showing
example listings across automotive and real-estate niches.
