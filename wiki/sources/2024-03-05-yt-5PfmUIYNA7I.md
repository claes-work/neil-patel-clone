# How to enrich your email list, Eric's thumbnail coaching call, Boosting YT CPM by 50%

## Summary
Three segments. (1) A brief, mostly-joint discussion of an email-list enrichment tool ("Megahit app," credited to Matt McGarry) that pulls LinkedIn/social data into a newsletter platform. (2) Neil describes a YouTube-thumbnail coaching call with a 3.2M-subscriber creator ("Mike Stack"), whose critique (large "idea TAM," constant thumbnail/headline testing via ViewStats, "below 5/10 in the first hour or two, keep changing it") led Neil to hire a professional thumbnail designer (who works with major YouTubers) and lifted the Marketing School channel's CTR from ~3–4% to ~7–7.5%. (3) The episode's most substantive Neil-attributed material: a detailed, quantified breakdown of NP Digital's enterprise sales/RFP process — multi-call cadence (discovery call, business-analysis call, presentation call with findings/projections), lead-qualification fields (budget, full name, decision-maker status), a 3–6 month typical enterprise close time, $100K–$200K spent per RFP pitch, and 6–8-figure target contract sizes — plus his conviction that fast lead follow-up (within the first 15 minutes) meaningfully lifts close rates. Closes with a monetization tip: Neil recommends podcast mid-rolls (promoting your own product/service) over standard pre/post-roll ads for long-form podcasts, citing Ryan Pineda's podcast as a good example, and a separate tweet-sourced tactic (enabling gambling/alcohol ad categories in AdSense to boost CPM by ~50%) that Neil says he personally doesn't worry about, since B2B content already commands strong CPMs regardless.

## Key claims
- **NP Digital's enterprise sales cycle, quantified.** At NP Digital ("my company"), the process for enterprise prospects is: an initial discovery call, a second call to go deeper into the business (by which point NP Digital has requested data access and analyzed it), then a presentation call breaking down findings/learnings, savings vs. growth opportunities, "pretty much certain" wins vs. "more experimental" bets, low-hanging fruit, and a full annual plan — followed by further follow-up calls, case-study reviews, value-add emails, and often multiple stakeholders/divisions requiring repeat pitches. Typical enterprise close time is 3 months on the fast end, more realistically 6 months; some RFP pitches cost NP Digital $100,000–$200,000 to prepare; target enterprise contract size is 6–8 figures (no 9-figure deals), with rare 6-figure "loss leader" starter divisions. (Neil, 2024-03-05)
- **Lead qualification fields NP Digital collects** (beyond just name/email): budget, company URL, full name (not just first name) — specifically to determine whether the lead is a decision-maker, since the sales pitch differs materially for decision-makers vs. non-decision-makers. This came up in response to a "just collect name/work email/phone and forget the rest" argument raised by someone at Neil's recent founders retreat (see [[wiki/sources/2024-02-23-yt-XsWZmxY33bc]]) — Neil says that minimal-field approach doesn't work for NP Digital's model. (Neil, 2024-03-05)
- **Speed of lead follow-up drives close rate.** Calling leads within the first 15 minutes (rather than a week later) makes "a big difference" on close rates — attributed to a friend/associate ("Stelly," who had an FD/bought a ".com," per the transcript) who evangelized the 15-minute rule. Reiterates "the fortune is in the followup." (Neil, 2024-03-05)
- **YouTube thumbnail/CTR improvement, with before/after numbers.** After a coaching call with a 3.2M-subscriber YouTuber ("Mike Stack") who criticized the Marketing School channel's thumbnails and headlines as weak despite strong content/audio, Neil hired a professional thumbnail designer (who also works with major creators) and says the channel's CTR rose from a baseline ~3–4% to ~7–7.5% on newer thumbnails — while noting the channel's B2B podcast format means overall growth is inherently slower than consumer content. (Neil, 2024-03-05)
- **Podcast-ad-monetization stance: prefers mid-rolls promoting owned offers over standard pre/post-roll ads**, especially for a channel like Marketing School that doesn't need incremental ad revenue since the business already monetizes via NP Digital/the Agency Owners Association; recommends this approach generally for long-form podcasters, citing Ryan Pineda's podcast as an example that does mid-rolls well. Neil reports making ~$0 in YouTube ad revenue by design (ads intentionally left off for marketing-related videos). (Neil, 2024-03-05)
