# How To Write Content That Ranks Page #1 on Google

## Summary
Neil breaks writing page-one content into four steps: (1) know your audience — write
content matched to who you're targeting, using Ubersuggest's demographic data and
content-ideas report (based on search traffic, backlinks, social shares) to pick
topics; (2) execute on-page SEO — cites Search Engine Journal that over 70% of users
ignore paid ads and focus on organic, so SEO matters alongside paid; use a WordPress
plugin (Rank Math or Yoast) and Ubersuggest's site audit report, prioritized by traffic
impact; (3) write content with practical value — illustrates with a personal anecdote
about a HowStuffWorks bidet-installation article that didn't actually teach him how,
versus content that lets the reader take action; integrate question-based keywords from
Ubersuggest into H1/title/meta; publish on a consistent schedule (he publishes every
Tuesday, ~50 weeks/year); (4) use the AIDA model (Awareness, Interest, Desire, Action)
— citing that 73% of buying decisions stem from the headline and that 8/10 people read
a headline but only 2/10 click through; recommends bullet points for desire and
multiple CTAs (as he does on neilpatel.com) for action.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-10, Neil cited Search Engine Journal data that over 70% of users ignore
  paid ads and focus on organic results, as a reason to invest in both SEO and paid.
- As of 2020-02-10, Neil said Ubersuggest's content-ideas report surfaces popular topics
  based on Google search traffic, backlinks, and social shares, to guide topic
  selection.
- As of 2020-02-10, Neil said Rank Math is, in his view, a bit better for SEO beginners
  than Yoast, though both work well.
- As of 2020-02-10, Neil said he publishes on neilpatel.com every Tuesday, for roughly
  50 weeks out of the year (skipping when Tuesday falls on Christmas).
- As of 2020-02-10, Neil cited a stat that 73% of buying decisions are made from the
  point readers encounter the title, and that roughly 8 out of 10 people read a headline
  but only 2 out of 10 click through — underscoring headline importance.
- As of 2020-02-10, Neil said he uses multiple calls-to-action throughout the
  neilpatel.com site (top bars, sliders, sidebar ads) because it increases conversion of
  readers into customers.
- As of 2020-02-10, Neil told a personal anecdote: years ago he searched via Google for
  "how to install a bidet," landed on a HowStuffWorks article that didn't actually teach
  him how, was frustrated, and never returned to the site — using it as an example of
  content that fails to deliver practical value.
