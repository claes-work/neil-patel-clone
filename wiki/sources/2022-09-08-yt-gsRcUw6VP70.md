# 7 Practical Content Marketing Tips

## Summary
Seven content tips: (1) "content isn't king, fresh content is king" — rankings decay
without continual, substantive updates (Wikipedia analogy repeated); (2) content
shouldn't be written purely for search engines or purely for users either — it should
also convert, by blending in product/service mentions naturally (worked example: an SEO
tips post that pitches NP Digital mid-content); (3) visuals (infographics, animated
GIFs) still outperform text-only for engagement and backlinks; (4) if a topic is
oversaturated (worked stat: 1.2B "auto insurance" results vs. 165K monthly US searches,
a ~7,272:1 supply/demand ratio), either find a genuinely new angle or use
AnswerThePublic to surface less-covered topics; (5) follow an 80/20 rule — spend roughly
4x longer promoting content than writing it, primarily via email, push notifications,
and social; (6) always be collecting emails via multiple methods (Linktree, exit
popups, content upgrades) — states a benchmark of roughly 2-3 emails collected per 100
visitors; (7) repurpose content across formats to avoid burnout — his own workflow is
video-first, cascading into blog posts, podcasts, infographics, Reels, and quote-image
social posts.

## Key claims
- Quantifies content oversaturation with the "auto insurance" example: ~1.2 billion

- States an email-collection benchmark: roughly 2-3 emails collected per 100 visitors
