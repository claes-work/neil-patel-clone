# Will Small Businesses Still Profit From Facebook Ads in 2023?

## Summary
In this solo talking-head video, Neil Patel argues Facebook ads are getting harder and more expensive for small businesses but remain profitable if you stop expecting a sale on first contact and instead run a full-funnel, retargeting-heavy strategy. He opens with market stats (millions of advertisers, rising CPC, falling CTR, ad "conversions" that may just be email opt-ins rather than sales) then gives tactics: (1) don't chase profit on the first ad — first drive cold traffic to an educational content piece, then remarket them to the product/service page, which he says lowers overall cost-to-conversion; (2) retarget qualified prospects, e.g. show checkout-abandoners a video that flips the persuasion mode (emotion if the landing page used logic, and vice versa) plus upsells/downsells to raise LTV; (3) use video ads because they're cheaper for brand exposure (invoking the "rule of seven") and Facebook favors them; (4) use 1% lookalike audiences off your converters; (5) test creatives often (he rotates two-to-three times a week, sometimes daily); (6) keep the whole funnel fresh (landing page, copy, testimonials, audience), not just the banner; (7) monitor ad frequency, since repeated impressions without conversion lower ad score and raise cost. He closes plugging Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said Facebook ads are getting harder and more expensive and will keep getting harder and harder.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil cited (attributing to Facebook COO Sheryl Sandberg) that Facebook has over seven million active advertisers, ~2.4 billion active users, and only ~342 active users per advertiser.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said news-feed Facebook CPC has risen year over year and news-feed ad CTR has fallen (he quoted a CTR drop to ~1.20% in Q2 2019), and cited Instapage that the average Facebook ad converts ~9.21% across industries — but noted a "conversion" may just be an email, not a sale, so it isn't necessarily positive ROI.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said don't go for profit on first contact: instead run an ad to an educational content piece, then remarket that user with an ad to the product/service page — showing the ad twice still yields a lower overall cost-to-conversion than selling directly.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said his highest-ROI Facebook campaign retargets checkout-page abandoners with a video showing what it'd be like to be a customer, flipping the persuasion mode: if the landing page sold via logic, the remarketing video uses emotion, and vice versa, because repeating the mode that already failed won't work.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said adding upsells and downsells increases LTV, which lets you spend more on Facebook.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said video ads are cheaper for brand exposure because even non-converters see your brand, invoking the marketing "rule of seven" (a brand is built by someone engaging with it seven times), and Facebook pushes video over text-based ads.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said to use 1% lookalike audiences built from your converters to find similar high-probability buyers and save money versus untargeted spend.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said to rotate creatives at least once a week — he personally rotates two-to-three times a week on the low end, sometimes daily on the high end — to reduce fatigue and improve ROI.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said keep the whole funnel fresh (landing pages, copy, testimonials, reviews, message, audience), not just the banner, to lift conversions.
- As of 2019-11-23, Neil said to monitor ad frequency: repeated impressions mean you're either not reaching new people or people are seeing ads without converting, which lowers ad score and raises cost.

## Notable verbatim quotes
