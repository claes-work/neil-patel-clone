# Are Virtual Summits Still Relevant?

## Summary
Eric interviews Neil about the virtual summit NP Digital had just run (~20,000-30,000
registrants). Neil details the mechanics and reports a 50-60% attendance rate — far
above typical webinar attendance (25-30%) — attributing the gap to multi-day email
(and selective SMS) reminders sent specifically to already-registered attendees, not
broader promotion. He describes monetization beyond ticket sales: case studies and
soft NP Digital service pitches woven into sessions, plus post-session lead capture
(sending slides with a "chat with the team" link to attendees). He names 12-13
speakers from major corporations (Google Italy CMO, American Express CMO, Sony Music
VP, Coca-Cola digital marketing lead, Marriott VP loyalty, BBVA, FedEx marketing
director, ESPN head of growth, Unilever brand director, former Loro Piana CEO) and
describes distribution tactics for people without a large list: reciprocal
promotion partnerships with other summit hosts, and asking speakers to promote to
their own social/email audiences (supplying them ready-made copy and graphics to
lower the friction of promoting). Overall verdict: a virtual summit is not a great
standalone business but a good profitable addition that helps grow the core
business.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- ★ Self-reported event scale: NP Digital's virtual summit drew roughly 20,000-30,000
  registrants, with 50-60% typical attendance (varies by session) — well above
  typical webinar attendance of ~25-30%. (Neil, 2023-11-18) —
  agency-entrepreneurship / content-marketing [self-reported].
- Attributes the high attendance rate specifically to multi-day email reminders sent
  to already-registered attendees (teasing upcoming sessions, e.g. "Neil's about to
  speak on X, going live in 3 hours") — reports attendance was only ~32-33% before
  they added this reminder cadence. (Neil, 2023-11-18) — content-marketing.
- Uses SMS reminders selectively (not for the whole audience) partly because SMS
  costs are much higher outside the U.S. (cites India as more expensive).
  (Neil, 2023-11-18) — content-marketing [self-reported].
- Monetization framework for summits: revenue isn't from ticket sales — it comes from
  woven-in case studies/soft-pitches for the core business (NP Digital) during
  sessions, plus post-event lead capture (sending session slides to attendees with a
  direct "chat with the team" link). (Neil, 2023-11-18) — agency-entrepreneurship /
  content-marketing.
- ★ Named the summit's speaker roster as evidence of drawing enterprise-level guests:
  CMO of Google Italy, CMO of American Express, a Sony Music streaming VP, Coca-Cola's
  digital marketing lead, Marriott's VP of loyalty & partnerships, someone from BBVA,
  FedEx's managing director of marketing, ESPN's head of growth, a senior Unilever
  brand director, and the former CEO of Loro Piana — ~12-13 speakers total.
  (Neil, 2023-11-18) — agency-entrepreneurship [self-reported].
- Distribution playbook for hosts without a large existing list: (1) reciprocal
  cross-promotion partnerships with other summit organizers ("you promote my summit,
  I'll promote yours"); (2) asking speakers to promote to their own social/email
  audiences, with the organizer supplying ready-made copy and social graphics to
  reduce friction. States NP Digital does not use Facebook ads to drive registrants,
  despite calling it "a great way" others use. (Neil, 2023-11-18) — content-marketing
  [self-reported].
- Overall verdict: a virtual summit is "easy revenue... a good business" but "not as
  good as a standalone business" — better framed as a profitable addition that helps
  grow the core business, not a business unto itself. (Neil, 2023-11-18) —
  agency-entrepreneurship / content-marketing.
