# Perplexity's keyword visibility up 86%, Why mainstream media is dead, SaaS spend $2.64 to earn $1 (Marketing School)

## Summary
★ Landmark-worthy for later L3 promotion: Neil's conference/event client-acquisition playbook — the concrete five-move sequence he runs whenever he speaks (talk + QR-code slide capture + "Ask Neil Anything" booth + curated private lunches + an invite-only session where an existing client tells the results on stage) is a reusable, teachable agency-growth framework. This co-hosted episode covers (a) Perplexity's programmatic-SEO surge per a Christy Hines / SEMrush / SimilarWeb data set [third-party, host attribution uncertain], (b) mainstream media "dying" vs. podcasts gaining power [uncertain], (c) Eric's presentation of David Splitz's SaaS data showing companies now spend ~$2.64 to generate $1 of ARR, (d) Neil's high-fidelity event lead-gen playbook and land-and-expand social proof, and (e) Eric relaying South-African agency-talent pricing plus a "the market is the market" hiring stance. Only the event-marketing/hiring material is cleanly Neil; the numeric stats and media/political tangents are Eric's or uncertain and are quarantined below.

## Key claims
- When speaking at a conference, run a five-part lead/client-acquisition sequence: (1) give the talk; (2) put a QR code on screen during the talk so attendees download the slides — this generates a lot of leads; (3) run a booth as an "Ask Neil Anything" (AMA/ANA) with the team on hand; (4) host private lunches, invite specific potential clients to your own table and discuss topics tailored to close revenue; (5) run an invite-only private session (often a lunch presentation) coordinating with the organizer on ideal-customer-type. (Neil, 2024-07-09)
- Of all those event tactics, the one that produces the best results is simply the speaking slot itself — and it doesn't have to be tied to a meal (he's done 10am and 5pm sessions equally well). (Neil, 2024-07-09)
- The strongest close is bringing an existing client on stage during your own private session to narrate the results — "they sell you, you don't have to sell yourself"; NP Digital doesn't pay for this. (Neil, 2024-07-09)
- Land-and-expand works: NP Digital started with one client on SEO alone and grew into email and conversion across the account, and that expansion story sells extremely well as social proof. (Neil, 2024-07-09)
- Throwing your own event so that big businesses show up is a high-leverage creative acquisition play — "fishing with dynamite" — and rewards thinking outside the box. (Neil, 2024-07-09)
- NP Digital is a global firm and does hire from South Africa; talent quality there is real, but it's hard to build a $100M agency out of South Africa (dentsu-scale players notwithstanding). (Neil, 2024-07-09)
- For the invite-on-stage client, cost is minimal because NP Digital is global and typically already has a client in whatever country/city he's speaking in — no fly-in needed, at most a dinner or lunch afterward. (Neil, 2024-07-09)
