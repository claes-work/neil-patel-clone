# Get On Forbes, HuffPost & NYTimes For Free - Module 4 - Lesson 2 - Content Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week-four lesson 2 of the **Content Marketing Unlocked** free course
([[2020-07-27-yt-9_rULUbLqE4]]), covering three "media outlet" traffic channels: guest
posting on major outlets (Forbes, HuffPost, NYT), podcasting, and social media. Gives a
three-email guest-posting outreach template (compliment + constructive critique → soft
follow-up → offer to ghostwrite the missing piece), and stresses `nofollow`-ing any
self-placed guest-post backlinks to avoid "manipulating Google" (naturally-earned links
don't need nofollow). Frames guest posting as brand-building, not a one-off — invokes
the Rule of Seven (repeated brand exposure → conversion). Covers podcasting: use
Ubersuggest's Content Ideas report (high estimated visits + high backlinks + high
social shares) to find episode topics, distribute everywhere (not just Apple/Android),
and cites Marketing School's own multi-platform distribution driving "over a million
views a month" (self-reported). Advises picking guest-podcast targets by size-fit
rather than aiming at top shows like Joe Rogan's out of the gate. On social media,
recommends platform selection by audience (LinkedIn for B2B, Instagram for
e-commerce, Facebook's 2.2B-user scale despite perception it's "ugly and dead"),
flags YouTube's ranking mechanic as inverted from text SEO (early-24-hour view velocity
matters most), and suggests paid influencer placement via influence.co.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-08-20, Neil's guest-posting outreach is a 3-email sequence: (1) compliment
  + a constructive content gap ("you hit on B and C, but not X, Y, Z"), (2) a
  no-ask follow-up compliment, (3) an offer to write the missing piece himself —
  designed to route him from the writer to that writer's editor.
- As of 2020-08-20, Neil's rule: self-placed guest-post links should be `nofollow`ed;
  only naturally-earned mentions can stay dofollow, to stay within Google's guidelines.
- As of 2020-08-20, Neil invoked the Rule of Seven — brand exposure seven times raises
  conversion likelihood — as the rationale for guest posting repeatedly, not once.
- As of 2020-08-20, Neil claimed Marketing School (his podcast, co-hosted with Eric
  Siu, distributed across YouTube/Spotify/iTunes/Android/Google Play/SoundCloud) gets
  "over a million views a month" (self-reported).
- As of 2020-08-20, Neil's YouTube ranking model: unlike long-and-steady text SEO,
  YouTube rewards the video that gets the most views in its first 24 hours, which then
  tends to keep ranking highest over time.
- As of 2020-08-20, Neil advised picking guest-podcast targets by achievable size first
  (not the "Joe Rogan podcast," which "is not going to feature you or me") and working
  upward.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "By giving people feedback and giving them positive feedback, but also giving them a critique that shows how they can improve, they're much more likely to want to respond to you, build that relationship, versus people who just keep emailing them asking them for things."

> "When someone sees or interacts with your brand seven times, they're much more likely to convert into a customer, and this is why you want to continually guest post and build up your brand over time."

> "It's the person who gets the most views in the first 24 hours [that] tends to rank the highest over time as well."
