# SEO Expert Reveals: The Easiest Way To Explode Your Blog Traffic

## Summary
Argues that answering the questions people search for is the fastest way to grow blog
traffic, using Quora (cited as "the 59th most popular site in the world" per
Similarweb, ~788M monthly visitors) as the proof-of-concept: a site built entirely on
Q&A content can command enormous traffic. Cites Ubersuggest data that roughly "8.1%" of
Google searches are questions, and extrapolates (using a cited "8.5 billion searches"
per day on Google) to roughly "688 million" daily and "251 billion" annual question
searches. Recommends a 7-step process: (1) go to AnswerThePublic.com, (2) type a 1–2
word seed keyword for the industry, (3) review the popular questions it surfaces
(sorted by popularity), (4) Google each question and study what's currently ranking,
(5) create content that answers each question, explicitly "one-upping" (not copying)
the top-ranking pages, (6) publish hundreds-to-thousands of such posts since each
individually drives modest traffic but the aggregate compounds into large volume, and
(7) repeat the process with other seed keywords in the space. Frames this as
specifically valuable for niche/industry-relevant questions rather than Quora's
everything-under-the-sun approach. Closes with an NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- Cites Similarweb: Quora is "the 59th most popular site in the world" with ~"788
  million visitors a month" (third-party stat as cited, dated 2022-11-19).
- Cites Ubersuggest-derived data: roughly "8.1%" of Google searches are questions,
  extrapolated against a stated "8.5 billion searches" per day figure to roughly 688M
  daily / 251B annual question searches (self-reported/proprietary Ubersuggest data
  plus Neil's own extrapolation math, dated 2022-11-19).
- Recommends building content volume (hundreds to thousands of question-answering
  posts) rather than relying on any single post to drive meaningful traffic (self-
  reported content strategy, dated 2022-11-19).
