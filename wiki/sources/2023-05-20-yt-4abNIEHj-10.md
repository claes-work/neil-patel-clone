# New to Digital Marketing: 10 Essential Tips for a Strong Start

## Summary
Narrated recap-style interview covering Neil's views across several beginner-facing topics.
On post-pandemic digital marketing: says COVID pushed previously offline demographics (his
own parents, cited as an example) into e-commerce for the first time, and that this habit
persists post-pandemic — raising both demand for digital marketing and its competitiveness
(more spend/time/resources needed for the same results). On content saturation: cites "over
a billion blogs" against ~7 billion people (roughly one blog per seven people) plus an
ever-growing platform list (TikTok, LinkedIn, Clubhouse, Instagram) as evidence that content
volume will keep rising, but coins the term **"content blindness"** (an analogy to banner
blindness) to argue audiences will increasingly tune out most of it regardless — meaning the
practical bar is standing out, not just producing more, even as AI tools (mentions Canva)
make production easier. Names two content-format bets: **simultaneous live-streaming across
platforms** (Facebook/LinkedIn/YouTube/Instagram at once, framed like a multi-platform podcast
interview) and **Reels/Shorts/short-form vertical video** generally, plus a passing mention
of Pinterest's then-new "story pins" (up to 20 pages) as a repurposing target for reels/TikTok
content. His beginner's path into digital marketing: build and market your own site (free via
WordPress.com or a DreamHost-hosted domain) rather than only reading about it — learning by
doing, reading either his own site or others'. On niche selection: focus on what you're
naturally skilled at rather than trying to do everything (uses "I can't be a doctor" as his
own limiting example). His personal information-diet: reads blogs over books for recency
(names **Search Engine Land, Search Engine Journal, Moz, and Backlinko**), tracks competitors'
content output, and monitors competitors' podcast interviews for tactics they reveal. Gives
a **content-time-allocation formula**: 40% beginner-level content (high search volume, low
social/backlink value), 10% advanced/data-driven content (high social/backlink value, lower
traffic), 20% rewriting/refreshing older underperforming articles (compares trailing-30-day
traffic year-over-year via Search Console to find candidates), and 30% promotion/outreach.
Advice to his 20-year-old self: focus — he says he had "ADD" and chased too many side
projects/investments (cites a hypothetical example of funding a random idea like
"Invisalign through 3D printers") instead of doubling down on marketing, and believes he'd
have built one much larger company had he focused. On social media strategy: explicitly
tells creators **not** to chase virality, crediting his own 37M video views to consistency
over any single viral hit, and framing "going viral" as outside one's control while
consistency/hard work is within it.
