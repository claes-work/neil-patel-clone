# The Easiest Way to Get Clients For Your Ad Agency

## Summary
Describes a specific cold-outreach tactic he says he used starting his own agency and
still uses: find recently-funded companies (up to $20M raised — he says the tactic
stops working above that) on Crunchbase, do a free, highly specific "teardown" of what
they're doing wrong in your service area (paid ads, CRO, design, etc.) with screenshots
and concrete step-by-step fixes — not just "hire me" — then email it to both the
investors *and* the founder in the same thread (he says response rates are higher than
emailing the founder alone, "from what I tested"), using hunter.io to find email
addresses if needed. Recording the teardown as a video/screen-recording converts better
but takes more work. Claims roughly 1-in-4-to-5 emails convert with a good teardown.
Self-cites NP Digital becoming "the sixth fastest-growing ad agency, according to
Adweek" from using this tactic early on.

## Key claims
- States a specific conversion rate for this outreach tactic: "typically one out of
  every four and five emails" (as of 2022-10-08, self-reported, no sample size given).
- States NP Digital became "the sixth fastest-growing ad agency, according to Adweek"
  using this tactic (as of 2022-10-08, self-reported award — consistent with the
  identical "sixth fastest growing ad agency in the world... per Ad Week" claim already
  recorded from the 2022-09-15 source; reinforces, does not newly establish, that
  self-reported marker).
- States a size ceiling for the tactic: works on companies that raised up to ~$20M;
  says it doesn't work well on larger, $100M+-raised corporations (as of 2022-10-08,
  tactical claim).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> It's funny, everyone thinks it's easy for me to close business... You always have to work hard to close deals.

> No, your solutions can't be hire me. You literally have to tell them how to fix everything step-by-step, even if they decide to do it on their own without paying you.

> If you send the email off to both the investors and the founder in the same thread, there's a good chance that they will respond, much higher than if you just email the founder alone, from what I tested.
