# An SEO Tip That Just Works (Especially If You Are a Beginner)

## Summary
A beginner-friendly long-tail-question SEO tactic modeled on Quora (cited as a top-100
global site per Similarweb). Steps: (1) use AnswerThePublic to surface popular questions
in a niche (low-competition long-tail keywords); check search volume in Ubersuggest;
(2) write a unique page per question with a substantive answer (300+ words minimum,
ideally 400-1,000+, no fluff) — illustrates with a worked "what is it like to be an
Olympian?" example vs. a trivial "what's 2+2?" non-example; (3) keep answers updated as
circumstances change (repeats the Wikipedia freshness analogy and the "165 searches vs.
2.6 billion results" dog-food oversaturation stat, previously logged with a different
number in an earlier source); (4) promote via free social sharing/internal linking, no
ad spend required; (5) scale to hundreds or 1,000+ questions — five or ten isn't enough
to move the needle.

## Key claims
- Recommends a minimum page length of "300 plus" words per question-answer page, ideally
  400-1,000+ (as of 2022-09-17, self-reported tactical guidance).
- Restates the "dog food" search-oversaturation example (165 monthly US searches vs. 2.6
  billion results) — a repeat/variant of the previously logged "auto insurance"
  oversaturation stat with different numbers for a different keyword.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> There isn't a specific number of questions that you need to create content around... but if you do this with a thousand questions or even a hundred, you'll notice nice traffic gains.
