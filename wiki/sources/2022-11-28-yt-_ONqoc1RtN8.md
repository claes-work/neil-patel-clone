# The Secret to Getting a 30% Email Open Rate

## Summary
Seven-strategy playbook for lifting email open rates above the ~20% industry average.
Self-reports getting 30%+ open rates on "almost every single" email he sends, and that
NP Digital clients often see 30-40%+. Strategies: (1) make emails conversational —
personalize subject lines with the recipient's name, keep subject lines to "three
words max" for his best results (vs. Campaign Monitor's cited 41-character optimum);
(2) segment subscribers (he segments mainly by location, given NP Digital's
country-specific services, and by buyer vs. non-buyer); (3) avoid spam folders by
warming a new sending IP gradually (100 → 500 → 1,000 → 2,000 → 3,000 → 5,000 → 10,000
emails/day across a week) or, his own preferred shortcut, piggybacking on a shared IP
(e.g., ConvertKit) to inherit its reputation instead of warming his own; also prunes
recipients who ignore four emails in a row into a re-engagement send before deleting
unresponsive addresses; (4) give value in every email, not just sell; (5) time sends
for weekday mornings, not nights/weekends, using time-zone segmentation; (6) write
casual, lowercase subject lines — self-reports a 3.43% open-rate lift from this; (7)
A/B test subject lines. Closes with an NP Digital plug (email marketing services).

## Key claims
- States average industry open rate is 20.33% (cites MailChimp) while his own emails
  and NP Digital client emails run 30%, "in many cases above 40%" (self-reported,
  dated 2022-11-28).
- Cites Constant Contact: $36 average ROI per $1 spent on email marketing, up to $70
  for top performers (as cited, dated 2022-11-28).
- Self-reports segmenting his list primarily by geography (NP Digital's services
  differ by country) and secondarily by buyer-vs-non-buyer status, sending different
  content to each (dated 2022-11-28).
- Self-reports a personal practice: recipients who don't open four consecutive emails
  over roughly a month get one high-value "re-engagement" email; if that's ignored too,
  he deletes the address rather than risk a spam-folder reputation hit (dated
  2022-11-28).
- Reports an all-lowercase subject-line style increases his own open rates by
  roughly 3.43% (self-reported, dated 2022-11-28).
