# The 5 Most Important Skills in SEO

## Summary
Neil argues SEO shifted from a purely technical discipline a decade ago into a
multidisciplinary field, and lays out five foundational skills. (1) Creativity: he
traces his own backlink-earning tactics evolving from detailed visual/infographic
content, to data-rich posts, to — his current favorite — releasing free tools; he cites
Ubersuggest (mostly free, "over 10,000 backlinks") and other free tools on
neilpatel.com, and suggests buying and white-labeling a cheap tool from CodeCanyon if
you can't build your own. (2) Understanding analytics: not just reading a Google
Analytics/Data Studio report, but knowing what specific changes to make to actually
improve conversions. (3) CRO (conversion rate optimization): even though SEO drives
traffic, that traffic must be converted — e.g. linking to product pages within
informational content (his toaster-oven example) — so SEOs need at least a working
understanding of CRO. (4) People skills: SEO has become a multi-person effort (content
writers, social promoters, outreach, copywriters, developers for technical
implementation), requiring project-management tools like Asana or Trello to coordinate
efficiently. (5) Experimentation: citing (dated, several-years-old) data that Google
makes roughly nine algorithm changes a day, he argues constant testing — of titles,
meta text, cross-linking, video embeds, international/hreflang expansion — is the only
way to keep up, since fluctuation (positive or negative) is informative.

## Key claims
- SEO shifted from a purely technical discipline about a decade before 2022-05-12 into
  a multidisciplinary field requiring several different skill sets (as of 2022-05-12).
- Neil's own backlink-earning approach evolved over time: detailed visual/infographic
  content → data-rich posts → (his current favorite) releasing free tools, because
  competitors copy each successive content tactic (self-reported, as of 2022-05-12).
- Ubersuggest, mostly free, has over 10,000 backlinks, per Neil's own account
  (self-reported, as of 2022-05-12) — consistent with the same 10,000+ backlink figure
  given in [[wiki/sources/2022-04-25-yt-yUvEB7yiBFk]].
- If you can't build a free tool yourself, buying and white-labeling a cheap tool from
  CodeCanyon and hosting it on your own site is a viable substitute (as of 2022-05-12).
- SEOs need at least a working understanding of CRO (conversion rate optimization)
  because driving traffic alone doesn't guarantee sales/leads — e.g. linking from
  informational content to relevant product pages (as of 2022-05-12).
- SEO has become a multi-person discipline (content, social promotion, outreach,
  copywriting, technical/dev implementation), requiring project-management tools such
  as Asana or Trello to run efficiently as a team (as of 2022-05-12).
- Citing data described as "from a few years ago," Google made roughly nine algorithm
  changes per day, and Neil says no newer public figure has since been released
  (as of 2022-05-12) — an unreconciled, likely-stale statistic flagged as such rather
  than treated as a current figure.
- Constant testing (titles, meta text, cross-linking, content updates, video embeds,
  international/hreflang expansion) is necessary to keep up with frequent algorithm
  changes, and fluctuating results (positive or negative) are informative rather than
  something to be afraid of (as of 2022-05-12).
