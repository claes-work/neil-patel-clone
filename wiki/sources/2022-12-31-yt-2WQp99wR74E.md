# Why Your Blog Strategy Isn't Driving Traffic

## Summary
Five reasons a blog underperforms even when following "Google's exact device"
(SEO best practices). Cites HubSpot: marketers who prioritize blogging are 13x more
likely to see positive ROI. (1) Missing images — cites Jeff Bullas that articles with
images get 95% more total views than those without; recommends infographics (via
Canva, free, or hiring designers via Dribbble/Upwork) and video, restating his belief
"if a picture says a thousand words, video, they say 10,000 words." (2) Poor linking
— cites long-form content gets 77% more links on average than short articles;
explains internal linking as how Google crawls and relates a site's articles (his
example: a digital-marketing article linking out to dedicated social-media-marketing
and SEO guides). (3) Not using topic clusters — cites a former Buffer product
marketer (Kevan Lee, garbled in captions as "Lua"/possibly "offered Lua") who built
Instagram/marketing topic clusters and grew weekly organic traffic 48% (27,000 to
40,000 visitors) in six months; cites Moz's "beginner's guide to SEO" as a topic-
cluster example. (4) Unoptimized meta descriptions/title tags — cites title tags of
15-40 characters get an 8.6% higher CTR than longer ones, and recommends leveraging
curiosity/emotion in titles (his example: "The 7 Benefits of Green Tea. Number 5 Will
Shock You"). (5) Insufficient social promotion — cites 73% of marketers believe
social media has been very effective for their business; states his own practice of
promoting every blog post across every major social platform, crediting it with
materially changing his business and revenue. Closes with an NP Digital plug.
