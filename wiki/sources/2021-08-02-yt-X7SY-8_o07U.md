# What Would Neil Patel Do If He Owned Your Website (And You Should Start Doing Too)

## Summary
A six-question diagnostic framework Neil says he'd apply if he owned the viewer's
business. (1) Winning product/service — word-of-mouth doctrine reinforced, gauged via
NPS (Net Promoter Score), with the recommendation to keep asking dissatisfied
respondents why and iterate. (2) Proven sales process — retells the **KISSmetrics vs.
Mixpanel** story: KISSmetrics raised ~$16M and had 2-3x Mixpanel's traffic at peak, yet
Mixpanel eventually raised over $50M+ because it out-executed on sales, not product or
channels — a specific competitive/funding detail not previously this explicit in the
wiki (existing KISSmetrics bio notes cite $16.4M/seven-rounds and a "$100M copycat"
figure; this is a same-story variant citing "50 plus million" for the copycat/rival,
worth a synthesis-pass reconciliation note, not a hard contradiction — both are
self-reported approximations). Recommends the book "Winning By Design" for sales.
(3) Marketing funnel with upsells/downsells — concrete NP Digital chain example
(SEO → paid-ad management → Amazon/Walmart/Target listings → CRO → email marketing) and
a Legion Athletics (supplement brand) product-upsell example (offer a "speed" upsell and
an "automation" upsell at checkout regardless of the first answer). (4) Lead collection —
credits **Frank Kern** with the line "the money's in the list" (list-as-ATM-machine
metaphor), plus an Overstock.com anecdote about email being one of its largest channels
years ago. (5) On-page optimization via the Ubersuggest Site Audit report, which
prioritizes fixes by impact. (6) Traffic-to-sales strategy — recommends outbound sales,
paid ads, partnerships/affiliates; an influencer-outreach tactic via influence.co
(negotiate a commission-per-sale deal, get permission to feature the influencer's image
on the landing page to lift conversions); and a B2B partnership example — NP Digital
trades referrals with e-commerce dev shops (they send messy-code clients needing SEO/
traffic help; NP Digital sends clients needing dev/platform cleanup work back).
