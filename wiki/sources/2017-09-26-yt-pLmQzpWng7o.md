# How to Get 7 Times More Twitter Traffic | Twitter Marketing Tips

## Summary
Neil argues that simply being on Twitter is not enough — you have to post regularly and often. He recounts a one-week test his team ran: posting only once per day averaged 749 visitors over the seven-day period; then, over a comparable period, they ramped up to posting 40 times per day, which lifted Twitter traffic from 749 to 5,623 visitors. He stresses that traffic did not scale linearly (posting 40x did not yield 40x traffic), but it rose substantially. His practical takeaway: post as much as your available content allows, ideally at least 10 times per day. Not every post needs a link — when he is not posting links he engages with the community, replies to people in his field, and has conversations, so that when he does post links back to his site more people click them.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-26, Neil states:
- Being on Twitter alone is not enough; you have to post regularly to get results (as of 2017-09-26).
- You should be posting multiple times per day, not just once or twice (as of 2017-09-26).
- In a one-week test, posting once per day averaged 749 visitors for the whole week (as of 2017-09-26).
- Ramping up to posting 40 times per day raised Twitter traffic from 749 to 5,623 — roughly a 7.5x increase, though not the 40x the posting volume might suggest (as of 2017-09-26).
- The only variable changed between the two tests was posting frequency (once/day vs 40x/day) (as of 2017-09-26).
- How often to post depends on how much content you have; if you only have enough for once or twice a day, that is fine, and you can fill in with status updates and photos (as of 2017-09-26).
- Ideally, aim to post on Twitter at least 10 times per day to get more traffic (as of 2017-09-26).
- Not all posts should carry links; Neil tries to put links on almost all of his, and otherwise engages with and replies to people in his field (as of 2017-09-26).
- Replying to and engaging others in your field earns more engagement, so that when you do post links, more people click through (as of 2017-09-26).
