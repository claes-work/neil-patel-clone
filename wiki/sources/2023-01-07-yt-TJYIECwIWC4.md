# This is KILLING Your SEO Strategy (Here's How to Fix It)

## Summary
Answers "if you could avoid one SEO mistake, what would it be?" — dismisses
the obvious answers (keyword stuffing, duplicate content, buying backlinks) in
favor of his actual pick: not optimizing UX. Argues search engines evaluate
pages on user experience, not just keywords/backlinks. Five UX tips: (1)
optimize page speed — cites 88.5% of visitors leave a website due to slow load
time; recommends a CDN so content serves from geographically nearby servers;
(2) optimize images/content — cites republishing old posts with new content/
images can increase traffic by up to 106%, and cites HTTP Archive that images
average 21% of total page weight, recommending file-size (not dimension)
compression; (3) perfect headlines — cites 36% of SEO experts rank headlines
as the most important SEO element, quotes David Ogilvy ("80 cents on the
dollar spent on the headline"), cites 8/10 people read a headline vs. 2/10
who read the rest, and recommends A/B testing headlines via paid ads; (4)
simplify navigation — cites 84.6% of web designers say crowded design is the
most common small-business mistake; recommends Crazy Egg (his own product) for
click-tracking and usertesting.com for user testing; (5) improve mobile
experience — cites Google reporting 60% of searches happen on phones;
recommends responsive design and browser testing across devices. Closes with
an NP Digital plug.
