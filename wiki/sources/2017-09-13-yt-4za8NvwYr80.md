# How to Learn SEO: My Secret Method For Search Engine Optimization

## Summary
Neil lays out a beginner's method for learning SEO by doing rather than studying: build a low-stakes "junk" website you're excited about instead of practicing on your real business. He recommends spinning up a self-hosted WordPress.org blog (via HostGator or BlueHost one-click installs), publishing content on any topic that interests you, then installing the Yoast SEO plugin to handle on-page optimization for Google (keywords in the title tag and meta description). From there he advises internally cross-linking related blog posts when it makes sense, and building backlinks by using Ahrefs to find who links to competitors and emailing those sites a short pitch to earn links. Neil frames these as the basics that generate traffic over time, after which learners can experiment; he stresses that real SEO is far more advanced and complex, but the way to learn is by practicing on your own site.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-13, Neil states:
- SEO is the best way to get consistent traffic to a website (as of 2017-09-13).
- The way to learn SEO is to practice, and you should not practice on your main business or main idea — build a fun "junk" website instead (as of 2017-09-13).
- Use a self-hosted WordPress.org blog on your own domain, not WordPress.com; HostGator or BlueHost offer one-click WordPress installs if you're unsure how to set it up (as of 2017-09-13).
- After writing content, install the Yoast SEO plugin, which acts as a shortcut that makes a blog highly optimized for Google, though you still must add keywords to the title tag and meta description (as of 2017-09-13).
- Cross-link all your relevant blog posts together when it makes contextual sense, and don't link them when it doesn't (as of 2017-09-13).
- Build links by entering a competitor URL into Ahrefs (ahrefs.com) to see who links to them, then emailing those sites to pitch your similar business and request a link (as of 2017-09-13).
- Many people will ignore outreach emails, but converting even five out of 100 to link back is a great result (as of 2017-09-13).
- Real SEO is far more advanced and complex than these steps, but you must start with the basics and learn them by doing it on your own website (as of 2017-09-13).
