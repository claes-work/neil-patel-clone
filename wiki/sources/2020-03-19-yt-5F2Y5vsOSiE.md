# 8 Things to Remove From Your Website Immediately If You Want to Rank on Google

## Summary
Neil lists eight site-level factors he says hold back Google rankings regardless of
content/technical-SEO/backlink effort: (1) outdated content — cites Hosting Tribunal's
1.7B+ websites and 2M+ blog posts published daily, and WorldWideWebSize's estimate of
63B+ pages indexed by Google; recommends using Google Search Console's date comparison
(now vs. a year ago) to find previously-strong pages that have decayed and refresh them,
since Google evaluates overall site quality, not just individual pages; (2) thin content
— notes 500 words used to be considered long-form "six years ago" (implying ~2014) but
no longer suffices; fix by expanding or 301-redirecting into deeper pages, assessed via
GA traffic/bounce-rate/time-on-page; (3) excessive/irrelevant ads — hurt load time and
UX; should blend in like Google's own ads rather than dominate the page; (4)
automatically generated content — doesn't deliver a good experience even if
customized; use Ubersuggest to benchmark top-ranking pages' backlinks/social
shares/domain score to gauge what a genuinely better page needs; (5) intrusive popups/
interstitials — cites Google's Webmaster Central Blog stance that pages with intrusive
interstitials give a poorer experience; recommends exit popups, inline opt-ins, ribbons,
top bars (e.g. Hello Bar), and in-text CTAs instead of entry popups; (6) irrelevant
internal links — over-linking or off-topic anchor text harms rather than helps SEO,
should only serve the user; (7) duplicate content — Google doesn't penalize duplicate
content per se but won't rank it over originals; includes both literal plagiarism and
"beaten to death" topics (his banana-nutrition-facts example) that add no new value;
(8) backlinks to spammy sites / reciprocal link schemes — Google treats "link to me,
I'll link to you" deals as a link scheme; only link where it serves the user, and
disclose/nofollow paid links. Closes with an agency plug.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-03-19, Neil cited Hosting Tribunal that there are over 1.7 billion
  websites and more than 2 million blog posts published daily, and WorldWideWebSize's
  estimate that Google has 63+ billion pages indexed.
- As of 2020-03-19, Neil said a 500-word page was considered long-form content "six
  years ago" but that standard no longer holds — thin pages tend not to rank.
- As of 2020-03-19, Neil said Google's own Webmaster Central Blog states that pages
  with intrusive interstitials provide a poorer user experience than pages where
  content is immediately accessible, and recommended exit-intent popups over
  entry popups.
- As of 2020-03-19, Neil said Google does not penalize duplicate content outright but
  will not rank it above original content, and extended "duplicate" to include
  over-saturated topics, not just literal plagiarism.
- As of 2020-03-19, Neil described reciprocal link exchanges ("I'll link to you if you
  link to me") as a Google link scheme that can hurt rankings.
- As of 2020-03-19, Neil recommended using Ubersuggest to compare a target page against
  top-ranking competitors' backlinks, social shares, and domain score before writing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "There are things on your website that are sabotaging your Google rankings and traffic. No matter how much effort you put into writing content, optimize your technical SEO, building backlinks, they're going to keep you down."

> "Google is looking at your whole site as a whole, and when it's all buttoned up and perfect, you're going to do much better."

> "It's about providing the best user experience. If you link to something and you think it provides value to a user, then link to it... If you feel that, hey, this link isn't going to provide any value but I just want to link to it for the sake of it because they're paying me money, don't do it."
