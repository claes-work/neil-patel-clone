# 7 Advanced Ways to Get Free Traffic That Converts

## Summary

Neil gives seven "advanced" free-traffic tactics for people who can't afford ads and find
SEO too slow. (1) **Post on Medium** — but only the first four or five paragraphs with a
"click to continue" link back to your site, where a popup/content-upgrade captures the
email; Medium distributes to its own community so you pick up its visitors. (2) **Post FULL
articles on LinkedIn** (not excerpts — LinkedIn now wants people to stay on-platform) and
offer a visual **cheat sheet** whose steps link back to your site. (3) **Guest on other
people's podcasts**, but warm them up first by asking for a **quote for an article** and
linking out; circle back a month later to ask to be a guest, or just ask them to reference
your data on their show. (4) **Expert roundups** — ask many marketers for one tip; they
share/link the result; then build **"ultimate list" posts** (top-100 blogs) with **badges**
(made cheaply on Fiverr) that people embed and link back. (5) **Contests/giveaways** with a
**point-based, referral system** (points for each share, more for blogging about it) rather
than a plain like-and-share. (6) **OnePress Social Locker** (WordPress) — gate bonus content
behind a share. (7) **Repurpose content into live video** across Facebook, YouTube, Twitter,
Instagram, and LinkedIn simultaneously — lower volume but higher-converting because live
viewers are more engaged.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-08-08, Neil said **Google does not penalize duplicate content**, so you can
  republish your own material on Medium/LinkedIn.
- As of 2019-08-08, Neil's Medium tactic: **post only the first ~4–5 paragraphs** with a
  click-to-continue link back to a site page that captures the email.
- As of 2019-08-08, Neil said **LinkedIn now rewards full articles kept on-platform** (posting
  excerpts to drive off-site "used to work years ago" but no longer), offset by a linked
  visual cheat sheet.
- As of 2019-08-08, Neil's podcast-guesting play: **lead with a quote request + link-out**,
  build rapport across several experts, then ask to guest or be referenced.
- As of 2019-08-08, Neil said **expert roundups work regardless of your traffic** because the
  contributors share/link; follow with "ultimate list" posts + Fiverr **badges** for backlinks.
- As of 2019-08-08, Neil said **contests only drive free traffic with a point/referral
  system**; plain like-and-share "doesn't work as well."
- As of 2019-08-08, Neil said **live video converts better** than text because viewers are
  "super engaged," and you can go live on five platforms at once.
