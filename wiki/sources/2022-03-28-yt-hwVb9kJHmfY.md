# How to Write a Blog Post Fast

## Summary
A tactical piece on writing blog content quickly, opened with a personal
success-attribution claim: 20 years of high-volume content writing that has attracted
"hundreds of millions" of visitors to his sites, roughly 2 million/month from SEO
alone, over a thousand career clients (many Fortune 500), and has grown client
companies into seven-, eight-, and nine-figure annual revenue through content
marketing. Contrasts writing short (~700-word) posts in his early days, which ranked
in days, against today's more competitive landscape where he now targets ~2,000-word
posts but has optimized for speed. Explicitly rejects the "10,000 hours" (Malcolm
Gladwell) framing as impractical advice, arguing practice is still necessary but
shortcuts exist to compress it. Recommends: Frase.io for automated competitor content
analysis (topics, heading counts, word counts, sources, audience questions); AI
writing tools (Jasper.ai, Copy.ai, Copyshark, Copysmith, Jarvis) to generate a fast
first draft that still needs editing, roughly halving drafting time; and reusable
content frameworks/templates (e.g. the AIDA formula — awareness, interest, desire,
action) to structure content quickly. Claims the combined process can cut content
production time by roughly 4–5x.
