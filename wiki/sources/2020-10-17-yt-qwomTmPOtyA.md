# How to Promote YouTube Videos (Even With 0 Subscribers) - Module 2 - Lesson 3 - YouTube Unlocked

## Summary
The sixth and final lesson of "YouTube Unlocked" (Module 2, Lesson 3; follows
[[wiki/sources/2020-10-15-yt-5BfdTwxVicw]]) — **this video COMPLETES the course**
launched at [[wiki/sources/2020-10-05-yt-BJhTePXFvGo]]. Covers video promotion and
discovery mechanics. Neil restates on-page video SEO (keywords in title/description/
tags/spoken-in-video, brand-recognizable thumbnails, correct category selection,
manual SRT transcription files, "cards" driving viewers to other videos to boost
session time) before making his central claim of the lesson: YouTube SEO inverts
traditional SEO — rankings are decided overwhelmingly by performance in the first
24 hours (and especially the first hour), not by a slow accretion of authority over
time. He gives his own promotion playbook for that window — email blast, push
notification, Twitter — and recommends cross-posting the same video to every other
platform (Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, Twitter, LinkedIn) plus embedding it on the
blog. On influencer/outreach growth, he frames influencers as a low-cost "moat":
interviewing them costs nothing and gets their audience exposed to the channel,
which he calls the easiest lever if you don't want to pay for promotion. He closes
the course with a practical adoption note: rewatch lessons as needed, apply the
worksheets incrementally (one or two action items a week, not everything at once).

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-17, Neil's central claim: "YouTube SEO is the opposite of
  traditional SEO" — whatever performs well in the first 24 hours (especially the
  first hour) tends to rank and keep ranking, versus the slow, sustained-authority
  model of traditional web SEO.
- As of 2020-10-17, Neil's first-hour promotion playbook: email blast, push
  notification blast, and social (Twitter) sharing, timed to the launch window
  rather than spread out.
- As of 2020-10-17, Neil frames interviewing influencers (rather than paying them)
  as the easiest zero-cost way to access their audience and grow subscribers.
- As of 2020-10-17, Neil recommends videos be 5-15 minutes — long enough to avoid
  ranking penalties for being "too short," but capped to avoid losing viewers.
- As of 2020-10-17, Neil recommends syndicating every video across all other owned
  channels (blog embed, other socials) because embedding/cross-posting increases
  view counts, which in turn signals positively to YouTube's ranking system
  (self-reported).
