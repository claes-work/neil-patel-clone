# THIS is Exactly Who I Admire the Most and Why

## Summary

A short, personal values monologue answering "Neil, who do you admire the most?" Neil
deliberately rejects the "cheesy" stock answers — his parents (whom he loves) or famous
billionaires like Elon Musk, Warren Buffett, or Bill Gates (whom he admires but won't name
as #1). His honest answer: he doesn't admire any single person the most; instead he admires
a **type** of person — those trying to make a change and make the world better, regardless
of scale. He points to the everyday people who "make the world go round" — doctors, dentists,
teachers, police — acknowledging imperfections (some cops/teachers fall short) while valuing
their contribution. The change he admires can be large or small: volunteering at a soup
kitchen, helping get humanity to Mars, running medical camps for clean water or disease in
Africa. He cites Steve Ballmer (ex-Microsoft CEO) working on the education problem as an
example of a wealthy person doing "amazing things." He closes with his standard offer to help
viewers for free.
