# The Power of No — The Best Sales Tactic

## Summary

★ L3-candidate — Neil's contrarian sales/pricing stance: saying **"no"** is the best sales
tactic and a persona-relevant belief about protecting margins and client respect. The core
story: clients constantly ask for discounts, extra work outside the contract, or free help with
things you don't specialize in; if you keep saying yes, margins collapse, you're miserable, and
clients respect you less and get worse results. Saying no earns respect *and* more money.
Tips: (1) **When someone asks for a discount or out-of-scope work, say no** — not rudely; you may
choose to go above-and-beyond for something small, but on *your* terms, not on client demand.
He analogizes to dating: someone who says yes to everything becomes unattractive, while playing
hard-to-get creates desire. (2) **Follow up with why** — explain the no ("my margins are
razor-thin; a discount means fewer services and worse results"), which makes it land as
reasonable. (3) **Use "no" in the workplace too** — decline unrealistic deadlines/requests from
your boss or coworkers, but never out of laziness (put in unpaid overtime as a team player).
When you say no to an unrealistic ask, offer a solution/compromise (cut scope, change approach),
which earns respect and helps you rise. His own salespeople now say no to discount requests
without even explaining, holding firm that a lower price means "we're not the right company for
you."

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The moment you keep saying yes and yes and yes to people, they realize like, oh, I can keep asking for the world and you're gonna give it. Your margins are gonna go down, you're gonna be miserable."

> "It's like dating. The moment you go after someone and they keep saying yes to everything, you're gonna get over them ... the moment something plays hard-to-get, you're like, oh my god, I'm in love. That's how it works in sales."

> "No, I can't give you a discount, I wish I could, but my margins are razor-thin, and if you want a discount ... I'm gonna have to give you less services, which means you're not gonna get the results that you're expecting."

> "This is what it's gonna take to give you the results. If you want something that's less, we're not the right company for you. The moment you put your foot down, that's when people will start respecting you."
