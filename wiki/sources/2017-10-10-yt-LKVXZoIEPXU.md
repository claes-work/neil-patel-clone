# How to Make Your Website More Linkable (4 Ways to Earn More Backlinks)

## Summary
Neil argues that one of the best ways to grow search rankings is to earn more links, but a website that isn't inherently linkable won't attract any — nobody links to product pages or contact pages. He gives four tactics to make a site linkable. First, add a blog, because people love linking to content and information, especially content that educates, is humorous, or otherwise entertains. Second, for B2B companies, publish case studies: the featured company will usually link back to and promote the study, so B2B firms should approach all their customers to create them. Third, create detailed, high-value guides and content — ebooks, white papers, advanced guides — so valuable that readers feel they'd have paid $50 for it on Amazon but got it free, which drives lots of links. Fourth, create infographics, which he says have fallen out of favor because people got lazy, yet still go viral and attract links more than any other content type; he points to Dribbble (dribbble.com) to find infographic designers.

- Case studies work extremely well for B2B companies because the featured company usually links back to and promotes the study; B2B firms should hit up all their customers to create case studies (as of 2017-10-10).
