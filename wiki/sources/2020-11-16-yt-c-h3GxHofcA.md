## Key claims
- As of 2020-11-16, cites (self-reported/aggregator statistics, not independently
  verified here): 46% of Google searches are local; the Google local 3-pack appears in
  93% of local-intent searches; 18% of mobile local searches convert to a sale within a
  day; 78% of local mobile searches lead to an offline purchase; 71% of people confirm
  a business's location before a first visit; ~89% of people search for a local
  business on mobile at least weekly (58% daily); over 35% of clicks go to the #1
  organic position.
- As of 2020-11-16, his local-SEO ranking-signal taxonomy: on-page (NAP consistency,
  title keywords, page authority), off-page (citations, aggregators, backlinks),
  review signals (volume, recency, depth, star rating), link signals, and behavior
  signals (CTR, pogo-sticking/quick-back-button as a negative signal) — paraphrase,
  consistent with and extending the corpus's existing local-SEO cluster.
- States a Google My Business listing alone does not guarantee top local rankings —
  "you need to do quite a bit more than that" (paraphrase).
- Quotes Jimmy Wales: "If it isn't on Google, it doesn't exist" — attributed
  third-party quote, not Neil's own words.
