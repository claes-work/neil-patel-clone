# Digital Marketing Strategies That Just Work

## Summary
A five-strategy starter playbook for beginners. (1) Email list-building first — visitors
rarely buy from an unfamiliar brand on a first visit, so collect emails (via Hello Bar)
with newsletters/coupons/ebooks, then nurture with a mix of pitches and educational
content. (2) Dedicated landing pages per product/offer (via Unbounce) instead of
funneling visitors to a menu of options, using a "candy store vs. grocery-store
checkout" analogy for choice overload. (3) Upsells/downsells (via SamCart/ClickFunnels),
citing McDonald's and Amazon as models — cheaper to monetize existing customers further
than acquire new ones. (4) Podcasting, framed as under-competed (citing "roughly 2
million podcasts" per Earth Web) — self-cites *Marketing School* at "roughly 1 million
listens a month," plus third-party podcast-listenership stats (Podcast Insights,
Convince & Convert, "Neil Shafer"). (5) Paid ads across Google/Facebook/Pinterest/
Twitter/LinkedIn/Snapchat with a small ($50–100/channel) test budget, then double down
on winners.

## Key claims
- States *Marketing School* gets "roughly 1 million listens a month" (as of 2022-10-01,
  self-reported).
- Cites third-party stats: ~2 million podcasts total (Earth Web); 51% of Americans /37%
  monthly listen to podcasts (Podcast Insights); 25% of 55+ consumers listen monthly
  (attributed to "Neil Shafer" — likely a transcript mis-hearing of a cited analyst,
  unresolved); average listener spends 6h39m/week on podcasts (Convince & Convert).
  These are third-party citations, not self-reported.
- Recommends a small paid-ad test budget of "$50 to $100 per channel" across six
  platforms as a beginner starting point (as of 2022-10-01, tactical advice, not a
  personal-performance claim).
