# You NEED to Learn How to Write This Type of Content…. RIGHT NOW!

## Summary
Advocates "long-form, data-oriented" content specifically (not long-form content in
general) as the highest-value content type for link acquisition. Cites a Backlinko
study putting the average first-page-ranking Google page at 1,447 words, while
cautioning length must fit the topic. Discloses first-party neilpatel.com data: long-
form content containing stats/data generates roughly 3x more backlinks than content
without data — but clarifies this data-heavy, link-generating content does not
necessarily capture high search *volume* (which skews toward simple queries like "what
is SEO"); the two content types serve different goals (links vs. volume). Gives a
production process: research competitor top pages and their linkers via Ubersuggest,
build a maximally thorough topic outline ("think Wikipedia"), source stats via Google
Scholar or paid researchers (Upwork/Fiverr) if needed, and link out to sources.
Connects data-driven content explicitly to Google's E-A-T (Expertise, Authority, Trust)
framework, including explicit self-deprecating example of topics he would NOT write on
(e.g. cancer, as a non-doctor).

## Key claims
- First-party data disclosure: long-form content with stats/data generates ~3x more
