# How to Increase Your Search Traffic by 58% in 30 Days

## Summary
A single-tactic case study: Neil attributes a ~58% month-over-month jump in Google search traffic on neilpatel.com to **removing dates from his URL structure** (from `/month/year/title` to `/blog/title`). His reasoning: Google reads a URL as a hierarchy, so a date-first permalink signals the page is tied to that date and looks stale a year later; a clean `blog/title` structure decouples the post from a date. He stresses the change is not just a settings flip — you must 301-redirect all old URLs to the new ones and update internal links. A recurring Neil theme lands at the end: this one structural change beat years of link building.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-17, Neil states:
- neilpatel.com went from 283,000 Google visitors in December 2016 to 449,000 in January 2017 — roughly a 58% increase — from one change, not new links and not a Google algorithm change (as of 2017-07-17). [Self-reported analytics figures.]
- The old URL structure was `neilpatel.com/month/year/title`; Google treats the URL as a hierarchy and correlates the page with the date, which makes evergreen posts look date-bound (as of 2017-07-17).
- The fix: move blog posts to `neilpatel.com/blog/title`, so Google stops relating them to a specific date (as of 2017-07-17).
- Migration requirements: (1) you can't just change the structure — 301-redirect every old URL to its new URL; (2) update all internal links from the old dated form to the new form (as of 2017-07-17).
- Recommended architecture: all blog posts under a `/blog/` folder; other sections under a category folder that describes them (e.g. `domain.com/flights/page-title`), with the folder above the page reflecting its category (as of 2017-07-17).
- Reader expectation-setting: you may not get 58% like he did, but you should see an increase (as of 2017-07-17).
- Reflection: everyone told him "more links = better," and he kept building links for years, but this one structural change increased his search traffic more than anything else (as of 2017-07-17).
