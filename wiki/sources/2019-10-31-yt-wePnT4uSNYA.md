# 9 Amazing Google Chrome Extensions That'll Improve Your Traffic

## Summary
In this solo listicle video, Neil Patel walks through nine Google Chrome extensions he says most marketers underuse for marketing and traffic generation. His picks span competitive SEO research (SEOquake, MozBar), outreach and link building (BuzzStream BuzzMarker, Check My Links), video optimization (TubeBuddy), social/personal branding (Buffer), writing quality (Grammarly), content research (BuzzSumo), and productivity/content curation (Save to Pocket). For each extension he explains the concrete workflow use case — e.g., using BuzzStream to surface editor/reporter contact details for guest posting, TubeBuddy to pull tags directly inside YouTube instead of switching back to a keyword tool, Buffer to one-click share content to all social profiles while browsing, and Check My Links to find broken-link opportunities to pitch a replacement link. He notes he uses the free version of Grammarly (which he calls his favorite Chrome extension overall) and that it catches errors Microsoft Word misses. He closes by plugging his ad agency, Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil said most marketers don't use Chrome extensions, and he is surprised how few of his friends and colleagues take advantage of ones that can help with marketing and traffic generation.
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil recommended **SEOquake** for understanding what competitors rank for in organic search, what paid ads they run, and which keywords they target; it has a SERP popover showing domain- and page-level metrics for ranking results.
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil recommended **MozBar** for link/date analysis, domain authority, and a page analysis feature reviewing a page's title, description, and schema.org markup, plus link metrics (page authority, domain authority, and number of linking root domains).
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil recommended **BuzzStream BuzzMarker** for outreach, networking, guest posting, and link building — it crawls a visited site and pulls contact details for associated people (editors, reporters, content marketers); he uses it on news/heavy-content sites to find who to reach out to for links or guest posts.
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil recommended **TubeBuddy** for video marketing — it shows tags, view counts, and more on any YouTube video, and suggests tags and helps optimize metadata when you upload your own video, so you don't have to switch back to a keyword tool (he mentions his own free tool Ubersuggest as such a tool).
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil recommended **Buffer** for personal branding and audience growth — one click shares content you're reading to all your social profiles and schedules it into a promotional calendar without manual posting to each platform.
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil said **Grammarly** is his favorite Chrome extension overall; he uses the free version for marketing emails and blog posts, and says it catches spelling/grammar errors that Microsoft Word does not catch.
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil recommended **BuzzSumo** for content creators — it shows social engagement, social shares, unique page views, and backlinks for any page (e.g., a competitor's), helping target what content to produce in real time.
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil recommended **Save to Pocket** to reduce distraction while researching and to save shareable content, noting that dozens of open tabs hurt productivity.
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil recommended **Check My Links** as essential for link building — it highlights working vs. broken links on any page, and broken links pointing to a competitor are one of the easiest ways to convince someone to link to you instead.
- As of 2019-10-31, Neil said broken links are "one of the easiest and simplest ways to convince people to link to you."
