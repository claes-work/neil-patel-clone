# Why AI Won't Kill Jobs (Marketing School)

## Summary
Co-hosted Marketing School episode reacting to an Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) piece arguing the "AI job apocalypse" narrative is a fantasy, walking through four a16z "narrative violation" charts: software-engineer demand rising, software devs as a growing share of new US jobs, above-trend wage growth in AI-exposed industries (computer systems design), and product-manager openings up 79% off their 2023 low. Eric Siu drives the chart presentation and supplies the macro/labor and startup-hiring framing (AI-pilled 100x/1000x engineers, token maxing, Coinbase/Brian Armstrong one-person teams, a PM candidate from Uber). Neil Patel supplies the marketing/agency-side reality: from a survey/study and a 300-person lecture in Spain, marketing departments are NOT shrinking, AI is causing people to work MORE (new tasks, not reduced headcount/hours), and total marketing spend is rising because fewer people do more AND organizations now pay for large volumes of AI tokens. Second half debates the GameStop-CEO-buys-eBay stunt as a lens on organizational bloat and spend-to-grow discipline.

Attribution split is roughly half/half. Eric is the presenter (shows charts, shares screen, addresses "Neil"); Neil is the responder and owns the marketing-survey and marketing-spend material. ★ L3-candidate: Neil's paired positions here — (1) "AI raises marketing spend (via tokens + more output) rather than cutting marketing headcount, and it makes people work more, not less" and (2) "you should not spend to grow at all cost; branding spend is fine only with a real payback" — are durable, reusable Neil positions worth promoting later. Do NOT inline-promote.

## Key claims (dated, speaker-tagged, paraphrase)
- The a16z data shows companies need MORE engineers because they want to keep using Claude and similar tools to run more efficiently and grow faster — rising demand, not job destruction. (Neil, 2026-05-11) — ai-marketing.
- In marketing (not just software), AI has NOT driven layoffs; observed AI usage in marketing is increasing while headcount holds. (Neil, 2026-05-11) — ai-marketing / agency-entrepreneurship.
- At a ~300-person university lecture in Spain (mostly employees of global publicly-traded brands), ~60% said they use Claude; almost none reported their marketing department getting smaller, and the few that did cited business performance, not AI. (Neil, 2026-05-11) — ai-marketing.
- The #1 thing marketers report AI has done is make them work MORE — it adds new tasks/new work even as it makes existing work more efficient; it has not reduced headcount or hours. (Neil, 2026-05-11) — ai-marketing.
- From NP Digital's own (unreleased) survey/study: adoption of real agentic AI in marketing (Claude Co-worker, Claude Code — not just ChatGPT) has drastically increased; organizations are not firing and are spending MORE on marketing in total, because a smaller number of people produce more AND the org pays for a lot of AI tokens. (Neil, 2026-05-11) — ai-marketing.
- Prediction: AI will not cause a big cut in marketing human capital; it will cause a big INCREASE in marketing spend driven by token costs. (Neil, 2026-05-11) — ai-marketing / agency-entrepreneurship.
- Most large organizations are bloated and do not need as many people; the staff needed to MAINTAIN a business is far smaller than the staff hired to GROW it — if a company isn't growing, the growth headcount isn't justified (examples: Coinbase, Block/Jack Dorsey ~40% cut). (Neil, 2026-05-11) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- eBay spending ~$2.5B/yr on sales & marketing to add ~1M users is not worth it; better to spend less, accept a lower growth rate, and run it as a cash cow. (Neil, 2026-05-11) — agency-entrepreneurship / ai-marketing.
- You should NOT spend to grow at all cost — it's a terrible model; branding spend is acceptable only if you genuinely believe it will pay back (long payback is fine), but foolish year-after-year spend, especially in an old company like eBay, is crazy. (Neil, 2026-05-11) — ai-marketing / agency-entrepreneurship.
- Technology has always created more abundance; every "this time it's different" claim ignores prior shifts (e.g., agriculture from ~90% of workers down to ~2%) — technology keeps making lives better. (Neil, 2026-05-11) — general/context.
- [Eric] Demand is rising for AI-pilled TALENT broadly, not just software engineers, because AI-pilled people can do far more; "10x engineers" is now 100x/1000x engineers. (Eric, 2026-05-11) — context, not persona.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Shows that people need more engineers because they want to keep using Claude and all these other solutions to make their companies more efficient, better, and grow faster.

> You want to know what the number one thing everyone said that it's caused with humans and workers? [...] It's caused them to have to work more. So, they've all found that AI has not reduced head count. It did not reduce the amount of hours they're working. It's actually causing more work.

> Not only are organizations consuming AI, at least in marketing, they're not really firing. And they're actually spending more money in marketing as an organization in total because [AI] people are getting more done and they're paying for a lot of AI tokens.

> You shouldn't spend to grow at all cost. That is a very terrible model. Yes, you can spend on things like branding if you genuinely believe they're going to have a payback... But spending foolishly year after year after year, especially in an older company like eBay... that's crazy.
