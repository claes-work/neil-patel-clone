# How to Hire an SEO Expert to Drive More Search Traffic to Your Website

## Summary
An operational playbook for hiring an SEO. Neil's throughline is proof over claims: hire people with a demonstrable track record, verify their results with references/case studies, and stress-test their skill on a live interview rather than a take-home. He gives sourcing channels (Google searches by city, LinkedIn, and job boards Indeed/AngelList/Craigslist), a specific live-interview test (name five SEO fixes for your site on the spot), and a paid trial with a measurable success signal (rising impression counts in Google Search Console, which lead clicks).

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-16, Neil states:
- Hire for a proven track record: knowing about SEO ≠ being good at it; if a candidate doesn't rank on the web, they're probably not great at SEO (as of 2017-07-16).
- Sourcing: Google "SEO company/consultant [+ your city]," search LinkedIn for SEO consultants (including ones working for companies that are doing well, whom you can try to poach), and post job listings on Indeed, AngelList, and Craigslist (as of 2017-07-16).
- Verify claims: ask what rankings they delivered for others and require references, testimonials, or case studies — candidates can make results up (as of 2017-07-16).
- Don't hire full-time immediately; test them first. Do it live over the phone, not as a homework assignment: ask them to list five SEO changes they'd recommend for your site (as of 2017-07-16).
- A genuinely good, experienced SEO can look at a site's source code and tell you within minutes what's wrong; putting them on the spot in a live interview prevents them from researching answers they don't actually know (as of 2017-07-16).
- Final step: trial them on a small, low-competition page or key phrase for 30–60 days; measure success via Google Search Console impression counts (impressions rise before clicks as rankings improve) (as of 2017-07-16).
- His route takes more time and effort, but candidates vetted this way tend to work out for the long haul; trusting-and-hiring blind risks paying a salary for no results (as of 2017-07-16).

## Notable verbatim quotes

A really good experienced SEO should just be able to look at a source code ... and just tell you within a few minutes what's wrong."
