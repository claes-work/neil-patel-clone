# 7 Marketing Lessons Learned From Billion-Dollar Companies You've Never Heard Of

## Summary
Using little-known billion-dollar-revenue companies (Danaher ~$29B/yr, Fortive ~$7B/yr, Ceridian
~$1B+/yr, contrasted with ByteDance's ~$58B/yr) as examples, Neil Patel lays out seven marketing
lessons: (1) multi-product companies are easier to market at scale than single-product ones (e.g.,
Apple's product range); (2) target large existing Total Addressable Markets (TAM) — citing Pew
Research that 97% of Americans have a mobile phone, versus the narrower buyer pool for a tool like
his own Ubersuggest; (3) word-of-mouth beats paid spend, illustrated by Statista figures that Ford
spent $4.1B on global ads versus Apple's $1.8B, yet Apple is "46 times roughly bigger" — attributed
to product love, not ad spend; (4) "ugly is sexy" — unglamorous, necessary categories (payroll,
water treatment) are large and less competitive; (5) go global rather than single-country, for a
larger addressable audience; (6) marketing is a team sport — his hiring heuristic is to look on
LinkedIn for people who worked at a competitor and were promoted multiple times, on the theory that
repeated promotion signals a company already validated their value; (7) brand-building requires
patience and ~10+ years, illustrated by branded search volume for Danaher outpacing "water
treatment" roughly 3-to-1.

## Key claims
- Cites Danaher (~$29B/yr), Fortive (~$7B/yr), and Ceridian (~$1B+/yr) as large, low-awareness
  companies, contrasted with ByteDance's ~$58B/yr, to argue brand fame and revenue scale are not
  correlated (as of 2022-07-28, third-party revenue figures presented without independent
  verification in this source).
- Lesson: multi-product portfolios are easier to market and monetize than single-product lines,
  citing Apple's product range as the familiar example (as of 2022-07-28).
- Lesson: prioritize markets with large TAM; cites Pew Research's 97%-of-Americans-have-a-mobile-phone
  figure versus the comparatively narrow buyer pool for an SEO tool like his own Ubersuggest (as of
  2022-07-28).
- Lesson: word-of-mouth is the most powerful and cost-efficient growth channel; cites Statista data
  that Ford's $4.1B global ad spend vs. Apple's $1.8B correlates with Apple being "46 times roughly
  bigger," attributed to product-driven evangelism rather than ad spend (as of 2022-07-28,
  third-party-sourced comparison, causal claim is Neil's own interpretation).
- Lesson ("ugly is sexy"): unglamorous necessity categories (payroll software, water treatment) are
  large, less-competitive markets, easier to market despite lacking glamour (as of 2022-07-28).
- Lesson: go global rather than single-market, to maximize addressable audience for marketing spend
  (as of 2022-07-28).
- Hiring heuristic: look for candidates on LinkedIn who worked at a competitor and were promoted
  multiple times, on the premise repeated promotion signals validated ability (as of 2022-07-28).
- Lesson: brand-building requires patience over 10+ years; cites Danaher generating roughly 3x more
  branded search volume than the generic term "water treatment" as evidence of accumulated brand
  equity (as of 2022-07-28).
