# SEO Beyond Google: How to Rank On The Best Alternative Search Engine | YouTube SEO

## Summary
In this solo talking-head video, Neil Patel makes the case that YouTube — not Bing — is the best alternative search engine, claiming he pulls 100,000–200,000+ visitors a month from it, and that YouTube SEO works very differently from Google (you can rank for a competitive term like "SEO" within 24–48 hours). His ranking tactics: (1) push the video hard in the first 24 hours (push notifications via subscribers.com, email blasts), since early performance largely determines long-term ranking; (2) only produce content people will love, because a month of disliked content lowers your overall channel score and suppresses browse/suggested distribution; (3) upload a manual SRT caption file (from rev.com) rather than relying on YouTube's auto-captions, which tells YouTube what the video is about and lifts rankings; (4) upload SRT files in multiple languages to grab easy, less-competitive overseas traffic; (5) use Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas "related" tab to find long-tail variations for your title and description; (6) write long, valuable descriptions to rank for more keywords; and (7) actually say the target keyword repeatedly in the video (keyword density), since YouTube deciphers spoken audio, not just the SRT. He closes plugging Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-11-28, Neil said YouTube — not Bing — is the best alternative search engine, and he consistently gets over 100,000 visitors a month (some months over 200,000) from it.
- As of 2019-11-28, Neil said YouTube SEO is totally different from Google: whereas Google takes a long time to decide page-one rankings, on YouTube you can rank for a competitive keyword like "SEO" within 24 to 48 hours at the top.
- As of 2019-11-28, Neil said the big component of YouTube's algorithm is first-24-hour performance — the better the video does in its first 24 hours, the better and faster it ranks long-term — so he pushes new videos via push notifications (subscribers.com) and email blasts.
- As of 2019-11-28, Neil said to only produce content people will love; a month of content people hate lowers your overall channel score and drags down rankings and browse/suggested distribution, so you're better off not releasing a piece you think will be bad.
- As of 2019-11-28, Neil said to upload a manual SRT caption file (e.g. from rev.com) instead of relying on YouTube's machine auto-captions, because it tells YouTube what the video is really about and you'll rank higher.
- As of 2019-11-28, Neil said to also upload SRT files in different languages, because YouTube is less competitive overseas, giving easy, quick traffic where rankings rise over time.
- As of 2019-11-28, Neil said to use Ubersuggest — enter your target keyword, open the keyword-ideas report, use the "related" tab for similar/long-tail keywords — and include those in your title and description.
- As of 2019-11-28, Neil said video descriptions shouldn't be a single paragraph; longer, valuable descriptions rank for more keywords, and keywords can also go in tags.
- As of 2019-11-28, Neil said to say your target keyword repeatedly within the video itself (keyword density), because YouTube tries to decipher spoken audio on its own, not just read the SRT — saying the right keywords multiple times signals the topic and lifts rankings.
