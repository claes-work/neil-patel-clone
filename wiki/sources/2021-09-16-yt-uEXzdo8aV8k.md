# How to Write Amazing Blog Posts WITHOUT Being an Expert in Your Niche

## Summary
A "copy and paste" blog-writing process for non-expert writers: (1) find a problem the
audience needs solved via Quora/Reddit/Ubersuggest's Questions/Prepositions/
Comparisons reports; (2) keyword research in Ubersuggest to decide standalone-post vs.
section-of-a-comprehensive-post; (3) competitor analysis (length, subheadings,
keyword usage, unanswered gaps); (4) pull supporting statistics (Statista, Google
Scholar, "keyword + statistics" search); (5) outline — headline variations (citing the
Copyblogger headline formula and an 80%-read/20%-click-through headline stat), keyword-
bearing subheadings, and a conclusion that **ends with a question** to drive comment
engagement. Bonus tip: uses the AI writing tool **Jarvis** (predecessor name of
Jasper) to speed up outlining/drafting by "30 minutes to an hour," explicitly not as a
full-post replacement ★ — an early, dated data point on his AI-in-content-workflow
stance, useful context for the ai-marketing domain's belief-evolution timeline.

## Key claims
- Uses the AI writing tool Jarvis for partial drafting/outlining assistance, framed as
  a time-saver rather than a ghostwriter — "it's not perfect... it won't write your
  whole blog post for you" (paraphrase + partial quote, dated 2021-09) — ai-marketing.
  New, dated data point: earliest AI-content-tool usage claim currently in the wiki
  (prior AI-marketing entries were forecasts/opinions from 2018, not tool usage).
- Recommends ending blog-post conclusions with a question to increase comments/
  engagement, calling it "a very effective trick" (paraphrase, dated 2021-09) —
  content-marketing.
- Cites an 80%/20% headline stat: roughly eight in ten people read a headline, but only
  two in ten click through (paraphrase, dated 2021-09) — content-marketing.
