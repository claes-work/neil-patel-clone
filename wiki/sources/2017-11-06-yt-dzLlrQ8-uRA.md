# Are You Getting Social Mentions? How to Find Out When Someone is Talking About You on Social Media

## Summary
Neil Patel explains how to monitor and respond to online mentions of your brand or name. He recommends three tools: SocialMention.com (free but not the best), Mention.com (paid, better, alerts you in near-real-time before others notice), and Google Alerts (free, which he has used for ages) — and says he uses a combination of all three to stay ahead. Beyond monitoring, he covers response strategy: when someone praises you, don't leave it there — comment, thank them, email them, and if they have a big audience give them free products/services to convert one-time reviewers into recurring brand evangelists (his example: Beats by Dre using celebrities and athletes). When someone criticizes you, if they have a small following you can ignore it, but he prefers to "rebuttal" graciously — thank them, take the feedback to heart, and show you're kind rather than fighting fire with fire — which converts haters into lovers and elicits transparent, useful feedback.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-06, Neil states:
- SocialMention.com is a free tool that tells you who's talking about you online, letting you decide whether to react (as of 2017-11-06).
- Mention.com is a paid tool that is better than SocialMention and alerts you in near-real-time — usually before everyone else knows something is happening about you or your business (as of 2017-11-06).
- When news breaks about you or your business it typically takes at least a few hours before others know; Mention.com notifies you right away so you can get ahead of it (as of 2017-11-06).
- Google Alerts is a free tool he has used "for ages" that alerts you every time someone mentions your brand or name, and is really effective (as of 2017-11-06).
- He personally uses a combination of all three tools to stay ahead of mentions (as of 2017-11-06).
- Positive-mention response: don't just leave praise there — comment, say thanks, email the person, and get to know them (as of 2017-11-06).
- For praise from someone with a big audience/influence, give them your product or service for free so they're more likely to keep promoting you — converting a one-time reviewer into a brand evangelist (as of 2017-11-06).
- Brand evangelism example: Beats by Dre's evangelists are essentially every celebrity, musician, and athlete wearing the headphones (as of 2017-11-06).
- Negative-mention response: if a critic has a small following you can leave it alone; otherwise he "rebuttals" — thanking them, appreciating the feedback, and promising to improve — to avoid fighting fire with fire (as of 2017-11-06).
- Responding transparently and kindly, and actually improving on valid criticism, converts haters into lovers and elicits more transparent, useful feedback (as of 2017-11-06).
