# How to Rank Your WordPress Website on Google

## Summary
A two-part WordPress optimization playbook: site performance, then SEO plugins.
States his own blog (WordPress-powered) attracts roughly 2.2 million visitors/month
from Google search alone, and that Ubersuggest gets even more traffic than that,
framing site-speed discipline as something he takes as seriously as SEO itself
because Google rewards low-friction user experience. Performance recommendations:
choose a dedicated server or managed WordPress host (names DreamHost, WP Engine) over
shared hosting for meaningful load-time gains (claims 5-10 seconds faster); use
caching + a CDN (recommends the free W3 Total Cache plugin, which bundles cache/CDN/
lazy-loading); lazy-load images so pages don't front-load every asset; compress images
(names the free Smush.it plugin) without visible quality loss. Mentions considering
building his own free WordPress SEO + image-compression plugin if enough viewer
demand exists. SEO-plugin section: recommends ensuring any chosen plugin covers
sitemaps, on-page SEO fundamentals, image SEO, structured markup, and robots
controls/redirects, naming Rank Math, Yoast, and All in One SEO as his three
recommended options.
