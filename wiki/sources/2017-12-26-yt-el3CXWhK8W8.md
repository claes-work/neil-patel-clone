# YouTube Video SEO: How to Get Over 25,000 Subscribers in a Year — 5 Video Marketing Tips

## Summary

In this solo tip video, Neil Patel shares five tactics he says drove over 25,000 YouTube subscribers in less than a year: make content emotional and passionate, explicitly ask viewers to subscribe (both mid-video and at the end), target the right keywords via research tools, upload transcripts so YouTube ranks videos higher, and pitch other bloggers to embed your videos for external traffic. He closes by stressing that videos should be long (five-plus minutes) and engaging to rank well and earn suggestions.

## Key claims

- As of 2017-12-26, Neil claims he generated over 25,000 YouTube subscribers in less than a year and frames subscribers as the prerequisite for becoming popular on YouTube.
- He argues content must be emotional and passionate; flat, unemotional delivery makes viewers bounce and exit, so it won't perform.
- Neil advises explicitly telling viewers to subscribe both during and at the end of videos; he says adding an end-of-video subscribe ask (with an exit screen) that he lacked on earlier videos produced a large increase — roughly three times more subscribers.
- He recommends using the right keywords, researched via Google Trends, Google's keyword traffic estimator / keyword planner, and Ubersuggest, then placing those keywords in video titles and descriptions to get more views.
- Neil claims YouTube's auto-captions ("cc") are good but not 100% accurate at identifying content, so uploading a transcript is necessary for a video to rank higher and get more views (and therefore more subscribers).
- He recommends outreach to other bloggers writing similar content — found via BuzzSumo by searching related keywords — asking them to embed your video; the pitch angle is that some people are visual learners.
- He states that getting embeds drives external traffic, and more external traffic makes videos perform better and rank higher.
- Neil advises making videos long and engaging; he says very short videos (one to four minutes) get little "clout" from YouTube, hurting rankings and suggested-video placement.
- He recommends videos of five or six minutes-plus (not necessarily 30–40 minutes); anything shorter is hard to rank unless targeting non-competitive terms, which won't generate much traffic or subscribers.
- He mentions that if you lack time, YouTube consulting companies like Viewership can do the keyword and transcript work for you.
- He closes by noting that subscribing, following, commenting, liking, and sharing all help gain traction on any social network.
