# 4 Marketing Strategy Principles - My Template for Marketing Anything

## Summary

Neil frames four fundamentals that, if broken, doom any marketing effort. (1) **Clear goals** —
know whether you're optimizing for visitors, brand awareness, sales, or profitability, because
the goal dictates the tactics (brand awareness = eyeballs/traffic; sales = conversion funnel
with up/downsells; profitability = cutting marketing cost). (2) **Understand your target
customer** — illustrated with a **Diamond Foundry** story ★: the lab-grown-diamond maker can
produce gem-quality diamonds "for pennies on the dollar" and priced them cheaply to help
people; revenue **tanked** because customers equate affordable with low quality. When they
**raised prices**, sales grew fast. Lesson: you learn what makes customers buy by talking to
them (coffee, in person, SurveyMonkey), not from a Google Analytics screenshot. (3)
**Differentiation** — without it, buyers only compare price/quality/reviews; find what
competitors aren't doing (his example: Apple's seamless device ecosystem justifies the premium).
(4) **Focus one channel at a time** — even though marketing is going omnichannel, master one
channel before adding a second; pick the channel you're best at, and use **Ubersuggest** on
competitors to see which channels drive their traffic.
