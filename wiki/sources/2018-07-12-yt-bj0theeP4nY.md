# Stop Wasting Time Trying to Rank for Competitive Keywords - Do THIS Instead

## Summary

A Q&A Thursday episode (Neil with interviewer Adam) answering Laxmi, who wants to rank a new
site for head terms like "best mobile phone" / "best laptop" within 2-3 months. Neil says
flatly it won't happen: new sites have low domain authority (the 0-100 link-based score from
Moz/Majestic/Ahrefs; Google/Facebook near 100, most sites under 20-30), and head terms are
too competitive on that timeline. His prescription is to **go after long-tail, buyer-intent
keywords** — e.g. "best laptops for under a thousand dollars" — which have lower volume but
convert better because the searcher has a specific budget and intent to buy. He recommends
using Ubersuggest (which pulls Google Suggest variations sorted by popularity) to find these,
and targeting 3-4-word phrases that a new site can rank for within 1-6 months and that
generate sales. The long-game payoff: as those long-tail posts earn links over time, the site
eventually ranks for the broad head term too. He also introduces **content clusters** — a
main pillar article (e.g. "what is SEO") linking out to non-overlapping subtopic articles
(link building, on-page SEO, title-tag A/B testing, algorithm changes; or, for laptops:
under-$1k, portable, lightweight-for-travel, rugged) — as a structure for owning a topic.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-07-12, a new low-authority site cannot rank for highly competitive head terms
  ("best laptops," "best cell phones") within 2-3 or even 6 months.
- As of 2018-07-12, domain authority is a 0-100 score based on inbound links, computed by
  Moz, Majestic, and Ahrefs; Google/Facebook sit near 100, most sites under 20-30.
- As of 2018-07-12, the fix is targeting long-tail, buyer-intent keywords (e.g. "best laptops
  for under a thousand dollars") — lower volume but higher purchase intent and rankable in
  1-6 months.
- As of 2018-07-12, Ubersuggest surfaces long-tail variations (via Google Suggest) sorted by
  popularity; target 3-4-word phrases.
- As of 2018-07-12, long-tail rankings accrue links over time and eventually lift the site
  for the broad head term ("it's a long tail").
- As of 2018-07-12, content clusters = a pillar article linking to non-overlapping subtopic
  articles, built to own a topic area.
