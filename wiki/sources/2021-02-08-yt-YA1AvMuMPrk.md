# A Dead Simple SEO Strategy That'll Generate 1 Million Visitors

## Summary
Standalone tactical video pitching **question-based content as a low-competition SEO
strategy**, demoed entirely inside Ubersuggest. Opens with a Quora traffic case study
(pulled from Ubersuggest's traffic-estimate tool): Quora ranks for an estimated 27.7
million keywords in positions 51–100 and 4.8 million keywords in positions 1–3, and its
top pages are almost all question phrasings ("How many ounces in a gallon," "What is
Delta S in chemistry," etc.). Walks through the workflow: type a seed keyword into
Ubersuggest's Keyword Ideas report, filter to the "Questions" tab, and pick items with
low SEO Difficulty, decent volume, and high CPC (high CPC as a proxy for commercial
traffic value) — demoed live for "marketing," "digital marketing," "Instagram," and
"dog food." Argues question-keyword competition is consistently low regardless of
niche (typically ~100 question suggestions per seed term), and that the resulting
articles can monetize via email capture + funnel, affiliate links, AdSense, or direct
product sales — using a "how dog food is made" example leading into an affiliate
premium-dog-food pitch. Closes with a volume-math pitch: 100 pages each driving 1,000
visitors/month compounds to 1.2 million visitors/month.

## Key claims
- Cites Ubersuggest's estimate that Quora ranks for ~27.7 million keywords in
  positions 51–100 and ~4.8 million keywords in positions 1–3, and that its top
  traffic-driving pages are overwhelmingly question-phrased (paraphrase, tool-estimated
  data, not independently re-verified).
- States that question-phrased keywords are consistently lower-SEO-difficulty than
  their non-question counterparts "on anything" — demoed across four unrelated niches
  in one video (paraphrase, previously-unlogged generalized claim; no independent
  verification given beyond the live demo).
- States a rule of thumb that a seed keyword in Ubersuggest's Questions filter
  typically surfaces "around a hundred" question suggestions to work through
  (paraphrase, self-reported tool-usage heuristic).
- Frames high CPC (not just high volume) as the signal to prioritize among low-
  difficulty question keywords, on the logic that high-CPC traffic is more likely to
  convert even from a non-commercial question article via remarketing/email capture
  (paraphrase).
