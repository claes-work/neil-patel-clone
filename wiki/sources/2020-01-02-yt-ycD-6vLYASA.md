# A Detailed, Newbie's Guide to Building Your Brand in 2020 ★

## Summary
Solo personal-branding playbook opening on his central thesis: **brand is the best moat in
business** — anyone can copy your features, product, services and marketing, but not your
brand. Seven tactics. (1) **Be an active, genuinely helpful member of targeted communities**
— comment on blogs, participate in niche Facebook groups, answer on Quora, Reddit and Yahoo
Answers; the "secret" is depth, because one-sentence answers are transparently self-serving,
and the pro move is turning the questions you answer into in-depth blog content (find them
in Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas → Questions tab). He cites a student, **Gerard**, whose skin-
care site "Skin Care by Elena" got 931 visitors and 12 sales in its first week off this.
(2) **Guest on other people's podcasts** — a podcast tour is a book tour you can run from
your living room; find shows via iTunes or by Googling `<industry> podcast`, and use the
already-interviewed-guest trick (search "Tony Robbins podcast interview" to find every show
that books coaches). Outreach pro tip: pitch **only three people first with three different
topics**, learn which topic resonates, then scale — otherwise you burn 20 podcasters with a
dud topic and they ignore your second email. (3) **Start your own podcast and interview
guests** — association with influencers boosts credibility; the key is asking questions the
guest hasn't answered elsewhere (listen to their prior appearances first) so the episode is
worth them sharing. (4) **Co-publish content** — he name-checks "The Advanced Guide to SEO"
with his cousin **Sujan Patel**, a personal-branding guide with **Aaron Agius**, his podcast
with co-host **Eric Siu**, and webinars with Optimizely and WordStream (**Larry Kim**);
co-publishing builds both authorities, halves the workload and lets both sides promote to
their lists. (5) **Be very active on Pinterest** — one of his sites (a health site, not
neilpatel.com, where he is not the front-facing brand) pulls ~413,000 monthly visitors from
Pinterest; the first three months are the hard part (build graphics in Canva from templates,
mini-infographics, join and contribute to group/public boards), after which momentum is
cheap to maintain. (6) **Bring people back repeatedly — the rule of seven**: seven brand
exposures make people remember and engage. His three return channels are push notifications
(Subscribers.com, ~6,000–7,000 visitors per push), email blasts (collect with **Hello Bar**
pop-ups/exit pop-ups/quizzes/sliders, send via ConvertKit or free Mailchimp) and chatbots
(ManyChat/MobileMonkey via Facebook Messenger). (7) **Launch your own free tool** — Ubersuggest
is his own example ("I took Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz and gave a version away for free") and he
says brand queries jumped after launching it; the cheap version is buying a $10–40 niche tool
on CodeCanyon and giving it away free.
