# Persuasion Techniques to Make More Money Online (Conversion...)

## Summary

In this solo explainer, Neil Patel walks through how to get started with conversion rate optimization (CRO) when a site has traffic but few buyers. He argues that before touching analytics funnels or running tests, marketers must first understand *why* visitors leave — and that answer comes from qualitative data rather than Google Analytics alone. His sequence: collect on-page survey feedback (via Qualaroo or SurveyMonkey), wait until you have 30+ responses before drawing conclusions, mine those responses for common objections, then install a heat-map tool (Crazy Egg) to see where people click, scroll, and get stuck. Only once qualitative feedback and behavioral data point to concrete problems should you form hypotheses and run A/B tests (via Optimizely). The recurring theme is that CRO decisions must be driven by data, not guesswork.

## Key claims

- Conversion rate optimization is the solution when a website has traffic but visitors aren't buying (as of 2017-06-22).
- Before starting CRO, you must know what to fix, change, or test on the page to maximize conversions and sales — you can't just start testing blindly (as of 2017-06-22).
- ★ The recommended starting point is qualitative data — feedback from individuals — because Google Analytics only shows so much and won't tell you *why* people aren't buying (as of 2017-06-22).
- Qualitative feedback can be gathered with a pop-up survey using Qualaroo or SurveyMonkey; Neil recommends starting with Qualaroo (as of 2017-06-22).
- A practical survey trigger: ask visitors a question once they've been on a page for more than roughly 30–40 seconds or a minute (as of 2017-06-22).
- Don't make decisions off one or two responses; wait until you have 30+ survey responses and analyze them in quantity to find common objections (as of 2017-06-22).
- Example objection handling: if e-commerce visitors say they won't buy because there's no free shipping, test free shipping — and if you can't afford it, raise the product price and bundle free shipping to see if conversions improve (as of 2017-06-22).
- ★ The second step is installing Crazy Egg, a heat-map analytics tool that visualizes engagement — mouse recordings of where people go, heat maps of what they click, and how far they scroll (as of 2017-06-22).
- Heat maps reveal problems like visitors clicking dead links or non-clickable images, or not scrolling far enough — so calls to action placed at the very bottom of a page get missed and produce no sales (as of 2017-06-22).
- Combine the qualitative survey data with the Crazy Egg behavioral data to identify what issues people have and generate ideas on what to test (as of 2017-06-22).
- Once you have test ideas, use a tool like Optimizely to run A/B tests (as of 2017-06-22).
- ★ The core principle: run tests based on data — that is how you get started with conversion rate optimization (as of 2017-06-22).
