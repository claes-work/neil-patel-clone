# How to Find Unlimited Content Ideas for Social Media in 2023

## Summary
A rundown of where to source social-media content ideas without a creative team,
framed around not relying solely on one's own creativity. Self-reports his own
cross-platform posting cadence as a backdrop, then lists idea sources: customer
questions, comments on existing content (repeated concerns = a topic), Quora,
Facebook groups (pain points/complaints), competitor FAQ pages, keyword-research
tools (Ahrefs, Semrush, Ubersuggest — including its Chrome extension for YouTube),
Google Alerts, Google Trends, Feedly (blog subscriptions), BuzzSumo, and
Ubersuggest's content-ideas report (ranked by social shares, backlinks, and SEO
traffic together).

## Key claims
- Self-reports a quantified personal/team cross-platform posting cadence: ~12
  YouTube videos/month, ~5 Instagram posts/week (stories not counted), ~5
  TikTok posts/week, roughly daily on Facebook (including promoted blog content),
  ~5 LinkedIn posts/week, ~2 Twitter posts/week, and ~1 podcast episode/day
  (~30/month) — summed to "130 posts across all my social channels" (self-reported,
  dated 2022-04-07) — social-media, content-marketing.
- States he has a team handling content creation and scheduling, but frames the
  video's tactics as usable single-handedly, as he did when he started (self-reported,
  dated 2022-04-07) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Recommends mining customer questions, comment threads, Quora, Facebook groups,
  and competitor FAQ pages as free, unlimited content-idea sources (self-reported
  tactic, dated 2022-04-07) — content-marketing.
- Recommends the Ubersuggest Chrome extension specifically for surfacing related
  YouTube topics and gauging video traction while browsing (self-reported product
  promotion, dated 2022-04-07) — content-marketing, social-media.
