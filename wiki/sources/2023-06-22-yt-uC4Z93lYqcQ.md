# What You Can Learn From The Best Newsletter Businesses

## Summary
Short co-hosted segment on newsletter-business economics, using financial-newsletter publisher
**Agora** (divisions cited: Stansberry Research, Agora Financial, Money Map Press; founder named
as Bill Bonner) as the case study — states NP Digital has done "quite a bit" of Agora's marketing
over the years and that Agora generates $1B+/year in revenue from newsletter-adjacent products
(masterminds, $5-10K/year subscriptions, proprietary research, paid events), not raw newsletter-
subscription fees alone. Neil generalizes the lesson beyond newsletters into a funnel-economics
principle he says he learned specifically from working with Agora: get a prospect into the funnel
cheaply (even a $1-5 purchase or free email opt-in) because "buyers are buyers" — it's far easier
to sell an existing buyer something bigger later than to convert a new person to any purchase at
all, regardless of price point. Cites Amazon Prime as a mainstream example of a company
intentionally selling at a loss on entry offers because it knows the downstream repeat-purchase
value more than compensates. Also restates the "go after a big TAM" principle (echoing the
niche-vs-big-market self-correction in [[2023-06-19-yt-Hlx9rxe7OH8]]) as something he specifically
credits learning from Agora's model: bigger addressable markets make marketing/targeting easier
because you avoid ad-frequency/saturation problems.

## Key claims
- States NP Digital has handled marketing for Agora (a $1B+/year financial-newsletter publisher)
  and cites it as the best newsletter business he's aware of (self-reported/client-data, dated
  2023-06-22).
- States the key lesson learned from Agora is the "tripwire" funnel principle: get buyers in
  cheaply (even $1-5), because it's much easier to upsell an existing buyer than to convert a new
  prospect at any price (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-06-22).
- Cites Amazon Prime as an example of a company deliberately losing money on an entry offer,
  knowing downstream repeat-purchase revenue more than compensates (self-reported opinion, dated
  2023-06-22).

  the niche-vs-big-market self-correction dated four days earlier in
  [[2023-06-19-yt-Hlx9rxe7OH8]] (self-reported opinion, dated 2023-06-22).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> If you can get someone in to your funnel, your marketing funnel, for something really cheap,
> free... it's really easy to start selling them things that are a hundred, a thousand, ten
> thousand.

> Buyers are buyers, and it's really easy to get people to buy more once they buy once, no matter
> how low that product was, but it's hard to get a new buyer to buy something.

> As a marketer, the bigger market you go after, the easier it is to do marketing, because you can
> just, you know, spray and pray in essence, target a lot of people.
