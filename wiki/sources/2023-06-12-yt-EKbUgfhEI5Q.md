# Twitter Doubles Down on Creators

## Summary
Short co-hosted segment on Twitter's (under Elon Musk) creator-monetization experiments. Neil
frames Twitter's strategy as pure iteration — borrowing concepts from other platforms (names
Patreon-style creator payouts) and running many small experiments rather than committing to one
big bet, arguing this is the correct posture in business generally because "none of us know the
right answers," not something unique to Musk. Speculates the end-goal is transforming Twitter
into a WeChat-style "super app" that handles payments/transactions, not just content, and
contrasts pre-Musk Twitter (text/occasional-video, intellectual/informational content in his own
industry) with the platform's broader move to add community features. Separately references a
BBC interviewer's on-air exchange with Musk about hate speech on Twitter (interviewer couldn't
produce concrete examples when pressed) — Neil reads Musk's response as genuine curiosity to find
and fix real problems rather than dismissiveness, and cites Musk publicly acknowledging (in
response to a Mark Cuban complaint about reduced reach) that reach/algorithm issues were real and
being investigated, as evidence Musk is unusually responsive to factual (not just opinionated)
platform criticism. Contrasts this with platforms that clamp down organic reach specifically to
force ad spend, framing Musk's approach (protect organic reach for brands/creators, monetize
via new mechanisms instead) as differentiated.
