# How to Use Customer Surveys to Boost Your Conversions

## Summary
Four-step framework for using customer surveys to drive conversions. Cites a 2019
Forrester study: companies investing in customer-experience programs outperform those
that don't by nearly 80% (as cited, dated 2023-02-16). Step 1: define what you want to
learn (product/service gaps, churn reasons, competitive gaps) and use tools like
SurveyMonkey (site-wide) or Hotjar (page-specific, segmentable by region/page context).
Step 2: choose a sample size — pushes back on the common "30+ responses" guidance,
recommending 100+ and noting 200–300 gives materially better data. Step 3: ask in
real time, citing an American Express stat that customers spend 17% more with
companies offering good customer service, and arguing real-time questions during
support interactions get higher response rates. Step 4: implement findings — cites
a "Genesis" stat that 9 of 10 customers value companies that remember past
purchases/preferences, uses Amazon's personalized-recommendation behavior as an
example (self-reported: Neil never sees pet-food ads because he has no pets, but sees
household staples like toothpaste), and draws an analogy to McDonald's
upsell/downsell prompts and his daughter always wanting the Happy Meal, arguing survey
data enables upsells/downsells that increase revenue per customer.

## Key claims
- Cites a 2019 Forrester study: companies investing in customer experience
  outperform non-investors by nearly 80% (as cited, dated 2023-02-16).
- Recommends 100+ survey responses as a minimum (explicitly more than the "30+"
  commonly cited), with 200–300 giving markedly better data (self-reported
  practitioner view, dated 2023-02-16).
- Cites American Express: customers are likely to spend 17% more with companies that
  have good customer service (as cited, dated 2023-02-16).
- Cites a "Genesis" statistic: 9 of 10 customers value a company knowing their
  previous purchases/preferences (as cited, dated 2023-02-16).
