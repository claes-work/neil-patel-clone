# Content Promotion For eCommerce - Module 2 - Part 3 - eCommerce Unlocked

## Summary
eCommerce Unlocked lesson on promoting content once created. Opens with the "even
Facebook still has a growth team" framing (no company is too big to stop growing) and
a scale point: "roughly one blog for every seven people" on Earth, so unpromoted content
won't be seen. Promotion channels covered: sidebar links to cornerstone content (his
own tactic on neilpatel.com, also cites NerdWallet doing the same; claims 6-12 months
for linked pages to "dominate Google"); social platforms (Facebook/Twitter/Instagram),
citing American Eagle and A&E as brand examples; live video for direct selling (Skincare
by Alana again); his own personal practice of posting daily across Facebook, Twitter,
LinkedIn, Instagram to build relationships and cross-promotion. **Case study: Grace &
Lace** (a "Shark Tank" company) — Year 1 ~$800K in sales via Instagram/Pinterest
influencer marketing, Year 2 $1.6M, Year 3 $20M+ — used to argue for **micro-influencers
over one large influencer** ("that person's usually the most expensive and they usually
don't provide the best ROI... get 10 of those smaller ones... it makes up then more
than one of those big influencers"). Also covers: Instagram/LinkedIn carousel
("swipe") content, push notifications via Subscribers (~6% CTR), backlink acquisition
via Ubersuggest's Backlinks/Top Pages reports (targeting high-domain-score, "Follow"
not "No Follow" links), a scripted outreach-email template (identify a gap in a
competitor's linked article, pitch your superior coverage), and chatbots (ManyChat,
Intercom, Drift) — citing Facebook Messenger's 1.3B monthly active users against the
"no one cares for it" skeptics.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-07-04, Neil said sidebar-linked cornerstone content pages take roughly
  6-12 months to "dominate Google" — a specific timeframe claim for internal-linking
  SEO payoff.
- As of 2020-07-04, Neil's stated influencer-marketing preference is 10 micro-
  influencers over 1 large one, on cost/ROI grounds — illustrated with the **Grace &
  Lace** Shark-Tank case study ($800K → $1.6M → $20M+ across three years via
  Instagram/Pinterest influencer marketing).
- As of 2020-07-04, Neil said he personally posts content daily across Facebook,
  Twitter, LinkedIn, and Instagram "literally wherever I can to help drive more sales"
  — a direct, dated confirmation of his personal daily-posting habit (voice/behavior
  data).
- As of 2020-07-04, Neil pushed back on Facebook Messenger skeptics, citing its 1.3
  billion monthly active users as grounds to still invest in chatbot-based marketing
  (ManyChat/Intercom/Drift) — dated 2020-era Messenger-marketing advocacy, consistent
  with (and reinforcing) the wiki's existing ⚠️-flagged Messenger-boosterism note,
  which the 2025-26 material shows was later superseded as Messenger's marketing
  relevance faded.
