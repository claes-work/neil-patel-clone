# Personal Motivation: What Drives You To Succeed? | Business Advice from Neil Patel ★

_Neil interviewed (off-camera interviewer, likely Adam). All Neil-attributed = persona data._

## Summary
Asked what motivates him, Neil answers bluntly: **losing** — beating and "crushing"
competitors, not hitting a dollar goal. He lays out his 2018 strategy: **release paid
competitor features for free** to win audience/mindshare, then monetize later off the
top ~1% (enterprise/Fortune-1000). He is making Ubersuggest's paid features free (no
login, unlimited), and plans to rebuild ~20 of SEMrush's most-used features and ~20–30%
of Moz's features and give them away free too. His thesis: technology (APIs) has made
delivery so cheap that "whoever controls the most eyeballs/mindshare in the long run
wins" — a media-company logic applied to SaaS. Invokes Bezos's "your margin is my
opportunity" and undercutting on price (ideally to free) while making money on the high
end. ★ L3-candidate: this is a core, unusually candid statement of his freemium-to-free
+ mindshare-first business philosophy and competitive motivation.

## Key claims
As of 2018-03-31, Neil states:
- **His motivation is competitive**, not monetary: "beat my competitors… crush them."
  He'll knowingly **lose revenue to crush competitors**.
- He **acquired Ubersuggest** (a keyword tool, likened to keywordtool.io) and **made its
  paid features free** — no login, unlimited — despite ongoing monthly cost to him.
- Next targets: **SEMrush** (rebuild ~20 of its most-used features free) and **Moz**
  (~20–30% of features + other SEO tools he likes), released free.
- Rejects standard freemium: **"my freemium is gonna be free," upsell nothing** — win
  audience and mindshare first, monetize later off the top ~1% (Fortune-1000 / enterprise).
- **Cheap delivery via APIs**: cites Moz's ~$200K/month server cost (from their annual
  report) vs. his own ~**$120/month to crawl 500K web pages** using someone else's API
  for SEO suggestions — unlimited sites/domains.
- Applies **Bezos "your margin is my opportunity"**: undercut competitors on price, make
  it free if possible, earn on the high end; a free product needs less marketing spend
  (avoids expensive AdWords/Facebook costs) because it "markets itself."
- Frames the company like a **media company**: control the most eyeballs in an industry,
  then figure out monetization; one enterprise customer can cover all expenses plus more.

> ⚠️ Moz "$200K/month server cost" is Neil's paraphrase of Moz's annual report; the
> $120/month crawl figure is self-reported. Competitor "free feature" plans are stated
> intent as of 2018-03, not confirmed outcomes.
