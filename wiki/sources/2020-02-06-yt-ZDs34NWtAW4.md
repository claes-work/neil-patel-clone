# How to Win on SEO Without Spending Money - The Penniless Marketer Full Strategy

## Summary
Neil frames SEO as achievable with time instead of money, via an 8-step "penniless
marketer" strategy: (1) develop a keyword strategy in Ubersuggest targeting high-CPC,
higher-volume, sub-40-difficulty keywords that fit the target audience's demographics
(also citing Facebook Audience Insights); (2) understand search intent — navigational,
transactional, informational — and prioritize transactional/informational keywords for
sales; (3) build a content calendar, attacking less-competitive keywords first and
staying consistent (he suggests starting at once a week); (4) research/curate before
writing — use statistics (Statista, IBISWorld, Helgi Library) and academic papers
(Google Scholar), and analyze top-ranking competitor content structure; (5) enrich
content with free media (video, infographics via Canva free tier); (6) speed up the
site (Cloudflare CDN, AMP pages, image compression via Smush); (7) use free tools
(Google Search Console) to track and improve; (8) stay consistent — he cites his own
inconsistency on Quick Sprout (topic-hopping) versus disciplined weekly consistency on
neilpatel.com as the reason for its stronger traffic growth.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-06, Neil said to prioritize keywords with high CPC, higher search
  volume, and an SEO difficulty score under 40 as the core "penniless" keyword strategy
  in Ubersuggest.
- As of 2020-02-06, Neil said Ubersuggest's Keyword Overview shows searcher age range,
  useful for checking audience-keyword fit, alongside Facebook Audience Insights.
- As of 2020-02-06, Neil classified search intent into navigational, transactional, and
  informational categories, and said transactional and informational keywords are the
  ones that generate sales (transactional faster, informational slower).
- As of 2020-02-06, Neil said to start a content cadence simply (e.g. once a week) and
  scale up rather than overcommitting and having to redo a large content backlog.
- As of 2020-02-06, Neil recommended Statista, IBISWorld, and Helgi Library for
  statistics, and Google Scholar for academic citations, to make content stand out.
- As of 2020-02-06, Neil said he and his team use the free tier of Canva (not the paid
  version) for charts/graphs in blog content.
- As of 2020-02-06, Neil recommended Cloudflare as a free CDN, AMP pages on WordPress
  for mobile load speed, and the Smush plugin for image compression.
- As of 2020-02-06, Neil said he no longer owns his first blog, Quick Sprout, and that
  his inconsistent topic-switching there (personal branding → SEO/marketing → business
  → SEO) caused traffic to move in waves, whereas his consistent focus on
  neilpatel.com produced continual traffic growth.
