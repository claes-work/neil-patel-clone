# Here's What I Would Do If I Had To Start Over and Rebuild My Business (It's NOT What You Think)

## Summary

A counterintuitive career-sequencing argument: rather than jumping straight into
entrepreneurship, Neil says if he restarted he would **work for other people for nine
years first**, because you learn far more (and avoid costly mistakes) than going solo from
day one. He lays out a specific four-employer sequence: (1) **2 years at a small,
struggling, under-funded startup** — teaches you to be scrappy, save money, problem-solve;
(2) **3 years at a huge company** (Microsoft/Dell/IBM/Costco/Amazon) — teaches how big
companies operate, going after a large TAM, politics, closing deals with big corporations,
managing employees, climbing ranks; (3) **2 years at a $100M-revenue-toward-$1B company** —
teaches how to scale and keep growing (most companies stall at $50–100M); (4) **2 years at
a fast-growing, chaotic, $10M+-raised hockey-stick startup** — teaches you to move with the
mess and clean it up. After those nine years you start your own company with savings, a
network for **recruiting co-founders** (you can't build big alone), and people you can lure
with **equity** even if you can't match their pay. He shares origin details: entrepreneur
for 17 years, wanted money at 15½–16, no high-school/college degree, first job **cleaning
toilets and picking up trash at a theme park for $5.75/hour**.

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION: This "work for others for nine years first" advice sits in tension
> with Neil's own path (he became an entrepreneur as a teenager without that apprenticeship).
> It is presented as retrospective "if I had to start over" advice, not a description of
> what he actually did — date and frame it as such.

- As of 2019-07-25, Neil said he'd been an entrepreneur for **17 years** (implying he
  started ~2002).
- As of 2019-07-25, Neil said his **first job was cleaning toilets and picking up trash at
  a theme park for $5.75/hour**, and that he had no high-school or college degree when he
  started because he was still in school.
- As of 2019-07-25, Neil said if he restarted he would **work for others for nine years**
  before starting his own company, because you learn more and make fewer mistakes.
- As of 2019-07-25, Neil's prescribed sequence: 2 yrs at a struggling small startup
  (scrappiness) → 3 yrs at a huge company (scale, politics, big deals, management) → 2 yrs
  at a $100M→$1B company (how to scale past the stall) → 2 yrs at a fast-growing chaotic
  startup (thrive in the mess).
- As of 2019-07-25, Neil said you **need co-founders** — you can't build a big company
  alone — and should recruit people met along the way, offering **equity** to offset lower
  pay.
