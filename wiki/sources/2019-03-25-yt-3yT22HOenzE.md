# The Number One Reason 99% of Your Visitors Don't Buy from You [Fast Conversion Rate Optimization]

## Summary

Neil's CRO method: most people fail to convert because they run A/B tests off **quantitative**
analytics data alone (numbers in Google Analytics), instead of first gathering **qualitative**
insight (surveys/interviews) and **visual** insight (heatmaps). His sequence: (1) survey/interview
real and potential customers (SurveyMonkey, Hotjar) until ~40–60 people give similar feedback about
what's blocking them; (2) run a Crazyegg heatmap study to see where people click/don't and where
they drop off; (3) combine those to find the roadblocks, then run A/B tests in Crazyegg to fix them.
Final tip: fix traffic *quality* too — use Ubersuggest to find competitors' money keywords (high
volume, high CPC, low SEO difficulty), because high CPC signals converting traffic.

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION / caption note: the transcript labels Google-Analytics numbers as "qualitative
> data," which is backwards. In context Neil means **quantitative** = the numeric analytics, and
> **qualitative** = talking to/surveying people (SurveyMonkey/Hotjar). Crazyegg heatmaps are the
> **visual** layer. This page uses the corrected terminology; treat the raw transcript's first
> "qualitative" as a slip/garble.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-03-25, Neil said the main reason traffic doesn't convert is relying on **quantitative**
  analytics data alone (Google Analytics) to decide A/B tests, which produces tests that often don't
  move — or even lower — conversion rates. [corrected from transcript's mislabeling; see note above]
- As of 2019-03-25, Neil's step 1: gather **qualitative** data by surveying/interviewing real and
  potential customers (tools: SurveyMonkey, Hotjar) until ~40–60 people give similar feedback about
  problems with your product, copy, or landing pages — that tells you what to fix first.
- As of 2019-03-25, Neil's step 2: run a **Crazyegg** heatmap study to see visually where people click,
  don't click, and drop off — the "visual data" that reveals conversion roadblocks.
- As of 2019-03-25, Neil's step 3: combine qualitative + visual insight, then run A/B tests (in
  Crazyegg) to solve those specific problems, rather than testing blindly off analytics numbers.
- As of 2019-03-25, Neil said to also fix **traffic quality**: in Ubersuggest, enter larger competitors'
  URLs → Top Pages → View All keywords, and target keywords with high volume, high CPC, and low SEO
  difficulty; high CPC signals the traffic converts (a high-volume/penny-CPC keyword means the traffic
  doesn't convert). [consistent with his "money keywords" definition in 2019-03-11]

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Most people take the opposite approach ... they just run AB tests based off their Google Analytics data and that's why they run all these tests that don't boost their conversion rates. Heck, in many cases they'll decrease your conversion rate."

> "Once you got 40, 50, 60 people giving you similar feedback, now you know what to focus on first to get more of those visitors to convert into customers."

> "If people wanna pay a lot of money for a key word, that means those visitors convert into customers. If you notice a key word drives a million visits but people only pay a penny per click, it means that no-one cares for that key word."
