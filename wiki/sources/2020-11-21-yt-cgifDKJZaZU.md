# How to OWN Google My Business Rankings - Module 1 - Lesson 3 - Local SEO Unlocked

## Summary
Local SEO Unlocked, lesson 3. A step-by-step setup/optimization walkthrough across four
map/listing platforms: Google My Business (account setup through verification, then
optimization via photos/video, business description, Q&A, GMB posts, review
management, search insights), Bing Places (setup steps, ~1B unique monthly visitors /
12B monthly searches cited), Facebook for Business (page creation, "tell your story"
description, first-post guidance, multi-location setup via Business Manager), and
Apple Maps (Apple Map Connect claim/add flow, keyword-rich listing via Ubersuggest).
Closes with a rhyming aphorism about hustle.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-11-21, cites (self-reported/aggregator statistics, not independently
  verified here): 60% of smartphone users have contacted a business directly from
  search results via click-to-call; 82% of customers read online reviews for local
  businesses; Bing gets over 1 billion unique monthly visitors and 12 billion monthly
  search requests; ~90 million small businesses use Facebook; Apple has over 1.5
  billion active devices.
- As of 2020-11-21, states review ratings are "the biggest driver of clicks to the
  local SERPs" and recommends actively requesting/managing reviews rather than only
  reacting to them, with explicit guidance not to fake reviews but to address negative
  feedback to improve future ratings (paraphrase).
- Recommends listing on all four platforms (Google, Bing, Facebook, Apple Maps) even
  where a given platform (Bing, Apple Maps) sends comparatively little traffic,
  reasoning that free, low-effort listings compound and multi-platform presence
  protects against over-reliance on Google (paraphrase, tactical breadth argument).
