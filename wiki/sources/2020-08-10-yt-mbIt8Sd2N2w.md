# Promoting Your Content With Zero Budget - Module 3 - Lesson 1 - Content Marketing Unlocked

## Summary
Week-three lesson 1 of **Content Marketing Unlocked**
([[2020-08-06-yt-nuU_yXiTDv4]]): content promotion and site architecture. Cites an
Advanced Web Ranking stat that the #1 Google listing gets 31.7% of clicks, framing
site structure as the lever that earns Google "sitelinks" (indented sub-links under
a site's main listing). States a niche-focus philosophy: Google increasingly prefers
topically-focused sites, so "they want me to stick within my wheelhouse" (SEO/digital
marketing) rather than covering unrelated topics. Recommends Think with Google and
Google Trends for validating topic/niche size. Repeats the on-page checklist from
earlier lessons (keywords in title/first paragraph/headings, thorough but
not-padded length, short readable URLs, meta description keywords, descriptive alt
tags, internal linking, fixing broken backlinks via Ubersuggest's Backlinks Report,
natural anchor-text variation, AMP/responsive design, HTTPS, avoiding stock photos).
Then covers **$0-budget promotion tactics**: leveraging online communities (cites
Airbnb's early growth via Craigslist), share-to-unlock referral loops (Dropbox extra
storage, early invite-only Gmail), word of mouth, guest posting (cites Buffer's
growth via guest posting on ~10+ sites), "growth hacking" broadly defined, email
newsletters built via Hello Bar (his own product), sidebar links (notes NerdWallet
also uses this pattern), and sharing content natively on Instagram.
