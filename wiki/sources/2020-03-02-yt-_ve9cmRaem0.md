# The Definitive Guide to Ubersuggest for SEO and Content Marketing

## Summary
Neil Patel delivers a top-to-bottom walkthrough of Ubersuggest, his own SEO tool, framed as an answer to users who see its data but want to know how to actually use it to grow traffic, create content, and rank higher on Google. He tours each major section in order: the Keyword Overview (volume, CPC, SEO and paid difficulty, links needed to rank top 10, plus mobile-vs-desktop split, click distribution across SEO/paid/no-click, and age demographics); the Keyword Ideas report (related terms, comparisons, prepositions, and questions, with per-keyword difficulty and competitor link/share data); the Content Ideas report (popular blog posts ranked by social shares, backlinks, and estimated traffic, drilling into the keywords a post ranks for and the sites/anchors linking to it); the Traffic Analyzer (domain traffic and keyword count over time, Top Pages, and a Keywords report); the Site Audit (SEO issues prioritized by impact and ease of implementation); the Backlink report (link growth plus new/lost links); and the Dashboard/projects feature for ongoing tracking of traffic, rankings, weekly SEO crawls, and competitor comparison. He ties the Content Ideas workflow to Brian Dean's Skyscraper Technique and closes with a plug for his agency, Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- Ubersuggest's Keyword Overview shows volume, CPC, SEO difficulty, paid difficulty, and roughly how many links are needed to rank in the top 10 for a keyword (as of 2020-03-02).
- What is unique about Ubersuggest is that Keyword Overview shows the mobile-vs-desktop search split, the share of clicks going to SEO results vs paid results vs no click at all, and the age-demographic range of people searching that query (as of 2020-03-02).
- The Keyword Ideas report returns a large list of related suggestions — related keywords Google suggests, comparisons, prepositions, and the questions people search — with difficulty, who ranks, their link counts and social shares, so a user can judge whether a keyword is easy or hard to rank for (as of 2020-03-02).
- The Content Ideas report is underused and worth more attention; it surfaces popular blog posts for a topic broken down by social shares, backlinks, and estimated traffic, and drilling in reveals the keywords a post ranks for, their positions, and every linking site and anchor tag (as of 2020-03-02).
- The recommended content workflow is to find what is already popular in Content Ideas and outdo it — creating something roughly 10 times better — an application of Brian Dean's Skyscraper Technique (as of 2020-03-02).
- The Traffic Analyzer shows a domain's Google traffic over time alongside how many keywords it ranks for over time; ranking for many keywords does not necessarily mean a lot of traffic, since keyword traffic varies widely (as of 2020-03-02).
- Traffic Analyzer includes Top Pages (top pages per domain with estimated visits, their ranking keywords, and backlinks) and a Keywords report listing all keywords a URL ranks for, sorted by popularity, revealing what a site targets (as of 2020-03-02).
- The Site Audit report lists a site's SEO issues and prioritizes recommendations by putting highest-impact, easiest-to-implement fixes at the top and harder, lower-impact ones at the bottom, so users should tackle the top items first (as of 2020-03-02).
- The Backlink report shows backlink growth over time, whether a site's links are increasing or decreasing, new and lost links on a daily basis, and each individual link for a page or whole domain (as of 2020-03-02).
- The Dashboard lets a user add a project to track SEO progress — daily rank tracking with up/down reporting, weekly site crawls flagging SEO errors to fix, and competitor comparison — and this feature is one that not many people talk about (as of 2020-03-02).
