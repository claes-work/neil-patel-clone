# How to Get More From Your Livestream After the Broadcast

## Summary
A live-streamed interview/Q&A hosted by "Anya" for **Restream** (a multi-platform
simultaneous-livestreaming tool), recorded live across YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, and
Twitter simultaneously, with audience questions worked in. Substantial, high-density
source on Neil's live-video doctrine and a chunk of new biography/voice material. Core
argument: social platforms are increasingly optimizing their algorithms to behave like
live television, spreading live content beyond your own network to keep viewers on-
platform (more ad revenue for them) — so live drives outsized reach versus recorded video,
and posting to multiple platforms/destinations costs almost no extra effort for
meaningfully more reach. Reiterates the YouTube first-24-hours ranking-velocity doctrine
(already documented) and the SEO angle of saying/writing target keywords explicitly during
a live stream. Gives a concrete case study: an unnamed hair-extensions e-commerce client
went from one Facebook livestream per month generating "five figures in revenue" each time
to 3x/week at Neil's recommendation — lower per-video revenue but total monthly revenue
approaching six figures. Discloses he is **an introvert** who has simply done enough
livestreams to be used to being on camera, and shares a self-deprecating first-livestream
story (Facebook, at a Starbucks, went live for only ~2 minutes despite 1,000+ concurrent
viewers, and regrets cutting it short). Attributes his adoption of Restream specifically
to **"a buddy named Eric Sue"** [sic — almost certainly Eric Siu, his Marketing School
co-host] introducing him to the tool after Neil had been trying to multi-stream manually
by buying multiple laptops for his team. Gives a content-repurposing pipeline: livestream
→ strip audio into a podcast (distributed to Spotify/iTunes/Google) → send the audio/video
to an Upwork freelancer to adapt (not transcribe verbatim) into a blog post — one piece of
raw content becoming four asset types. Explains an "indirect ROI" framing for social
platforms: driving people off-platform ("click the link below") gets algorithmically
penalized, so brand/sales lift from social content is real but hard to track directly —
he cites a self-run Ubersuggest purchase survey where >20% of buyers said they discovered
the product via YouTube, even though direct tracking showed near-zero attributed
conversions from that channel. Advises agencies to niche down into live-streaming-as-a-
service specifically, citing it as a less-competitive market than SEO/PPC (name-checks
Gary Vaynerchuk's voice-search conference as an analogous first-mover niche play). Closes
with a "your mom" quote on always including a pitch/ask.
