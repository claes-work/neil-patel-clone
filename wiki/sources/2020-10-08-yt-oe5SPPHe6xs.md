# How to Build Your YouTube Channel The Right Way - Module 1 - Lesson 2 - YouTube Unlocked

## Summary
Lesson 2 of the "YouTube Unlocked" course (continues from
[[wiki/sources/2020-10-05-yt-BJhTePXFvGo]]), covering channel setup and branding.
Neil frames research as the foundation: use Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas report (and
its YouTube-focused Chrome extension) to find search demand, then apply a specific
video-vetting heuristic — a fairly new video with a lot of views on a channel that
isn't itself popular signals a genuinely hot topic worth replicating, whereas a
popular video on a 10M+-subscriber channel proves nothing about the topic itself.
He then walks channel-identity mechanics: choose a sticky, brandable channel name
(not just a category description, and avoid numbers), write a keyword-rich
description that signals topical focus to YouTube's algorithm, invest in
professional channel art that states upload cadence and links to other socials, cap
tags at roughly five secondary keywords plus long-tail phrases, fill out the About
section thoroughly, and use the Community tab (polls, text posts, images) to drive
engagement outside of uploaded videos. On cadence, Neil discloses his own schedule —
Monday/Thursday/Saturday — as an example of deliberate, sustainable consistency
rather than overcommitting to daily uploads, and notes there is no universal
best-time-to-post (his own B2B audience responds best on weekday early mornings).

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-08, Neil's heuristic for identifying a genuinely hot topic: a
  relatively new video getting strong views from a channel that is not itself
  popular indicates real demand, whereas a popular video from a 10M+-subscriber
  channel would do well regardless of topic quality.
- As of 2020-10-08, Neil recommends capping channel tags at roughly five secondary
  keywords (up to ten) and prioritizing long-tail phrases found via Ubersuggest.
- As of 2020-10-08, Neil states his own upload schedule is Monday, Thursday,
  Saturday, and frames not overcommitting to seven-day-a-week posting as
  intentional schedule management (self-reported).
- As of 2020-10-08, Neil states there is no single generically-best posting time —
  for his own B2B audience, weekday early mornings perform best, but he says
  audience geography and B2B-vs-B2C timezone differences change the answer.
- As of 2020-10-08, Neil frames the Community tab as a secondary engagement channel
  (polls, text, images) that boosts subscriber growth and video performance even
  though it sits outside uploaded video content.
