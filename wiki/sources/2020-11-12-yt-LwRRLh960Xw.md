# An Easy Way to Find More Keywords (Without Spending Lots of Time or Using a Ton of Tools)

## Summary
A solo screen-share tutorial: Neil walks through finding keywords using the Ubersuggest
Chrome Extension. Two methods: (1) run a Google search for a seed term and read the
Ubersuggest overlay (volume, mobile/desktop split, CPC, SEO difficulty, searcher age
range, click distribution) plus the sidebar's related/suggested/question/preposition/
comparison keywords, filtering for SEO difficulty under 50 and high CPC; (2) point the
extension at a competitor's site (his live example: Nextiva) to see their organic
keywords, top pages by country, and estimated visits per page, then build better
versions of what is already working for them, optionally extending into backlink
outreach via the same competitor's backlink list.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-11-12, his standing heuristic for keyword selection: SEO difficulty score
  under 50 (easier to rank) combined with a meaningfully high CPC (a few dollars or
  more), with higher search volume and older average searcher age both treated as
  positive value signals (paraphrase, tactical heuristic, consistent with the corpus's
  recurring Ubersuggest-metrics-first approach).
- As of 2020-11-12, recommends reverse-engineering competitor keyword strategy by
  running the Ubersuggest extension against a rival's homepage and individual top
  pages, then creating "better versions" of the pages that are already ranking for
  them, rather than starting keyword research from scratch (paraphrase).
- Live demo data points (not durable facts, just illustrative examples used in the
  tutorial): "VoIP" ~ significant monthly search volume with an approximate $55 CPC
  quoted mid-video (vs. an initial $34 CPC figure shown earlier in the demo for the
  same/adjacent term) — the discrepancy reflects two different live searches
  ("VoIP" vs. "voice over IP") rather than a correction, flagged for caption-garble
  awareness, not a durable claim.
