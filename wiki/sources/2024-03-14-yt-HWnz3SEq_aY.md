# Will RetellAI.com kill the customer service industry?, I lost $600 on my AI Dentist, and more

## Summary
Three segments. (1) A discussion of RetellAI (retellai.com), a conversational-voice-API startup praised by Y Combinator's Garry Tan as the best demo of its class, with commentary that AI voice agents (customer support, dental-appointment booking demoed) will displace human sales/support agents and that agencies have a near-term window to resell AI transformation services to clients; largely undiarized. (2) A first-person, extensively narrated Neil anecdote: he spent $600 (later another $600 at a second office) on dental cleanings/scans in Beverly Hills — contrasted with the ~$100–125 he could pay near his parents in Arcadia — after an AI diagnostic tool flagged an anomaly on his tooth scan that a second office's AI, and eventually a human, determined was just normal bone; he frames the $600 loss (plus ~1h40m of walking instead of driving, deliberately, to avoid spending on gas/rideshare) as a "short-term bearish, long-term bullish" case for AI-in-dentistry accuracy, while airing genuine frustration and reaffirming an anti-waste money mindset. He also covers dental-hygiene habits (never had a cavity, doesn't drink coffee, drinks non-caffeinated tea, brushes 2–3x/day, switched floss brands on his dentist's recommendation) and mocks premium "wellness marketing" pricing (a $30 box of "Cocoa Floss," expensive "Hostage Tape" sleep tape). (3) A glass-water segment: Neil explains he now drinks Aquapanna glass water because Eric kept sending him viral microplastics articles and appealed to concern for his kids' health; he describes his small Beverly Hills condo (no counter space for a water filtration system, a kids' closet "the width of two [conference] chairs"), recounts a friend wasting an unfinished $2–3 bottle, and connects his irritation at wasted money to two older anecdotes: walking home from a Beverly Hills dentist rather than pay for a ride, and — from his younger days — walking home from a Las Vegas nightclub, furious at himself, after spending thousands of dollars on bottle service.
