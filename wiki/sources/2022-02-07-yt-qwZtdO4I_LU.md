# 7 Social Media Content Ideas That'll Attract Likes, Organic Reach, and Engagement

## Summary
A tactical rundown of seven social-media content formats for likes, organic reach, and
engagement: (1) image carousels breaking down technical topics (best on Instagram/
LinkedIn, made with free tool Canva, no design skill required), (2) short how-to video
tutorials (must be genuinely instructional to perform, not just filmed), (3) "this or
that" polls (deliberately avoids political examples despite citing one he saw), (4)
partnering with other content creators of similar size (start small — pair with
similarly-sized accounts, not accounts far bigger), (5) going live (frames platforms as
competing with reality TV/"the Kardashians" for engagement and algorithmically
rewarding live content), (6) curated lists (works in carousel, image, or text form), and
(7) infographics/data-visualization carousels broken into multiple slides rather than
one long graphic. Closes plugging NP Digital.

## Key claims
- Image carousels breaking down technical/complex topics are a top-engagement format on
  Instagram and LinkedIn specifically, made via the free tool Canva without design
  skills (self-reported practice, dated 2022-02-07) — social-media, content-marketing.
- How-to video tutorials only perform when genuinely instructional; a non-instructional
  how-to (his own failed toilet-installation example) underperforms (self-reported,
  dated 2022-02-07) — social-media.
- "This or that" binary-choice polls are framed explicitly as engagement bait that
  works, with an explicit caution to avoid political topics in polls even though a
  political example is cited as effective (self-reported, dated 2022-02-07) —
  social-media.
- Creator partnerships should match audience size — pair with similarly-sized accounts
  (e.g. both around 500 followers) rather than approaching accounts orders of magnitude
  bigger; going live together is the easiest cross-promotion mechanism (self-reported,
  dated 2022-02-07) — social-media.
- Frames social platforms' promotion of live video as platforms competing with
  traditional reality TV ("keeping up with the Kardashians") for attention/engagement,
  incentivizing their algorithms to reward live content (paraphrase/opinion, dated
  2022-02-07) — social-media.
