# Should Your Domain Name Contain Keywords to Boost SEO Rankings?

## Summary
Neil answers whether a domain name should contain a target keyword. His verdict: a keyword in the domain does help ranking and makes it easier (e.g. hotels.com ranking for hotel terms), so if you can get a short, keyword-rich domain, grab it — but it is not necessary. Booking.com outranks hotels.com without the keyword in its domain, and neilpatel.com ranks for "online marketing" even though those words aren't in the domain. What actually matters is having the keyword in the page's **URL**. He warns against buying a long keyword-stuffed domain (e.g. "awesomebesthotels.com") just for SEO — it's not memorable or brandable and people won't type it — so choose a memorable, brandable domain that resembles your business and put keywords in the URL instead.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-25, Neil states:
- Having a keyword in your domain name does help you rank higher and makes ranking easier — e.g. hotels.com ranks well for hotel terms partly because "hotels" is in the domain (as of 2017-07-25).
- It is not required: booking.com (the most popular hotel-booking site, larger than hotels.com) ranks without the keyword in its domain, and neilpatel.com ranks for "online marketing" without those words in the domain (as of 2017-07-25).
- What matters more is that the target keyword appears in the **URL of the page** — the page ranking for "online marketing" has that phrase in its URL (as of 2017-07-25).
- Don't buy a long, keyword-stuffed domain (e.g. awesomebesthotels.com) just for SEO — it isn't memorable or brandable and people won't type it into Google (as of 2017-07-25).
- If you can get a short keyword domain (cars.com, hotels.com) and have the money, buy it; otherwise pick a memorable, brandable domain resembling your business and include keywords within the URL (as of 2017-07-25).
- Industry note: hotels.com is owned by Expedia; Priceline is a much larger company (as of 2017-07-25).
