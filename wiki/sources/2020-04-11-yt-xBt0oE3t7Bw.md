# 9 Things You Didn't Know About The World's Top Websites in Search Traffic

## Summary
Neil analyzes Alexa's top-traffic-website list for common traits. (1) Branding is
their biggest traffic asset — cites Craigslist ranking 22nd globally (per Rank Ranger)
almost entirely on branded local-search terms; (2) established authority — cites
Copyblogger that domain trust/authority represents 23.87% of Google's ranking
algorithm, so trusted brands can rank with subpar content; (3) social + user-generated
content at scale (Airbnb's Instagram reposting followers' photos; Wikipedia, YouTube,
Twitter, Amazon, IMDb, TripAdvisor, Urban Dictionary as UGC examples); (4) sites without
UGC lean on content-production scale — cites the New York Times publishing ~230
pieces/day and getting ~59M organic visitors/month ranking for ~45M keywords; (5)
authority alone can carry non-marketing sites — cites an unnamed "Serrata" example
ranking for 5,000+ keywords doing "virtually no marketing"; (6) big brands barely need
to actively link-build: newsworthy content earns natural links (NYT cited at ~300M
backlinks, 93% dofollow) and brand trust alone earns backlinks (WebMD ~21M backlinks,
contrasted against "Neil's medical blog" as a joke); (7) UX/brand-promise delivery
(Nike, Geico's "15 minutes... 15%" tagline as marketing-strategy backbone, quoting Ron
Girgis of Edelman that people buy companies/values, not just products); (8) page-speed
as a ranking factor, recommending Ubersuggest's site-audit report; (9) personalization
of the on-site/email experience based on purchase history (Amazon example: no
dog-product recommendations because Neil has no pets). Closing thesis: "SEO is not just
about backlinks. It's about the total experience."

## Key claims
- As of 2020-04-11, Neil cited Copyblogger that domain trust/authority represents
  23.87% of Google's ranking algorithm.
- As of 2020-04-11, Neil cited that Craigslist ranks 22nd globally for organic traffic
  (per Rank Ranger), largely via branded local-search terms, beating Apple, Netflix,
  and Walmart in organic search traffic.
- As of 2020-04-11, Neil cited that the New York Times gets ~59M organic
  visitors/month, ranks for ~45M organic keywords, and publishes ~230 pieces of content
  per day; and has ~300M backlinks, 93% of them dofollow.
- As of 2020-04-11, Neil cited WordStream research: average conversion for the top 10%
  of landing pages is 11.45%, while average long-tail-keyword conversion is 36%
  (referenced in support of the broader "top sites deliver on UX/promise" point).
- As of 2020-04-11, Neil's closing thesis: "SEO is not just about backlinks. It's about
  the total experience," citing Google's 2019 diversity update (max ~2 SERP slots per
  domain) as evidence Google increasingly rewards experience over brute-force authority.
