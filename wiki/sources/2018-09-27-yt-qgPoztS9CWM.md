# Backlink HACKS (The Easy way to Index Faster)

## Summary

Q&A Thursday, co-hosted with **Adam Lodolce** (from Viewership). Viewer question (Jonathan
D'Arcio): he's building high-DA (50+) backlinks to his site but they aren't indexing —
how do you get backlinks indexed faster? Neil's answer reframes the premise. First, a
caveat: **third-party SEO tools are not Google** — Ahrefs, SEMrush, Majestic (and even
Neil himself) missing a link doesn't mean Google missed it; Google spends "hundreds…if
not billions" on servers and will pick things up before anyone else, and many sites block
the Ahrefs/SEMrush crawlers, so those tools never see every backlink. So (1) **ignore
whether the tools index the link** — just keep building high-quality, relevant links, and
(2) **don't obsess over Domain Authority**: DA is an SEO-industry metric, not a number
Google uses, and it varies wildly between Ahrefs/Moz/Majestic. What matters is natural,
relevant, in-niche links (a dental site linking to a computer site is fine but doesn't
help much; press from a magazine/newspaper is "amazing"). To help Google crawl a link
faster: **share the linking URL on social**, **visit the URL in your Chrome browser**, and
**do a Google search for that URL** — these signals raise the chance it gets crawled
faster. Beyond that, don't worry: "do your job of building good links, let the search
engines do their job." Adam adds a promotion tactic (attributed to Adam, not Neil): when
you land a press mention (NYT, Boston Globe), promote it on Facebook and retarget/direct-
market anyone who clicks — raising perceived authority — and you can "boost" the linking
article the same way to help it get picked up faster.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-09-27, **third-party SEO tools (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Majestic) are not Google** —
  a link they don't show may still be indexed by Google; many sites block those crawlers.
- As of 2018-09-27, **Domain Authority is an SEO-industry metric, not a Google ranking
  number**, and it varies drastically across tools; chase natural relevant links instead.
- As of 2018-09-27, **link relevancy matters more than raw authority** — in-niche links
  and legit press mentions help; off-topic links (dental→computer) barely help.
- As of 2018-09-27, to speed indexing of a backlink: **social-share the linking URL, view
  it in Chrome, and Google-search the URL** — engagement signals raise crawl likelihood.
- As of 2018-09-27, Neil's stance is to **stop worrying about what tools index** and let
  Google (a near-trillion-dollar company whose mission is to organize information) do it.
