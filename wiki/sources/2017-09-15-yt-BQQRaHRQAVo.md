# What Are Carousels And Should You Use Them On Your Site

## Summary
Neil explains what a website carousel is — a box, often placed in the header or hero area above the fold, whose content automatically rotates every few seconds so visitors can move left or right through images or information. He argues that in most cases you should not use carousels, because they are distracting and keep visitors browsing rather than taking action, and cites his own testing showing that 9 out of 10 times a carousel decreases conversion rate. He carves out exceptions for highly visual services such as photographers, wedding sites, and artists, and for content like testimonials — but insists that even then carousels should never go above the fold, which should instead be reserved for stating the problem you solve, the solution you provide, and how to buy or sign up.

## Key claims

- Carousels are acceptable for testimonials or for a photographer's portfolio of many photos (as of 2017-09-15).
- Above the fold should present the problem you solve, the solution you provide, and how to get in touch to buy or sign up — not a carousel (as of 2017-09-15).
- Below the fold you can consider using a carousel (as of 2017-09-15).
- Unless you provide a very visual service such as a wedding photographer or artist, Neil does not recommend using carousels at all (as of 2017-09-15).
