# How I Get Over $500,000 Worth of FREE Traffic Each Month to My Website

## Summary
Neil explains his approach to generating what he describes as over half a million dollars' worth of free (organic) traffic to his website every month, framing it as a repeatable process rather than a single tactic. He argues that consistent traffic comes from leveraging all major channels together — email marketing, SEO, content marketing, and social media — because any single channel fluctuates while a combination provides stability and compounding growth. He then walks through his content and engagement tactics: producing content that visitors (not the author) judge amazing, using BuzzSumo to find what drives traffic for competitors and one-upping it, using Google Analytics to identify already-popular pages and creating more of the same, building and nurturing a community across a Facebook page and email list, and actively participating in the social web by responding to comments and helping people rather than only self-promoting. The recurring theme is that helpfulness and thoroughness build a community that continually promotes his content.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-24, Neil states:
- The traffic he receives is worth over $500,000 per month, which he presents as free/organic traffic (self-reported figure, unverified; as of 2017-10-24).
- Viewers can verify his traffic value by entering "neilpatel.com" into SEMrush (self-reported verification method; as of 2017-10-24).
- Getting large, consistent traffic is a process, not something obtained from a single channel (as of 2017-10-24).
- Because individual channels rise and fall (e.g. LinkedIn one day, Google another), combining email marketing, SEO, content marketing, and social media marketing produces more stability and continually growing traffic (as of 2017-10-24).
- Content quality should be judged by what visitors find amazing, not by what the creator thinks is amazing (as of 2017-10-24).
- Marketers should use BuzzSumo with competitor URLs to see what is driving traffic and then do more of that, rather than recreating the wheel (as of 2017-10-24).
- The right move is to one-up competitors' content — e.g. turning a competitor's "10 ways" article into a "101 ways" article — being so thorough that no one wants to copy it, rather than plagiarizing (as of 2017-10-24).
- Google Analytics reveals a site's most popular pages, and creating more content in those popular topics (e.g. SEO, social media marketing) generates more traffic (as of 2017-10-24).
- Building a community across a Facebook page and email list — and nurturing it by continuing to email, help, and communicate even when members do not need help — creates a thriving community that promotes your content (as of 2017-10-24).
- Simply being present on Facebook and Twitter is not enough; marketers must participate by responding to comments and helping people to give them a reason to return (as of 2017-10-24).
- On social profiles, one should not only talk about one's business but also discuss other topics that engage and help the audience, since it is about building a community (as of 2017-10-24).
