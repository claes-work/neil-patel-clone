# How to Protect Your SEO Rankings (While Using Google to Help You)

## Summary
Short solo tutorial on using Google Search Console (GSC) as an early-warning system to
maintain rankings rather than just chase new ones. Walks the basic GSC setup (search
"google search console", add/verify a property) and then the actual tactic: GSC sends
monthly performance-overview emails (clicks, impressions, top content, top gains, top
performing queries, "new top growing queries" as a keyword-focus signal) plus ad hoc
alerts whenever Google detects a site issue (his live examples on neilpatel.com: a
breadcrumb-markup issue and an AMP issue flagged as "referenced AMP URL is not an AMP
page", plus a 500-server-error coverage issue). His workflow: forward each alert to his
developer (or fix it himself), submit for validation, and repeat — framed explicitly as
not a ranking guarantee but an odds-improver ("it increases the chances that you stay at
the top... or that you rank higher"). Closes with the standard NP Digital plug.

## Key claims
- Google Search Console's monthly email digest (clicks/impressions/top content/top
  growing queries) is a low-effort way to keep monitoring rankings without logging in
  daily (self-reported workflow, 2021-06-03) — seo.
- Google proactively emails site-health issues (breadcrumb markup, AMP validity, server
  errors) via GSC; fixing them promptly is presented as increasing (not guaranteeing) the
  odds of maintaining or improving rankings (self-reported, 2021-06-03) — seo.
- States he routes these GSC alerts to his developer or fixes them himself, then
  resubmits for Google's validation as a recurring maintenance loop (self-reported,
  2021-06-03) — seo.
