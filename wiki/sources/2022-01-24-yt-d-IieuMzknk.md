# What You Should Do When People Have Money And Can't Afford Your Product | Sales Advice

## Summary
A sales-objection framework for when a prospect claims they "can't afford" something
they actually could. Reasons cited: wrong buyer targeted (illustrated with a personal
anecdote — he drives a Honda Odyssey, not a Rolls-Royce/Bentley/Ferrari, because he
"doesn't really care about driving" and finds it anxiety-inducing) or wrong decision-
maker (a dental-chain client wanted kid-targeted viral content, which drove
engagement/rankings but no revenue because kids aren't the buyers — parents are); lack
of confidence the product solves their problem; lack of trust/relationship (compared
to proposing marriage to a stranger — "you want to date them first"); the prospect
not trusting their own ability to use the product. Prescribes asking directly about
budget and whether a given price would close the deal (without pre-emptively
discounting), using the answers to diagnose whether the objection is price or
value/fit, then adjusting messaging/offer to make the ROI case explicit. Cites an
internal example: a $5K/month ($60K/rep/year) sales-efficiency tool NP Digital
initially balked at, later adopted once they'd fixed upstream sales-process gaps, and
which then made reps "four times more efficient" — cheaper than hiring four more
people.

## Key claims
- Frames a stalled sale primarily as a targeting/trust problem, not a price problem:
  the fix is diagnostic questions about budget/price-at-which-they'd-buy, not
  discounting (self-reported sales framework, dated 2022-01-24) — agency-
  entrepreneurship.
- Cites a first-person NP Digital case: paid $5K/month for a sales-efficiency tool
  that was initially rejected as too expensive, later adopted, and reportedly made
  reps "four times more efficient" (self-reported business anecdote, dated
  2022-01-24; unaudited/self-reported figure) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- States a real client anecdote: a multi-location dental chain wanted kid-targeted
  viral content; it drove leads/engagement/rankings but no revenue because children
  are not the purchasing decision-makers (self-reported client case, dated
  2022-01-24) — agency-entrepreneurship, content-marketing.
