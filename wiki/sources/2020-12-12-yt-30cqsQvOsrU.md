# How to Find 1000s of Lucrative Keywords in Under a Minute

## Summary
Screen-recorded Ubersuggest workflow: enter your own domain (or a top competitor's, if
your site is new) into the "Competing Domains" report, prune irrelevant broad-traffic
sites (his example: excluding Forbes and Entrepreneur.com as non-direct competitors
despite shared topical overlap), then work down the resulting competitor list using
the "Keyword Gap" report (keywords competitors rank for that you don't) rather than
"Common Keywords" (shared rankings, lower priority). Selection criteria for viable
targets: high search volume, high CPC (a proxy for commercial/conversion intent), and
SEO-difficulty score under 50 — with a tip to page deep into results ("next, next,
next") because two/three-word long-tail phrases with better difficulty-to-volume
ratios tend to surface only after the obvious head terms are exhausted.

## Key claims
- Workflow (paraphrase): Ubersuggest → enter domain (yours if established, a top
  competitor's if your site is new/small) → Competing Domains report → manually
  exclude non-direct competitors from the list → Keyword Gap report (not Common
  Keywords) → export/review keywords.
- Selection heuristic (paraphrase, standing tactic): prioritize keywords with high
  search volume, high CPC (higher CPC correlates with higher conversion likelihood,
  by his framing), and SEO difficulty under 50; a low-CPC keyword like "Yahoo News"
  is deprioritized despite volume because it doesn't convert as well.
- Advises paging deep through gap-report results rather than stopping at page one,
  because one-word head terms exhaust quickly and better-value two/three-word long-
  tail phrases (his examples: "Google review[s]," "stock photography," "link
  shortener" [Pro Tools]) surface further down the list (paraphrase).
- Reiterates the standing "don't reinvent the wheel" doctrine: build content around
  what's already proven to work for competitors rather than inventing net-new topics
  from scratch (paraphrase, reinforcement, not new).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Don't reinvent the wheel. Instead, just look to see what's working for your
> competition and go create content similar to them.
