# 7 Free Tools to Rank #1 in Google

## Summary

In this solo video, Neil Patel walks through seven free tools he recommends for improving Google rankings without necessarily building links. The set spans on-page optimization (Quick Sprout, Yoast SEO), indexing and performance data (Google Search Console), demand and brand-signal research (Google Trends), and technical/performance auditing (a responsive-design tester, Screaming Frog, and Google PageSpeed Insights). His through-line is that much ranking improvement comes from fixing on-page issues, optimizing existing content for click-through, and ensuring the site is fast and mobile-friendly.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-01-11, Neil names seven free tools to help rank #1 on Google: (1) Quick Sprout analyzer, (2) Yoast SEO plugin, (3) Google Search Console, (4) Google Trends, (5) websiteresponsivetest.com, (6) Screaming Frog, (7) Google PageSpeed Insights.
- **Quick Sprout** (quicksprout.com) is described as a free, gamified SEO application: you enter your URL and it breaks down what's wrong (e.g., missing keywords in title tags, descriptions, or alt image tags), walks you through fixes step by step, shows which keywords to add where, and — for non-technical users — can publish the changes for you without needing to know coding.
- **Yoast SEO** is a WordPress plugin and one of Neil's favorite tools; it has a free and a paid version (he personally uses the paid one but says either works). Each time you publish a blog post it gives a score and guidance on your title tag and meta description, lets you pick keywords and show search engines something different than users, lets you optimize every post for maximum traffic, and automatically updates your sitemap on each new post.
- **Google Search Console**: you submit your XML sitemap (likely created by the Yoast plugin, which Neil says he and most experts use). It shows impressions, clicks, popular pages, and which keywords get many impressions but few clicks — you then add those keywords and adjust title tags/meta descriptions to maximize click-through rate. Neil claims this increases rankings without building a single link.
- **Google Trends**: Neil frames brand queries as a Google ranking factor. In his car-industry example, General Motors and BMW both have millions of dollars and millions of backlinks; because more people search "BMW," Google ranks the more popular manufacturer ahead of the other when other SEO factors are similar.
- **websiteresponsivetest.com**: Neil claims more people use Google from mobile/tablet devices than from desktop/laptop; this free tool shows how mobile visitors see your website, so you can ensure it's responsive and loads fast.
- **Screaming Frog** is described as a super-technical SEO tool: enter a URL and it shows everything wrong — titles, headings, keywords, links — analyzing every detail from an advanced technical framework.
- **Google PageSpeed Insights** shows how fast your site loads and breaks down what to improve for both desktop and mobile. Neil argues slow-loading sites lose visitors (especially on mobile, and in areas with poor reception), so making the site load fast matters.
- Neil's framing throughout is that rankings can be improved via on-page fixes, click-through optimization, and speed/mobile-readiness — several tools, he notes, help without requiring link building.
