# How to Get More SEO Traffic Using The Ubersuggest Chrome Extension

## Summary
Neil Patel demonstrates how to use the free Ubersuggest Chrome extension to find more search traffic, framing the whole workflow around two payoffs: uncovering keyword opportunities and uncovering link opportunities. He walks through the extension's on-SERP data (search volume by desktop/mobile, SEO vs. paid clicks, searcher age range, CPC, ranking difficulty), the related-keyword sidebar for finding variations a single page can target, and the per-result metrics shown on every Google listing (domain score, domain traffic, social shares, and backlink counts). His signature move is expanding a competitor's backlinks to see who links to them and then pitching those same sites for your own links. He also covers the pinned "U" button that pulls up any site's organic keywords, monthly traffic, backlinks, domain score, and top pages — usable on competitors like Moz or on your own domain (neilpatel.com) — plus per-country/region keyword breakdowns and the ability to drill into the full Ubersuggest app. His closing advice: target keywords your competitors already profit from, favor longer three-to-four-word longtail phrases because they convert better even if they drive less volume, and replicate competitors' top-performing pages when you are stuck on what content to create. He points viewers to his agency, Neil Patel Digital, for done-for-you help.

## Key claims
- The Ubersuggest Chrome extension is free to install from the Chrome Web Store (search "Ubersuggest") and is used to get more search traffic once installed (as of 2020-09-05).
- After any Google search, clicking "View All" on a keyword reveals data including how many people search it on desktop vs. mobile, how many click from SEO vs. paid results, the searchers' age range, cost-per-click data, and the difficulty to rank for it (as of 2020-09-05).
- The right-hand sidebar surfaces related keywords similar to what competitors rank for; these are variations you should also target, and a single page can often be used to target multiple variations to capture more traffic (as of 2020-09-05).
- Each Google search result is annotated with domain score, domain traffic, social shares, and backlink count for that URL (as of 2020-09-05).
- Clicking the down arrow on a result's backlinks reveals who is linking to that page; you can then approach those same websites and try to get them to link back to you, which builds more links over time (as of 2020-09-05).
- Scrolling to the very bottom of the search page shows additional related keywords based on data coming directly from Google, which Neil often starts including within his own webpages (as of 2020-09-05).
- Once installed, a pinned "U" button lets you pull any site's information — organic keywords, monthly traffic, backlinks, domain score, overall traffic, and top pages — while browsing that site (Neil uses Moz as the competitor example) (as of 2020-09-05).
- The top-pages view shows a domain's most popular pages and their visit counts; clicking "View All" opens the Ubersuggest app to drill into those top pages and the keywords driving traffic to the domain (as of 2020-09-05).
- The extension shows the keywords a site ranks for per region/country (Neil cites India as an example), and this works on your own site too — e.g., viewing neilpatel.com's traffic over time, keyword counts, and "View All Keywords" per country (as of 2020-09-05).
- Keyword and link research is a prerequisite for traffic: creating content without targeting the right keywords generally will not earn much traffic (as of 2020-09-05).
- Targeting keywords that competitors already rank for and profit from raises the odds those keywords will also perform well for you, so start by checking what works for competitors and where they get their links (as of 2020-09-05).
- Prefer longer-tailed keywords of three or four words over very popular single-word phrases; longtail terms drive less traffic but tend to drive more conversions (as of 2020-09-05).
- When unsure what content to create, use the top-pages report for any site (via the "U" button) to see what is working for them and replicate it on your own site (as of 2020-09-05).
