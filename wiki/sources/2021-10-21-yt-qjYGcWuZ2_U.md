## Summary
A 7-step framework for future-proof SEO content: (1) solve the reader's specific
problem rather than just target a keyword (his tie-shop example: keep it short, use
video/GIFs); (2) spend 40% of writing time on the headline — curiosity-driven, not
clickbait, backed by a real payoff ("Number Six Will Shock You" only works if point six
actually delivers); Ubersuggest's "content ideas" report surfaces high-share headlines
per keyword; (3) anchor supporting keywords tightly to one main topic rather than
spreading thin across many short pieces; (4) build the content outline from what's
already ranking (including a "go to page 3-4 of Google" exercise to see what's missing
there vs. page 1); (5) give genuinely practical, actionable advice — illustrated with a
personal anecdote about a "how to install a toilet" article that failed to actually
cover installing a bidet; (6) do relevant internal linking without over-optimizing
anchor text; (7) nail on-page SEO fundamentals (title tags, metadata, alt text, H2s,
URL structure), using Ubersuggest's site audit report to prioritize fixes.
