# 4 Simple Hacks to Bring Dead Pages Back to Life With Massive SEO Gains

## Summary
Solo tactical SEO walkthrough on reviving decayed content. The setup: pages get traffic for
a while and then stop entirely — a month, six months or a year later — so re-optimizing
beats writing more. **Step 1**: export the full page list from Google Analytics (Behavior →
Overview → View full report → Export). **Step 2**: choose which pages to optimize by
*conversion* quality — good conversion rate, time on page, bounce rate — not popularity;
Neil stresses these are often not the highest-traffic pages, and that boosting random pages
gets traffic while leads and sales stay flat. **Step 3**: apply one or a combination of four
hacks. *Hack 1 — internal linking*: identify your top pages (via the Analytics export or by
running your URL through **Ubersuggest**) and link from those already-Google-loved pages to
the high-conversion/low-traffic pages, passing "juice" so they rise. *Hack 2 — combine
similar/duplicate pieces*: use Google Search Console to see which articles rank for similar
terms, merge the content into the most popular URL and 301 the weaker one into it, so Google
no longer has to choose between two articles on one topic — the reason Wikipedia performs
so well is one continually expanded page per topic rather than ten; update internal links
and detect outdated/broken pages with **Screaming Frog**. *Hack 3 — consolidate cannibalizing
content with redirects*: merge no-traffic pages into related ones even when the keyword
isn't identical, deleting the junk pages Google won't rank so site authority concentrates
in pages that matter instead of being spread across thousands, always 301-ing the removed
URLs to the revived or most relevant page. *Hack 4 — "land and expand"* (his stated favorite):
pull the queries already driving traffic to your winning URLs from Search Console, run those
keywords through Ubersuggest to get long-tail variations, and add the relevant ones back into
the page — since you already rank for the head term, he says he typically reaches page one
for the long-tails within 30 days. Closes with a pitch for his agency, Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil described content decay as normal — a page can get traffic and then stop altogether after a month, two months, six months or a year — and framed reviving dead pages as better use of time than writing content nobody reads.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil's page-inventory method was Google Analytics → Behavior → Overview → View full report → Export, to get a full list of pages and posts.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil said to select refresh candidates by conversion rate, time on page and bounce rate — the pages producing the most sales, leads and revenue — explicitly noting these are often **not** the most-trafficked pages.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil warned that optimizing random pages produces more traffic while sales, leads and conversions stay the same.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil said internal links from your most popular (already Google-loved) pages to high-converting low-traffic pages pass authority and lift those pages' rankings over time.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil's consolidation method was: find articles on similar/duplicate topics (confirming via Google Search Console that they rank for similar terms), merge everything into the most popular URL, and **301 the less popular version** into it.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil argued two articles on the same topic leave Google unable to decide which to rank, and cited **Wikipedia** as the model — one page per topic, continually expanded, rather than ten pages on the same topic.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil recommended **Screaming Frog** to update internal links and identify old, outdated or broken pages that should be deleted.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil said consolidating cannibalizing content removes "junk pages Google doesn't want to rank" and concentrates site authority into pages that matter, instead of spreading it across thousands of mostly non-ranking pages.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil said deleted/merged pages must be 301-redirected to the revived URL or the most relevant one.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil named "land and expand" as one of his favorite tactics: take the Search Console queries driving traffic to already-winning URLs, run them through **Ubersuggest** for long-tail variations, and add the relevant variations into the existing page.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil claimed that ranking for a head term makes the long-tail variations easy, and that with land-and-expand he **usually reaches page one for the long-tail variations within 30 days**.
- As of 2019-12-26, Neil directed viewers needing help to his agency, then named "Neil Patel Digital" (the agency now known as NP Digital).
