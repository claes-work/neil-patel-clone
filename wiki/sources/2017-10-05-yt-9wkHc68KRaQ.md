# 3 Ways to Sell More Products Using Affiliate Marketing | Try THIS With a Small Marketing Budget

## Summary
Neil explains how to sell more products without a large marketing budget by leaning on affiliate marketing, where you pay other people to promote your product and only pay them when they drive a lead or a sale. He walks through three moves: first, set a bounty that is high enough to genuinely incentivize affiliates but capped below your hard product cost so you still profit; second, study competitors' affiliate pages and aim to match or beat their offers, dropping the bounty only when the math forces it; and third, optimize your landing pages with Crazy Egg (scroll tracking, click tracking, and A/B testing) so higher conversion rates let you both make more sales and, if you convert better than competitors, pay a lower bounty per affiliate.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-05, Neil states:
- The simplest way to sell more is to scale marketing spend, but that is unrealistic for most people, so affiliate marketing is the alternative (as of 2017-10-05).
- Affiliate marketing means paying others to market your product, and you only pay them per lead or sale you choose which (as of 2017-10-05).
- The key is to incentivize affiliates with a nice bounty for every sale or lead they drive (as of 2017-10-05).
- You must first know your product cost: if a $200 product has a $100 hard cost, you cannot pay an affiliate more than $100, and paying the full $100 leaves you breaking even with no profit (as of 2017-10-05).
- Offering too little (e.g. $10) will not incentivize affiliates to push your offer over a competitor's, so the bounty must feel like a lot of money to them (as of 2017-10-05).
- You should inspect competitors' affiliate pages to see what they offer, then aim to match or beat it, giving a lower bounty only when your numbers do not allow beating it (as of 2017-10-05).
- Optimizing landing pages with Crazy Egg reveals how far people scroll, where they click, and where they get stuck, and supports A/B testing to lift conversions (as of 2017-10-05).
- A better-converting landing page lets you pay a lower bounty, because an affiliate driving 100 visitors that yields three sales on your optimized page gets a better ROI than one sale on a competitor's page (as of 2017-10-05).
