# Domain Authority vs Google PageRank

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that domain authority is a more useful metric to optimize for than Google PageRank because PageRank updates too infrequently to be a reliable feedback signal for SEO work. He explains what each metric is, why PageRank's slow refresh makes it a poor progress indicator, and recommends tracking domain authority (via Open Site Explorer or Ahrefs) as a leading indicator that rankings and traffic will improve. Note: the source ledger title ("Get More Visitors And Sales - Free Webinar") is mislabeled; the actual transcript is this SEO lesson on domain authority versus PageRank.

## Key claims
- Google PageRank is a 0-to-10 score whose algorithm was created by Google co-founder Larry Page; a 0 signals a non-authoritative site and a 10 signals a highly authoritative one (Google itself being a 10) (as of 2017-01-18).
- PageRank's core weakness is that it is updated infrequently — potentially only every six months to a year — so it cannot tell an SEO whether recent changes are helping or hurting quickly enough to be actionable.
- The truest measure of success is whether rankings and traffic are rising, but those also take a long time to move (Neil says it took him six months to a year and a half to rank page-one for terms like "online marketing" and "SEO" on NeilPatel.com).
- Domain authority served as Neil's leading indicator: it began climbing steadily before his rankings did, signaling he was on the right track.
- Domain authority runs on a 0-to-100 scale; higher is better. Two main tools report it: Open Site Explorer (free but usage-limited, owned by Moz) and Ahrefs (paid). Neil prefers Ahrefs because it updates more frequently.
- Link-building tactic: pursue links from sites whose domain authority is higher than your own, which raises your rankings and traffic at a faster pace (as of 2017-01-18).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Google PageRank is a number from zero to 10 and the algorithm for Google PageRank was created by one of their founders, Larry Page.
