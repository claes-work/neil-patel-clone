# How to Make Sure Algorithm Changes Don't Destroy Your Business

## Summary

Neil argues that Google, Facebook, and other platform algorithm updates can destroy a business overnight — and that one company's traffic loss is usually a competitor's gain — so marketers must algorithm-proof themselves with five tactics. First, **don't put all your eggs in one basket**: build presence one platform at a time (start where your ideal customer already is), but pursue an **omnichannel approach** in the long run so that one platform's decline can't sink you; what works on one social network or search engine typically transfers to another, so replicate winners and drop losers. Second, **when a tactic sounds too good to be true it probably is** — automated follow/unfollow bots, link-building schemes, and automated content/traffic generation get over-used and penalized; algorithms "don't forgive and forget," and recovery is far harder than doing it right the first time. Third, **you don't work for the algorithm, you help humans**: focus on what your audience wants, drive genuine engagement (comments, replies), and avoid controversial/polarizing content. Fourth, **always adopt new platform features early** (Google FAQ snippets, Google My Business, Facebook Live, Instagram Stories, Facebook Groups) because platforms give extra algorithmic reach to promote them. Fifth, **keep testing new formats** — study "unicorn" content outperforming yours with tools like Ubersuggest, don't just follow the saturated bandwagon, and listen to platform earnings calls and roadmaps for an edge.
