# 5 Ways to Market Your Business and Generate Instant Sales Without Ads

## Summary
Neil gives five no/low-budget tactics for fast sales (as of 2022-05-23), framed
against SEO (too slow, 6+ months) and paid ads (too expensive with no budget).
(1) Affiliate programs (Hotmart, ClickBank, JVZoo) — pay per sale/lead, set a payout
below your margin so you keep some profit even after paying the affiliate. (2) Leave
helpful comments/responses on other sites (TechCrunch, Mashable, competitor blogs) —
Neil says he did this ~1 hour/day early in his career and it generated 1-2 customers a
month; he credits the tactic to **Pete Cashmore of Mashable**, consistent with the
existing [[2019-07-22-yt-LdnWx49LUJc]] attribution. (3) "Talk crap, but nicely" — write
a public teardown blog post critiquing a company's marketing, then cold-email their
investors and CEO/execs (sourced via Crunchbase); gives a **new named example, RIYA**
(a photo app company), where this got him hired on the spot. Adds a funding-size rule
of thumb: works well on companies that raised under $10M, still works up to ~$30-40M,
but **doesn't work well above ~$100M raised**. This is a new concrete instance of the
existing Crunchbase-cold-outreach-teardown playbook
([[2019-05-23-yt-YymWhauqjoA]], extended [[2021-10-14-yt-Xz_W96Zmz3s]]). (4) Run joint
webinars with adjacent (non-competing) companies with an overlapping audience — names a
recent one with TrustPilot, says NP Digital runs these monthly and each nets 1-3
customers "like clockwork." (5) Publish YouTube videos 5-10 minutes long, keyword-
optimized, with an SRT transcript uploaded; reiterates the first-24-hours virality
mechanic (push notifications/email/social pushed hard in the first 24 hours to win
YouTube's algorithm), a recurrence of [[2022-04-30-yt-kgk5OKV82YY]].

## Key claims
- Affiliate payouts should be set below your own margin per sale so a sale still nets
  profit after paying the affiliate (as of 2022-05-23).
- Neil self-reports spending over an hour a day on blog/forum commenting early in his
  career, generating 1-2 customers/month; credits the tactic to Pete Cashmore of
  Mashable (self-reported, as of 2022-05-23; consistent with
  [[2019-07-22-yt-LdnWx49LUJc]]).
- New named example of the Crunchbase-teardown cold-outreach playbook: a public
  critique blog post about **RIYA** (a photo app) plus a direct outbound email to its
  investors got Neil hired immediately (self-reported, as of 2022-05-23).
- Funding-size rule of thumb for the cold-outreach playbook: works well under ~$10M
  raised, still works up to ~$30-40M, but not well above ~$100M raised — a specific
  upper-bound figure not previously recorded (self-reported, as of 2022-05-23).
- NP Digital runs joint webinars with non-competing, audience-adjacent companies (e.g.
  TrustPilot) at least monthly, self-reported to reliably net 1-3 customers each (as of
  2022-05-23).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> When I first started out in this world of marketing, I spent over an hour a day responding to people who needed help and it generated me one to two customers every single month.

> I ended up breaking down how they were doing all their marketing wrong in a blog post, and then I sent an outbound email to their investors... I even sent it out to the CEO and exec and I got a call right away being like I want to hire you to fix this.
