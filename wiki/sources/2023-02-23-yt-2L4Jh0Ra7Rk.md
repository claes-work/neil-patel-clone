# What I Wish I Knew When I Started My Blog

## Summary
Retrospective "what I'd change" list from his own blogging history, mixing biography
with tactical advice. (1) Pick a niche sized to expand into a big Total Addressable
Market (TAM) rather than "the riches are in the niches" — self-reports he originally
blogged narrowly about conversion optimization before broadening into marketing, and
explicitly ranks TAM size: marketing > paid advertising > SEO > conversion-rate
optimization > a micro-niche like "best man speeches" (which he says can't expand).
(2) Don't limit distribution to one platform — cites a Harvard Business Review study
of 46,000 shoppers finding ~73% prefer multi-channel shopping, and self-reports
posting to Instagram Stories, Twitter, LinkedIn, Facebook, and even smaller networks
like Reddit regardless of perceived popularity ("something is better than nothing").
(3) Always link back to the blog post from social platforms. (4) Use visuals — cites
32% of marketers believe visuals are the most important content type, self-reports
adding infographics later in his blogging career boosted social shares, backlinks,
and SEO. (5) Update existing content instead of only publishing new content
(Wikipedia analogy). (6) Don't publish near-duplicate posts on the same topic
(confuses Google's ranking signal) — one detailed post beats several thin variants.
(7) Be consistent — self-reports that on his earlier blog "Crick Sprout" [sic;
QuickSprout], a single one-month gap in publishing took more than three months to
recover lost traffic.

## Key claims
- States a TAM-based niche-selection philosophy explicitly ranking marketing > paid

  framework, dated 2023-02-23).
- Cites a Harvard Business Review study of 46,000 shoppers: ~73% prefer shopping

- Cites a stat: 32% of marketers believe visuals are the most important content type
