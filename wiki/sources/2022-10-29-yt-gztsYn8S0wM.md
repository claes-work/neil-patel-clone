# 7 Tips For Selecting a Performance Marketing Agency

## Summary
A buyer's-guide video for choosing a marketing/performance agency, framed around his
20+ years in the industry and NP Digital as a recurring example. Seven tips: (1) look
for agencies with awards, since awards are judged by peer marketers and are hard to
win — cites NP Digital winning "Agency of the Year by Media Post" and "dozens of
awards" despite being "only... around for five years"; (2) require industry-specific
experience (analogy: don't hire an ear-doctor for a nose job); (3) always call
references, even glowing ones, since an occasional unhappy reference can save "a ton
of pain and money"; (4) hire based on the specific people/team assigned to your
account, not the agency's brand name or founder — explicitly warns against hiring NP
Digital "because of me" ["that's a big mistake"], reprising a "vet the people, not the
brand" stance seen elsewhere in the corpus; (5) prefer agencies that are both
established (3-4+ years) AND still growing, reasoning that longevity without growth
signals weak referral generation from unhappy clients; (6) insist on a dedicated (or
at least semi-dedicated) account manager who advocates internally for your account's
resources; (7) favor agencies offering an omnichannel service mix (Google, Bing,
Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Twitter, Amazon, Walmart, "even BeReal") since
no single channel reliably grows a business amid constant algorithm change. Closes
with an NP Digital plug reiterating the "Marketing Agency of the Year" claim and
dedicated-account-manager offering.
