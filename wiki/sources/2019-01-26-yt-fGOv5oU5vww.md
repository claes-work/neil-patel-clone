# Don't Use Paid Ads: My #1 Organic Marketing Strategy

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that while paid advertising can deliver a positive ROI and is worth doing when profitable, the majority of search traffic goes to organic listings rather than paid ones, so he prefers an organic "hack" for driving traffic. His core method: find pages you already rank for (via Google Search Console), pull long-tail variations of those keywords in Ubersuggest, then rewrite and expand the existing content in depth to naturally incorporate the relevant long-tail phrases. He stresses that keyword stuffing does not work and creates a bad user experience — the content must be genuinely adjusted and expanded so it still flows. He adds two supporting tactics: placing popular long-tail phrases into the title tag and meta description, and linking out to other relevant sites then notifying those site owners to earn social shares and backlinks. He illustrates the payoff with his own digital-marketing guide, which he says grew from roughly 9,000 to 17,000 monthly visitors after adding long-tail phrases.

## Key claims
- The majority of search traffic goes to organic listings rather than paid listings (as of 2019-01-26).
- Paid advertising is worthwhile if it produces a positive ROI, but organic is his preferred primary channel (as of 2019-01-26).
- To find what you rank for, log into Google Search Console, which lists all the terms you rank for; identify the most popular ones and the page they point to (as of 2019-01-26).
- Put a head term you already rank for (e.g. "digital marketing") into Ubersuggest to surface long-tail variations, which it lists in order of popularity (as of 2019-01-26).
- Rewriting existing ranking content to be more detailed and include relevant long-tail phrases will get you ranking for those phrases within 30 to 60 days at the latest (as of 2019-01-26).
- Keyword stuffing does not work and harms user experience, causing people to bounce; the content must be genuinely adjusted, not just have phrases inserted (as of 2019-01-26).
- Placing some popular long-tail keywords in the title tag and meta description makes it easier and faster to rank for them (as of 2019-01-26).
- Linking out to other relevant sites and notifying those owners earns social shares and backlinks, which boost rankings for both the main and secondary terms (as of 2019-01-26).
- Neil says he applied this to his own digital-marketing guide (~10,000 words) and grew it from over 9,000 monthly visitors to 17,000 (as of 2019-01-26).
- Neil asks viewers not to replicate the tactic in the online-marketing vertical because he does not want more competitors (as of 2019-01-26).
