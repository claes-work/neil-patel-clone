# The Best LinkedIn Growth Strategy For 2025 (Full Course)

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out an end-to-end organic system for growing high-paying leads on LinkedIn without paid ads, aimed at founders, consultants, and coaches building a profitable personal brand. His central premise is that LinkedIn is not saturated but "starving for content": the platform generates enormous weekly impression volume while only a small fraction of users post, leaving an open lane for anyone who publishes consistently to become a top voice in their niche. The system has several sequenced parts. First, apply Russell Brunson's "Dream 100" concept in reverse — rather than pursuing your 100 ideal targets directly (who will likely ignore you), connect with the people in their networks who share your topical interests, so the algorithm surfaces your content to them and connections compound toward the Dream 100. Second, engage before you post: leave detailed, thoughtful comments on the content of the people you've strategically connected with to build authority and algorithmic recognition. Third, source content ideas from other platforms (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram) using tools like Answer the Public, because LinkedIn has limited trend data and other networks reveal emerging waves earlier. Fourth, engineer each post for the algorithm: a short punchy hook that triggers the "see more" click, curiosity-driven structure, and a thoughtful closing question that provokes meaningful comments — then reply to every comment to feed the algorithm. Finally, post at peak times aligned to the audience's time zone rather than your own. Neil closes by recommending viewers build a personal brand to amplify results.

## Key claims
- LinkedIn is not saturated but "starving for content"; despite that, it generates over 9 billion weekly impressions while only 1% of users post content weekly, leaving a massive untapped opportunity (as of 2025-05-23).
- Unlike Instagram or TikTok, LinkedIn's feed is not oversaturated, so good content travels further, faster, and lasts longer (as of 2025-05-23).
- Anyone posting consistently can quickly become a top voice in their niche and attract clients without paid ads (as of 2025-05-23).
- The "Dream 100" concept (from Russell Brunson) should be applied indirectly on LinkedIn: instead of connecting with your ideal 100 targets (who will likely ignore you), connect with the people in their networks, which makes your Dream 100 more likely to see your content and connect with you (as of 2025-05-23).
- Strategic connections must be within your space; the algorithm shows your content to your connections, so connecting with people whose interests do not match your topic produces low engagement and hurts you algorithmically (as of 2025-05-23).
- The right approach is to examine your Dream 100's connections and connect only with those interested in similar content, building organic impressions instead of relying on cold messaging (as of 2025-05-23).
- Leaving detailed comments and replying to others demonstrates thoughtful insight, builds authority, and brings platform recognition — and this should be done consistently before you post your own content (as of 2025-05-23).
- Because most LinkedIn users don't post, LinkedIn has limited trend data; using a tool like Answer the Public to find up-and-coming content on YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram and adapting it for LinkedIn keeps content fresh and timely, making it easier to go viral (as of 2025-05-23).
- The most important part of a LinkedIn post for the algorithm is a short one-or-two-line intro that naturally triggers the "see more" link; each click signals engagement and curiosity to the algorithm, prompting wider distribution (as of 2025-05-23).
- Posts need powerful hooks, clear and concise thoughts, and curiosity that makes people want to click "see more" (as of 2025-05-23).
- Ending a post with a thoughtful question (not a yes/no one) drives meaningful comments; responding to every comment brings people back and gives the algorithm more fuel to show the content widely (as of 2025-05-23).
- The LinkedIn algorithm does not care how often you post; it cares about posting at the right time, based on the audience's time zone rather than yours, aligned to the ideal customer profile's active/engaging hours (as of 2025-05-23).
- Posting consistently at peak times maximizes exposure, builds audience trust, and turns visibility into qualified leads (as of 2025-05-23).
- To further explode LinkedIn growth, building a personal brand is recommended (as of 2025-05-23).
