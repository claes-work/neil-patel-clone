# 6 Reasons Why Your Site Will NEVER Rank (STOP Doing This)

## Summary
Neil gives six reasons a page fails to rank, citing Ahrefs and Authority Hacker studies
throughout: (1) no backlinks — backlinks correlate strongly with search traffic, and
amazing content/tools/products/PR earns them naturally; (2) not leveraging search
snippets/FAQ schema markup, which Authority Hacker found present in up to 75% of top
spots for high-volume keywords (featured snippets alone are 20% of results); (3) the
page targets a topic without enough search volume, even with 200+ referring domains —
he recommends Ubersuggest for keyword research; (4) not using target keywords in meta
tags, H1s, and title tags, which correlates with ranking; (5) no HTTPS, now a de facto
SEO standard; (6) a poor Google Lighthouse score, checkable via Ubersuggest's site audit
report. He frames user experience/page speed as feeding into Google's broader ranking
signals.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-01, Neil said backlinks show a linear correlation with search traffic
  (citing an Ahrefs study), and Authority Hacker found backlinks the most strongly
  correlated factor for SEO success.
- As of 2020-02-01, Neil said you don't need to proactively do outreach for backlinks if
  you're producing amazing content, tools, products/services, or getting press —
  backlinks accrue naturally.
- As of 2020-02-01, Neil said search snippets appear in up to 75% of top spots for
  high-volume keywords (per Authority Hacker), with featured snippets alone making up
  20% of all results, and FAQ schema markup helped him rank for terms like "digital
  marketing."
- As of 2020-02-01, Neil said Ahrefs found that even pages with 200+ referring domains
  won't get traffic if they don't target keywords with real search volume — use
  Ubersuggest for keyword research.
- As of 2020-02-01, Neil cited an Authority Hacker study showing a strong correlation
  between keywords in H1/title tags and ranking.
- As of 2020-02-01, Neil said HTTPS used to be optional but is now a de facto SEO
  standard.
- As of 2020-02-01, Neil said over 90% of page-one Google results have high average
  Google Lighthouse scores (per Authority Hacker), and recommended Ubersuggest's site
  audit report (pulls Lighthouse data) to check page speed.
- As of 2020-02-01, Neil said Google looks at overall user experience signals (bounce,
  engagement) regardless of whether the underlying issue is on-page SEO, content, or
  load time.
