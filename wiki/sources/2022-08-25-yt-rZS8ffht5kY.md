# The Easiest Way to Make a WordPress Blog SEO Friendly

## Summary
Neil argues plugins (Yoast, All in One SEO) solve only surface SEO; the two real blockers
to a WordPress blog ranking well are topic-duplication and content staleness, neither
fixable by a plugin. For duplication: use Google Analytics to find near-duplicate posts
on the same topic, merge them into one authoritative post, 301-redirect the losers, and
update internal links. For staleness: cites Wikipedia (7th most popular site per
SimilarWeb) as the model of continual updating, claims 98% of NP Digital's incoming
clients had never updated old content before working with the agency, and recommends
using Google Search Console to find pages that have dropped in traffic as the priority
list for refreshes — then benchmarking against the pages currently outranking them.

## Key claims
- Identifies topic-cannibalization (multiple posts targeting the same near-identical
  keyword over time, e.g. three different "SEO for beginners" posts) as a primary
  reason WordPress blogs underperform on SEO, unfixable via plugins (as of 2022-08-25).
- Recommends the fix: find the most-trafficked of the overlapping posts via Google
  Analytics, merge the others into it, 301-redirect, and update internal links (as of
  2022-08-25).
- Self-reports (via NP Digital client data) that 98% of the agency's clients had never
  updated their old content prior to engagement (as of 2022-08-25, self-reported).
- Recommends using Google Search Console to identify traffic-dropping pages as the
  priority order for content refreshes, benchmarked against currently-outranking
  competitor pages (as of 2022-08-25).
- Repeats the Wikipedia-freshness analogy (7th most popular site per SimilarWeb,
  continual updates) already present elsewhere in the corpus.
