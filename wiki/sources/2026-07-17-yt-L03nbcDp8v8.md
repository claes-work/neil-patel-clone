# Your Software Is Now Your Best Marketing Channel (Marketing School)

## Summary
Co-hosted Marketing School episode on the thesis that in the AI/vibe-coding era your **product/software is itself a marketing channel** — the software becomes the lead magnet that a PDF or quiz used to be. Eric Siu opens and frames the thesis (software becoming marketing; every feature launch a "marketable moment"; prototype to 60–70% then hand to devs), and narrates his own operations throughout: ClickFlow's free API/MCP tier, his "AEO SEO autopilot system 2026," Single Grain / SingleBrain client workflows, his own speaking/YPO/"YO Global AI Summit" plans, and a personal 5-minute-journal / two-kids-by-42 goal story. Those Eric-operating passages are quarantined below.

Neil's persona-relevant contribution is a **qualified agreement plus a real dissent** on the thesis, concentrated in four threads: (1) he pioneered product-as-marketing ~7–8 years ago at NP Digital — bought Ubersuggest for ~$120k, then deliberately spent millions on it over years, and crucially released product **for free** when competitors charged, because "free goes a very long way" and is what makes it shareable/marketable — with the caveat that paid "marketable moment" features work far less well; (2) product-as-marketing still works but **"not as well as it used to"** because everyone builds product now, so it's noisy and competitive; (3) his own current needle-movers are NOT product releases but **conferences/events** ("three-for-one": speak + sponsor + side events + branding), plus content, podcasting, partnerships — with the honest caveat that events have a very slow payback (no money back in year one) and he speaks mainly for branding, not enterprise pickup; (4) a hiring/comp position — he puts **no premium specifically on "AI talent," a premium on all good talent**, because "the more you can produce, the more you can earn," and a pragmatic anti-vision-board take (the value people credit to vision boards is really just effort/focus). These are solid, on-domain, reusable Neil positions that **refine** his known product-led-growth and channel-mix views rather than introduce a new named framework, so this is **NOT flagged ★ L3-candidate** — though the "free is the real lever / product-as-marketing is decaying / my biggest driver is now conferences" nuance is worth carrying into synthesis.

## Key claims (dated, speaker-tagged, paraphrase)
- Neil pioneered product-as-marketing ~7–8 years ago at NP Digital: the whole premise was to release software; the first was Ubersuggest, bought for ~$120k, then he deliberately spent millions on the product over years (not instantly) because it was gaining traction. (Neil, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship / ai-marketing.
- Everything is far cheaper now because of AI, so building product-as-marketing is much more accessible than when he started. (Neil, 2026-07-17) — ai-marketing.
- The real lever is FREE, not feature cadence: NP Digital didn't just ship "marketable moments" — they released product for free while competitors charged, which made it far more marketable because everyone wants to share something free ("whether it's better, worse, or the same, free goes a very long way"). (Neil, 2026-07-17) — content-marketing / ai-marketing.
- Paid "marketable moment" features underperform: releasing a new feature every week works much less well if people have to pay more for it — you can still use it to generate buzz, but "we just see it not working as well." (Neil, 2026-07-17) — ai-marketing.
- Product-as-marketing still works and Neil believes it works well, but "not as well as it used to" — because so many people build product now, it's very competitive and much harder to get traction/noise than it used to be. (Neil, 2026-07-17) — ai-marketing.
- Neil's current lead-driving channel mix: events, awards, sponsoring and speaking at events, holding their own small events, creating content, podcasting, other interviews, getting their team to speak more, and partnerships (hiring people specifically to ink out more partnerships). (Neil, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- If he had to pick the single biggest needle-mover it's **conferences**, not shipping more free Ubersuggest/AnswerThePublic features — a conference is a "three-for-one": you speak, you sponsor, and you throw side events, plus amazing branding (people hear your corporate brand even if you don't pick up revenue). (Neil, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- The problem with events is a very slow payback period: "we do not make our money back in year one" after spending. (Neil, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Neil spoke at INBOUND for years primarily for **branding**, not enterprise pickup — didn't win enterprise from it but did pick up a few (smaller, still-current) clients from a talk ~2 years ago; still worth it for branding because the stage and audience are large. (attributed Neil — see notes, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Neil got cut from HubSpot INBOUND (now rebranded "unbound"); declined the offered panel slot because a panel doesn't get you remembered / doesn't drive revenue. (attributed Neil — see notes, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Employee-generated speakers (a company's own team members on stage) are more effective long-run than outside celebrity speakers if you're actually trying to generate revenue. (attributed Neil — see notes, 2026-07-17) — content-marketing.
- Hiring/comp: NP Digital does not put a premium specifically on "AI-pilled" talent — "We're putting a premium on all good talent, whether it's AI or non-AI." They hire on need + what the person can provide, and negotiate off that. (Neil, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Comp is production-based and "black and white": the more you can produce the more you can earn. He'd pay a lot more for a marketer who does 10x with equal/better quality, or a closer who closes 5x AND whose metrics hold (low churn, didn't over-promise to close). (Neil, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Talent quality is age/tenure-agnostic: doesn't matter if someone is an intern or 50 years into their career — "Good is good. Crap is crap." (Neil, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Anti-vision-board / pragmatic mindset stance: he doesn't believe in the vision-board concept as such — mindset is a problem when it gets in the way of doing logical things and just working. What people credit to a vision board is really **effort**: writing/focusing on a goal makes you more likely to pursue it, but the outcome comes from pushing harder, not the board (runner analogy: writing "fast runner" changes nothing if you never run). (Neil, 2026-07-17) — agency-entrepreneurship.
