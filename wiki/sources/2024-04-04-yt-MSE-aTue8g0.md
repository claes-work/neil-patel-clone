# The ROI of AI in sales, The ideal content length for Facebook, What's wrong with our YouTube? & more

## Summary
Three segments, undiarized. (1) A recap of a Gong Labs case study: sales conversations mentioning AI are up 2,200% since ChatGPT's Nov-2022 launch, AI-generated sales emails up 464%, win rates up 50% for completed AI-assisted to-dos and a further 26% with AI-informed deal strategy (based on 1M+ sales opportunities across ~1,400+ orgs using Gong's AI features, deals lasting 7+ days). Framed as directional evidence that AI-assisted (not AI-replaced) selling improves win rates. (2) A joint analysis of ~2.38M pieces of social content to derive ideal content length per platform/format: Instagram Reels ~11-17s (entertainment) / 38-51s (educational), carousels 7-10 slides (both slide 1 AND 2 need a hook), LinkedIn short video ~40-46s, long-form ~3-4 min (despite a 10-min cap), motivational text ~20-38 words, educational text ~150-291 words; X (Twitter) motivational ~14-21 words, entertainment ~9-35 words, educational ~173-200 words; TikTok entertainment ~18-31s, educational ~42-54s; YouTube Shorts entertainment ~10-20s, educational ~28-57s; long-form YouTube best-performers average ~7 min 9 sec. Facebook had no clear pattern. A personal aside recounts a podcast guest's daughter telling him "Dad only likes to work," which the host says made him rethink always being on his phone, followed by a family-dinner story where his own daughter asked him to not be on his phone at dinner — since then he doesn't use his phone at family dinners. (3) A closing riff on a conversation with a YouTube-growth advisor (helped grow "Jubilee" to 15M subs / ~5B views) about under-investing in thumbnail/headline concepting; the advisor's benchmark CTR targets were ~7-10% for consumer/B2C video and ~5-8% for more B2B-leaning content.
