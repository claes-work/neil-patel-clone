# Can You STILL Do Digital Marketing for Free? (The REAL Cost of Digital Marketing)

## Summary
Neil argues digital marketing can be done for free if you substitute time for money,
giving eight tactics: (1) give away something genuinely valuable (guides, courses,
assessments, proprietary reports) rather than just a blog post; (2) leverage third-party
audiences via co-hosted webinars/live sessions and roundups, taking an omnichannel
approach across blog, video, and podcast; (3) content marketing via Ubersuggest's
content-ideas report to find evergreen topics with proven traction; (4) push video
content across platforms and simulcast live video, noting his own YouTube channel gets
20-30%+ of monthly views from search; (5) podcasting — cites Nielsen that 51% of the US
population has listened to a podcast and 80% of listeners finish most of an episode,
and that his own Marketing School podcast (co-hosted with Eric Siu) gets over a million
downloads a month; (6) build a benefit-oriented Facebook group, keeping content
90% education / 10% promotion; (7) build an email list and use automation (Hello Bar
for list capture, push notifications via Subscribers); (8) use funnels instead of
hard-selling, illustrated via a dating analogy — build a relationship (content, email
sequences) before asking for the sale.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-13, Neil said his own YouTube channel gets more than 20% of its views
  from search in a typical month, and over 30% in good months.
- As of 2020-02-13, Neil cited Nielsen data that 51% of the US population has listened
  to a podcast, and that 80% of listeners who start an episode listen to almost the
  whole thing.
- As of 2020-02-13, Neil said his Marketing School podcast, co-hosted with Eric Siu,
  gets over one million downloads a month.
- As of 2020-02-13, Neil recommended Hello Bar (his own product) for free email-list
  capture via sliders and exit popups, and push-notification automation via the tool
  Subscribers.
- As of 2020-02-13, Neil said content mix on platforms like Facebook groups should be
  roughly 90% education/help and 10% product/service promotion, and that he blends
  promotion into educational content rather than separating it.
- As of 2020-02-13, Neil said there are over a billion blogs on the web (roughly one per
  seven people), making differentiated, non-generic content essential.
- As of 2020-02-13, Neil used a dating analogy for marketing funnels: going straight to
  "buy, buy, buy" without building a relationship first (via content, email sequences)
  underperforms compared to educating prospects before selling.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "What doesn't cost you money, will cost you time. You can do digital marketing for free, as long as you're willing to make a consistent investment in time and effort."

> "This strategy is so effective, that my Podcast marketing school that I co-host with Eric Siu, gets over one million downloads a month, literally, one million downloads a month."

> "Someone comes to your website, you're like, 'Marry me, marry me, give me your wallet. Spend, spend, spend.' That doesn't work as well as educating, getting to know people."

> "In other words, you want to continue to add value, and don't just put selling first, but put people first and help them out before you try to ask for anything."
