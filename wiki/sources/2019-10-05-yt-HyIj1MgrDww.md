# How to Leverage Instagram to Actually Make You More Money

## Summary

Neil argues that "likes and followers don't pay the bills" is only true if you use Instagram wrong, and lays out a monetization playbook. The foundation is to **build the account around one specific problem for one audience** — a focused community converts far better than a random audience or a "cool life / half-naked photos" influencer play, which he says no longer works. He tells creators to **differentiate their content** and experiment from day one (video vs. image, long vs. short captions, live, Stories, educational vs. entertaining vs. curated), **double down on what works, cut what doesn't, and retest every few months** because Instagram constantly changes its algorithm. Monetization/growth tactics: **run ads to your top-performing content** to compound reach; **run giveaways, promotions, and challenges** (giveaways only work if the prize is valuable and niche-relevant; a challenge like a fitness "30-day, lose 5 pounds" journey drives follows); attach **rules** to giveaways/challenges (follow the page, tag someone, click the bio link to your site to capture email so you can notify winners and keep selling). He also recommends **sponsored posts on other relevant influencer accounts** (never random ones), **micro-influencers over celebrities** — citing a man who paid Kris Jenner ~$75,000 and made less than $75,000 back, a loss after shipping and overhead — and **affiliate products** sourced from affiliate networks (JVZoo, CJ Affiliate, ShareASale, etc.) rather than Google. He points to influence.co for finding targeted influencers and closes by pitching NP Digital.
