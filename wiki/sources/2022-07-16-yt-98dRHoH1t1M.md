# Why Social Media Marketing Doesn't Work (For Most People)

## Summary
Neil Patel argues that social media marketing fails for most people not from a follower-count
deficit but from optimizing for the wrong metric: chasing followers with broad, generic content
instead of chasing leads/customers with niche content. He gives a first-person example: his own
social team pushes him to post more generic content because it grows followers faster, but he
resists because generic growth produced "a whopping zero" extra leads, clients, or revenue in his
own repeated testing. He contrasts his most-viewed content — lifestyle material (fancy clothes,
lessons from 100+ first-class flights) — with his SEO/marketing-tips content, which gets far fewer
views but converts a portion of viewers into leads and customers. The takeaway: build followers, but
target content at the *ideal customer*, not the masses, or the follower count is a vanity metric that
doesn't pay.

## Key claims
- Growing followers via generic, mass-appeal content produced zero incremental leads, clients, or
  revenue for his agency, despite testing "hundreds of times" (as of 2022-07-16, self-reported,
  unaudited methodology).
- His highest-viewed content is lifestyle-oriented (fancy-clothes spending, lessons from over 100
  first-class flights), but this content does not convert into agency leads (as of 2022-07-16).
- His SEO/marketing-tips content gets fewer views and likes than lifestyle content, but converts a
  portion of viewers into leads and some into customers (as of 2022-07-16).
- Core prescription: social media strategy should target content at the ideal customer segment, not
  optimize for follower-count as a vanity metric (as of 2022-07-16).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> Social media marketing just doesn't work, or at least for most people. And no, it's not because you don't have enough followers, instead, it doesn't work because you only focus on getting more followers.

> It gets us a whopping zero extra leads clients and revenue. And I've tested this out hundreds of times, literally.

> The big mistake that people make, it's that they create content that focuses on the masses so they can boost this vanity metric AKA follower account, instead of focusing on creating content that targets their ideal customer.
