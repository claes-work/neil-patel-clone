# Marketing Hack to Get Big Customers, Social Media Democracy, Sam Sulek's Million Views & More

## Summary
A shared favorite lead-gen tactic: run a podcast/interview show specifically to reach
"whale" prospects — cold-message well-known executives on LinkedIn (higher response rate
than email), interview them with no vanity-metric pitch required, and close with an ask
for an introduction to the right person on their team. The hosts trade origin-story
anecdotes for why this works (a friend, Jason Nazar, who built relationships via podcast
interviews that eventually led to a company sale to ZoomInfo; early fireside-chat
networking at the Milken Institute in Santa Monica; the "Growth Everywhere"-turned-
"Leveling Up" podcast's founding rationale of interviewing marketing leaders, inspired by
Andrew Warner) — the un-diarized transcript makes exact speaker turns hard to pin down
here (see Attribution notes), so this whole origin-story stretch is treated as uncertain
rather than confidently assigned. What is clearly Neil-attributed (he is addressed by
name) is his own current experience: people pitch him ("hey Neil, do you want to be on a
podcast") and he says yes without ever asking about audience size or ratings. The episode
then pivots to a recap of a Chris Do appearance on "Leveling Up" (Eric's channel) about the
"democratization" of social platforms — subscriber counts no longer matter, algorithms
now surface content purely on quality/engagement — illustrated by Sam Sulek's YouTube
fitness channel getting millions of views on a relatable, low-production format. Neil ties
this to his own content-quality data: across billions of tracked keywords (Ubersuggest +
AnswerThePublic), 94.29% of web pages get zero organic Google traffic, and of 1,511
marketers surveyed, ~922 create AI or AI+human content (i.e. not fully manual) — his
conclusion is the bar for both SEO and social content keeps rising and only genuinely
new/authentic content wins attention.
