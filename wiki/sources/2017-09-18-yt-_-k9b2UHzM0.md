# 5 WAYS TO MAKE MONEY BLOGGING (Simple Ways to Monetize Your Blog's Audience and Make Money Online)

## Summary
Neil lays out five ways a blogger with existing traffic can monetize an audience that isn't yet making money. Starting with the easiest, he recommends Google AdSense (display ads placed throughout text and sidebar), then affiliate programs (promoting products via networks like ClickBank or JVZoo and earning per sale), selling digital products such as e-books and courses (delivered via a tool like Kajabi and best sold through a webinar funnel), selling your own ad space directly to advertisers to cut out the AdSense middleman fee, and selling physical products (citing Legion Athletics, a fitness blog selling supplements that he says does over $10 million a year on blog traffic). Neil advises combining one or two methods (up to three at most) rather than all five, which he argues would overwhelm visitors.

## Key claims
As of 2017-09-18, Neil states:
- AdSense is the easiest monetization method — Google-provided ads placed throughout text and sidebar earn money on each click (as of 2017-09-18).
- Affiliate programs let you promote a product/service and get paid per sale, with networks like ClickBank and JVZoo offering offers to sign up for and promote on your blog (as of 2017-09-18).
- Selling digital products (e-books, courses) offers near-infinite possibilities; a tool like Kajabi can deliver them, and the best selling approach is typically a webinar funnel that converts registrants into leads and customers (as of 2017-09-18).
- Selling your own ad space directly to advertisers removes the AdSense middleman fee; most prospects will say no, but hitting enough people yields buyers and more money kept (as of 2017-09-18).
- Selling physical products works well with strong products people want — Legion Athletics runs a fitness blog selling proteins, supplements and vitamins and does over $10 million a year on blog traffic (as of 2017-09-18).
- Bloggers can combine multiple methods but should avoid using all five, as it overwhelms visitors; one or two (up to three) is recommended (as of 2017-09-18).
