# Should You Put Categories in Your Blog URL?

## Summary
A short on-page-SEO video in which Neil Patel answers whether to include the category in a
blog's URL structure. His answer is "it depends on blog size." Small blogs (under ~100
pages) shouldn't bother. Large blogs planning thousands of posts across many topics should,
because the category segment tells Google what the post is about — e.g.
blog.com/mens-fashion/name-of-post, or his own neilpatel.com/conversion-rate-optimization/
post. He adds a slug-formatting rule: separate multi-word category words with single
hyphens (mens-fashion), never double hyphens (mens--fashion). Including the category
reinforces the post's topic/keywords to Google so it can rank the post correctly. Exception:
if a blog only ever covers one narrow topic (only men's fashion, with few sub-sections),
categories in the URL aren't necessary.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-26, Neil states:
- Whether to put categories in a blog URL depends on the blog's size — there is no
  universal answer (as of 2017-08-26).
- Small blogs (under ~100 pages) shouldn't put categories in the URL; large blogs aiming
  for 1,000+ posts across many topics should (as of 2017-08-26).
- The category segment tells Google what the post is about, reinforcing its topic/keywords
  so Google can rank it correctly — e.g. blog.com/mens-fashion/post or
  neilpatel.com/conversion-rate-optimization/post (as of 2017-08-26).
- Slug rule: join multi-word category words with a single hyphen (mens-fashion), never a
  double hyphen (mens--fashion) (as of 2017-08-26).
- Exception: a blog that only ever covers one narrow topic with few sub-sections doesn't
  need categories in the URL (as of 2017-08-26).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The short answer is, I don't know. And what I mean by this is I don't know your blog and the size of it."

> "By putting the category it tells Google what this blog post is all about ... so that way they know where to properly rank the post and what keywords and phrases, and what topic the post should be ranked around."

> "You don't want to put a double dash in between category words. For example, you don't want mens--fashion. You can do mens-fashion."
