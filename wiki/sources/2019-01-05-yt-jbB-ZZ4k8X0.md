# The Best SEO Tools for Beginners | Neil Patel

## Summary

A round-up of beginner-friendly SEO tools. (1) **Moz** — simple, pretty UI that audits
on-page errors (duplicate content, broken title tags), monitors the site, and flags which
issues are urgent to fix first. (2) **Ubersuggest** — find more keywords: take head terms
you rank for and pull long-tail (3–5 word) variations, then integrate them naturally into
the ranking page (expand content where needed, don't just stuff), and you start ranking
for the long-tails within ~30 days. (3) **BuzzSumo** — enter keywords to see the most
socially shared articles in your space, so you model what people actually read/share
rather than writing content nobody wants. (4) **Keywords Everywhere** — a free Chrome
extension showing related keywords and search volume right on Google searches. (5)
**Ahrefs** and its **Link Intersect** — find sites linking to your three competitors but
not you (sites linking to multiple competitors are the receptive outreach targets). (6)
**Google Trends** — track your brand size vs. competitors over time and by region, since
Google keeps pushing brands; grow it via an omni-channel approach (content, links, keyword
expansion). (7) **AnswerThePublic** (in transcript "Answer The Public") — surfaces the
questions people ask Google around a keyword (Quora-style), so you can answer them on your
own site and capture that traffic.
