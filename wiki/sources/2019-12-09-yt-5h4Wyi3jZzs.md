# STOP Paying for SEO Tools - The Only 4 Tools You Need to Rank #1 in Google

## Summary
Solo tool round-up arguing that the proliferation of SEO tools (from dozens when he started to
hundreds now) has made paid tools optional. Neil names four free tools and how he uses each.
(1) **Google Search Console** — compare the last 30 days against six months ago and find the
pages with the biggest drop in impressions and clicks; those are the pages to update. His
refresh method: add the latest information, embed relevant YouTube videos (which lifts time on
site and engagement, and therefore rankings), cut fluff left over from keyword stuffing, and
compare against the pages currently ranking for the keyword to find what they have that you
don't; expect traffic on those pages to recover within ~30 days. He frames this against a
content-glut argument — over a billion blogs, roughly one per seven people. (2) **Structured
Data Markup Helper** — structured data differs by site type (local, national, e-commerce, lead
gen/consulting), so use the helper to get star ratings, rich snippets and breadcrumbs that lift
CTR. (3) **Google Trends** — track your brand's search volume against competitors, because
Google and the social platforms are combating fake news by favoring brands they consider less
likely to publish it; grow brand search using Hello Bar for email capture, free Mailchimp for
sends, and Subscribers for push notifications. (4) **Ubersuggest** (his own tool) — he claims
it offers 80%+ of the features of Moz, SEMrush and Ahrefs for free: SEO/site audit report
(load time, title tags, meta descriptions, code/URL/redirect errors, prioritized by traffic
impact), keyword ideas (question/comparison/preposition keywords as high-intent answers to the
49% no-click problem, citing Jumpshot), backlinks report (for competitor link outreach), top
pages report, and traffic analyzer. Closes plugging Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil said that when he got started in SEO there were dozens of expensive tools, whereas now there are hundreds — and consequently many good free ones.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil's Google Search Console workflow was to compare the last 30 days of traffic against six months prior and target the pages with the biggest drop in impressions and clicks for updating.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil said there are over a billion blogs on the internet — roughly one per seven people — and argued that is far more than the world needs.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil said updating a decayed page (fresh information, embedded video, fluff removed) usually recovers its traffic within about 30 days.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil's two methods for improving a decayed page were: search the keyword and add whatever the ranking pages have that you don't; and embed relevant YouTube videos, which most people skip, to raise time on site and engagement and therefore rankings.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil recommended Google's Structured Data Markup Helper because correct structured data varies by site type (local, national, e-commerce, lead-gen/consulting) and unlocks star ratings, rich snippets and breadcrumbs that raise click-through rate.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil said rising brand searches raise your rankings, and that Google, Facebook and the social sites are combating fake news by ranking brands better on the belief that big brands (a Nike, a CNN) are less likely to publish false information — a belief he said is true.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil recommended tracking your brand name against competitors in Google Trends, and growing brand search with Hello Bar (email capture), free Mailchimp (email sends) and Subscribers (push notifications, free plan) to keep pulling people back to the site.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil claimed Ubersuggest offers 80-plus percent of the features of Moz, SEMrush and Ahrefs for free.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil described Ubersuggest's SEO audit report as breaking down load time, title tags, meta descriptions, code errors, URL issues and redirect issues, with fixes prioritized by maximum traffic gain.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil cited Jumpshot that roughly 49% of Google searches get no clicks, and positioned Ubersuggest's question/comparison/preposition keyword ideas as the high-intent answer — traffic that converts rather than generic keywords.
- As of 2019-12-09, Neil described the Ubersuggest backlinks report as a competitor-outreach engine (find who links to a competitor, pitch them your better content) and the Top Pages report as a content-planning tool (competitor pages ranked by search traffic, backlinks and social shares), plus a traffic analyzer for competitive comparison.
