# 3 Ways to Make Your Blog Posts Go Viral | Viral Marketing Blog Tips!

## Summary
Neil Patel lays out three tactics to make blog content go viral. First, virality is fundamentally about emotion — sad, happy, or furious — and content with no emotion has very little chance of spreading. Given emotional content, tip one is to "fuel the fire" by paying to seed early traffic (boosting a Facebook post, StumbleUpon, Twitter paid ads), since a million visitors to a post can be worth $100K–200K in ad-spend equivalent. Tip two is to use BuzzSumo to find what has already gone viral in your space over the last 12 months and create a better, "one-upped" version (e.g., 101 ways vs. their 10 ways), while also making the page beautifully designed because ugly pages don't get shared — the reason his Quick Sprout advanced guides use elaborate designs. Tip three is that virality is a numbers game you can't predict; he averages one viral post every two weeks by publishing more than one post a day, and volume is what makes it work.

## Key claims
As of 2017-11-05, Neil states:
- Virality is all about emotions — if content evokes sadness, happiness, or fury it's much more likely to go viral; content with no emotion has very little chance of going viral (as of 2017-11-05).
- Tip 1 — "Fuel the fire" by driving early paid traffic to a post (boosting it on Facebook, StumbleUpon, paying for Twitter traffic) to spur momentum (as of 2017-11-05).
- A million visitors to a blog post could be worth roughly $100K–$200K in ad spend, so it is worth paying to fuel it (as of 2017-11-05).
- Tip 2 — Use BuzzSumo to find keywords/posts in your space that already went viral in the last 12 months, then recreate better versions rather than reinventing the wheel (as of 2017-11-05).
- Apply the "one-up" / skyscraper approach: if a viral post was "10 ways to drive more traffic," write "101 ways to drive more traffic" (as of 2017-11-05).
- Page design matters as much as content — beautiful designs are much more likely to go viral; ugly pages deter sharing. His Quick Sprout advanced guides use beautiful designs on purpose for this reason (as of 2017-11-05).
- Tip 3 — Virality is a numbers game and largely unpredictable; if you could predict hits you'd be rich (as of 2017-11-05).
- He averages one viral blog post roughly every two weeks, and achieves that by publishing more than one blog post a day; publishers like BuzzFeed also rely on quantity (as of 2017-11-05).
- Self-deprecating aside: he claims to be "terrible at writing" with poor grammar/spelling, framing that if he can do it, the viewer can too (as of 2017-11-05).
