# How Books Can Make You More Money Even If You Don't Read Them

## Summary

Neil teaches the "free book + shipping" front-end funnel, crediting marketer **Frank Kern** as
the model (Kern spends ~$10–20 to acquire a customer who pays ~$7 shipping for a free ebook, yet
nets millions via the back-end). Neil notes he personally doesn't read many books (he reads
articles) — the strategy works regardless. Steps: (1) **Write a book** tied to a product/service
you already sell; if you can't, hire a ghostwriter or **white-label** a less-popular ebook (buy
the rights, change the name). (2) **Don't print it** — make a digital ebook plus a free audio
version (recorded yourself or by a hire) to avoid storage/shipping costs. (3) **Drive traffic to
a landing page** offering the free book + audio where the buyer only pays handling (~$3 for
downloads/server costs, not shipping). His own example book: doubling search traffic in 60 days.
(4) **Add a checkout bump** — a small tick-box irresistible offer before the buy button (e.g. a
$20–50 tool that speeds up results). (5) **Offer upsells and downsells** (ideally three) to
squeeze more revenue per visitor — essential because you're paying $10–20 per customer up front.
Use ClickFunnels or SamCart to build the upsell/downsell flow. (6) **Follow up by email** — sell
buyers more products; re-pitch non-takers of the upsells/downsells with *different* copy
(if the original pitch was logical, make the email emotional).
