# ChatGPT gets banned - The 110M impression LinkedIn page

## Summary
A long-form Marketing School episode covering several threads: why broad, viral
"spectacle" content (using Daniel Murray's Marketing Millennials LinkedIn page as the
example) drives huge reach but not necessarily enterprise leads, vs. narrowly-targeted
content for enterprise buyers; two solo-creator monetization funnels Neil observed at a
YouTube creators' event; ★ his personal philosophy on making money "work for you"
through investments (venture/PE/hedge funds, stocks) rather than only through active
marketing income; ★ NP Digital's explicit remote-first policy and his own
travel/office-space tradeoffs; and Italy's ChatGPT ban, which he frames as an early
instance of AI-content copyright/privacy regulation to watch, especially out of
GDPR-strict Europe.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Broad, mass-appeal "spectacle" content (memes, simple universal visuals) can rack up
  huge impression counts — cites Daniel Murray's "Marketing Millennials" LinkedIn page
  (self-reported: 100M+ impressions, "multiple seven figures a year") — and that volume
  is genuinely valuable if your business monetizes via ads/newsletters/broad audience.
  But for enterprise-targeted sales (his own example: NP Digital selling to marketing
  managers, VPs of marketing/growth, CMOs), broad viral content does not convert well;
  narrowly-targeted content (case studies, topics like cookie-list-world compliance for
  large companies, informed by conversations with Forrester) generates far fewer
  impressions but qualified enterprise leads. (Neil, 2023-10-14) — social-media /
  agency-entrepreneurship.
- From a YouTube creators' event: describes two solo-operator monetization funnels he
  found notable — (a) an OnlyFans creator driving traffic entirely from viral photo posts
  on Twitter/X with a simple "subscribe" call-to-action in comments; (b) a separate
  creator using TikTok virality to funnel into Instagram and then a phone-call sales
  process for a $2,000 course, self-reported at ~$20M/year run solo with no paid ads.
  Notes he isn't passing judgment on either business model, only observing the funnel
  mechanics. (Neil, 2023-10-14) — social-media / agency-entrepreneurship.
- ★ States his core wealth philosophy: he no longer makes the majority of his income from
  active marketing work, but from "business and investments" that compound over time —
  parking money in venture funds, private equity funds, and hedge funds for consistent
  (if less liquid) returns "for ages," plus a meaningful stock allocation, to the point
  he says he could live off it and never work again; notes some banks will now lend
  against private-equity holdings directly. Frames the goal as "how do you get your
  money to work for you more and more over time" rather than only reinvesting profits
  into more marketing activity. (Neil, 2023-10-14) — agency-entrepreneurship / wealth
  (self-reported, unaudited).
- ★ Confirms NP Digital is explicitly "committed to remote first," while maintaining
  WeWork/satellite offices in some cities based on (a) what employees want and (b) what
  certain enterprise clients require for in-person presence. Argues remote-vs-office
  effectiveness depends on employee type: self-directed high performers do fine remote;
  people who need to be pushed/micromanaged need in-person structure — and he says he
  tries to avoid hiring the latter. Predicts a hybrid model will dominate industry-wide
  within 5–10 years, and floats a theory that some large companies (Meta, Amazon, etc.)
  push return-to-office mandates as a low-cost way to induce voluntary attrition instead
  of paying severance for layoffs. (Neil, 2023-10-14) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- Self-reported personal/biographical color: describes seriously researching buying a
  private jet or chartering one to cut international travel time away from his wife and
  kids, and considering (but not building) a ~10,000-sq-ft private-equity-style personal
  office space (build-out cost estimated at $3–4M) — decided against both, partly at his
  wife's urging since he already travels internationally most weeks and would rarely use
  an office. Recalls that his past content-marketing lead "Sean" ran NP Digital's/his
  prior blogs to millions of monthly visitors while based in Orange County to Neil's
  Seattle, entirely virtually, pre-dating Zoom (via Skype/phone). (Neil, 2023-10-14) —
  biography / agency-entrepreneurship (self-reported).
- On Italy's ChatGPT ban: frames ChatGPT as effectively an advanced, web-scraping
  "article spinner" that recombines scraped source content into new text, which is why
  Europe (GDPR-strict) is leading on legal/regulatory pushback; expects more countries
  and lawsuits to follow, alongside continued mainstream AI adoption in business — not
  just for content generation, but especially for real-time analytics/insight generation
  that shifts marketing decisions from monthly/quarterly to daily cadence, which he
  argues could save large advertisers (his example: a company spending $8B+/year like
  Procter & Gamble) "hundreds of millions" via less wasted ad spend. (Neil, 2023-10-14)
  — ai-marketing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> The idea of spectacle marketing versus relationship building marketing... you're going
> to get a lot of views... but relationship marketing... you're going deep on the
> relationship there. [Eric's framing, endorsed by Neil in the ensuing discussion]

> I don't make the majority of my money from marketing, I make it from business and
> investments and it compounds over time. It's how do you get your money to work for you
> more and more over time. That is really the key to succeeding.

> We are committed to remote first. We do have WeWork still... it really is based on two
> factors. One, it's what the employees want. Two, it's also what certain clients want.

> If it's a high performer, remote first is fine for the most part, unless it's like you
> have to collaborate... if it's someone that needs to be micromanaged or needs to be
> pushed, I cannot, I can't, I just can't.

> Chat GPT is a much, much, much, much better version than a article spinner when it
> comes to creating content... it's using original pieces of article or information as
> sources to go and create something new. And Italy doesn't like that.
