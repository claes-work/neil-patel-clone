# The #1 Tip To Grow Your Business and Attract New Customers

_Solo Neil-to-camera monologue. All material Neil-attributed → persona data._

## Summary

Neil's single growth tip: **stop over-investing in customer acquisition and shift a large share of
effort to conversion optimization** on the traffic you already have. His framing of the market
shift: companies used to just keep spending more to acquire, hit a point where **acquisition stops
being profitable**, and have since moved money into **support/upsells** to extract more revenue per
customer — but most still **ignore converting existing visitors**. CRO is **cheaper and more
effective than acquisition**, and — crucially — raising your conversion rate 10–40% lets you
**afford to spend more to acquire and out-bid competitors**, eventually taking them out as ad costs
rise. He stresses CRO is **not only A/B testing**: sometimes it's **making the product/service
better, adjusting price, or improving the experience** (e.g. an 800-number footer so prospects get
objections answered before buying). Practical thresholds and methods: once you're above **~10,000
visitors/month and ~100+ sales/month**, spend **20%+ of your time on CRO**; below A/B-test scale,
use **user testing, surveys, and session/mouse-movement recordings** (he names usertesting.com and
Crazy Egg). Bottom line: businesses that focus purely on acquisition won't survive rising ad costs;
CRO is how you keep competing.
