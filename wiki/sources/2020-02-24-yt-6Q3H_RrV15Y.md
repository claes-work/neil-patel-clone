# 4 USELESS Marketing Tools You're Still Using (STOP Wasting Your Money)

## Summary
Neil warns against four outdated/risky marketing tactics. (1) Automated link-building
tools (e.g. Scrape Box, SEnuke) — useful "six to eight years ago" but now flagged by
Google's post-Penguin algorithms as artificial backlink patterns, risking penalties;
recommends value-first tactics instead (Ubersuggest generated 10,000+ backlinks purely
by giving away a free tool) or buying pre-made tools from CodeCanyon for $10-20. (2)
Text/content spinners — post-Panda, Google detects low-value spun content and can hurt
rankings sitewide, not just per-page; recommends original research-backed content
instead. (3) LinkedIn lead-generation automation bots — against LinkedIn's rules,
perceived as spammy, doesn't convert; recommends valuable content and native LinkedIn
video/live instead (cites LinkedIn favoring video). (4) Comment bots — he ran an
experiment confirming comment links DO affect rankings, but automated/spammy comments
just get deleted or caught in spam traps; recommends genuinely helpful, non-automated
comments.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-02-24, Neil said Google's Penguin update made automated link-building
  tools (Scrape Box, SEnuke) risky — they leave footprints that Google can detect as
  artificial backlink patterns, which can flatten or hurt rankings (though outright
  penalties are rare).
- As of 2020-02-24, Neil said he has generated over 10,000 backlinks to Ubersuggest
  purely by giving away the free tool, without automated link-building.
- As of 2020-02-24, Neil said Google's Panda update penalizes low-value spun/rewritten
  content, and that this can hurt an entire site's rankings, not just the offending
  pages.
- As of 2020-02-24, Neil said LinkedIn prohibits automated lead-generation/outreach
  tools and is actively working to shut down the Chrome extensions that enable them;
  recommends valuable content and native video/live video instead, noting "LinkedIn
  loves videos."
- As of 2020-02-24, Neil said he personally ran an experiment and confirmed that
  comment links DO have an effect on search rankings — but automated/spammy comments
  get deleted or caught in spam traps rather than helping.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "So, instead of using these tools, what I want you to do is do research, people love research, they're more likely to link to articles that have research, look for reputable sources and tie in that data into your article and site your sources."

> "Now, I even conducted an experiment to determine or not whether comment links have affect on rankings. And believe it or not, they do."

> "My recommendation is for you to think long-term. Don't take those quick hits... Anyone that tells you something that's too good to be true, chances are it is and it's just going to hurt you in the long run."
