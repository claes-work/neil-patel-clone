# The Best SEO and Growth Tactics for 2025 (Marketing School)

## Summary
★ Landmark-worthy for later L3 promotion: Neil's "search everywhere optimization" framing — SEO has evolved from *search engine* optimization (traditional Google) to *search everywhere* optimization, because attention is divided across every platform people search on (Google, ChatGPT, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Amazon, App Store, LLMs). Neil attributes the term to NP Digital ("what we call at our agency").

A co-hosted Marketing School year-end episode on the best SEO and growth tactics for 2025. Eric opens by arguing the best SEO tactic is to *diversify away from SEO* (citing a Google-algorithm "exploit" video and Rand Fishkin's line that SEO is now "a side effect of all the work you're doing"). Neil reframes that as "search everywhere optimization," rattling off (approximate, recalled) daily search-volume figures across platforms and arguing companies obsess over likes/engagement while ignoring the huge, monetizable search volume on Instagram/TikTok. Neil's favorite 2025 tactic is Global SEO with AI: use AI to translate/transcribe content, human-review the keywords/titles/meta, apply hreflang, and build links — because English is usually <30% of SEO traffic when global SEO is done right. On growth tactics, Eric reads a Single Grain sponsor spot (his agency) then gives his #1 (build products/tools — "highest ROI," citing Neil's chart and Neil's own tool track record); Neil's #1 is auto-translate/transcribe content *within the platform* (YouTube's built-in auto-dub feature, with Meta/TikTok building the same) so one social profile serves all regions. Eric closes on AI browser agents (Gemini 2.0 / Claude computer-use) needing a human "AI agent manager," which both peg as more of a 2026 tactic. No guest; Neil + Eric only.

## Key claims
- SEO has evolved from "search engine optimization" (traditional Google) to "search everywhere optimization": you must optimize across every platform and channel people search on, because attention is truly divided. Neil credits the term to NP Digital. (Neil, 2024-12-27)
- Daily search-volume estimate across platforms: ~45.1 billion searches/day total; Google ~18% of the market at ~8.5 billion/day; ChatGPT ~1 billion/day; Instagram ~6.5 billion/day; the Amazon app ~3.5 billion/day; Bing "quite a bit"; the Apple App Store ~500 million/day. (Neil, 2024-12-27 — approximate/recalled figures)
- Search intent varies by platform, but all of it is monetizable: App Store searches are to download an app, Amazon searches are transactional/buy-intent ("very key money searches"), Instagram searches span content/education/entertainment/shopping/research. (Neil, 2024-12-27)
- Instagram and TikTok have enormous daily search volume, yet companies fixate on likes/engagement and rarely optimize to appear when someone searches a product term (e.g., "purse"); as these platforms make it easier to open a shop and let people click-to-buy, search optimization there will drive real revenue. (Neil, 2024-12-27)
- Search everywhere optimization spans YouTube, Amazon, App Store optimization, and LLMO / generative engine optimization — anywhere humans can search — because humans will always search as long as they're curious. (Neil, 2024-12-27)
- Neil's favorite 2025 tactic is Global SEO with AI: for most big corporations English is usually less than 30% of SEO traffic when global SEO is done right — the majority comes from many other languages that add up. (Neil, 2024-12-27)
- Global SEO play: use AI to translate and transcribe your content, have a human review to ensure the right keywords and adjust titles/meta descriptions, use hreflang, and build links to the translated/transcribed pages — this scales a global SEO strategy far faster without hiring lots of people per country and opens up new markets to sell to. (Neil, 2024-12-27)
- Neil's #1 growth tactic for 2025: automatically translate and transcribe your content *within the platform*. YouTube now has a one-click auto-dub/transcribe feature (not yet available to everyone, but rolling out); Neil says he's spoken to Meta and TikTok engineers building the same — a question of "when," not "if." One social profile then serves all regions/languages (no need for 10–20 separate profiles), boosting reach, likes, and engagement. (Neil, 2024-12-27)
- Tools/products have worked very well as a growth channel for Neil ("tools have done really well for us"), agreeing with Eric that building products is a top-ROI tactic. (Neil, 2024-12-27)
