# How to Edit Your Content For SEO - Content Marketing Part 2 - SEO Unlocked

## Summary
Editing-focused lesson in the SEO Unlocked content-marketing module. Opens with
keyword validation via Ubersuggest before writing (search volume, SEO difficulty —
under 40 recommended for newer/lower-budget sites, "pay difficulty," and CPC as a
proxy for commercial intent), then the Keyword Ideas and Content Ideas reports for
related terms and competitive benchmarking (social shares, estimated monthly Google
visits, backlink counts). Introduces his "STEPS" editing checklist: Strive for
brevity (cut filler words like "just," "really," "perhaps," "literally," "very"; cut
redundant words); Technical jargon (avoid Ivy-League-style vocabulary and
non-translating idioms for a global/non-native audience; recommends the free
Hemingway App for a readability score); Ensure flow (use subheadings throughout,
always label a "Conclusion" subheading; argues outlining first prevents flow problems
that are expensive to fix after writing); Pain points (match the reader's problem/story
without over-pushing negativity — "be nice and kind"); SEO (natural keyword placement
in title/body/conclusion/meta description, warns against keyword stuffing, points to
an on-page checklist resource). Closes with user experience as an extension of SEO:
recommends setting up a Crazy Egg heatmap on published content to see where readers
drop off and iterating from there. Recommends Grammarly (Chrome extension) for
grammar/spelling, self-deprecating that his own spelling/grammar is weak.
