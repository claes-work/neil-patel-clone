# My Favorite Marketing Channel (It's NOT What You Think)

## Summary

A belief/philosophy monologue. Neil predicts viewers will guess his favorite channel is Google (SEO/content marketing, where he makes the most money and gets the most traffic) — then reveals it's actually **social media marketing**, despite Google earning him more revenue. His reasoning is relationship-driven, not revenue-driven: social media lets him connect one-on-one with his audience (responding to YouTube/Facebook/LinkedIn/blog comments, emails, WhatsApp messages), understand their needs and pain points, and use that understanding to build better products, sharpen messaging, and optimize conversions — which is *why* his Google traffic converts so well. He argues you should evaluate channels by the value they provide (the relationship), not by the money they directly generate. He closes with a second point: even experts (he name-checks Elon Musk, Gary Vaynerchuk, Tony Robbins) keep learning from their audience — no one is perfect, everyone makes mistakes, and audience feedback on social makes you better as an individual.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-12-01, Neil said his favorite marketing channel is social media marketing, even though he makes more revenue and gets more traffic from Google/SEO.
- As of 2018-12-01, Neil said he prefers social because it lets him connect one-on-one with his audience — via YouTube, Facebook, LinkedIn, and blog comments, emails, and WhatsApp — which Google traffic doesn't reveal about a visitor's intent.
- As of 2018-12-01, Neil said this connection lets him understand audience needs and pain points, build better products and services, and know what messaging relates to them — which is why his Google traffic converts better than his Facebook/YouTube traffic.
- As of 2018-12-01, Neil said you should judge marketing channels by the value they provide (for him, the one-on-one relationship), not by how much money they directly make.
- As of 2018-12-01, Neil said surveys (e.g. SurveyMonkey) can capture some of this data but not as well as social media does.
- As of 2018-12-01, Neil said even experts like Elon Musk, Gary Vaynerchuk, and Tony Robbins learn from their audiences; audience feedback on social media helps you improve as an individual because no one is perfect.
