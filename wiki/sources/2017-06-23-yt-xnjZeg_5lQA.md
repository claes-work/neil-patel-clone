# 3 Steps to Driving More Website Traffic By Repurposing Your Blog Content

## Summary
A short tactical video in which Neil Patel rejects the common advice that driving more traffic requires constantly creating new content, arguing instead that marketers can grow traffic by repurposing and re-sharing their existing ("old") content across three social channels. He walks through re-posting old articles repeatedly on a Facebook fan page, scheduling roughly ten tweets a day with a tool like Buffer, and re-publishing the first few paragraphs of old posts natively on LinkedIn with a "click to continue reading" link back to the site — each method aimed at squeezing continued traffic out of content that has already been produced.

## Key claims
- Getting more traffic does not require creating new content; the common advice that it does is false, and marketers can drive more traffic using their old content instead (as of 2017-06-23).
- **Step 1 — Facebook:** Take old content and continually re-post it on your Facebook fan page over and over again, including articles published a year ago (as of 2017-06-23).
- Re-sharing old content on Facebook accounts for more than 60% of Neil's own Facebook traffic (as of 2017-06-23).
- **Step 2 — Twitter:** Tweet out roughly 10 times a day, using old content, so it continually drives traffic back to your site; use a tool like Buffer to schedule the tweets (as of 2017-06-23).
- Neil states that when he tweeted only once a day he got less than 1,000 visitors a month, and when he tweeted over 10 times a day he got over 4,000 visitors a month (as of 2017-06-23).
- **Step 3 — LinkedIn native publishing:** Beyond just sharing links, write articles natively on LinkedIn — put the first few paragraphs of your old content on LinkedIn and end with a "click to continue reading" link that drives people back to your site; Neil does this roughly four times a week (as of 2017-06-23).
- ★ Neil reports that native LinkedIn publishing of old-content excerpts took him from under 2,000 visitors a month from LinkedIn to over 33,000 visitors a month, purely from posting the first few paragraphs plus a "click to continue reading" link (as of 2017-06-23).
- Other social networks such as Reddit, StumbleUpon, and Google Plus can also be used, but these three networks (Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn) drive most of the traffic from old content back to your site (as of 2017-06-23).
