# You Have Been Thinking About AI The Wrong Way - You're Missing the Big Picture

## Summary
Marketing School (co-hosted) episode arguing that most businesses are focused on the
wrong AI use case. Neil's thesis: most companies fixate on generative AI for content
creation (images/text at scale), but content creation is already cheap ("dirt cheap")
for the vast majority of businesses that aren't platforms like Google or Facebook —
generating more content doesn't move the needle much, and AI-generated content without
human editing is currently low-quality ("you're going to get whacked" eventually).
Instead, he argues the bigger opportunity is AI-driven, real-time analytics: pooling all
marketing data into one place and having AI surface actionable insights (wasted spend,
reallocation opportunities) in real time rather than the current norm of infrequent
(monthly/quarterly), low-adoption manual analytics review — he says at a London ADCON
talk, when he asked how many attendees make decisions from analytics daily, only ~2–3%
raised their hands. Frames this as the biggest AI-marketing opportunity for the next
6–12 months (as of 2023-09-09).

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- Content creation is already inexpensive for almost every business ("dirt cheap") — a
  blog article or a podcast episode costs little regardless of AI; therefore AI's
  content-generation cost savings are a minor opportunity for non-platform businesses.
  (Neil, 2023-09-11) — ai-marketing.
- AI-generated content without human "last mile" editing is currently low quality and
  unsustainable as a strategy long-term ("you end up getting whacked"); expects this may
  change in "two, three years." (Neil, 2023-09-11) — ai-marketing / content-marketing.
- At an ADCON talk in London, when Neil asked how many marketers act on their analytics
  daily/weekly, only ~2–3% of the room raised their hands — true across SMBs and large
  corporations alike. (Neil, 2023-09-11) — analytics-cro.
- The biggest under-discussed AI-marketing opportunity (as of 2023-09-11): centralizing
  all marketing data (own systems, Tableau, Google Data Studio, etc.) and using AI to
  analyze it in real time to surface wasted ad spend and reallocation opportunities —
  while keeping a human in the loop for the actual spend decision (doesn't yet trust AI
  to auto-execute budget changes). (Neil, 2023-09-11) — analytics-cro / ai-marketing.
- Cites total digital ad spend in the hundreds of billions, with Google alone at roughly
  ~$100B+ (~$60B-ish cited in the moment) annually, as the scale argument for why
  real-time AI-driven spend optimization matters more than content-generation savings.
  Self-reported, approximate figures, dated 2023-09-11. — paid-ads / analytics-cro.
