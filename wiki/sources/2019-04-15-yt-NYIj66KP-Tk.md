# Can You Still Make a Living Off Affiliate Marketing? The TRUTH About Affiliate Marketing

## Summary

Neil says affiliate marketing "doesn't work as well as it used to" but still works — the shift is
from **paid traffic** (Facebook/AdWords → landing page) to **SEO/organic**, which takes longer but
carries far higher margins since you don't pay for traffic. His process: (1) browse affiliate
networks (e.g. **ClickBank**) within a vertical you're **passionate about** to find high-payout
products (call their support for advice); (2) Google those products, list the ranking URLs, and
run each through **Ubersuggest** (overview → Top Pages → View All keywords) to find **money
keywords** (high traffic, high CPC, low difficulty) and backlink sources; (3) create content that
**one-ups** competitors (Skyscraper: turn a 2,000-word post into 4,000–5,000 words with video and
images); (4) **promote** it by emailing everyone who linked to or shared competitors' articles
(via search.twitter.com) to earn backlinks and traffic, then monetize with the affiliate offers.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-04-15, Neil said affiliate marketing still works but not as easily as the old
  paid-traffic era (Facebook ads / AdWords → landing page); the durable new path is **SEO/organic**,
  slower but higher-margin because you don't pay for traffic.
- As of 2019-04-15, Neil said to pick a vertical you're **passionate about** (not just "to make
  money") and mine affiliate networks like **ClickBank** for popular, high-payout products,
  using their support staff for recommendations.
- As of 2019-04-15, Neil reiterated his **Ubersuggest** competitor workflow (overview → Top Pages →
  View All keywords) to find money keywords — high traffic, expensive CPC (converts better), low
  search difficulty (ranks faster) — plus backlink data.
- As of 2019-04-15, Neil said to out-create competitors (**Skyscraper Technique**): if theirs is a
  2,000-word post, make yours 4,000–5,000 words with video and images so readers need no other site.
- As of 2019-04-15, Neil's promotion method: email everyone who linked to competitors' articles
  (and everyone who shared them, found via search.twitter.com) with a short pitch — send 100–300
  such emails to start earning backlinks and traffic — then monetize via the affiliate offers.
