# What I'm Most Excited About in 2023 as a Digital Marketer

> ⚠️ TITLE/CONTENT YEAR MISMATCH: The YouTube title says "2023," but this video was published 2019-10-03 and the transcript is entirely about **2020** predictions ("today, I want to show what I'm most excited about in **2020**"). Neil re-optimizes/A-B-tests titles, so the title was almost certainly edited later. Every prediction below is a **forward-looking 2019 bet about 2020** — do not read them as current or as 2023 content.

## Summary

Solo Neil Patel video (published 2019-10-03) listing seven things he was excited about for **2020** as a digital marketer — despite the later-edited title saying "2023." He opens by predicting an **omni-channel approach**: existing channels (SEO, PPC) are saturating and algorithms are getting tougher, but new, less-competitive channels are opening — notably **voice search** (he cites Comscore's claim that half of all searches would be voice-related by 2020) monetized via tools like Jetson AI on Google Voice and Alexa. Second, **content marketing beyond text**: he claims ~21% of Google results are video-based (attributed to "Myles") and that Google now ranks/transcribes **podcasts**, so video and podcasting will drive SEO traffic. Third, **personalization** through offline/smart devices (fridges, stoves, the June Oven). Fourth, **live chat** (citing Nextiva/Intercom, ~one-third of a peer's sales) trending toward video-based chat. Fifth, **chatbots**, especially Facebook Messenger (ManyChat, MobileMonkey), boosted by expected WhatsApp/Instagram integration. Sixth, **Internet of Things / smart-device marketing** (Alexa, mobile, iPad). Seventh — the one he's most excited about — **Ubersuggest**, which he wants to evolve into a tool that "does the SEO for you" and automates SEO. Closes promoting his agency, Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims

- As of 2019-10-03, Neil said the biggest 2020 opportunity is an **omni-channel approach**: existing channels are saturated and algorithms tougher, but new, less-competitive channels keep opening up and marketers who leverage them all will win.
- As of 2019-10-03, Neil said **voice search** would be huge, citing Comscore that half of searches would be voice-related by 2020, and pointed to Jetson AI for monetizing traffic from Google Voice and Alexa Home.
- As of 2019-10-03, Neil said **content marketing has moved well beyond text** — claiming ~21% of Google listings are video-based (attributed to "Myles") and that Google now ranks and transcribes **podcasts** — so marketers should produce video and podcasts, not just text, for SEO.
- As of 2019-10-03, Neil said **personalization** would grow via offline/smart devices (fridges, stoves, the June Oven) delivering customized marketing, though the technology "isn't fully there yet" in 2019.
- As of 2019-10-03, Neil said **live chat** already works extremely well (citing a peer, Yanee at Nextiva, getting ~one-third of sales via Intercom) and would grow in 2020, trending toward video-based chat — but only with humans available to take over from bots for a high ROI.
- As of 2019-10-03, Neil said **chatbots**, specifically Facebook Messenger via ManyChat / MobileMonkey, would be huge in 2020 as Messenger integrates with WhatsApp and Instagram, noting WhatsApp already outpaces SMS in many countries where texting costs money.
- As of 2019-10-03, Neil said the **Internet of Things / smart-device marketing** (Alexa, mobile, iPad) would open more monetization channels, with many players beyond Jetson AI building the tech.
- As of 2019-10-03, Neil said he was **most excited about Ubersuggest** — after adding free rank tracking, alerts, and a bigger keyword database in 2019, he was building a tool that "does the SEO for you" and automates SEO so users don't do it manually.
