# The Only Rule In Marketing You Have To Follow (The Rule of Seven)

## Summary

Neil's "one unbreakable rule" is the **rule of seven**: people need to see/interact with your
brand roughly **seven times before they convert**. Two shifts make it binding: single-channel
growth is dead (Facebook grew on invite emails, Dropbox on Twitter shares, others on one AdWords/
SEO channel — "you can no longer grow through one channel"), and heavy competition means one touch
won't convert. He gives five ways to rack up the seven touches: (1) **be social and interact** —
not just post, but reply to comments/messages; (2) **leverage SEO** (Google is the world's most
popular site) plus content marketing, social, link building, interlinking; (3) **paid ads** across
Google, Facebook, Taboola, Outbrain, banner/media buys — spy on competitors via **Facebook Info
and Ads** and **Ubersuggest**; (4) **update your content** — most people don't, and stale content
loses rankings because "there's roughly a billion blogs… one blog for every seven people" and
Google ranks fresh; (5) **keep re-sharing content** 3–5× a year because restrictive algorithms show
each post to only ~10–20% of your audience. Bonus: use **Hello Bar** and **Subscribers** (push
notifications/emails) to pull people back. The through-line: an **omnichannel** presence engineered
to hit the brand-frequency threshold.
