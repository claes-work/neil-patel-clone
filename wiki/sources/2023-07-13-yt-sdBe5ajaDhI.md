# Hacks To Grow Your Business When Sales Drop

## Summary
Solo return-guest "Productive Insights" podcast interview (same show as
[[2023-06-24-yt-0nwnNDiY-vo]]; host explicitly references a prior interview and CPA/MBA
background) focused on growth strategy during a weak economy, plus NP Digital's international
expansion. On growing during a bad market: argues expanding into new (as opposed to mature/
saturated) geographic markets is close to a free option in a downturn — an established market
can only get hurt by a bad economy, while a brand-new market (starting from $0 revenue) can only
improve regardless of macro conditions, just more slowly in a bad market than a good one. Applies
this directly to NP Digital: states international revenue grew ~74% year-over-year even in the
current weak economy, and details the company's regional expansion: started the year already in
Australia, UK, Brazil, India, and Canada (five non-US regions); closed and wound down a Germany
division earlier in the year ("the Germany guy didn't work out... didn't want to do really any
work," framed as a mutual, non-adversarial parting); has since added Italy, Spain, Singapore,
Mexico, Colombia, Malaysia, Hong Kong, and Japan (eight new regions in roughly five months); and
projects roughly 23-25 total regions/countries (including the US) by year-end. States expansion
costs "millions of dollars per region" (original internal projection was ~$1M/region, since
revised upward) and that the growth so far is still coming predominantly from the original five
established regions rather than the newly-added ones, since new-region revenue takes time to
ramp — states he expects "2025 will be our year" for the newer regions to contribute meaningfully.
Reiterates a profit-over-revenue business philosophy, again tying it explicitly to his Gujarati
upbringing ("a business is someone that makes profit, not revenue. Revenue is an irrelevant
number"). Discloses that NP Digital's near-term profit is intentionally depressed — not from
declining underlying profitability, but because profit is being reinvested into the international
expansion (on the advice of auditors RSM and BDO, for tax/accounting-compliance reasons) —
resulting in "terrible cashflow" even while underlying profit is "great," a tradeoff he says he
accepts for 1-2 years for the sake of long-term growth. Confirms his co-founder is Mike (Kamo),
credited with an "amazing," strong operations/recruiting/sales background, and separately confirms
his CEO/COO is also named Mike (Gullickson), previously president of the global agency iProspect.
Restates the hiring philosophy from [[2023-06-26-yt-Z4mnr8BITMk]]: prioritize candidates who've
worked at multiple competitors and been repeatedly promoted there, and prefers indirect outreach
("we're looking for someone just like you... do you know anyone who'd be a great fit?") over
direct recruitment pitches, which he says perform worse.
