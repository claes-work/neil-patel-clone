# How to Skyrocket Your Google Adwords ROI | PPC Advertising Tips

## Summary

In this short 2017 video, Neil Patel gives two levers for improving Google AdWords ROI. First, use SKAGs (single keyword ad groups): instead of writing one ad text for hundreds of keywords in one ad group, build ad groups around a single keyword (or a handful at most) so the ad copy is tightly relevant to that keyword — which raises Quality Score, lifts click-through, lowers cost, and improves conversions. Second, stop treating AdWords ROI as purely an ad/bid-tuning problem: ad optimization eventually caps out, so the bigger ROI lever is the landing page, product, and offer. He recommends installing Crazy Egg (which he owns) to see scroll depth and clicks via heat map reports, then running A/B tests through its WYSIWYG editor to lift conversions. He illustrates the math: at $1/click with a 1-in-100 conversion rate you pay $100 per conversion, but quadrupling landing-page conversions cuts that to $25 per conversion. His closing point: to do well you must optimize both ads and landing pages.

## Key claims

As of 2017-10-27, Neil states:
- If you're a business, you're likely spending on Google, and a business not spending on Google is doing something wrong (as of 2017-10-27).
- Cramming ~100 keywords into one ad group means the ad text can't be relevant to all of them; a SKAG (single keyword ad group) tied to one keyword makes the ad copy highly relevant (as of 2017-10-27).
- High keyword-to-ad relevance makes Quality Score skyrocket, which increases clicks, saves money, and converts better (as of 2017-10-27).
- Write ad text for one keyword — or a handful at most, but ideally just one — rather than reusing the same ad text across hundreds of keywords (as of 2017-10-27).
- AdWords ROI is not purely about the ads; the common belief that you just fine-tune ads and adjust bids is incomplete — you eventually cap out on ad optimization (as of 2017-10-27).
- The real ROI lever is the landing page, the product being sold, and the offer being made (as of 2017-10-27).
- Install Crazy Egg to see how far people scroll and where they click via reports like heat maps; Neil notes he owns the company (as of 2017-10-27).
- After gathering engagement/click data, run A/B tests through Crazy Egg's WYSIWYG editor, make tweaks, and test to optimize conversions (as of 2017-10-27).
- You don't have to use Crazy Egg specifically — any A/B testing tool works (as of 2017-10-27).
- Conversion math: at $1 per click with 1 lead/sale per 100 clicks you pay $100 per conversion; quadrupling landing-page conversion rate drops that to $25 per conversion (as of 2017-10-27).
- Many advertisers focus only on ad campaigns and forget their landing pages; to do well you have to do both (as of 2017-10-27).
