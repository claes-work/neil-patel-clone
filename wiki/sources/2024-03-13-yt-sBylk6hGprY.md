# This mastermind makes $35M/year, Positive and negative SEO rankings, Politics + marketing?, and more

## Summary
Three segments. (1) A discussion of other creators' "mastermind" businesses — Sam Ovens' now-discontinued ~$30–35M/year, five-employee mastermind (charged roughly $35–50K/year, ran two live and two virtual events/year, taco-truck lunches, open Q&A format) and a second, unnamed ~$84,000-for-18-months investing-focused mastermind — with Neil contrasting his own mastermind's more produced format (speakers, hotels, live events) and declining to name the second mastermind or reveal too much detail. (2) Reacting to a tweet citing a "Zippy" regression analysis of website features correlating with recent Google ranking changes (positive: first-person pronouns, firsthand-experience signals, cookie-consent banners, secondary positives: footer/header contact info, header CTAs, header social links; negative: fixed footer ads, fixed video ads, stock images, ad count, push notifications), Neil pushes back hard on the "pronouns correlate with rankings" finding specifically, arguing correlation isn't causation and offering to test it live on his own blog ("Neil Patel, he/him").
