# 7 AWESOME Google Search Tricks You Should Be Using For Market Research

## Summary
Neil walks through seven advanced Google search operators (queries beyond basic keyword searches) and explains how each supports market research, competitor analysis, and link building. He covers the minus/exclusion operator to filter out unwanted brand associations, `intext:` to find pages mentioning a brand for link-reclamation outreach, `intitle:` to surface pages truly on-topic, `inurl:` to spy on competitors' affiliate pages, `site:` to check how many of a site's pages Google has indexed, `-inurl:` to see who else discusses a brand while excluding one's own site, and `related:` to reveal sites Google considers competitors. He frames the payoff as saving time, building more backlinks, and growing the business.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-20, Neil states:
- Most people use Google only for basic keyword searches and web-page results, missing more advanced query capabilities (as of 2017-10-20).
- The exclusion operator (keyword space minus term, e.g. `neil patel -kissmetrics`) removes unwanted meanings or brand associations so results are not the wrong type; Neil is associated with several companies including KISSmetrics (as of 2017-10-20).
- The `intext:` operator followed by a brand name surfaces all pages mentioning that brand in their content; reviewing them to find mentions that are not linking back, then requesting a link, is a simple way to build backlinks that boost search engine rankings (as of 2017-10-20).
- If someone includes your keyword within the title of their page, there is a high chance that page is genuinely on-topic; the `intitle:` operator returns only pages with the keyword in the page title, saving time (as of 2017-10-20).
- The `inurl:` operator (e.g. `inurl:affiliate`) returns pages with that word in the URL, which is useful for viewing competitors' affiliate pages to create a better offer than theirs (as of 2017-10-20).
- The `site:` operator (e.g. `site:yourdomain.com`) lists the pages Google has indexed from a site; a large gap between pages created and pages indexed signals a problem to investigate in Google Search Console (as of 2017-10-20).
- The minus-inurl operator (e.g. `crazy egg -inurl:crazyegg.com`) returns results from across the web about a brand while excluding the brand's own site, which Google would otherwise show first (as of 2017-10-20).
- The `related:` operator (e.g. `related:yourdomain.com`) shows other websites Google considers related — essentially competitors in Google's eyes (as of 2017-10-20).
