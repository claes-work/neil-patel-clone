# Content Marketing Part 1 - SEO Unlocked - Free SEO Course with Neil Patel

## Summary
First of a two-part "SEO Unlocked" content-marketing module, covering fundamentals
before next lesson's tactics. Opens with a consistency argument: his old QuickSprout
blog saw a 30%+ traffic dip after one month without posting, taking three months to
recover — the basis for his "consistency over talent" thesis. Cites adoption stats
(content marketing yields 6x higher conversion rates for adopters; 91% of B2B
marketers use it; 27% of US internet users have ad blockers, pushing marketers toward
content). Gives two case studies: Skin Care by Alana (150%+ revenue growth, 275%
organic traffic growth in a year) and Private Label Extensions (revenue $3.1M to
$7.9M, two-time Inc. 500 company, SEO traffic +400% in six months). Uses Crazy Egg
(his own company) as the flagship B2B proof point: grew to 100,000+ users significantly
via content, using pre-launch customer interviews to shape topics, and guest-posting on
design-community sites/galleries rather than only publishing on the Crazy Egg blog
itself — plus performance-based PR via a named contact (Chris Barrett, Prserve.com).
Introduces "Franklin's Law of Content Success" as his content framework's first two
steps: (1) String Ideas — mining Ubersuggest's keyword-ideas/top-pages/content-ideas/
backlinks reports (reusing "Musketeer Intent" terminology from an earlier keyword
-research lesson) to find topics with the intent-tier your business needs at its stage
(low-volume/under-1,000-visit keywords recommended for newer sites); (2) Target Kite —
deep-diving competitor top-ranking pages (format, media mix, clarity of offer) to beat
them qualitatively, not just match keywords.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-05-18, Neil said a one-month posting gap on his old QuickSprout blog
  produced a 30%+ traffic dip that took over three months to recover, framing this as
  proof that content-marketing consistency outweighs individual post quality.
- As of 2020-05-18, Neil cited stats: content-marketing adopters see 6x higher
  conversion rates than non-adopters; 91% of B2B marketers use content marketing;
  27% of US internet users have ad blockers installed (used to argue ads alone are an
  incomplete acquisition strategy).
- As of 2020-05-18, Neil said Crazy Egg (his own co-founded analytics tool) grew past
  100,000 users substantially through content marketing, including pre-launch customer
  interviews to shape topics and guest content/paid placements on design-community
  websites and galleries rather than solely on Crazy Egg's own blog.
- As of 2020-05-18, Neil named a performance-based PR contact, Chris Barrett at
  Prserve.com ("if he gets you press, he charges you, if he doesn't, he doesn't"), as
  an alternative to hiring a PR agency.
- As of 2020-05-18, Neil introduced "Franklin's Law of Content Success" (named for
  Benjamin Franklin/lightning) with its first two steps: String Ideas (keyword/topic
  mining via Ubersuggest's several reports) and Target Kite (competitor page teardown
  to identify what to beat).
