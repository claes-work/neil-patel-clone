# 7 Nifty Marketing Tactics to Deploy Now

## Summary
A tactic roundup beyond the standard SEO/paid-ads/email playbook: (1) posting
"guest carousels" on other creators' Instagram profiles (similar follower count/industry)
to borrow reach; (2) building free tools (his own Ubersuggest example: 24,000+ unique
backlinks) or buying ready-made tools from CodeCanyon to generate links passively; (3)
aggressively cross-posting winning content across every platform (Instagram carousels →
LinkedIn, Instagram Reels → YouTube Shorts/TikTok); (4) browser push notifications
(subscribers.com) as a permission channel that doesn't require an email/phone handoff;
(5) TubeSift for finding better-targeted YouTube ad placements; (6) adding
charts/data/citations to blog content, which he says produces roughly 32% more backlinks
even without necessarily driving more traffic; (7) SMS follow-up, contrasted directly
against email performance figures.

## Key claims
- Self-reports the Ubersuggest tool page has generated 24,000+ unique backlinks as a
  passive link-building result of releasing a free tool (as of 2022-08-27, self-reported).
- Quantifies a content tactic: adding data/charts/stats to blog posts produces "roughly
  32% more backlinks," though not necessarily more traffic — framed as an authority
  (not traffic) play (as of 2022-08-27, self-reported).
- Gives concrete channel-performance figures: ~30% average email open rate for his own
  sends vs. 18% click-through rate on SMS follow-ups vs. under 4% (typically closer to
  2%) email click-through rate — used to argue SMS outperforms email on engagement (as
  of 2022-08-27, self-reported).
- Names specific tools: subscribers.com (push notifications), TubeSift (YouTube ad
  targeting), CodeCanyon (buy ready-made tools), Minichat (SMS/chat automation).
