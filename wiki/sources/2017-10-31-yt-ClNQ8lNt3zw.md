# How to Get Lots of REAL Yelp Reviews Fast | 5 Yelp Marketing Tips to For Reputation Management

## Summary
Neil argues that for a localized business, especially a US-based one, being on Yelp is a prerequisite for success, and he lays out low-friction, terms-of-service-safe tactics to accumulate real reviews faster. The core constraint is that you cannot solicit specific ratings or reviews without risking being kicked off Yelp, so every tactic is about passive exposure and invitation rather than directive asking: neutrally inviting people to "check us out on Yelp," placing a Yelp badge on your website, displaying the Yelp sticker/sign on your storefront, adding your Yelp listing to your email signature, and sharing your best reviews across social networks. He closes on social proof — the "People Love Us on Yelp" sticker earned by performing well functions like a street musician seeding their own tip jar, signaling to others that reviewing is the norm and prompting more people to leave ratings.

## Key claims
As of 2017-10-31, Neil states:
- A localized business, particularly one based in the US, needs Yelp reviews and will not be successful if it is not on Yelp (as of 2017-10-31).
- You must not break Yelp's terms of service or policies, or you risk getting kicked off the platform (as of 2017-10-31).
- Explicitly telling everyone to write an amazing review, leave five stars, or write a long multi-paragraph review will eventually get you in trouble; instead, neutrally invite people to "check us out on Yelp" and let them decide whether to review (as of 2017-10-31).
- Every business should have a website, and if you lack one you can build it through WordPress.com (as of 2017-10-31).
- Placing a Yelp badge on your website lets visitors who came to view your menu or services see that you're on Yelp, check your reviews, and later return to leave their own rating and review (as of 2017-10-31).
- Displaying the Yelp sticker or sign on your storefront (as seen in many restaurants) makes people aware you're on Yelp; satisfied customers will tend to leave a good review and dissatisfied ones a poor review (as of 2017-10-31).
- Including your Yelp listing in your email signature leverages email — still one of the internet's backbones and used across age groups — to link people to your Yelp profile with every message you send (as of 2017-10-31).
- Sharing your best Yelp reviews on social networks like Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, or Instagram both drives more business and raises awareness that you're on Yelp, making satisfied audiences more likely to leave good reviews (as of 2017-10-31).
- Performing well on Yelp earns you a "People Love Us on Yelp" sticker, which you should display on your storefront to drive more business and prompt others to leave good reviews and ratings (as of 2017-10-31).
- Social proof drives review behavior the way a street musician seeding their own tip jar prompts passersby to add money — seeing that others already love a company on Yelp makes people feel they should review it too (as of 2017-10-31).
