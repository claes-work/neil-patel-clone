# WooCommerce SEO woes, Creator-led demand gen, Top 100 B2B brands up by 10% vs. last year

## Summary
Four segments. (1) A cautionary SEO case study: WooCommerce migrated its site to a new domain (wo.com) and, five months later, reverted the entire migration after traffic collapsed to near-zero and only partially recovered (~40% of prior levels) months later — framed as a lesson that large-scale domain/site migrations must be executed carefully, since even a business the size of WordPress.com can suffer real (if survivable) damage. Mentioned in the same breath: WordPress's parent company had recently acquired Beeper (the iMessage-on-Android app) for $125M and text.com the previous October, prompting skepticism about the strategic rationale for some of these purchases. (2) A related case study on product-market-fit failure: Amazon's discontinued "Fire Phone" (referencing the book *Working Backwards*) over-invested in a single differentiating feature (a 3D/parallax camera-based UI) without adequate customer-development validation, and failed even after being given away free with Prime sign-up — paralleled with Snap Spectacles and Meta Ray-Ban smart glasses as similarly early/underwhelming wearable-camera attempts; the hosts express interest in a hypothetical future AR-glasses product that combines Apple Vision Pro-style content display with genuine everyday wearability, saying they would pay a premium for it. (3) A personal anecdote: Neil describes being naturally curious about how businesses operate, to the point that his wife has had to physically stop him (elbowing him) from asking small-business owners in stores probing questions about their daily sales volume, monthly revenue, and cash vs. card split, because it makes them think he might be casing the business to rob it. This leads into a discussion of Chamath Palihapitiya's claim that "content creators are the new form of demand gen" (cited from an "All-In" podcast episode): Neil describes that NP Digital's organic strategy has shifted away from platform-first thinking (e.g., "we're on Instagram" or "we're on TikTok" for its own sake) toward creator-first thinking — identifying individuals who already command attention relevant to a client's product/service and partnering with them, citing MrBeast's ability to drive demand for whatever product he promotes as the extreme case; influencer engagement fee structures are described as either cost-plus (agency fee on top of the influencer's own rate) or a percentage cut, depending on the client's internal accounting/approval preferences. (4) A closing item: B2B brand value is reported up ~10% year-over-year (per Marketing Week) despite general economic caution, framed through a "be greedy when others are fearful" (Warren Buffett) lens — Neil states NP Digital is actively acquiring companies during this period specifically from founders who are "burnt out" and want to exit, citing an in-progress deal (closing the following month) involving a company serving enterprise brands including Levi's, Domino's, Grand Hyatt, and Canon. Financing structures discussed for these acquisitions include SBA loans (up to $5M, though the founder reportedly cannot retain equity under SBA terms per Neil's understanding), seller financing, equity rollovers, and combinations thereof; retaining the founder post-acquisition (via an earn-out or ongoing equity stake) is described as valuable for institutional knowledge and goodwill, since acquirers otherwise only see a company's "best face" during diligence.
