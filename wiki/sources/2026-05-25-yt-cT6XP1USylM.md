# The AI Search Strategy That Actually Works (Marketing School)

## Summary
Co-hosted Marketing School episode (Neil Patel + Eric Siu) on how LLMs cite content and what that means for AEO/GEO. The high-value core is an Evertune AI dataset (the 6,000 most-cited URLs per model per platform, March + April 2026) showing listicles are the single most-cited content type across every LLM surface, led by Gemini at 65.1% of citations, then Google AI mode 62.3%, Perplexity 60.4%, Google AI overview 55.2%, ChatGPT 50.1%, and Copilot 39.8%. The strategic twist: Google is now playing "whack-a-mole" specifically against *self-published* listicles (a company ranking its own products #1 on its own site), while ChatGPT/Anthropic have not yet built equivalent "search spam" enforcement. The hosts pair this with Lily Ray's study (presented by Eric) on 220 sites using AI content tools showing rapid growth then steep decline, and a long token-economics riff (token-optimization consulting, Chamath's 80/90, whether cost reduction is a durable service). Roughly half the airtime is Eric — the Lily Ray data, the Chamath/80/90 material, and the "pick Google from the Magnificent Seven" call are all Eric and are quarantined below.

## Key claims (dated, speaker-tagged, paraphrase)
- LLMs are citing listicles more than any other content type; per Evertune AI's chart of the 6,000 most-cited URLs per model per platform (March + April 2026), listicle citation share is: Gemini **65.1%** (highest), Google AI mode **62.3%**, Perplexity **60.4%**, Google AI overview **55.2%**, ChatGPT **50.1%**, Copilot **39.8%**. (Neil — inferred presenter, 2026-05-25) — seo / ai-marketing.
- If you want to get cited by an LLM platform, listicles are currently your best-performing content format. (Neil — inferred, 2026-05-25) — ai-marketing / seo.
- Google is now "playing whack-a-mole" specifically against **self-published** listicles — a business publishing listicles on its own site that rank its own products/services #1 — which its long-standing search-spam team treats as manipulated results; ChatGPT is not going after this nearly as hard. (Neil — inferred, 2026-05-25) — seo.
- This is the same manipulation-and-enforcement cycle from 15-20 years ago in SEO; Anthropic and OpenAI will eventually build their own "search spam"-style teams. (Eric, 2026-05-25) — seo / ai-marketing.
- Any marketing tactic that produces high short-term ROI and is very easy to do will, ~9 out of 10 times, not last; avoid them (rare edge cases aside). Long-term strategies that don't produce immediate ROI and aren't easy compound over ~3 years into much higher ROI. (Neil, 2026-05-25) — content-marketing / seo.
- Do "black hat things in a white hat way": instead of self-serving self-published listicles, collaborate with other websites so *they* list you; the durable value isn't the link in the listicle, it's the relationship/partnership. Self-serving listicle gaming is trivially easy for an algorithm to detect. (Neil, 2026-05-25) — seo / content-marketing.
- Content-creation (AI content) tools appear to be on a downward trend: one content-creation tool dropped ~80% in revenue; a GEO tool founder pitched Neil but wouldn't share product details or traction. (Neil, 2026-05-25) — ai-marketing / agency-entrepreneurship.
- Lily Ray's study ("AI content creates rapid growth before a steep decline") analyzed 220 sites using AI-content tools; from peak organic traffic, **54% lost 30%+**, **39% lost 50%+**, **22% lost 75%+**. Recurring trajectory: rapid organic-page growth over 6-12 months, traffic peak ~3-6 months after the content peak, then steep decline erasing most gains and often dropping below the prior baseline within the following year (Ahrefs index). (Eric, 2026-05-25) — seo / content-marketing.
- SEO evaluates the whole site as an entity, not page-by-page; piling on junk AI pages drags down the good human-written pages, so mass AI-content sites end up needing a massive, hard-to-recover cleanup. (Eric, 2026-05-25) — seo.
- Prediction: Google will look more like ChatGPT and ChatGPT will look more like Google. (Neil, 2026-05-25) — ai-marketing / seo.
- Excluding AI overviews, ChatGPT is by far the leader in pure LLM search usage; Google is #2 (AI overviews, Gemini, AI mode). Google Lens runs ~20 billion searches/month; Google gets ~13.7 billion searches/day. (Neil — self-reported, 2026-05-25) — ai-marketing / analytics.
- Google launched an "intelligent search box" (called the biggest change to the search box in 25 years): a "+" button opens AI mode, create images, deep search, canvas, gallery, camera, files — e.g., select two dresses + a color-scheme image and it returns similar dresses in that color; you can even build an app by typing a prompt into search ("seamless Google search to AI mode," first launched January, now fully live). (Neil describes; Eric names it, 2026-05-25) — ai-marketing / seo.
- Business idea: a consulting company focused not on AI *integration* (saturated) but on **token optimization / fee reduction**, paid as a percentage of the savings — modeled on AWS cloud-cost optimizers; many companies spend millions to tens/hundreds of millions on tokens (rumored Anthropic ~$45B ARR); "boring and ugly" but would blow up. (Neil, 2026-05-25) — agency-entrepreneurship / ai-marketing.
- Counter-view: token optimization is unlikely to be a durable standalone service because the large AI providers themselves will eventually help you fine-tune and cut costs (like AWS reserved instances / Google Cloud switching pitches); if one doesn't, a competitor like Google will offer "half the token price." (Neil, 2026-05-25) — ai-marketing / agency-entrepreneurship.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> any marketing tactic that produces high short-term ROI that's really easy to do… usually, nine out of 10 times, won't last long. And as a marketer or a business owner, you should avoid those tactics.

> I've always said this, that you want to do black hat things in a white hat way.

> it's not even the link that you're going to get from it and the listicle at the end of the day, it's the relationship that you're going to get. You're going to be able to get a lot more from that partnership.

> my prediction is Google will look more like ChatGPT and ChatGPT will look more like Google in the future.

> It's a biggest change to search… to the search box in 25 years.

> I think that's a win-win model and that business would blow up even though it's boring and ugly.

> the LLMs themselves or the AI companies will try to save you as much money as possible in the long run, because if they don't, a competitor will come by… and say, "Hey, we can do similar things and we can do it for half the token price."
