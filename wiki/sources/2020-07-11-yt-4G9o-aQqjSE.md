# B2B VS B2C eCommerce Marketing - Module 3 - Part 3 - eCommerce Unlocked

## Summary
eCommerce Unlocked lesson pivoting from the course's B2C focus to B2B online sales.
Neil frames the core B2B/B2C contrast: B2B buyers are less price-sensitive, involve
more decision-makers, longer sales cycles, and are typically closed through
salespeople rather than self-serve checkout. He walks through a five-stage B2B sales
process — call preparation/lead qualification, discovery call, strategic-plan
presentation, objection handling, and decision/close — with a homework assignment at
each stage. He then covers where to find B2B leads: LinkedIn (690M+ users) and its
Sales Navigator tool for targeting by job title (manager/director/VP), importing leads
into a CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot) or outreach directly on LinkedIn. He stresses that B2B
relationships take time (90–120 days to close, longer for six/seven-figure deals) and
that a complete, credibility-signaling LinkedIn profile (headshot, bio, endorsements,
group participation) speeds relationship-building. Closes citing knobs.co as a strong
B2B eCommerce conversion-process example.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-07-11, Neil said B2B deals can take 90 to 120 days to close, and even
  longer for six- or seven-figure price points — framing patience as a required
  B2B sales trait.
- As of 2020-07-11, Neil said under 690 million LinkedIn users make it (in his view)
  "not the sexiest social network" but "the best place for B2B," and recommended
  LinkedIn Sales Navigator for targeting leads by group, industry, and interest.
- As of 2020-07-11, Neil advised targeting leads at manager, director, or VP level
  specifically, as the titles most likely to hold purchase-decision authority.
- As of 2020-07-11, Neil cited knobs.co as a strong example of a B2B eCommerce
  business with "a really strong conversion process" worth studying (self-reported
  opinion/recommendation, not independently verified).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "In B2B, what you'll find is relationships are everything. It can take you 90 to 120 days to close. Sometimes even longer if your price points are in the six figures or seven figures, so you need to be patient."

> "LinkedIn... it's not the sexiest social network, but hey, they have over 690 million users and it's the best place for B2B."

> "It's not like B2C where everyone can just pay, you know, 10, $5, whatever it may be to buy from you. And sometimes you're selling things that are 10,000, a hundred thousand. So the buyer market is much more limited."
