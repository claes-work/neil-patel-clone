# How to Use Technology to Grow Faster - Low-Cost Growth Hacks That Scale - Growth Hacking Unlocked

## Summary
Growth Hacking Unlocked Week 2, Lesson 2, on using technology as a growth "shortcut."
Opens with the Airbnb/Craigslist-scraping origin story (leveraging an existing community
to bootstrap listings) as the model for using communities (Craigslist, subreddits,
Facebook groups, Quora, niche forums, Twitter, Instagram) to grow without building an
audience from scratch. Cites Instagram's early growth focus on the photo-filters feature
and Spotify's Facebook-sharing integration as feature-led growth examples. Covers
building/installing tools (SEO tools, quizzes, calculators — can be bought cheaply
white-labeled via CodeCanyon) as a durable traffic and backlink driver, citing Bankrate's
loan calculators and NerdWallet's checking-account/credit-card comparison tools. States
he "technically bought" Ubersuggest from a prior owner then rebuilt it from scratch, and
that it now drives **over 2 million visits a month** (self-reported, consistent with the
2017-02-13 acquisition already on file). Gives checkout-optimization tactics: two-step
checkout (asking for email/name first, payment details second) reportedly lifts sales
~10%; trust seals used to lift conversion ~28% (now less, still positive); product shots
and progress bars both help. Cart-abandonment stats: 69.23% average cart-abandonment
rate; $260B recoverable industry-wide via checkout optimization (uncited); recommends
recovery via email sequences, exit popups, and lead magnets. Closes with a lead-response
stat from a Harvard Business Review study of 2,241 U.S. firms: contacting leads within
the first hour makes a meaningful conversation with a decision-maker 7x more likely;
~80–85% response rate within the first hour, dropping to ~half by four hours, and
further with each additional day. Plugs NP Digital.
