# 3 Things to Do If Your Google Rankings Drop

## Summary
Neil frames ranking loss as inevitable — not if, but when — and says even after ~16 years as an SEO his rankings still drop. He gives a three-step recovery playbook driven by Google Search Console. Step 1: identify which pages are dropping by checking average position per page/keyword in Search Console (graph up = good; flat or down = work needed), then study the top-ranking competitors' content to see what's different (usually theirs is more thorough with better keywords). Step 2: redo/update your page to include the right keywords and phrases (Search Console reveals related keywords you're missing) and make it more thorough than the competition. Step 3: ensure the title tag and meta description contain the target keyword, then submit the URL to Search Console to be re-indexed; rankings should climb back within a week to a month. The underlying principle: Google doesn't want to rank old, stale content, so continually update your pages.

## Key claims
As of 2017-07-28, Neil states:
- Losing top rankings is inevitable — "it's not a question of if ... the real question is when"; even after ~16 years as an SEO, his rankings still drop (as of 2017-07-28; implies he started in SEO ~2001).
- Step 1 — use Google Search Console to find which pages/keywords are climbing vs. dropping (it shows average position per keyword and per page); a flat or declining graph signals work is needed. Compare against the content of the top sites ranking for those phrases (as of 2017-07-28).
- Step 2 — competitors that outrank you usually have more thorough content with better keywords; redo your page to include the right keywords/phrases (Search Console shows related keywords you're missing) and make it better and more thorough — essentially updating it (as of 2017-07-28).
- Step 3 — make sure the title tag and meta description contain the keyword you want to rank for, then submit the URL to Google Search Console; it will re-index and rankings should climb back within a week to a month (as of 2017-07-28).
- Underlying principle: Google doesn't want to rank old, stale content — it wants "the best of the best" — so continually update and adjust pages (as of 2017-07-28).
