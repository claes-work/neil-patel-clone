# WordPress SEO Tutorial for Beginners | Step-by-Step to Ranking a WordPress Site

## Summary
A plugin-driven WordPress SEO tutorial organized around four factors: (1) site speed/
performance (caching, CDN, image minification, lazy load, JS bloat), citing an Amazon
slow-load-costs-billions anecdote paraphrased from a third-party article; (2) technical
SEO (sitemaps, title/meta tags, robots.txt, broken links, AMP, redirects, schema
markup, SSL); (3) content marketing/promotion (push notifications, grammar, social
sharing, email collection — "the money's in the list"); (4) security (malicious code
injection, Sucuri/Wordfence). Recommends specific plugins per factor throughout and
closes with a pitch for his agency NP Digital for done-for-you execution.

## Key claims
- Site load speed affects conversion rate; cites a third-party report that Amazon's
  slow-load incidents cost it billions, while framing that smaller sites lose
  proportionally more due to weaker brand trust (paraphrase, dated 2021-09) —
  analytics-cro.
- Reiterates the "money's in the list" email doctrine as a reason to prioritize email
  collection alongside content publishing (paraphrase, dated 2021-09) — content-marketing.
- Frames technical SEO (crawlability, indexability) as a prerequisite that makes link
  building and content worthless if unmet (paraphrase, dated 2021-09) — seo.
