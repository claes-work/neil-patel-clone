# A Simple Way to Build More Links

## Summary
A solo screen-share tutorial demonstrating a competitor-backlink-mining outreach method
using Ubersuggest, walked through live on the term "best web hosting." Neil pulls the
Backlinks report for each page ranking on page one for the target term, reviews their
domain score and link counts, then manually eyeballs each competing page's usability
(paragraph density, images, ads, skimmability) before deciding whether he could out-produce
it. He then works down each competitor's backlink list looking for genuinely relevant,
reachable link sources (skipping directories, irrelevant coupon/aggregator sites, and
"editorial staff"-bylined pages with no named author), and pitches a plain, honest
outreach email framed around better usability, not tricks. The video doubles as a light
demonstration of qualitative page-quality judgment (usability > raw backlink count) beside
the mechanical outreach process.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-10-24, Neil states that more backlinks does not always mean higher rankings —
  he attributes ranking outcomes to a mix of factors: page usability, on-page content
  quality, and domain score, not links alone (paraphrase, self-reported observation from
  live analysis of a "best web hosting" SERP).
- As of 2020-10-24, his recommended manual process: run the Backlinks report on each
  page ranking above you for the target keyword, review who links to them and when, then
  go through their linking pages individually, filtering out irrelevant, low-value, or
  hard-to-reach targets before emailing.
- As of 2020-10-24, he recommends skipping any linking page attributed only to "editorial
  staff" (no named author) as harder to convert into an outreach connection, and
  prioritizing named authors instead (paraphrase, tactical opinion).
- As of 2020-10-24, Neil states this manual, unpaid link-building process at volume
  (hundreds of emails weekly) compounds into visible ranking gains over roughly six to
  twelve months, and that once the process is proven it can be outsourced (e.g., via
  Upwork) to scale (paraphrase, self-reported).
- Nofollow vs. dofollow is explicitly deprioritized in his own targeting: he says he is
  "not too concerned" whether a link is nofollow, prioritizing link quality/relevance
  over link-attribute engineering (paraphrase).
