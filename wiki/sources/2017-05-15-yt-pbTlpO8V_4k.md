# How to Break Through and Get Your First 1,000 Followers on Facebook for Free

## Summary
In this short 2017 tactical video, Neil Patel lays out a free, organic playbook for growing a Facebook fan page from zero to its first 1,000 followers without spending on advertising. The method is built on competitor mining and content borrowing: seed the page by inviting your existing Facebook friends, then search Facebook for competing fan pages in your niche, study which of their posts earn the most likes and shares, and re-post similar content (found via Google) on your own page. He stresses that images and videos outperform article links for engagement, and recommends using Facebook Page Insights (specifically the "Pages to Watch" competitor tracking at the bottom of the Insights view) to monitor rival pages' weekly reach and identify their top-engaging posts to replicate. Once follower count grows, he advises shifting toward sharing your own website's content to drive fans back to your site, converting some into customers, and offering Facebook-exclusive promos to deepen engagement and spur purchases. The advice is characteristic of 2017-era organic Facebook tactics, several of which (friend-invite mechanics, borrowing competitor content, high organic reach) reflect a platform environment that has since changed substantially.
