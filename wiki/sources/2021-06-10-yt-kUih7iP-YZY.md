# How to Boost Your SEO Traffic with Sleeper Content (And Stop Promoting Worthless Content)

## Summary
Solo tutorial naming and explaining his "sleeper content" tactic. Observation: his own
blog posts have wildly uneven comment counts (some 0, some 300+) because he selectively
promotes (social pushes, cross-links, email blasts, push notifications) only a subset —
promotion capacity is finite, so he can't blast every post without irritating his
audience. His workaround: publish a large volume of unpromoted "sleeper" posts quietly,
then watch organic ranking movement; a post that climbs unaided to the bottom of page
two/three is a signal worth reinforcing with active promotion (cross-linking, manual
outreach, social pushes, email). Walks a live example (a "free Google website" post stuck
at the bottom of page three) and a second, better-performing example ("subscriber count",
sitting on page two, ~22,200 monthly searches per the Ubersuggest Chrome extension) to
illustrate the triage point. States results typically show within three to four weeks of
publishing.

## Key claims
- Names and defines the **"sleeper content"** tactic: publish broadly without promotion,
  then reserve promotion effort (social, email, push notifications, manual link
  outreach) only for posts that show organic traction on their own — a capacity-
  allocation answer to the finite-promotion-budget problem (self-reported doctrine,
  2021-06-10) — content-marketing, seo.
- States results from freshly published, unpromoted content typically become visible
  within three to four weeks (self-reported, 2021-06-10) — seo.
- Attributes his own traffic growth partly to this strategy over "years" of practice,
  explicitly framing most of his sleeper content as unseen by his own audience because
  it's deliberately not marketed (self-reported, 2021-06-10) — content-marketing.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> I create a ton of sleeper content... if you're publishing it, you're not really
> telling too many people about it, you're not marketing it, it doesn't all rank.

> The moment it gets to page two, I'll start doing more marketing to try to get it to
> page one.

> I've been doing this strategy for years. It works really well. And you probably
> haven't seen a lot of this content because I don't push it out in email blasts or
> social media mentions.
