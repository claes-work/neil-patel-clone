# Here's an Ad Hack That Will Produce Instant Sales

## Summary
Neil describes a remarketing tactic he says he's used for 5+ years and that self-
reportedly drives 6% of his sales (as of 2022-05-26), for both B2B and B2C. Standard
remarketing (retargeting visitors back to the same page/offer they already declined)
usually fails because they already saw that pitch and weren't convinced. His twist:
diagnose whether the original landing page was logic-driven (facts/data) or emotion-
driven (story), then retarget with a remarketing ad and landing page that uses the
**opposite** persuasion mode — data-and-testimonials if the original was emotional,
story/emotional appeal if the original was data-heavy — on the reasoning that a
different pitch has a better shot than repeating the one that already failed.

## Key claims
- Neil self-reports this "switch the persuasion mode" remarketing tactic has produced
  ~6% of his sales, used consistently for over five years without decline (self-
  reported, as of 2022-05-26).
- Standard remarketing (same offer, same pitch, back to the same page) usually fails
  because the visitor already saw and rejected that exact pitch (as of 2022-05-26).
- Recommends diagnosing whether an underperforming landing page skewed logical/data-
  driven or emotional/story-driven, then building the remarketing ad + landing page
  around the opposite persuasion mode (as of 2022-05-26).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> It doesn't matter if you're in B2B or B2C, this works. It works so well, I've been using it for over five years and I haven't seen a slowdown from it. And best of all, it's responsible for 6% of my sales.

> If someone came to your website they didn't purchase the first time, you can't get them back to your website and use the same pitch all over again... You got to switch it up and try a different type of pitch.
