# How to Generate Leads When You Have Little to No Traffic

## Summary
Neil lays out a tactical playbook for generating leads on a low-traffic site: (1) create
and promote lead magnets (ebook, quiz/poll, infographic, webinar, demo, contest, free
trial, special offer) — cites HubSpot that 99% of consumers check email daily and 59%+
of marketers call email their top ROI source — promoted free via social or paid via
Google/Facebook/LinkedIn ads; (2) link lead magnets contextually within content (top,
body, end) since most sites skip this, and 96% of visitors aren't ready to buy but may
give contact info after reading; (3) write headlines with strong verbs and curiosity
gaps (his "7 benefits of green tea, #6 will shock you" example), citing a CityCliq test
where a verb-based headline lifted conversions 90%, and ClickFlow's A/B-testing finding
that verbs increase clicks — better headlines also send Google positive CTR signals;
find proven headline patterns via Ubersuggest's Content Ideas report (ranked by social
shares, backlinks, and estimated traffic); (4) run Ubersuggest's Site Audit report to
fix page-speed and top-impact SEO errors first, in descending priority order. Closes
with a plug for his agency, Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2020-03-14, Neil cited HubSpot data that 99% of consumers check their email
  every day, and over 59% of marketers say email is their biggest source of ROI.
- As of 2020-03-14, Neil said 96% of website visitors aren't ready to buy on a given
  visit, but may give contact info after consuming valuable content — hence linking
  lead magnets within content (not just standalone pages).
- As of 2020-03-14, Neil cited a CityCliq test of four headline variants where the
  verb-based headline outperformed, producing a 90% increase in conversion rate.
- As of 2020-03-14, Neil cited ClickFlow (a headline A/B-testing tool) finding that
  verbs in headlines generate more clicks.
- As of 2020-03-14, Neil said 8 of 10 people read a headline but only 2 of 10 click
  through, and framed strong CTR as a Google ranking signal relative to competitors.
- As of 2020-03-14, Neil recommended Ubersuggest's Content Ideas report to find proven
  headlines (ranked by social shares, backlinks, estimated traffic) and its Site Audit
  report to prioritize page-speed and SEO fixes by impact.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "When you have a website that generates dozens and thousands of visitors a month, it's easy to generate leads. What happens if you have little to no traffic to your website? How do you generate leads?"

> "Remember, 96% of website visitors aren't ready to buy, but they may be willing to provide you their contact information after having read some of your content."

> "If your headline's appealing and more people click it, it sends user signals to Google that you should be ranking higher, especially if more people are clicking on your listing versus the listing that's ranking above you."
