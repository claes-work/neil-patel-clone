# How to Create the Perfect Omnichannel Marketing Strategy

## Summary
Live fireside Q&A (interviewer unnamed) where Neil argues no single marketing channel
builds a big business anymore — every channel saturates, so brands need an omnichannel
mix. He contrasts "sexy" channels (TikTok) with "boring" high-volume ones (Google Ads,
Facebook, email, CRO), reframes paid ads as an investment rather than a cost, explains
CPA and upsell/downsell with a McDonald's example, cites an Expedia-family internal test
on omnichannel CPA reduction, and gives budget-based advice on DIY vs. hiring an agency.
He also states his age (38) and a "five years ago" founding marker for NP Digital.

## Key claims (dated, paraphrase unless noted)
- "You cannot build a business through one channel anymore" — Instagram, TikTok, SEO,
  and Google all eventually get saturated, competitive, and expensive; brands leveraging
  Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, SEO, CRO, paid ads, media buys, and influencer marketing
  together saw CPA (cost per acquisition) drop roughly 9–11%, a pattern he says was first
  demonstrated by an internal Expedia-family test (Expedia/Hotels.com/ex-Trivago/
  ex-TripAdvisor) across TV, radio, and online ads. (Neil, 2023-09-28) — paid-ads.
- TikTok showed a ~54% cheaper CPA than Facebook ads in "last month" (self-reported,
  April vs. an unspecified year, likely 2023) at NP Digital, but he says TikTok still
  can't drive comparable *volume* to Google/Facebook for most D2C/e-commerce clients, so
  brands shouldn't neglect the "boring" high-volume channels for the "sexy" new one.
  (Neil, 2023-09-28) — paid-ads.
- Frames paid ad spend as a moral/business obligation, not vanity: if you believe your
  product genuinely helps people, "it's rude" not to spend money getting it in front of
  more people; the real profit driver in e-commerce paid ads is optimizing for customer
  lifetime value (repeat purchases, upsells/downsells) rather than single-transaction
  revenue. (Neil, 2023-09-28) — paid-ads.
- Budget-tiered advice for a marketing beginner: with only $10–20K to start, DIY (Shopify
  templates, cheap contractors, online courses) stretches further than hiring an agency;
  NP Digital's own ideal client is publicly traded/enterprise, so a small agency isn't
  necessarily the best early fit. Scaling past the first $5–30K/month in revenue is where
  outside expertise starts to matter more. (Neil, 2023-09-28) — agency-entrepreneurship.
- His own background, self-reported: 22 years in marketing, age 38 (as of this taping,
  consistent with a ~1985 birth year), career spent across agencies and analytics/software
  companies; founded NP Digital "five years ago" (self-reported approximate marker,
  ~2018 — looser than the registry-verified 2016-12-29 formation / public 2017 launch
  already on record), now in 16 countries, expanding to 20 by year-end. (Neil, 2023-09-28)
  — agency-entrepreneurship / biography.
- Recommends A/B testing bigger creative/messaging variables (emotional vs. logical-data
  ad angles, images vs. video) over cosmetic tweaks (button color), name-checking
  Optimizely, Crazy Egg, and VWO as testing tools. (Neil, 2023-09-28) — analytics-cro.
