# 3 Simple Tricks to INCREASE Traffic to Your Blog Without Writing More Content

## Summary

A solo tactical video giving three ways to grow blog traffic without writing new content or building new links. (1) Rewrite title tags and meta descriptions using Google Search Console: find pages with high impressions but low clicks (a click-through rate under 5%), identify the keywords they already rank decently for, and insert those keywords into the title tag and meta description — higher CTR signals relevance to Google, which over about three months nudges rankings up. (2) Continually re-share old evergreen posts: schedule repeated shares (e.g., via Buffer), use Open Graph (via the Yoast SEO plugin) to tweak the social title/description so re-shares feel fresh, and spread ~12 shares of one article across a year; more social signals and referral clicks can even earn natural links. (3) Repurpose content into new formats — video (YouTube/Facebook), podcast episodes (iTunes), and full re-posts on LinkedIn — to reach new audiences and build relationships even when those viewers never return to your site.

## Key claims

- As of 2018-05-01, Neil advises using Google Search Console to find posts with many impressions but a click-through rate under 5%, then adding the keywords they already rank for into the title tag and meta description to lift CTR.
- As of 2018-05-01, Neil claims Google demotes listings that rank well but get few clicks and promotes those getting more clicks, so improving CTR raises rankings over roughly three months (not within a week).
- As of 2018-05-01, Neil recommends continually re-sharing old evergreen posts on Twitter and Facebook rather than sharing once, because even with 100,000 followers rarely more than 5–10% of your audience sees a given post.
- As of 2018-05-01, Neil suggests using Open Graph (via the Yoast SEO plugin) to change the social-only title/description so a re-shared article feels new, and spreading ~12 shares of the same article across a year (not 10 times in one day).
- As of 2018-05-01, Neil says sustained re-sharing generates social signals and repeat visits that can prompt Google to rank a post higher and can earn organic links as people discover and link to it.
- As of 2018-05-01, Neil recommends content repurposing — turning a blog post into YouTube/Facebook video, a podcast episode on iTunes, and a full LinkedIn re-post — to get more mileage per article and build audience relationships off-site.
