# 3 Tips to Driving More Visitors Back to Your Website

## Summary
A short retention/CRO video in which Neil Patel gives three tactics to bring visitors back
to a website. (1) Remarket/retarget past visitors via Google AdWords, Facebook, or YouTube
so they see ads that pull them back — worthwhile when selling a paid product/service or
collecting leads, because prior visitors already know and partly trust you and convert
better than cold traffic. (2) Collect emails using Hello Bar (his own tool) via exit
popups, sliders, content upgrades, and scrolling top bars, then email those subscribers
new content, offers, and promos to draw them back. (3) Use browser push notifications via
pushcrew.com so visitors can subscribe through their browser and get a "come back"
notification while browsing elsewhere.

## Key claims
As of 2017-08-20, Neil states:
- Tip 1 — Remarket/retarget visitors with Google AdWords, Facebook, or YouTube ads so they
  see ads bringing them back. Prior visitors already know you and have built at least some
  trust, so they're much more likely to convert than random cold visitors; retargeting is
  well worth it when selling a paid product/service or collecting leads (as of 2017-08-20).
- Tip 2 — Collect emails with Hello Bar (his own tool) using exit popups, sliders, content
  upgrades, and scrolling top-of-site bars; the capture method is your choice. Then email
  subscribers new content, offers, and promos to keep them returning (as of 2017-08-20).
- Tip 3 — Add browser push notifications via a service called pushcrew.com; visitors opt
  in through their browser (e.g. Chrome) and later get a quick notification prompting them
  to return to your site while browsing elsewhere (as of 2017-08-20).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The people who are more likely to buy aren't just random people who haven't been to your website. It's people who've already been there once, they already know you, you've already built some sort of trust at least."

> "Using Hello Bar you can collect emails through Exit Popups, Sliders, Content Upgrades, and a lot of other different various options, such as even bars at the top of your website that scroll with the user."

> "Using a service called pushcrew.com you can put push notifications, so that way when someone's browsing your website it'll ask them, 'Hey would you like to subscribe through your browser?'"
