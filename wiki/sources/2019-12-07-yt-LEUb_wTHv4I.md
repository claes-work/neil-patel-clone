# How To Build Pages That Rank #1 On Google Consistently | SEO Tips

## Summary
Solo data-driven on-page SEO checklist. Neil opens by claiming SEO is old enough that a
copyable formula exists, and credits the underlying data to Backlinko, Authority Hacker and
Ahrefs. Eight tips: (1) ask a question in the title tag — question titles see ~14% higher CTR,
and CTR that beats the listing above you signals to Google that you should outrank it (he notes
Eric Siu's ClickFlow found the same); (2) keep title tags short — 15–40 characters perform best
now that mobile searches outnumber desktop, and Etsy found shorter URLs also performed better;
(3) actually write meta descriptions — Ubersuggest's daily crawl finds over 30% of sites have
none and let Google generate one, while adding one yields ~5.8% more clicks; (4) optimize for
featured snippets with structured markup, since Ahrefs finds 50–65% of #1 spots are dominated
by featured snippets; (5) keyword placement and ~1% density still matter (Authority Hacker's
million-SERP analysis shows keywords in H1/title/meta correlate with page-one rankings) but
4–5% is stuffing, because Google's built-in thesaurus understands related terms; (6) put the
keyword in the URL — keyword URLs average 45% higher CTR; (7) optimize for the parent topic,
using Moz's SEO Beginner's Guide hub-and-spoke internal linking as the model; (8) long-form
content dominates page one per Brian Dean, getting 77% more clicks — but only where the intent
warrants length ("two plus two" doesn't need 5,000 words). He points to Ubersuggest's site
audit report to automate the on-page checks, and closes plugging Neil Patel Digital.

## Key claims
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil said he had been doing SEO for over 10 years and that enough data exists that a repeatable ranking formula can be copied.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil said question-based title tags typically see a 14% higher CTR, citing Backlinko and corroborating data from ClickFlow (Eric Siu's tool).
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil argued CTR is a ranking signal in practice: if the #2 listing gets more clicks than #1, that tells Google #2 should be #1.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil said title tags between 15 and 40 characters tend to do really well — shorter beats keyword-stuffed length — because more Google searches now come from mobile than desktop.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil cited an Etsy study that shortening URLs (not just title tags) performed better than longer ones.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil said Ubersuggest's own daily crawling found over 30% of sites have no meta description and let Google write one for them, and that adding a meta description can get 5.8% more clicks by controlling the message.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil cited Ahrefs that 50–65% of all #1 spots are dominated by featured snippets, and recommended structured markup both to win the snippet box and to improve mobile-friendliness.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil cited an Authority Hacker analysis of a million SERPs finding keywords placed in H1 tags, meta descriptions and title tags correlate strongly with page-one rankings — the old-school basics still matter.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil recommended roughly 1% keyword density and called 4–5% keyword stuffing, arguing Google's built-in thesaurus/dictionary already understands related terms (dog food ~ puppy food; apple the fruit vs Apple the computer) so heavy stuffing is obsolete.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil said URLs containing the searched keyword average 45% higher CTR than URLs without it, because the URL signals relevance to the searcher.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil recommended optimizing for the parent topic, citing Moz's SEO Beginner's Guide: a main overview page with sub-pages per subtopic all internally linking back up to the parent ranks higher.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil cited a Brian Dean study that long-form pages are most prevalent on page one and get 77% more clicks plus more social engagement — but qualified that length only works when the search intent warrants it; a "two plus two" query wants the answer, not a 5,000-word article.
- As of 2019-12-07, Neil recommended running a URL through Ubersuggest's site audit report to get the specific title/meta/H1 changes needed instead of doing the optimization manually.
