# AI Marketing — hub

Neil Patel's 2025–26 AI-marketing thesis holds that AI is simultaneously a **business
model** (anyone can build an AI-services company from zero), a **distribution shift**
(discovery has fractured off Google into a cross-platform "search everywhere" landscape
where brands must be *chosen*, not just found), and a **channel change inside Google
itself** (ads and organic merging into an AI mode driven by conversation intent). Across
all of it his stance is that AI augments rather than replaces the marketer. The
forward-looking bets below are dated so they can be scored against reality later.

## AI-service business models

Framed in "How to Get Rich in the New Era of A.I." as a 2025 "AI Gold Rush" comparable
in scale to the arrival of Google and smartphones, with a business openable from zero.

- **Niche-selection front-end (2025-02-18):** start with what you know and are
  passionate about (or burn out); evaluate a vertical by looking up incumbents' market
  cap — the larger (into the billions+), the bigger the opportunity — and start with a
  *subset* before expanding, citing Facebook (originally Ivy League students) and Amazon
  (originally only books) [[2025-02-18-yt-IaJKo9CxxXA]].
- **The "brain vs. body" framing (2025-02-18):** models are the brain, agents are the
  body. Verbatim: *"If ChatGPT, Gemini, Grok, etc. are like the brain, the AI agents are
  like the body that can actually go and do things on their own."* [[2025-02-18-yt-IaJKo9CxxXA]]
- **Five money-making models (2025-02-18)** [[2025-02-18-yt-IaJKo9CxxXA]]:
  1. **AI agents** — buildable with no coding on **CrewAI**; use cases: sales lead
     scoring, marketing personalization, analytics (which traffic drives revenue),
     finance/LTV analysis.
  2. **Translation services** — localize websites, content and video with **ElevenLabs**;
     rationale that combined non-US markets hold more money than the US alone.
  3. **AI podcast services** — clone a voice to turn blogs/newsletters into podcasts;
     use **Opus** to cut long-form into short-form clips.
  4. **AI data visualization / infographics** — a **Piktochart-style** tool; sold because
     infographics earn backlinks (rankings) and lift conversions.
  5. **AI website building** — on **10Web**, targeting newly formed LLCs, with SEO and
     blog content as upsells.
- **Endorsed 2025 tool stack:** CrewAI, ElevenLabs, Opus, a Piktochart-style
  infographic tool, 10Web [[2025-02-18-yt-IaJKo9CxxXA]].
- **Getting customers is the constraint (2025-02-18):** the models matter less than
  acquisition. Self-reported origin story — at 16 he cold-called ad-spenders off Google
  after school and offered "pay only if you like it" work to build case studies; would
  today target recently funded companies on Crunchbase [[2025-02-18-yt-IaJKo9CxxXA]].
- Verbatim: *"The AI Gold Rush is here, the depth of Google and smartphones has arrived.
  Vertical AI agents are about to start replacing the entire teams."* [[2025-02-18-yt-IaJKo9CxxXA]]
- Self-reported market datum cited as support: global podcast market projected from
  $36.2B (2024) to $47.83B (2025), CAGR 31.8%, reaching $134.4B by 2029
  [[2025-02-18-yt-IaJKo9CxxXA]].

## AI search visibility / Search Everywhere Optimization

Central 2026 reframe: SEO isn't dead, it got bigger — the job shifted from *getting
found on Google* to *getting chosen across the entire internet*.

- **"Search Everywhere Optimization" (2025-07-23):** optimize to be chosen on every
  platform where decisions get made, not just found on Google. Verbatim: *"Traditional
  SEO was about getting found on Google. Search everywhere optimization is about getting
  chosen across the entire internet."* [[2025-07-23-yt-39o0uYPo4jU]]
- **~73% of search is off-Google (2025-07-23):** Google handles ~13.7B searches/day but
  that is only ~27% of search activity; the other ~73% is scattered across Instagram,
  TikTok, Amazon, Reddit, YouTube and ChatGPT [[2025-07-23-yt-39o0uYPo4jU]].
- **Constellation of micro-decisions (2025-07-23):** the funnel is replaced by
  simultaneous platform-specific decisions — what to click (Google), what to trust
  (Reddit/reviews), what to buy (Amazon/TikTok Shop), what to try (app store), what to
  think (YouTube/podcasts), what to believe (ChatGPT/Claude), who to follow
  (Instagram/LinkedIn). Verbatim: *"The modern consumer journey isn't a funnel anymore.
  It's a constellation of micro decisions."* [[2025-07-23-yt-39o0uYPo4jU]]
- **The "Google trap" (2025-07-23):** rankings can look healthy while conversions stay
  flat because customers decide elsewhere [[2025-07-23-yt-39o0uYPo4jU]].
- **Validation over visibility / ChatGPT as a ranking system (2025-07-23):** AI
  summarizes based on who is mentioned most and trusted fastest, so third-party mentions
  (Reddit, citations, reviews, podcasts) beat self-published visibility. Verbatim: *"AI
  doesn't scroll through search results the way humans do. AI summarizes. And it
  summarizes based on who gets mentioned the most and trusted the fastest."* [[2025-07-23-yt-39o0uYPo4jU]]
- **Prioritize with RICE (2025-07-23):** score Reach × Impact × Confidence × Ease to
  pick 2–3 platforms rather than chasing omnipresence — *"The goal isn't omnipresence,
  it's strategic presence."* [[2025-07-23-yt-39o0uYPo4jU]]
- **"Digital PR is the new SEO" (2025-12-10):** win ChatGPT-as-ranking-system by getting
  *other* authoritative sources to cite you (not citing yourself), plus niche
  listicles/top-10s, correct formatting (headers, bullets, tables), last-updated dates,
  schema, and case studies/stats. Verbatim: *"This is going to be the new SEO. Almost no
  one is doing it… digital PR where it's really at and that makes the difference."*
  [[2025-12-10-yt-hXPALnu3Y6I]] Self-reported: NP Digital pitches a 100+-person digital
  PR team as the done-for-you path [[2025-12-10-yt-hXPALnu3Y6I]].

## Google AI-mode (organic + paid, 2026)

From "How Google Organic and Paid Search Will Work in 2026" — framed as Google's biggest
business-model change in 25 years, forced by ChatGPT's growth (self-reported 700M active
users in ~3 years) against Google's self-reported $200B+ search-ad revenue.

- **Keyword → conversation-intent relevance (2025-09-10):** targeting moves from
  individual keywords to full conversation context; ads trigger on inferred need even
  when the user never types the advertiser's words. Verbatim: *"Keywords are dead.
  Google's new AI mode doesn't just read what you type. It reads your entire
  conversation."* Intent signals described as ~10x richer [[2025-09-10-yt-Cglbi0yslMw]].
- **The whole brand surface trains the AI (2025-09-10):** Google builds a "brand
  profile" from website, reviews and social; a weak surface trains the system against
  you, and "feed hygiene" (rich, current product data) drives match quality — "garbage
  in, garbage out." Verbatim: *"Your Google Ads account isn't just an ad platform
  anymore. It's an AI training system, and you're either teaching it to win or lose."*
  [[2025-09-10-yt-Cglbi0yslMw]]
- **Mechanics agencies were briefed on (2025-09-10):** Performance Max plus a new "Max
  for Search" campaign type auto-surface in AI mode; Google briefed that ads in AI mode
  would roll out fully before Q4 2025; self-reported 100M+ users already on AI mode
  [[2025-09-10-yt-Cglbi0yslMw]].
- **Playbook (2025-09-10):** stop micromanaging keywords/bids; give the AI good
  instructions plus high-quality conversion data via smart bidding; track *real/repeat
  customers*, not raw leads; move early because performance compounds weekly "like
  interest" and first-movers become hard to catch [[2025-09-10-yt-Cglbi0yslMw]].
- **Open questions flagged (2025-09-10):** whether users will click inside AI
  conversations, and whether Google shifts to per-impression or per-conversion pricing
  [[2025-09-10-yt-Cglbi0yslMw]]. Self-reported: NP Digital manages $1B+ in Google ad
  spend [[2025-09-10-yt-Cglbi0yslMw]].

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION / tension: For Google AI-mode ad targeting Neil declares "keywords are
> dead" [[2025-09-10-yt-Cglbi0yslMw]], yet his Search-Everywhere guidance still tells
> marketers to weave keywords into scripts, captions, on-screen text and descriptions to
> rank across discovery surfaces [[2025-12-10-yt-hXPALnu3Y6I]]. Reconciles as
> channel-specific: keyword *bidding* is dead inside Google's conversational ad system,
> while keyword *packaging* still aids ranking on non-AI discovery platforms — noted, not
> hidden.

## The 2026 bets (dated)

An explicit, dated prediction cluster from "The 8 Marketing Trends Neil Patel Is Betting
On in 2026" (2025-12-10), to be scored against reality later. Anchor data (as of
2025-12-10): 26% of searches end without a click; Instagram sees ~6.5B searches/day
(YouTube 3B+); Snap signed a ~$400M deal with Perplexity to power its search
[[2025-12-10-yt-hXPALnu3Y6I]]. Verbatim: *"26% of searches now end without a click.
Instagram alone sees over 6.5 billion searches every day. And Chad GPT is about to launch
ads that behave like Facebook, not Google."* [[2025-12-10-yt-hXPALnu3Y6I]]

All eight bets dated **2025-12-10** [[2025-12-10-yt-hXPALnu3Y6I]]:

1. **In-platform lead capture** — stop sending social traffic off-platform (platforms
   throttle reach/raise ad cost when you do); keep the flow in-platform via
   comment-triggered DM automation (e.g. ManyChat) for higher-intent leads.
2. **Search Everywhere Optimization** — format/package content to rank across all
   discovery surfaces; build topic clusters; optimize for conversational full-phrase
   search. Verbatim: *"Creating content that's formatted, structured, and packaged in a
   way that ranks across all major discovery surfaces, not just traditional search
   engines."*
3. **AI slop is a liability** — an NP Digital study found free and paid AI tools had
   nearly identical hallucination rates; top performers use AI for first drafts/ideation
   only and cross-reference every stat. Verbatim: *"We ran a study comparing free and
   paid AI tools, and the hallucination rates were almost identical."* Sub-bet: companies
   blame AI for layoffs as a cleaner public-markets story than admitting over-hiring.
4. **ChatGPT-as-ranking / "digital PR is the new SEO"** — win AI citation via
   third-party authoritative mentions, niche listicles, formatting, last-updated dates
   and schema (see Search-Everywhere section).
5. **Identity-based AI ads** — when ChatGPT launches ads they combine Facebook-style
   identity/behavior targeting with Google-level intent (OpenAI hiring ex-Facebook/IG ad
   engineers); brands compete for *category associations*, not keywords. Verbatim:
   *"Google shows you ads based on what you type. Facebook shows you ads based on who you
   are. Chat GBT will do both."* and *"You no longer are competing for keywords. You're
   competing for associations."*
6. **Browser AI** — browsers become AI platforms (Chrome's Gemini, ChatGPT's Atlas,
   Perplexity's Comet) that summarize/recommend/advertise before users reach any site;
   prepare via Google AI Max (AMAX) campaigns and structured data. Verbatim: *"Treat
   your site as a data source, not just a destination. The browser will pull from it even
   if users never click through."*
7. **Live video** — an under-supplied trust signal amid AI-generated everything; YouTube
   reports nearly 30% of users watch ≥1 live/week while few brands go live, and
   auto-generates horizontal/vertical clips. Go live weekly, repurpose 60-second
   moments.
8. **Auto-dubbing** — language is no longer a distribution barrier; YouTube multi-language
   audio and tools like HeyGen dub into dozens of languages in the creator's own voice.
   Self-reported: *"Creators are getting 30 to 50% of their views from languages they
   don't even speak."*

## AI + humans

Consistent through-line: AI augments the marketer rather than fully replacing them.

- **AI + humans combined (2025-03-25):** AI (Adobe Firefly, ChatGPT, Gemini, native
  Facebook/Google ad AI) speeds content creation and data analysis but wins paired with
  people; its real value is freeing you for strategy and execution over mundane tasks.
  Verbatim: *"what we found is AI isn't better than humans and humans aren't better than
  AI the winning combination at least for marketing is AI and humans combined."*
  [[2025-03-25-yt-6RNJpItArSQ]]
- **AI multiplies judgment (2025-12-10):** even amid the AI-slop warning, AI is a
  first-draft/ideation and discovery tool, not a replacement for judgment. Verbatim:
  *"AI doesn't replace your judgment, it multiplies it."* [[2025-12-10-yt-hXPALnu3Y6I]]
- **Layoffs narrative (2025-12-10):** Neil argues AI is often *blamed* for layoffs
  rather than being the actual cause — reinforcing the augment-not-replace stance.
  Verbatim: *"A lot of companies are blaming AI for the layoffs. Not because AI actually
  replaced people, but because it's a cleaner story for the public markets."*
  [[2025-12-10-yt-hXPALnu3Y6I]]
