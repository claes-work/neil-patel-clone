# Email Marketing — hub

Email is the channel Patel repeatedly names as his highest-converting and highest-revenue asset — an *owned* audience that survives platform algorithm changes. This page synthesizes his framework across four moves: building the list, timing and sequencing the sends, wiring email into a sales funnel, and treating the list as the backstop to platform risk. Where he cites his own numbers, they are marked self-reported; nothing here is his advice divorced from the source that grounds it.

## List building

Patel's fast-list-growth playbook centers on two on-site capture tactics plus owned-audience discipline.

- **Two tactics to grow a list fast: exit popups and content upgrades** (2017-05-16). Built with the free tool Hello Bar, no design or technical skill required. [[2017-05-16-yt-3WX54dRt0CU]]
- **His best-converting opt-in is an exit popup with an animated GIF background that previews the offer** (self-reported, 2017-05-16). The GIF should show exactly what the visitor will receive (e-book, newsletter) so they feel comfortable subscribing.
  > "That's one of my best converting exit pop-ups. It kills any other opt-in that I have when it comes to numbers." [[2017-05-16-yt-3WX54dRt0CU]]
  > "This shows people what they're going to get before they subscribe, it makes them more comfortable, they'll opt-in."
- **Popups convert despite being disliked** (2017-05-16). His ubiquity argument:
  > "You may say that exit pop-ups are tacky, everyone hates them and you know what? I don't want to use them, but they work. If they didn't work you wouldn't see them everywhere." [[2017-05-16-yt-3WX54dRt0CU]]
- **Content upgrades are post-specific bonuses** — a cheat sheet or checklist repackaging a post's key points — placed within the first few paragraphs of the blog post, triggering a name-and-email capture (2017-05-16). He self-reports including a cheat sheet on every post he writes. [[2017-05-16-yt-3WX54dRt0CU]]
- **Lead magnets to trade for the email**: PDFs, quizzes, and action plans; neilpatel.com uses an exit-intent quiz that trades an action plan for an email (2025-04-29). WordPress integrates with major email providers (e.g. Mailchimp) via a simple plugin. [[2025-04-29-yt-ybttlXsLPXc]]
- **"You can't build a brand without collecting emails"** (2019-04-22). In the DIY-marketing workflow, email collection (via Hello Bar sliders/popups) plus one-click push-notification subscription (the free tool Subscribers) is the brand/distribution step — strongest when email and push are combined; getting people back ~7 times makes brand-building much likelier. [[2019-04-22-yt-DoLzQN1m7sU]]
  > "It's not just about getting people to your site, it's about building a brand. You can't build a brand without collecting emails." [[2019-04-22-yt-DoLzQN1m7sU]]

## Send timing & cadence

The signature contrarian claim: send *against* the crowd, and sequence follow-ups on fixed intervals keyed to business type.

- **Contrarian send-timing heuristic** (2021-01-25): Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday are the most popular send days, so inboxes are bombarded then — send **Monday and Friday** for the most opens, and **Sunday** has also performed well for him (self-reported).
  > "Tuesdays, Wednesdays and Thursdays are the most popular days, but if you want the most opens, because a lot of people get bombarded on those days, I recommend sending emails on Mondays and Fridays, which has been super effective for me. I also do Sundays, which has done really well too." [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
- **Abandoned-cart cadence (e-commerce / digital goods)**: email at **4 hours → 16 hours → 2 days** to recover the sale; image-rich or text-rich both work (2021-01-25). Sending three abandoned-cart emails drives ~69% more orders than one. [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
- **Nurture cadence (new lead / customer)**: **immediately → +2 days → +4 days**, focused on helping and guiding rather than only selling; keep following up a week, a month, or years later as long as you keep building value (2021-01-25). [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
- **Flash-sale cadence** (e.g. Black Friday, Christmas): four emails — **day 1, day 2, skip day 3, day 4, day 5** (2021-01-25). [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
- **Win-back**: for buyers lapsed a few months, send a coupon/discount code or news about what's new to win them back (2021-01-25). [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
- **Opens are not the goal — action is** (2021-01-25):
  > "If everyone opens your emails and reads them but doesn't act on them, it doesn't really matter and it doesn't do much for you." [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
- **His own send frequency**: two to three emails per week, which he calls super effective (self-reported, 2021-01-25). [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
- **Supporting best-practice stats** (2021-01-25): personalized subject lines lift open rates ~50%; video in email can raise click rates ~300%. [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
- **A/B test subject lines** (2025-03-25): among the three things worth A/B testing (creatives, landing pages, email subject lines); tools like Mailchimp or ConvertKit have A/B testing built in. [[2025-03-25-yt-6RNJpItArSQ]]

### Case study — Semantics FM (self-reported)

A site selling mostly music-related courses targeted Generation Z via an email funnel and generated **$4.5 million over 10 launches**; email alone accounted for **$1.2 million of revenue in a 12-month period** and **29% of total revenue**, with a record March of $125,000, and a single "Dubstep, Start to Finish" course email generating over $100,000 (self-reported case study, 2021-01-25). [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]

Voice anchors for why he favors email (2021-01-25):
> "You don't need a fortune to be fortunate. And that's why I love email marketing." [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]
> "I once met a guy named John Reese who used to run a site called income.com. And he once told me his email list is like his digital ATM machine. Sends out an email and makes a ton of money." [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]

## Email in the funnel

Email is the follow-up engine behind Patel's automated-webinar product funnel — and, by his account, the single highest-revenue channel in it.

- **Automated-webinar funnel** (2018-11-27): a $1,000+ digital product sold primarily through an automated webinar (WebinarJam) reinforced by a multi-day **InfusionSoft** email sequence with countdown-timer urgency. [[2018-11-27-yt-dU4rWLHAcoo]]
- **Live/follow-up split ~50/50**: the webinar converts at **3.6 sales per 100 registrations**, split roughly **1.8 from the live webinar and 1.8 from the email follow-up sequence** — i.e. the email follow-up matches the live event (self-reported, 2018-11-27). [[2018-11-27-yt-dU4rWLHAcoo]]
  > "For every 100 webinar signups how many sales do you think i'm getting? ... i get 3.6 ... a hundred webinar registrations would just name an email and i get 3.6 sales." [[2018-11-27-yt-dU4rWLHAcoo]]
- **The email stack**: a seven-day InfusionSoft sequence whose last two days use a "Plus This" countdown timer that expires links to create urgency, plus list segmentation (separate buckets for people who clicked the buy button but didn't buy, or watched only the first five minutes of the webinar) (2018-11-27). Handwritten-style images (PicSnippets) with the recipient's name are sent to non-attendees, and next-day (never same-day) SMS via TurboDial hands warm responders to a phone rep. [[2018-11-27-yt-dU4rWLHAcoo]]
- **Email is his highest-converting, highest-revenue channel in this model** (self-reported, 2018-11-27): he cited ~103,000 unique visitors from email and said email drives ~40-something percent more revenue than anything else, despite not being the majority of traffic. [[2018-11-27-yt-dU4rWLHAcoo]]
- **The general workflow claim** (2021-01-25): email is the highest-converting marketing channel he's seen — with the caveat that recipients must *act*, not just open.
  > "Your highest converting marketing channel from what I've seen, everything I've experienced, is going to be email marketing. If it's not, that means you're doing something wrong." [[2021-01-25-yt-Gxd4NeKQg58]]

## Owned-audience backstop

The strategic case: platforms can change algorithms or disappear overnight, but the email list is yours.

- **Email survives algorithm changes** (2025-04-01): social media could vanish overnight if a platform changes its algorithm, so an email list is an owned channel you keep selling through. Set one up for free (e.g. Mailchimp) in minutes.
  > "The best part about email list, it's that alternative secret weapon that's yours forever that people can't take away from you." [[2025-04-01-yt-E6ZSIZ89Ekg]]
- **Email amplifies platform content** (2025-04-01): YouTube heavily weights a video's engagement in its first 24 hours, so emailing the list to watch a new video can help it go viral — email as a *lever on* the platforms, not just a hedge against them. [[2025-04-01-yt-E6ZSIZ89Ekg]]
- **Own the audience; direct traffic to email** (2025-04-29): relying on social platforms means you don't truly own your audience — drive traffic to your own website/landing pages and collect emails for direct communication and selling. Email subscribers are more valuable than social followers because the emailed subscriber is likely to see the message, whereas most social followers won't see a post. [[2025-04-29-yt-ybttlXsLPXc]]
  > "Long-term wealth comes from leveraging an audience, not short-term money grabs. However, if you rely on social media platforms, that means you truly don't own your audience." [[2025-04-29-yt-ybttlXsLPXc]]

## Deliverability & owned-channel tactics (2017 solo era)

Two 2017-dated additions that sit *upstream* of the sending playbook above — getting into the
inbox at all, and mining an overlooked owned surface.

- **Email deliverability system (2017-08-17).** Deliverability (inbox placement) is the
  precondition for opens: **gradually warm up the sending IP/list** (ramp 1k → … → 100k rather
  than blasting cold), use **casual, non-salesy subject lines**, **scrub non-openers**, and
  keep emails **text-based / low-HTML** to dodge spam filters and the Promotions tab
  ([[2017-08-17-yt-7l7RocbBS4M]]).
- **Email signature as a traffic channel (2017-08-13).** Turn your signature into
  distribution: link your site, add **one** social-follow ask, a "check out my latest post"
  link, and a link to press about you. Governed by his recurring **overwhelm cap — never more
  than ~2 asks at once** (the same "don't overwhelm" heuristic he applies to opt-ins and CTAs)
  ([[2017-08-13-yt-LeRnQlUrwl4]]).
- **Double your open rates — four levers (2017-10-07).** Test the **sender / "from" name**, write
  **casual, lowercase subject lines**, **personalize the recipient's name**, and keep testing —
  concrete, dated open-rate mechanics that sit upstream of the "opens aren't the goal, action is"
  point above ([[2017-10-07-yt-E5UZug6310g]]).
- **Email click-through hacks (2017-10-18).** Inside the email: **tell a story**, use only **2–3
  links**, and write **casual, benefit-driven anchor text** ([[2017-10-18-yt-cio1MNOYiaw]]).
- **Sell more to existing customers (2017-10-01).** Three levers on the base you already own:
  **upsells/downsells** (speed + automation drive conversion), **email-marketing automation**
  (Hello Bar capture + sequences), and **keep shipping new products to existing buyers** (Amazon as
  exemplar) — an early statement of his retention/LTV lever ([[2017-10-01-yt-GAfvXdWt3a8]]).

## 2018 solo-era email tactics (@neilpatel P2)

- **Deliverability via engagement + list hygiene (2018-06-07).** Above ~100K subscribers open
  rates tank, so **scrub the list monthly**; **engagement drives inbox placement**, so favor
  **text-based emails with ≤3 links** and white-list via **Return Path**. He cites **Ryan Deiss**
  landing a single monetized email at ~**$100K+** ([[2018-06-07-yt-tlUeqiRw15g]]).
- **List growth = give a worthwhile offer; content-upgrades convert best (2018-05-03).** Grow the
  list with **content upgrades** (the highest-converting opt-in), an **animated exit pop-up**, and
  **webinars/quizzes** for higher-quality subscribers — all runnable free via his own **Hello Bar**
  ([[2018-05-03-yt-G6oDfZhxWgc]]).
