# NP Digital (Neil Patel Digital, LLC)

Neil Patel's flagship venture since ~2016/2017: a **global performance-marketing agency**
(SEO, content, paid media, CRO, PR). Co-founded with **[[mike-kamo]]**. The commercial engine
behind the neilpatel.com brand — the free content and [[ubersuggest]] tool funnel into the
agency's retainers.

## What it does

Full-service digital-marketing agency [📰/🗣️: npdigital.com/about; neilpatel.com/about]:

- **SEO & content** (~65% of revenue per a 2020 report), **paid media / performance**,
  **CRO, analytics, PR/earned media, creative**. Serves both enterprise and SMB.
- Owns/operates a portfolio of **martech tools**: [[ubersuggest]] (acquired 2017) and
  **AnswerThePublic** (acquired 2022-05-31, terms undisclosed, brokered by FE International)
  [📰: searchengineland.com/np-digital-acquires-answerthepublic-385501].

## Controversy — FTX clawback suit [✅ court-documented]

> ⚠️ In **Nov 2024** the **FTX** bankruptcy estate sued Neil Patel and his entities in the
> **U.S. Bankruptcy Court, District of Delaware** (headlined "**$55M**"), seeking to claw back
> **~$30.8M** paid **Oct 2021–Oct 2022** to Patel-controlled entities — **Neil Patel Digital
> LLC (~$16M+)** and **"I'm Kind of a Big Deal LLC" (~$14.8M)**. FTX alleged duplicative,
> exaggerated, and partly undelivered marketing work, and that NP Digital charged FTX ~**12×**
> its normal rate. ✅ (seroundtable.com; ppc.land; Kroll FTX docket)
> **Patel's response:** "We aren't being sued for $55 million… they sued 70-plus other
> companies the same week … to try and claw back money." 🗣️ A clawback/preference action is
> not a finding of wrongdoing; **outcome unresolved** as of 2026-07-14.

Diffuse third-party criticism also frames NP Digital's free tools/blog as **lead-bait** into
$5k–$20k+/mo retainers, with scattered BBB/Reddit complaints about deliverables. 📰-ish (not a
landmark exposé — do not overstate). See [[../sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §14.
