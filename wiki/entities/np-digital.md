# NP Digital (Neil Patel Digital, LLC)

Neil Patel's flagship venture since ~2016/2017: a **global performance-marketing agency**
(SEO, content, paid media, CRO, PR). Co-founded with **[[mike-kamo]]**. The commercial engine
behind the neilpatel.com brand — the free content and [[ubersuggest]] tool funnel into the
agency's retainers.

## What it does

Full-service digital-marketing agency [📰/🗣️: npdigital.com/about; neilpatel.com/about]:

- **SEO & content** (~65% of revenue per a 2020 report), **paid media / performance**,

## AnswerThePublic acquisition — self-reported economics (2023, vs. registry "undisclosed")

Across five 2023 interviews Neil discloses financial specifics the registry-verified press
(searchengineland.com) records only as "terms undisclosed": price **~$8.6M total** (variously
described as "$6M upfront + $2.6M over six quarters" or "originally negotiated toward ~$6M with an
earn-out"), an explicit self-described **"overpay"** at an **~8x multiple**, doing no more than
**~$1.2M/year** (~$100-130K/month, "not fully burdened" — the seller had no dedicated team) at
acquisition, grown post-deal to **~$200-400K/month EBITDA**. 🗣️ The acquisition thesis was never
standalone-SaaS profitability but replicating **Ubersuggest's lead-gen halo** (Ubersuggest drives
**~40% of NP Digital's paying clients**; AnswerThePublic reached ~70-80% of Ubersuggest's traffic
but under 5% of agency revenue) via **land-and-expand cold-calling**: diligence showed ~70% of
Fortune 1000 companies already used the free tool, so NP Digital cold-called those users
division-by-division, in one case growing a single enterprise account to **$5.5M/year in services
revenue**. 🗣️

## Financing history — declined VC, bank AR-financing instead (2023 self-report)

Neil states NP Digital **declined venture-capital pitches** (a Sequoia conversation, a call with
Bezos Expeditions) once a contact told him the business was profitable enough to get **banks to
float accounts-receivable on ad spend** instead of raising equity — financed via a relationship
with Canadian bank **CIBC** at roughly Fed-rate + 3% (vs. ~11-13% for higher-risk deals). The
pitch, as he frames it: most marketing-industry money is spent on **services, not software**
(HubSpot's market cap vs. Omnicom's comparable EBITDA), so the opportunity was disrupting
old-line holding companies (WPP, Omnicom), not competing with software incumbents. 🗣️ NP Digital's
revenue mix has shifted toward **~71-72% word-of-mouth** (2023), driven by enterprise
land-and-expand referrals across sibling divisions of the same conglomerate client; separately,
**~76-77% of client acquisition** overall is attributed to employee/client referrals, RFPs, and
word-of-mouth. 🗣️
([[../sources/2023-05-04-yt-lA55yrabOSQ]], [[../sources/2023-04-06-yt-Ji5urXQRoLo]],
[[../sources/2023-04-24-yt-tJekpDfTp00]], [[../sources/2023-04-29-yt-rifqO7we8uo]])

States NP Digital has **received and declined multiple acquisition offers** — private equity and
IPO-adjacent bank conversations including Goldman Sachs over "the prior two years," plus a
separately-cited **$515M offer** "in a bad economy" — framing the business as something he does
not want to sell ("the business is like a baby... I don't want to give my kids away"). 🗣️
([[../sources/2023-03-30-yt-fslIfiEouqs]], [[../sources/2023-06-10-yt-4bs4lxQsvGQ]])

## Brazil expansion — origin story (2023 self-report)

Traces the Brazil office to a **Hotmart conference** speaking invitation and a multi-hour
conversation with **Andre Esteves** (founder of BTG Pactual), who framed Brazil as "the wild, wild
west... like it was in America" — after which Neil expanded, initially serving US public companies
entering Brazil before winning Brazil-based clients directly. States the company's **first
employee was hired ~April 2018** (officially in the US; the Brazil office itself was stood up
earlier by a colleague named "Marine"). 🗣️ ([[../sources/2023-05-27-yt-luLpx7-Pdtg]])

## Controversy — FTX clawback suit [✅ court-documented]

> ⚠️ In **Nov 2024** the **FTX** bankruptcy estate sued Neil Patel and his entities in the
> **U.S. Bankruptcy Court, District of Delaware** (headlined "**$55M**"), seeking to claw back
> **~$30.8M** paid **Oct 2021–Oct 2022** to Patel-controlled entities — **Neil Patel Digital
> LLC (~$16M+)** and **"I'm Kind of a Big Deal LLC" (~$14.8M)**. FTX alleged duplicative,
> exaggerated, and partly undelivered marketing work, and that NP Digital charged FTX ~**12×**
> its normal rate. ✅ (seroundtable.com; ppc.land; Kroll FTX docket)
> **Patel's response:** "We aren't being sued for $55 million… they sued 70-plus other
> companies the same week … to try and claw back money." 🗣️ A clawback/preference action is
> not a finding of wrongdoing; **outcome unresolved** as of 2026-07-14.

Diffuse third-party criticism also frames NP Digital's free tools/blog as **lead-bait** into
$5k–$20k+/mo retainers, with scattered BBB/Reddit complaints about deliverables. 📰-ish (not a
landmark exposé — do not overstate). See [[../sources/2026-07-14-research-biography-dossier]] §14.
